10\. THE DEATH OF NANABUSHU’S NEPHEW, THE WOLF.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Waasaagoneshkang (Part I Series 1, Bois Fort — per Corbiere roster; per task spec). <span class="l-orig">(1) Mīdạc kä∙i∙jipa‘kạguzitä‘tiwāt. Mīdạc kä∙i∙jikạbäciwāt, misa´ kägä‘t unisäni inī‘ᵘ awäsiyạn. Änte kāwinigu pä∙i∙gwạnunk ayäsiwạg. Pạnägu pạpämigusiwạg. Mīdạc kägä‘t ugitcimämi‘kawi∙ä∙nini inī‘ᵘ awäsiyạn. Kägä‘t mino∙ä∙yä.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-bakagozitaadiwaad. Mii dash gaa-izhi-gabeshiwaad, mii sa gegeph onisaanii iniw awesiyan. Aandi gaawiinigo bezhigwaning ayaasiwag. Banaagigo babaamigoziwag. Mii dash gegeph ogichimamikawiniwaa\* iniw awesiyan. Gegeph minwaayaa.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon they separated from one another. And when (he and the Wolf) went into camp, it was truly (the Wolf) that killed the game. Naturally not in one spot they remained, always from one place to another they went. And so truly was (the Wolf) ever killing the pick of game. Truly was he living well.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-bakagozitaadiwaad (and so then they parted from each other) · gaa-izhi-gabeshiwaad (when they made camp) · mii sa gegeph onisaanii iniw awesiyan (truly it was he that killed those game-animals) · aandi gaawiinigo bezhigwaning ayaasiwag (nowhere in one place did they stay) · banaagigo babaamigoziwag (always they kept moving camp about) · mii dash gegeph ogichimamikawiniwaa iniw awesiyan (and so truly he kept coming easily upon those game-animals) · gegeph minwaayaa (truly he was living well).</span>

*Jones’s English footnote ¹ at the title (p.89): "¹ For other versions see Nos. 31, 45."*

<span class="l-orig">(2) Ningutingigu pạpämigusiwāt, äcimawingwämini᷑ inī‘ᵘ unicōmäyạn. ‘A°a‘ᵘ ma∙i∙ngạn äcinimini‘ka᷑mạt, “Intạckä ninitug mänäpạmigwän,” udinän ‘i‘i‘ᵘ kiciginipänit.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningodingigo babaamigoziwaad, ezhi-mawingwaamini\* iniw oziishenyan. A’aw ma’iingan ezhi-iniminika\*mad, “Intashke\* nindinitog menaapamigwen,” odinaan iw gii-giniginibenid.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Now, once while they were moving about, in his sleep was (Nanabushu) weeping. The Wolf indifferently signed to him with the hand, “I fancy that probably he may be having a bad dream about me,” he said of him who then was taking his nap.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ningodingigo babaamigoziwaad (now once while they were moving camp about) · ezhi-mawingwaamini\* iniw oziishenyan (his uncle was weeping in his sleep) · a’aw ma’iingan ezhi-iniminika\*mad (that wolf gestured toward him with his hand) · “Intashke\* nindinitog menaapamigwen” ("I suppose he must be having a bad dream") · odinaan iw gii-giniginibenid (he said of the one who was napping).</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Kinship: Jones’s English p.89 names the dreamer’s relation reciprocally (uncle/nephew). The Ojibwe oziishenyan marks the one spoken-of. Jones’s English preserved verbatim; pronoun reference FLAGGED for M.*</span>

<span class="l-orig">(3) Mīdạc kä∙i∙cikuskusinit, cigwa kägä‘t uwindạmägōn: “Kägätsa, nintōjim, kigic‘tcimanäbạmin. Ạmbäsạnō, mänü pisindawicin kä∙i∙ninän; mänü icietcigän ‘i‘i‘ᵘ käininän. <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-goshkozinid, zhigwa gegeph owiindamaagoon: “Gegezha, nindoozhim, gigichi-jimanaabamin. Mii sa go bana getoodaman.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon when (Nanabushu) woke, then truly was (the Wolf) informed by him, saying: “Verily, my nephew, have I had an exceedingly bad dream about you. <span class="l-fiero">**Aanish mii sa gegeph ezhichiged mizhishewe-go zhigwa adimad. Aanish ningodingigo bimini-zhaawaad iniw awesiinhyan — aaniish mii go bana iw ezhichiged — gegezha zhiigaji. <span class="l-eng">*Accordingly that truly was what he did whenever he was about to overtake (the game). Now, once while in pursuit of some game, — for that was what he always was doing, — truly he grew tired (of throwing a stick into the dry bed of brooks). For when once away from a certain place he was in pursuit of a moose, — though it was now nearly spring, — this was the feeling of the Wolf when in pursuit of the moose, truly a big cow was he following after. And then presently, when he came in sight of her, — indeed, when he was on the very point of seizing her, — he saw the dry bed of a small brook.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Giiwenh gaa-inendang: “Ambesano, mii go iw ge-ni-aji-aazhawi-gweshkwaniyaan iwmaa zibinzhiwa’kamigenig.” Banaagigo naawagaam gaa-bangishing iwmaa gichi-zibing, banaagigo gaa-zeeswenik odawagan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*So this was the feeling that came to him: “Ay, this is where I will leap across (the dry bed of) the brook.” But instead into the middle (he fell) of a great river, and instead were his ears (set) ringing.*</span>
