(17) Nanabushu Pretends to be a Woman

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa eni-izhi-maajaad (and so then off he set out) babimosed (travelling along on foot).</span>

1)  > **Mii sa eni-izhi-maajaad babimosed.**

> And then away he started upon his journey, travelling afoot.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ogii-debitawaa’ ikwewa’ (and so he came within hearing of some women) manisenid (who were gathering firewood); aaniish ogiimitawaa’ (now he secretly overheard them):</span>

2)  > **Mii sa ogii-debitawaa’ ikwewa’ manisenid; aaniish ogiimitawaa’:**

> And so he came within the sound of some women who were gathering fire-wood; now he secretly overheard them saying:

<span class="l-gloss">“Amanjigish ge-izhchige’ongobanen (“I wonder however we might manage it) ji-wiidigemang a’aw inini?” (that we could marry that man?”) ikidowa’ (they said).</span>

3)  > **“Amanjigish ge-izhchige’ongobanen ji-wiidigemang a’aw inini?” ikidowa’.**

> “(I) wonder how we can bring it to pass so that we can marry that man\!” they said.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ambe sa noo (“now then), wawiyazh ninga-doodawaag (I will play a trick on them) awegweniwigwenag,” (whoever they may be,”) gii-inendam Nenaboozhoo (thought Nanabushu).</span>

4)  > **“Ambe sa noo, wawiyazh ninga-doodawaag awegweniwigwenag,” gii-inendam Nenaboozhoo**

> “Now, a trick I am going to play on them, whoever they are,” thought Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Ogikenimaan (he knew of her) ge-mawinid (that she would cry) wegwisisinid (the one who was his mother).</span>

5)  > **Ogikenimaan ge-mawinid wegwisisinid.**

> He knew that the mother (of the man) would cry.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-wawezhi’od (and so then he dressed himself up finely) gaa-izhi-ikwekaazod (after he had made himself out to be a woman).</span>

6)  > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-wawezhi’od gaa-izhi-ikwekaazod.**

> And so he got into gay attire after he had taken on the form of a woman.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash adikoo’obiinisagosiin (and there was a caribou spleen) mii dash iniw gaa-aawechiged i’iw ikweng (and that was the thing he made into a woman’s part).</span>

7)  > **Mii dash adikoo’obiinisagosiin mii dash iniw gaa-aawechiged i’iw ikweng.**

> There was a caribou spleen which he turned into a woman’s thing.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhinaagwo’od (after he had given himself that look), gaa-izhi-naazikawaad i’iw ikwewa’ (after he had gone over to those women), o’ow idash ogii-inaa’ (this he then said to them) apii gaa-odisaad (when he came upon them):</span>

8)  > **Gaa-izhinaagwo’od, gaa-izhi-naazikawaad i’iw ikwewa’, o’ow idash ogii-inaa’ apii gaa-odisaad:**

> After he had taken on the form (of a woman), (and) after he had gone over to where the women were, this he then said to them when he came upon them:

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniindi ayaad a’aw inini (“where is that man) zhiingenimaad i’iw ikwewa’ (who hates women) gaa-inind?” (so it is said of him?”)</span>

9)  > **“Aaniindi ayaad a’aw inini zhiingenimaad i’iw ikwewa’ gaa-inind?”**

> “Where is the man who is said to be a hater of women?”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-igod (whereupon he was told): “Mii omaa naaw-oodena ayaad,” (“here in the middle of the town he is,”) ogii-igoo’ (he was told by them).</span>

10) > **Mii dash gaa-igod: “Mii omaa naaw-oodena ayaad,” ogii-igoo’.**

> Whereupon he was told: “Here in the centre of the town he is,” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gagwaanisagizi (“he is hopeless), endogwen ji-inendang.” (who knows what he would think.”)</span>

11) > **“Gagwaanisagizi, endogwen ji-inendang.”**

> “He is hopelessly impossible, it is uncertain what his feeling would be (concerning you).”

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga shkomaa (“then come now), awii-inik,” (go and tell him,”) odinaa’ (he said to them); “Nin-bi-izhi-nisha’ogoo (“I have been sent here) ni-niigi’igoog,’” (by those who gave me birth,”) odinaa’ iw ikwewa’ (he said to the women).</span>

12) > **“Daga shkomaa, awii-inik,” odinaa’; “Nin-bi-izhi-nisha’ogoo ni-niigi’igoog,’” odinaa’ iw ikwewa’.**

> “Then pray, do you go and give him a message,” he said to them; “I have been sent hither by my parents,” he said to the women.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa geget gaa-izhi-giiwed bezhig (and so truly, when one of them went back home), gaa-izhi-wiindamawind wa’aw mindimooyenh (then this old woman was given the message) wegosisid (the one who was his mother).</span>

13) > **Mii sa geget gaa-izhi-giiwed bezhig, gaa-izhi-wiindamawind wa’aw mindimooyenh wegosisid.**

> And so truly, when back one (of them) went, then was the old woman who was mother (to the man) given the message.

<span class="l-gloss">E-kidod a’aw ikwe (then said the woman) mayaajii’aajimod (who had carried the message): “Biiwide omaa ayaa.” (“a stranger is here.”)</span>

14) > **E-kidod a’aw ikwe mayaajii’aajimod: “Biiwide omaa ayaa.”**

> Then said the woman who had conveyed the message: “A stranger is here.”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow dash ikido (and this she said): “Nimbi-izhinizha’ogoo (“I have been sent here) ni-niigi’igoog,” (by those who gave me birth,”) ikido (she said).</span>

15) > **O’ow dash ikido: “Nimbi-izhinizha’ogoo ni-niigi’igoog,” ikido.**

> And this she said: “I have been sent hither by my parents,” she said.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii dash gaa-pi-izhi-maajinizha’od (“and so when I was sent off this way), ‘awi-dibaajimon,’ (‘go and tell the news,’) nindig (I was told).</span>

16) > **“Mii dash gaa-pi-izhi-maajinizha’od, ‘awi-dibaajimon,’ nindig.**

> “And so when I was set upon my way hitherward, ‘Go give the news,’ I was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Ni-zhaagwenim (I was unwilling). ‘Daa-bi-izhaawag nindaangwayag.’ (‘let my sisters-in-law come here.’)”</span>

17) > **Ni-zhaagwenim. ‘Daa-bi-izhaawag nindaangwayag.’([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))”**

> I was loath (to go). ‘Let my friends come hither.’ (said the woman([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))).”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash e-kidod aw mindimooye (thereupon the old woman said): “Aaniin dash i’iw andawaabamaasiweg,” (“why is it you do not go seek her,”) odinaa’ i’iw odaanisa’ (she said to her daughters).</span>

18) > **Mii dash e-kidod aw mindimooye([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)): “Aaniin dash i’iw andawaabamaasiweg,([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))” odinaa’ i’iw odaanisa’.**

> Thereupon said the old woman([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)): “Why do you not go look for her([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))?” she said to her daughters.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget ba-izhi-nandawaabamaawaad igiw ikwewag (and so truly those women came seeking her), mii sa gaa-giiwewiijiiwaawaad igiw ikwewag (whereupon those women went back home taking her along).</span>

19) > **Mii dash geget ba-izhi-nandawaabamaawaad igiw ikwewag, mii sa gaa-giiwewiijiiwaawaad igiw ikwewag. ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))**

> And so truly came the women seeking for her, whereupon back home the women went, taking her([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)) with them.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi’onoode’ind (and then a place was made for her) iwidi wendabinid iniw niniwan (there where that man was seated).</span>

20) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi’onoode’ind iwidi wendabinid iniw niniwan. ([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))**

> And then a place was made for her there where the man([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)) was seated.

Mii sa zhigwa gii-onaabemid (and so now she had a husband).

21) > **Mii sa zhigwa gii-onaabemid. ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)) ([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))**

> Therefore she([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)) now had a husband([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)).

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa owiishaamaa’ odaangweya’ (by and by she invited her sisters-in-law along) ji-manisewaad (so that they would gather firewood).</span>

22) > **Zhigwa owiishaamaa’ odaangweya’ ji-manisewaad([<span class="underline">5</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)).**

> By and by she wished her sisters-in-law to go with her to gather firewood([<span class="underline">5</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html)).

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish ajina go gii-mamadwe’igewan (now, in just a little while the sound of her chopping was heard), aazha niibiwa misan (already there was much firewood).</span>

23) > **Aaniish ajina go gii-mamadwe’igewan, aazha niibiwa misan.**

> So in a little while after the sound of her chopping was heard, already (was there) much firewood.

<span class="l-gloss">“Awenen dash aw memindage (“who then is she that is so exceedingly) ge-zhi-nshawisid?” (such a good worker?”) odinaawaan (they said to her), owiindamawaawaan ogiwaan (they told their mother).</span>

24) > **“Awenen dash aw memindage ge-zhi-nshawisid?” odinaawaan, owiindamawaawaan ogiwaan.**

> “Who is she that is such a remarkable worker?” they said to their mother, they said to her, telling her about it.

“Geget sa gii-zhi-nshawisii a’aw nindaangwewaan.” (“truly such a good worker is our sister-in-law.”)

25) > **“Geget sa gii-zhi-nshawisii a’aw nindaangwewaan.” ([<span class="underline">6</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))**

> “Truly a good worker is our sister in law.” ([<span class="underline">6</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish geget sa minwendam a’aw mindimooye (now, truly pleased was the old woman), gaye a’aw akiwenzii (and so too the old man) gii-zhinshawisinid ona’aanganikwemiwaan (that their daughter-in-law was such a good worker).</span>

26) > **Aaniish geget sa minwendam a’aw mindimooye, gaye a’aw akiwenzii gii-zhinshawisinid ona’aanganikwemiwaan.([<span class="underline">7</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))**

> Now, thoroughly pleased was the old woman, as was also the old man, that such a good worker was their daughter-in-law.([<span class="underline">7</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-pretends-to-be-a-woman-footnotes.html))

Mii dash gaa-izhi-ganoonaad waabizheshiwan (and then she addressed the Marten):

27) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-ganoonaad waabizheshiwan:**

> And then she (Nanabushu) addressed the Marten, saying:

<span class="l-gloss">“Ambe sa noo wiidookawishin (“come now, help me) o’ow ezhichigeyaan,” (in this that I am doing,”) ogii-inaan (she said to it).</span>

28) > **“Ambe sa noo wiidookawishin o’ow ezhichigeyaan,” ogii-inaan.**

> “I wish you would help me in this that I am undertaking,” she said to it.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash iniw gaa-oniijaanisid (and so that was the one she had for a child); o’ow idash ogii-inaan (and this she said to it):</span>

29) > **Mii dash iniw gaa-oniijaanisid; o’ow idash ogii-inaan:**

> And so that was the creature she had for child; and this she said to it:

<span class="l-gloss">“Ambe sa noo (“come now), moozhag mawin,” (cry all the while,”) ogii-inaan (she said to it).</span>

30) > **“Ambe sa noo, moozhag mawin,” ogii-inaan.**

> “Come, now, all the while do you cry,” she said to it.

Mii dash geget gaa-izhichigenid (and that truly was what it did),

31) > **Mii dash geget gaa-izhichigenid,**

> And that truly was what (the Marten) did.

<span class="l-gloss">dakobinaad ezhichiged (binding it up, her way of doing it) mii eta i’imaa shkiinzhigoning zagapinaad (was to lash it only as far as over the eyes); dakobinaad bimoomaawizod (binding it up, she carried it about on her back as a nurse).</span>

32) > **dakobinaad ezhichiged mii eta i’imaa shkiinzhigoning zagapinaad; dakobinaad bimoomaawizod.**

> When she had it strapped to the cradle-board, her arrangement was such that she has it bound up as far as over the eyes; with it bound to the cradle-board, she played the nurse carrying it about on her back.

Mii sa go pane mawinid (and so all the while it wept).

33) > **Mii sa go pane mawinid.**

> And so all the while did (the Marten) weep.

<span class="l-gloss">“Wo’ow idash ikidon,” (“and this do you say,”) ogii-inaan (she said to it).</span>

34) > **“Wo’ow idash ikidon,” ogii-inaan.**

> “Now, this do you say,” she said to it.

<span class="l-gloss">“Dagwaagishoob niwii-amwaa,’ (“‘some tenderloin I want to eat,’) ikidon i’iw ji-mamawiyan,” (say it so that you will cry,”) ogii-inaan (she said to it).</span>

35) > **“Dagwaagishoob niwii-amwaa,’ ikidon i’iw ji-mamawiyan,” ogii-inaan.**

> “Some tenderloin do I wish to eat,’ do you say, so that you may cry,” she said to it.

Mii dash geget enwed a’aw abinoojii (and that truly was what the infant cried).

36) > **Mii dash geget enwed a’aw abinoojii.**

> And that truly was what the infant cried.

<span class="l-gloss">“Dagwaagishoob niwii-amwaa\!” (“some tenderloin I want to eat\!”) inwed (it cried).</span>

37) > **“Dagwaagishoob niwii-amwaa\!” inwed.**

> “Some tenderloin do I want to eat\!” it cried.

Zhayiigwa nisidotawaa (presently he was understood).

38) > **Zhayiigwa nisidotawaa.**

> Presently they understood what it wanted.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish zhigwa zaagidoowan ozhinisan (now then out went her father-in-law to cry it aloud), aaniish ogimaawiwan (for he was a chief); booch gii-zhichigenid i’iw anishinaabe (the people had of necessity to do it) ge-ikidod a’aw akiwenzii (whatever the old man should say).</span>

39) > **Aaniish zhigwa zaagidoowan ozhinisan, aaniish ogimaawiwan; booch gii-zhichigenid i’iw anishinaabe ge-ikidod a’aw akiwenzii.**

> Now then out went her father-in-law to cry aloud, for he was chief; for of necessity were the people bound to do whatever the old man should say.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniish, anishinaabedog (“now then, O people), e-kidod wa’aw noozhishenh (thus says this grandchild of mine), ‘dagwaagishoob niwii-amwaa,’” (‘some tenderloin I want to eat,’”) ikido (he said).</span>

40) > **“Aaniish, anishinaabedog, e-kidod wa’aw noozhishenh, ‘dagwaagishoob niwii-amwaa,’” ikido.**

> “Now, O ye people\! Thus says my grandchild, ‘Some tenderloin do I want to eat,’” he said.

Mii dash geget ge-bi-izhi-miinind Nenaboozhoo dagwaagishoobiin (and so truly Nanabushu was given some tenderloin).

41) > **Mii dash geget ge-bi-izhi-miinind Nenaboozhoo dagwaagishoobiin.**

> And so truly was Nanabushu given some tenderloin.

Bizaan apii gaa-onizhishininig maajid (it grew quiet while it was given something good to eat).

42) > **Bizaan apii gaa-onizhishininig maajid.**

> It hushed while it was given something good to eat.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash waawiidigemaad iniw ininiwan (and so while she went on living as wife to that man), zhigwa ogikenimaan bigishkananinid iniw obiinisagosiin (she then became aware that the spleen was rotting).</span>

43) > **Mii dash waawiidigemaad iniw ininiwan, zhigwa ogikenimaan bigishkananinid iniw obiinisagosiin.**

> And so while she (Nanabushu) continues living (as a wife) with the man, she then became aware that the spleen was decaying.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gigizheb (and so one morning) aazha namadabiwan ozhinisan gaye ozigosisan (now her father-in-law and her mother-in-law were seated) mii sa zhigwa gikenimaad bigishkanijijaakaamaad (and so now she realized she was going rotten between the loins).</span>

44) > **Mii dash gigizheb aazha namadabiwan ozhinisan gaye ozigosisan mii sa zhigwa gikenimaad bigishkanijijaakaamaad.**

> And so one morning, while her father-in-law and mother-in-law were seated, she then began to realize that she was becoming rotten between the loins.

<span class="l-gloss">“Bisoo,” (“phew\!”) ikidowan ozhinisan (said her father-in-law).</span>

45) > **“Bisoo,” ikidowan ozhinisan.**

> “Phew\!” said her father-in-law.

<span class="l-gloss">“Wegonenda gaa-izhimaagwag?” (“what is it that smells so?”) ikidowan ozhinisan (said her father-in-law).</span>

46) > **“Wegonenda gaa-izhimaagwag?” ikidowan ozhinisan.**

> “What is that which smells so?” said her father-in-law.

<span class="l-gloss">Geget mamiidaawendam (truly she was troubled about it); e-zhi-bazigwiid (when she rose to her feet), aano-anishigaskabenid (in vain she tried to hold it from falling).</span>

47) > **Geget mamiidaawendam; e-zhi-bazigwiid, aano-anishigaskabenid.**

> Truly was she worried about it; when she rose to her feet, in vain she tried to keep it from falling.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-bangishimaad ozhinisan e-naasamabinid (when she let it drop in front of where her father-in-law sat), ezhi-maajiibatood (then off she started running).</span>

48) > **Ezhi-bangishimaad ozhinisan e-naasamabinid, ezhi-maajiibatood.**

> When she dropped it in front of where her father-in-law was seated, then away she started running.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gegeti go ikwe inendamoog\!” (“truly a real woman they took her for\!”) ikidowan Nenaboozhoowan (said Nanabushu).</span>

49) > **“Gegeti go ikwe inendamoog\!” ikidowan Nenaboozhoowan.**

> “Truly a real woman they thought\!” said Nanabushu.

Footnotes :

(1) The woman impersonated by Nenaboozhoo.

(2) A woman to whom the message had been given.

(3) The woman-hater.

(4) The formality of leading a daughter to that part of the lodge where a man seats himself, and having her seat herself by him, is the public announcement that she is his wife.

(5) One of the first things a bride does is to go for fire-wood or for water, a convention by which she enters her new station.

(6) A pleasing compliment which a wife enjoys is to hear it said of her that she is a good worker.

(7) Pleased because their maintenance is assured. A son is seldom encouraged to marry a woman simply because she is handsome or that she is of a pleasing disposition; but if she can work, if she can turn her hands to something useful, then she is the one for him to get. Behind all this is the desire of the old folks to be cared for in their old age.
