(26) Nanabushu and Windigo

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo (and once) babimaazhagaamed (as he walked along the shore) inaabid (looking off) iwidi agaami-gichi-gami (over there across the great sea) awiya owaabamaan (he saw someone) i’iw bimaazhagaamenid (passing along the shore), aapiji sa mindidowan (tremendously big was that one).</span>

1)  > **Ningoding igo babimaazhagaamed inaabid iwidi agaami-gichi-gami awiya owaabamaan i’iw bimaazhagaamenid, aapiji sa mindidowan.**

> And once, while walking along the shore (and) looking towards the other side of the sea, he saw one passing along the coast, tremendously huge was the being.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin ganage (not at all, nowhere near) eko-niingidawidiyenid (as high as his buttocks) akoozisiiwan i’iw mitigoon (did the trees reach in height).</span>

2)  > **Gaawiin ganage eko-niingidawidiyenid akoozisiiwan i’iw mitigoon.**

> Nowhere near to his buttocks were the trees in their height.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish (well now), mii wa iniw Wiindigo (it was that Wiindigo).</span>

3)  > **Aaniish, mii wa iniw Wiindigo.**

> Well, of course it was Wīndigo.

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinen (foolishly, what-on-earth-like) obiibaagimaan (he called out to him):</span>

4)  > **Wegoneniiwinen obiibaagimaan:**

> Like a fool he called aloud to him :

<span class="l-gloss">“Nishiime sa (O my little brother), wajaka’eaanowan (upon a dried beaver tail) gaa-maamiijidaman (is what you have eased yourself upon)\!</span>

5)  > **“Nishiime sa, wajaka’eaanowan gaa-maamiijidaman\!**

> “O my younger brother \! upon the dry tail of a beaver did you ease yourself.

<span class="l-gloss">Bemaazhagaameyan (you who pass along the shore), woii\!” (halloo\!”)</span>

6)  > **Bemaazhagaameyan, woii\!”**

> You passer along the shore, halloo \!"

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish (well), naa maa waasa inendam Nenaboozhoo (at a good distance off Nenaboozhoo thought himself to be).</span>

7)  > **Aaniish, naa maa waasa inendam Nenaboozhoo.**

> Well, at a safe distance away Nänabushu thought (he was).

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji dash onishkimigoon Wiindigoo (and very angry was Wiindigo made) Nenaboozhoowan (by Nenaboozhoo).</span>

8)  > **Aapiji dash onishkimigoon Wiindigo Nenaboozhoowan.**

> Now, very angry was Wīndigo made by Nänabushu.

“Geget maamakaaj (truly it is astonishing/absurd).

9)  > **“Geget maamakaaj.**

> "It is really absurd.

<span class="l-gloss">Gonige (perhaps), ‘Niin eta ni-manidoow,’ (‘I alone am a manidoo,’) inendamodog a’aw Nenaboozhoo (so must Nenaboozhoo be thinking).</span>

10) > **Gonige, ‘Niin eta ni-manidoow,’ inendamodog a’aw Nenaboozhoo.**

> ‘Perhaps I am the only manitou existing’, may be Nänabushu’s thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Ambe sa noo (well, come now), ninga-giiwitaagaamebatoo.” (I will run around the shore.”)</span>

11) > **Ambe sa noo, ninga-giiwitaagaamebatoo.”**

> Well, I will run round (to where he is), keeping to the shore."

<span class="l-gloss">Geget maajiibatoo Wiindigoo (truly off ran Wiindigo), gichi-wiiba odoo-ditaan (very quickly he reached) imaa bimaazhagaamenid iniw Nenaboozhoowan (the place where Nenaboozhoo was coming along the shore).</span>

12) > **Geget maajiibatoo Wiindigoo, gichi-wiiba odoo-ditaan imaa bimaazhagaamenid iniw Nenaboozhoowan.**

> Truly off went running Wīndigo, in a very little while he arrived at the place where Nänabushu was coming along the shore.

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinen (what should he do but) ezhi-jaangidiyeshing a’aw Wiindigoo (Wiindigo lay down with his bottom pointing up).</span>

13) > **Wegoneniiwinen ezhi-jaangidiyeshing a’aw Wiindigoo.**

> What did Wīndigo do but get down and lay with his bottom up.

<span class="l-gloss">Naagaj igo zhigwa (after a while, now) bagamaazhagaamewan Nenaboozhoowan (Nenaboozhoo came up along the shore).</span>

14) > **Naagaj igo zhigwa bagamaazhagaamewan Nenaboozhoowan.**

> It was but a short while when up came Nänabushu to where he was on the shore.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa owaabamigoon (then he was seen by him); aaniin ge-izhinang (what should he see but) awiiya gii-jaangidiyeshinoon (someone lying with bottom pointing up).</span>

15) > **Zhigwa owaabamigoon; aaniin ge-izhinang awiiya gii-jaangidiyeshinoon.**

> Then (by Nänabushu) was he observed ; what was he to see but somebody lying with bottom pointing up.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go iw aanawi-izhi-gaazootawaad (and so when in vain he tried to hide from him) omadwe-kanoonigoon Nenaboozhoo (Nenaboozhoo heard him being addressed):</span>

16) > **Mii go iw aanawi-izhi-gaazootawaad omadwe-kanoonigoon Nenaboozhoo:**

> Accordingly, when he made an attempt to hide from him, Nänabushu heard (Wīndigo) saying to him:

<span class="l-gloss">“Ondaas (come here), akawe (wait), ninga-nawajiin i’iw giiyaw (I will make a little meal of your body).</span>

17) > **“Ondaas, akawe, ninga-nawajiin i’iw giiyaw.**

> "Come hither, wait, I want to make a small meal out of you.

<span class="l-gloss">Wewiib (hurry), ondaas.” (come here.”)</span>

18) > **Wewiib, ondaas.”**

> Make haste, come\!"

<span class="l-gloss">Etawaa (alas), Nenaboozhoo ani-gaagiimoodademo (Nenaboozhoo went along weeping quietly) ani-naazikawaad (as he made his way over to him).</span>

19) > **Etawaa, Nenaboozhoo ani-gaagiimoodademo ani-naazikawaad.**

> Alas \! Nänabushu began weeping silently on his way over to where (Wīndigo) was.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa imaa odoo-disaan (now he came to where he was); geget sa mangijiidiiwan (truly big was his anus), mizhisha go i’iw ode’ini (and in plain view was his heart).</span>

20) > **Zhigwa imaa odoo-disaan; geget sa mangijiidiiwan, mizhisha go i’iw ode’ini.**

> Presently he came to where (Wīndigo) was ; truly big was his anus, and in plain view was his heart.

<span class="l-gloss">Oganoonigoon (he was addressed): “Nenaboozhoo, manisen (Nenaboozhoo, gather firewood).</span>

21) > **Oganoonigoon: “Nenaboozhoo, manisen.**

> By him was he addressed, saying: "Nanabushu, go gather some fire-wood.

Ninga-akawe-abwen i’iw giiyaw.” (first I will roast your body.”)

22) > **Ninga-akawe-abwen i’iw giiyaw.”**

> I will first roast your body."

<span class="l-gloss">Geget ezhi-gopiid Nenaboozhoo (truly then up from the shore went Nenaboozhoo) manised (to gather firewood).</span>

23) > **Geget ezhi-gopiid Nenaboozhoo manised.**

> Truly then up from the shore went Nänabushu to gather fire-wood.

<span class="l-gloss">Etawaa (poor fellow), moozhag mawi (all the while he wept).</span>

24) > **Etawaa, moozhag mawi.**

> Poor fellow\! all the while was he crying.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa niibiwa gaa-okwaakwisidood iniw misan (now after he had piled up much firewood) oganoonigoon (he was addressed):</span>

25) > **Zhigwa sa niibiwa gaa-okwaakwisidood iniw misan, oganoonigoon:**

> After he had piled up much fire-wood, he was addressed:

“Mii iw izhi-boodawen.” (now then, kindle a fire.”)

26) > **“Mii iw izhi-boodawen.”**

> "Now, do you kindle a fire."

Nenaboozhoo ezhi-boodawed (Nenaboozhoo then kindled the fire).

27) > **Nenaboozhoo ezhi-boodawed.**

> Nänabushu then kindled the fire.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-poodawed (after he had kindled the fire) oganoonigon (he was addressed):</span>

28) > **Gaa-poodawed oganoonigon:**

> After he had kindled the fire, he was addressed:

<span class="l-gloss">“Nenaboozhoo, mii i’iw izhi-andawaabandan (Nenaboozhoo, now go and look for) i’iwe ge-wadabwaanaakoyaan (something I can use as a roasting-spit) i’iw giiyawizh (for your wretched body).</span>

29) > **“Nenaboozhoo, mii i’iw izhi-andawaabandan i’iwe ge-wadabwaanaakoyaan i’iw giiyawizh.**

> "Nänabushu, now go you hence to seek for something which I can use for a spit to roast your old body on.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji wenaakwak (very straight), maagizhaa dagidaakwidin i’iw giwiisenshis.” (or else perhaps your old spleen might be forced out.”)</span>

30) > **Aapiji wenaakwak, maagizhaa dagidaakwidin i’iw giwiisenshis.”**

> (Let it be) very straight, else perhaps your old spleen might be forced out."

Geget ani-maajaa Nenaboozhoo (truly off went Nenaboozhoo).

31) > **Geget ani-maajaa Nenaboozhoo.**

> Truly away went Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish moozhag mawi (now all the while he wept), epiichi-segizid (so frightened was he).</span>

32) > **Aaniish moozhag mawi, epiichi-segizid.**

> Now, all the time was he weeping, he was so scared.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji go i’iwe wayaaninaakwadinig i’iwe mitig (a very crooked stick) omikaan (he found), gaye iw zesagadikwaniwaninig (and one with many branching twigs).</span>

33) > **Aapiji go i’iwe wayaaninaakwadinig i’iwe mitig omikaan, gaye iw zesagadikwaniwaninig.**

> So a stick with a very big curve he found, and it had many twigs.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-maajaad izhaad ezhi-miinaad (then he set off, going to give it to him); o’ow odinaan (this he said to him): “Dawat, dawat, dawat\!”(1) (“Dawat, dawat, dawat\!”(1))</span>

34) > **Ezhi-maajaad izhaad ezhi-miinaad; o’ow odinaan: “Dawat, dawat, dawat\!”([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-windigo-footnotes.html))**

> Then he started going over to give it to (Wīndigo); this he said to him: "Oh, oh, oh\!"(­­[<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-windigo-footnotes.html))

E’iindijin a’aw Nenaboozhoo (such was the way Nenaboozhoo behaved).

35) > **E’iindijin a’aw Nenaboozhoo.**

> Such was the way Nänabushu acted.

“Gaawiin ina wiin dagidaakwidin iwe giwiizenshizh? (would not your wretched old spleen be forced out by that?)

36) > **“Gaawiin ina wiin dagidaakwidin iwe giwiizenshizh?**

> "Would not your old spleen be forced out by that?

<span class="l-gloss">Bekaanag iwe mitig nandawaabandan (look for a different stick), wenaakwak.” (one that is straight.”)</span>

37) > **Bekaanag iwe mitig nandawaabandan, wenaakwak.”**

> For a different one do you seek, one that is straight."

Etawaa Nenaboozhoo (poor, hapless Nenaboozhoo)\!

38) > **Etawaa Nenaboozhoo.**

> Hapless Nänabushu\!

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget ani-mawid (thereupon truly off he went weeping), biinish igo waasa ani-dagoshin (until he had come a long way off).</span>

39) > **Mii dash geget ani-mawid, biinish igo waasa ani-dagoshin.**

> Thereupon truly off he went crying, (going) till he a long way off was come.

<span class="l-gloss">Baamaa go ani-inaabid (presently, looking about as he went), detiwe (why, there\!), zhingosan gii-bimibatoowan (a weasel went running past) ezhi-ganoonaad (whom he addressed):</span>

40) > **Baamaa go ani-inaabid, detiwe, zhingosan gii-bimibatoowan ezhi-ganoonaad:**

> And suddenly, while looking about as he went, why, here was a Weasel whom he addressed as it went running past:

<span class="l-gloss">“Nishiime sa (my little brother), ni-nib noongom.” (I am going to die now.”)</span>

41) > **“Nishiime sa, ni-nib noongom.”**

> "My little brother, I am now going to die."

Gibichisewan iniw zhingosan (the weasel stopped short in its dash).

42) > **Gibichisewan iniw zhingosan.**

> In its flight stopped the Weasel.

<span class="l-gloss">Odaataganaabamigoon (he was gazed up at by it) ezhi-ganoonigod (when it addressed him):</span>

43) > **Odaataganaabamigoon ezhi-ganoonigod:**

By it was he gazed up at when by it he was addressed:

“Nenaboozhoo, wegonesh waa-onji-niboyan?” (Nenaboozhoo, why is it you are going to die?”)

44) > **“Nenaboozhoo, wegonesh waa-onji-niboyan?”**

"Nänabushu, why are you going to die?"

<span class="l-gloss">“Atawaa (alas), mii go niin i’iw wii-akawe-nawajiid wa’aw Wiindigoo i’iw niiyaw (it is that this Wiindigo means first to make a little meal of my body).</span>

45) > **“Atawaa, mii go niin i’iw wii-akawe-nawajiid wa’aw Wiindigoo i’iw niiyaw.**

"Alas\! because a light meal does that Wīndigo intend to make of my body.

Gaawiin ina gidaa-nisaasii?” (could you not kill him?”)

46) > **Gaawiin ina gidaa-nisaasii?”**

Could you not kill him?"

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-ganoonigod (then it addressed him): “Namadabi na go a’aw Wiindigoo?” (is that Wiindigo sitting down?”)</span>

47) > **Ezhi-ganoonigod: “Namadabi na go a’aw Wiindigoo?”**

Then he was addressed by it saying : " And is the Wīndigo sitting down ?"

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin (no), jaangidiyeshin (he lies with bottom pointing up), mizhisha go iwe ojiid (in full view is his anus), gaye i’iw ode.” (likewise his heart.”)</span>

48) > **“Gaawiin, jaangidiyeshin, mizhisha go iwe ojiid, gaye i’iw ode.”**

"No, he lies with bottom pointing upward, and in full view is his anus, likewise his heart."

“Nenaboozhoo, miinooch isa ninga-awi-gagwe-nisaa (Nenaboozhoo, even so I will go and try to kill him).

49) > **“Nenaboozhoo, miinooch isa ninga-awi-gagwe-nisaa.**

"Nänabushu, nevertheless I will try to slay him.

Meshkwad idash wiin giga-pagosenimin gegoo (and in return I will ask something of you).

50) > **Meshkwad idash wiin giga-pagosenimin gegoo.**

And as a reward for myself I shall expect some kind of blessing from you.

<span class="l-gloss">Indawaa dash giishpin gegoo doodawisiwan (so then, if you do nothing for me) gaawiin nindaa-nisaasii.” (I would not kill him.”)</span>

51) > **Indawaa dash giishpin gegoo doodawisiwan gaawiin nindaa-nisaasii.”**

So, therefore, if you fail to do something for me, I would not kill him."

<span class="l-gloss">Giigidoowan Nenaboozhoowan (Nenaboozhoo spoke up): “Meshkwad giishpin nisad (in return, if you kill him), ji-pishigendaman i’iw giiyaw giga-izhiyin.” (I will make you so that you take pride in your body.”)</span>

52) > **Giigidoowan Nenaboozhoowan: “Meshkwad giishpin nisad, ji-pishigendaman i’iw giiyaw giga-izhiyin.”**

Up spoke Nänabushu: "As your reward for killing him, I will make you proud of yourself."

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-kanoonind Nenaboozhoo (then Nenaboozhoo was told): “Mii i’iw izhi-piindoomon i’iwe niiyawizh.” (now then, tuck me into the bosom of your garment.”)</span>

53) > **Ezhi-kanoonind Nenaboozhoo: “Mii i’iw izhi-piindoomon i’iwe niiyawizh.”**

Then was Nänabushu told: "Therefore do you put me in the bosom of your garment."

<span class="l-gloss">Aatayaa (ah), geget minwendam Nenaboozhoo (truly glad was Nenaboozhoo).</span>

54) > **Aatayaa, geget minwendam Nenaboozhoo.**

Ah, truly pleased was Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">I’iw abwaanaak wenizhishininig i’iwe mitig andawaabandang (the roasting-spit of good wood that he had been looking for), gaye aapiji wenaakwadinig (and very straight); mii dash i’iw gaa-kiizhka’ang (and that is what he cut).</span>

55) > **I’iw abwaanaak wenizhishininig i’iwe mitig andawaabandang, gaye aapiji wenaakwadinig; mii dash i’iw gaa-kiizhka’ang.**

> The roasting-spit which he sought to find was of excellent wood and very straight ; and that was what he had cut.

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-izhi-maajiidood (as he carried it along to him), geyaabi gii-jaangidiyeshinoon iniw Wiindigoon (still Wiindigo lay with bottom pointing up).</span>

56) > **Eni-izhi-maajiidood, geyaabi gii-jaangidiyeshinoon iniw Wiindigoon.**

When he went thither taking it to him, still yet was Wīndigo lying with bottom pointing up.

<span class="l-gloss">Oganoonaan (he spoke to him): “O’owe odabwaanaakon.” (here is your roasting-spit.”)</span>

57) > **Oganoonaan: “O’owe odabwaanaakon.”**

He spoke to him, saying: "Here is your spit."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash imaa ezhi-gijibagwiid iniw zhingosan (then it was he flung the weasel out from his bosom), bane go eni-odaanawesenid (and its tail whirled away as it darted in).</span>

58) > **Mii dash imaa ezhi-gijibagwiid iniw zhingosan, bane go eni-odaanawesenid.**

> Then it was that he pulled the Weasel forth from the bosom of his garment, and away whirled its tail as it flew in.

<span class="l-gloss">“Etawaa (oh, well now), ambe sa noo giinitam giga-ozhitoon abwaanaak.” (come, this time you make the roasting-spit.”)</span>

59) > **“Etawaa, ambe sa noo giinitam giga-ozhitoon abwaanaak.”**

"Oh, well\! then do you take a turn at making a roasting-spit."

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin,” (no,) odigoon iniw Wiindigoon (he was told by Wiindigo): “giin igo ozhitoon.” (you make it.”)</span>

60) > **“Gaawiin,” odigoon iniw Wiindigoon: “giin igo ozhitoon.”**

> "No," he was told by Wīndigo: "do you make it."

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo odoo-daapinaan i’iwe mitig (Nenaboozhoo took up the stick); bijiinag igo wedaapinang (just as he picked it up) oganoonigoon (he was addressed):</span>

61) > **Nenaboozhoo odoo-daapinaan i’iwe mitig; bijiinag igo wedaapinang oganoonigoon:**

> Nänabushu took up the stick ; as soon as he picked it up, he was addressed by the other saying:

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawesa, Nenaboozhoo (no, it cannot be, Nenaboozhoo), nin-gichi-sesegide’e (my heart pounds with great fear).</span>

62) > **“Gaawesa, Nenaboozhoo, nin-gichi-sesegide’e.**

"Impossible, Nänabushu, my heart beats with great fear.

Nindigo awiya owii-pakandaan i’iwe ninde’eyaab.” (it is as if someone is about to bite through my heart-string.”)

63) > **Nindigo awiya owii-pakandaan i’iwe ninde’eyaab.”**

It seems as if something is about to bite off the cord of my heart."

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo oganoonaan (Nenaboozhoo addressed him): “Geshidine onakaako’ojin (hurry and impale me on the spit)\!</span>

64) > **Nenaboozhoo oganoonaan: “Geshidine onakaako’ojin.**

Nänabushu addressed him, saying: "Make haste and impale me upon (the spit)\!

Wewiib igo abwen iw niiyaw (quickly, roast my body)\!

65) > **Wewiib igo abwen iw niiyaw.**

Hurry and roast my body\!

Gemaa gaye wii-saasakokwaadaman iwe niiyaw.” (or, if you wish, you may fry my body.”)

66) > **Gemaa gaye wii-saasakokwaadaman iwe niiyaw.”**

Or, if you wish, you may fry my body."

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa giigidowan (again he spoke): “Iii, gaawesa, Nenaboozhoo (oh, it cannot be, Nenaboozhoo)\!</span>

67) > **Miinawaa giigidowan: “Iii, gaawesa, Nenaboozhoo\!**

Again he spoke: "Oh, impossible, Nänabushu\!

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawesa gosha (truly it cannot be). Mii gosha geget i’iw wii-wanendamaan.” (indeed I am about to lose my senses.”)</span>

68) > **Gaawesa gosha. Mii gosha geget i’iw wii-wanendamaan.”**

Impossible really \! Perhaps, indeed, I am now growing unconscious."

Nenaboozhoo omaa odani-gaakigaabiwitawaan (Nenaboozhoo went up close and stood right beside him).

69) > **Nenaboozhoo omaa odani-gaakigaabiwitawaan.**

Nänabushu then went up close and stood beside him.

<span class="l-gloss">“O’o’o (oh, oh), geshidine onakaako’ojin\!” (do hurry and impale me on the spit\!”)</span>

70) > **“O’o’o, geshidine onakaako’ojin\!”**

"Oh, do hurry and impale me upon (the spit)\!"

<span class="l-gloss">Inaabid Nenaboozhoo (as Nenaboozhoo looked on), ezhi-kawaakwidiyesenid (down he fell flat on his bottom); mii sa go i’iw geget gii-ani-nibonid (and thereupon truly he was dying).</span>

71) > **Inaabid Nenaboozhoo, ezhi-kawaakwidiyesenid; mii sa go i’iw geget gii-ani-nibonid.**

> While Nänabushu looked on, then down to the ground fell (Wīndigo) with his bottom ; thereupon truly was he dying.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-nibonid (when he was dead) bi-onji-saagijisewan zhingosan (out from there the weasel came running).</span>

72) > **Gaa-nibonid bi-onji-saagijisewan zhingosan.**

When he was dead, then out from thence came the Weasel running.

<span class="l-gloss">“Etawaa, ni-shiime sa (well now, my little brother)\! Amanji go aapiji ge-izhi-maamooyaawagendamogobanen? (I wonder what would make him truly grateful?)</span>

73) > **“Etawaa, ni-shiime sa\! Amanji go aapiji ge-izhi-maamooyaawagendamogobanen?**

"How now, my little brother\! Wonder what (I can do) so that he may be very thankful \!

Indawaa sa ninga-wawezhi’aa.” (so then I will adorn him.”)

74) > **Indawaa sa ninga-wawezhi’aa.”**

Therefore then will I paint him."

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo odoo-daapinaan (Nenaboozhoo took him up) ezhi-giziibiigawaad (and then washed him).</span>

75) > **Nenaboozhoo odoo-daapinaan ezhi-giziibiigawaad.**

Nänabushu took him up (and) then washed him in water.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji sa gaa-piinaabaawanaad (after he had washed him thoroughly clean), wegoneniiwinen waabaabiganan onandawaabamaan (what should he do but go seeking white clay).</span>

76) > **Aapiji sa gaa-piinaabaawanaad, wegoneniiwinen waabaabiganan onandawaabamaan.**

After he had made him thoroughly clean, what should he do but seek for white clay.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-mikawaad (after he had found it) nawaj ojaagizwaan iniw waabaabiganan (he burned the white clay whiter still).</span>

77) > **Gaa-mikawaad nawaj ojaagizwaan iniw waabaabiganan.**

After he had found it, whiter still he burned the white clay.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji gaa-izhi-waabishkizinid gaa-kiizhi’aad (after it had been made exceedingly white and he had finished it), mii dash i’iw miziwe ezhi-wawezhi’aad zhingosan (then all over he adorned the weasel); wanakwaano omakadewaanawenaan (at the tip of the tail he painted its tail black).</span>

78) > **Aapiji gaa-izhi-waabishkizinid gaa-kiizhi’aad, mii dash i’iw miziwe ezhi-wawezhi’aad zhingosan; wanakwaano omakadewaanawenaan.**

> After it was made exceedingly white, then he had it finished, whereupon all over he painted the Weasel ; at the end of the tail he painted it black.

“Ningadaga \[nindigo?\] gagwejibatoon.” (“now then \[or: I tell you?\], just try running.”)

79) > **“Ningadaga \[nindigo?\] gagwejibatoon.”**

“Now, just you try and see how you run."

Geget zhingos gagwejibatoo (sure enough the weasel set off running).

80) > **Geget zhingos gagwejibatoo.**

To be sure, the Weasel started running.

<span class="l-gloss">Etiwe (why now), geget bishigenimo (truly he was proud of himself). Nenaboozhoo oganoonaan (Nenaboozhoo spoke to him):</span>

81) > **Etiwe, geget bishigenimo. Nenaboozhoo oganoonaan:**

Oh, how really proud he was\! Nänabushu spoke to him, saying:

“Mii sa go naa iw e-zhi-maamooyawaminaan (this then is the way I give thanks to you).

82) > **“Mii sa go naa iw e-zhi-maamooyawaminaan.**

"Therefore in this manner do I render thanks to you.

Ni-nibonaaban zhingos (I was about to die, weasel).

83) > **Ni-nibonaaban zhingos.**

I was dying at the time, Weasel.

Mii dash iw ge-izhinaagoziyan beboongin eta (and that is how you shall look, in winter only).

84) > **Mii dash iw ge-izhinaagoziyan beboongin eta.**

And that is the way you shall look only in the winter-time.

Mii dash i’iw minik ge-akiiwang ge-izhinaagoziyan (and for as long as the world endures, this is how you shall look).

85) > **Mii dash i’iw minik ge-akiiwang ge-izhinaagoziyan.**

And as long as the world lasts, this is the way you shall look.

Ambe dash giga-bakewinin.” (and now I take leave of you.”)

86) > **Ambe dash giga-bakewinin.”**

Therefore I now take leave of you."

Nenaboozhoo ezhi-maajiiyaazhagaamed i’iw gichigami (Nenaboozhoo then set off along the shore of the great sea).

87) > **Nenaboozhoo ezhi-maajiiyaazhagaamed i’iw gichigami.**

Nänabushu then departed, keeping along the shore of the sea.
