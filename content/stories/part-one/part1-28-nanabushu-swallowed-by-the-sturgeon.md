28\. NÄNABUSHU SWALLOWED BY THE STURGEON.

<span class="l-fiero">**28. NAANABUSHU GAA-GONID MICHI-NAMEGWAN.**</span>

28\. NÄNABUSHU SWALLOWED BY THE STURGEON.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Midaasoganzh (probable) — Part I Series 2 (Nos. 19–32), Bois Fort, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Mīsa’ äjitibi‘kạtinik; weyābạninig ayạbi ändānsiwiwāt. Abā‘pic nāwa‘kwänig ugạnōnän ō‘kumisạn: “Nō‘ko, kanabạtc ningacacigạtab. Kāwīninā wäwäbạnābān kitayāsīn?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa ezhi-dibikadinig; weyaabaninig ayaabi endaanzhiwiwaad. Abaapizh naawakwenig oganoonaan ookomisan: “Nooko, ganabazh ningazhazhigatab. Gaawiin ina wewebanaabaan gidayaasiin?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And then night came on; on the morrow he remained idle at their little home. And when it came noon, he spoke to his grandmother, saying: “My grandmother, perhaps I shall grow weary with being idle. Have you not a hook and line?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ezhi-dibikadinig (and so then night came on), weyaabaninig (when it was morning), ayaabi endaanzhiwiwaad (he stayed idle at their little dwelling), Abaapizh naawakwenig (and when it came noon), oganoonaan ookomisan (he spoke to his grandmother), “Nooko (my grandmother), ganabazh ningazhazhigatab \*flagged\* (perhaps I will grow weary/idle), Gaawiin ina wewebanaabaan gidayaasiin? (have you not a hook-and-line?)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(2) “Nōjis, kägä‘t nintaiyān.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Noozhis, geget nindayaan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“My grandchild, to be sure, I have one.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Noozhis (my grandchild), geget (truly), nindayaan (I have one).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(3) Ā‘tayä, kägä‘t minwäntạm Nänabucu. “Nō‘kō, ạmbāsa niwīwäwäbạnābī. Intawä iᵘ kitcīmān ningayābạtci‘tōn.” Nänabucu äjipōsit micawagām äjii‘cāt. Ā‘pidci micawạgām kā‘tạgwicing, änic kitcigạmi, “Mīsa’ omā kädạcigwāgwäskwäpitcigäyān,” i‘kido. Nänabucu ubōna‘kạtōn ‘iᵉi’ᵘ umīgiskạn; kämica‘kīsänik mädci‘ạ‘m:</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aataye, geget minwendam Naanabozhо. “Nookoo, ambe sa niwii-wewebanaabii. Indawaa iw gichi-jiimaan ningad-aabajitoon.” Naanabozho ezhi-boozid michaawagaam ezhi-izhaad. Aapizhi michaawagaam gaa-dagwishing, aaniish gichigami, “Mii sa omaa gedazhi-gwaagwaashkwebijigeyaan,” ikido. Naanabozho oboonakadoon i’iw omiigiskan; gaa-mizhakiizenig \*flagged\* madwe-’am:**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Oh, truly pleased was Nänabushu. “My grandmother, therefore do I wish to fish with hook and line. Accordingly your canoe will I use.” When Nänabushu got into (the canoe), then out to sea he put. When very far out on the water he was come, for it was the sea, “Here is where I will fish with my hook and line,” he said. Nänabushu cast his hook into the water; when it touched the floor (of the sea), he began singing:*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aataye, geget minwendam Naanabozho (oh, truly pleased was Naanabozho). “Nookoo (my grandmother), ambe sa niwii-wewebanaabii (come now, I wish to fish with hook-and-line). Indawaa iw gichi-jiimaan ningad-aabajitoon (so then your big canoe I will use).” Naanabozho ezhi-boozid (Naanabozho got aboard), michaawagaam ezhi-izhaad (out on the open water he went). Aapizhi michaawagaam gaa-dagwishing (when he arrived very far out on the water), aaniish gichigami (for it was the sea/great-water), “Mii sa omaa gedazhi-gwaagwaashkwebijigeyaan (here is where I will bob my line),” ikido (he said). Naanabozho oboonakadoon i’iw omiigiskan (Naanabozho set down his hook), gaa-mizhakiizenig \*flagged\* (when it touched the bottom), madwe-’am (he began to sing):</span>

<span class="l-orig">(4) “Micīnạmägwä, pinawa‘kuntci‘ạn i‘ku‘käyaⁿ. Micīnạmägwä, pinawa‘kuntci‘ạn i‘ku‘cäyaⁿ.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Michiinamegwe \*flagged\*, binawakojii’an \*flagged\* i’ku’kayanh \*flagged\*. Michiinamegwe \*flagged\*, binawakojii’an \*flagged\* i’ku’zhayanh \*flagged\*.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“O big sturgeon\! come swallow me, here is my decoy. O big sturgeon\! come swallow me, here is my decoy.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Michiinamegwe (O big sturgeon), binawakojii’an \*flagged\* (come swallow me), i’ku’kayanh \*flagged\* (here is my decoy). Michiinamegwe (O big sturgeon), binawakojii’an \*flagged\* (come swallow me), i’ku’zhayanh \*flagged\* (here is my decoy).” \[song-vocables carried verbatim; the two lines differ only in käyaⁿ/cäyaⁿ as printed\]</span>

<span class="l-orig">(5) Mīdạc i‘i‘mā na‘i‘tāg ayāt aᵉa’ᵘ micīnạmägwä. Mämwä‘tcidạc imā unowängigạnig mīsa imā siniguskāgut. Käga‘pi ingutci ānu‘i‘cā, mīsa’ untcita ‘iᵉi’ᵘ siniguskāgut ‘iᵉi’ᵘ migiskạn. Käga‘pīsa ogạnōnān ạdi‘kamägwạn: “Adi‘kạmäg, ạmbāsanō, nawatantama’ᵘ Nänabucu iwä wäwäbanābān.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash i’imaa na’itaag ayaad a’aw michiinamegwe. Mamwezhi dash imaa onowengiganig mii sa imaa zhinigoskaagod. Gega-bii ingoji aanawi-izhaa, mii sa onjida i’iw zhinigoskaagod i’iw miigiskan. Gega-bii sa oganoonaan adikamegwan: “Adikameg, ambe sa noo, nawadandama’ \*flagged\* Naanabozho iwe wewebanaabaan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And so at that very place the big sturgeon happened to be. And it was there that he felt a rubbing on the cheeks (by the hook). At last away he tried in vain to go, but exactly as before he felt the rubbing of the hook. Then finally he spoke to the Whitefish, saying: “O Whitefish\! please seize that bobbing (hook) of Nänabushu’s with your mouth.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’imaa na’itaag (and so right at that place), ayaad a’aw michiinamegwe (the big sturgeon happened to be). Mamwezhi dash imaa onowengiganig \*flagged\* (and there against his cheeks), mii sa imaa zhinigoskaagod (it was there it rubbed him). Gega-bii ingoji aanawi-izhaa (at last he tried in vain to go somewhere), mii sa onjida i’iw zhinigoskaagod i’iw miigiskan (but on purpose/exactly the hook kept rubbing him). Gega-bii sa oganoonaan adikamegwan (at last he spoke to the whitefish): “Adikameg (O whitefish), ambe sa noo (come now), nawadandama’ \*flagged\* Naanabozho iwe wewebanaabaan (seize with your mouth that bobbing hook of Naanabozho’s).”</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Ạdi‘kamägwan, “white fish;” literally, “caribou fish.”*</span>

<span class="l-orig">(6) Kägä‘t äcinawatatạnk ‘iᵉi’ᵘ uwäwäbạnābānini. Ki‘tciwīckāni ‘iᵉi’ᵘ owäwäbạnābān. Äjiwi‘kubidōt, “‘Ā‘tā, mīsa’ mīsa’, mīsa’ wā‘tiyān\!” Cigwa omō‘kibinān ạdi‘kạmägwạn. “Isä, kāwīn kīn kīnantawänimisinōn\! Kīwīnantān ‘iᵉi’ᵘ wäwäbanābān.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geget ezhi-nawadandang i’iw owewebanaabaanini. Gichi-wiishkaani i’iw owewebanaabaan. Ezhi-wiikobidood, “Aataa, mii sa mii sa, mii sa waa-’idiyaan\!” Zhigwa omookibinaan adikamegwan. “Isе, gaawiin giin giwii-nandawenimisinoon\! Giwii-nandaan i’iw wewebanaabaan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Truly then it seized that bobbing (hook) with its mouth. There was a hard pull on the bobbing (hook). When he pulled on it, “Ah, that’s it, that’s it, that’s what I want\!” Presently he drew the Whitefish to the surface of the water. “Bah\! I don’t want you. You befoul the bobbing (hook).”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Geget ezhi-nawadandang i’iw owewebanaabaanini (truly it seized the bobbing hook with its mouth). Gichi-wiishkaani i’iw owewebanaabaan \*flagged\* (there was a hard pull on the bobbing hook). Ezhi-wiikobidood (when he pulled on it), “Aataa, mii sa mii sa, mii sa waa-’idiyaan\! \*flagged\* (ah, that’s it, that’s what I want\!)” Zhigwa omookibinaan adikamegwan (presently he drew up the whitefish). “Ise (bah\!), gaawiin giin giwii-nandawenimisinoon (I don’t want you)\! Giwii-nandaan \*flagged\* i’iw wewebanaabaan (you befoul the bobbing hook).”</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Isä, “Bah\!” an exclamation of reproach; with most Ojibwa dialects it is uttered only by the feminine sex.*</span>

<span class="l-orig">(7) Kägät intawa upagidạndān ‘iᵉi’ᵘ wäwäbạnābān. Intawä äjikiwät ạdi‘kamäg. Cigwa ạnitạgwicin. Micīnạmägwän äjikakwätcimigut: “Anīndạc ä‘kitut?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geget indawaa obagidandaan i’iw wewebanaabaan. Indawaa ezhi-giiwed adikameg. Zhigwa ani-dagwishin. Michiinamegwan ezhi-gagwejimigod: “Aaniindash ekidod?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Truly, therefore, (the Whitefish) let go from his mouth the bobbing (hook). And so back home went the Whitefish. Soon he came home. By the big Sturgeon was he asked: “And what did he say?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Geget indawaa obagidandaan i’iw wewebanaabaan (truly then he let go the bobbing hook from his mouth). Indawaa ezhi-giiwed adikameg (so back home went the whitefish). Zhigwa ani-dagwishin (soon he arrived home). Michiinamegwan ezhi-gagwejimigod (by the big sturgeon he was asked): “Aaniindash ekidod? (and what did he say?)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(8) “Kā, ‘Kīwīnantān ‘iᵉi’ᵘ wäwäbạnābān,’ i‘kitō Nänabucu. ‘Micīnạmägwä yä‘ta ninantawänimā.’”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaa, ‘Giwii-nandaan i’iw wewebanaabaan,’ ikido Naanabozho. ‘Michiinamegwe yeta ninandawenimaa.’”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Oh, ’you befoul the bobbing (hook),’ said Nänabushu. ’It is the big Sturgeon I want.’”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaa (oh), ‘Giwii-nandaan \*flagged\* i’iw wewebanaabaan (you befoul the bobbing hook),’ ikido Naanabozho (said Naanabozho). ‘Michiinamegwe yeta ninandawenimaa (it is only the big sturgeon I want).’”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(9) Mīsa keyābi onowängikạnig sinigwisänig ‘iᵉi’ᵘ uwäwäbạnābānini. A‘pidcisa umiguckätäntän micīnạmägwä. “Taga, kīn, namägus, awinawatantan.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa geyaabi onowengiganig zhinigwisenig i’iw owewebanaabaanini. Aapizhi sa omigoshkaadendam michiinamegwe. “Daga, giin, namegos, awi-nawadandan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And so once more against his cheeks rubbed the bobbing (hook). Very much was the big Sturgeon annoyed: “I say, you, Trout, go seize it with your mouth.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa geyaabi onowengiganig \*flagged\* zhinigwisenig i’iw owewebanaabaanini (and so once more against his cheeks the bobbing hook rubbed). Aapizhi sa omigoshkaadendam michiinamegwe (very much was the big sturgeon annoyed). “Daga, giin, namegos (now then, you, Trout), awi-nawadandan (go seize it with your mouth).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(10) Tōkisäni ‘iᵉi’ᵘ wäwäbạnābān. Äjiwi‘kubitōt Nänabucu uwäwäbạnābān, mīsa’ nãsão a‘kitut: “Mīsa wā‘tiyān.” Omō‘kibạnān ini’ namägusan. “Isä, kāwīn kīn kīnantawänimisinōn\! Kīwīnantān ‘iᵉi’ᵘ niwäwäbạnābān.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Dookizeni i’iw wewebanaabaan. Ezhi-wiikobidood Naanabozho owewebanaabaan, mii sa naasaab ekidod: “Mii sa waa-’idiyaan.” Omookibanaan iniw namegosan. “Ise, gaawiin giin giwii-nandawenimisinoon\! Giwii-nandaan i’iw niwewebanaabaan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*There was a gentle pull on the bobbing (hook). As on his bobbing (hook) Nänabushu pulled, so the same thing as before he said: “This is what I want.” Out of the water he pulled the Trout. “Bah\! you are not the one I want. You befoul my bobbing (hook).”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Dookizeni \*flagged\* i’iw wewebanaabaan (there was a gentle pull on the bobbing hook). Ezhi-wiikobidood Naanabozho owewebanaabaan (as Naanabozho pulled his bobbing hook), mii sa naasaab ekidod (the same as before he said): “Mii sa waa-’idiyaan \*flagged\* (this is what I want).” Omookibanaan iniw namegosan (he drew up the trout). “Ise (bah\!), gaawiin giin giwii-nandawenimisinoon (you are not the one I want)\! Giwii-nandaan \*flagged\* i’iw niwewebanaabaan (you befoul my bobbing hook).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(11) Mīnawa ubagitạntān namägus. Äjimādcāt; tägwicing udōgimäman ugạnōnigōn. “Ānic ä‘kitut?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Miinawaa obagidandaan namegos. Ezhi-maajaad; dagwishing odoogimaaman oganoonigoon. “Aaniish ekidod?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*The Trout was the next to let go from his mouth the bobbing (hook). Then he departed; when he got home, by his chief was he asked: “What did he say?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa obagidandaan namegos (again the trout let go the hook from his mouth). Ezhi-maajaad (then he departed); dagwishing odoogimaaman oganoonigoon (arriving, by his chief he was spoken to). “Aaniish ekidod? (what did he say?)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(12) “Kāwīn kuca kīn kīnantawänimisinōn. Micīnạmägwä yäta ninantawänimă.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaawiin gosha giin giwii-nandawenimisinoon. Michiinamegwe yeta ninandawenimaa.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“You are not the one I really want. It is only the big Sturgeon I wish.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin gosha giin giwii-nandawenimisinoon (you are truly not the one I want). Michiinamegwe yeta ninandawenimaa (it is only the big sturgeon I want).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(13) Mīsa ‘iᵘ ingutci äji‘i‘cāt micīnạmägwä. Käwäsa ugacki‘tōsin pa‘kän tciwäwäbạnäbinit. “Ạcimädcisawina‘a’ᵘ Nänabucu\! Mini‘k ningitạci‘kāg winicki‘i‘t\!” Nänabucowan unawatantamawan ‘iᵉiwa uwäwäbạnābānini.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa iw ingoji ezhi-izhaad michiinamegwe. Gawesa ogashkitoosiin bakaan ji-wewebanaabinid. “Azhi-maajisawina a’aw Naanabozho\! Minik ningidazhi’kaag wii-nishki’id\!” Naanabozhowan onawadandamawaan i’iwe owewebanaabaanini.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon away went the big Sturgeon. He found it impossible to make (Nänabushu) fish elsewhere with his hook and line. “Confound that Nänabushu\! He has been pestering me so long as to anger me\!” He then seized hold of Nänabushu’s bobbing (hook) with his mouth.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa iw ingoji ezhi-izhaad michiinamegwe (thereupon the big sturgeon went off somewhere). Gawesa ogashkitoosiin \*flagged\* bakaan ji-wewebanaabinid (he could not make him fish elsewhere). “Azhi-maajisawina \*flagged\* a’aw Naanabozho\! (confound that Naanabozho\!) Minik ningidazhi’kaag \*flagged\* wii-nishki’id\! (so long has he pestered me, to anger me\!)” Naanabozhowan onawadandamawaan i’iwe owewebanaabaanini (he seized Naanabozho’s bobbing hook with his mouth).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(14) Nänabucu mīgu i‘u äjiki‘tciwibitōt ‘iᵉi’ᵘ wäwäbạnābān. Inābit micawagām, minisäns inäntạm wändcimo‘kibinik. Minangwana i‘u ucigwạnani ini’ micīnạmägwän. Cigwasa ningutingigu panägu kāsaswänik u‘tawagan. Minangwana i‘u kōgạmigut mīgu iᵘ kigitcimān. Mīsagu a‘pan äciwanäntank; wi‘käsa mi‘kawi. Mä‘kawit, “Wâginugāning nintaiyā,” inäntạm. Inābit, icpiming ki‘tcimaskimut ạgōtänik;</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Naanabozho mii go iw ezhi-gichi-wiibidood i’iw wewebanaabaan. Inaabid michaawagaam, minisens inendam waanji-mookibinig. Minangwana iw ozhigwanani iniw michiinamegwan. Zhigwa sa ningoding igo banegо gaa-zaaswenig odawagan. Minangwana iw googamigod mii go iw ogichi-jiimaan. Mii sa go apane ezhi-waanendang; wiikaa sa mikawi. Mekawid, “Waaginogaaning nindayaa,” inendam. Inaabid, ishpiming gichi-mashkimod agoodenig;**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Nänabushu thereupon held tight to the bobbing (hook). As he looked out over the broad sea, an island he thought was appearing on the surface of the water. But it happened to be the tail of the big Sturgeon. Then of a sudden there was a continuous ringing in his ears. It happened that he was being taken down into the water together with his canoe. And so straightway he lost his wits; after a long while he came to. When he was revived, “In a circular place am I,” he thought. While looking around, up overhead a huge bag was hanging; it hap-*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Naanabozho mii go iw ezhi-gichi-wiibidood i’iw wewebanaabaan (Naanabozho thereupon held tight to the bobbing hook). Inaabid michaawagaam (as he looked out over the broad water), minisens inendam waanji-mookibinig (an island he thought was rising to the surface). Minangwana iw ozhigwanani iniw michiinamegwan (but it was the tail of the big sturgeon). Zhigwa sa ningoding igo banego \*flagged\* gaa-zaaswenig odawagan (then suddenly there was a ringing in his ears). Minangwana iw googamigod \*flagged\* mii go iw ogichi-jiimaan (it happened he was taken down into the water with his big canoe). Mii sa go apane ezhi-waanendang (and so straightway he lost his wits); wiikaa sa mikawi (after a long while he came to). Mekawid (when he revived), “Waaginogaaning nindayaa (in a circular/round place am I),” inendam (he thought). Inaabid (looking around), ishpiming gichi-mashkimod agoodenig (up above a huge bag was hanging);</span>

<span class="l-orig">(15) mimīgu ju äcimamāsi‘kānik. Mīnawa iwiti inābit wä‘kwayai awiya owâbaman tacika‘kawatabinit. Äcipasigwit Nänabucu; äciwạnabi‘tawāt, äjikạnōnāt: “Atawa nicimisä, anīndac äcia‘yāyạn?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**mii mii go zhо ezhi-mamaazikaanig. Miinawaa iwidi inaabid weekwayaa’ii awiya owaabamaan dazhika gawadabinid. Ezhi-bazigwiid Naanabozho; ezhi-wanabi’tawaad, ezhi-ganoonaad: “Adawe nishimise, aaniindash ezhi-ayaayan?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*pened to be in motion. Again while looking yonder at the other end he saw some sort of a creature seated, swinging back and forth. Then up rose Nänabushu to his feet; taking his seat beside him, he then addressed him, saying: “My poor little brother, and what is the matter with you?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">mii mii go zho ezhi-mamaazikaanig \*flagged\* (it happened to be in motion). Miinawaa iwidi inaabid weekwayaa’ii (again as he looked yonder at the far end), awiya owaabamaan dazhika gawadabinid \*flagged\* (he saw some creature seated, swaying). Ezhi-bazigwiid Naanabozho (then Naanabozho rose to his feet); ezhi-wanabi’tawaad (taking his seat beside him), ezhi-ganoonaad (he addressed him): “Adawe nishiimise (my poor little brother), aaniindash ezhi-ayaayan? (what is the matter with you?)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(16) Agawagu pimādisiwan. Cigwa kigitōwan: “Nänabucu, mīsa kayä kīn ki‘kuni‘k aᵉa’ᵘ micīnạmägwä.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Agawaa go bimaadiziwan. Zhigwa gigidowan: “Naanabozho, mii sa gaye giin gigonik a’aw michiinamegwe.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And barely alive was the other. Presently he said: “Nänabushu, and so you too have been swallowed by the Big Sturgeon.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Agawaa go bimaadiziwan (barely alive was the other). Zhigwa gigidowan (presently he spoke): “Naanabozho, mii sa gaye giin gigonik a’aw michiinamegwe (and so you too have been swallowed by the big sturgeon).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(17) “Abā‘pinisiwâgan\!” inäntạm Nänabucu. “Ambäsanō, windạmawicin anti ka‘tạciguni‘k.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Abaapinisiwaagan\!” inendam Naanabozho. “Ambe sa noo, wiindamawishin aandi gaa-dazhi-gonik.”**</span>
