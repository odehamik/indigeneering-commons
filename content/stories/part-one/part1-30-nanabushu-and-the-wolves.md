30\. NÄNABUSHU AND THE WOLVES.

<span class="l-fiero">**30. Nenabozho gaye ma’iinganag.**</span>

30\. Nänabushu and the Wolves.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Midaasoganzh (probable) — Part I Series 2 (Nos. 19–32), Bois Fort, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Ningutingigu sāga‘i‘gạn omạdā‘kun. Äjimādcī‘ā‘dạgā‘kut, wạbigạmānig päcwâbandạnk, awiya owâbạmān pimādạgā-‘kupạ‘tōnit; aca minawa, niwiwaᵉ. Kuniginin, ma‘i‘nganaR\! Äjipipägimāt: “Nistcimistca, a‘kawä, kawâbạmininim\!”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningodingigo zaaga’igan omadaakon. Azhi-maadaji’aadagaakod, waabigamaanig baeshwaabandang, awiya owaabamaan bimaadagaa-gobadoonid; azhe minawaa, niiwiwag. Goniginin, ma’iinganag\! Azhi-biibaagimaad: “Nizhishenh-isa, akawe, gawaabamininim\!”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Now, by and by he came out upon a frozen lake. As he started forth on the ice, as nigh to the narrows of the lake he drew, he saw some one running past over the ice; then some more, four of them. Behold, (they were) Wolves\! Then he called aloud to them: “O my little brothers\! wait, I wish to see you.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ningodingigo zaaga’igan omadaakon (once, a lake he comes out upon) ∙ Azhi-maadaji’aadagaakod (as he set out walking on the ice) ∙ waabigamaanig baeshwaabandang (the narrows of the lake, coming near in sight of it) ∙ awiya owaabamaan bimaadagaa-gobadoonid (someone he sees running past over the ice) ∙ azhe minawaa, niiwiwag (and then again, there were four of them) ∙ Goniginin, ma’iinganag\! (behold, wolves\!) ∙ Azhi-biibaagimaad (then he called out to them) ∙ “Nizhishenh-isa, akawe, gawaabamininim\!” (“my little brothers, wait, I will see you\!”)</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Jones English p.235 fn1: “For other versions see Nos. 8 (p. 73), 9 (p. 85), 44 (p. 373).” Jones Ojibwe p.234 fn1: “See Series 1, No. 7, p. 49.”*</span>

<span class="l-orig">(2) Kägät kipitciba‘towaᵉ; äciwawänạbinit ạninäsi‘kawat. Äjiganōnät: “Nitcisasi‘kizi, anti äcäyäg?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geged gii-bidijiba’towag; eshi-wawenabinid aninaazikawaad. Azhi-ganoonaad: “Niijisazigizh, aandi ezhaayeg?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*To be sure, they came to a sudden halt; then they sat down, while he went up to where they were. Then he spoke to them, saying: “My old friend, whither are you going?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Geged gii-bidijiba’towag (truly they came running to a stop) ∙ eshi-wawenabinid (as they sat down) ∙ aninaazikawaad (he going up to them) ∙ Azhi-ganoonaad (then speaking to them) ∙ “Niijisazigizh, aandi ezhaayeg?” (“my old friend, where are you going?”)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(3) “Kā, o‘ō‘witi, kicika‘tikwaning, mī‘i‘witi äcāyāng. Nibinunk ki‘ạ‘sạntcigōbạnig ogō‘ᵘ kitōcimạg, ki‘tci‘ạ‘yābän uginisāwâbạnīn. Midạc iwiti äcāyāng.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaa, o’oowidi, gizhigaatigwaning, mii-iwidi ezhaayaang. Niibinong gii-asanjigoobanig ogow giddoozhimag, gichi-ayaaben oginisaawaabaniin. Mii dash iwidi ezhaayaang.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Oh, over here, for the place of cedar boughs, is where we are bound. Last summer did these nephews of yours make a cache there, a great bull (moose) they killed then. Now, that is the place (for which) we are bound.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaa, o’oowidi (oh, over here) ∙ gizhigaatigwaning (at the place of cedar boughs) ∙ mii-iwidi ezhaayaang (that is where we are going) ∙ Niibinong gii-asanjigoobanig (last summer they made a cache) ∙ ogow giddoozhimag (these your nephews) ∙ gichi-ayaaben oginisaawaabaniin (a great bull-moose they killed) ∙ Mii dash iwidi ezhaayaang (and that is where we are going)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(4) “Ä‘tiwä, mi gayä nin iwiti äcäyan, kicika‘tikwaning, — misa’ ‘i‘i‘ᵘ tci‘ạ‘niwâwitciwinạguk.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aatiwe, mii gaye niin iwidi ezhaayaan, gizhigaatigwaning, — mii-sa ’i’iw ji-aniwaa-wiijiiwinagog.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Why, that is the place, too, for which I am bound, to the place of cedar boughs, so therefore it is my wish to go along with you.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Aatiwe (why) ∙ mii gaye niin iwidi ezhaayaan (that too is where I am going) ∙ gizhigaatigwaning (to the place of cedar boughs) ∙ mii-sa ’i’iw ji-aniwaa-wiijiiwinagog (so it is that I will go along with you)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(5) Anic, mii‘ᵘ cigwa wunāgucininig.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aniish, mii-iw zhigwa wanaagoshininig.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Well, it was then evening.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aniish (well) ∙ mii-iw zhigwa wanaagoshininig (then it was now evening)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(6) “Anic ‘i‘i‘ᵘ, Tcitcigwanowis,¹ ạninantạ‘u‘ninạmạsiwan, magicā takisinā tibi‘kạt. Tạga, kimicōmä‘i‘wä tạni‘ạ‘ntō‘u‘ninạmā.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aniish ’i’iw, Jiijiigwanowizh, ani-nandawi’oninamasiwan, maagizhaa dakisinaa dibikad. Daga, gimishoomi’iwe dani-andawi’oninamaa.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Now, Thin-Tail, do you go find a place where to camp, for perhaps it will be cold in the night. I say, let your uncle find a place where to camp.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Aniish ’i’iw, Jiijiigwanowizh (now then, Thin-Tail) ∙ ani-nandawi’oninamasiwan (go and look for a place to camp) ∙ maagizhaa dakisinaa dibikad (perhaps it will be cold in the night) ∙ Daga (come now) ∙ gimishoomi’iwe dani-andawi’oninamaa (let your uncle there look for a place to camp)”</span>

*Jones Ojibwe p.236 footnote¹ (to Tcitcigwanowis): “The name by which one of the young Wolves was called.”*

<span class="l-orig">(7) Anic, misa’ gägä‘t Nänabucu ạninantạ‘u‘ninạmāt. Kāwäsā ni‘tāwusäsi, ānuwītciwāt ‘i‘i‘ᵘ ma‘i‘ngana. Äjikanōnint Nänabucu: “Ambäsino, äntōtamängigu pimiba‘tōyäng, mi kayä kin kätōtaman.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aniish, mii-sa geged Nenabozho ani-nandawi’oninamaad. Gaawesaa nitaawizesii, aanawii-wiijiiwaad ’i’iw ma’iingana. Azhi-ganoonind Nenabozho: “Ambesino, endoodamaangigo bimiba’toyaang, mii gaye giin ge-doodaman.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*So thereupon, truly did Nänabushu go to find a place to camp. Not at all was he familiar with (their way of) travelling, as he tried going along with the Wolves. Then was Nänabushu told: “Come, as we do when we run along, so in like manner should you do too.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aniish, mii-sa geged Nenabozho ani-nandawi’oninamaad (so truly Nänabushu went to look for a camping place) ∙ Gaawesaa nitaawizesii (not at all was he skilled at it) ∙ aanawii-wiijiiwaad ’i’iw ma’iingana (though he tried to go along with the wolf) ∙ Azhi-ganoonind Nenabozho (Nänabushu was told) ∙ “Ambesino, endoodamaangigo bimiba’toyaang (come, just as we do when we run along) ∙ mii gaye giin ge-doodaman (that too is what you should do)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(8) Tāyā, kaya win tōtam. Anīc mīdạc iu kāwīn kägō ‘i‘i‘ᵘ osō, mīdạc ‘i‘i‘ᵘ winag ‘i‘i‘ᵘ wäsowat. Kawin kanaga nōmag cigwa animaskawa‘kwatininig.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Daayaa, gaye wiin doodam. Aniish mii dash iw gaawiin gego ’i’iw ozow, mii dash ’i’iw winag ’i’iw wezowad. Gaawiin ganage noomag zhigwa ani-mashkawaakwadininig.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Ah, and he did the same. Naturally, there was nothing in the way of a tail, therefore his penis was what he used for a tail. It was but a very little while before it was frozen stiff.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Daayaa, gaye wiin doodam (ah, he too did so) ∙ Aniish mii dash iw gaawiin gego ’i’iw ozow (and there was nothing for a tail) ∙ mii dash ’i’iw winag ’i’iw wezowad (so his penis was what he used for a tail) ∙ Gaawiin ganage noomag zhigwa ani-mashkawaakwadininig (it was not even a little while before it froze stiff)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(9) A‘pidcigu wasi‘tāwināgusi ānupimiba‘tōd. Cigwasa’ umi‘kän a‘pidci tabinawānig. “Mīsa omā tcinibaiyạng.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aapijigo wazhitaawinaagozi aanu-bimiba’tood. Zhigwa-sa omikaan aapiji dabinawaanig. “Mii-sa omaa ji-nibaayaang.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And very awkward was his aspect as he tried in vain to run along. In time he found where there was excellent shelter from the wind. “Now, here is where we will sleep,” said Nänabushu.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aapijigo wazhitaawinaagozi (very clumsy he appeared) ∙ aanu-bimiba’tood (as he tried in vain to run along) ∙ Zhigwa-sa omikaan aapiji dabinawaanig (in time he found a very sheltered, windless place) ∙ “Mii-sa omaa ji-nibaayaang” (“here is where we will sleep”)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(10) “Ei, käwäsa’, kitägi‘katcimin.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Eya, gaawesaa, gidaa-gii-gikajimin.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Why, impossible\! we might be cold.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Eya, gaawesaa (oh, impossible) ∙ gidaa-gii-gikajimin (we might be cold)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(11) “Taga, kin Tcitcigwanowis,” inimāwan ini’ a‘kiwä”zī ma‘i‘nganan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Daga, giin Jiijiigwanowizh,” inimaawan iniw akiwenzii ma’iinganan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“I say, you, Thin-Tail, (go look for a camping-place),” thus to one said the old Wolf.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga, giin Jiijiigwanowizh (come, you, Thin-Tail)” ∙ inimaawan iniw akiwenzii ma’iinganan (thus said the old Wolf to him)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(12) Gägä‘t mīdạc ‘i‘u nantukabäcit a‘pidci omi‘kān änāsa‘a‘maninig. “Mīsa’ oma tcinibayang.” Anic misạn utō‘kwakuwäbinanan. “Aa‘”, Nänabucu, pōdawän,” inā Nänabucu. Anic misa’ Nänabucu anawibōtawät. A‘kawä kici kisagōn ugīcibäwäbinän. Äjiganōnint: “Nänabucu, änïnnä äntōtaman wi‘pōtawayan? Nackäsa, kanawabamicin äntōtamink wâpōdawängin.” Kuniginin, ini’ a‘kiwä”zī maīnganan pāsigwiwan, u‘kwa‘kwisininig ini’ misan äcipacitcikwäskwaninit; misa’ ickutä äjipiskā‘kunänik.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geged mii dash iw nandawi-gabeshid aapiji omikaan enaaza’amaninig. “Mii-sa omaa ji-nibaayang.” Aniish misan oto-gwaakogwaabinanan. “Aa, Nenabozho, boodawen,” inaa Nenabozho. Aniish mii-sa Nenabozho aanawi-boodawed. Akawe gizhi-gizaagon ogiizhibewebinaan. Azhi-ganoonind: “Nenabozho, aaniin-na endoodaman wii-boodawayan? Nashkesa, ganawaabamishin endoodaming waa-boodawengin.” Goniginin, iniw akiwenzii ma’iinganan baazigwiiwan, ogwaakwizininig iniw misan eshi-bazhijigwaeshkwaninid; mii-sa ishkode azhi-bishkaakonenig.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*It was so that when he looked for a place to camp, he found a place that was exceedingly windy. “Here is where we will sleep.” So a great pile of fire-wood he heaped on. “All right, Nänabushu, do you kindle the fire,” was told Nänabushu. So accordingly Nänabushu tried in vain to kindle the fire. He first tried twirling a piece of cedar wood. Then he was addressed: “Nänabushu, how do you go about it when you want to make a fire? Just look, observe me (and see) the way it is done when fire is made.” Behold, the old Wolf then rose to his feet, (and) over where lay piled the heap of fire-wood he leaped; thereupon the fire blazed up.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Geged mii dash iw nandawi-gabeshid (truly then, looking for a camping place) ∙ aapiji omikaan enaaza’amaninig (he found a very windy place) ∙ “Mii-sa omaa ji-nibaayang” (“here is where we will sleep”) ∙ Aniish misan oto-gwaakogwaabinanan (so he heaped on the firewood) ∙ “Aa, Nenabozho, boodawen,” inaa Nenabozho (“all right, Nänabushu, make a fire,” he was told) ∙ Aniish mii-sa Nenabozho aanawi-boodawed (so Nänabushu tried in vain to make a fire) ∙ Akawe gizhi-gizaagon ogiizhibewebinaan (first he twirled a piece of cedar wood) ∙ Azhi-ganoonind (then he was addressed) ∙ “Nenabozho, aaniin-na endoodaman wii-boodawayan? (“Nänabushu, how do you go about making a fire?) ∙ Nashkesa, ganawaabamishin endoodaming waa-boodawengin” (just look, watch me how it is done when a fire is made”) ∙ Goniginin, iniw akiwenzii ma’iinganan baazigwiiwan (behold, the old Wolf rose to his feet) ∙ ogwaakwizininig iniw misan eshi-bazhijigwaeshkwaninid (over the piled firewood he leaped) ∙ mii-sa ishkode azhi-bishkaakonenig (and then the fire blazed up)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(13) Cigwasa’ tibi‘kadini, äjikawicimut Nänabucu. Ä‘tawa Nänabucu\! a‘pidci nōndāgusi, ä‘pi‘tcigī‘katcit.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa-sa dibikadini, azhi-gawishimod Nenabozho. Aataawaa Nenabozho\! aapiji noondaagozi, e-apiichi-giikajid.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*It was now getting night, when down to sleep lay Nänabushu. Poor Nanabushu\! he was heard making a very loud noise, he was so cold.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa-sa dibikadini (now it was getting night) ∙ azhi-gawishimod Nenabozho (when Nänabushu lay down to sleep) ∙ Aataawaa Nenabozho\! (poor Nänabushu\!) ∙ aapiji noondaagozi (he was heard making a great noise) ∙ e-apiichi-giikajid (he was so cold)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(14) “Ictä, kimicōmānān mimäwinii’ wikawatcit, intawa pi‘tawacä‘i‘k,”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Ishte, gimishoominaan mimaawinii’ wii-gawatshid, indawaa biitawashi’ig,”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“I declare, our uncle no doubt is about freezing to death, therefore put another cover over him.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Ishte (I declare) ∙ gimishoominaan mimaawinii’ wii-gawatshid (our uncle no doubt is about to freeze to death) ∙ indawaa biitawashi’ig (so put another cover over him)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(15) Päjik pai‘jinānawäninit.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Bezhig baezhi-naanawaeninid.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*One then laid his tail over him.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Bezhig (one) ∙ baezhi-naanawaeninid (laid his tail over him)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(16) Wibago amatcibiso. “Ci, kägätsa ou animwanowic nindạbwäckāgun\!” Anic wibagu minawa gi‘katci. Äjinōndāgusit, “Aninta, pi‘tawacä‘i‘k mīnawā kimicōmä‘i‘wā.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Wiiba-go amajibizo. “Zhi, geged-isa ow animwanowizh nindabweshkaagon\!” Aniish wiiba-go minawaa giikaji. Azhi-noondaagozid, “Aaniin-da, biitawashi’ig minawaa gimishoomi’iwaa.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*In a little while he became awake because of the warmth. “Oh, how really much am I made to sweat by this confounded tail of a dog\!” So in a little while he was again cold. When he was heard making a noise, “Why, put another cover over your uncle,” (said the old Wolf).*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Wiiba-go amajibizo (in a little while he woke from the warmth) ∙ “Zhi, geged-isa ow animwanowizh nindabweshkaagon\! (“oh, this confounded dog-tail really makes me sweat\!”) ∙ Aniish wiiba-go minawaa giikaji (so in a little while he was cold again) ∙ Azhi-noondaagozid (when he was heard making a noise) ∙ “Aaniin-da, biitawashi’ig minawaa gimishoomi’iwaa” (“why, put another cover over your uncle”)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(17) Päjik äjigikitut: “Ingutci kuca ugia‘pagitōn anubi‘tawaca‘ag.” Kägä‘t minawa pai‘ci‘i‘nanowäninit ini‘ ma‘i‘nganan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Bezhig azhi-gigidod: “Ingoji gusha ogii-abagidoon aanu-biitawashi’ag.” Geged minawaa baezhi-inanowaeninid iniw ma’iinganan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*One then spoke up: “Why, off he flung the cover when I tried to put it over him.” Truly again the Wolf laid his tail (over him).*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Bezhig azhi-gigidod (one then spoke up) ∙ “Ingoji gusha ogii-abagidoon aanu-biitawashi’ag (“he flung off the cover when I tried to put it over him”) ∙ Geged minawaa baezhi-inanowaeninid iniw ma’iinganan (truly again that Wolf laid his tail over him)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(18) Midạc na‘i‘ äjiwâbaninig. Cigwa kigicäp äjikanōnint Nänabucu: “Nawa‘kwäg mi a‘‘pi kä‘u‘di‘tamạng ‘i‘i‘ᵘ asạntcigwan.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash na’iiw azhi-waabaninig. Zhigwa gigizheb azhi-ganoonind Nenabozho: “Naawakweg mii apii ge-odi’tamang ’i’iw asanjigwan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And so by that time it was day. Then in the morning was Nänabushu addressed: “By noon is when we shall arrive at the cache.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash na’iiw azhi-waabaninig (and so by then it was day) ∙ Zhigwa gigizheb azhi-ganoonind Nenabozho (then in the morning Nänabushu was addressed) ∙ “Naawakweg mii apii ge-odi’tamang ’i’iw asanjigwan” (“by noon is when we will reach the cache”)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(19) Äjimādcāwāt, udānāng pimusäwag ini‘ᵘ wīda‘kiwä”zīyan mōjạg unīganī‘i‘gōn. Ningutingigu uganönigōn a‘kiwänzī mainganan: “Nänabucu, ambäsanō, intawā, kägu’ usowa‘kän ‘i‘i‘wä kīnạg, osām mācimagwat. Intawāgu äcictcigäyamban mii‘ icictcigän tcipimusäyan.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Azhi-maajaawaad, odaanaang bimosewag iniw wiidakiwenziiyan moozhag onigaani’igoon. Ningodingigo oganoonigoon akiwenzii ma’iinganan: “Nenabozho, ambesino, indawaa, gego ozowaken ’i’iwe giinag, osaam maajimagwad. Indawaa-go eshichigeyamban mii izhichigen ji-bimoseyan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*When they started, behind walked he and his old companion who always had him keep ahead. And once he was told by the old Wolf: “Nänabushu, I beg of you, really, do not use your penis for a tail, for it smells too vile. Therefore, according to the manner you are accustomed (to), so you do when you travel.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Azhi-maajaawaad, odaanaang bimosewag (when they set out, behind walked) ∙ iniw wiidakiwenziiyan moozhag onigaani’igoon (his old companion who always kept him ahead) ∙ Ningodingigo oganoonigoon akiwenzii ma’iinganan (once the old Wolf told him) ∙ “Nenabozho, ambesino, indawaa, gego ozowaken ’i’iwe giinag (“Nänabushu, I beg you, do not use your penis for a tail) ∙ osaam maajimagwad (it smells too vile) ∙ Indawaa-go eshichigeyamban mii izhichigen ji-bimoseyan” (the way you are accustomed, that is how you should do when you travel”)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(20) Nänabucu misa’ gägä‘t äcictcigät, mīsa’ pitcīnạg minopimusät. A‘pidcigu udibia ‘i‘i‘ᵘ mai‘ngana. Cigwasa‘ tagwicinōg asantciguning. Aca tana‘kamigisiwa mōna‘a‘minit asantcigun. Oganönigön wita‘kiwänziyan: “Nänabucu, mīsa cigwa tciwisiniyang. ‘A‘a‘”, witci‘i‘wän tcimō‘ka‘a‘sandcigung.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Nenabozho mii-sa geged eshichiged, mii-sa bijiinag mino-bimosed. Aapijigo odibi’aa ’i’iw ma’iingana. Zhigwa-sa dagwishinoog asanjiguning. Azhe dana’kamigizi-waa moona’aminid asanjigon. Oganoonigoon wiidakiwenziiyan: “Nenabozho, mii-sa zhigwa ji-wiisiniyang. Aa, wiiji’iwen ji-mooka’a-asanjigong.”**</span>
