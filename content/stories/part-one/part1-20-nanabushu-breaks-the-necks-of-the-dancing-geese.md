20\. NÄNABUSHU BREAKS THE NECKS OF THE DANCING GEESE.

<span class="l-fiero">**Naanabozhoo obookogwebinaan iniw niimi’idiijin nikag**</span>

20\. Nänabushu Breaks the Necks of the Dancing Geese.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Midaasoganzh (probable) — Part I Series 2 (Nos. 19–32), Bois Fort, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Mīsagu ‘iᵉi′ᵘ bā‘pic ạnimādcānit. Nänabucu ạnibạbimusät cigwadạc umạdābī sībīns; udạnibạbima∙a∙tōn kägä‘pīᵉiᵉgu misᵉtcänig ‘iᵉi′ᵘ sībi. Cigwadạc udäbābạtān ‘iᵉi′∙ᵘ sāga∙i∙gan, ā‘pidci unicicinini imā wändcisāgitawänig ‘iᵉi′ᵘ sībi. Inäbit Nänabuco mīnisi∙u∙tāwạngänig; owâbamäᵉ niᵉkānsạᵉ, ā‘pidcisa pā‘tinīnōwạᵉ. Nänabucu wägunänīwinän, nātạgāmäba‘tō, udawī∙u∙dā‘pinän inī′ᵘ cingubīn; uᵉkunācicing ugackīwäginän. Uciwạniᵉkä iᵘ cingubī, ā‘pidcisa ubwāwạnänäᵉ iᵘ cingubī. Mīdạc ‘iᵉi′ᵘ änicimādcät udani∙ā∙sunän ‘iᵉi′∙ᵘ umiᵉkwābān. Änisāgawät kīgito aⁿ kisᵉtciniᵉka: “Nänabucu sāgäwät. Ucimuyuᵉk, kägūgu kīga∙i∙gunān.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa go i’iw baa-pii ani-madaajaanid. Naanabozhoo ani-babimosed zhigwa dash omadaabii ziibiins; odani-babimaa’adoon gaye-gizhi-go mishtaanig i’iw ziibi. Zhigwa dash odaababaadaan i’iw zaaga’igan, aapiji onizhishini imaa wenji-zaagidawenig i’iw ziibi. Inaabid Naanabozhoo minisi-odaawangenig; owaabamaan niikaansan, aapijisa baatiininowan. Naanabozhoo wegonen \*wewenii\*-naa, naadagaameba’too, odawii-odaapinaan iniw zhingoban; o’konaajijing \*ogashkiiweginaan\*. Ojiwani’ge iw zhingobii, aapijisa obwaawaninen iw zhingobii. Mii dash i’iw ani-zhi-madaajaad odani-aasoonan i’iw omikwaaban. Ani-zaagewed gii-ikido a’aw gichi-niikaa: “Naanabozhoo zaagewed. Oojimoyok, gegoo giga-i’igonaan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And so in course of time he was upon his way. While Nänabushu was travelling about, he soon came out upon a brook; he followed its course till at last it (opened out into) a large river. Then presently he came in sight of a lake, very beautiful was the place where the river flowed out (into the lake). While Nänabushu was looking about, (he saw) an island of sand; he saw some Goslings, very numerous were they. Nänabushu, simpleton that he was, went running out towards the land, he went to get some balsam-boughs; in his old soiled blanket he wrapped them. He made a pack of the balsams, a very heavy pack did the balsams make. And so when he started on his way, he had his hands on the tump-line (running from the forehead back over the shoulders). When he came into view (round a point of land), up spoke a big Goose: “Nänabushu is coming into view (round the point). Do you flee, for something shall we be told.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa go i’iw baa-pii (and so it was at that time) ∙ ani-madaajaanid (as he set off moving along). Naanabozhoo ani-babimosed (Nänabushu, as he walked along) ∙ zhigwa dash omadaabii ziibiins (then he came out upon a little river); ∙ odani-babimaa’adoon (he followed it along) ∙ gaye-gizhi-go mishtaanig i’iw ziibi (and big indeed grew that river). Zhigwa dash odaababaadaan i’iw zaaga’igan (then he came in sight of the lake), ∙ aapiji onizhishini imaa wenji-zaagidawenig i’iw ziibi (very lovely was the place where the river flowed out). Inaabid Naanabozhoo minisi-odaawangenig (as Nänabushu looked, an island of sand); ∙ owaabamaan niikaansan (he saw the goslings), ∙ aapijisa baatiininowan (very many were they). Naanabozhoo wegonen \*wewenii\*-naa (Nänabushu, foolish one — particle/stem flagged), ∙ naadagaameba’too (he ran off toward shore), ∙ odawii-odaapinaan iniw zhingoban (he wanted to take those balsam boughs); ∙ o’konaajijing \*ogashkiiweginaan\* (in his soiled blanket he wrapped them — stem flagged). Ojiwani’ge iw zhingobii (he made a pack of the balsam), ∙ aapijisa obwaawaninen iw zhingobii (very heavy was that balsam pack). Mii dash i’iw ani-zhi-madaajaad (and so as he set off) ∙ odani-aasoonan i’iw omikwaaban (he leaned forward on his tump-line). Ani-zaagewed gii-ikido a’aw gichi-niikaa (as he came into view, the big goose said): “Naanabozhoo zaagewed (Nänabushu is coming into view). ∙ Oojimoyok (flee, you all), ∙ gegoo giga-i’igonaan (something he will tell us).”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gaawiin dash wiin bazigwii-siiwag igiw niikaansag. Giniginin, oganoonigowaan Naanabozhoowan: “Geget sa go nindani-gaawendam \*enuwaabamagwaanin\* igiw nishiimeyag. Geget iginin be’pinaakamigad wendoosaayaan; mii sa iwidi niimi’idiiwaad anishinaabeg. Gagwaanisagimaaminwaa’igaadeniwan iniw nagamonan negamowaadin. Mii sa go ono’ \*beemoondaman\*-in iniw nagamonan. <span class="l-eng">*But the Goslings did not fly away. Lo, they were addressed by Nänabushu saying: “Truly am I sad at heart whenever I fail to see my little brothers. Now is the time for us to act. According as I sing, so do you when you dance. Wonderfully well made are the songs whenever they sing them. Now, these are the songs that I carry along. Pray, let me sing-dance for you. Right here will I set down the songs. I will make a place where you shall perform when I dance you all. Handsome will be the dance-lodge that I make.” Nänabushu thereupon set to work making it, at only such a height that it could not be taken at a leap was how high he made his dance-lodge.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin dash wiin bazigwii-siiwag igiw niikaansag (but those goslings did not fly up). Giniginin (lo, behold), ∙ oganoonigowaan Naanabozhoowan (they were spoken to by Nänabushu): “Geget sa go nindani-gaawendam (truly I am made sad) ∙ \*enuwaabamagwaanin\* igiw nishiimeyag (whenever I fail to see those, my little brothers — stem flagged). Geget iginin be’pinaakamigad wendoosaayaan (truly it is the day-event for which I have come); ∙ mii sa iwidi niimi’idiiwaad anishinaabeg (it is yonder the people dance). Gagwaanisagi-maaminwaa’igaadeniwan iniw nagamonan negamowaadin (wonderfully well-fashioned are the songs when they sing them). Mii sa go ono’ \*beemoondaman\*-in iniw nagamonan (it is these songs that I carry — stem flagged). Ambesano ga-nimi’ininim (come now, I will sing-dance for you). Mii omaa ji-bagidoondamaan iniw nagamonan (right here I will set down these songs). <span class="l-orig">(3) “Mī′∙ᵘ cigwa tcimādci‘taiyang tcinīmi∙i∙tiyank. Ạmbäsa, mī′∙iᵘ ici∙a∙gwä‘taiyuᵉk.” Kägä‘t niᵉkānsạg agwä‘tāwạg.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Mii iw zhigwa ji-maajii’taayang ji-niimi’idiyang. Ambesa, mii iw izhi-agwe’taayok.” Geget niikaansag agwe’taawag.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“It is now time for us to begin dancing together. Hither, now come you out of the water.” To be sure, the Goslings came out of the water.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii iw zhigwa ji-maajii’taayang ji-niimi’idiyang (it is now time for us to begin to dance together). ∙ Ambesa (come now), ∙ mii iw izhi-agwe’taayok (come you all up out of the water).” Geget niikaansag agwe’taawag (truly the goslings came up out of the water).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(4) “Ạmbäsạnō, kawīndạmōninim kä∙a∙i∙ciyäg. Migü i∙ᵉi′∙ᵘ käni∙i∙nạ∙a∙mān a∙i∙ciyuᵉk ‘iᵉi′∙ᵘ tcinīmiyäg. Ayangwāmisiyuᵉk. Kägu′ wīwạnīwisi‘kägun. Mīsạgu iᵘ cigwa tcipīndigäyäk ‘iᵉi′mā nīmi∙i∙tīwigạmigunk.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Ambesano, ga-wiindamooninim ge-izhichigeyeg. Mii go i’iw ge-ni-ina’amaan izhichigeyok i’iw ji-niimiyeg. Ayaangwaamisiyok. Gego wiiwaniiwisikegon. Mii sa go iw zhigwa ji-biindigeyeg i’imaa niimi’idiiwigamigong.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Harken\! I will tell you how you are to act. According as I sing, so do you when you dance. Do you take pains. Don’t you fail in anything. Now is the time for you to enter into the dance-lodge.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Ambesano (come now), ∙ ga-wiindamooninim ge-izhichigeyeg (I will tell you what you are to do). ∙ Mii go i’iw ge-ni-ina’amaan (just as I beat the song along) ∙ izhichigeyok i’iw ji-niimiyeg (so do you when you dance). ∙ Ayaangwaamisiyok (take care, you all). ∙ Gego wiiwaniiwisikegon (don’t any of you fail). ∙ Mii sa go iw zhigwa ji-biindigeyeg i’imaa niimi’idiiwigamigong (now is the time for you to enter into the dance-lodge).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(5) Äjipīndigäwät niᵉkānsạg, kayä wīn māng; kayä wīn dạc cingibis äjipīndigät. Kaᵉkina kā‘pīndigänit Nänabucu ugī‘pa∙a∙n udickwändäm. <span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-biindigewaad niikaansag, gaye wiin maang; gaye wiin dash zhingibis ezhi-biindiged. Gakina gaa-biindigenid Naanabozhoo ogii-baa’aan odishkwaandem. <span class="l-eng">*Then in went the Goslings, likewise the Loon; and also the Diver entered in. When all had gone inside, Nänabushu closed up the doorway. <span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-biindigewaad niikaansag (so in went the goslings), ∙ gaye wiin maang (and the loon too); ∙ gaye wiin dash zhingibis ezhi-biindiged (and the diver too went in). <span class="l-eng">*Nänabushu then spoke up: “Behold, now am I in a transport of delight\!” Rising to his feet, Nänabushu moved about, dancing here and there, approaching (the Goslings) while he danced. <span class="l-orig">(11) Äjitōckābit ä‘tawä, inäbit māng, na∙i∙tāg ubimibō‘kugwänäni Nänabucōwạn niᵉkānsạᵉ. Äjikīgitut māng: “Äᵉēᵉeⁱ, Nänabucu kidickwạnigunān\!” Mīsa′ a‘kawä ä‘kitut māng, mịgu iᵘ ānawitäbācitcipạgisut.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-doshkaabid e’tawe, inaabid maang, naa-itaa obimi-bookogwenaani Naanabozhoowan niikaansan. Ezhi-giigidod maang: “Aa’ii, Naanabozhoo gidishkwaaninigonaan\!” Mii sa aakawe e’kidod maang, mii go iw aanawi-debaashi-jibagizod.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*By the doorway yonder the Loon was dancing. Now, it seemed as if he heard the sound of the breaking of necks, thus it seemed to the Loon. Happening to open his eyes a little, why, when the Loon looked, it was to see Nänabushu at just the time when he was among the Goslings breaking their necks. <span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-doshkaabid e’tawe (as he opened his eyes a little), ∙ inaabid maang (the loon looked), ∙ naa-itaa obimi-bookogwenaani Naanabozhoowan niikaansan (and lo, Nänabushu was breaking the necks of the goslings).
