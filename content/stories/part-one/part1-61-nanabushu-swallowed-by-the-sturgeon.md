<span class="l-gloss">## **(61) Nanabushu swallowed by the Sturgeon ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-swallowed-by-the-sturgeon-footnotes.html))**</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash giiwenh (and so they say) a’iin daawaad (they were dwelling there) Nenaboozhoo ookomisan gaye (Nenaboozhoo and his grandmother too).</span>

1)  > **Mii dash giiwenh a’iin daawaad Nenaboozhoo ookomisan gaye.**

> And so it is said that Nanabushu and his grandmother continued living there.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding giiwenh (once they say) Nenaboozhoo ezhi-naanaagadawendang (as Nenaboozhoo was pondering deeply) inendam (he thought).</span>

2)  > **Ningoding giiwenh Nenaboozhoo ezhi-naanaagadawendang inendam:**

> Once on a time they say that while Nanabushu was meditating, he thought :

<span class="l-gloss">“Indige sa (“I wonder indeed) mii go niin gaa-izhi-bezhigowaanen,” (whether I am the only one) inendam Nenaboozhoo (thought Nenaboozhoo).</span>

3)  > **“Indige sa mii go niin gaa-izhi-pezhigowaanen,” inendam Nenaboozhoo.**

> "I am curious to know if I was the only one," thought Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash e-nendang (and then he thought): “Ninga-gagwejimaa nookomis.” (“I will ask my grandmother.”)</span>

4)  > **Mii dash e-nendang: “Ninga-gagwejimaa nookomis.”**

> Thereupon he thought: "I will ask my grandmother."

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish inaa aadizookaan (so, according to the sacred story), mii dash geget e-naad ookomisan (then truly he said to his grandmother).</span>

5)  > **Aaniish inaa aadizookaan, mii dash geget e-naad ookomisan:**

> So, according to the story, he then truly said to his grandmother :

“Nookomis,” odinaan (“my grandmother,” he said to her).

6)  > **“Nookomis,” odinaan;**

> "My grandmother," he said to her,

<span class="l-gloss">“mii na go giinawind eta go bimaadiziyang?” (“is it really only we two who are living?”) odinaan (he said to her).</span>

7)  > **“mii na go giinawind eta go bimaadiziyang?” odinaan.**

> "is it possible that you and I are the only ones living?" he said to her.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin na wiikaa niin ningii-ogiisii?” (“have I never had a mother?”) odinaan ookomisan (he said to his grandmother).</span>

8)  > **“Gaawiin na wiikaa niin ningii-ogiisii?” odinaan ookomisan.**

> "Have I never had a mother?" he said to his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii na go ge-izhi-biinishi-bimaadiziyaan?” (“is it that I simply came to live for no reason?”) odinaan ookomisan (he said to his grandmother).</span>

9)  > **“Mii na go ge-izhi-piinishi-bimaadiziyaan?” odinaan ookomisan.**

> "Is it possible that simply without cause I came into being?" he said to his grandmother.

Wiikaa dash oganoonigoonigod (and after a long while he was answered by her).

10) > **Wiikaa dash oganoonigoonigod:**

> So after a long while he was given reply, he was told :

“Noozhis,” odigoon (“my grandson,” he was told).

11) > **“Noozhis,” odigoon,**

> "My grandson," he was told,

“mii sa go iw gegaa ge-kidoyaambaan (“it was nearly so, as I shall tell).

12) > **“mii sa go iw gegaa ge-kidoyaambaan.**

> "it was almost like that, (as you will see from what) I shall say.

<span class="l-gloss">Namadabin (sit down), nashke (look here), giga-wiindamoon (I will inform you).</span>

13) > **Namadabin, nashke, giga-wiindamoon.**

> Be seated, listen, I shall inform you.

<span class="l-gloss">Bizindawishin weweni,” (“listen to me carefully,”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

14) > **Bizindawishin weweni,” odigoon.**

> Listen to me with care," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Geget gii-anishinaabekaa (“truly there were people living); mii dash igo gii-ani-jaajaagi-onishinoowaad (and so as time went on they gradually passed away one by one), biinish gaye wiinawaa igiw gi-niigii’igoog (until they too, those who gave birth to you) ged-inadwaaban (whom you would call your parents) gii-bezhigo gaye gi-sayenh,” (and there was also one who was your elder brother,”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

15) > **“Geget gii-anishinaabekaa; mii dash igo gii-ani-jaajaagi-onishinoowaad, biinish gaye wiinawaa igiw gi-niigii’igoog ged-inadwaaban gii-pezhigo gaye gi-sayenh,” odigoon.**

> "Verily, there were some people living; but then as time went on they gradually passed away one by one, till at last also went they whom you would call your parents. And there was also one that was your elder brother," he was told.

“Gaye wiin dash gii-wanishin (“and he too was lost).

16) > **“Gaye wiin dash gii-wanishin.**

> "And he too disappeared.

<span class="l-gloss">Giin dash gaye (and as for you too), nashke giga-wiindamoon (look, I will tell you) gaa-onji-mookinaagoziyan (from what source you came forth) omaa dash noongom gii-ayaayan (and why you are here now).</span>

17) > **Giin dash gaye, nashke giga-wiindamoon gaa-onji-mookinaagoziyan omaa dash noongom gii-ayaayan.**

> Now about you, listen, (and) I will explain to you from what source you came, and why now you are here.

<span class="l-gloss">Awe gi-sayenh mii aw gaa-niigid (that elder brother of yours, he who was born), i’iw idash ishkwaach e-yaamagag anishinaabe naagishin (and the thing that comes last when a person is born) webinigaade dash iw (was thrown away); mii dash e-zhinikaadeg (and this is what it is called) abinoojiinh odapikweshimon (a babe’s little head-rest), izhinikaate (so it is named).</span>

18) > **Awe gi-sayenh mii aw gaa-niigid, i’iw idash ishkwaach e-yaamagag anishinaabe naagishin webinigaade dash iw; mii dash e-zhinikaadeg abinoojiinh odapikweshimon, izhinikaate.**

> After that elder brother of yours was born, then that which is the last to come when one is born was thrown away ; for it is called a place-for-the-babe-to-rest-its-head, such is its name.

Mii dash imaa giin wendaadiziyan, noozhis (and it was from there that you came to be, my grandson).

19) > **Mii dash imaa giin wendaadiziyan, noozhis.**

> So it was from that source that you came, my grandson.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii gaa-niigid gi-sayenh (at the time your elder brother was born), mii dash gii-saagijigaadeg (then it was carried out of doors) ingoji dash gii-awi-agoojigaade (and somewhere it was hung up).</span>

20) > **Apii gaa-niigid gi-sayenh, mii dash gii-saagijigaadeg ingoji dash gii-awi-agoojigaade.**

> At the time when your elder brother was born, it was then taken somewhere out of doors and hung up.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash naagaj gaa-izhi-noondawind abinoojiinh (and then after a while a babe was heard) madwe-mawid (crying) iwidi agoodeg iw bikweshimonens (yonder where that little head-rest hung).</span>

21) > **Mii dash naagaj gaa-izhi-noondawind abinoojiinh madwe-mawid iwidi agoodeg iw bikweshimonens.**

> And so after a while there was heard the cry of a babe at yonder place where hung the little-rest-for-the-head.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-izhaayaan (so then I went there), mii dash imaa gii-mikoonaam (and there I found you).</span>

22) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-izhaayaan, mii dash imaa gii-mikoonaam.**

> Thereupon I went to the place, and there I found you.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash niin gaa-izhi-nitaawigi’inaan,” (and so it was I who raised you,”) odinaan (she said to him).</span>

23) > **Mii dash niin gaa-izhi-nitaawigi’inaan,” odinaan.**

> Therefore it was I who reared you," she said to him.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaye wiin dash gi-ga (“and your mother herself) gi-sayenh ogii-nitaawigi’aan (raised your elder brother).</span>

24) > **“Gaye wiin dash gi-ga gi-sayenh ogii-nitaawigi’aan.**

> "And your mother herself brought up your elder brother.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhinikaazod aw gi-sayen’iban (and the name of that late elder brother of yours) Nenaapaadam (was Nenaapaadam).</span>

25) > **Mii dash ezhinikaazod aw gi-sayen’iban Nenaapaadam.**

> And the name of your elder brother was Nanapatam.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaye giin gaa-izhi-wiinigooyan Nenaboozhoo,” (and the name you were given was Nenaboozhoo,”) odigoon ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother).</span>

26) > **Mii dash gaye giin gaa-izhi-wiinigooyan Nenaboozhoo,” odigoon ookomisan.**

> And so the name Nanabushu was what you were called," he was told by his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo gichi-naanaagadawendam (thereupon Nenaboozhoo pondered deeply), gashkendam gaye (and he grew sad too) mikwenimaad nangwana gii-osayen’id (remembering that he had indeed had an elder brother).</span>

27) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo gichi-naanaagadawendam, gashkendam gaye mikwenimaad nangwana gii-osayen’id.**

> Thereupon Nanabushu seriously began pondering, sad too he became at the thought that forsooth he had an elder brother.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo zhayiigwa gii-wawenendang (and then Nenaboozhoo at once made up his mind) aaniin waa-izhichiged (what he would do), mii dash e-naad ookomisan (and so he said to his grandmother).</span>

28) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo zhayiigwa gii-wawenendang aaniin waa-izhichiged, mii dash e-naad ookomisan:**

> And then Nanabushu straightway made up his mind what he would do, and so he said to his grandmother :

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookomis (“my grandmother), maamakaadakamig (it is astonishing) wiikaa gegoo gii-izhisiwan (that you never told me anything) iwi nangwana gii-izhiwebiziwaangen.” (about what had actually happened to us.”)</span>

29) > **“Nookomis, maamakaadakamig wiikaa gegoo gii-izhisiwan iwi nangwana gii-izhiwebiziwaangen.”**

> "My grandmother, it is strange that never did you say anything to me concerning what had actually happened to us."

<span class="l-gloss">“Onjida sa gaawiin gegoo gigii-wiiwiindamawasinoon,” (“it was on purpose that I told you nothing,”) odigoon ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother).</span>

30) > **“Onjida sa gaawiin gegoo gigii-wiiwiindamawasinoon,” odigoon ookomisan,**

> "It was for a purpose that I told you of nothing," he was told by his grandmother,

<span class="l-gloss">“anishaa ji-kahkendaman (“so that you would not be sad for nothing), gaye ji-gwii’inawi’inendanziyan,” (and that you would not be troubled in mind,”) odigoon ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother).</span>

31) > **“anishaa ji-kahkendaman, gaye ji-gwii’inawi’inendanziyan,” odigoon ookomisan.**

> "that for no cause you should be sad, and that you should not be disturbed in your peace of mind," he was told by his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“Bizaan igo ji-bimaadiziyan (“so that you would live in peace); enishigo waabandaman giizhig baaji-wayaaseyaagin (that you would simply behold the day as its light breaks forth); gaye zhayiigwa giizis ba-mooka’angin (and that when the sun comes up) ezhi-onaanigwendaagwag (as all things turn glad), ji-izhi-onaanigwendaman (you too would be glad).</span>

32) > **“Bizaan igo ji-pimaadiziyan; enishigo waabandaman giizhig baaji-wayaaseyaagin; gaye zhayiigwa giizis ba-mooka’angin ezhi-onaanigwendaagwag, ji-izhi-onaanigwendaman.**

> "And that in peace you should live; that you should behold with a feeling of contentment the light of day when it comes ; and that whenever the sun comes forth, when a sense of gladness pervades all things, you should be joyful too.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii i’iw wiikaa gaa-onji-kegoo-inisinowaan,” (that was why I never told you anything,”) odigoon ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother).</span>

33) > **Mii i’iw wiikaa gaa-onji-kegoo-inisinowaan,” odigoon ookomisan.**

> Now, that was why I never imparted any thing to you," he was told by his grandmother.

Mii dash Nenaboozhoo zhayiigwa inaad ookomisan (thereupon Nenaboozhoo presently said to his grandmother).

34) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo zhayiigwa inaad ookomisan:**

> Thereupon Nanabushu presently said to his grandmother :

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookomis,” odinaan (“my grandmother,” he said to her), “ninga-nandobani (“I am going to war).</span>

35) > **“Nookomis,” odinaan, “ninga-nandobani.**

> "My grandmother," he said to her, "I am going to war.

<span class="l-gloss">Ninga-nandawaabamaag awenenan gaa-nisigowaad ni-gii’igoog (I am going to look for whoever it was that killed those who gave birth to me) gaye ni-sayen.” (and my elder brother.”)</span>

36) > **Ninga-nandawaabamaag awenenan gaa-nisigowaad ni-gii’igoog gaye ni-sayen.”**

> I am going to seek those who slew my parents and my elder brother."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash e-god ookomisan (and then he was told by his grandmother): “Gego, noozhis (“don’t, my grandson).</span>

37) > **Mii dash e-god ookomisan: “Gego, noozhis.**

> And then he was told by his grandmother: "Don't, my grandson.

<span class="l-gloss">Ingoji gigad-inigaatoon giiyaw,” (“somewhere you will bring harm upon your own body,”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

38) > **Ingoji gigad-inigaatoon giiyaw,” odigoon.**

> Somewhere will you bring ruin upon yourself," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin,” odinaan ookomisan (“no,” he said to his grandmother), “booch igo ninga-nandawaabamaag.” (“I am bound to look for them anyway.”)</span>

39) > **“Gaawiin,” odinaan ookomisan, “booch igo ninga-nandawaabamaag.”**

> "No," he said to his grandmother, "I am determined to look for them."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo (so then Nenaboozhoo set off) awi-bisa’ang (going to split wood) wii-ozhitood bikwakoon gaye sawaanan (so that he might make arrows and spears).</span>

40) > **Mii dash ezhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo awi-pisa’ang wii-ozhitood bikwakoon gaye sawaanan.**

> So then off went Nanabushu, he went to seek (for a small straight tree, easy) to split, to the end that he might make some arrows and some spears.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash giiwenh ba-izhi-kiiwed wayiiba go (and so they say he came back home soon), mii dash e-god ookomisan (and then he was told by his grandmother).</span>

41) > **Mii dash giiwenh ba-izhi-kiiwed wayiiba go mii dash e-god ookomisan:**

> And now it is said that on his early return to his home he was then told by his grandmother : "

“Aaniin, noozhis?” odigoon (“what is it, my grandson?” he was told).

42) > **“Aaniin, noozhis?” odigoon.**

> What now, my grandson ?" he was told.

“Gaawiin gegoo,” ikido Nenaboozhoo (“nothing,” said Nenaboozhoo).

43) > **“Gaawiin gegoo,” ikido Nenaboozhoo.**

> "Nothing," said Nanabushu.

“Gaawiin biigwaasesinoon ni-waagaakwad (“my axe will not cut).

44) > **“Gaawiin biigwaasesinoon ni-waagaakwad.**

> "My axe does not cut.

Shkomaa siboojigan, nookomis (let me have the whetstone, my grandmother).

45) > **Shkomaa siboojigan, nookomis.**

> Please let me have the whetstone, my grandmother.

Niwii-kiiniboodoon waagaakwad.” (“I want to sharpen the axe.”)

46) > **Niwii-kiiniboodoon waagaakwad.”**

> I want to sharpen the axe."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget apagizod aw mindimooye (it was true that the old woman leaned over), giiskabag idash naanikibidood siboojigan (and from along the wall’s edge she pulled out a whetstone) miinaad (giving it to him).</span>

47) > **Mii dash geget apagizod aw mindimooye, giiskabag idash naanikibidood siboojigan miinaad.**

> It was true that over leaned the old woman, and from the meeting-place of the wall and the ground she drew forth a whetstone and gave it to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-odaapinang Nenaboozhoo (whereupon Nenaboozhoo reached and took it), mii dash ezhi-maajii-asiboojiged (and then he began whetting).</span>

48) > **Mii dash ezhi-odaapinang Nenaboozhoo, mii dash ezhi-maajii-asiboojiged.**

> Whereupon over reached Nanabushu, taking it, and then he began the work of sharpening (the axe).

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo gomaa apii megwaa asiboojiged (and so after a while, as Nenaboozhoo was whetting) mii noondang e-god (he then heard it saying to him).</span>

49) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo gomaa apii megwaa asiboojiged mii noondang e-god:**

> And so later on, while Nanabushu was at work with the whetstone, he then heard that which he was told:

<span class="l-gloss">“Goos, gi-ga,” (“your father, your mother,”) inweweg siboojigan (was the sound the whetstone made).</span>

50) > **“Goos, gi-ga,” inweweg siboojigan.**

> "Your father, your mother," was the sound of the whetstone.

“Wegonen wenji-ikidod?” inendam (“why does it say that?” he thought).

51) > **“Wegonen wenji-ikidod?” inendam.**

> "Why does it say that?" he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">“Maagizhaa nookomis onjida niwii’ik (“perhaps my grandmother is misleading me on purpose) gigaa-gwejimag,” inendam (about what I asked her,” he thought).</span>

52) > **“Maagizhaa nookomis onjida niwii’ik gigaa-gwejimag,” inendam.**

> "Perhaps my grandmother for some reason is deceiving me about what I had asked her," he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa ezhi-maajii-siboojiged (once more as he began whetting) mii miinawaa e-nitang (so again he heard it): “Goos, goos, goos, gi-ga, gi-ga, gi-ga.” (“your father, your father, your father, your mother, your mother, your mother.”)</span>

53) > **Miinawaa ezhi-maajii-siboojiged mii miinawaa e-nitang: “Goos, goos, goos, gi-ga, gi-ga, gi-ga.”**

> Once more as he began using the whetstone, so again he heard it: "Your father, your father, your father, your mother, your mother, your mother."

Aapiji Nenaboozhoo nishkaadizi (exceedingly angry was Nenaboozhoo).

54) > **Aapiji Nenaboozhoo nishkaadizi.**

> Exceedingly angry was Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">“Na, nashke e-goyaan iw waagaakwad\!” (“hey, listen to what the axe is saying to me\!”) odinaan ookomisan (he said to his grandmother).</span>

55) > **“Na, nashke e-goyaan iw waagaakwad\!” odinaan ookomisan.**

> "Hark, listen to what the axe is saying to me\!" he said to his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“Anishaa gosha gid-initam (“you are merely hearing) apane iw izhi-kizhibendaman,” (the very thing you keep turning over in your mind,”) odinaan (she said to him).</span>

56) > **“Anishaa gosha gid-initam apane iw izhi-kizhibendaman,” odinaan.**

> "Simply are you hearing the sound of what you are always revolving in your mind," she said to him.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin,” odinaan Nenaboozhoo (“no,” said Nenaboozhoo to her), “anishaa go ninga-gaanjigidemigon.” (“it is goading me to anger for no reason.”)</span>

57) > **“Gaawiin,” odinaan Nenaboozhoo, “anishaa go ninga-gaanjigidemigon.”**

> "No," to her said Nanabushu, "simply am I chided to anger."

Mii dash Nenaboozhoo geget nishkaadizid (thereupon Nenaboozhoo was truly angry).

58) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo geget nishkaadizid.**

> Thereupon Nanabushu truly was angry.

Mii dash ezhi-odaakinang miinawaa siboojigan (and so once more he took up the whetstone).

59) > **Mii dash ezhi-odaakinang miinawaa siboojigan.**

> And so he once more took up the whetstone.

<span class="l-gloss">Siboodood waagaakwad (whetting the axe), mii go miinawaa e-god (then again it said to him).</span>

60) > **Siboodood waagaakwad, mii go miinawaa e-god:**

> While whetting the axe, then again was he told:

<span class="l-gloss">“Goos, goos, gi-sayenh, gi-sayenh,” (“your father, your father, your elder brother, your elder brother,”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

61) > **“Goos, goos, gi-sayenh, gi-sayenh,” odigoon.**

> "Your father, your father, your elder brother, your elder brother," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-bazigonjised (thereupon he leaped to his feet); mii dash ezhi-nawajibidood waagaakwat (then he seized and held the axe fast).</span>

62) > **Mii dash ezhi-pazigonjised; mii dash ezhi-nawajibidood waagaakwat;**

> Thereupon he leaped to his feet thereupon he grabbed and fastened down the axe ;

<span class="l-gloss">ezhi-gichi-babakiteowaad asiniin (he pounded it hard with a stone) enaabidagaawaninig (right along the sharp cutting edge).</span>

63) > **ezhi-kichi-pabakiteowaad asiniin enaabidagaawaninig.**

> he thoroughly pounded it with a stone right on the sharp edge.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget gichi-ozhiitaad wii-maajaad (thereupon truly he was fully ready to go); niibiwa ogii-ozhitoonan obikwakoon osawaanan gaye (many arrows and spears he had made).</span>

64) > **Mii dash geget gichi-ozhiitaad wii-maajaad; niibiwa ogii-ozhitoonan obikwakoon osawaanan gaye.**

> Thereupon truly was he fully prepared to go ; many arrows and spears had he made.

Anishinaa aadizookaan (so far as the sacred story tells).

65) > **Anishinaa aadizookaan,**

> So far as the story goes,

<span class="l-gloss">gaawiin dibaajigaadesinoon wegonen gaayoogwen (it is not told with what) gii-ozhitood gichi-niibiwa obikwakoon gaye odasawaanan (he made his very many arrows and spears).</span>

66) > **gaawiin dibaajigaadesinoon wegonen gaayoogwen gii-ozhitood gichi-niibiwa obikwakoon gaye odasawaanan,**

> it is not told what he used when he made the great number of his arrows and spears,

iw giikiizhkaabidesidood owaagaakwad (for he had blunted the edge of his axe).

67) > **iw giikiizhkaabidesidood owaagaakwad;**

> for he had dulled his axe ;

mii eta go debaajimind gii-kiizhiitaa’idizod (it is only told of him that he had got himself ready).

68) > **mii eta go debaajimind gii-kiizhiitaa’idizod.**

> it is only told of him how that he had made himself prepared.

Mii dash giiwenh Nenaboozhoo ezhi-maajaad (and so they say Nenaboozhoo started off).

69) > **Mii dash giiwenh Nenaboozhoo ezhi-maajaad.**
