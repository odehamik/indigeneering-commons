<span class="l-gloss">## (42) Nanabushu and the Woodpecker ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html))</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Weyaabaninig (when it was the next day) miinawaa aano-giiyose (again he hunted in vain), mii sa onjida (and so as it happened) gaawiin gegoo onitoosiin (he killed nothing).</span>

1)  > **Weyaabaninig miinawaa aano-kiiyose, mii sa onjida gaawiin gegoo onitoosiin.**

> On the morrow he went on another fruitless hunt for game, and it was just his luck not to kill a thing.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa go ani-maajaa (again he set off); mii onjida (and as ill luck had it) gaawiin gegoo onitoosiin (he killed nothing).</span>

2)  > **Miinawaa go ani-maajaa; mii onjida gaawiin gegoo onitoosiin.**

> An other time he set out ; but, as ill luck would have it, he did not kill a thing.

<span class="l-gloss">Gegapii ezhi-giigidod wiiwan (at last his wife spoke up): “Geget gii-goopadis (“truly you have been of no use). Awashime indawaa \*gisjikii-oodeyan\* (it would be far better for you to go visiting — \*stem flagged: ‘go on a visit’\*); mii eta ge-izhi-wiisiniyangiban (only thus could we have food to eat), mii eta ji-waabanishiyang.” (only thus shall we see the morning through.”)</span>

3)  > **Gegapii ezhi-kiigidod wiiwan: “Geget gii-goopadis. Awashime indawaa gisjikii-oodeyan; mii eta ge-izhi-wiisiniyangiban, mii eta ji-waabanishiyang.”**

> At last then up spoke the woman: "Really, you are of no use. It would therefore be much better for you to go on a visit among (your friends); for only by such means shall we obtain food to eat, only in that way shall we live through the winter."

Gigizheb ezhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo (in the morning Nenaboozhoo set off).

4)  > **Gigizheb ezhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo.**

> In the morning then departed Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaago apii degwishing (when he had come some distance), zaaga’igan owaabandaan (he saw a lake).</span>

5)  > **Gomaago apii degwishing, zaaga’igan owaabandaan.**

> When some distance away he was come, a lake he saw.

<span class="l-gloss">Ayinaabid (as he looked about), awiiya owaabamaan (he saw someone) bebaamaadagaakonid (walking about on the ice).</span>

6)  > **Ayinaabid, awiiya owaabamaan bebaamaadagaakonid.**

> While looking around, he saw somebody walking about on the ice.

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-izhaad (as he went toward him), owaabamaan ininiwan (he saw a man).</span>

7)  > **Eni-izhaad, owaabamaan ininiwan.**

> When he started hitherward, he saw a man.

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga, ga-ani-wiijiiwin (“pray, let me go along with you) wii-giiweyan.” (when you go home.”)</span>

8)  > **“Daga, ga-ani-wiijiiwin wii-kiiweyan.”**

> "Pray, let me go with you when you depart for home \!"

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa owaabandaan wiigiwaam (presently he saw a wigwam); ani-biindigewan (the other went in) gaye wiin (and so did he too). Gaa-onabid (when he was seated) owaabamaan ikwewan (he saw a woman) dazhi-mashkimodikenid (there making a bag).</span>

9)  > **Zhigwa owaabandaan wiigiwaam; ani-piindigewan gaye wiin gaa-onabid owaabamaan ikwewan dazhi-mashkimodikenid.**

> Presently he saw a wigwam ; when in the other went, so then (did) he. When he was seated, he saw a woman busily making a bag.

<span class="l-gloss">Naanaa gego zhigwa genoonimaawan (after a while she was spoken to): “Daga naa, agoozh a’aw akik.” (“please now, hang up that kettle.”)</span>

10) > **Naanaa gego zhigwa genoonimaawan: “Daga naa, agoozh a’aw akik.”**

> After a while she was then spoken to: "Please hang up the kettle."

Geget ikwe akikwan odoonagoonaan (truly the woman hung up the kettle).

11) > **Geget ikwe akikwan odoonagoonaan.**

> Truly, the woman arranged (the kettle) so as to hang.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-giizhiitaad (when she had finished), baanimaa go (then all at once) namadabinid onji-bazigwa’owan (the one seated flew up), abazhing abagizowan (lighting on the lodge-pole) noondaagoziwan (and was heard): “Gwo, gwo, gwo, gwo\!” inwewan (“Gwo, gwo, gwo, gwo\!” was his cry).</span>

12) > **Gaa-kiizhiitaad, baanimaa go namadabinid onji-pasigwa’owan, abazhing abagizowan noondaagoziwan: “Gwo, gwo, gwo, gwo\!” inwewan.**

> When she had finished, then of a sudden he that was seated flew up, a-lighting yonder on the lodge-pole, (and) could be heard saying: "Kwu, kwu, kwu, kwu\!" such was his cry.

Mii nangwana memen wedisaad (it happened to be Red-Head he was visiting).

13) > **Mii nangwana memen wedisaad.**

> It happened to be the Red- Head that he was visiting.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa, gaa-tagwishininid iwidi soowaaganing (now, when he had reached up there at the meeting of the lodge-poles), maatigwe’igewan (he began pecking at the wood).</span>

14) > **Zhigwa, gaa-tagwishininid iwidi soowaaganing, maatigwe’igewan.**

> Now, when (the Red-Head) was come at the meeting of the lodge-poles, he then began pecking.

<span class="l-gloss">Naagaj igo mandaamina’ bi-siigisewa’ (after a while the kernels of corn came pouring in) mii imaa akikong (there into the kettle), mii dash iw ezhi-mooshkinaanid (and so it filled up) iniw odakikoowaan (their kettle).</span>

15) > **Naagaj igo mandaamina’ bi-siigisewa’ mii imaa akikong, mii dash iw ezhi-mooshkinaanid iniw odakikoowaan.**

> And after a while some corn came pouring into the kettle there, whereupon full of it became their kettle.

<span class="l-gloss">Ba-izhi-niisigwaashkwaninid (down he came hopping), mii sa naasaab anishinaabe (and so once more he was a person).</span>

16) > **Ba-izhi-niisigwaashkwaninid, mii sa naasaab anishinaabe.**

> Down he came hopping ; and when (he was come), then back again (was he in) human (form).

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, mii go miinawaa iw gii-wawenabinid iniw ikwewan (well, and again the woman sat down) mashkimodikenid (making her bag), giigidonid iniw (she spoke): “Wegonesh ina ge-yapaabooweyang? (“what shall we use for the broth?) Mii na zhigwo naa iw?” (or is it ready just so?”)</span>

17) > **Aaniish, mii go miinawaa iw gii-wawenabinid iniw ikwewan mashkimodikenid, giigidonid iniw: “Wegonesh ina ge-yapaabooweyang? Mii na zhigwo naa iw?”**

> Well, and so another time was the woman seated, making her sack, when she said: "What shall we put (into the corn) for seasoning, or shall it be just so?"

Goniginiin, ogaa-kaakaasiiyaabikinaan i’iw mookomaan (lo, he wiped clean the blade of that knife).

18) > **Goniginiin, ogaa-kaakaasiiyaabikinaan i’iw mookomaan.**

> Lo, he now wiped the blade of his knife.

<span class="l-gloss">Goniginiin, ezhi-maadagwashaabinaad iniw wiiwani (behold, he uncovered his wife), gonigosha bikwanaaning ezhi-maadishwaad (and right on her back he began carving), gomaa go minik obakweshwaan (rather large pieces he sliced off).</span>

19) > **Goniginiin, ezhi-maadagwashaabinaad iniw wiiwani, gonigosha bikwanaaning ezhi-maadishwaad, gomaa go minik obakweshwaan.**

> Behold, when he uncovered his wife, plump on her very back he then began slicing her, rather large pieces he sliced off.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-bakweshwaad (when he had finished slicing her), mii dash i’iw ozikoon ezhi-sinigwonamawaad (he rubbed his spittle over) imaa gii-bakweshwed (where he had carved).</span>

20) > **Gaa-pakweshwaad, mii dash i’iw ozikoon ezhi-sinigwonamawaad imaa gii-pakweshwed.**

> When he had done with carving her, he then rubbed his spittle over (the place) where he had carved her.

Mii dash imaa odakikowaang ezhi-boodaakwe’amowaad i wiiyaas (and so into their kettle they put the meat to boil).

21) > **Mii dash imaa odakikowaang ezhi-poodaakwe’amowaad i wiiyaas.**

> And then into their kettle they put the meat to boil.

Ezhi-onzonid odakikowaang (then their kettle began boiling).

22) > **Ezhi-onzonid odakikowaang.**

> Then their kettle began boiling.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa gaa-giizhidenig (now, when the food was done cooking), ezhi-agwaabiiga’ang (she dipped it out); Nenaboozhoo bagidinamawaa ji-wiisinid (and she set it before Nenaboozhoo to eat) gaye i mandaamina’ (and the corn too).</span>

23) > **Zhigwa gaa-kiizhidenig, ezhi-agwaabiiga’ang; Nenaboozhoo bagidinamawaa ji-wiisinid gaye i mandaamina’.**

> Now, after the food was done cooking, then out she dipped it ; and in front of Nanabushu, that he might eat, she placed (the meat) and the corn.

Etawaa, mii dash geget Nenaboozhoo mino-wiisinid\! (oh, and truly Nenaboozhoo ate well\!)

24) > **Etawaa, mii dash geget Nenaboozhoo mino-wiisinid\!**

> Oh, but Nanabushu truly had a pleasant time eating\!

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji gaa-de-wiisinid (when he had eaten his fill), mii iw ezhi-aanizhiitang (he then left off eating).</span>

25) > **Aapiji gaa-de-wiisinid, mii iw ezhi-aanizhiitang.**

> After he was quite satisfied with food, he accordingly ceased eating.

Mii sa miinawa ezhi-gaanaad ogishoote’ona’ (and then again he hid his mittens).

26) > **Mii sa miinawa ezhi-kaanaad ogishoote’ona’.**

> And then again he hid his mittens.

“Mii iw maajaayaan giiweyaan.” (“now I will set off home.”)

27) > **“Mii iw maajaayaan giiweyaan.”**

> "Now I should start back home."

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo inaa (Nenaboozhoo was told): “Mii go iw ji-giiwewidaw.” (“take some home with you, then.”)</span>

28) > **Nenaboozhoo inaa: “Mii go iw ji-giiwewidaw.”**

> Nanabushu was told: "Therefore do you take them home (some food)."

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa, gomaago apii eni-dagwishing (again, when he had gone some distance), biipaagi Nenaboozhoo (Nenaboozhoo called out): “Giwanichigaagime\!” (“I have forgotten something\!”)</span>

29) > **Miinawaa, gomaago apii eni-dagwishing, biipaagi Nenaboozhoo: “Giwanichigaagime\!”**

> Again, after some distance he was come, out called Nanabushu: "I have forgotten something\!"

Gaawiin aano-debwetawaasii (but in vain — he was not heeded).

30) > **Gaawiin aano-debwetawaasii.**

> But in vain was he not listened to.

<span class="l-gloss">Eshkam enigok biipaagi (louder and louder he shouted), gegapii (until at last), “Maanoo, izhiwidawik Nenaboozhoo ogiishoote’ona’.” (“very well, take Nenaboozhoo his mittens.”)</span>

31) > **Eshkam enigok biipaagi, gegapii, “Maanoo, izhiwidawik Nenaboozhoo ogiishoote’ona’.”**

> Louder still he shouted, till finally, "Well, then do you take to Nanabushu his mittens."

Geget gwiiwizensag odi-shiiwinaawaa (sure enough, the boys took them to him).

32) > **Geget gwiiwizensag odi-shiiwinaawaa.**

> To be sure, the boys took them to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, waasa go odashaapagidawaawaa (now, from far off they tossed them to him) ezhi-ganoonaad (when he said to them): “Ni-shiimisha, besho go bi-izhaayok (“my little brothers, come up close). Gi-bakadem awa sa giinawaa (you must be hungry). Waabang goosiwaa da-bi-izhaa (tomorrow let your father come over), naawakweg oga-bi-naadin ge-miijiyeg.” (at noon let him come fetch food for you to eat.”)</span>

33) > **Aaniish, waasa go odashaapagidawaawaa ezhi-ganoonaad: “Ni-shiimisha, besho go bi-izhaayok. Gi-bakadem awa sa giinawaa. Waabang goosiwaa da-bi-izhaa, naawakweg oga-pi-naadin ge-miijiyeg.”**

> Now, from afar were they throwing them to him, when he said to them: "My little brothers, up close do you come. You must be hungry. To-morrow let your father come over, at noon let him come to get some food for you to eat."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ezhi-giiwed Nenaboozhoo (and so Nenaboozhoo went home) gaa-tagwishing endaawaad (when he reached where they lived), mii sa naa miinawaa wiisiniwaad (and so once more they ate).</span>

34) > **Mii sa ezhi-kiiwed Nenaboozhoo gaa-tagwishing endaawaad, mii sa naa miinawaa wiisiniwaad.**

> And so it is said that when Nanabushu was come at where they lived, then the poor things ate again.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii wi iniw gii-ganoonaad wiiwan (thereupon he spoke to his wife): “Nashkenaa\! mashkimodiken.” (“look here\! make a bag.”)</span>

35) > **Mii wi iniw gii-kanoonaad wiiwan: “Nashkenaa\! mashkimodiken.”**

> There upon he spoke to his wife, saying : " Now, come and make a bag\!"

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-ganoonigod Nenaboozhoo wiiwan (then Nenaboozhoo was answered by his wife): “Indaskaa miinawaa iw awiya gegoo \*gii-shinawaawaden\* (“no doubt you have again seen someone doing something — \*stem flagged\*). Edadagwashako gii-menishi ishkwaakamik gii-doodanziiwan\!” (oh, how you shame me, always trying to do everything\!” — \*first clause stem uncertain, flagged\*)</span>

36) > **Ezhi-kanoonigod Nenaboozhoo wiiwan: “Indaskaa miinawaa iw awiya gegoo gii-shinawaawaden. Edadagwashako gii-menishi ishkwaakamik gii-doodanziiwan\!”**

> Then was Nanabushu answered by his wife saying: "No doubt but that you have again seen somebody doing something. Oh, how you make me ashamed in your trying always to do everything \!"

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo ezhi-giigidod (Nenaboozhoo then spoke up): “Maanoo\! mashkimodiken.” (“never mind\! just make the bag.”)</span>

37) > **Nenaboozhoo ezhi-kiigidod: “Maanoo\! mashkimodiken.”**

> Nanabushu then spoke up: "Never you mind\! just you make the bag."

“Wegonesh ina ge-aabajitooyaan i’iw ji-mashkimodikeyaan?” (“pray, what shall I use to make the bag?”)

38) > **“Wegonesh ina ge-aabajitooyaan i’iw ji-mashkimodikeyaan?”**

> "Pray, what shall I use to make the bag?"

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, wiigobiizh odayaan aw ikwe (now, the woman had some basswood bast), mii sa naa iw wenaabiiginang (and so with that she began weaving).</span>

39) > **Aaniish, wiigobiizh odayaan aw ikwe, mii sa naa iw wenaabiiginang.**

> Now, some bast did the woman have, and so with that she began weaving (a bag).

Aaniish baabii’od Nenaboozhoo (meanwhile Nenaboozhoo waited).

40) > **Aaniish baabii’od Nenaboozhoo.**

> In the mean while waited Nanabushu.

Zhigwa naawakwenig dagwishinoon iniw ininiwan (when it was noon the man came).

41) > **Zhigwa naawakwenig dagwishinoon iniw ininiwan.**

> When it was noon, then came the man.

<span class="l-gloss">Baapiindigenid (when he had come in), “Wegonesh ina ge-ishangeyang?” (“pray, what shall we offer him to eat?”)</span>

42) > **Baapiindigenid, “Wegonesh ina ge-ishangeyang?”**

> When he entered within, "Pray, what shall we offer (him) to eat?"

Nenaboozhoo biiwaabikozh ogiikaashiboodoon (Nenaboozhoo sharpened an old piece of metal).

43) > **Nenaboozhoo biiwaabikozh ogiikaashiboodoon.**

> Nanabushu sharpened an old piece of metal.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-biindigenid (after the other had entered), Nenaboozhoo bazigonjise (Nenaboozhoo sprang up).</span>

44) > **Gaa-piindigenid, Nenaboozhoo bazigonjise.**

> After the other had come in, Nanabushu then sprang up.

“Gwo, gwo, gwo, gwo\!” inwed (“Gwo, gwo, gwo, gwo\!” was the sound he made).

45) > **“Gwo, gwo, gwo, gwo\!” inwed.**

> "Kwu, kwu, kwu, kwu \!" was the sound he uttered.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-ishisidood obiiwaabikoni imaa oshingwanaang (when he set the metal into his nose), zhigwa akwaandawe (then he climbed up).</span>

46) > **Ezhi-ishisidood obiiwaabikoni imaa oshingwanaang, zhigwa akwaandawe.**

> When he placed the metal into his nose, then up he climbed.

Zhigwa iwidi gashki’o disawaaganing (presently he managed to reach up at the meeting of the lodge-poles).

47) > **Zhigwa iwidi gashki’o disawaaganing.**

> Presently he was able to reach the meeting of the lodge-poles.

Zhigwa obakii’aan odabanzhiiwa’ (then he pecked at their lodge-poles).

48) > **Zhigwa obakii’aan odabanzhiiwa’.**

> Then he pecked at their lodge-poles.

<span class="l-gloss">Etawaa\! wendago geget waasa inaakwijid (alas\! truly far in it lodged) bangishing (when he fell), “joen\!” inweweshin Nenaboozhoo (“joen\!” was the sound Nenaboozhoo made as he hit).</span>

49) > **Etawaa\! wendago geget waasa inaakwijid bangishing, “joen\!” inweweshin Nenaboozhoo.**

> Alas \! right straight in for a long way it truly entered. When he dropped, "ten\!" was the sound Nanabushu made.

Etawe\! onawadinaan abiiwide (poor thing\! the visitor grabbed him).

50) > **Etawe\! onawadinaan abiiwide.**

> Poor thing\! him the visitor grabbed.

Maanoo, indawaa owiikobidawaan iw biiwaabik (well then, he pulled the metal out of him).

51) > **Maanoo, indawaa owiikobidawaan iw biiwaabik.**

> However, he then pulled out the metal.

<span class="l-gloss">Bangii wiin go ganage miskwiiwan (he had bled just a little), gaasiiyaabanaawaad miskwiiwinid (and he washed him where he was bloody).</span>

52) > **Bangii wiin go ganage miskwiiwan, gaasiiyaabanaawaad miskwiiwinid.**

> And (he saw that Nanabushu) had nevertheless bled a little, (whereupon) he then washed him where he was bloody.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo zhigwa ani-bagakaadizi (Nenaboozhoo was now coming to his senses) ezhi-giigidod (when he spoke): “Maanoo go, mashkimodiken\!” (“never mind, make the bag\!”) odinaan iniw wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

53) > **Nenaboozhoo zhigwa ani-pagakaadizi ezhi-kiigidod: “Maanoo go, mashkimodiken\!” odinaan iniw wiiwan.**

> Nanabushu was now becoming conscious when he said : "Never you mind, but make the sack\!" he said to his wife.

Aaniish, geget maajitaa a’aw ikwe mashkimodaakaad (now, truly the woman set to work making the bag).

54) > **Aaniish, geget maajitaa a’aw ikwe mashkimodaakaad.**

> Now, truly the woman started upon the work of making the sack.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii iw zhigwa gii-odaapinang iw mookomaan (then, when he took up the knife), zhigwa omanibidawaan i’iw obabiinzikawaaganishini (he stripped off her old jacket).</span>

55) > **Mii iw zhigwa gii-odaapinang iw mookomaan, zhigwa omanibidawaan i’iw obabiinzikawaaganishini.**

> Then, when he took up the knife, he began removing her old jacket.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-maadishwaad bikwanaaning (when he began carving her at the back), bane go noondaagozinid (at once she was heard crying out) aapiji owiisagishwen (so painfully did he cut her).</span>

56) > **Ezhi-maadishwaad bikwanaaning, bane go noondaagozinid aapiji owiisagishwen.**

> When he began carving her at the back, straightway she began to be heard (crying aloud). Very painfully did he hurt her with the knife.

<span class="l-gloss">“Bizaan\! mii gosha ko iw endoodamaan (“hush\! this is what I always do) waakiige’iweyaan.” (whenever I wish to entertain.”)</span>

57) > **“Bizaan\! mii gosha ko iw endoodamaan waakiige’iweyaan.”**

> "Hush\! for this is what I always do whenever I wish to entertain."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-ganoonigod biiwiden (then the guest spoke to him): “Bekaa\! ganabaj giga-nisaa gi-mindimooyemish (“stop\! you may well kill your old woman). Indawaa biidoon iw mookomaan.” (so hand over the knife.”)</span>

58) > **Mii dash ezhi-kanoonigod biiwiden: “Bekaa\! ganabaj giga-nisaa gi-mindimooyemish. Indawaa biidoon iw mookomaan.”**

> Then was he addressed by the guest saying: "Stop\! perhaps you will kill your old woman. Therefore fetch hither the knife."

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-bakweshomind iniw wiiwan (when a piece was sliced from his wife), ezhi-boodaakwe’aminid imaa odakikoowaang (it was put into their kettle to boil).</span>

59) > **Ezhi-pakweshomind iniw wiiwan, ezhi-poodaakwe’aminid imaa odakikoowaang.**

> When (the visitor) sliced off a piece from the wife of (Nanabushu), he put it into their kettle to boil.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa, bazigwa’onid imaa disowaaganing (next, flying up to the meeting of the lodge-poles), mii dash imaa maadikwe’igenid (he began pecking there).</span>

60) > **Miinawaa, bazigwa’onid imaa disowaaganing, mii dash imaa maadikwe’igenid.**

> Next, flying up to the meeting of lodge-poles, (the visitor) then began to peck.

<span class="l-gloss">Naagaj igo mandaamina’ bi-siigibizowa’ (after a while the kernels of corn came pouring), odakikoowang i’imaa ezhi-siigibizonid (into their kettle they poured).</span>

61) > **Naagaj igo mandaamina’ bi-siigibizowa’, odakikoowang i’imaa ezhi-siigibizonid.**

> After a while some corn came pouring out, into their kettle it poured.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-mooshkinenid iniw odakikoowaan (after their kettle was full), mii sa iw Nenaboozhoo izhi-wiisinid (then Nenaboozhoo ate).</span>

62) > **Gaa-mooshkinenid iniw odakikoowaan, mii sa iw Nenaboozhoo izhi-wiisinid.**

> After their kettle was full, then did Nanabushu eat.

“Mii iw nin-giiwe.” (“now I am going home.”)

63) > **“Mii iw nin-giiwe.”**

> "Now I am going home."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa iw (and so it is) binewijiid (egoodeg) (the rump of the ruffed grouse — hanging aloft) (the carrier closing, left to the listener’s wondering).</span>

64) > **Mii sa iw binewijiid (egoodeg). ([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html))**
