(39) Nanabushu and the Soaring Eagle

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa wii-biboonishi (and now he was going to make his winter camp) megiziwaash (Soaring-Eagle).</span>

1)  > **Mii sa wii-biboonishi megiziwaash.**

> And now Soaring-Eagle was planning to go into camp for the winter.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa wii-maajitaa (and in a while he meant to set to work) giigoonyan wii-nooji’aad (intending to go after fish), mii dash i’iw ezhi-waabamaad (whereupon he saw) gii-dagwishininid iniw Nenaboozhoowan (Nenaboozhoo, who had arrived).</span>

2)  > **Zhigwa sa wii-maajitaa giigoonyan wii-nooji’aad, mii dash i’iw ezhi-waabamaad gii-dagwishininid iniw Nenaboozhoowan.**

> And in a while he intended to set about to get some fish, whereupon he then saw Nänabushu, who now arrived (at his place).

<span class="l-gloss">“Naabwiinaa maamawijigeyang (“would it not be well that we work together) ji-nooji’angwaa igiw giigoonyag?” (to go after those fish?”)</span>

3)  > **“Naabwiinaa maamawijigeyang ji-nooji’angwaa igiw giigoonyag?”**

> “Would it not be well for us to go together to get the fish?” (said Nänabushu).

<span class="l-gloss">“Aw, mii iw ezhi-minwendamaan (“well, that is what would please me) omaa go bi-izhi-gozin.” (just move your camp over here.”)</span>

4)  > **“Aw, mii iw ezhi-minwendamaan omaa go bi-izhi-gozin.”**

> “Very well, and in that case I should be pleased if you would move your belongings over to this place.”

Geget, Nenaboozhoo bi-gabeshi (sure enough, Nenaboozhoo came over and camped).

5)  > **Geget, Nenaboozhoo bi-gabeshi.**

> Sure enough, hither came Nänabushu to camp.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa zhigwa maajitaawaad (and so then they set to work); giigoonyan niibiwa onisaawaan (many fish they killed).</span>

6)  > **Mii sa zhigwa maajitaawaad; giigoonyan niibiwa onisaawaan.**

> So thereupon they set to work; many fish they killed.

Giigoonya’ odajidagoonaawa’ (they hung the fish up on racks).

7)  > **Giigoonya’ odajidagoonaawa’.**

> They hung the fish upon racks, with the heads down.

Aapiji’sh isa niibiwa onisaawaa (and ever so many they killed).

8)  > **Aapiji’sh isa niibiwa onisaawaa.**

> Ever so many they killed.

Zhigwa gashkadinini i’iw zaaga’igan (then the lake froze over).

9)  > **Zhigwa gashkadinini i’iw zaaga’igan.**

> In time frozen became the lake.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ezhi-gozinid Nenaboozhoowan (then Nenaboozhoo moved camp); gakina omaajiinaa’ giigoonya’ (all the fish he took away with him).</span>

10) > **Mii sa ezhi-gozinid Nenaboozhoowan; gakina omaajiinaa’ giigoonya’.**

> Thereupon Nänabushu moved camp; all the fish he took away with him.

Mii sa pane gaawiin gegoo oga-miijisiin megiziwaash (and so nothing at all would Soaring-Eagle have to eat).

11) > **Mii sa pane gaawiin gegoo oga-miijisiin megiziwaash.**

> And so not a single thing was left for Soaring-Eagle to eat.

“Aaniish ge-diyaang?” (“what will become of us?”)

12) > **“Aaniish ge-diyaang?”**

> “What will become of us?”

<span class="l-gloss">Aanawi go baatayiinowa iwe anishinaabe (yet for all that, many were the people) imaa sa endanizid (there where he lived).</span>

13) > **Aanawi go baatayiinowa iwe anishinaabe imaa sa endanizid.**

> Yet, for all that, many were the people at the place where he was.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa gii-piboonini (then winter came on), mii sa zhigwa bakaded (and so then he went hungry).</span>

14) > **Zhigwa gii-piboonini, mii sa zhigwa bakaded.**

> In time the winter came, whereupon he then lacked food.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo, onaagoshininig (and once, one evening) aapiji wii-wiisini (he wanted very much to eat); gaye iniw wiiwan (and so too his wife) gaye i’iw oniijaanisa’ niizhiwa’ (and his children, two of them).</span>

15) > **Ningoding igo, onaagoshininig aapiji wii-wiisini; gaye iniw wiiwan gaye i’iw oniijaanisa’ niizhiwa’.**

> Now, one evening he craved exceedingly for some food to eat; so too (did) his wife, and his children, two in number.

Goshkwaawaadiziwa’ (they were keeping still and quiet).

16) > **Goshkwaawaadiziwa’.**

> They were living quietly (there).

<span class="l-gloss">Awiya biidweweshinoon (someone came with the sound of footsteps) ezhi-biindigenid (and then came inside).</span>

17) > **Awiya biidweweshinoon ezhi-biindigenid.**

> They heard the footsteps of somebody approaching, who then came inside.

“Megiziwaash, gi-wiikomigoo.” (“Soaring-Eagle, you are invited to a feast.”)

18) > **“Megiziwaash, gi-wiikomigoo”**

> “Soaring-Eagle, you are invited to a feast.”

<span class="l-gloss">Odoonaagan gaa-odaapinang (taking up his bowl), ezhi-maajaad (he then set off).</span>

19) > **Odoonaagan gaa-odaapinang, ezhi-maajaad.**

> Taking up his bowl, he then departed.

<span class="l-gloss">Miziwe aano-piindige iniw wiigiwaaman (everywhere he entered the wigwams in vain), indawaa ezhi-giiwed (so then he went back home).</span>

20) > **Miziwe aano-piindige iniw wiigiwaaman, indawaa ezhi-giiwed.**

> In every wigwam he entered, but to no purpose, for he did not find the place (of the feast); accordingly, when into all the wigwams he had entered in vain, he then went back home.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-biindiged endaad (when he entered his home), oganoonigoon iniw wiiwan (he was addressed by his wife):</span>

21) > **Ezhi-biindiged endaad, oganoonigoon iniw wiiwan:**

> On entering into the place where he dwelt, he was addressed by his wife saying:

“Aaniin dash i’iw gii-wiikomigoowin?” (“and where is what you were invited to be fed?”)

22) > **“Aaniin dash i’iw gii-wiikomigoowin?”**

> “Where is the food you got when invited?”

“Gaawiin ningoji ni-mikanziin ji-wiikonding.” (“nowhere did I find where the feast was.”)

23) > **“Gaawiin ningoji ni-mikanziin ji-wiikonding.”**

> “Nowhere did I find the place of the feast”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa apii ezhi-gichi-mawinid iniw wiiwan gaye oniijaanisa’ (then it was that his wife and his children wept bitterly) indawaa ezhi-gawishimowaad (so then they lay down to sleep); weyaabaninig mii sa go gaawiin gegoo omiijisiinaawaa (when morning came, there was nothing at all for them to eat).</span>

24) > **Mii sa apii ezhi-gichi-mawinid iniw wiiwan gaye oniijaanisa’ indawaa ezhi-gawishimowaad; weyaabaninig mii sa go gaawiin gegoo omiijisiinaawaa.**

> Thereupon then bitterly wept his wife and his children. Accordingly went they to bed; in the morning there was nothing for them to eat.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa miinawaa dibikadini (then again it was night), mii dash geget wii-wiisiniwaad (whereupon truly they longed to eat).</span>

25) > **Zhigwa miinawaa dibikadini, mii dash geget wii-wiisiniwaad.**

> In time it was night again, whereupon truly did they yearn for food to eat.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa, ani-dibikadini (then, as it grew dark), baamaa go ba-dapaabinid awiya (of a sudden someone came and peeped in).</span>

26) > **Zhigwa, ani-dibikadini, baamaa go ba-dapaabinid awiya.**

> Now, it was beginning to grow dark, when of a sudden some one came up (and) peeped in.

“Megiziwaash gi-wiikomigoo.” (“Soaring-Eagle, you are invited to a feast.”)

27) > **“Megiziwaash gi-wiikomigoo.”**

> “Soaring-Eagle, you are invited to a feast.”

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-oditinang odoonaagan (seizing his bowl), zaagijikwaashkoni (he leaped out of doors), agaawaa go ogezakawaabamaan animibatoonid (scarcely did he catch sight of him running away).</span>

28) > **Gaa-oditinang odoonaagan, zaagijikwaashkoni, agaawaa go ogezakawaabamaan animibatoonid.**

> Seizing his bowl, out of doors he leaped, and scarcely did he catch sight of him who went running away.

Gichi-eyaapiisikaad omaajinizhawaan (as fast as he could go he chased after him).

29) > **Gichi-eyaapiisikaad omaajinizhawaan.**

> As fast as he could go he pursued after him.

<span class="l-gloss">Goniginiin, odani-gabikamii iniw wiigiwaaman (lo, the other sped on past the wigwams); ziibi giishkaabikaanig ani-biindigesewan (into the falls of a river the other ran in); mii go i’iw eni-danizid (and that was where the other dwelt).</span>

30) > **Goniginiin, odani-gabikamii iniw wiigiwaaman; ziibi giishkaabikaanig ani-biindigesewan; mii go i’iw eni-danizid.**

> Lo, the other sped past the wigwams; into the falls of a river the other ran, whereupon in he rushed.

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-piindigewaad (as they went on in), mooshkinebiwa’ waakondinid (the place was filled with guests).</span>

31) > **Eni-piindigewaad, mooshkinebiwa’ waakondinid.**

> As they went on in, (he found) the place filled up with guests.

Gichi-paapi’aa (he was much laughed at).

32) > **Gichi-paapi’aa.**

> He was made much fun of.

<span class="l-gloss">Namegosan waakondinid (it was trout that the feast was for), gichi-niibiwa go ashamaa (and with a great deal of it he was fed).</span>

33) > **Namegosan waakondinid, gichi-niibiwa go ashamaa.**

> (It was to eat) trout that the invitation was given, and with a great deal of it (Soaring-Eagle) was fed.

Geyaabi go ishkosewan iniw jiibaakwaanan (there still remained some of the cooked food).

34) > **Geyaabi go ishkosewan iniw jiibaakwaanan.**

> There yet remained some more of the food that had been cooked.

Zhigwa ganoonaa megiziwaash (then Soaring-Eagle was spoken to):

35) > **Zhigwa ganoonaa megiziwaash:**

> Presently Soaring-Eagle was spoken to:

“Mii maawiin i’iw geget ji-noondaapanishiyan. (“it may well be that you will indeed starve.)

36) > **“Mii maawiin i’iw geget ji-noondaapanishiyan.**

> “It is indeed quite possible that you may starve before the winter is over.

Indawaa giga-kikinoo’amaagoo ge-izhichigeyan. (therefore you will be taught what you are to do.)

37) > **Indawaa giga-kikinoo’amaagoo ge-izhichigeyan.**

> Therefore you will be taught what you shall do.

Waabang gi-mindimooye’imish da-biiminakwe. (tomorrow your old woman is to make twine.)

38) > **Waabang gi-mindimooye’imish da-biiminakwe.**

> To-morrow your old woman shall make some twine.

<span class="l-gloss">Gii-kiizhitood biiminakwaan (when she has finished the twine), mii dash i’iw ge-izhi-kichi-dwaa’igeyan (then you are to chop a big hole in the ice) imaa wabigamaag i’iw zaaga’igan. (there at the narrows of the lake.)</span>

39) > **Gii-kiizhitood biiminakwaan, mii dash i’iw ge-izhi-kichi-dwaa’igeyan imaa wabigamaag i’iw zaaga’igan.**

> After she has finished the twine, then you shall make a large hole in the ice over at yonder narrows of the lake.

Mii dash iw ozidaang giga-danapinaa a’aw gi-niijaanis. (and then by its feet you shall tie your child.)

40) > **Mii dash iw ozidaang giga-danapinaa a’aw gi-niijaanis.**

> Accordingly, then by its feet shall you tie your child with the cord.

<span class="l-gloss">Ga-boodaakwewaa (you shall let it down into the hole); mizhakiise dash mii iw ge-izhi-wiikobinad (and when it reaches bottom then you shall pull it back), ji-enigok ji-kijiwebinad. (with all your might shall you yank it.)</span>

41) > **Ga-boodaakwewaa; mizhakiise dash mii iw ge-izhi-wiikobinad, ji-enigok ji-kijiwebinad.**

> You shall put it down into the hole; and when it has reached the bottom then you shall draw it out, with all your power shall you pull on it.

Mii dash imaa ji-waabamad a’aw namegos. (and then there you shall see the trout.)

42) > **Mii dash imaa ji-waabamad a’aw namegos.**

> And then there shall you see the trout.

<span class="l-gloss">Giin igo giga-dibaabamaag (you yourself shall judge by sight) mii maawiin minik ge-debisewaad. (when it seems there are enough.)</span>

43) > **Giin igo giga-dibaabamaag mii maawiin minik ge-debisewaad.**

> And you yourself shall see when you think that (the fishes) are enough.

ge-apiichi-biboong mii iw ge-izhi-shkwaataayan. (at the end of winter then you shall stop.)

44) > **ge-apiichi-biboong mii iw ge-izhi-shkwaataayan.**

> At the end of the winter then shall you cease.

Mii sa go naa iw ezhi-shawenimigooyan. (and this is how you are being blessed.)

45) > **Mii sa go naa iw ezhi-shawenimigooyan.**

> And this is the way that you shall be blessed.

Mii iw megiziwaash, izhi-giiwen. (so then, Soaring-Eagle, go on home.)

46) > **Mii iw megiziwaash, izhi-giiwen.**

> Therefore, Soaring-Eagle, do you return home.

<span class="l-gloss">Maagizhaa aabiding giga-wiisinim (perhaps for once you all will have enough to eat), mii iw izhi-giiwen. (so then go on home.)</span>

47) > **Maagizhaa aabiding giga-wiisinim, mii iw izhi-giiwen.**

> Perhaps for once you (and your family) will have food (enough) to eat, therefore do you go on back home.

Giiwewish namegos.” (carry some trout back home.”)

48) > **Giiwewish namegos.”**

> Take back some trout.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw ezhi-piindiged iw endaad (and so when he entered his home), geget moojigiziwan iniw wiiwan gaye oniijaanisa’ (truly glad were his wife and his children); ji-enigok wiisiniwa’ (with great eagerness they ate).</span>

49) > **Mii dash i’iw ezhi-piindiged iw endaad, geget moojigiziwan iniw wiiwan gaye oniijaanisa’; ji-enigok wiisiniwa’.**

> And so when he entered his home, truly pleased were his wife and his children; with great eagerness did they eat.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa go iw zhigwa maajitaad aw mindimooye (and so then the old woman set to work) biiminakwed gabe-dibik (making twine all night long); weyaabaninig ogiizhitoon i’iw obiiminakwaan (when morning came she finished her twine).</span>

50) > **Mii sa go iw zhigwa maajitaad aw mindimooye biiminakwed gabe-dibik; weyaabaninig ogiizhitoon i’iw obiiminakwaan.**

> Thereupon then did the old woman set to work weaving twine all night long; when it was morning, she finished the twine.

<span class="l-gloss">Gigizheb ezhi-maajaad megisiwaash (in the morning Soaring-Eagle set out); wiiwan wiijiiwaad zaaga’iganing izhaad (going with his wife to the lake).</span>

51) > **Gigizheb ezhi-maajaad megisiwaash; wiiwan wiijiiwaad zaaga’iganing izhaad.**

> In the morning then departed Soaring-Eagle; along with his wife, he went on his way to the lake.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-tagwishing wabigamaang i’iw zaaga’igan (when he reached the narrows of the lake) ezhi-dwaa’iged (he chopped a hole in the ice).</span>

52) > **Gaa-tagwishing wabigamaang i’iw zaaga’igan ezhi-dwaa’iged.**

> When he got to the narrows of the lake, then he made a hole in the ice.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-kiizhitood odwaa’igan (after finishing his ice-hole), odakobinaan iniw oniijaanisan (he bound his child), ozidaaning danapinaad (binding it by its feet).</span>

53) > **Gaa-kiizhitood odwaa’igan, odakobinaan iniw oniijaanisan, ozidaaning danapinaad.**

> After finishing the hole in the ice, he then bound one of his children; by its feet was where he bound it.

Mii dash i’iw ezhi-boodaakwe’owaad (then they let it down into the hole).

54) > **Mii dash i’iw ezhi-boodaakwe’owaad.**

> Thereupon they put it down into the hole.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa mizhakiisewan (when it reached the bottom), enigok ezhi-wiikobinaad (with all his might he pulled it back).</span>

55) > **Zhigwa mizhakiisewan, enigok ezhi-wiikobinaad.**

> When it got to the bottom, then with all his might he drew it out.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-kidishkobinaad (after he had pulled it out), namegosan bigijiskosewan (the trout came out of the ice).</span>

56) > **Gaa-kidishkobinaad, namegosan bigijiskosewan.**

> After he had pulled it out, then the trout came out of the ice.

Megiziwaash geget minwendam (Soaring-Eagle was truly pleased).

57) > **Megiziwaash geget minwendam.**

> Soaring-Eagle was really happy.

“Gaawin nindayaanashiitanzii gabe-giizhig.” (“I will not stop the whole day long.”)

58) > **“Gaawin nindayaanashiitanzii gabe-giizhig.”**

> “I will not stop throughout the whole day.”

Miinawaa ezhi-boodaakwewaad iniw oniijaanisan (again he let another of his children down into the hole).

59) > **Miinawaa ezhi-boodaakwewaad iniw oniijaanisan.**

> Then another of his children he put down through the hole.

Zhigwa mizhakiisewan ji-enigok owiikobinaan (when it reached bottom, with all his power he pulled on it).

60) > **Zhigwa mizhakiisewan ji-enigok owiikobinaan.**

> When it got to the bottom, with all his power he pulled upon it.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-gichiwebinaad (when he gave it a great throw), namegosan owaabamaan (he saw a trout).</span>

61) > **Ezhi-gichiwebinaad, namegosan owaabamaan.**

> When he gave it a great throw, a trout he saw.

Mii dash geget minwendang aw megiziwaash (and then truly pleased was Soaring-Eagle).

62) > **Mii dash geget minwendang aw megiziwaash.**

> And then truly pleased was Soaring-Eagle.

Zhigwa onaagoshinini (then evening was coming on).

63) > **Zhigwa onaagoshinini.**

> In a while the evening was drawing in.

“Ambe sa noo, mii dash eta iw ge-nisag a’aw namegos.” (“come now, just this one more trout will I kill.”)

64) > **“Ambe sa noo, mii dash eta iw ge-nisag a’aw namegos.”**

> “Behold, just one more trout I will kill.”

Geget niibiwa (truly there were many).

65) > **Geget niibiwa.**

> To be sure, (there were) many trout.

“Mii maawiin i’iw minik ge-debisewaad gayapiichi-biboong. (“it may be there are now enough to last through the winter.)

66) > **“Mii maawiin i’iw minik ge-debisewaad gayapiichi-biboong.**

> “It may be that they are now enough to last through the winter.

Aw mii dash eta iw bezhig ji-nisag.” (so just this one more will I kill.”)

67) > **Aw mii dash eta iw bezhig ji-nisag.”**

> Therefore only one more will I kill.”

Oboodaakwewaan iniw oniijaanisan (he let one of his children down into the hole).

68) > **Oboodaakwewaan iniw oniijaanisan.**

> Down into the hole he put one of his children.

Zhigwa mizhakiisewan, owiikobidoon (when it reached bottom, he drew it back).

69) > **Zhigwa mizhakiisewan, owiikobidoon.**

> As soon as it got to the bottom, he drew it back.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-bwaawipodood ayangwajizh (as he was losing his pull on it the longer he held), ji-enigok odaanawiikobidoon (with all his might he tried in vain to draw it back).</span>

70) > **Ezhi-bwaawipodood ayangwajizh, ji-enigok odaanawiikobidoon.**

> As he was losing his pull on it the longer (he held on), then with all his power he tried drawing it back.

<span class="l-gloss">Gegapii ezhi-pakibidood (at last he broke the line), mii sa pane oniijaanisan (and so then gone was his child).</span>

71) > **Gegapii ezhi-pakibidood, mii sa pane oniijaanisan.**

> At last he broke the line, and then gone was his child. ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-soaring-eagle-footnotes.html))

Ji-enigok mawi megiziwaash, gaye owiiwan (bitterly wept Soaring-Eagle, and his wife too).

72) > **Ji-enigok mawi megiziwaash, gaye owiiwan.**

> Very bitterly wept Soaring-Eagle, and also his wife.

<span class="l-gloss">Indawaa, gaa-ishkwaa-mawid (so then, when he had finished crying), ogiigoo’imiwa’ ogiiwewinaawaa gakina endaawaad (all their fish they carried back home to where they lived).</span>

73) > **Indawaa, gaa-ishkwaa-mawid, ogiigoo’imiwa’ ogiiwewinaawaa gakina endaawaad.**

> Accordingly, when he had ceased crying, they carried all their fish back to where they lived.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhiwinaawaad i’iw giigoonya’ (after they had brought the fish), maajaa awi-gagwedwed (he set off to go ask about it); mii dash izhaad iniw nigigwan (and so he went to the Otter).</span>

74) > **Gaa-izhiwinaawaad i’iw giigoonya’, maajaa awi-gagwedwed; mii dash izhaad iniw nigigwan.**

> After they had dressed the fish, he departed thence to make inquiry; and so he went to where the Otter was.

Zhigwa oganoonaan (then he spoke to him):

75) > **Zhigwa oganoonaan:**

> In time he spoke to him, saying:

“Mii sa iw gii-nisangid a’aw ni-niijaanisinaan.” (“and so now we have killed our child.”)

76) > **“Mii sa iw gii-nisangid a’aw ni-niijaanisinaan.”**

> “Therefore now have we slain our child.”

Ezhi-ganoonind megiziwaash (then Soaring-Eagle was addressed):

77) > **Ezhi-ganoonind megiziwaash:**

> Then was Soaring-Eagle addressed by him saying:

Ambe sa, nawaj niibiwa ogawa-zhitoon gi-mindimoowemizh biiminakwaan. (“come now, let your old woman make more twine.)

78) > **Ambe sa, nawaj niibiwa ogawa-zhitoon gi-mindimoowemizh biiminakwaan.**

> “Behond, let your old woman make some more cord.

Gii-kiizhitood, mii dash i’iw ge-izhi-maajaayan, giga-izhaa. (when she has finished it, then you shall set off, you shall go.)

79) > **Gii-kiizhitood, mii dash i’iw ge-izhi-maajaayan, giga-izhaa.**

> When she has finished it, then shall you depart, you shall go to the place where you broke the line (that held) your child.

Megiziwaash, giga-izhaa imaa gii-pakibinad aw gi-niijaanis? (Soaring-Eagle, do you know why your child was broken away from you? — \*literal reading of giga-izhaa here uncertain against Jones’s English question; rendered per the corpus\*)

80) > **Megiziwaash, giga-izhaa imaa gii-pakibinad aw gi-niijaanis?**

> Soaring-Eagle, do you know why your child was taken from you?

Ozaam niibiwa gigii-nisaag igiw giigoonyag. (too many of those fish you have killed.)

81) > **Ozaam niibiwa gigii-nisaag igiw giigoonyag.**

> Too many of the fishes have you slain.

Gii-nishki’aa a’aw mishinamegwe. (you have angered the Great Sturgeon.)

82) > **Gii-nishki’aa a’aw mishinamegwe.**
