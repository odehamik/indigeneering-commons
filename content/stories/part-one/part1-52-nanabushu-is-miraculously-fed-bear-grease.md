<span class="l-gloss">## **(52) Nanabushu is Miraculously fed Bear-Grease ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-is-miraculously-fed-bear-grease-footnotes.html))**</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding isa (now, once upon a time) ani-babimosegoban Nenaboozhoo (along Nänabushu went walking), mii dash apii (and it was then) wedisaad ajidamoon daanid (that he came upon the squirrel where he dwelt); gii-piindige dash (and he went inside) endaanid (where (the squirrel) lived).</span>

1)  > **Ningoding isa ani-pabimosegoban Nenaboozhoo, mii dash apii wedisaad ajidamoon daanid; gii-piindige dash endaanid.**

> Now, once upon a time on his way went Nänabushu walking, and it was then that he came where a squirrel lived; and he went into where (the squirrel) lived.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash enendang ajidamoo (now, this is what the squirrel thought): “Ningad-ashamaa Nenaboozhoo (I will feed Nänabushu).”</span>

2)  > **Mii dash enendang ajidamoo: “Ningad-ashamaa Nenaboozhoo.”**

> Now, this thought the squirrel: “I will feed Nänabushu.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash baate-wiiyaas (and so some dried meat) gii-atood onaaganing (when he had placed it into a bowl), miinawaa dash bezhig onaagaans (and next a small dish) ogii-mamoon wii-atood bimide (he took, to put some grease in it).</span>

3)  > **Mii dash baate-wiiyaas gii-atood onaaganing, miinawaa dash bezhig onaagaans ogii-mamoon wii-atood bimide.**

> And so when some dry meat he had placed into a bowl, he next took a small vessel in which to put some fat.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-adaapinaad odisiniiman (accordingly, when he took hold of his stone), mii dash mookomaan (then with a knife) ezhi-pazhiba’odizod (he pierced himself).</span>

4)  > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-adaapinaad odisiniiman, mii dash mookomaan ezhi-pazhiba’odizod.**

> Accordingly, taking hold of his stone ([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-is-miraculously-fed-bear-grease-footnotes.html)), he then pierced himself (there) with a knife.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash imaa bimide (whereupon from there grease) wenjijiwaninig (flowed out), bekish noondaagozi (at the same time he was heard sounding): “Jii ji ji jii ji jii, mako-bimide\!” (“Jii ji ji jii ji jii, bear-grease\!”)</span>

5)  > **Mii dash imaa bimide wenjijiwaninig, bekish noondaagozi: “Jii ji ji jii ji jii, mako-bimide\!”**

> Whereupon from thence flowed some grease, at the same time he was heard saying: “Tci tci tci tci tci tci, bear-grease\!”

Mii dash gii-ashamaad Nenaboozhoon (thereupon he fed Nänabushu).

6)  > **Mii dash gii-ashamaad Nenaboozhoon.**

> Thereupon he fed Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-ishkwaa-wiisinid Nenaboozhoo (after Nänabushu had finished eating), “Gaye niin ningad-ashamaa ajidamoo (I too will feed the squirrel),” inendam (he thought).</span>

7)  > **Gaa-ishkwaa-wiisinid Nenaboozhoo, “Gaye niin ningad-ashamaa ajidamoo,” inendam.**

> After Nänabushu had finished eating, “So will I too feed the squirrel,” he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash mookomaan odoopinang, \[daapinang?\] (thereupon he took up a knife), mii dash ezhi-odaapinaad odasiniiman (and then he took hold of his stone).</span>

8)  > **Mii dash mookomaan odoopinang, \[daapinang?\] mii dash ezhi-odaapinaad odasiniiman.**

> Thereupon taking a knife, he reached hold of his stone.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-pazhibawaad odasiniiman (and so when he had pierced his stone), mii iw gii-nisidizod (he accordingly killed himself).</span>

9)  > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-pazhibawaad odasiniiman, mii iw gii-nisidizod.**

> And so when he had pierced his stone, he accordingly killed himself. ([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-is-miraculously-fed-bear-grease-footnotes.html))

<span class="l-gloss">Ajidamoo dash ogii-paapoodaanaan Nenaboozhoon (now, the squirrel breathed upon Nänabushu), mii iw gii-pimaaji’aad (whereupon he brought him back to life).</span>

10) > **Ajidamoo dash ogii-paapoodaanaan Nenaboozhoon, mii iw gii-pimaaji’aad.**

> Now, the squirrel breathed upon Nänabushu, whereupon he fetched him back to life.

**Footnotes :**

<span class="l-gloss">(1) For other versions see Nos. 36 (p. 311) and 40 (p. 341).</span>

(2) A synonym for “testes”.

(3) It is common with the Ojibwas of Canada to have Nänabushu die and then be fetched back to life, as here; the same, too, with his grandmother. This element is rather out of keeping with the tales of other Ojibwas.
