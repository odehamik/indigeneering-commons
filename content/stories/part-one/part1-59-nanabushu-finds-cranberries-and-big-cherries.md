**(59) Nanabushu finds Cranberries and Big Cherries**

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget (and so truly) gaa-izhi-gondang ozikwaagan mindimooye (after the old woman had swallowed her spittle), mii dash miinawaa giiwenh (then again, it is told) Nenaboozhoo ani-babimibatood enigok (Nanabushu went running along at full speed); wiinge zhayiigwa aapiji ayekozi (utterly worn out he now was), mii go gaa-izhi-bimibatood (for he had been running) giizhigadinig gaye dibikadinig (by day and by night).</span>

1)  > **Mii dash geget mindimooye gaa-izhi-gondang ozikwaagan, mii dash miinawaa giiwenh Nenaboozhoo ani-babimibatood enigok; wiinge zhayiigwa aapiji ayekozi, mii go gaa-izhi-bimibatood giizhigadinig gaye dibikadinig.**

> And so truly, after the old woman had swallowed her spittle, then again they say that Nanabushu went running along at the top of his speed; ever so tired had he now become, for he had been running day and night.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding idash igo (and once) ani-babimibatood Nenaboozhoo (while Nanabushu was running along) dibikadinigoban (it being night), mii dash giiwenh e-nendang (whereupon, it is told, he thought): “Mii maawiin zhayiigwa (“it must be that by now) waasa dagwishinoowaanen,” (I have come a long way,”) inendam (he thought).</span>

2)  > **Ningoding idash igo ani-babimibatood Nenaboozhoo dibikadinigoban, mii dash giiwenh e-nendang: “Mii maawiin zhayiigwa waasa dagwishinoowaanen,” inendam.**

> And once while Nanabushu was running along, it happened to be in the night, whereupon it is said that he thought : "It is plain that I must now have come a long way," he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Nawaj idash igo aniigakipichibatoo (slower then he ran); megwaa dash babimosed (and while he was walking along), gegoo odakokaadaanan (something he stepped upon with his foot), dibishkoo asiniinsag (it seemed like little stones).</span>

3)  > **Nawaj idash igo aniigakipichibatoo; megwaa dash babimosed, gegoo odakokaadaanan, dibishkoo asiniinsag.**

> Slower then he ran; and while he was walking along, something he then stepped upon, it seemed like pebbles.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash animaanag (and so, puzzled by them) ezhi-andoojiibidood (he felt about for them by hand); mii dash ezhi-mikoojiinang (and as he found them by touch), odaapinang (he took them up).</span>

4)  > **Mii dash animaanag ezhi-andoojiibidood; mii dash ezhi-mikoojiinang, odaapinang.**

> And so, not making out what they were, he felt of them; and as he felt of them, he took them up.

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookomis (“my grandmother), naske gosha owe\! (just look at this\!)</span>

5)  > **“Nookomis, naske gosha owe\!**

> "O my grandmother, do look at these\!

<span class="l-gloss">“Wegonen owe?” (“what is this?”) odinaan (he said to her).</span>

6)  > **“Wegonen owe?” odinaan.**

> What are these things?" he said to her.

Mii dash mindimooye odaapinang (and then the old woman took them up).

7)  > **Mii dash mindimooye odaapinang.**

> And then the old woman took them up.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mashkiigimin iw,” odinaan (“cranberries those,” she said to him); “onizhishinoon miijinaniwang,” odinaan (“they are good when one eats them,” she said to him).</span>

8)  > **“Mashkiigimin iw,” odinaan; “onizhishinoon miijinaniwang,” odinaan.**

> "Cranberries these," she said to him. "Good are they to eat," she said to him.

Aapiji mamaangiminagadiniwan (very large in their berry-size they were).

9)  > **Aapiji mamaangiminagadiniwan.**

> Very large were they in size.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash miinawaa maajaawaad (thereupon again they set out), ani-babimosed miinawaa Nenaboozhoo (on his way again Nanabushu went walking).</span>

10) > **Mii dash miinawaa maajaawaad, ani-babimosed miinawaa Nenaboozhoo.**

> Thereupon again they started, on his way again went Nanabushu walking.

<span class="l-gloss">Ani-daatagikwenid (as he went along with head tilted up), gegoo obi-taakoskaanan oshkiizhigong (something brushed against him on the eye/face); mii dash ezhi-andoojiibijiged (and as he felt about for them), mii nangwana miinan (they turned out to be berries).</span>

11) > **Ani-daatagikwenid, gegoo obi-taakoskaanan oshkiizhigong; mii dash ezhi-andoojiibijiged, mii nangwana miinan.**

> As he went along with his head lifted up, something touched him on the face ; and as he felt of them, they turned out to be berries.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-odaapinang (and as he picked them), ezhi-waabanda’aad ookomisan (showing them to his grandmother), inaad: “Wegonen owe?” (saying: “what is this?”) odinaan ookomisan waabanda’aad (he said to his grandmother as he showed her).</span>

12) > **Mii dash ezhi-odaapinang, ezhi-waabanda’aad ookomisan, inaad: “Wegonen owe?” odinaan ookomisan waabanda’aad.**

> And as he picked them, he showed them to his grandmother, saying to her: "What are these things?" he said to his grand mother as he showed them to her.

“Oow, gichi-asasaweminan,” odinaan (“why, big cherries,” she said to him).

13) > **“Oow, gichi-asasaweminan,” odinaan.**

> "Why, big cherries," she said to him.

“Onizhishinoon miijigaadeg,” odigoon ookomisan (“they are good when eaten,” he was told by his grandmother).

14) > **“Onizhishinoon miijigaadeg,” odigoon ookomisan.**

> "Good are they to eat," he was told by his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii zhayiigwa ji-ani-andawaabandaman (“now you shall go and seek out) geda-daayang,” odigoon (where we are to dwell,” he was told).</span>

15) > **“Mii zhayiigwa ji-ani-andawaabandaman geda-daayang,” odigoon.**

> "Soon shall you now look for a place where we are to dwell," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget Nenaboozhoo (and then truly Nanabushu) gii-ani-pagijiwanaanaad ookomisan (let down his pack-burden, his grandmother, from his back).</span>

16) > **Mii dash geget Nenaboozhoo gii-ani-pagijiwanaanaad ookomisan.**

> And then truly did Nanabushu put his grandmother down from off his back.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gii-ozhiged Nenaboozhoo (thereupon Nanabushu made camp) megwe-nengawiminikaang (in among the sand-berry patch), aaniindi niibiwa gegoo nitaawiging maaniwang (where many sorts of things were growing in abundance).</span>

17) > **Mii dash gii-ozhiged Nenaboozhoo megwe-nengawiminikaang, aaniindi niibiwa gegoo nitaawiging maaniwang.**

> Thereupon did Nanabushu make a camp in among the sand-berries, in where there was an abundance of various kinds of berries growing in the ground.

Mii imaa gii-onaabandang ji-daawaad (and there he chose the place for them to dwell).

18) > **Mii imaa gii-onaabandang ji-daawaad.**

> And there he chose a place for them to live.

Mii dash imaa gaa-daawaad (so there was where they came to dwell).

19) > **Mii dash imaa gaa-daawaad.**

> So there was where they continued.
