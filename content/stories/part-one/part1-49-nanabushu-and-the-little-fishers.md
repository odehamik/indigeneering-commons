(49) Nanabushu and the Little Fishers

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding idash (and once) ani-babimosed (as he was walking along), ogii-mikawaa’ (he found them) ojiigensa’ (the little fishers) e-yaanid (who were there).</span>

1)  > **Ningoding idash ani-pabimosed, ogii-mikawaa’ ojiigensa’ e-yaanid.**

> And once, when travelling along, he came upon some young Fishers.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash e-naad (and so this he said to them): “Aaniindi (where is) gigawaa? (your mother?)”</span>

2)  > **Mii dash e-naad: “Aaniindi gigawaa?”**

> And this he said to them : " Where is your mother?"

“Babaanandawenjige. (She is off somewhere hunting for food.)”

3)  > **“Babaanandawenjige.”**

> "She is off somewhere hunting for game."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii iw dash (and so then) gaa-izhi-poopookojiibinaad (when he had broken them in two at the wrist), ogii-miijinaa’ (he ate them).</span>

4)  > **Mii iw dash gaa-izhi-poopookojiibinaad, ogii-miijinaa’.**

> And when he had broken them in two at the wrist, he eased upon them.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and so) gii-ani-maajaad (then he went off on his way).</span>

5)  > **Mii dash gii-ani-maajaad.**

> And then on his way he went.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii dash degoshing (and when she came home) ojiig (the Fisher) ogii-mikawaa’ (she found them) oniijaanesa’ (her children) nibonid (being dead).</span>

6)  > **Apii dash degoshing ojiig ogii-mikawaa’ oniijaanesa’ nibonid.**

> And when home was come the Fisher, she found that her children were dead.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and so) ezhi-noopinanaad (then she pursued him) Nenaboozhoon (Nanabushu).</span>

7)  > **Mii dash ezhi-noopinanaad Nenaboozhoon.**

> Thereupon she pursued Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii dash edimind (and when he was overtaken) Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu), ani-nagamo (he was going along singing) Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu):</span>

8)  > **Apii dash edimind Nenaboozhoo, ani-nagamo Nenaboozhoo:**

> And when Nanabushu was overtaken, he was going along singing a song :

<span class="l-gloss">“Ojiigensag (little fishers) inaa (indeed) gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa (are the ones I broke in two at the wrists).</span>

9)  > **“Ojiigensag inaa gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa.**

> "Little fishers are the ones that I have broken in two at the wrists.

<span class="l-gloss">Ojiigensag (little fishers) inaa (indeed) gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa (are the ones I broke in two at the wrists).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ojiigensag (little fishers) iinaa (indeed) gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa (are the ones I broke in two at the wrists).</span>

10) > **Ojiigensag inaa gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa.**

> Little fishers are the ones that I have broken in two at the wrists.

<span class="l-gloss">Ojiigensag (little fishers) iinaa (indeed) gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa (are the ones I broke in two at the wrists).”</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ojiigan idash (and the Fisher) oganoonigoon (addressed him): “Giin maawiin (then it was surely you), gigii-nisaag (you killed them) ni-niijaanisag\! (my children\!)”</span>

11) > **Ojiigensag iinaa gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa.**

> Little fishers are the ones that I have broken in two at the wrists.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin\! (No\!)” ikido (says) Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu).</span>

12) > **Ojiigensag iinaa gaa-poopookojiibinagwaa.**

> Little fishers are the ones that I have broken in two at the wrists."

<span class="l-gloss">Apii dash edimind (and when he was overtaken), mitigoong (onto a log) gii-apagizo (he threw himself). (1)</span>

13) > **Ojiigan idash oganoonigoon: “Giin maawiin, gigii-nisaag ni-niijaanisag\!”**

> By the Fisher was he addressed: "Then it was you, you slew my children \!"

<span class="l-gloss">Ojiig idash (and the Fisher) ogii-miigaadaan (fought it) i’iw mitig (that log).</span>

14) > **“Gaawiin\!” ikido Nenaboozhoo.**

> "No\!" said Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget (and so truly) Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu) wiisagendang (felt pain) odiyaang (in his bottom).</span>

15) > **Apii dash edimind, mitigoong gii-apagizo. (**[<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-little-fishers-footnotes.html)**)**

> And when he was overtaken, he turned instantly into a log. ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-little-fishers-footnotes.html))

16) > **Ojiig idash ogii-miigaadaan i’iw mitig.**

> And the Fisher fought the log, ever so much did she tear the log with her teeth.

17) > **Mii dash geget Nenaboozhoo wiisagendang odiyaang.**

> Thereupon truly did Nanabushu suffer pain in his bottom.

**Footnotes :**

(1) Mitigong gii’aapagizo, “he turned instantly into a log;” literally, “into or on to a log he threw himself,” but the sense is as given in the translation.
