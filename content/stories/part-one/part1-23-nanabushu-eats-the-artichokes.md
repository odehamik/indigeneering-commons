(23) Nenabosho eats the Artichokes

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo babimooded (and once, as he was crawling about) owaabandaan gegoo zaagakiinig (he saw something growing out of the ground), gomaa omoona’aan (so he dug it up a little); gegoo ezhi-mikwaanikaadang (something he found on digging it up), go maa go akwaani (a certain length it extended).</span>

1)  > **Ningoding igo babimooded owaabandaan gegoo zaagakiinig, gomaa omoona’aan; gegoo ezhi-mikwaanikaadang, go maa go akwaani.**

> And once, when he was crawling about, he saw something that grew out of the ground, accordingly he dug it up; something he then found on digging it up, a certain length it extended.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw ezhi-ganoodang (whereupon he spoke to it): “Gaawiin ina giitamagoosiin? (“are you not eaten for food? — \*passive stem flagged\*) Giinwiizom ina? (have you a name?) Aaniin iw ezhinikaazoyeg?” (what are you called?”)</span>

2)  > **Mii dash i’iw ezhi-ganoodang: “Gaawiin ina giitamagoosiin? Giinwiizom ina? Aaniin iw ezhinikaazoyeg?”**

> Whereupon he then spoke to it, saying: “Are you not eaten for food? Have you a name? What are you called?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Askibwaa (Ashkibwaa) nindigoomin.” (“Artichoke we are called.”)</span>

3)  > **“Askibwaa (Ashkibwaa) nindigoomin.”**

> “Artichoke we are called.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniin enikaakeyeg (“what effect do you produce — \*stem flagged\*) giishpin niibiwa emokooyegon? (if much of you be eaten?) Gegoo na gidinikaagen?” (do you cause some bodily ill?”)</span>

4)  > **“Aaniin enikaakeyeg giishpin niibiwa emokooyegon? Gegoo na gidinikaagen?”**

> “What physical effect do you produce if much of you be eaten? Do you cause some sort of bodily ill?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin sa go (“not at all); ningiizhiwashkade’iwemin (we cause a pain in the stomach), gaye dash nimpoogisikaagemin (and we cause wind). Aaniish mii sa go iw enikaageyaang.” (now, that is the effect we produce.”)</span>

5)  > **“Gaawiin sa go; ningiizhiwashkade’iwemin, gaye dash nimpoogisikaagemin. Aaniish mii sa go iw enikaageyaang.”**

> “Not at all; we produce a pain in the stomach, and we cause wind. Now, that is the bodily effect we produce.”

<span class="l-gloss">Nenabosho omaa ezhi-maajitaad (Nenabosho here set to work), niibiwa omoonawaa i’iwe ashkibwaa (he dug up many of those artichokes); ezhi-giziibiiginaad (when he washed them clean with his hands), ezhi-maadanjige (he began eating), wenjida go de-wiisini (until he ate quite to his fill).</span>

6)  > **Nenabosho omaa ezhi-maajitaad, niibiwa omoonawaa i’iwe ashkibwaa; ezhi-giziibiiginaad, ezhi-maadanjige, wenjida go de-wiisini.**

> Nenabosho then here set to work, he gathered many artichokes; when he wiped them clean with his hands, then he began eating them, till he was quite satisfied he ate.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji niibiwa gaagidamwaad (when he had eaten a great many of them) goji-bazigwii (he tried to rise to his feet).</span>

7)  > **Aapiji niibiwa gaagidamwaad goji-bazigwii.**

> When he had eaten a bountiful supply of them, he tried to rise to his feet.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin ganage minaan izhi-ayaasii (not in the least did he feel unwell — \*minaan flagged\*), geget minomaji’oo (truly well did he feel). \*\*</span>

8)  > **Gaawiin ganage minaan izhi-ayaasii, geget minomaji’oo.**

> He was feeling very well, truly very well was he feeling.\*\*

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-maajaad (when he started off), geget minobimose (truly he walked along easily).</span>

9)  > **Ezhi-maajaad, geget minobimose.**

> When he started to go, really he went walking easily.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaa go apii degwishing (after he had come a certain distance) geget giizhiwashkade (truly his stomach ached).</span>

10) > **Gomaa go apii degwishing geget giizhiwashkade.**

> After he was come a certain distance, truly his stomach ached.

Zhigwa sa aapiji giizhiwashkade (presently his stomach ached very much).

11) > **Zhigwa sa aapiji giizhiwashkade.**

> Presently he had a very painful ache in the stomach.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo megwaa bimosed (then by and by, while he was walking along) ezhi-poogidid (he broke wind).</span>

12) > **Ningoding igo megwaa bimosed ezhi-poogidid.**

> Then by and by, while walking along he broke wind.

<span class="l-gloss">Geget goshkopagizo (truly he was startled); ezhi-kwekipagizod (then he whirled about).</span>

13) > **Geget goshkopagizo; ezhi-kwekipagizod.**

> Truly was he startled with surprise; then he whirled about.

“Awenen genoozhid?” (“who spoke to me?”)

14) > **“Awenen genoozhid?”**

> “Who spoke to me?”

Aazha miinawaa iwidi obikwannaang (again the same thing, there behind him).

15) > **Aazha miinawaa iwidi obikwannaang.**

> The same thing over again (he heard) there behind him.

Gegapii omitigwaabiin odikwadewaan (finally he strung his bow — \*odikwadewaan flagged\*).

16) > **Gegapii omitigwaabiin odikwadewaan.**

> Finally he strung his bow.

“Aaw, ganoozhishiyog.” (“now, do you speak to me.”)

17) > **“Aaw, ganoozhishiyog.”**

> “Now, do you speak to me.”

<span class="l-gloss">Megwaa go wiipimojiged (and as he was about to shoot), obikwanaang aazha miinawaa, “Poo\!” (behind him once again, “poo\!”)</span>

18) > **Megwaa go wiipimojiged, obikwanaang aazha miinawaa, “Poo\!”**

> And as he was going to shoot, then behind him once again, “poo\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Iwidi obikwannaang (over there at his back) Nenabosho gaawiin gegoo owaabandaziin (Nenabosho saw not a thing).</span>

19) > **Iwidi obikwannaang Nenabosho gaawiin gegoo owaabandaziin.**

> Over there at his back Nenabosho saw not a thing.

“Wegonen dash iw nwaandamaan?” (“and what is this I hear?”)

20) > **“Wegonen dash iw nwaandamaan?”**

> “And what do I hear?”

<span class="l-gloss">Aazha go miinawaa (again the same thing), mii sa go bezhigwanong enitang (and it was the same sound that he heard).</span>

21) > **Aazha go miinawaa, mii sa go bezhigwanong enitang.**

> And the same thing over again (he heard), and it was the same sound that he heard.

<span class="l-gloss">Gegapii maajiibatoo (finally he started running), mii go iw ezhi-kaade-bagizod (and so at every step he took), “Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh” inweni (“Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh” was the sound made).</span>

22) > **Gegapii maajiibatoo, mii go iw ezhi-kaade-bagizod, “Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh” inweni.**

> Finally he started running, whereupon at every step he made, “Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh”, was the sound made.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo bimipatood (and once, as he was running along) gegapii noogibatoo (at last he came to a halt), gibijise (he stopped short).</span>

23) > **Ningoding igo bimipatood gegapii noogibatoo, gibijise.**

> And once, as he was running along, he came to a sudden halt, he ceased running.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenabosho ezhi-giigidod (Nenabosho then said): “Aaw, miigaazhishiyog\!” (“now, do you fight me\!”)</span>

24) > **Nenabosho ezhi-giigidod: “Aaw, miigaazhishiyog\!”**

> Nenabosho then said: “Now, do you fight me\!”

Aaniish gaawiin gegoo owaabandaziin (of course, not a thing did he see).

25) > **Aaniish gaawiin gegoo owaabandaziin.**

> Of course, not a thing did he see.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo miinawaa (so another time again) mii dash gegt enigok ezhi-maajiibatood (when he really set off running with all his speed — \*gegt printed; read geget\*), mii sa onjida (the same thing happened on purpose), “Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh”, inwenig (“Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh” was the sound made).</span>

26) > **Ningoding igo miinawaa mii dash gegt enigok ezhi-maajiibatood, mii sa onjida, “Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh”, inwenig.**

> So another time, when he really started to run with all his speed, the same thing happened as before, “Ponh, ponh, ponh, ponh”, was the sound that was made.

Nenabosho gibijise (Nenabosho stopped short).

27) > **Nenabosho gibijise.**

> Nenabosho stopped in his career.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaagibijised (when he had come to a stop), mikwendaan i’iw gii-igod iiniw ashkibwaan (he thought of what he had been told by that Artichoke).</span>

28) > **Gaagibijised, mikwendaan i’iw gii-igod iiniw ashkibwaan.**

> When running he had come to a stop, he thought of what he had been told by the Artichoke.

“Maagizhaa nanda nimpoogid.” (“perhaps it is that I am breaking wind.”)

29) > **“Maagizhaa nanda nimpoogid.”**

> “Perhaps I am breaking wind.”

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-ezhi-maajaad (then he started on his way); eni-noomageshkaad (when he had gone a little ways) miinawaa boogidi (again he broke wind).</span>

30) > **Eni-ezhi-maajaad; eni-noomageshkaad miinawaa boogidi.**

> Then he started on his way; when he was come a little ways, again he broke wind.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ii\!, nimpoogid\! (“hallo\! I am breaking wind\!) Mii sa ge-izhi-nikaadang bijiinag ge-ani-bimaadizid (and this is what the one who is yet to live will call it), ‘nimpoogid,’ da-ikido. (‘I am breaking wind,’ he will say.)</span>

31) > **“Ii\!, nimpoogid\! Mii sa ge-izhi-nikaadang bijiinag ge-ani-bimaadizid, ‘nimpoogid,’ da-ikido.**

> “Hallo\! I am breaking wind\! And this is what he who will live in the future shall call it, ‘I am breaking wind,’ he will say.

<span class="l-gloss">Bebezhig da-poogisikaagewan (one thing and another will make one windy), ashkibwaa gakina go gegoo (the artichoke and all sorts of things), gaye opiniig gaye waakonag.” (and potatoes and lichens.”)</span>

32) > **Bebezhig da-poogisikaagewan, ashkibwaa gakina go gegoo, gaye opiniig gaye waakonag.”**

> Various things will make (one) windy, (such as) the artichoke and all sorts of things, and potatoes and lichens.”
