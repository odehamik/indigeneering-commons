<span class="l-gloss">## **(44) Nänabushu and the Wolves ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/-naumlnabushu-and-the-wolves-footnotes.html))**</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding ayindaawag (once on a time they were dwelling) wiigiwaaming (in a wigwam) Nenaboozhoo oosan, Gwesind (Nenaboozhoo’s father, Gwesind/Kwesind), wiiwan gaye (and his wife) niizh oskinaweg gaye (and two young men too), Nenaboozhoo miinawaa dash Baaninii (Nenaboozhoo and Baaninii/Panini).</span>

1)  > **Ningoding ayindaawag wiigiwaaming Nenaboozhoo oosan, Gwesind, wiiwan gaye niizh oskinaweg gaye, Nenaboozhoo miinawaa dash Baaninii.**

> Once on a time in a wigwam dwelt Nänabushu’s father Kwäsind, with his wife and two youths, Nänabushu and also Panini.

<span class="l-gloss">Moozhag nandawenjigewag (often they hunted), awesiiyan nisaawaad (killing game animals); mitigwaabiin odaabaji’aawaan (they used bow and arrow) bimwaawaad awesiian (when they shot at the game-kind) – binewan, zhiishiiban, nikan (ruffed grouse, ducks, geese), anooji gego onitoonaawaa (all sorts of things they killed).</span>

2)  > **Moozhag nandawenjigewag, awesiiyan nisaawaad; mitigwaabiin odaabaji’aawaan bimwaawaad awesiian – binewan, zhiishiiban, nikan, anooji gego onitoonaawaa.**

> Often they went hunting, (and) game they killed; bow and arrows they used when they shot at the game-kind, - at ruffed grouse, ducks, geese, - and various kinds of them they killed.

Ginwenzh gii-daawag imaa (a long while they dwelt there).

3)  > **Ginwenzh gii-daawag imaa.**

> A long while they tarried there.

<span class="l-gloss">Gegapii Nenaboozhoo odinaan oosan (at last Nenaboozhoo said to his father): “Noose, ninga-maajaa.” (“my father, I am going away.”)</span>

4)  > **Gegapii Nenaboozhoo odinaan oosan: “Noose, ninga-maajaa.”**

> At last Nänabushu said to his father: “O father\! I am going away.”

“Aandi waa-izhaayan?” (“where do you intend to go?”)

5)  > **“Aandi waa-izhaayan?”**

> “Where do you expect to go?”

“Niwii-babaamaadis.” (“I want to travel about.”)

6)  > **“Niwii-babaamaadis.”**

> “I want to go upon a journey.”

“Booshke giin maajaan.” (“as you please, then go on.”)

7)  > **“Booshke giin maajaan.”**

> “If it be your pleasure, then go on.”

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo gaa-izhi-maajaad (then Nenaboozhoo started off); miziwe gii-izhaa (everywhere he went), omitigwaabiin odayaawaan (he took his bow and arrows along).</span>

8)  > **Nenaboozhoo gaa-izhi-maajaad; miziwe gii-izhaa, omitigwaabiin odayaawaan.**

> Nänabushu then started away; everywhere he went, his bow and arrows he took along.

Onooji gegoo \[wa\] nitoon maajid (the various things he killed he ate as he went) \[\*editorial \[wa\] preserved as printed\*\].

9)  > **Onooji gegoo \[wa\] nitoon maajid.**

> Of the various kinds (of game) he killed he ate.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding idash wiigiwaam owaabandaan (and once he saw a wigwam), mii dash odoo-daapinaan obikwak (so he took up his arrow) omadwe’aan ishkwaandem (and with it knocked on the door).</span>

10) > **Ningoding idash wiigiwaam owaabandaan, mii dash odoo-daapinaan obikwak omadwe’aan ishkwaandem.**

> So once on a time a wigwam he saw, whereupon he took his arrow (and) with it was heard knocking on the door.

<span class="l-gloss">Madwe-giigido ikwe (a woman’s voice was heard): “Biindigen\!” ikido (“come in\!” she said).</span>

11) > **Madwe-giigido ikwe: “Biindigen\!” ikido.**

> The voice of a woman called: “Come in\!” she said.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-piindiged Nenaboozhoo (after Nenaboozhoo went in), owaabamaan ikwewan (he saw a woman) niizh gaye abinoojiiya’ (and two children too).</span>

12) > **Gaa-izhi-piindiged Nenaboozhoo, owaabamaan ikwewan niizh gaye abinoojiiya’.**

> After Nänabushu went in, he saw a woman and two children.

<span class="l-gloss">Ikwe ogii-odaapinaan mitig (the woman picked up a stick); ikwe obakitewaan okaading (the woman struck them on the leg) memeshkod (one after the other).</span>

13) > **Ikwe ogii-odaapinaan mitig; ikwe obakitewaan okaading memeshkod.**

> The woman picked up a stick, the woman struck them on the leg; first one, then the other.

“Gego, gego\!” ikido Nenaboozhoo (“don’t, don’t\!” said Nenaboozhoo).

14) > **“Gego, gego\!” ikido Nenaboozhoo.**

> “Don’t, don’t\!” said Nänabushu.

“Ninga-awi-nandawenjige,” ikido Nenaboozhoo (“I am going off to hunt for game,” said Nenaboozhoo).

15) > **“Ninga-awi-nandawenjige,” ikido Nenaboozhoo.**

> “I am going off on a hunt for game,” said Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-nisaad oniijaanisan bezhig (and so after she had killed one of her children), mii dash gaa-izhi-ozhitood goke’awan (he then made some bait), giingoonh goke’awan (fish-bait), niibiwa gaye wiigob biiminakwaan (and a lot of basswood-bark twine), migiskanan gaye (and some hooks).</span>

16) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-nisaad oniijaanisan bezhig, mii dash gaa-izhi-ozhitood goke’awan, giingoonh goke’awan, niibiwa gaye wiigob biiminakwaan, migiskanan gaye.**

> And so after she had slain one of her children, he then fixed up some bait, some fish-bait, and also a large amount of linden-bark twine, and some hooks.

Mii dash gii-ozhitood obimiwanaan (thereupon he made up his pack).

17) > **Mii dash gii-ozhitood obimiwanaan.**

> Thereupon he made up his pack.

“Gichi-gichi-gamiing ninga-bagidaabii. (“in the great sea I am going to set my line.)

18) > **“Gichi-gichi-gamiing ninga-bagidaabii.**

> “In the sea I am going to lay a line of bone hooks.

<span class="l-gloss">Nisagwa giingoonyag ninga-biinaag (the fish I kill I will bring home), ji-wiisiniwaad abinoonjiyag (that the children may eat).</span>

19) > **Nisagwa giingoonyag ninga-biinaag, ji-wiisiniwaad abinoonjiyag.**

> The fish I kill I will fetch home, that the children may eat.

Naanagonagak ninga-dagwishin.” (“in five days I will return.”)

20) > **Naanagonagak ninga-dagwishin.”**

> In five days I will return.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gii-maajaad (thereupon he started off). Nitam idash dibikadinig gii-gabeshi (and the first time night came on he made camp); gii-nibaa (he slept).</span>

21) > **Mii dash gii-maajaad. Nitam idash dibikadinig gii-gabeshi; gii-nibaa.**

> Thereupon he started away. The first time that night came on, there he stopped to camp; he slept.

<span class="l-gloss">Wayaabaninig idash gii-ani-maajaa (and in the morning he went on his way); gwayak giiwedinong izhaa (straight toward the north he went).</span>

22) > **Wayaabaninig idash gii-ani-maajaa; gwayak giiwedinong izhaa.**

> And then in the morning he started on; straight towards the region of the north wind he went.

<span class="l-gloss">Gikizheb, jibwaa-naawakweg (in the morning, before noon), gii-madaabii zaaga’igan (he came out onto a lake).</span>

23) > **Gikizheb, jibwaa-naawakweg, gii-madaabii zaaga’igan.**

> In the morning, before it was noon, he came out upon a lake.

<span class="l-gloss">Ogii-waabamaan niswi ma’iingana’ bimipatoonid (he saw three Wolves running along); oganawaabamaan (he watched them).</span>

24) > **Ogii-waabamaan niswi ma’iingana’ bimipatoonid; oganawaabamaan.**

> He saw three Wolves running along; he gazed at them.

<span class="l-gloss">Oo, biibaagi Nenaboozhoo (oh, Nenaboozhoo called out): “E, bekaa, niijii\! Bii’ishiyok\!” (“hey, hold on, my friends\! Wait for me\!”)</span>

25) > **Oo, biibaagi Nenaboozhoo: “E, bekaa, niijii\! Bii’ishiyok\!”**

> Oh, then out cried Nänabushu: “Hey, hold on, my friends\! Wait for me\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Ma’inganag ganoonidiwag (the Wolves spoke to one another): “Aa, mii awe Nenaboozhoo\!” (“ah, that is Nenaboozhoo\!”)</span>

26) > **Ma’inganag ganoonidiwag: “Aa, mii awe Nenaboozhoo\!”**

> The Wolves spoke one to another, saying: “Why, that is Nänabushu\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Bezhig akiwenzii ma’iingan (one was an old Wolf), niizh ogwisan ma’inganag (two of the Wolves were his sons).</span>

27) > **Bezhig akiwenzii ma’iingan, niizh ogwisan ma’inganag.**

> One was an old Wolf, and two (other) Wolves (were) his sons.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gego, gego ganoonaakegon. (“don’t, don’t you speak to him.) Maajaag, maajaag\!” (“keep going, keep going\!”)</span>

28) > **“Gego, gego ganoonaakegon. Maajaag, maajaag\!”**

> “Don’t, don’t you speak to him\! Keep on going, keep on going\!”

Aah, gichi-pimipatoo Nenaboozhoo\! (ah, with great speed ran Nenaboozhoo\!)

29) > **Aah, gichi-pimipatoo Nenaboozhoo\!**

> Oh, with what great speed then ran Nänabushu\!

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawa biibaagi (again he called out): “Bekaa, bekaa, niijii\! (“hold on, hold on, my friends\!) Bii’ishin akawe, giwii-kanoonin\!” (“wait for me awhile, I want to speak to you\!”)</span>

30) > **Miinawa biibaagi: “Bekaa, bekaa, niijii\! Bii’ishin akawe, giwii-kanoonin\!”**

> Once more he cried aloud: “Hold on, hold on, my friends\! Wait for me awhile, I wish to speak to you\!”

Gegapii gii-kibijigaabawi (at last he stopped and stood still).

31) > **Gegapii gii-kibijigaabawi.**

> Finally (the old Wolf) stopped and stood.

<span class="l-gloss">E Nenaboozhoo gii-dagwishin imaa (so Nenaboozhoo arrived there). Aah, boozhoo, boozhoo, niijii\!” (“ah, hello, hello, my friends\!”)</span>

32) > **E Nenaboozhoo gii-dagwishin imaa. Aah, boozhoo, boozhoo, niijii\!”**

> So Nänabushu arrived over there. “Well, halloo, halloo my friends\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Gaye odoozhima’ Nenaboozhoo ogagwejimaan (and Nenaboozhoo questioned his nephews): “Aandi e-zhaayeg?” (“where are you going?”)</span>

33) > **Gaye odoozhima’ Nenaboozhoo ogagwejimaan: “Aandi e-zhaayeg?”**

> And of his nephews Nänabushu inquired: “Where are you going?”

“Oh, waasa waabanong.” (“oh, far away toward the east/dawn.”)

34) > **“Oh, waasa waabanong.”**

> „Oh, far away towards the dawn.”

“Aaniin ezhinikaadeg?” (“what is the place called?”)

35) > **“Aaniin ezhinikaadeg?”**

> “What is the name of the place?”

“Giizhikidigwanishing.” (“Place-of-Cedar-Knots.”)

36) > **“Giizhikidigwanishing.”**

> “Place of Cedar-Knots.”

<span class="l-gloss">“We\!” Nenaboozhoo ikido (“oh\!” Nenaboozhoo said), “mii iwe gaye niin izhaayaan.” (“that is the very place I too am going.”)</span>

37) > **“We\!” Nenaboozhoo ikido, “mii iwe gaye niin izhaayaan.”**

> “Oh\!” Nänabushu said, “that is the very place where I too am going.”

<span class="l-gloss">Ma’iingan ogagwejimaan Nenaboozhoo (the Wolf questioned Nenaboozhoo): “Wegonen gaa-bimoondaman?” (“what is it you are carrying on your back?”)</span>

38) > **Ma’iingan ogagwejimaan Nenaboozhoo: “Wegonen gaa-bimoondaman?”**

> The Wolf asked Nänabushu: “What are you carrying on your back?”

“Nim-biinjigosaan gaa-bimoondamaan.” (“it is my bag I am carrying on my back.”)

39) > **“Nim-biinjigosaan gaa-bimoondamaan.”**

> “My bag (with personal belongings) is what I have on my back.”

<span class="l-gloss">Aa, Nenaboozhoo ikido (well, Nenaboozhoo said): “Giga-wiijiiwininim. (“I will go along with you.) Wegonen nendawaabandameg? (“what are you looking for?) Gaye niin mii iwidi ezhaayaan.” (“I too am going there.”)</span>

40) > **Aa, Nenaboozhoo ikido: “Giga-wiijiiwininim. Wegonen nendawaabandameg? Gaye niin mii iwidi ezhaayaan.”**

> Why, Nänabushu said: „I will go along with you. What are you looking for? I too am bound for that place.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Niibinong gii-kii’osewag gidoozhimag (“last summer your nephews went on a hunt); niibiwa awesiiyan ogii-nisaawaan (much game they killed); mii dash niibiwa wiiyaas baateg bimide gaye (and a lot of dried meat and grease) mii dash gii-asanjigowaad (is what they cached); mii wendazhi-izhaayang. (“that is why we are going there.)</span>

41) > **“Niibinong gii-kii’osewag gidoozhimag; niibiwa awesiiyan ogii-nisaawaan; mii dash niibiwa wiiyaas baateg bimide gaye mii dash gii-asanjigowaad; mii wendazhi-izhaayang.**

> “Last summer on a hunt were your nephews; much game they killed, and a good deal of dry meat and grease was what they cached; that is what they are going there for.

Miinawaa dash, wii-kii’osewag gidoozhimag.” (“and besides, your nephews wish to go on another hunt.”)

42) > **Miinawaa dash, wii-kii’osewag gidoozhimag.”**

> Furthermore, on another hunt your nephews wish to go.”

Mii i’iw ekidod a’aw akiwenzii-ma’iingan (that is what the old Wolf said).

43) > **Mii i’iw ekidod a’aw akiwenzii-ma’iingan.**

> Thus spoke the old Wolf.

<span class="l-gloss">“We,” Nenaboozhoo ikido (“oh,” Nenaboozhoo said), “Giga-wiijiwiininim.” (“I am going along with you.”)</span>

44) > **“We,” Nenaboozhoo ikido, “Giga-wiijiwiininim.”**

> “Oh,” Nänabushu said, “I am going along with you.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa, gaawiin\!” ikido akiwenzii-ma’iingan (“oh, no\!” said the old Wolf). “Gaawiin gidaa-dibi’aasig gidoozhimag.” (“you could not keep pace with your nephews.”)</span>

45) > **“Aa, gaawiin\!” ikido akiwenzii-ma’iingan. “Gaawiin gidaa-dibi’aasig gidoozhimag.”**

> “Oh, no\!” said the old Wolf. “You cannot keep pace with your nephews.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aah, maanoo\! (“ah, never mind\!) Ninga-bimibatoo gaye niin.” (“I myself will run too.”)</span>

46) > **“Aah, maanoo\! Ninga-bimibatoo gaye niin.”**

> “Ha, ha\! never mind\! I myself will run too.”

“Aaniish, booshke giin.” (“well then, just as you please.”)

47) > **“Aaniish, booshke giin.”**

> “Very well, just as you please.”

Ezhi-maajaawaad ma’iinganag bimibatoowaad (so the Wolves started off running).

48) > **Ezhi-maajaawaad ma’iinganag bimibatoowaad.**

> Then off started the Wolves running.

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-kopiiwaad (as they went up away from the shore) odoo-daapinaan obimiwanaan (he picked up his pack) ogii-apagidoon mitigoong (and flung it onto a log/tree).</span>

49) > **Eni-kopiiwaad odoo-daapinaan obimiwanaan ogii-apagidoon mitigoong.**

> As they went their way up from the shore, he picked up his pack and flung it upon a log

<span class="l-gloss">“Bikwaandag da-izhinikaade ji-ani-akiiwang.” (“‘knotty-bough/jack-pine’ shall it be called to the end of the world.” — \*plant-name carried; Jones’s English ‘Pinus resinosa (red pine?)’ is his own gloss-note\*)</span>

50) > **“Bikwaandag da-izhinikaade ji-ani-akiiwang.”**

> “Pinus resinosa (red pine?) shall it be called till the end of the world.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gii-maajaawaad (thereupon they started off). Moozhag bimibatoowag ma’iinganag (the Wolves ran steadily), Nenaboozhoo gaye wiin gichi-pimipatoo (and Nenaboozhoo too ran with great speed).</span>

51) > **Mii dash gii-maajaawaad. Moozhag bimibatoowag ma’iinganag, Nenaboozhoo gaye wiin gichi-pimipatoo.**

> Thereupon they started off. Always running were the Wolves, and Nänabushu himself ran with great speed.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa bizhishig \[?\] zaaga’igan omadaabiinaawaa (out onto another open lake they came) \[\*editorial \[?\] preserved\*\]; gwayak naawakwam gii-izhaawag (straight across the middle of the ice they went).</span>

52) > **Miinawaa bizhishig \[?\] zaaga’igan omadaabiinaawaa; gwayak naawakwam gii-izhaawag.**

> Out upon another lake they came, straight across the ice they made their way.

<span class="l-gloss">Anishinaabeg i’imaa daawag (some people were dwelling there), owaabamaawaan ma’iingana’ (they saw the Wolves).</span>

53) > **Anishinaabeg i’imaa daawag, owaabamaawaan ma’iingana’.**

> Some people were abiding at the place; they saw the Wolves.

<span class="l-gloss">“Hehii, naske ma’iinganag bemipatoowaad\! (“halloo, look at the Wolves running by\!) Niiwiwag, niiwin\!” (“there are four of them, four\!”)</span>

54) > **“Hehii, naske ma’iinganag bemipatoowaad\! Niiwiwag, niiwin\!”**

> “Halloo, see the Wolves that are running by\! They are four, four (is their number)\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo gaye owe doodam onikan (and Nenaboozhoo was doing this with his arm) owe babimipatood (as he ran along).</span>

55) > **Nenaboozhoo gaye owe doodam onikan owe babimipatood.**

> And Nänabushu was using his hand \[arm\] as he went running by.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa biibaagi anishinaabe (again the people yelled): “Aaniin izhinaagozid bezhig ma’iingan? (“what does that one Wolf look like?) Gaawiin gegoo ozowaasii\!” (“he has no tail at all\!”)</span>

56) > **Miinawaa biibaagi anishinaabe; “Aaniin izhinaagozid bezhig ma’iingan? Gaawiin gegoo ozowaasii\!”**

> Again yelled the people: “Like what is the look of one of the Wolves? He is entirely with a tail\!”

Aa, baapiwag anishinaabeg\! (oh, how the people laughed\!)

57) > **Aa, baapiwag anishinaabeg\!**

> Og, how the people laughed\!

“Shkaa mii iidog Nenaboozhoo\!” (“why, it must be Nenaboozhoo\!”)

58) > **“Shkaa mii iidog Nenaboozhoo\!”**

> “It must be Nänabushu\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Gwayak gii-maajaawag (straight on they kept going). Aa, aazha ayekozi Nenaboozhoo\! (oh, now Nenaboozhoo was getting tired\!)</span>

59) > **Gwayak gii-maajaawag. Aa, aazha ayekozi Nenaboozhoo\!**

> Straight on they kept going. Oh, but how tired now was Nänabushu becoming\!

Apii dash wenaagoshig gii-kabeshiwag (and when it was evening they made camp).

60) > **Apii dash wenaagoshig gii-kabeshiwag.**

> When it was evening, they made camp.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji ezhi-kisinaag jiigibiig zaaga’iganing (where it was very cold by the shore of a lake) mii imaa gii-kabeshiwaad (was where they camped).</span>

61) > **Aapiji ezhi-kisinaag jiigibiig zaaga’iganing mii imaa gii-kabeshiwaad.**

> Where it was exceedingly cold by the shore of a lake was the place where they camped.

Gaawiin gego ishkode (there was no fire).

62) > **Gaawiin gego ishkode.**

> There was no fire.

<span class="l-gloss">Bangii gii-moona’igewag goonikaang (a little they dug into the snow), mii imaa gii-kawishimowaad (and there they lay down to sleep).</span>

63) > **Bangii gii-moona’igewag goonikaang, mii imaa gii-kawishimowaad.**

> A shallow place in the snow they dug, and that was where they lay down to sleep.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo bangii gii-moona’ige goonikaang (Nenaboozhoo too dug a little into the snow), mii maa gii-kawishimod (and there he lay down to sleep).</span>

64) > **Gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo bangii gii-moona’ige goonikaang, mii maa gii-kawishimod.**

> Likewise Nänabushu dug a shallow place in the snow, and there he lay down to sleep.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji ayekozi (very tired he was), aapiji gaye gii-abwezo (and he was sweating a great deal), gii-kichi-bimipatood (for he had been running hard).</span>

65) > **Aapiji ayekozi, aapiji gaye gii-abwezo, gii-kichi-bimipatood.**

> Very tired he was, and very much was he sweating, for hard had he been running.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin ogashkitoosiin ji-nibaad (he could not sleep); zhayiigwa giikaji, gichi-giikaji (now he was getting chilled, very cold).</span>

66) > **Gaawiin ogashkitoosiin ji-nibaad; zhayiigwa giikaji, gichi-giikaji.**

> He was not able to sleep; now was he becoming chilled, ever so cold was he.

<span class="l-gloss">Akiwenzii-ma’iingan oganoonaan ogwisan (the old Wolf spoke to his son). “Daga, awi bezhig gi-konaas.” (“come now, lend him one of your robes.”)</span>

67) > **Akiwenzii-ma’iingan oganoonaan ogwisan. “Daga, awi bezhig gi-konaas.”**

> The old Wolf spoke to his little son saying: “I say, lend him one of your blankets.”

Ma’iinganens inaanowe bagizo Nenaboozhoo nibaanid (the little Wolf flung his tail over where Nenaboozhoo lay asleep).

68) > **Ma’iinganens inaanowe bagizo Nenaboozhoo nibaanid.**

> The little Wolf threw his tail over where Nänabushu lay asleep.

<span class="l-gloss">Aa\! Nenaboozhoo aapiji giizhoozi (ah\! Nenaboozhoo became very warm). Mii dash gii-nibaad (and then he slept).</span>

69) > **Aa\! Nenaboozhoo aapiji giizhoozi. Mii dash gii-nibaad.**

> Ah\! but Nänabushu then became exceedingly warm. Upon that he went to sleep.

Mii dash goshkozid aapij abwezo (and when he woke he was sweating heavily).

70) > **Mii dash goshkozid aapij abwezo.**

> And when he awoke, he was in a very heavy sweat.

> Then there was where they slept.

> Thereupon on the next evening they went into camp.

> And then spoke the old Wolf.

> In the morning they set out together.

> Ah\! once more on the run started Nänabushu.

> When it was noon, they came out upon a lake.

<span class="l-gloss">Giigido akiwenzii-ma’iingan (the old Wolf spoke): “Nashke, Nenaboozhoo\! (“look, Nenaboozhoo\!) ganabaj mii sha’ajigewag gidoozhimag.” (“perhaps your nephews have made a kill.”)</span>

> Up spoke the old Wolf: “Look, Nänabushu\! maybe your nephews have shot and hit something.”

<span class="l-gloss">Akiwenzii-ma’iingan ani-daatagikweni (the old Wolf lifted his head, scenting about), ominaamaan moozoon (he smelled a moose).</span>

> Thereupon they kept on till they reached the place where the being was sitting.

> Whereupon Nänabushu was greatly delighted with he thought of eating.
