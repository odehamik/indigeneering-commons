<span class="l-orig">(1) Mī∙i∙dạc Nänabuju kā∙i∙jipīndigäd aka‘kwidcīciwājing. Ā‘pidạc kā‘pīndigäd Nänabuju kayä wīn, aka‘kwidcīci kī‘pīndigä, mīidạc kā∙i∙jikibā∙ạ∙ng uwāc. Pānimā∙i∙dec kā‘pimidciwaninig nipi, mīnawā gīsāga∙ạ∙m Nänabuju. Ningudingidac äjipîmosäd, onōndawān awiyᵃ nạgạmonit:</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash Nenabozho gaa-izhi-biindiged aakakojiijiwaajing\*. Aapidash gaa-biindiged Nenabozho gaye wiin, aakakojiiji gii-biindige, mii dash gaa-izhi-gibaa-ang\* owaazh. Baanimaa-idash gaa-bimijiwaninig nibi, minawaa gii-zaaga’am Nenabozho. Ningoding idash ezhi-bimosed, onoondawaan awiya nagamonid:**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*So Nänabushu came into the Woodchuck’s hole.² So after Nänabushu had gone inside, then the Woodchuck went in too, whereupon he closed (the entrance of) his hole. <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-naazikawaad\*, mii dash gii-waabamaad mindimooyeyan, omakakiin; wiigobiin obimoondaanan, gaye-idash jiichiigwanan ojaagwaanzonan\* a’aw mindimooye. <span class="l-eng">*After that he sought, listening for (the singer), whereupon he saw an old woman, a toad; some linden-bark she carried upon her back, and rattles too were hanging from the old woman’s girdle. <span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-naazikawaad (and so then when he approached her — \*naazikaw- ‘go up to’, flagged\*), mii dash gii-waabamaad mindimooyeyan (then he saw an old woman), omakakiin (a toad). Wiigobiin obimoondaanan (she carried linden-bark on her back — \*wiigob ‘inner bark of basswood/linden’\*), gaye-idash jiichiigwanan ojaagwaanzonan a’aw mindimooye (and rattles hung from that old woman’s girdle — \*jiichiigwan ‘rattle’; ojägwānson ‘her girdle/belt’, flagged\*). <span class="l-orig">(4) Mīdạc ä‘kidot mindimoyäⁿ: “Nänabujū kuca ogī‘pîmwaᵉ mạnido⁸, nīnidạc nīnạnāndawi∙ā∙g igi‘ᵘ mạnidōg. <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash ekidod mindimooye: “Nenabozho gosha ogii-bimwaa\* manidoon, niin-idash nii-nanaandawi’aag igi’iw manidoog. Gaawiin ina-giin\* Nenabozho gidaawisii?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon said the old woman: “Why, Nänabushu indeed has shot the manitous, and so it is I who am going to heal the manitous. <span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ekidod mindimooye (and so the old woman said): “Nenabozho gosha ogii-bimwaa manidoon (Nänabushu indeed shot the manitous — \*bimwaa- ‘shoot at’; manidoon obviative, plain gloss only\*), niin-idash nii-nanaandawi’aag igi’iw manidoog (and it is I who am to heal those manitous — \*nanaandawi’- ‘doctor, treat’, plain gloss; flagged\*). Nenabozho-idash wii-nanaandawaabini’kaanaa onowe wiigobiin (and for Nänabushu these linden-bark snares are made — \*form flagged\*); miziwe akiing wii-inaabiiginigaadewan (all over the earth they will be strung — \*inaabiigin- ‘string out as cord’, flagged\*). Giishpin-idash doodookaabiigishkang (and if he steps and pulls the cord — \*form flagged\*), mii-iwe ji-gikenimind aaniindi ayaad Nenabozho (that is how it will be known where Nänabushu is). Gaawiin ina-giin Nenabozho gidaawisii? (Are you not yourself Nänabushu?)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(5) “Kāwīn,” i‘kido Nänabuju. “Kīdābimādci∙i∙kîna Nänabuju wâbạmạd?” Mīdạc änād: “Ānindi äyāyạn?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaawiin,” ikido Nenabozho. “Gidaabi-maaji’ik ina\* Nenabozho waabamad? Mii dash enaad: “Aaniindi eyaayan?”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“No,” said Nänabushu. “Do you suppose that you would be permitted to live if you should see Nänabushu?” And then he said to her: “Where do you abide?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin,” ikido Nenabozho (“No,” said Nänabushu). “Gidaabi-maaji’ik ina Nenabozho waabamad? <span class="l-orig">(6) “Mī∙i∙māⁿ pācu ugimāg äyāwād. Nōngum unāngucik mī∙i∙‘we kägä‘t wīkitcinạnāndawiiwäyān, mī∙i∙‘we Nänabuju odōjimạn usạgīn kädạcimigōyàn nōngum unāngucik.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Mii imaa\* baazho\* ogimaag eyaawaad. Noongom onaagoshig mii-iwe geget wii-gichi-nanaandawi’iweyaan, mii-iwe Nenabozho odoojimaan\* osagin, gedazhimigooyaan\* noongom onaagoshig.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Yonder, near by where the chiefs are. On this evening is truly when I will do some wonderful healing, whereupon the upper arm of Nänabushu’s nephew shall I be given to eat this evening.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii imaa baazho ogimaag eyaawaad (Yonder, close by where the chiefs are — \*pācu ‘near’, flagged\*). <span class="l-orig">(9) Ā‘pī∙i∙dạc ka‘kina kāwīndạmāgut mī∙i∙‘ᵘ kā∙i∙jinisāt. Mī∙i∙dạc kā∙i∙jipa‘kunāt mē∙i∙dec kā∙i∙i∙jipizi‘kawād, mē∙i∙dạc kī∙o∙dā‘pinạng wīgobīn kīpîmōndạng; kayä dạc jīcīgwạnạn ugijägwạsonạn. Mē∙i∙dạc kīmādcād äjäni‘pạn mindimōyäyạn. Kayäwīndạc kī∙ạ∙ninạgamo:</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash ezhi-biibaagiwaad: “Ä, mii sa gii-niboowaad manidoog\! ‘Aaaw, maajiidooyok wiigobiin miziwe akiing da-inaabiiginamon\*; mii dash ji-gikenimind aaniindi ayaagwen Nenabozho, giishpin doodookaabiigishkang.” Mii dash ga’kina gaa-izhi-maajaawaad, gii-bimaabiiginamowaad wiigobiin.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And they then cried aloud: “Alas\! now and there are the manitous. <span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-biibaagiwaad (and so they cried aloud): “Ä, mii sa gii-niboowaad manidoog\! <span class="l-eng">*And when all had started away, Nänabushu cut (the manitous) into pieces, and made a great cooking of the manitou-flesh. <span class="l-gloss">Aapii-idash ga’kina gaa-maajaawaad (and when they had all gone away), Nenabozho ogii-bigishkizhwaan gichi-jiibaakwe manidoowiyaas (Nänabushu cut up and made a great cooking of the manitou-flesh — \*bigishkizhw- ‘cut to pieces’; jiibaakwe ‘cook’; manidoo-wiiyaas plain gloss only\*).
