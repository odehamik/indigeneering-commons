<span class="l-gloss">## (35) Nenabosho and the Woodpecker ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html))</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa bakadewag aapiji (now they were very hungry), mii sa ezhi-maajaad Nenabosho (and so off went Nenabosho).</span>

1)  > **Zhigwa bakadewag aapiji, mii sa ezhi-maajaad Nenabosho.**

> So were they much in need of food, whereupon off went Nenabosho.

Zhigwa, miinawaa odishiwe (now, again he went visiting).

2)  > **Zhigwa, miinawaa odishiwe.**

> Now, again was he a-visiting.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa miinawaa ezhi-nawaad anishinaabe (and so again the ones he saw were like people), niizhiwa oniijaanisini (and they had two children).</span>

3)  > **Mii sa miinawaa ezhi-nawaad anishinaabe, niizhiwa oniijaanisini.**

> And so again they whom he saw seemed like people, (and) they had two children.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin gegoo waabandamawaasiin (he saw nothing of what they had); wekwaagaan i’iw endaanid (a long lodge it was that they lived in).</span>

4)  > **Gaawiin gegoo waabandamawaasiin; wekwaagaan i’iw endaanid.**

> He saw nothing of what they had; a long lodge they occupied for a home.

<span class="l-gloss">“Wegodogwenigish maajiiwaagwen\!” (“I wonder what in the world they eat\!” — \*dubitative\*) inendam (he thought).</span>

5)  > **“Wegodogwenigish maajiiwaagwen\!” inendam.**

> “Wonder what in the world they eat\!” he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">“Zhigwa, aaniin dash naa iw (“now, what then) giige-aasiwang a’aw gibiiwideminaan?” (shall we feed our visitor?”)</span>

6)  > **“Zhigwa, aaniin dash naa iw giige-aasiwang a’aw gibiiwideminaan?”**

> “Now, what had we better feed our visitor?”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa geget zhigwa ozhiitaawan iiniw ininiwan (and so truly now the man got ready); wawezhi’oowan ogatigwaning (he adorned himself on the forehead), odishishimaani iiniw miskoosaanamanan (the red paint he laid on); miinawaa odoonjibidoonini biiwaabik (and he took up a piece of metal) aapiji gaa-zhi-boodeni (filed to a very keen edge — \*boodeni stem flagged\*).</span>

7)  > **Mii sa geget zhigwa ozhiitaawan iiniw ininiwan; wawezhi’oowan ogatigwaning, odishishimaani iiniw miskoosaanamanan; miinawaa odoonjibidoonini biiwaabik aapiji gaa-zhi-boodeni.**

> Thereupon truly the man began getting ready; he painted himself on the forehead, the kind of paint he used was red; furthermore, he took up a piece of metal, with a very keen edge due to filing.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, onisitawinaan eyaawaninig (now, he recognized what it was), miinangwana i’iw izhichigewaad (it turned out that what they did) iiniw okonaasanwiin(2) zhegonaanid i’imaa oshangwanaaning (was to set the bone-pointed spear into his nostrils) aaniish megwaa go gii-namadabinid (while he sat there).</span>

8)  > **Aaniish, onisitawinaan eyaawaninig, miinangwana i’iw izhichigewaad iiniw okonaasanwiin([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html)) zhegonaanid i’imaa oshangwanaaning aaniish megwaa go gii-namadabinid.**

> Now, (Nenabosho) recognized what it was, for the thing that they did was to put the bone-pointed spear into the nostrils while he was seated there.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii gaa-giizhi’onid (when he had finished adorning himself), baanimaa go wanjinawatinaanid iiniw mitigoon (suddenly he sprang at a post) bedakisonid i’imaa endaanid (that stood there where they lived); noondaagosiwan (the sound of his voice was heard).</span>

9)  > **Apii gaa-giizhi’onid, baanimaa go wanjinawatinaanid iiniw mitigoon bedakisonid i’imaa endaanid; noondaagosiwan.**

> When he had finished painting himself, he suddenly made for a post that was standing there where they lived; the sound of his cry could be heard.

<span class="l-gloss">“Kokok, kokok\!” (vocable, carried as printed) inwewan (such was his cry).</span>

10) > **“Kokok, kokok\!” inwewan.**

> “Kokok, kokok\!” was his cry.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish inaabid Nenabosho (now, when Nenabosho looked), memen owaabamaan (he saw the red-head woodpecker); zhigwa odanigwekwenibaabamaani iiniw mitigoon (and he kept bobbing his head along, eyeing the post).</span>

11) > **Aaniish inaabid Nenabosho, memen owaabamaan; zhigwa odanigwekwenibaabamaani iiniw mitigoon.**

> Now, when Nenabosho looked, he saw the red-head (woodpecker); and (the bird) kept busily nodding his head to see where to find something on the post.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigo ko odanikojisagawaani (at last he started pecking up along the post); mii go ezhi-gokweninid(3) i’imaa jiigaatig (and he would tip his head close to the post — \*gokweni stem flagged; cf. footnote 3\*).</span>

12) > **Zhigo ko odanikojisagawaani; mii go ezhi-gokweninid([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html)) i’imaa jiigaatig.**

> At last he began to try pecking the post; and occasionally, cesaing from his work, he held his head close to the post.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, biinish igo i’iwidi shpiming ani-ayaawan (now, little by little he worked his way up high), abijiinag (abiijinaag) iiniw zhayiigwa maajikwe’igewan (every now and then he would start pecking away).</span>

13) > **Aaniish, biinish igo i’iwidi shpiming ani-ayaawan, abijiinag (abiijinaag) iiniw zhayiigwa maajikwe’igewan.**

> Now, by degrees he began making his way upward, every now and then he would begin pecking away.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ningoding igo, inikwenininid (and so by and by, as he pecked), esibanan bimibatoowan (a raccoon came running out), mii sa miinawaa bezhig bimibisoowan (and then another came falling down).</span>

14) > **Mii dash ningoding igo, inikwenininid, esibanan bimibatoowan, mii sa miinawaa bezhig bimibisoowan.**

> And so by and by, as he pecked, (Nenabosho saw) a raccoon come running (out), and then another came falling down.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa niizh gii-nisaad i’iw esibana (and so, after he killed the two raccoons), baa-izhi-niisaakwashimoononid (he came down from the post).</span>

15) > **Mii sa niizh gii-nisaad i’iw esibana, baa-izhi-niisaakwashimoononid.**

> And so, after killing the raccoons, he came down from the post.

Mii sa ezhi-mamoonid i’imaa gaa-atoonid oshangwanaaning i’iw biiwaabik (thereupon he took out the metal he had put there in his nose).

16) > **Mii sa ezhi-mamoonid i’imaa gaa-atoonid oshangwanaaning i’iw biiwaabik.**

> Thereupon he removed the metal which he had placed there in his nose.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ezhi-gabadawind i’iw esibana (and then the raccoons were boiled for him); zhigwa agwaa’amawaa ji-wiisinid (then they were dipped out for him to eat).</span>

17) > **Mii sa ezhi-gabadawind i’iw esibana; zhigwa agwaa’amawaa ji-wiisinid.**

> And then the raccoons were cooked for (Nenabosho); then they were dipped out for him to eat.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa zhigwa gii-wiisinid, zhigwa ganoonaa (and so when he was eating, he was spoken to); “Aaniish mii sa ge-zhi-kiiwewidawadwaa giniijaanisag (“now then, carry back to your children) minik eshkwanjigeyan,” (as much as you leave uneaten,”) gii-inaa (he was told).</span>

18) > **Mii sa zhigwa gii-wiisinid, zhigwa ganoonaa; “Aaniish mii sa ge-zhi-kiiwewidawadwaa giniijaanisag minik eshkwanjigeyan,” gii-inaa.**

> And so then was he eating when he was addressed: “Now, therefore, do you carry back to your children as much as you do not not eat,” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa miinawaa wanimook (thereupon again, unseen), igo imaa gaa-onji-shegoondaginaad miinawaa i’iw ogii-shoode’ona (he tucked away his mittens there — \*shegoond-/shoode’on stem flagged\*).</span>

19) > **Mii sa miinawaa wanimook, igo imaa gaa-onji-shegoondaginaad miinawaa i’iw ogii-shoode’ona.**

> Thereupon again, without being seen, he slipped his mittens (in the balsam boughs under the mat).

Mii sa miinawaa ani-maajaad (and so again he set out).

20) > **Mii sa miinawaa ani-maajaad.**

> So then again he set out on his way.

<span class="l-gloss">Baanimaa go madwe-piibaagi (after a while a voice could be heard calling), “Ay\!” medwe’ikidowan (“Hey\!” someone was heard saying).</span>

21) > **Baanimaa go madwe-piibaagi, “Ay\!” medwe’ikidowan.**

> After a while he heard somebody calling aloud: “Hey\!” he heard someone saying.

<span class="l-gloss">“Giwanishigaagome\!” (“you’ve forgotten something\!”) madwe-ikidowan (he was heard saying).</span>

22) > **“Giwanishigaagome\!” madwe-ikidowan.**

> “You have forgotten something\!” he heard him say.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniish, wegodogwenigish?” (“why, what in the world is it?” — \*dubitative; cf. footnote 4\*) ikidowan ininiwan (said the man).</span>

23) > **“Aaniish, wegodogwenigish?” ikidowan ininiwan.**

> “Why, what in the world is it?”([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html)) said the man.

Miinangwana gosha geget gaabonid i’iw ogiizhoode’ona (it turned out, sure enough, that his mittens were left where he had been).

24) > **Miinangwana gosha geget gaabonid i’iw ogiizhoode’ona.**

> It was really true that where (Nenabosho) had been were his mittens.

“Waasa go onji-webinamawik (“from far off fling them to him).

25) > **“Waasa go onji-webinamawik.**

> “From afar do you fling them to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gego wiin i’iwidi beshoshaakegon,” (don’t go near him over there,”) odinaa i’iw oniijaanisa (he said to his children).</span>

26) > **Gego wiin i’iwidi beshoshaakegon,” odinaa i’iw oniijaanisa.**

> Do not go near to him,” he said to (his boys).

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaye go debiwebinamaweg mii imaa apii onji-webinamawik,” (“and from as far as you can throw, that is where you fling them to him,”) odinaa i’iw oniijaanisa (he said to his children).</span>

27) > **“Gaye go debiwebinamaweg mii imaa apii onji-webinamawik,” odinaa i’iw oniijaanisa.**

> And from as far as you can throw is the distance you fling them to him,” he said to his children.

<span class="l-gloss">Anishaa geget zhigwa o’ow idash odinaa i’iw oniijaanisa (now, truly just in jest he said this to his children): “Gaawiin ningodinoo gii-ga-bwaa’igosiiwaa.” (“in no way will he refrain from saying something to you.”)</span>

28) > **Anishaa geget zhigwa o’ow idash odinaa i’iw oniijaanisa: “Gaawiin ningodinoo gii-ga-bwaa’igosiiwaa.”**

> Now, really, for fun only did he say this to his children: “In no way will he refrain from saying something to you.”

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa geget odaanawi-apagidawaawaan (now, truly when they were set to throw them to him), o’ow idash odinaa Nenabosho (this Nenabosho said to them): “Gego gaawanaagonewebinaawaag.” (“don’t fling them, lest they be lost in the snow.”)</span>

29) > **Zhigwa sa geget odaanawi-apagidawaawaan, o’ow idash odinaa Nenabosho: “Gego gaawanaagonewebinaawaag.”**

> When they were really in earnest about throwing them to him, then this Nenabosho said to them: “Do not fling them lest they become lost in the snow.”

Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-miinaawaad (whereupon truly they handed them to him).

30) > **Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-miinaawaad.**

> Whereupon they truly went and handed them to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Aanawi bimaajiba’idiwag iigiw gwiiwizhenshag (the boys were already starting to run off) gaa-izhi-inaad: “Akawe, i’imaa ayaayog\!” (when he said to them: “Hold on, stay there\!”)</span>

31) > **Aanawi bimaajiba’idiwag iigiw gwiiwizhenshag gaa-izhi-inaad: “Akawe, i’imaa ayaayog\!”**

> In the act of starting to run away were the boys when (this) he said to them: “Hold, wait there\!”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow dash odinaa (and this he said to them): “Geget isa gibakademwaasa,” (“truly, you must be quite hungry,”) odinaa (he said to them).</span>

32) > **O’ow dash odinaa: “Geget isa gibakademwaasa,” odinaa.**

> And this he said to them: “Really, you must be pretty hungry,” he said to them.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii gosha wiini iw bebaadaashiikewaad iigiw giijikiwe’iwaag (“that is just the way of your brothers wherever they go about); mii go bimide bebaa’opagamaaganiwaad bebaadashiikewaad iigiw giijikiweniwaag (it is grease your brothers hand out as they go about), i’iw mashkawajibimide (the hard-frozen grease).</span>

33) > **“Mii gosha wiini iw bebaadaashiikewaad iigiw giijikiwe’iwaag; mii go bimide bebaa’opagamaaganiwaad bebaadashiikewaad iigiw giijikiweniwaag, i’iw mashkawajibimide.**

> “That is exactly the way of your brothers wherever they are; it is grease that your brothers give so bountifully wherever they are, the hard frozen grease.

<span class="l-gloss">Ambe sa noo, apii waabang koosiwaa da-bii-izhaa,” (“come now, when tomorrow comes let your father come over,”) odinaa (he said to them).</span>

34) > **Ambe sa noo, apii waabang koosiwaa da-bii-izhaa,” odinaa.**

> Pray, when the morrow comes, let your father come over,” he said to them.

Mii sa eni-maajaad (and then on his way he went).

35) > **Mii sa eni-maajaad.**

> And then on his way he went.

<span class="l-gloss">Degoshing endaawaad (when he arrived at where they lived), ozhiitaa mitigoon (he got a post ready); obadakinaan endaawaad (he stood it up where they lived), wekwaagaan ogii-ozhitoon gaye (and he too had made a long lodge).</span>

36) > **Degoshing endaawaad, ozhiitaa mitigoon; obadakinaan endaawaad, wekwaagaan ogii-ozhitoon gaye.**

> When he arrived at where they lived, he made ready for a post; he placed it standing in (the place) where they lived, for he too had made a long lodge.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash egod iiniw wiiwan (and this his wife said to him): “Indashke miinawaa gaa-izhi-nawaagwen awiya,” (“no doubt you must have seen someone else doing this,” — \*dubitative\*) odigoon iiniw wiiwan (he was told by his wife).</span>

37) > **Mii dash egod iiniw wiiwan: “Indashke miinawaa gaa-izhi-nawaagwen awiya,” odigoon iiniw wiiwan.**

> And this he was told by his wife: “No doubt but that he must have seen somebody else doing that,” he was told by his wife.

<span class="l-gloss">“Amanjig isa, ezhimiwanen gegoo aanawi zhichigeyaanin?” (“why on earth would you talk me out of something I mean to do?”) odinaan iiniw wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

38) > **“Amanjig isa, ezhimiwanen gegoo aanawi zhichigeyaanin?” odinaan iiniw wiiwan.**

> “Now, why should you want to dissuade me from something I wish to do?” he said to his wife.

Aaniish, ogii-oninaan gaye iiniw osaanamanan gaye iiniw okanaasanwiin (now, he set in order his paint and his bone spear).

39) > **Aaniish, ogii-oninaan gaye iiniw osaanamanan gaye iiniw okanaasanwiin.**

> Now, he put in order for use his paint and his bone spear.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa gii-giizhiitaad zhayiigwa (and so when he was ready) giigidoowa oniijaanisa: “Biiwide\!” ikidoowa (his children spoke up: “A visitor\!” they said).</span>

40) > **Mii sa gii-giizhiitaad zhayiigwa giigidoowa oniijaanisa: “Biiwide\!” ikidoowa.**

> And so by the time he was ready, then he said his children: “A visitor\!” they said.

Aaniish mii sa ezhi-piindigaagowaad iiniw biiwiden (so thereupon the visitor came in to them).

41) > **Aaniish mii sa ezhi-piindigaagowaad iiniw biiwiden.**

> So thereupon in where they were came the visitor.

<span class="l-gloss">Miingwana dash iiniw memen (it turned out to be the red-head), aaniish, miiwaniniw dinowan gaa-odisaad (why, the very same one he had visited).</span>

42) > **Miingwana dash iiniw memen, aaniish, miiwaniniw dinowan gaa-odisaad.**

> It happened to be the red-head, why, the very same one whom he had visited.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa oganoonaan iiniw wiiwan (then he spoke to his wife): “Aaniin dash inaa iw ambe, gige-aasiwang a’aw biiwide?” (“why, pray, do we not feed this visitor?”) odinaan iiniw wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

43) > **Zhigwa sa oganoonaan iiniw wiiwan: “Aaniin dash inaa iw ambe, gige-aasiwang a’aw biiwide?” odinaan iiniw wiiwan.**

> Then he spoke to his wife saying: “Why is it, pray, we don’t feed the guest?” he said to his wife.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa oganoonigoon (then he was answered): “Wegoneshi iw eyaayang gegiige’iweyang?” (“what on earth do we have to feed him with?”)</span>

44) > **Zhigwa oganoonigoon: “Wegoneshi iw eyaayang gegiige’iweyang?”**

> Then he was answered: “What on earth have we to feed him?”

Aaniish onishkimigoon iiniw wiiwan (naturally his wife made him angry).

45) > **Aaniish onishkimigoon iiniw wiiwan.**

> Naturally he was angered by his wife (for speaking so).

<span class="l-gloss">“Zhayiigwa sa biija’onagoosh a’aw akik,” (“now then, hang up that kettle,”) odinaan iiniw wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

46) > **“Zhayiigwa sa biija’onagoosh a’aw akik,” odinaan iiniw wiiwan.**

> “Now, you hang up the kettle,” he said to his wife.

Mii sa geget a’aw ikwe anagoonaad iiniw odakikoon (thereupon truly the woman hung up her kettle).

47) > **Mii sa geget a’aw ikwe anagoonaad iiniw odakikoon.**

> Thereupon truly the woman hung up her kettle.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, mii sa zhayiigwa waweshi’od Nenabosho (so it was then that Nenabosho adorned himself), ezhi-miskonang i’iw oshangwan (painting his nose red).</span>

48) > **Aaniish, mii sa zhayiigwa waweshi’od Nenabosho, ezhi-miskonang i’iw oshangwan.**

> So it was then that Nenabosho began painting himself, painting his nose red.

Aaniishi, zhigwa, miinawaa oshegwanaan i’iw biiwaabik i’imaa ashangwanaang (now, then, next he set the metal there in his nose).

49) > **Aaniishi, zhigwa, miinawaa oshegwanaan i’iw biiwaabik i’imaa ashangwanaang.**

> Now, then, next he stuck the metal in his nose.

<span class="l-gloss">Megwaa sa go namadabid weji-basingwijised (and while seated, from there he sprang up) nawadinaad iiniw mitigoon gaa-padakinaad (seizing the post he had stood up); ani-akwaandawed (on up he climbed).</span>

50) > **Megwaa sa go namadabid weji-basingwijised nawadinaad iiniw mitigoon gaa-padakinaad; ani-akwaandawed.**

> And while seated, up from there he leaped, seizing the post that he had put up; on up the post he went.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhayiigwa noondaagozi (presently he was heard): “Gokok, gokok, gokok, gokok,” (vocable, carried as printed) inwe (such was his cry).</span>

51) > **Zhayiigwa noondaagozi: “Gokok, gokok, gokok, gokok,” inwe.**

> Presently he was heard uttering: “Gokok, gokok, gokok, gokok,” (such) was the cry he uttered.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhayiigwa sa aaniish gaa-izhi-nawaadigo izhichige (now then, just as he had seen done he was doing), zhigwa sa owiipaakwewaan iiniw mitigoon (now he was pecking the post).</span>

52) > **Zhayiigwa sa aaniish gaa-izhi-nawaadigo izhichige, zhigwa sa owiipaakwewaan iiniw mitigoon.**

> Now, what he had previously seen them do he was doing now, he was now pecking the post.

<span class="l-gloss">Bijiinag aabiding enikwenid (at the very first peck he made), ezhi-padakishkaagod (he was pierced by the metal), pane mii igo gii-bimibizo (and straightway down he fell); bangishing pane go gaa-bidigokamigishing (falling, he struck the ground with a thud).</span>

53) > **Bijiinag aabiding enikwenid, ezhi-padakishkaagod, pane mii igo gii-bimibizo; bangishing pane go gaa-bidigokamigishing.**

> At the first peck he made, he was pierced by the metal, whereupon down he fell; when he fell; he struck the ground with a thud.

Aaniish, miskwi oshangwanaang (well, his nose was bleeding).

54) > **Aaniish, miskwi oshangwanaang.**

> Well, his nose was bleeding.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ekidonid iiniw obiiwidemiwaan (thereupon their visitor said): “Aaniinaa iw mayaawibinaasiwaad?” (“why do you not lift him up and set him right?”) iiniw maawan iiniw ikwewan (he said to the woman).</span>

55) > **Mii sa ekidonid iiniw obiiwidemiwaan: “Aaniinaa iw mayaawibinaasiwaad?” iiniw maawan iiniw ikwewan.**

> Thereupon said their visitor: “Why do you not lift and set him up?” was what he said to the woman.

Mii sa geget ezhi-mayaawibinaad a’aw ikwe (thereupon truly the woman lifted and set him up).

56) > **Mii sa geget ezhi-mayaawibinaad a’aw ikwe.**

> Thereupon truly the woman lifted and set him up.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash odigoon iiniw biiwidemiwaan (and this their visitor said to her): “Odishkonaan maawiin iiniw osaanamanan (“no doubt he has left over some of his paint).</span>

57) > **O’ow idash odigoon iiniw biiwidemiwaan: “Odishkonaan maawiin iiniw osaanamanan.**

> And this she was told by their visitor: “Doubtless he may have left unused some of his paint.

<span class="l-gloss">Ambe sa noo biish,: (“come now, give it here,”) odigoon (she was told).</span>

58) > **Ambe sa noo biish,: odigoon.**

> Pray give it to me,” she was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget, ezhi-miinaad (and so truly, when she gave it to him), ezhi-miskonamid i’iw ogatigoni (he reddened the forehead).</span>

59) > **Mii dash geget, ezhi-miinaad, ezhi-miskonamid i’iw ogatigoni.**

> And so truly, when she gave it to him, he painted the forehead (of her husband) red.
