<span class="l-gloss">(24) Nänabushu and the Winged Startlers ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-winged-startlers-footnotes.html))</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa iw (and so it was) ezhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo (that Nenaboozhoo set out) babimosed (walking about).</span>

1)  > **Mii sa iw ezhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo babimosed;**

> Thereupon departed Nänabushu, travelling about;

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaago apii (after a certain distance) degoshing (when he arrived) owaabamaa’ binensa’ (he saw some young birds — little ruffed grouse) wadiswaning (in a nest), aapiji go mooshkinewa’ (very full did they fill it) i’imaa wadiswaning (there in the nest).</span>

2)  > **gomaago apii degoshing owaabamaa’ binensa’ wadiswaning, aapiji go mooshkinewa’ i’imaa wadiswaning.**

> when he was come a certain distance, he saw some young ruffed rouse in a nest, and very full they filled the place in the nest.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo odonabiitawaa’ (Nenaboozhoo sat down beside them), aapiji ozhawenimaa’ (very tender was his feeling toward them).</span>

3)  > **Nenaboozhoo odonabiitawaa, aapiji ozhawenimaa’.**

> Nänabushu sat down beside them, very tender was his feeling for them.

<span class="l-gloss">Odagimaa’ endashinind (he counted how many they were); midaaswi-ashi-niizh dashiwa’ (twelve was their number).</span>

4)  > **Odagimaa endashinind; midaaswi-ashi-niizh dashiwa’.**

> He counted how many they were ; twelve was their number.

Zhigwa dash oganoonaa’ (and now he spoke to them):

5)  > **Zhigwa dash oganoonaa’:**

> And then he spoke to them, asking:

“Aaniin ezhinikaazoyeg? (How are you called?)”

6)  > **“Aaniin ezhinikaazoyeg?”**

> "By what name are you called?"

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish zegiziwag (naturally they were afraid) igiw binensag (those little grouse).</span>

7)  > **Aaniish zegiziwag igiw binensag.**

> Naturally afraid were the little ruffed grouse.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin ogashkitoosiinaawaa’ (they were not able) ji-giigidowaad (to speak).</span>

8)  > **Gaawiin ogashkitoosiinaawaa’ ji-giigidowaad.**

> Not were they able to speak.

<span class="l-gloss">Bezhig giigido (one spoke up): “Gaawin ni-wiinzosiimin (we have no name).”</span>

9)  > **Bezhig giigido: “Gaawin ni-wiinzosiimin.”**

> One spoke up: "We have no name."

Nenaboozhoo niskaaji-giigidoo (Nenaboozhoo spoke angrily):

10) > **Nenaboozhoo niskaaji-giigidoo:**

> Nänabushu spoke in an angry way:

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniin gegii zhinaagwag (how can it be) ge-wiinzosiweg? (that you would have no name?)</span>

11) > **“Aaniin gegii zhinaagwag ge-wiinzosiweg?**

> "How is it possible for you not to have a name?

<span class="l-gloss">Giishpin dibaajimosiweg (if you do not tell) e-zhinikaazoyeg (what you are called), ganaaniiwano’inim (I will club you to death).”</span>

12) > **Giishpin dibaajimosiweg e-zhinikaazoyeg, ganaaniiwano’inim.”**

> If you do not tell me what you are called, I will club you to death."

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish aapiji ozegimaa’ (naturally he frightened them greatly); wiikaa giigidowa’ (after a long while they spoke):</span>

13) > **Aaniish aapiji ozegimaa’; wiikaa giigidowa’:**

> Naturally much did he alarm them ; after a long while they said:

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniish, goshkongeshiins (why, Little Frightener) mii i’iw e-zhinikaazoyaang (is what we are called).”</span>

14) > **“Aaniish, goshkongeshiins mii i’iw e-zhinikaazoyaang.”**

> "Why, Little Frightener is the name we are called."

<span class="l-gloss">“Oonh,” Nenaboozhoo ikido (“Oh,” said Nenaboozhoo); “mii nangwana iw\! (so that’s it\!)”</span>

15) > **“Oonh,” Nenaboozhoo ikido; “mii nangwana iw\!”**

> "Oh," Nänabushu said; "that is it\!"

Ezhi-pazigwiid Nenaboozhoo (then Nenaboozhoo rose to his feet);

16) > **ezhi-pazigwiid Nenaboozhoo;**

> Then up to his feet rose Nänabushu ;

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-nisawawaad (standing over them with legs apart) ezhi-miijinaad (he relieved himself upon them).</span>

17) > **Ezhi-nisawawaad ezhi-miijinaad.**

> standing over them with legs spread apart, he eased himself upon them.

<span class="l-gloss">Bane go gaawasibitoonid (as they floundered about in it), Nenaboozhoo oganoonaa’ (Nenaboozhoo spoke to them):</span>

18) > **Bane go gaawasibitoonid, Nenaboozhoo oganoonaa’:**

> (Observing) them suddenly groping about in the slush, Nänabushu addressed them, saying:

<span class="l-gloss">“Naapisawogo goshkongeshiins\!(2) (Yes indeed, a little frightener you are\!) Mwn\! (Phew\!)” inwe Nenaboozhoo (so said Nenaboozhoo) ezhi-gichi-baapi’aad (laughing hard at them).</span>

19) > **“Naapisawogo goshkongeshiins\!([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-winged-startlers-footnotes.html)) Mwn\!” inwe Nenaboozhoo ezhi-gichi-baapi’aad.**

> "Yes, you are a little frightener\!([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-winged-startlers-footnotes.html)) Phew\!" exclaimed Nänabushu, laughing heartily at them.

<span class="l-gloss">“Weweni wiindamawik (rightly tell her) gi-maamaa’iwaa (your mother) dagwishing (when she arrives).”</span>

20) > **“Weweni wiindamawik gi-maamaa’iwaa dagwishing.”**

> "Correctly inform your mother when she arrives."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa go iw (and so it was) e-ni-izhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo (that Nenaboozhoo went on his way);</span>

21) > **Mii sa go iw e-ni-izhi-maajaad Nenaboozhoo;**

> And so upon his way went Nänabushu;

<span class="l-gloss">Besho go eni-dagwishing (a little way along when he arrived), bane go mishawaabaminaagwadini (suddenly a wide view opened out) e-izhaad (where he was headed).</span>

22) > **Besho go eni-dagwishing, bane go mishawaabaminaagwadini e-izhaad.**

> when a little way on his journey he was come, immediately a wide view opened out, whither he was bound.

<span class="l-gloss">“Onjida maawiin (it must be on purpose) zaaga’igan medaabiiyaan (that I come out upon a lake),” inendam (he thought).</span>

23) > **“Onjida maawiin zaaga’igan medaabiiyaan,” inendam.**

> "It seems as if out upon a lake I am coming," he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Geget zaaga’igan omadaabiin (truly he came out upon a lake), aapiji sa giishkaabikaani (and there was a very steep cliff).</span>

24) > **Geget zaaga’igan omadaabiin, aapiji sa giishkaabikaani.**

> In truth, out upon a lake he came, and there was a very steep precipice.

<span class="l-gloss">Geget naawinaagwadini (truly far down it appeared) i’iw nibi (that water).</span>

25) > **Geget naawinaagwadini i’iw nibi.**

> Truly distant was the sight of the water.

<span class="l-gloss">Geget sa onizhishinini (truly it was beautiful) mii i’imaa ogidaabik (there on the top of the rock).</span>

26) > **Geget sa onizhishinini mii i’imaa ogidaabik.**

> Really beautiful was it there on the summit.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji i’imaa egookiishkaabikaanig (right there at the edge of the cliff) gaa-izhaad (when he had gone), ezhi-waaniinigaadenid Nenaboozhoo (Nenaboozhoo swung his leg out over) ezhi-giigidod (and said):</span>

27) > **Aapiji i’imaa egookiishkaabikaanig gaa-izhaad, ezhi-waaniinigaadenid Nenaboozhoo ezhi-giigidod:**

> When he had gone over to the very edge of the cliff, then about over the verge Nänabushu swung his leg, saying :

<span class="l-gloss">“Etiwe (ah), ambegish sa ataading (would that there were a wager), maamwechi go weshkiniigid ikwe (especially a youthful woman) ataading (set up as the stake), gaye go dataakojiingwaned (and that she be short from knee to groin), aapiji go gaye minogid (and very fine of figure too);</span>

28) > **“Etiwe, ambegish sa ataading, maamwechi go weshkiniigid ikwe ataading, gaye go dataakojiingwaned, aapiji go gaye minogid;**

> "Ah, would that there were a wager, and that a particularly youthful woman were up as the prize, and that she were short from the knee to the groin, and that she were of a very handsome figure \!

<span class="l-gloss">onjida maawiin nindaa-izhigwaashgon (on purpose I would jump off), awegwen ge-izhigwaashgonigwen (whoever would leap off) mii a’aw ge-wiidigemaad (he would be the one to marry her) ikidong ikwe (the woman, it is said).</span>

29) > **onjida maawiin nindaa-izhigwaashgon, awegwen ge-izhigwaashgonigwen mii a’aw ge-wiidigemaad ikidong ikwe.**

> if such could be, I would jump off, if it were said of the woman that whosoever would leap off would be the one to have her for a wife.

Gwayak igo nindaa-izhigwaashgon (truly I would jump off).”

30) > **Gwayak igo nindaa-izhigwaashgon.”**

> Actually would I jump off."

Mii go miinawaa ezhi-waaniinigaadenid (and so again he swung his leg out over).

31) > **Mii go miinawaa ezhi-waaniinigaadenid.**

> And so again he swung his leg out over the cliff.

“Onjida nindaa-izhigwaashgon (on purpose I would leap off).

32) > **“Onjida nindaa-izhigwaashgon.**

> "For a purpose would I leap off."

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa dash iwidi bine obiidawaa’ (and now over there the Ruffed Grouse brought to them) ge-miijinid oniijaanisa’ (food for her children to eat), aaniin ge-izhinawaad oniijaanisa’\! (how was she to find her children\!)</span>

33) > **Zhigwa dash iwidi bine obiidawaa’ ge-miijinid oniijaanisa’, aaniin ge-izhinawaad oniijaanisa’\!**

> And when at yonder place the Ruffed Grouse was come, fetching home to her children some food for them to eat, how was she to find her children \!

<span class="l-gloss">Etawaa (alas), wendago gaawosibitoonid (utterly were they buried in it).</span>

34) > **Etawaa, wendago gaawosibitoonid.**

> Oh, they were completely submerged in it.

“Awenesh gaa-inigaa’ineg (who has harmed you)?”

35) > **“Awenesh gaa-inigaa’ineg.”**

> "Who has done you the injury?"

“Nenaboozhoo.”

36) > **“Nenaboozhoo.”**

> "Nänabushu."

“Gegoo na gidigoowaaban? (were you told something?)”

37) > **“Gegoo na gidigoowaaban?”**

> "Were you told something?"

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniin e-zhinikaazoyeg? (How are you called?) ikido (he said).</span>

38) > **“Aaniin e-zhinikaazoyeg? ikido.**

> “By what are you called?’ he asked.

<span class="l-gloss">‘Goshkongeshiins (Little Frightener),’ nindinaanaaban (we said to him), mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-miijishiiyangid (and that was when he relieved himself upon us).</span>

39) > **‘Goshkongeshiins,’ nindinaanaaban, mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-miijishiiyangid.**

> ‘Little Frightener,’ we said to him, and thereupon was when we were eased upon.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii dash eni-maajaad (and when he was leaving) gichi-baapi’igonaan (he laughed at us greatly).</span>

40) > **Apii dash eni-maajaad gichi-baapi’igonaan.**

> And when he started away, we were very much laughed at.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningii-kanoonigonaan dash (and he spoke to us): ‘Weweni wiindamawik (rightly tell) gi-maama’iwaa (your mother) dagwishin (when she arrives).’</span>

41) > **Ningii-kanoonigonaan dash: ‘Weweni wiindamawik gi-maama’iwaa dagwishin.’**

> And we were told : Correctly inform your mother when she arrives.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw gaa-ani-zhi-maajaad (and so when he was setting out) ezhi-giigidod (he said):</span>

42) > **Mii dash i’iw gaa-ani-zhi-maajaad ezhi-giigidod.**

> And so when he was setting out, he then said:

<span class="l-gloss">‘Nepisane goshkongeshiins\! (Yes indeed, a little frightener\!)’ nindigonaan (we were told).”</span>

43) > **‘Nepisane goshkongeshiins\!’ nindigonaan.”**

> Yes, you are a little frightener\! we were told."

<span class="l-gloss">Ataaya (well), bine onawadamaan bezhig (the Ruffed Grouse took up one in her mouth) gaye odoodebinaan bezhig (and another with her claws);</span>

44) > **Ataaya, bine onawadamaan bezhig gaye odoodebinaan bezhig;**

> Well, the Ruffed Grouse took up one with her mouth and another with her claws ,

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-bazigo’od (then flying up), zaaga’iganing odizhiwinaan (to a lake she carried them) giziibiiginaad (to wash them).</span>

45) > **Ezhi-bazigo’od, zaaga’iganing odizhiwinaan giziibiiginaad.**

> then, flying up, to a lake she carried them to wash them.

Mii sa iw gaa-izhi-biini’aad (and so when she had made them clean),

46) > **Mii sa iw gaa-izhi-biini’aad,**

> And when she had made them clean,

<span class="l-gloss">mii sa gwa pane gaa-toodawaad (the same she did to each) biinish ashi-niizh dashinid (until the twelve of them).</span>

47) > **mii sa gwa pane gaa-toodawaad biinish ashi-niizh dashinid.**

> then the same thing she did to the rest until (she had finished with) the twelve.

<span class="l-gloss">Oniijanisa’ gakina gaa-izhi-gisiibiiginaad (when she had washed all her children), abaapish gaa-kiizhiitaad (by the time she had finished), ezhi-giigidod (she said):</span>

48) > **Oniijanisa’ gakina gaa-izhi-gisiibiiginaad, abaapish gaa-kiizhiitaad, ezhi-giigidod:**

> When she had made all her children clean, and by the time she was done with her work, then she said:

<span class="l-gloss">“Ambe sa (come now), ninga-noosone’waa a’aw Nenaboozhoo (I will track down that Nenaboozhoo), gaye wiin wawiyazh niwii-doodawaa (and on him too I mean to play a trick).”</span>

49) > **“Ambe sa, ninga-noosone’waa a’aw Nenaboozhoo, gaye wiin wawiyazh niwii-doodawaa.”**

> "Now, then, I am going to follow up Nänabushu, and I intend doing him a trick too."

Ezhi-maajaad omaada’anaan (when she set out, she followed his trail).

50) > **Ezhi-maajaad omaada’anaan.**

> When she started, she followed his trail.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo (by and by) mii i’imaa dayoozh odebitawaan (she came within hearing of him) medwe-gaagiigidoonid iniw Nenaboozhoowan (Nenaboozhoo, who could be heard talking).</span>

51) > **Ningoding igo mii i’imaa dayoozh odebitawaan medwe-gaagiigidoonid iniw Nenaboozhoowan.**

> So by and by she came within hearing distance of Nänabushu, who still could be heard talking.

<span class="l-gloss">Diwe (ah), onjida gosha inaabid (just at the moment she looked) na’idaa bimiwaaniinigaadenowan (right then he was swinging his leg out over).</span>

52) > **Diwe, onjida gosha inaabid na’idaa bimiwaaniinigaadenowan.**

> Ah, it was precisely at the moment she looked when he was swinging his leg out over (the precipice).

<span class="l-gloss">Bine inendam (the Ruffed Grouse thought): “Wiikaa ge miinawaa da-doodam (would that he do it once again).”</span>

53) > **Bine inendam: “Wiikaa ge miinawaa da-doodam.”**

> The Ruffed Grouse thought: "Would that he might do it again \!"

Nawaj besho ani-izhaa (nearer she went).

54) > **Nawaj besho ani-izhaa.**

> Nearer to the place she went.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa miinawaa oniinigeniwan (and now again as he made ready) wegoneniiwinen ezhi-bazigo’od (just then she flew up);</span>

55) > **Zhigwa miinawaa oniinigeniwan wegoneniiwinen ezhi-bazigo’od;**

> When again he was making ready (to swing his leg), she happened then to fly up ;

<span class="l-gloss">aapiji i’imaa jiigidawag ani-izhisewan (very close past his ear she shot by), baamaa go Nenaboozhoo gaa-initang (and then Nenaboozhoo heard): “D\!”(3)</span>

56) > **aapiji i’imaa jiigidawag ani-izhisewan, baamaa go Nenaboozhoo gaa-initang: “D\!”(3)**

> ever so near past his ear she flew, and suddenly Nänabushu heard the sound of “T\!"([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-winged-startlers-footnotes.html))

<span class="l-gloss">Aataa (oh), Nenaboozhoo dabazi (Nenaboozhoo dodged), etawaa ezhi-kakaabikised (but alas he tumbled over the cliff), anishaa go gaa-shookaanig iniw otawagan (the wind just whistling past his ears).</span>

57) > **Aataa, Nenaboozhoo dabazi, etawaa ezhi-kakaabikised, anishaa go gaa-shookaanig iniw otawagan.**

> Well, Nänabushu dodged, but unfortunately over the precipice he fell, going so fast that the wind went whistling past his ears.

<span class="l-gloss">Aataa (oh), ezhi-pangishing nibiikaang (when he fell into the water), “Dshamw,” inwewegamishin (that was the sound of his splash).</span>

58) > **Aataa, ezhi-pangishing nibiikaang, “Dshamw,” inwewegamishin.**

> Ah, when he fell into the water, "Tcam" was the sound of his fall there.

<span class="l-gloss">Aataa (oh), gichi-wiikaa bapimagoojing (a long while he hung drifting) mizhakiised (sinking down through the water).</span>

59) > **Aataa, gichi-wiikaa bapimagoojing mizhakiised.**

> Oh, for a great while was he falling through the water.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash iw gaa-mizhakiised (and so when he sank), gichi-enigok gii-ziinjigamii (with great effort he struggled in the water);</span>

60) > **Mii dash iw gaa-mizhakiised, gichi-enigok gii-ziinjigamii;**

> And so when he got up to the surface ;

<span class="l-gloss">Agaawaa mooshkamo (barely did he reach the surface), aapiji gegaa wayekwaanaamo (very nearly was he out of breath).</span>

61) > **Agaawaa mooshkamo, aapiji gegaa wayekwaanaamo.**

> barely was he able to reach the surface, to the bottom, all his strength he used in pushing back almost was he on the very point of losing his breath.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii i’iw bine namadabid (naturally there sat the Ruffed Grouse) ganawaabamaad Nenaboozhoowan (watching Nenaboozhoo).</span>

62) > **Aaniish mii i’iw bine namadabid ganawaabamaad Nenaboozhoowan.**

> Naturally there was the Ruffed Grouse seated watching Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa mooshkamowan (when he came up), deye (ah), mii go iwidi gaa-pi-inaabinid (then up from there he looked) giigidowan (saying):</span>

63) > **Zhigwa mooshkamowan, deye, mii go iwidi gaa-pi-inaabinid giigidowan:**

> When he came to the surface, ah, then up from where he fell he looked, saying:

“Iii, geget sa goshkongeshi (well, truly a frightener).”

64) > **“Iii, geget sa goshkongeshi.”**

> "Well, (that) really is a little frightener."

<span class="l-gloss">Aataa (oh), Nenaboozhoowan madwe-baapiwan (Nenaboozhoo could be heard laughing).</span>

65) > **Aataa, Nenaboozhoowan madwe-baapiwan.**

> Oh, how Nänabushu could be heard laughing\!

<span class="l-gloss">“Bine (Ruffed Grouse), mii gwayak doodawiyan (it was right what you did to me) gii-miijinagwaa igiw gi-niijaanisag (for my having relieved myself upon your children).</span>

66) > **“Bine, mii gwayak doodawiyan gii-miijinagwaa igiw gi-niijaanisag.**

> "Ruffed Grouse, it was right what you did to me for easing upon your children.

Gaawiin wiikaa miinawaa ninga-izhichigesii (never again will I do so).”

67) > **Gaawiin wiikaa miinawaa ninga-izhichigesii.”**

> Never again will I do so."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ezhi-agwaataad Nenaboozho (and so Nenaboozhoo came up out of the water), ezhi-maajaad babimosed (and set off walking about).</span>

68) > **Mii sa ezhi-agwaataad Nenaboozho, ezhi-maajaad babimosed.**

> And so when out of the water came Nänabushu, then off he started walking about.

**Footnotes :**

(1) For another version see No. 5.

(2) Naapisawogo goshkongeshiins\! “Yes, you are a little frightener\!” This could have been given in other ways; as, “Like the deuce you are a little frightener\!” “The idea of your being a little frightener\!” etc.

(3) “D\!” trill with the lips, imitating the whir of the grouse in flight.
