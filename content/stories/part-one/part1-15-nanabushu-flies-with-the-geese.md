<span class="l-fiero">**(15) Nanabush Flies with the Geese**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Fiero restoration by the Ojibwe Project / Baadwewdangig — Alan Corbiere, with Dr. Alana Johns & U-Toronto LIN458 (ojibweproject.weebly.com). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Corbiere’s Fiero and Jones’s 1919 English preserved verbatim (the source's table layout was rebuilt into clean lines); gloss line added.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding igo ani-babimosed zaaga’igan izhi-madaabiid, apane go nika’ manoominikewa’**</span>

Now, once while walking along, out upon a lake he came, and everywhere were Geese feeding on rice.

<span class="l-eng">*Ningoding igo (once, at one time) ani-babimosed (as he was walking along) zaaga’igan (a lake) izhi-madaabiid (he came out into the open toward it), apane go (everywhere, all over) nika’ (geese) manoominikewa’ (were gathering/feeding on rice).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geget sa omisawinawaa’, aaniish ezhi-ganoonaad: “Ambe sa noo, giga-wiijiiwininim,” odinaa’**</span>

Truly much he envied them, so he then addressed them saying: “I pray you, let me in among you,” he said to them.

<span class="l-eng">*Geget sa (truly indeed) omisawinawaa’ (he envied them, longed for what they had), aaniish (so, well then) ezhi-ganoonaad (the way he addressed/spoke to them): “Ambe sa noo (come now, do please), giga-wiijiiwininim (I will go along with you / let me accompany you),” odinaa’ (he said to them).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Awawa, Nenaboozhoo,” odigoo’. “Mii go i’iw akawe ozhitooyaang waa-nawapoyaang.”**</span>

“Very well, Nanabushu,” he was told. “But we first are laying in store the food that we shall eat on the way.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Awawa (very well, all right), Nenaboozhoo,” odigoo’ (he was told by them). “Mii go i’iw (it is just that) akawe (first, beforehand) ozhitooyaang (we are preparing/making) waa-nawapoyaang (the food we will eat as provisions on the journey).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aaniish ezhi-bakobiid gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo. Mii sa gaye wiin endoodaminid aano-doodang, mii sa gaawiin omikoobiigaansiin i’iw manoomin.**</span>

So into the water went Nanabushu too. And what they did he tried to do too, but he found no rice in the water.

<span class="l-eng">*Aaniish (so, well) ezhi-bakobiid (the way he went down into the water) gaye wiin (he too, also) Nenaboozhoo. Mii sa gaye wiin (and so he too) endoodaminid (what they were doing) aano-doodang (he tried in vain to do), mii sa gaawiin (and so not) omikoobiigaansiin (he did not find it by groping in the water) i’iw manoomin (the rice).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aaniin, Nenaboozhoo? indigoo gosha go gibwaanawitoon i’iw manoomin.”**</span>

“What, Nanabushu? Seemingly, indeed, are you without success in finding rice.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Aaniin (what, how is it), Nenaboozhoo? indigoo gosha go (so he was told indeed) gibwaanawitoon (you are unable to do it / without success) i’iw manoomin (the rice).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Eye’, nimbwaanawitoon.”**</span>

“Ay, I am not successful.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Eye’ (yes, ay), nimbwaanawitoon (I cannot manage it / I am without success).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aa Aw, indawaa mii iw minik. Bebangiins i’iw manoomin.” idiwaa’ i’iw nika.**</span>

“There, now, that is enough. Each (of us) now has a small bit of rice,” to one another said the Geese.

<span class="l-eng">*“Aa Aw (there now), indawaa (well then, in that case) mii iw minik (that is the amount, that is enough). Bebangiins (each a little bit, just a small bit) i’iw manoomin (the rice).” idiwaa’ (they said to one another) i’iw nika (the geese).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding igo zhigwa odigoo’: “Mii sa waabang wii-maajaayaang.” odigoo’ i’iw nika.**</span>

And presently then he was told: “Therefore on the morrow shall we expect to start,” he was told by the Geese.

<span class="l-eng">*Ningoding igo (presently, at one point) zhigwa (now) odigoo’ (he was told): “Mii sa (therefore, and so) waabang (tomorrow, on the morrow) wii-maajaayaang (we intend to start/depart).” odigoo’ (he was told) i’iw nika (by the geese).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-inaad: “Anigekaa gidaa-gashkii’siim gaye niin ji-bimiseyaambaan?”**</span>

And this he then said to them: “I want to know if you could fix me up so that I too might fly.”

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and then) gaa-inaad (what he said to them): “Anigekaa (I wonder, I should like to know) gidaa-gashkii’siim (could you manage/fix it for me) gaye niin (me too, also I) ji-bimiseyaambaan (that I might fly)?”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Nenaboozhoo, aanawi giga-gashkii’igoo ji-bimiseyan, gaawiin idash gidaa-bizindanzii i’iw ge-igooyambam.”**</span>

“Nanabushu, it is quite within our power to fix you up so that you can fly, but you would not listen to what would be told you.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Nenaboozhoo, aanawi (to be sure, even so) giga-gashkii’igoo (you can be fixed up / it can be done for you) ji-bimiseyan (that you may fly), gaawiin idash (but not) gidaa-bizindanzii (you would not listen to / heed) i’iw ge-igooyambam (that which would be told to you).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Oonh, maanoo, nishimise, izhi-ishiyok ji-bimiseyaan\!”**</span>

“Do please, my little brothers, fix me up so that I may fly\!”

<span class="l-eng">*“Oonh (oh), maanoo (do please, let it be), nishimise (my little brothers), izhi-ishiyok (do thus for me / fix me up, you all) ji-bimiseyaan (that I may fly)\!”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget aw ogii-igoon: “Ayaangwaamizin dash wiin. oo omaa aabitawaiya’ii anishinaabeg baata’iinowag.**</span>

Whereupon truly he was told: “Take care that you do it\! Off here about halfway are many people.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash geget (and so truly) aw ogii-igoon (that one told him): “Ayaangwaamizin dash wiin (do be careful, take care). oo omaa (here about) aabitawaiya’ii (at about halfway) anishinaabeg (people) baata’iinowag (are many, are numerous).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Apii imaa ani-dagwishinang, Gego inaabiken, Giga-saakwaanigonaanig. Ayaangwaamizin, gego inaabiken; mii go i’iw ji-bangishinan giishpin inaabiyan. Mii eta go i’imaa ezhi-zhaagwenimigooyan,” odigoo’.**</span>

When we are coming there, don’t you look down, for we shall be whooped at. Do you be careful, don’t you look down; for you will surely fall if you look down. That is the only thing we fear you might do,” he was told.

<span class="l-eng">*Apii imaa (when there) ani-dagwishinang (as we arrive), Gego inaabiken (do not look), Giga-saakwaanigonaanig (they will whoop/holler at us). Ayaangwaamizin (be careful), gego inaabiken (do not look); mii go i’iw (it is just that) ji-bangishinan (you will fall) giishpin inaabiyan (if you look). Mii eta go i’imaa (that is the only thing) ezhi-zhaagwenimigooyan (that we are anxious/fearful about you),” odigoo’ (he was told).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash endazhinid gakina bebezhig omiigwanini gaa-izhi-miinigod.**</span>

Accordingly by every one that was there was he given a feather.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and then) endazhinid (by every one of them present) gakina bebezhig (each and every one) omiigwanini (a feather of his) gaa-izhi-miinigod (was the one he was given by).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Daga shkomaa, goji-bazigwa’on.”**</span>

“Therefore, now, just you try to fly up.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Daga shkomaa (come now, just try), goji-bazigwa’on (try to rise up on the wing / fly up).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-bazigwa’od, gaa-izhi-giiwitaagaamesed.**</span>

Thereupon truly up he rose on the wing, then round over the lake he flew.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash geget (and so truly) gaa-izhi-bazigwa’od (the way he rose up on the wing), gaa-izhi-giiwitaagaamesed (the way he flew around over the lake).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Mii sa iw ji-wiijiiwigooyan. Mii sa waabang ji-maajaayang.”**</span>

“Therefore now you may go with us. Now, tomorrow is when we shall start.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Mii sa iw (therefore, that is it) ji-wiijiiwigooyan (that you may be taken along / go with us). Mii sa waabang (it is tomorrow) ji-maajaayang (that we shall start).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa waabanini, zhigwa bazigwa’ owa’ aapiji naawaya’ii izhinoo’amawaa.**</span>

Now was the morrow come, now was when they flew away; in the very centre was he given a place where to fly.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa waabanini (now it became morning), zhigwa bazigwa’ owa’ (now those ones rose up on the wing) aapiji naawaya’ii (right in the very middle) izhinoo’amawaa (he was shown/assigned his place there).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Mii omaa ji-bimiseyan. Nawaj igo onaagoshig ji-waabamangwaa igiw anishinaabeg.”**</span>

“It is here you are to fly. Along towards evening shall we see the people.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Mii omaa (it is here) ji-bimiseyan (that you are to fly). Nawaj igo onaagoshig (later on, toward evening) ji-waabamangwaa (we shall see them) igiw anishinaabeg (those people).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa geget oganoonigoon: “Mii zhigwa debaabandamang i’imaa ayaawaad igiw anishinaabeg.”**</span>

Sure enough, was he addressed by one saying: “There, now, are we coming into view of the place where the people are.”

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa geget (now truly) oganoonigoon (he was addressed/spoken to): “Mii zhigwa (it is now) debaabandamang (that we come into view of) i’imaa ayaawaad (the place where they are) igiw anishinaabeg (those people).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhayiigwa geget madwe-saakwaanigowaa’: “E e e, nikag\! Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa\!” madwe’inaa.**</span>

Already could they truly hear (the people) whooping at them: “Halloo, Geese\! Nanabushu is flying along with them\!” was the sound of them speaking to him.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhayiigwa geget (already truly) madwe-saakwaanigowaa’ (they could be heard whooping/hollering at them): “E e e (halloo, hey), nikag (geese)\! Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa (Nanabushu flies along with them)\!” madwe’inaa (was the sound of what was said to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii madwe-ikidowan: “Ganawaabamik, nashke gosha bangishin\!”**</span>

At last he heard them saying: “Why, look, he is really falling\!”

<span class="l-eng">*Gegapii (at last, finally) madwe-ikidowan (they could be heard saying): “Ganawaabamik (look at him, watch him), nashke gosha (why, see indeed) bangishin (he is falling)\!”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gego inaabiken, Nenaboozhoo,” odigoo i’iw nika’.**</span>

“Don’t you look, Nanabushu,” he was told by the Geese.

<span class="l-eng">*“Gego inaabiken (do not look), Nenaboozhoo,” odigoo (he was told) i’iw nika’ (by the geese).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii madwe-nagamowan:**</span>

Finally he heard them sing:

<span class="l-eng">*Gegapii (finally, at last) madwe-nagamowan (they could be heard singing)—*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa oshiimisa’, oshiimisa’. Da-bangishin. Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa oshiimisa’, oshiimisa’. Da-bangishin. Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa oshiimisa’, oshiimisa’. Da-bangishin.”**</span>

“Nanabushu flies in company with his little brothers, with his little brothers. May he fall. Nanabushu flies in company with his little brothers, with his little brothers. May he fall. Nanabushu flies in company with his little brothers, with his little brothers. May he fall.”

<span class="l-eng">*\[song, carried verbatim\] “Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa (Nanabushu flies along with them) oshiimisa’ (his little brothers), oshiimisa’ (his little brothers). Da-bangishin (may he fall / he will fall). Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa oshiimisa’, oshiimisa’. Da-bangishin. Nenaboozhoo owiijisemaa oshiimisa’, oshiimisa’. Da-bangishin.”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Daga bina, ninga- inaab,” inendam.**</span>

“I say, really, I will take a look,” he thought.

<span class="l-eng">*“Daga bina (come now, really), ninga- inaab (I will take a look),” inendam (he thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-inaabid, ataa, gichi-oodena\! Aaniin ge-initang bane go gaa-zhookaanig otawagan.**</span>

When he looked, oh, what a great town\! Then what was he to hear but a continuous whizzing in his ears.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-inaabid (the way he looked, when he looked), ataa (oh\!), gichi-oodena (a great big town)\! Aaniin ge-initang (what was he to hear) bane go (but a steady, continuous) gaa-zhookaanig (whizzing/rushing sound) otawagan (in his ears).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aano-daataaganaabid, aazha waasa owiijiiwaagana’. Aapiji naa oodena izhi-bangishin.**</span>

In vain he tried keeping his look upwards, but already afar (had gone) his companions. In the very centre of the town was where he fell.

<span class="l-eng">*Aano-daataaganaabid (in vain he tried to hold his gaze upward), aazha waasa (already far off) owiijiiwaagana’ (his companions/fellow travelers). Aapiji naa oodena (right in the middle of the town) izhi-bangishin (the way he fell).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-mamaanjigwapinind. “Ambe sa noo, miijinaadaa\!” ikidowag.**</span>

Thereupon was he then thoroughly bound with cords. “Come on, let us ease ourselves upon him\!” they said.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and then) gaa-izhi-mamaanjigwapinind (the way he was bound up tight with cords). “Ambe sa noo (come on now), miijinaadaa (let us relieve ourselves upon him)\!” ikidowag (they said).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa geget gaa-izhi-miijinind, gakina minik imaa waadetoonid omiijinigoo.à**</span>

Whereupon truly was he then eased upon, by everyone there in the town was he eased upon.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa geget (and so truly) gaa-izhi-miijinind (the way he was relieved upon), gakina minik (all of them, the whole number) imaa waadetoonid (those dwelling there in the town) omiijinigoo (relieved themselves upon him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Abaapish zhigwa negashkijinigod zhiipidoonenid.**</span>

In the course of time he became engulfed so deep in the dung that he had to purse his lips.

<span class="l-eng">*Abaapish zhigwa (in the course of time, by and by) negashkijinigod (as he was buried/engulfed by it) zhiipidoonenid (so that he pursed/drew in his lips).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Awiiya onoondawaa: “Aandi gaa-miijinind?” ikidowa’.**</span>

Somebody he heard (asking): “Where is he who was eased upon?” they were saying.

<span class="l-eng">*Awiiya (somebody) onoondawaa (he heard): “Aandi (where) gaa-miijinind (the one who was relieved upon)?” ikidowa’ (they were saying).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Goniginiin, mindimooyeya’, niishiwa gagiibiingwewa’.**</span>

Lo, (there were) some old women, two that were blind.

<span class="l-eng">*Goniginiin (lo\! behold), mindimooyeya’ (old women), niishiwa (two of them) gagiibiingwewa’ (who were blind).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aandi gaa-miijinind?” Bi-ikidonid.**</span>

“Where is he that was eased upon?” they said as they came.

<span class="l-eng">*“Aandi (where) gaa-miijinind (the one relieved upon)?” Bi-ikidonid (they said as they came along).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-ganoonaad: “Omaa nindayaa.” odinaa’. Zhigwa odoodisigo.**</span>

Then he spoke to them, saying: “Here I am,” he said to them. Then to where he was they came.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-ganoonaad (the way he spoke to them): “Omaa nindayaa (here I am),” odinaa’ (he said to them). Zhigwa odoodisigo (then they came to where he was).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhayiigwa owii-miijinigoon iniw mindimooyeyan, ezhi-ganoonaad: “ish,” odinaan; “Gaagishwiin, gosha mii go i’iw waa-izhi-miijishiyan? Akawe niin iko nindaapaa’ogo waa-miijinigowaanin.”**</span>

Already was he desired by an old woman to ease herself upon when he addressed her: “Wait,” he said to her; “really, now, is this the position you mean to relieve yourself upon me? Usually I am first unbound before I am relieved upon.”

<span class="l-eng">*Zhayiigwa (already) owii-miijinigoon (he was about to be relieved upon) iniw mindimooyeyan (by that old woman), ezhi-ganoonaad (the way he addressed her): “ish (wait, hold on),” odinaan (he said to her); “Gaagishwiin (really now, is it so), gosha mii go i’iw (is this indeed) waa-izhi-miijishiyan (the way you mean to relieve yourself on me)? Akawe niin iko (usually I first) nindaapaa’ogo (am unbound, untied) waa-miijinigowaanin (before I am relieved upon).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Oonh,” odigoon. “Gegaa gi-wanidoodawaanaan a’aw gaa-miijinind.”**</span>

“Really,” he was told. “Nearly did we do a mistake to him who has been eased upon.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Oonh (oh, really),” odigoon (he was told). “Gegaa (nearly, almost) gi-wanidoodawaanaan (we did wrong by / made a mistake with) a’aw gaa-miijinind (that one who was relieved upon).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa geget ezhi-aabiskwamigod ganawaabamaad; gaa-mamoowaabidenid igo iw mindimooyeya’.**</span>

And so truly, while being unbound, was he watching them; (he saw that) the old women had dung in their teeth.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa geget (and so truly) ezhi-aabiskwamigod (the way he was being unbound/untied) ganawaabamaad (while watching them); gaa-mamoowaabidenid igo (that they had it caught in their teeth) iw mindimooyeya’ (those old women).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gaa-kiizhiikaagod ezhi-naaniiwanowaad.**</span>

When he was set free, then he clubbed them to death, first one, then the other.

<span class="l-eng">*Gaa-kiizhiikaagod (when he was finished being freed/loosed) ezhi-naaniiwanowaad (the way he clubbed them to death, one after another).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Eni-izhi-maajaad o’omaa.**</span>

Then on his way he continued from this place.

<span class="l-eng">*Eni-izhi-maajaad (the way he went off, departed) o’omaa (from this place, here).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ani-babimosed zaaga’igan omadaabiin, mii go iw ezhi-bakobiid ezhi-giziibiigid.**</span>

As he went walking along, out upon a lake he came, whereupon into the water he went (and) washed himself.

<span class="l-eng">*Ani-babimosed (as he walked along) zaaga’igan (a lake) omadaabiin (he came out into the open toward it), mii go iw (and so) ezhi-bakobiid (the way he went into the water) ezhi-giziibiigid (and washed himself).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ganawaabandang i’iw nibi mii go iw ezhi-bimangodenig i’iw moo.**</span>

While looking at the water, he then saw the dung floating thereon.

<span class="l-eng">*Ganawaabandang i’iw nibi (while looking at the water) mii go iw (and so) ezhi-bimangodenig (the way it was floating along/drifting) i’iw moo (the dung).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gaa agwaasiid mii dash ekidod: “Ge-izhinikaadamowaad anishinaabeg nizhisheyag, Wiinibiig\!’ Da-izhinikaade iw zaaga’igan.”**</span>

When he came out of the water , this then he said: “The name which the people my uncles shall call it – ‘ah, filthy water\! such shall be the name of the lake.”

<span class="l-eng">*Gaa agwaasiid (when he came up out of the water) mii dash ekidod (then this he said): “Ge-izhinikaadamowaad (the name they shall give it) anishinaabeg nizhisheyag (the people, my uncles), Wiinibiig (Filthy-Water, dirty water)\!’ Da-izhinikaade iw zaaga’igan (such shall be the name of that lake).”*</span>
