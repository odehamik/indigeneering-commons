19\. Nanabushu and the Caribou.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Midaasoganzh (probable) — Part I Series 2 (Nos. 19–32), Bois Fort, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Nänabucusa pạpimusä; ningutingigu pạpimusät sībins umạtạbī; ā‘pidcisa mạckusi‘kāni. Inābit ạdi‘kwạn pimimạdāpiwạn; nibiwa äckạnaᵉ udayāwäni. Kwaya‘kigu pimiạiyawakusiwäwạn, mīdạc i∙i′∙ᵘ äjipīpāgimät: “Nīcimicä, a‘kawä, kīwīwâpạmin\!”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Naanabozhoo sa babimose; ningoding igo babimosed ziibiins omadaabii; aapiji sa mashkosiikaani. Inaabid adikwan bimimadaabiiwan; niibiwa eshkanan odayaawaan. Gwayak igo bimi’ayawakozhiwewan, mii dash i’iw ezhi-biibaagimaad: “Nishiimishe, akawe, gii-wiiwaabamin\!”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Now, Nänabushu was travelling about; now, once while travelling about, he came out upon a brook; an exceedingly broad meadow was there. While looking around, (he saw) a caribou moving out upon the meadow; many the prongs he had on his antlers. And straight across the meadow was (the caribou) moving, whereupon (Nänabushu) called to him with a loud voice: “O my younger brother\! wait, I want to see you\!”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Naanabozhoo sa (now, Nänabushu) ∙ babimose (was walking about); ningoding igo babimosed (once while walking about) ∙ ziibiins omadaabii (he came out upon a brook); aapiji sa mashkosiikaani (an exceedingly broad meadow it was). Inaabid (as he looked) ∙ adikwan bimimadaabiiwan (a caribou moving out into the open); niibiwa eshkanan odayaawaan (many prongs he had — many horns he had). Gwayak igo (and straight) ∙ bimi’ayawakozhiwewan (it was moving across the meadow), mii dash i’iw ezhi-biibaagimaad (whereupon he called to him): “Nishiimishe (my little younger brother), ∙ akawe (wait), ∙ gii-wiiwaabamin (I want to look at you)\!”</span>

<span class="l-eng">*(1) Nîbiwa äckạnaᵉ udayāwäni, “many the prongs he had on his antlers;” literally, “many the horns he had.”*</span>

<span class="l-orig">(2) Kägä‘t kibi‘tcikāpawiwạn.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geget gibitchikaabawiwan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Truly he stopped (and) stood.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Geget (truly) ∙ gibitchikaabawiwan (he stopped and stood still).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(3) Änicinäsi‘kawät; payäcwâbạmät ọgạnōnän: “Kägä‘t sinägō kinisitiwạg ininiwibạnīg, mīgū ∙i∙ᵘ tābita kinisitiwät. Kägä‘t sạnagat. Nīnạnaginiwänäbạn, mīdạcigu iᵘ piminicimoyän. O∙o∙wisagu tōtạmōbạnīg.” Odōtā‘pinäni Nänabucōwạn īni‘ᵘ umi‘tigwābīni; äci‘kwa‘täwänit; kā∙ī∙‘kwa‘täwänit ubi‘kwa‘kuni, cigwasa unạnīmā‘kwīnu‘tāgōn. Cigwadạc kīgitōwạn: “Mīsa o nāsāb ändōdamowā‘pạn.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Eni-zhiikawaad; bayeshwaabamaad oganoonaan: “Geget zhinaagong giniziwag ininiwibaniig, mii go i’iw daabita giniziwaad. Geget zanagad. Niinaaginiwenebaan, mii dash igo iw bimini-zhimoyaan. O’ow sa go doodamoobaniig.” Odoodaapinaan Naanabozhoowan iniw omitigwaabiini; ezhi-gwatewaanid; gaa-iikwatewaanid obikwakoni, zhigwa sa onaniimaakwiino’taagoon. Zhigwa dash giigidoowan: “Mii sa o naasaab endoodamowaaban.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Then (Nänabushu) walked over to where he was; when he was in close view of him, he addressed him, saying: “Truly, a wicked time is going on at the place from whence I come, from a town is where I come. Day before yesterday were the men killing one another, actually on both sides were they killing one another. It is truly desperate. I tried to stop the fight, and then I fled away. Thus were they doing at the time.” He took Nänabushu’s bow; (then he aimed at him); when he had aimed (he set) the arrow, and then he drew the bow upon him. And now (Nänabushu) spoke: “This is the same as what they did before.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-zhiikawaad (then he walked over to him); bayeshwaabamaad (when he was close in view of him) ∙ oganoonaan (he spoke to him): “Geget zhinaagong (truly at the place I come from) ∙ giniziwag ininiwibaniig (the men were being killed), mii go i’iw daabita giniziwaad (so it was, on both sides they were being killed). Geget zanagad (truly it is hard / desperate). Niinaaginiwenebaan (I tried to stop them), mii dash igo iw bimini-zhimoyaan (and so I fled away). O’ow sa go doodamoobaniig (this is what they were doing).” Odoodaapinaan iniw omitigwaabiini (he took his — Nänabushu’s — bow); ezhi-gwatewaanid (he aimed at him); gaa-iikwatewaanid obikwakoni (when he had aimed the arrow), zhigwa sa onaniimaakwiino’taagoon (now he drew the bow on him). Zhigwa dash giigidoowan (and now (Nänabushu) spoke): “Mii sa o naasaab endoodamowaaban (this is the same as what they did before).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(4) Tä‘tiwä, äcipimugut, wäntcitạgu wäcä‘pīt u‘tininägōn. Änicnä piwâbi‘k sägāpi‘kisini i∙i∙mä ubi‘kwa‘kuning. Anukwäskuni a∙a∙wisa ạdi‘k wändcitạgu ä‘kōnāmut; mīsạgu iᵘ kinibut.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Daa’tiwe, ezhi-bimood, wenjida go wezhaabiid o’iniinaagoon. Aaniish ina biwaabik zaagaabikizini i’imaa obikwakoning. Aano-gwaaskoni a’aw sa adik wenjida go ekonaamod; mii sa go iw ginibod.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Poor creature\! when he was shot by (Nänabushu), right in the side behind the shoulder was he shot. According to the story, a metal was sticking out of the arrow. In vain did the caribou try to leap away, even till he could breathe no more; and then he died.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Daa’tiwe (poor creature\!), ezhi-bimood (when he was shot), wenjida go wezhaabiid (right in the side behind the shoulder) ∙ o’iniinaagoon (he was shot). Aaniish ina (according to the story) ∙ biwaabik zaagaabikizini (a metal was sticking out) ∙ i’imaa obikwakoning (there in the arrow). Aano-gwaaskoni a’aw sa adik (in vain the caribou leaped) ∙ wenjida go ekonaamod (until he could breathe no more); mii sa go iw ginibod (and so then he died).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(5) Tayā, Nänabucu gägä‘t minwäntạm wäntcitạgu wininōwạn ini‘ᵘ udadi‘kuman. Āᵉ, mīsạgu cigwa mädä‘kawät wininu∙ā∙t. A‘kawä, udạgwäwānā‘k udōci∙tōn; kā‘kīci‘tōd mīdạc imä ạni∙a∙gōtōd ‘i‘i′ᵘ uwīninōn. Pạbā‘pic kā‘kīci‘kawät ā‘pidcisa pa‘kạdä. “Pāmāgu wawāni gīci‘tāyän ningawīsin,” inạndạm. Wīyāsi dạc mīnawā ābi∙ta pimidä udcībā‘kwädän. Cigwasa kā‘kīcitänik, “Mīsa cigwa tcimādạntcigäyän,” inändạm. Wigwās unạntawâbạndän mīdạc imä äcägwā∙a∙nk. Kāyagwā∙a∙nk Nänabucu kīgito: “Ä‘tawä, ningakīwīyagạntän. A‘pägicsa i‘kwätug witō‘pạmag ā‘pidcigu tatä‘kwutcīngwạnät.” Tä, cigwa upa‘kwäcän ‘i‘i′ᵘ winin. “Kägä‘tsa ningawī∙a∙ngạtän.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Daayaa, Naanabozhoo geget minwendam wenjida go wininoowan iniw odadikoman. Aa, mii sa go zhigwa madekawed wininiwaad. Akawe, odagwewaanaak odoozhitoon; gaa-giizhitood mii dash imaa ani-agotood i’iw owininoon. Babaa’pich gaa-giizhikawed aapiji sa bakade. “Baamaa go wawaani giizhitaayaan ninga-wiisin,” inendam. Wiiyaas idash miinawaa aabita bimide odachiibaakwaadaan. Zhigwa sa gaa-giizhitenig, “Mii sa zhigwa ji-maadanjigeyaan,” inendam. Wiigwaas onandawaabandaan mii dash imaa ezhi-agwaa’ank. Gaa-yagwaa’ank Naanabozhoo giigido: “Aatawe, ninga-giiwiiyagandaan. Apegizh sa ikwetog wii-doopamag aapiji go da-dekwajiingwanaad.” Daa, zhigwa obakweshan i’iw winin. “Geget sa ninga-wii’aangadaan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Oh, now truly pleased was Nänabushu that so unusually fat his caribou should be. Ah\! and then was when he began work upon it, cutting up its meat. First, he made a roasting-rack; when he had finished it, he thereupon hung up his fat. By the time he had finished his work on (the caribou), very hungry was he. “Not till I have put (things) in good order will I eat,” he thought. So some meat partly fat he cooked. And when it was done cooking, “It is now time that I eat,” he thought. For (some) birch-bark he sought, upon which he spread out (what he had cooked). After Nänabushu had dipped it out of (the kettle), he said: “Ah\! but I shall spoil it by eating it (alone). Would that I might eat with a woman who was short from groin to knee\!” However, presently he sliced off a piece of the fat. “Verily, I shall spoil it by eating it (alone).”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Daayaa (oh\!), Naanabozhoo geget minwendam (Nänabushu was truly glad) ∙ wenjida go wininoowan (that so very fat it was) ∙ iniw odadikoman (that caribou of his). Aa, mii sa go zhigwa madekawed (so then he began work on it) ∙ wininiwaad (cutting up the meat). Akawe (first), odagwewaanaak odoozhitoon (he made a roasting-rack); gaa-giizhitood (when he had finished it) ∙ mii dash imaa ani-agotood i’iw owininoon (then he hung up his fat there). Babaa’pich gaa-giizhikawed (by the time he had worked on it) ∙ aapiji sa bakade (he was very hungry). “Baamaa go wawaani giizhitaayaan (not until I have put things in order) ∙ ninga-wiisin (will I eat),” inendam (he thought). Wiiyaas idash miinawaa aabita bimide (and some meat half fat) ∙ odachiibaakwaadaan (he cooked). Zhigwa sa gaa-giizhitenig (and when it was cooked), “Mii sa zhigwa ji-maadanjigeyaan (now it is time I begin eating),” inendam. Wiigwaas onandawaabandaan (birch-bark he sought) ∙ mii dash imaa ezhi-agwaa’ank (and there he dipped it out). Gaa-yagwaa’ank Naanabozhoo giigido (when Nänabushu had dipped it out, he said): “Aatawe (too bad), ninga-giiwiiyagandaan (I shall spoil it eating alone). Apegizh sa ikwetog wii-doopamag (would that I might eat with a woman) ∙ aapiji go da-dekwajiingwanaad (one short from groin to knee).” Daa, zhigwa obakweshan i’iw winin (presently he sliced off a piece of the fat). “Geget sa ninga-wii’aangadaan (truly I shall spoil it eating alone).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(6) Mīsa‘ upạgitinän Nänabucu wäca‘kamu‘pạn. “Kägä‘tsa ningīkagwänisạginawämigun.” Wäwīp äcipa‘kwäcạnk ‘i‘i′ᵘ mīsa ‘i‘i′ᵘ äjita‘kwạmigut ini‘ᵘ mi‘tigōn; payä‘ta∙u∙cigo pīdänimạtini. Kwätcigu pämi‘ku‘pīnit äjipīpāgimāt: “Nī‘tcīnistcä\!” udinäᵉ.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa’ obagidinaan Naanabozhoo wesha’kamoban. “Geget sa ningii-gagwenizaginawemigon.” Wewiib ezhi-bakweshank i’iw mii sa i’iw ezhi-dakwamigod iniw mitigoon; bayeta-o-zhi go biidenimaadini. <span class="l-eng">*And so Nänabushu let go of that which he had been chewing. “Truly, did I almost gnaw it off\!” Quickly when he had bitten off (the meat), then was when his hand was caught by the wood (of the rack); only towards (himself) was he able to draw it. Now, accordingly there he hung. Naturally he tried hard, but he could not free his hand. Now, once while he was hanging there, looking towards the place whence he had come afoot, he saw the Wolves coming over the rise; another, (and) still another came straight on out into the meadow. As soon as they were coming near, then he called aloud to them: “My little brothers\!” he said to them.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa’ obagidinaan (and so he let it go) ∙ Naanabozhoo wesha’kamoban (Nänabushu, what he had been chewing). “Geget sa ningii-gagwenizaginawemigon (truly I almost gnawed it off).” Wewiib ezhi-bakweshank i’iw (quickly when he had bitten it off) ∙ mii sa i’iw ezhi-dakwamigod iniw mitigoon (then his hand was caught by the wood); bayeta-o-zhi go biidenimaadini (only toward himself could he draw it). Aaniish mii sa i’iw ayagoozid (so there he hung). Aaniish aanawi odanawi-gwajitoon (he tried hard in vain) ∙ gaawiin ogashkitoosiin i’iw oninj (he could not free his hand). Ningoding igo ayagoozid (once while hanging there) ∙ inaabid gaa-bi-ondozed (looking toward where he had come from), owaabamaan ma’iinganan bimataabiskoziwenid (he saw wolves coming over the rise); miinawaa bezhig (another), geyaabi bezhig (and still another) ∙ gwayak igo bimi’ayawashkoziwewan (came straight out into the meadow). Gwech igo bemi-go-biinid (as soon as they were coming near) ∙ ezhi-biibaagimaad (he called to them): “Niitchiinishe (my little younger brother)\!” odinaan (he said to them).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(7) Kibi‘tcikāpawiwaᵉ. Änic ‘a∙a∙widac a‘kiwäⁿzīma∙i∙ngạn ọganōnä ‘i‘i′ᵘ unidcänisaᵉ: “Mīsa‘ aᵘ Nänabucu kāgạnōninạnk. Kāgō ugīni‘tōnätug i∙i∙mä kibāskinawät. Kāgōdạc iciwābisitug ‘i‘i′mä kī∙a∙gōsit. ‘A∙āⁿ, mädcädā′, ijätā′ ‘i‘i′mä kibāskinawät.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gibitchikaabawiwan. Aaniish a’awidash akiwenziima’iingan oganoonaan i’iw onijaanisan: “Mii sa’ a’aw Naanabozhoo gaaganooninang. Gegoo ogiini’toonedog i’imaa giba-zhkinawed. <span class="l-gloss">Gibitchikaabawiwan (they stopped and stood). <span class="l-orig">(9) Änīn kā∙ī∙cinạmowāt ma∙i∙ngạnạg pạnāgu ạdi‘kwạn nạnawicigāsunit. Änīc kā∙i∙nābạmäwāt, mīsạgu cigwa mätạmāwāt. Kāwīn kạnagä nōmạg kitạcitāsiwạg ‘i‘i′ᵘ kī‘kitạmwāwāt. Ā‘pidcigu wäwīp cigwa ạnimādcäwạg.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aaniin gaa-izhinamowaad ma’iinganag apane go adikwan nanawizhigaazonid. Aaniish gaa-inaabamewaad, mii sa go zhigwa medamaawaad. Gaawiin ganage noomag gida-zhitaasiwag i’iw gii-gidamwaawaad. Aapiji go wewiib zhigwa ani-maajaawag.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*What should the Wolves have appear but a vast store of caribou already prepared. Now, they had nothing to gain by simply looking upon it, so thereupon they set to work eating it. <span class="l-gloss">Aaniin gaa-izhinamowaad ma’iinganag (what did the wolves see) ∙ apane go adikwan nanawizhigaazonid (nothing but caribou all prepared). <span class="l-orig">(11) Ma∙i∙ngạnạg äcitä‘taganäbiwāt, ä‘tiwä, änīn äcinạmowāt pạnāgu winin agōtäni. Wäntcitạgu kāmạma‘kantiwād midciwād. Kāwīn kạnagä nōmạg kītạci‘tāsiwạg ki‘kitạmōwāt. <span class="l-fiero">**Ma’iinganag ezhi-da-daganaabiwaad, aatiwe, aaniin ezhinamowaad apane go winin agotenid. Wenjida go gaa-mamakaadendiwaad midjiwaad. Gaawiin ganage noomag giidazhi’taasiwag gii-gidamoowaad. Ezhi-niisandawed, aatawe Naanabozhoo geget goopadendam. Ezhi-gichi-mawid, aaniish aapiji wii-wiisini. Indawaa i’iwe miskwiwakamigenig mii iw idinong mwezhaganjiged. Aapiji go wasi’taawi. “Ambegizh sa’ gashkitooyaan i’iw ji-ginebigoonsiwiyaan.” Aaniigok owiikwajitoon i’iw wii-ginebigoonsiwid. Geget ogashkitoon i’iw ginebigoonsiwid, mii sa dash iw bitchinag minwiid i’iw wiisinid. Aaniiwek ani-detewiisini. Mii dash iw oshtigwaan aaniiwek i’iw bazigaanagatinig aaniiwek odoonjimii’ken i’iw bimide. Megwaa go i’iw widi wiisini bazigaanagatinig, aatiwe, ezhi-noonde’aa-anishinaabewid. Wendago imaa ogadigweng mii imaa ezhi-da-oozod.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*As the Wolves looked up, why, what were they to behold but a great heap of fat hanging aloft. What truly amazed them was what they ate.
