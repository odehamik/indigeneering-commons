3\. NĀNABUSHU SLAYS HIS YOUNGER BROTHER.

<span class="l-fiero">**Nenabozho Gii-nisaad Oshiimeyan**</span>

Nanabushu Slays His Younger Brother

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Waasaagoneshkang (Part I Series 1, Bois Fort — per Corbiere roster; not printed at story head, attribution per series rubric)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Ānīc mīsai iᵘ cigwa kī·o·tickutämiwāt. Ānīc mīsai kā·i·jiwāwītigämāt īni′ᵘ ō‘kumisạn wīn kāwi‘kā pizān kī·a·yāsinōn wuᵋō ki‘tcigạmi. Wâbōs nämatạpit ijini‘kātätug woᵋo˙ ki‘tcigạmi. Mīdạc a‘pạnä mōjạg ‘iᵋiwidi kīa′winạmạtạpit. O·o·widạc ogī·i·nān īni′ᵘ ō‘kumisạn: “Ambäsạnō, nō‘kō, mī·i′·ᵘ mini‘k käwâbōⁿsōyān.” Mīdạc ‘iᵋ·i·widi ki‘tciyānäyābi‘kāgwän, mīdạc i·i·widi ugitạpi‘k panä kī·u·nạbit. Mīdạc kā·i·cināgwa‘k ‘iᵋi′ᵘ ạsin. Mīdạc kā·i·kitut: “Wâbōs nämadạbit uga·i·cini‘kātānāwa ogūᵘ ạnicinābäg.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aanish miisa-i’iw gizhwa gii-odishkodademiwaad. Aanish miisa-i’iw gaa-izhi-waawiitigamaad iniw ookomisan wiin gaawiikaa bizaan gii-ayaasinoon wewe(?) gichigami. Waabooz namadabid izhinikaadedog wewe(?) gichigami. Miidash apane moozhag i’iwidi gii-aa-winamadabid. Oo-owidash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan: "Ambesano, nookoo, mii-i’iw minik gewaaboonzoyaan." Miidash i’iwidi gichi-aayaanebi’agwaan(?), miidash i’iwidi ogidaabi’k bane gii-onabid. Miidash gaa-izhi-inaagwa’k i’iw asin. Miidash gaa-izhi-gidod: "Waabooz namadabid oga-izhinikaadaanaawaa ogow anishinaabeg."**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Now, at the time when the people were given knowledge (lit. set in their ways). And so it was that, after he had lived there with his grandmother, never had this sea ever been quiet. A Hare was seated as it were upon this sea. And so it was that always continually was he wont to sit gazing upon (the water). Then once he said to his grandmother: "Pray, my grandmother, that is as long as I shall (live a) Hare." And so over there a high precipitous rock was, and there upon (it) used he forever to sit. And so it had the appearance of a rock. Whereupon he said: "A Hare seated they will name yonder rock by these people."*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aanish miisa-i’iw gizhwa (now, that is the time) · gii-odishkodademiwaad (when they were given their way of life) · gaa-izhi-waawiitigamaad iniw ookomisan (after he stayed there with his grandmother) · wiin gaawiikaa bizaan gii-ayaasinoon gichigami (the great water was never calm) · waabooz namadabid izhinikaadedog gichigami (a Hare sits there, so the great water is named, it seems) · miidash apane moozhag (and so always, continually) · gii-aa-winamadabid (he kept sitting and gazing) · ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (he said to his grandmother) · "ambesano, nookoo" (pray, grandmother) · "mii-i’iw minik gewaaboonzoyaan" (that is how long I shall be a Hare) · i’iwidi gichi-... ogidaabi’k bane gii-onabid (over there on the high rock he always sat) · gaa-izhi-inaagwa’k i’iw asin (so it looked like that rock) · "waabooz namadabid oga-izhinikaadaanaawaa ogow anishinaabeg" (these people will call it ’where the Hare sits’)</span>

*Printed footnote on p.14: "¹ That is, as if he had been scorched by fire." (refers to käwâbōⁿsōyān, the Hare’s color)*

<span class="l-orig">(2) Ānīc mīsa iᵘ cīgwa kī·ạ·nicināpäwit. Kāwīn käyābͥ kī·a·binōtci·ī·wisī. Wu·ō· ugī·i·nān īni′ᵘ ō‘kumisạn: “Kigi‘känimîna ayāwiyān?”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aanish miisa-i’iw gizhwa gii-anishinaabewid. Gaawiin geyaabi gii-abinoojii-wisii. Wewe(?) ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan: "Gigikenimin na ayaawiyaan?"**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*So, therefore, were they then in possession of fire. So now afterwards, while he lived with his grandmother, never still had this sea perhaps been quiet... \[English facing, p.15\] Now longer was he a child. This he said to his grandmother: "Do you know who I am?"*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aanish miisa-i’iw gizhwa gii-anishinaabewid (now this is when he became a person) · gaawiin geyaabi gii-abinoojii-wisii (no longer was he a child) · ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (he said to his grandmother) · "gigikenimin na ayaawiyaan?" (do you know who I am?)</span>

<span class="l-orig">(6) Ōᵋ·ō·widec ogī·i·nān: “Ạmbäsanō mānū wīndạmawicin,” ogī·i·nān īni′ᵘ ō‘kumisạn. “Kunigä ningi·o·o·simitug\!”</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And this he said to her: "I wish that you would please tell me," he said to his grandmother. "I wonder if I may have a father\!"*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**"Aye’," ogii-igoon; "gaawiin gii-naagozisii awegwen a’aw gaa-oosiyan," ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan. Oo-owidash ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan: "Ginibo a’aw ogii’-ayaambon: miisa-gu-i’iw ezhi-wiindamoonaan. Gaawiin gidaa-gaagiidamosinoon(?)."**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*"Yes," he was told. "They could not see whoever he was that was your father," he was told by his grandmother. And this he was told by his grandmother: "Dead is she who was your mother: that is all I can tell you. Not would I hide it from you."*</span>

<span class="l-orig">(8) Oᵋ·o·widạc ogī·i·nān ‘aᵋa′ᵘ Nänabucu: “Ānīn käkicināgwa‘k käpäciguyān? kägātạmawiyạn,” ogī·i·nān īni′ᵘ ō‘kumisạn. Mīdạc cayīgwa kī·i·‘kitut: “Ānīn wändci·i·citcigäyạn ‘iᵋi′ᵘ kātạmawiyạn ‘iᵋiwisa kā·i·jiwäbisiyāng? Kāwīn pō‘tc niwạnändazin i·i·wisa kā·i·jiwäbisiyāng. Ayāwagîsa nintinändạm niⁿtcikiwäyạng. <span class="l-fiero">**Oo-owidash ogii-inaan a’aw Nenabozho: "Aaniin gekizhinaagwa’k gebezhigoyaan? gegaadamawiyan," ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan. Miidash zayiigwa gii-ikidod: "Aaniin wenji-izhichigeyan i’iw gaadamawiyan i’iwisa gaa-izhiwebiziyaang? Gaawiin booch niwanendaziin i’iwisa gaa-izhiwebiziyaang. Ayaawagisa nindinendam ninjikiwenyang(?). Maanoo daga wiindamawishin i’iwisa gaa-izhiwebiziyaang."**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And this he said to her, said Nänabushu: "Is she who was your mother: that is all I can tell you. Not would I hide it from you." Thereupon frightened became the old woman. So this she said to her grandson: "Well, I will tell you about it. Of a truth were you not alone at the time when you (and they) were born. As true as I speak,¹ this was what happened to you (and them): you (and they) killed your mother at the time when you (and they) were born. Verily, had I not carried out the purpose³ of my mind, I could never have reared you."*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">ogii-inaan a’aw Nenabozho (Nenabozho said to her) · "aaniin gekizhinaagwa’k gebezhigoyaan?" (how does it appear that I am alone?) · "gegaadamawiyan" (that you keep it from me) · miidash zayiigwa gii-ikidod (and then he said) · "aaniin wenji-izhichigeyan i’iw gaadamawiyan..." <span class="l-fiero">**Miidash gii-zegimigod a’aw mindimooye. Oo-owidish ogii-inaan iniw ozhishenyan: "Aanish, gigawiindamoon, gegget gaawiin gigii-bezhigozii i’iwisa gii-niigiyeg. <span class="l-orig">(10) O·ō·widạc ogī·i·nān īni′ᵘ ō‘kumisạn: “Ōⁿ, mīnạngwana ‘iᵋi′ᵘ kā·i·jiwäbisiyān ‘iᵋi′ᵘ kānīgiyān\! <span class="l-fiero">**Oo-owidash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan: "Oon, miinangwana i’iw gaa-izhiwebiziyaan i’iw gaa-niigiyaan\! Aanish, gaawiin niin ningii-nisaasii a’aw ninga. <span class="l-eng">*So thereupon started they to fight with each other, and then soon were they shooting at each other. While Nänabushu was being forced back, he came to where his arrows were, there where he had concealed them. Thereupon back from that place in turn he chased by the other, and again back to where he had concealed his arrows when he was driven. Whereupon in turn from that place again he was driven back, once more he came to the place of his arrows. And so there he thought: "It is possible that I may be vanquished." <span class="l-eng">*"Yea, overmuch (and) too soon will this earth fill up. Where will live the people who in after time are to be born? Now, therefore, this is what shall come to pass, that people are to die. So, therefore, this is why I have brought it about that people should die, that people who later on are to come into being may have room to fill this earth, this earth which I have created. Although we could bring it to pass that not till they have reached old age should they die, yet nowhere would they have room if this should take place. Therefore this is how it shall come to pass that while they are yet in infancy they shall die. Such, therefore, is what I now see. It is the same thing that will happen to them who in the future are to live, and like unto what is now happening to you. It is only a change of going from one earth to another. To you where you are shall come they who shall cease to live (here)."*</span>
