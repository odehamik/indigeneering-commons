<span class="l-gloss">(14) Nanabushu and the Caribou ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-caribou-footnotes.html))</span>

Mii eni-izhi-maamaajaad (thereupon he kept right on moving along).

1)  > **Mii eni-izhi-maamaajaad.**

> Thereupon he continued steadily on his way.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo ani-babimosed (now, once as he was walking along), ji-mishawashkosiwagaani wedidang (he came to where there was a wide field of high grass).</span>

2)  > **Ningoding igo ani-babimosed, ji-mishawashkosiwagaani wedidang.**

> Now, once while he was walking along, to a wide field of high grass he came.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-inaabid i’iwidi wayekwaashkosiw (as he looked off to the far end of the meadow), gichi-ayaabe adikwan (a big bull caribou) gii-pimadaabiishkosiwewan (came walking out along the grass).</span>

3)  > **Ezhi-inaabid i’iwidi wayekwaashkosiw gichi-ayaabe adikwan gii-pimadaabiishkosiwewan.**

> As he looked toward the other end of the meadow, (he saw) a big bull Caribou come walking out upon the stretch of grass,

Geget sa omisawenimaan (and truly he wanted him).

4)  > **Geget sa omisawenimaan.**

> and so he (Nanabushu) wanted to get him.

“Amanj igo’sh ge-inaapinanaawagipanen?” (“wonder how I could get hold of him\!”)

5)  > **“Amanj igo’sh ge-inaapinanaawagipanen?”**

> “Wonder how can I get hold of him\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-waabamigod (when he was seen by him), “Indash iidog ge-pasemid,” (“without fail he will have something to say to me,”) inendam aw adik (thought the caribou).</span>

6)  > **Ezhi-waabamigod, “Indash iidog ge-pasemid,” inendam aw adik.**

> When (Nanabushu) was seen, “Without fail he will have something to say to me,” thought the Caribou.

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga ndawaa ninga-ashimaa,” (“come, I think I will draw him on,”) ani-maajiibatoo a’aw adik (off running started the caribou).</span>

7)  > **“Daga ndawaa ninga-ashimaa,” ani-maajiibatoo a’aw adik.**

> “I think I will draw him on.” Off running started the Caribou.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa geget omadwe-kanoonigoon (then truly the voice of the other was heard calling to him): “Ai, ni-shiimisa, (“hey, my little brother,) amanj wiin i’iw gii-doodaman (wonder why you do that) ningoji wayaabaminaanin? (wherever I see you?)</span>

8)  > **Zhigwa geget omadwe-kanoonigoon: “Ai, ni-shiimisa, amanj wiin i’iw gii-doodaman ningoji wayaabaminaanin?**

> The truly the voice of Nanabushu was heard saying to him: “Hey, my little brother, would that I knew why you act so whenever I see you anywhere\!

Akawe, giwii-dibaajimotawin\! (wait, I want to tell you something\!)

9)  > **Akawe, giwii-dibaajimotawin\!**

> Wait, I wish to tell you something\!

<span class="l-gloss">Geget gichi-inakamigad o’owidi wendoseyaan,” (“truly a great event is going on over there where I have come from,”) odinaan (he said to him).</span>

10) > **Geget gichi-inakamigad o’owidi wendoseyaan,” odinaan.**

> Truly, a great time is going on over there from whence I have come,” he said to him.

“Awawa, ondaas, bi-izhaan\! (“come now, hither, come here\!)

11) > **“Awawa, ondaas, bi-izhaan\!**

> “Ay, hither, come here\!

Gaawiin gidaa-naniizaanenimisii.” (you have no need to fear me.”)

12) > **Gaawiin gidaa-naniizaanenimisii.”**

> You have no cause to fear me.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget gaa-bi-izhi-izhaanid (and so truly he went over) i’imaa ayaad (to where the other was).</span>

13) > **Mii dash geget gaa-bi-izhi-izhaanid i’imaa ayaad.**

> It was true that he went to where (Nanabushu) was.

<span class="l-gloss">“Diwe, geget igo gichi-inakamigad bijiinaago, (“why, truly a great thing went on yesterday,) gii-nisidiwag; (they were killing one another;) gichi-waawiiyag gii-onji-nanidiwag; (for no real reason they were slaying one another;) gipishkwatagiinodaadiwag.” (they shot one another every which way.”)</span>

14) > **“Diwe, geget igo gichi-inakamigad bijiinaago, gii-nisidiwag; gichi-waawiiyag gii-onji-nanidiwag; gipishkwatagiinodaadiwag.”**

> “Ah, but there truly was a great time going on yesterday, they were killing one another; for no reason were they slaying one another; they shot one another indiscriminately with arrows.”

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii go imaa epiichi-danaajimotawaad (now, while he was telling him the story there) ezhi-ikwateshkawaad iniw omitigwaabiin (he was stringing his bow).</span>

15) > **Aaniish mii go imaa epiichi-danaajimotawaad ezhi-ikwateshkawaad iniw omitigwaabiin.**

> Now, while he was telling him the story, he was stringing his bow.

“O’ow isa go doodamobaniig.” (“this was the very way they did it.”)

16) > **“O’ow isa go doodamobaniig.”**

> “This was the very way they did at the time.”

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-nanimaakwiinoodawaad (meanwhile he kept aiming at him) i’imaa waashepiinid (there at his side).</span>

17) > **Ezhi-nanimaakwiinoodawaad i’imaa waashepiinid.**

> All the while he kept aiming there at his ([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-caribou-footnotes.html)) side.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii sa o’ow endoodamowaapan,” (“this was just the way they did,”) odinaan (he said to him).</span>

18) > **“Mii sa o’ow endoodamowaapan,” odinaan.**

> “This was just the way they did,” he said to him.

Ezhi-bimwaad i’imaa waashepiinid (then he shot him there in the side).

19) > **Ezhi-bimwaad i’imaa waashepiinid.**

> Then he shot him in the side.

“Mishanim Nenaboozhoo\! (“confound Nanabushu\!)

20) > **“Mishanim Nenaboozhoo\!**

> “Confound Nanabushu\!

Geget aana gii-nenimagiban.” (truly, that was just what I thought he would do.”)

21) > **Geget aana gii-nenimagiban.”**

> Truly, that was the very thing I thought he would do.”

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa gaa-izhi-nisaad (so after he had killed him), ezhi-maajiikawaad (he set to work skinning and cutting him up).</span>

22) > **Aaniish mii sa gaa-izhi-nisaad, ezhi-maajiikawaad.**

> So after he had killed (the Caribou), he then set to work flaying (and) cutting him up.

<span class="l-gloss">Bapenaginange ikaamoowan (exceedingly fat was he); ezhi-pabimagoodood iniw owiininoman (then he went and hung up his fat); ezhi-gabaashimaad gakina (then he boiled all of it).</span>

23) > **Bapenaginange ikaamoowan; ezhi-pabimagoodood iniw owiininoman; ezhi-gabaashimaad gakina.**

> Exceedingly fat was (the Caribou); then he went (and) hung up his fat; then he boiled it all.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-giizizwaad (after he had finished cooking it), ogiidashkwemag ogii-agwaawaan (he dipped the meat out onto a sheet of birch-bark).</span>

24) > **Gaa-izhi-giizizwaad, ogiidashkwemag ogii-agwaawaan.**

> After he had finished cooking it, then out upon a sheet of birch-bark he dipped the meat.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-ikidod apii gaa-onabiitawaad (and this is what he said when he sat down to it): “Dibigizh ge-ondamaawagen?” (“wonder from what part I shall take my eating\!”) gii-ikido (he said).</span>

25) > **Mii dash gaa-ikidod apii gaa-onabiitawaad: “Dibigizh ge-ondamaawagen?” gii-ikido.**

> And this was what he said when he came to where it lay: “Wonder from what part of the body I shall take (what I am to eat)\!” he said.

“Indawaa sa oshtigwaaning ninga-ondamaa. (“I think I will eat from the head.)

26) > **“Indawaa sa oshtigwaaning ninga-ondamaa.**

> “I think that from the head I will take what I am to eat of him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa maawiin nindaa-minochigesii, (perhaps it would not be right for me to do that,) nindaa-paapi’igoog niinimooshenyag (I would be laughed at by my loves) ogii-ashewebamaan,” (for starting at the wrong end,”) gii-inendam (he thought).</span>

27) > **Gaa maawiin nindaa-minochigesii, nindaa-paapi’igoog niinimooshenyag ([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-caribou-footnotes.html)) ogii-ashewebamaan,” gii-inendam.**

> Perhaps it would not be proper for me to do that, for I should be made fun of by my loves on account of my having begun at the opposite end,” (thus) he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">“Nindaa-baapi’igoog niinimooshenyag,” (“I would be laughed at by my loves,”) gii-nendam (he thought).</span>

28) > **“Nindaa-baapi’igoog niinimooshenyag,” gii-nendam.**

> “I should be laughed at by my loves,” (thus) he thought.

“Amanj isa wiin i’iwidi obikwanaang ondamag? (“wonder if it would be well to eat of him at the back?)

29) > **“Amanj isa wiin i’iwidi obikwanaang ondamag?**

> “Wonder if it would be well for me to eat of him at the back\!

Gaawiin sa, ganabaj nindaa-baapi’igoog. (no indeed, for probably I would be laughed at.)

30) > **Gaawiin sa, ganabaj nindaa-baapi’igoog.**

> No, indeed, for probably I should then be laughed at.

<span class="l-gloss">Ganabaj ogii-kaanjwebamaan gichi-ayaabe-adikwan, (‘perhaps he shoved the big bull caribou forward while eating on him,’) nindaa-igoog maawiin niinimoshenyag. (would be said of me perhaps by my loves.)</span>

31) > **Ganabaj ogii-kaanjwebamaan gichi-ayaabe-adikwan, nindaa-igoog maawiin niinimoshenyag.**

> ‘Perhaps he shoved the big bull Caribou forward while eating upon him,’ would be said of me perhaps by my loves.

<span class="l-gloss">Amanj isa wiin bimichaya’ii ondamag?” (wonder if it would be well to eat of him at the side?”) gii-inendam (he thought).</span>

32) > **Amanj isa wiin bimichaya’ii ondamag?” gii-inendam.**

> Wonder if it would be well for me to eat of him at the side\!” (thus) he thought.

“Gaawiin sa, ganabaj nindaa-paabi’igoog. (“no indeed, for perhaps I would be laughed at.)

33) > **“Gaawiin sa, ganabaj nindaa-paabi’igoog.**

> “No indeed, for perhaps I should be laughed at.

<span class="l-gloss">Ogii-pimijwebamaan nindaa-igoog gichi-ayaabe-adikwan, (‘he pushed a great bull caribou sidewise while eating on him,’ would be said of me,) niinimooshenyag nindaa-igoog.” (it would be said of me by my loves.”)</span>

34) > **Ogii-pimijwebamaan nindaa-igoog gichi-ayaabe-adikwan, niinimooshenyag nindaa-igoog.”**

> ‘He tried to push a great bull caribou sidewise when eating upon him,’ would be said of me by my loves.”

<span class="l-gloss">Megwaa go danwewetood (and while he kept talking away) miziwe jiigaatig ogii-babaa-atoon omama’ibimaan (he went about laying all his rendered grease around the foot of a tree).</span>

35) > **Megwaa go danwewetood miziwe jiigaatig ogii-babaa-atoon omama’ibimaan.**

> And while engage in this talk, he laid round about the foot of a tree all the grease (which he had boiled down).

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash apii ezhi-noondaagwadinig giziibaakwad, (and so when the creaking of the trees was heard,) “Geget sa nin-gichi-sasiskimigon. (“truly someone is beseeching me hard.)</span>

36) > **Mii dash apii ezhi-noondaagwadinig giziibaakwad, “Geget sa nin-gichi-sasiskimigon.**

> And so when the creaking of trees rubbing (together) was heard, “Truly by some one am I sought beseechingly.

Ganabaj sa gaye wiin wii-wiisini.” (perhaps it too wants to eat.”)

37) > **Ganabaj sa gaye wiin wii-wiisini.”**

> Perhaps, indeed, some one too wants to eat.”

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-pakwezhwaad i’imaa owiininoowinid (after slicing off a piece from a fatty part of him), ezhi-akwaandawed (then up the tree he climbed).</span>

38) > **Gaa-pakwezhwaad i’imaa owiininoowinid, ezhi-akwaandawed.**

> After slicing off a piece from a fatty part (of the caribou), then up the tree he climbed.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash iwidi awi-poozinang; (and so there he went and set the fat;) gaye go wiininoon ogiziindaakwishimaan (and he wedged the fat into where it was creaking).</span>

39) > **Mii dash iwidi awi-poozinang; ([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabushu-and-the-caribou-footnotes.html)) gaye go wiininoon ogiziindaakwishimaan.**

> And so there he went and placed the fat; and he put the fat in where it was creaking.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-gichi-piidaanimadinig (when a great gust of wind came up), ezhi-dakwamigod i’iw giziibaakwad (he was caught fast by the creaking tree).</span>

40) > **Ezhi-gichi-piidaanimadinig, ezhi-dakwamigod i’iw giziibaakwad.**

> When a great gust of wind came up, he was then caught fast by the creaking tree.

Ay, mii sa imaa e-indanagoozid\! (ah, and so there he hung\!)

41) > **Ay, mii sa imaa e-indanagoozid\!**

> Ah, and so there he hung\!

Biinish igo gabeya’ii agoozi (even for a long while he hung there).

42) > **Biinish igo gabeya’ii agoozi.**

> Even till a long while after was he hanging.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo inaabid iwidi wayekwaakosiw (then suddenly, as he looked off to the far end of the meadow) ma’iingana’ ondadaabiipa’idiwa’, (some wolves came running this way into the meadow,) mii dash ezhi-ganoonaad: (whereupon he called to them:) “Gego wiin o’omaa bi-izhaakegon\!” (“don’t you come this way\!”)</span>

43) > **Ningoding igo inaabid iwidi wayekwaakosiw ma’iingana’ ondadaabiipa’idiwa’, mii dash ezhi-ganoonaad: “Gego wiin o’omaa bi-izhaakegon\!”**

> The suddenly, while looking toward the other end of the meadow, (he saw) some wolves running hitherward into the meadow, whereupon he addressed them, saying: “Don’t you come this way\!”

“Gegoo maawiin ogii-nitoonaadog Nenaboozhoo. (“Nanabushu must surely have killed something.)

44) > **“Gegoo maawiin ogii-nitoonaadog Nenaboozhoo.**

> “Something doubtless must Nanabushu have slain.

A’aw, daga apa’ididaa\!” (come on, let us run over there\!”)

45) > **A’aw, daga apa’ididaa\!”**

> Come on, do let us run over there\!”

Indigomi gii-bi-gagwejikanidiwa’ (it seemed as though they tried to race one another there).

46) > **Indigomi gii-bi-gagwejikanidiwa’.**

> It seemed as if they tried to race (to where he was).

<span class="l-gloss">Zhayiigwa bagamisewa’ (now, when they came running up), ezhi-waabamaanid iniw odadikoman (they saw his caribou).</span>

47) > **Zhayiigwa bagamisewa’, ezhi-waabamaanid iniw odadikoman.**

> Now, when they came running up to the place, they saw his caribou.

Apane go gaa-makandinid (at once they fell to grabbing it from one another).

48) > **Apane go gaa-makandinid.**

> Straightway did they fall upon it, grabbing it from one another.

<span class="l-gloss">Aiii, aaniish ge-gii-doodang (alas, what could he do) mii sa apane odadikoman (for his caribou was entirely gone).</span>

49) > **Aiii, aaniish ge-gii-doodang mii sa apane odadikoman.**

> Alas\! Naturally was there nothing for him to do, for entirely gone was his caribou.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-ganoonaad: (then he called to them:) “Ni-shiimisa, gego wiin jaajiigaatig baa-inaabikegon\!” (“my little brothers, don’t you go looking around the foot of this tree\!”)</span>

50) > **Ezhi-ganoonaad: “Ni-shiimisa, gego wiin jaajiigaatig baa-inaabikegon\!”**

> Then he addressed them, saying: “O my little brothers\! Don’t you come and look round about this tree\!”

“A’aw, gegoo ogii-atoonaadog\!” (“come on, he has probably laid out something there\!”)

51) > **“A’aw, gegoo ogii-atoonaadog\!"**

> “Come on, something he probably has laid out (there)\!”

Mii dash geget gaa-makandinid igo omama’ibimaan (whereupon truly they grabbed his rendered grease from one another).

52) > **Mii dash geget gaa-makandinid igo omama’ibimaan.**

> Whereupon truly they grabbed away from one another the grease (which he had boiled down).

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa waa-ani-maajiiba’idiwa’, (presently, as they were about to race away,) “Gego wiin, ni-shiimisadog, ishpiming inaabikegon\!” (“don’t now, my little brothers, don’t you look up\!”)</span>

53) > **Zhigwa waa-ani-maajiiba’idiwa’, “Gego wiin, ni-shiimisadog, ishpiming inaabikegon\!”**

> Presently were they about to race away when, “Don’t now, O my little brothers, don’t you look up\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish ezhi-daataganaabiwaad igiw ma’iinganag (so when the wolves looked up), bane go wiinin gaa-inagoodenig (nothing but fat was hanging there).</span>

54) > **Aaniish ezhi-daataganaabiwaad igiw ma’iinganag, bane go wiinin gaa-inagoodenig.**

> So when up the Wolves looked, nothing but fat (did they see) hanging there.

Aaniish gaye gaa-makandinid i’iw ma’iingana’ (so the wolves too grabbed that away from one another).

55) > **Aaniish gaye gaa-makandinid i’iw ma’iingana’.**

> So of course the Wolves also grabbed that away from one another.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-gidaanawenid eni-maajiiba’idinid; (after they had eaten it up they went racing off;) ezhi-bagidamigod (then he was let loose).</span>

56) > **Gaa-gidaanawenid eni-maajiiba’idinid; ezhi-bagidamigod.**

> After they had eaten it up, then away they went racing; then was he let loose from the grip.

Napem obagidamigon i’iw giziibaakwad (at last he was set free by the creaking tree).

57) > **Napem obagidamigon i’iw giziibaakwad.**

> When it was all over, he was set free by the creaking tree.

Ezhi-niisaandawed (then down he climbed).

58) > **Ezhi-niisaandawed.**

> Then down he climbed from the tree.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa aano-gwiinawii-izhiikang, (thereupon he went in vain to see what he had left,) aaniish mii eta odoo-shtigwaanim etenig (for only his caribou head remained).</span>

59) > **Mii sa aano-gwiinawii-izhiikang, aaniish mii eta odoo-shtigwaanim etenig.**

> Thereupon he went in vain to see what he yet had left, for there remained only his (caribou) head.

Aaniish odaano-jiichiigwandaan i’iw odoo-shtigwaanim (so he tried in vain to gnaw on his caribou head).

60) > **Aaniish odaano-jiichiigwandaan i’iw odoo-shtigwaanim.**

> So he tried in vain to gnaw upon what was left (of the meat of) his (caribou) head.

Aaniish mii eta wiinindib e-yaanig (now only the brain was left).

61) > **Aaniish mii eta wiinindib e-yaanig.**

> Now only the brain was left.

Aaniish ogwiinawii-inaapinadoon (naturally he had no way of getting at it).

62) > **Aaniish ogwiinawii-inaapinadoon.**

> Naturally he had no means of getting at it.

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga indawaa, ninga-kinebigoonsikaas,” (“come then, I will turn myself into a little snake,”) gii-inendam (he thought).</span>

63) > **“Daga indawaa, ninga-kinebigoonsikaas,” gii-inendam.**

> “Then, therefore, I will take on the form of a little snake,” he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget gaa-izhinaagozid, (and so truly that was how he looked,) mii iwedi wiinindib wenjitood (the reason being the brain in there).</span>

64) > **Mii dash geget gaa-izhinaagozid, mii iwedi wiinindib wenjitood.**

> Thereupon truly that was the form he took upon himself, the reason he did it was on account of the brain there (in the skull).

<span class="l-gloss">Megwaa go dazhiikang ezhi-anishinaabewid, (and while busied with it he became a person again,) mii sa maajaad (whereupon off he started).</span>

65) > **Megwaa go dazhiikang ezhi-anishinaabewid, mii sa maajaad.**

> And while busied with the brain he became a human being, whereupon off he started.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-odeshkaniimagadinig, (and so when there were horns upon him,) ay, aaniish ge-izhichiged\! (ah, what was he to do\!)</span>

66) > **Mii dash ezhi-odeshkaniimagadinig, ay, aaniish ge-izhichiged\!**

> And so when there were horns (upon his head), ah, what was then to do\!

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-pidaakwishing, (when he bumped against a tree,) “Giidawenenaatigoo, ni-shiimisa?” (“what kind of tree are you, my little brother?”)</span>

67) > **Ezhi-pidaakwishing, “Giidawenenaatigoo, ni-shiimisa?”**

> When he bumped against a tree, “What sort of a tree are you, my little brother?” (he asked.)

“Eye’, niin isa go bagwadakamig gaa-naaniibawiyaan.” (“yes, deep in the wilderness is where I always stand.”)

68) > **“Eye’, niin isa go bagwadakamig gaa-naaniibawiyaan.”**

> “Ay, in the deep solitude of the forest ever do I stand.”

“Oon, ni-shiimisa, gi-mina’igoowim iizan.” (“oh, my little brother, you must be a tamarack then.”)

69) > **“Oon, ni-shiimisa, gi-mina’igoowim iizan.”**

> “Then really, my little brother, you must be a tamarack.”

“Eye’,” ogii-igoon (“yes,” he was told).

70) > **“Eye’,” ogii-igoon.**

> “Yes,” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa ezhi-pidaakwishing, (again he bumped against a tree,) “Gidawenenaatigo, ni-shiimisa?” (“what kind of tree are you, my little brother?”)</span>

71) > **Miinawaa ezhi-pidaakwishing, “Gidawenenaatigo, ni-shiimisa?”**

> When he bumped against another tree, “What kind of a tree are you, my little brother?” he asked.

“Niin isa go wajiwing gaa-naaniibawiyaan.” (“upon the mountain is where I always stand.”)

72) > **“Niin isa go wajiwing gaa-naaniibawiyaan.”**

“Always upon the mountain do I stand.”

“Oon, gi-shingwaakoowim iizan.” (“oh, you must be a pine then.”)

73) > **“Oon, gi-shingwaakoowim iizan.”**

“Then, indeed, you must (then) be a pine.”

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa ezhi-pidaakwishing, (again he bumped against a tree,) “Gidawenenaatigo?” (“what kind of tree are you?”)</span>

74) > **Miinawaa ezhi-pidaakwishing, “Gidawenenaatigo?”**
