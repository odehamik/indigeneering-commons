(32) Nenabosho slays Toad-Woman, the healer of the Manitous

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa neyaap (thereupon back again) eko-biiyaanigiban (to its former depth — \*preterite\*) gii-ko-biiyaanig (where the water had stood).</span>

1)  > **Mii sa neyaap eko-biiyaanigiban gii-ko-biiyaanig.**

> Thereupon back to its former depth did the water recede.

<span class="l-gloss">Abaapish neyaap gaa-ako-biiyaanig (when the water had gone back to where it stood before), miisa zhigwa (and so then) weweni go (carefully) odoonaabandaan (he chose with his eyes) waa-daad (the place where he would dwell).</span>

2)  > **Abaapish neyaap gaa-ako-biiyaanig, miisa zhigwa weweni go odoonaabandaan waa-daad.**

> When the water got to where it was before, he accordingly then with care selected a place where he would have his lodge.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’imaa (and so from there) waa-onjiid (where he meant to set out) i’iw dayoosh\* (still — \*editorial mark carried\*) wii-nandone’iged (intending to search).</span>

3)  > **Mii dash i’imaa waa-onjiid i’iw dayoosh\* wii-nandone’iged.**

> And so from there he intended yet to seek (for his nephew).

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa geyaabi babaamademod (and so still as he wandered about weeping), ningoding sa awiya (once someone) onoondawaan biminagamonid (he heard going along singing):</span>

4)  > **Mii sa geyaabi babaamademod, ningoding sa awiya onoondawaan biminagamonid:**

> And so while wandering about weeping, he once heard somebody going along singing:

<span class="l-gloss">(song carried verbatim, sung again) “Aki ekwaag (from the ends of the earth) nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.”</span>

5)  > **“Aki ekwaag nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin saa**

> **Aki ekwaag nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin saa.([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-slays-toad-woman-the-healer-of-the-manitous-footnotes.html))**
> 
> “From the ends of the earth do I come with the sound of my rattles, saa (X2)”

<span class="l-gloss">Odinitawaan awiya (such was how he heard someone) biminagamonid (going along singing).</span>

6)  > **Odinitawaan awiya biminagamonid.**

> Such was the way he heard some one sing while going along.

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinaan (what should he do but) onaazitawaan (go toward the sound of it).</span>

7)  > **Wegoneniiwinaan onaazitawaan.**

> What should he do but go towards the sound of the being.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii debaabamaad (when he caught sight of it), goniginiin (behold), omakakiin (a toad) bimiyaanjigwaashkoniwan (leaping along from place to place).</span>

8)  > **Apii debaabamaad, goniginiin, omakakiin bimiyaanjigwaashkoniwan.**

> When he came in sight of the being, lo, (he saw that) it was a toad([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-slays-toad-woman-the-healer-of-the-manitous-footnotes.html)) leaping along from place to place.

<span class="l-gloss">Wiigobiin bemoondaminid (some basswood bark she carried on her back), gaye zhiishiigwanan (and some rattles) obimiwinaanini (she carried along) odoodanaaning dakobizowan (bound at her heels).</span>

9)  > **Wiigobiin bemoondaminid, gaye zhiishiigwanan obimiwinaanini odoodanaaning dakobizowan.**

> Some bast she bore upon her back, and some rattles she carried bound to her heels.

<span class="l-gloss">Niibiwa dash iiniw wiigobiin (now, a good deal of that basswood bark) bemoondaminid (she carried on her back) ezhi-mawinanaad (when he rushed up at her).</span>

10) > **Niibiwa dash iiniw wiigobiin bemoondaminid ezhi-mawinanaad.**

> Now, a good deal was the bast she carried on her back when he rushed up to her.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-odisaad (on coming up to her), aaniish (why), aapiji mindimooyenyan (a very old woman she was).</span>

11) > **Ezhi-odisaad, aaniish, aapiji mindimooyenyan.**

> On coming up to her, why, she was a very old woman.

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookoo (O my grandmother), aaniin wenji-nagamoyan? (why is it you are singing?)”</span>

12) > **“Nookoo, aaniin wenji-nagamoyan?”**

> “O my grandmother\! For what reason are you singing?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Kaa (oh), Nenabosho gosha (for Nenabosho indeed) wii-nandobikaaso (a snare is to be set).</span>

13) > **“Kaa, Nenabosho gosha wii-nandobikaaso.**

> “Oh, a snare is really to be laid for Nenabosho.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii wa nino\* (and these — \*editorial mark carried\*) wayaabadakin (that are to be used) onoow wiigobiin (these basswood barks) gaa-bimoondamaan (which I carry on my back).</span>

14) > **Mii wa nino\* wayaabadakin onoow wiigobiin gaa-bimoondamaan.**

> And this bast which I carry upon my back is the thing to be used for the purpose.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenabosho gosha wiin (it was Nenabosho indeed) gaa-bimwaad (who shot) iiniw ogimaa mishibizhiin (the chief of the big lynxes).”</span>

15) > **Nenabosho gosha wiin gaa-bimwaad iiniw ogimaa mishibizhiin.”**

> It was Nenabosho who really shot the chief of the big lynxes.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookoo (O my grandmother), aaniin wiin i’iw (why is it) waa-onji-nandoobikaazod (that a snare is to be set for) a’aw Nenabosho? (that Nenabosho?)”</span>

16) > **“Nookoo, aaniin wiin i’iw waa-onji-nandoobikaazod a’aw Nenabosho?”**

> “O my grandmother\! Pray, why is a snare to be set for Nenabosho?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Kaa, gaawiin ash(dash?) wiin (oh, well — \*editorial query carried\*) mii go iw ezhi-saagisininig (it is because it is sticking out) i’iw obikwak (that arrow) a’aw Nenabosho (of Nenabosho).</span>

17) > **“Kaa, gaawiin ash(dash?) wiin mii go iw ezhi-saagisininig i’iw obikwak a’aw Nenabosho.**

> “Oh, well\! It is for the arrow of Nenabosho, which is now sticking out of (the chief of the big lynxes).

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow oodena (this town) mi i’imaa baa-onjiiyaan (it is from there I come).</span>

18) > **O’ow oodena mi i’imaa baa-onjiiyaan.**

> From this town yonder do I come.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash imaa ayaawa (and there) bimaadizi a’aw ogimaamishibizhii (the chief big-lynx is barely alive).”</span>

19) > **Mii dash imaa ayaawa bimaadizi a’aw ogimaamishibizhii.”**

> And over there hardly alive is the chief of the big lynxes.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookomis\! (O my grandmother\!) Aaniin dash wiin i’iw i’iwe (and what was that) gibiminagamoyan? (that you were singing along?)”</span>

20) > **“Nookomis\! Aaniin dash wiin i’iw i’iwe gibiminagamoyan?”**

> “O my grandmother\! Pray, what was that you were singing about?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Kaa, gaawiin-ash wiin (oh, why) niinanaandawi’aanaan a’aw ogimaa (we are doctoring the chief), mii dash iw ena’amaan (and this is what I sing) i’iwe nanaandawi’ag (when I am healing him):</span>

21) > **“Kaa, gaawiin-ash wiin niinanaandawi’aanaan a’aw ogimaa, mii dash iw ena’amaan i’iwe nanaandawi’ag:**

> “Oh, why, we are ministering to the chief; and this is what I sing when I am attending him:

<span class="l-gloss">(song carried verbatim) ‘Aki ekwaag (from the ends of the earth) nimbishinawashin (I come with the sound of my rattles).’</span>

22) > **‘Aki ekwaag nimbishinawashin.’**

> “From the ends of the earth do I come with the sound of my rattles”

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii sa iw aapiji giniin (it is so much) niminotaagoo (pleasing is the sound of me when I sing).</span>

23) > **“Mii sa iw aapiji giniin niminotaagoo.**

> It is so much pleasure I impart when I sing.

Nagamon onishishin (the song is fine).”

24) > **Nagamon onishishin.”**

> The song is fine.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookoo\! (O my grandmother\!) Aandi dinong (at what place) namadabiyan? (do you sit?)”</span>

25) > **“Nookoo\! Aandi dinong namadabiyan?”**

> “O my grandmother\! At what place do you sit?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aapiji go mayaawishkaand (right in the very middle of the doorway) mii go imaa endanabiyaan (that is where I always sit).</span>

26) > **“Aapiji go mayaawishkaand mii go imaa endanabiyaan.**

> “In the very middle of the doorway is where I always sit.

<span class="l-gloss">Iw idash gaa-izhichigewaad (now this is what they have done), aabita gashkiikibide (a partition divides it in half); mii dash iwiti awasaya’ii (and so over on the other side) zhingishing a’aw nindoo-gimaaminaan (lies that chief of ours).”</span>

27) > **Iw idash gaa-izhichigewaad, aabita gashkiikibide; mii dash iwiti awasaya’ii zhingishing a’aw nindoo-gimaaminaan.”**

> Now, this is what they have done: a partition divides the space in two equal parts; and so over on the other side lies the chief of ours.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aandi dash wiin i’iw (and where is it) endaayan? (that you dwell?)”</span>

28) > **“Aandi dash wiin i’iw endaayan?”**

> “And where is it you dwell?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii go imaa ekwaakwaag (why, yonder at the edge of the woods) endaayaan (is where I dwell).</span>

29) > **“Mii go imaa ekwaakwaag endaayaan.**

> “Why, yonder at the edge of the forest do I dwell.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji go agaansaa (and very small) i’iw niwiigwaamens (is that little wigwam of mine).</span>

30) > **Aapiji go agaansaa i’iw niwiigwaamens.**

> And very small is that little wigwam of mine.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaye dash niizhiwag noozhisag (and there are two of my grandchildren), aapiji go babiiwishiiyoowag gwiiwizensag (and very tiny are the boys); mii eta go iigiw (now, they are the only ones) waadigemagwaa (with whom I live).”</span>

31) > **Gaye dash niizhiwag noozhisag, aapiji go babiiwishiiyoowag gwiiwizensag; mii eta go iigiw waadigemagwaa.”**

> And there are two of my grandchildren, and very tiny are the boys; now, they are the only ones with whom I live.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Nookoo\! (O my grandmother\!) Aaniin wiin ii’iw (how was it) gaa-izhinawe’aad (that he angered) iiniw Nenaboshowan? (that Nenabosho?)”</span>

32) > **“Nookoo\! Aaniin wiin ii’iw gaa-izhinawe’aad iiniw Nenaboshowan?”**

> “O my grandmother\! How was it that (the chief) angered Nenabosho?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Kaa, kaaanawiin (why, actually) ogii-odaapinamawaan (he took away from him) iiniw odoozhimini (his nephew).</span>

33) > **“Kaa, kaaanawiin ogii-odaapinamawaan iiniw odoozhimini.**

> “Why, he actually took his nephew away from him.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji Nenabosho ozaagi’aabaniin (very fond was Nenabosho — \*preterite\*) iiniw odoozhiman (of his nephew).</span>

34) > **Aapiji Nenabosho ozaagi’aabaniin iiniw odoozhiman.**

> Very fond was Nenabosho of his nephew.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw gii-nishki’aad (it was on that account that he angered him), gaa-onji-pimogod (which was why he was shot by him).”</span>

35) > **Mii dash i’iw gii-nishki’aad, gaa-onji-pimogod.”**

> It was on that account (the chief) angered him, which was why he was shot (by Nenabosho).”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aah, aaniin gota wiin (now, why should it be) ezhiikawaagobanen (that he was so treated — \*preterite dubitative\*) aw ogimaamshibizhiin (by the chief big-lynx) i’iwe mamawaagobanen (as to be robbed) iiniw odoozhimini? (of his nephew?)</span>

36) > **“Aah, aaniin gota wiin ezhiikawaagobanen aw ogimaamshibizhiin i’iwe mamawaagobanen iiniw odoozhimini?**

> “Now, pray why should he be so treated by the chief of the big lynxes as to be deprived of his nephew by him?

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin sa wiin agaanhsi manidoowisii (by no means a small manidoo is he) a’aw Nenabosho ezhinikaazod (the one called Nenabosho).”</span>

37) > **Gaawiin sa wiin agaanhsi manidoowisii a’aw Nenabosho ezhinikaazod.”**

> By no means a small manitou is he who goes by the name of Nenabosho.”

<span class="l-gloss">Odaadaganaabamigoon (she lifted her head and looked up at him): “Nyaanh (ah, me), maagizhaa giin Nenabosho\! (perhaps you are Nenabosho\!)”</span>

38) > **Odaadaganaabamigoon: Nyaanh, maagizhaa giin Nenabosho\!”**

> “She lifted her head and looked up at him: “Ah, me\! Perhaps you are Nenabozho\!”

<span class="l-gloss">“Tediwe\! (nonsense\!) Gaawiin minik i’iw (not so long as this) gidaa-kaganoonigosii (would you be held in talk) Nenabosho aawid (if it were Nenabosho).</span>

39) > **“Tediwe\! Gaawiin minik i’iw gidaa-kaganoonigosii Nenabosho aawid.**

> “Nonsense\! Not so long as this would you be held in conversation if it were Nenabosho.

<span class="l-gloss">Mewizha gidaa-niiwana’og (long ago you would have been clubbed to death) giishpin aawiyaan Nenabosho (if I were Nenabosho).</span>

40) > **Mewizha gidaa-niiwana’og giishpin aawiyaan Nenabosho.**

> Long ago would you have been clubbed to death if I were Nenabosho.

<span class="l-gloss">Nookoo\! (O my grandmother\!) Daga, miinawaa maajii’an ginagamon\! (come, start your song once more\!)</span>

41) > **Nookoo\! Daga, miinawaa maajii’an ginagamon\!**

> O my grandmother\! Do start that song of yours once more\!

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin gwej ningikendaziin (not exactly do I yet know) i’iw ginagamon (that song of yours).”</span>

42) > **Gaawiin gwej ningikendaziin i’iw ginagamon.”**

> Not exactly yet do I know that song of yours.”

Aaniish geyaabi (well, once more):

43) > **Aaniish geyaabi:**

> so once more:

<span class="l-gloss">(song carried verbatim, sung again) “Aki ekwaag (from the ends of the earth) nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.”</span>

44) > **“Aki ekwaag nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.**

> **Aki ekwaag nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.”**
> 
> “From the ends of the earth do I come with the sound of my rattles, saa.”

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinen Nenabosho (what did Nenabosho do but) oniiwanawaan (club her to death).</span>

45) > **Wegoneniiwinen Nenabosho oniiwanawaan.**

> What did Nenabosho do but club her to death.

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga, aweneniwiinena (well, what a fool) aw maji-mindimooyenshish (this wretched old woman)\!”</span>

46) > **“Daga, aweneniwiinena aw maji-mindimooyenshish.”**

> “Well, what a fool this wretched old woman (was)\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Aatayaa (ah), Nenabosho omaajiikawaan (Nenabosho set to work on her) i’iw bakonaad (flaying her), wawiinge obakonaan (thoroughly he skinned her).</span>

47) > **Aatayaa, Nenabosho omaajiikawaan i’iw bakonaad, wawiinge obakonaan.**

> Ah, Nenabosho then set to work flaying her, from every part he removed the skin.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaagiizhi-pakonaad (after he had finished skinning her), aapiji agaashiinyikaaso Nenabosho (very small Nenabosho made himself).</span>

48) > **Gaagiizhi-pakonaad, aapiji agaashiinyikaaso Nenabosho.**

> After he had finished flaying her, very small then Nenabosho made himself.

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinen (what should he do but) ezhi-piisikawaad (get into and wear) iiniw omakakiiwayaanan (the toad-skin), wawiinge ode-bishkawaan (ode-bisikawaan?) (perfectly he fit into it — \*editorial query carried\*).</span>

49) > **Wegoneniiwinen ezhi-piisikawaad iiniw omakakiiwayaanan, wawiinge ode-bishkawaan (ode-bisikawaan?).**

> What should he do but get into the toad-skin to wear it, in every respect did he fit into it.

<span class="l-gloss">Bangii omaa opwaaning (obwaaning?) (a little, here at the hip — \*editorial query carried\*) ogaa-kikiishkawaan (he tore it).</span>

50) > **Bangii omaa opwaaning (obwaaning?) ogaa-kikiishkawaan.**

> Slightly here on the hip he tore it.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-piisikawaad (after he had got into it), iiniw ozhiishiigwanan (those rattles) odoondanaang odakobinaan (he bound at his heels), gaye iiniw wiigobiin odoomboondaanan (and the basswood bark he put on his back).</span>

51) > **Gaa-piisikawaad, iiniw ozhiishiigwanan odoondanaang odakobinaan, gaye iiniw wiigobiin odoomboondaanan.**

> After he had got into it, then he bound the rattles to his heels, and put the bast upon his back.

<span class="l-gloss">Aah, Nenabosho ezhi-gwaashkwanid (ah, as Nenabosho went leaping along) ezhi-maajii’ang (he then started up the song):</span>

52) > **Aah, Nenabosho ezhi-gwaashkwanid ezhi-maajii’ang:**

> Ah\! As Nenabosho went leaping along, he then began to sing:

<span class="l-gloss">(song carried verbatim, sung again) “Aki ekwaag (from the ends of the earth) nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.”</span>

53) > **“Aki ekwaag nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.**

> **Aki ekwaag nimbishinawishin, nimbishinawishin, saa.”**
> 
> “From the ends of the earth do I come with the sound of rattles, saa.”

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji go gizhiiwe (giizhiwe\*) (and very loud he was — \*editorial spelling variant carried\*) biminagamod (as he went along singing).</span>

54) > **Aapiji go gizhiiwe (giizhiwe\*) biminagamod.**

> And very loud was the sound of his voice as he went singing.

Zhayiigwa odebaabandaan oodena (presently he came in sight of the town).

55) > **Zhayiigwa odebaabandaan oodena.**

> In a while was he come in sight of the town.

<span class="l-gloss">Abaapish ekwaakwaanig degoshing (when he had arrived at the edge of the woods), geget owaabandaan wiigiwaamens (sure enough he saw a small wigwam).</span>

56) > **Abaapish ekwaakwaanig degoshing, geget owaabandaan wiigiwaamens.**

> When he was come at the edge of the forest, sure enough, he saw a small wigwam.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii maa wiin i’iw gaa-itang (this must be what she spoke of),” inendam (he thought).</span>

57) > **“Mii maa wiin i’iw gaa-itang,” inendam.**

> “This must be what she spoke of,” he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaa go abiisinaagwadiniwan (somewhat farther on were in view) iiniw wiigiwaaman (those wigwams).</span>

58) > **Gomaa go abiisinaagwadiniwan iiniw wiigiwaaman.**

> Farther on was a view of the wigwams.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish aapiji enigok biminagamo (now ever so loud he sang as he went), noondawaa biminagamod (he was heard going along singing).</span>

59) > **Aaniish aapiji enigok biminagamo, noondawaa biminagamod.**

> Now ever so loud he sang as he went; he was heard as he went singing along.

<span class="l-gloss">Bezhig ezhi-giigidod (one then spoke up): “Eh, zhigwa miinawaa (yea, now again) gookoonaan biidwewidam (the sound of our dear grandmother comes singing).</span>

60) > **Bezhig ezhi-giigidod: “Eh, zhigwa miinawaa gookoonaan biidwewidam.**

> One then spoke up: “Yea, now once again comes the sound of our dear grandmother singing.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii iw zhigwa miinawaa (therefore now again) ji-awisagaswe’eg (you should invite her to come and smoke).”</span>

61) > **Mii iw zhigwa miinawaa ji-awisagaswe’eg.”**

> Therefore now again should you invite her to the gathering to smoke.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii zhigwa ani-oditang wiigiwaamens (then as he was nearing the little wigwam), neyaag\* igo bi-zaaga’amoo gwiiwisensa (but before that, out came the boys — \*editorial mark carried\*).</span>

62) > **Mii zhigwa ani-oditang wiigiwaamens, neyaag\* igo bi-zaaga’amoo gwiiwisensa.**

> Then presently, as he was about to arrive at the little wigwam, but before he got there, out came the boys.

<span class="l-gloss">“Nooko (O my grandmother), gidagwishin ina? (have you arrived home?)”</span>

63) > **“Nooko, gidagwishin ina?”**

> “O my grandmother\! Have you come home?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Eye (yes), nooshisidog (my grandchildren), nindagwishin (I have arrived).”</span>

64) > **“Eye, nooshisidog, nindagwishin.”**

> “Yes, my grandchildren, I am come home.”

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-izhi-piindiged (as she entered), wagijijiingwan namadabiwa i’iw ooshisa (upon her lap sat her grandchildren).</span>

65) > **Eni-izhi-piindiged, wagijijiingwan namadabiwa i’iw ooshisa.**

> When in she entered, then on her lap sat her grandchildren.

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinen bezhig iiniw ooshisan (what should happen but that one of his grandchildren) owaabamigoon i’imaa (saw him there) giigaagikiishkawaad\* iiniw omakakiiwayaanan (where he had torn open the toad-skin — \*editorial mark carried\*).</span>

66) > **Wegoneniiwinen bezhig iiniw ooshisan owaabamigoon i’imaa giigaagikiishkawaad\* iiniw omakakiiwayaanan.**

> What should happen to him([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nenabosho-slays-toad-woman-the-healer-of-the-manitous-footnotes.html)) but to be seen by one of his grandchildren at the place where he had torn open the toad-skin.

<span class="l-gloss">“Nooko (O my grandmother), aaniin wiin i’imaa (why is it there) gii-izhinaagoziyan? (that you look that way?)</span>

67) > **“Nooko, aaniin wiin i’imaa gii-izhinaagoziyan?**
