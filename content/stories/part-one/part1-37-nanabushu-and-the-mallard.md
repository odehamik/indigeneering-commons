37\. NANABUSHU AND THE MALLARD.

37\. Nanabushu and the Mallard.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Midaasoganzh (probable) — Part I Series 3 (Nos. 33–38), Bois Fort, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Ānic, cayīgwa mīnawä pa‘kadä Nānabucu. Ningutingiku minawä udōtisän ạnicinābä‘, mi käyābi nⁱᵉciwa‘ unitcānisinini. Mīsa‘ ạni∙‘nābît, “Wägutugwän mādciwāgwän?” inändạm.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aaniish, zhigwa miinawaa bakade Nanabozho. Ningodingigo miinawaa odoodisaan anishinaaben, mii gayaabi niiyawaa’ onijaanisinini. Miisa’ ani-naabid, “Wegonen-igo madiiwaagwen?” inendam.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Well, already was Nänabushu again becoming hungry. And one other time he came to some people, and they also had two children. And now, as he looked about, "What in the world must they have to eat?" he thought.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish (well), zhigwa miinawaa bakade Nanabozho (now again Nanabozho is hungry), ningodingigo miinawaa odoodisaan anishinaaben (one time again he visits some people), mii gayaabi niiyawaa’ onijaanisinini (they too have two children), miisa’ ani-naabid (so as he looks about), Wegonen-igo madiiwaagwen (what could there be to eat), inendam (he thinks).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(2) Cigwa kigitōwạn inⁱⁱᵘ ininiwạn: “Ạmbäsinō, unạgōta‘ki‘kwän,” inimäwạn inⁱⁱᵘ i‘kwäwạn.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Miisa’ geget anagota’ ki-gikwewan iniw ikwewan. Zhigwa waawezhi’owan iniw ininiwan ozhaawashko’ozhaanaman\*flagged\*, owiinge oshtigwaaning odizhizhimaanini\*flagged\*. <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon truly she hung up the kettle. Presently the man painted himself with a green paint, all over the top of his head he put the painting. And while he was seated, of a sudden, he started forth from the place, uttering: "Kwish, kwish, kwish, kwish\!" (such) was the sound of his voice. Up flew a Mallard³ that alighted yonder on the cross-pole, he was heard saying: "Kwish, kwish, kwish, kwish\!" was the sound of his voice. (Nänabushu) observed him muting, while at the same time (he heard him) saying: "Ho, ho, ho, old woman\! keep it stirring," he said to her. And all the while (the Mallard) muted, (he was saying): "Ho, ho, ho, old woman\! keep it stirring."*</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Ininicibạn, "mallard;" literally, "man-duck."*</span>
