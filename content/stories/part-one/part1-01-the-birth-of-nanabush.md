(1) Gaa-zhi-ndaadizid Nenabosho

Classic The Birth of Nenabosho

1)  > **Owiigiwaaming anishinaabeg, mindimooyenh odaanisan owiidigemaan.**

> In a wigwam lived some people, an old woman with her daughter dwelt.

<span class="l-gloss">Owiigiwaaming anishinaabeg (in their wigwam, some people), mindimooyenh odaanisan owiidigemaan (an old woman dwelt with her daughter).</span>

2)  > **Ningoding oganoonaan iniw odaanisan: “Ambe sa noo nindaanis aiyaangwaamizin. Wii noondawishin ge-ininaan.**

Once she spoke to her daughter, saying: “I beg of you, my daughter, be on your guard. I would have you listen to what I am going to tell you.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding oganoonaan iniw odaanisan (once she spoke to her daughter): “Ambe sa noo nindaanis (“come now, my daughter) aiyaangwaamizin (be on your guard). Wii-noondawishin ge-ininaan (I want you to listen to what I am going to tell you).</span>

3)  > **Geget sa ningichi-gotaaj, ginaniizaanenimin.**

Verily, am I greatly afraid, I am in fear for you.

<span class="l-gloss">Geget sa ningichi-gotaaj (truly I am greatly afraid), ginaniizaanenimin (I fear for you — I hold you in danger).</span>

4)  > **Gego wiikaa izhichigeken oowidi ningaabii’anong ji-inaasamabiyan zaaga’aman.**

Never bring to pass when you go out that you sit facing toward this westward way.

<span class="l-gloss">Gego wiikaa izhichigeken (never do this) oowidi ningaabii’anong (toward this western way) ji-inaasamabiyan (that you sit facing) zaaga’aman (when you go outside).</span>

5)  > **Gego giga-izhi-ayaa giishpin i’iwidi inaasamabiyan.**

Something will happen to you if toward that way you sit facing.

<span class="l-gloss">Gego giga-izhi-ayaa (do not let something happen to you) giishpin i’iwidi inaasamabiyan (if you sit facing toward that way).</span>

6)  > **Mii i’wi sa ezhi-naniizaaneniminaan.**

That is what causes me to entertain fears for you.

Mii i’wi sa ezhi-naniizaaneniminaan (that is why I hold you in fear for your safety).

7)  > **Aiyaangwaamizin i’iw \[noongomeninaan\] ji-izhichigeyan; giga inigaatoon kiiyaw.**

<span class="l-gloss">Be careful to give heed to that which I now tell you to do; (or else) you will bring (an evil) fate upon yourself.</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aiyaangwaamizin i’iw \[noongomeninaan\] ji-izhichigeyan (be careful about this that I now tell you to do); giga-inigaatoon kiiyaw (or you will bring harm upon your own body).</span>

8)  > **Mii sa iw eninaan.”**

> Now that was what I had to tell you.”

Mii sa iw eninaan.” (that is what I have to tell you.”)

9)  > **Aanish mii sa go iw, aaniish mii sa geget aiyaangwaamizigobanen a’aw ikwe weshkiniigikwewid.**

Now such was the way it was, for it was true that at the time heedful was this woman who was a maiden.

<span class="l-gloss">Aanish mii sa go iw (well, so it was), aaniish mii sa geget aiyaangwaamizigobanen (and truly she was heedful) a’aw ikwe weshkiniigikwewid (that woman who was a young maiden).</span>

10) > **Gaawiin wiikaa ininiwan besho ogii-waabamaasiin.**

> Never with men had she intimate association.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin wiikaa ininiwan (never with men) besho ogii-waabamaasiin (had she been intimate — lit. had she seen them up close).</span>

11) > **Ningoding igo gaa-izhi-waniwisid aw oshkiniigikwe;**

But once on a time unmindful became the maiden;

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo gaa-izhi-waniwisid (but once she became careless / forgetful) aw oshkiniigikwe (that young woman);</span>

12) > **mii dash apii zaaga’amogobanen gaa-izhi-inaasamabid ningaabii’anong**

so when out of doors she went (and) afterwards sat down facing the west,

<span class="l-gloss">mii dash apii zaaga’amogobanen (so then when she had gone outdoors) gaa-izhi-inaasamabid ningaabii’anong (and sat down facing the west),</span>

13) > **mii dash apii noondamogobanen biidweweyaanimadinig.**

then heard she the sound of wind coming hitherward.

<span class="l-gloss">mii dash apii noondamogobanen (then she heard) biidweweyaanimadinig (the sound of wind coming hither).</span>

14) > **Gaa-izhi-kikendang dakaashid i’iwidi ozaaga’amoowining.** When she felt it, she was chilled there at the place of the passage out.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-kikendang dakaashid (when she felt herself grow cold) i’iwidi ozaaga’amoowining (there at the place of the doorway / passage out).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-pasigonjised (then she sprang quickly to her feet). “Nimaamaa, nashke giniin (“my mother, look at me), gaa-izhi-ayaayaan\! (the state I am in\!)</span>

15) > **Mii dash ezhi-pasigonjised. “nimaamaa, nashke giniin, gaa-izhi ayaayaan\!**

Accordingly she quickly leaped to her feet. "Oh my mother, behold the state that I am in\!

16) > **Mii ganabach gaa-izhiyan ezhi-ayaayaan.”**

It may be that what you told me of is the matter with me."

Mii ganabach gaa-izhiyan ezhi-ayaayaan.” (it may be that what you told me of is what is happening to me.”)

17) > **Gaa-izhi-kanoonaad a’aw mindimoyenh iniw odaanisan: “Memindage gigii-inigaatoon kiiyaw.”**

Then spoke the old woman to her daughter, saying: “Exceeding harm have you done to yourself.”

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-kanoonaad a’aw mindimoyenh iniw odaanisan (then the old woman spoke to her daughter): “Memindage gigii-inigaatoon kiiyaw.” (“exceedingly you have harmed yourself.”)</span>

18) > **Aaniish mii sa iw zhigwa gii-mawid aw mindimoyenh. “Aaniish mii go iw nindaanis, inigaatooyan kiiyaw.**

So therefore then did the old woman weep. “Now therefore, my daughter, have you done yourself a hurt.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa iw zhigwa gii-mawid aw mindimoyenh (and so then the old woman wept). “Aaniish mii go iw nindaanis (“so it is, my daughter), inigaatooyan kiiyaw (you have done harm to your own body).</span>

19) > **Nashke goto ge-izhiwebiziyan.**

You shall learn what will happen to you.

Nashke goto ge-izhiwebiziyan (behold what is going to happen to you).

20) > **Awiya gii-piindigewag kiiyawing, mii go iw nindaanis, inigaaziyan.**

> Certain beings have entered into your body: therefore, my daughter, you are in a pitiable state.

<span class="l-gloss">Awiya gii-piindigewag kiiyawing (certain beings have entered into your body), mii go iw nindaanis, inigaaziyan (and so, my daughter, you are in a pitiable state).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin anishinaabewisiiwag (they are not human beings) gaa-piindigewaad imaa sa kiiyawing (those that have entered there into your body).</span>

21) > **Gaawiin anishinaabewisiiwag gaa-piindigewaad imaa sa kiiyawing.** They are not human beings that have gone inside of you there.

Gaawiin waasa iw ji-niigiwaad (it is not far off, the time they will be born).

22) > **Gaawiin waasa iw ji-niigiwaad.**

The time is not far distant before they will be born.

23) > **Na, mii dash igiw gaa-gosagwaa.”**

Therefore it was they whom I feared.”

Na, mii dash igiw gaa-gosagwaa.” (look, these are the ones I feared.”)

24) > **Aaniish nashke dash gomaa go apii mii sa go gii-noondawaad a’aw isa mindimoyenh awiya madwegiikaandinid.**

Now, lo, in the course of time did the old women hear the sound of beings that were quarrelling one with another.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish nashke dash gomaa go apii (now, behold, after a while) mii sa go gii-noondawaad a’aw isa mindimoyenh (the old woman heard) awiya madwegiikaandinid (the sound of beings quarrelling with one another).</span>

25) > **Ogii-kikenimaa anaamaya’ii danwewedaminid.**

She knew by the sound of their voices that they were inside.

Ogii-kikenimaa anaamaya’ii danwewedaminid (she knew by their voices that they were inside her).

26) > **Mii dash moozhag gii-mawid a’aw mindimoyenh.**

And so without ceasing did the old woman weep.

Mii dash moozhag gii-mawid a’aw mindimoyenh (and so the old woman wept without ceasing).

27) > **Mii dash geget i’imaa gii-kwayakwenimaad ji-pimaadizinid iniw odaanisan.**

It was true that then was she sure that her daughter would not live.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget i’imaa gii-kwayakwenimaad (it was then she was sure / convinced) ji-pimaadizinid iniw odaanisan (that her daughter would not live).</span>

28) > **Aaniish ogii-noondawaa sa i’iw gaakaandinid, i’imaa omisadaaning danwewedaminid.**

Now she heard them quarrelling one with another, there in her (daughter's) belly the sound of their voices could be heard.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish ogii-noondawaa sa i’iw gaakaandinid (now she heard them quarrelling), i’imaa omisadaaning danwewedaminid (there in her belly the sound of their voices could be heard).</span>

29) > **O’ow idash madwe-ikidowan: “Niin ninga-saziikis.”**

This was what one was heard to say: “I wish to be the first brought forth.”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash madwe-ikidowan (and this is what one was heard to say): “Niin ninga-saziikis.” (“I will be the first-born / come out first.”)</span>

30) > **“Gaawiin” madwe-ikido bezhig biinish go ikido aw bezhig: “Gaawiin gidaa-saziikizisii. Niin isa ninga-saziikis.”**

“No,” one was heard saying, even did one say, “you cannot be the first-born. I am the one to be the eldest.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin” madwe-ikido bezhig (“no,” one was heard to say) biinish go ikido aw bezhig (until that one said): “Gaawiin gidaa-saziikizisii (“you cannot be the eldest). Niin isa ninga-saziikis.” (I am the one to be first-born.”)</span>

31) > **Aaniish mii sa pane mawid aw mindimoyenh bizindawaad i’iw gaakaandinid.**

It was natural that all the while the old woman should weep as she listened to them quarrelling one with another.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa pane mawid aw mindimoyenh (so all the while the old woman wept) bizindawaad i’iw gaakaandinid (as she listened to them quarrelling).</span>

32) > **Ogii-kikenimaa aw mindimoyenh I’iw waa-dazhinid i’iw ooshisheya’.** Knowledge of them had the old woman as to how many would her grandchildren be.

Ogii-kikenimaa aw mindimoyenh i’iw waa-dazhinid i’iw ooshisheya’ (the old woman knew how many her grandchildren would be).

<span class="l-gloss">Na, mii dash ii’iw ekidowaad (look, this is what they said) nagaawebinidiwaad (as they pushed one another back) (ayaani) aanawi saaga’amowaad (when in vain they tried to go out).</span>

33) > **Na, mii dash ii’iw ekidowaad nagaawebinidiwaad (ayaani) aanawi saaga’amowaad.**

Hark\! this was what they said as they pushed one another back from the place where they tried in vain to go out.

34) > **Igiw dash aanind aano-gii-ikidowag: “gego bina\! Kiga-iniga’aanaan gosha gi-maamaanaan.**

But others of them tried, but to no purpose, to say: “Don't, please\! We shall surely do injury to our mother.

<span class="l-gloss">Igiw dash aanind aano-gii-ikidowag (but some of them tried in vain to say): “Gego bina\! (“don’t, please\!) Kiga-iniga’aanaan gosha gi-maamaanaan (we will surely hurt our mother).</span>

35) > **Weweni pina go zaaga’andaa,” aano-ikidowag.**

In proper order please let us go out," (thus) in vain they said.

Weweni pina go zaaga’andaa,” aano-ikidowag (“let us go out in proper order,” they said in vain).

36) > **Gaawiin idash izhi-minwedaziiwag igiw waa-saziikizijig.** But not content with the idea were they who wished to be the eldest.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin idash izhi-minwedaziiwag (but they were not content) igiw waa-saziikizijig (those who wished to be the eldest).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw ekidowaad (so then they said) zhigwa anooj igo wii-izhi-saaga’amowaad (that now they wished to go out from different places).</span>

37) > **Mii dash i’iw ekidowaad zhigwa anooj igo wii-izhi-saaga’amowaad.** Therefore then they said that now from different places they wished to go out.

Bezhig ogii-waabandaan waasakonenig (one of them saw where there was light).

38) > **Bezhig ogii-waabandaan waasakonenig.**

One saw where there was light.

39) > **“Aaniish mii imaa gwayak niin waa-izhaayaan.”**

“Now, straight by this very way do I wish to go.”

“Aaniish mii imaa gwayak niin waa-izhaayaan.” (“now, straight through here is where I wish to go.”)

40) > **Mii dash apii gii-gagwedanimidiwaad awenen aw nitam ge-saaga’ang,**

And so while they were debating among themselves as to who should be the first to go out,

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash apii gii-gagwedanimidiwaad (and so while they were debating among themselves) awenen aw nitam ge-saaga’ang (as to who should be the first to go out),</span>

41) > **mii dash iw gaa-izhi-piigoshkawaawaad iniw omaamaawaan.**

then was when they burst open their mother.

mii dash iw gaa-izhi-piigoshkawaawaad iniw omaamaawaan (then it was that they burst their mother open).

42) > **Wiikaa go ningoji babaa-inaabid mindimoyenh ogii-mikaan miskwi bangii.**

After a while at a certain place where round about the old woman was looking she found a clot of blood.

<span class="l-gloss">Wiikaa go ningoji babaa-inaabid mindimoyenh (after a while, looking about here and there, the old woman) ogii-mikaan miskwi bangii (found a little blood — a clot of blood).</span>

43) > **Mii sa wiigwaas ezhi-paapagonang.**

Thereupon some birch-bark she began peeling (from a tree).

Mii sa wiigwaas ezhi-paapagonang (thereupon she began peeling some birch-bark).

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash imaa gaa-izhi-atood wiigwaasing i’iw miskwi (and then she put the blood there on the birch-bark) gaa-izhi-kashkashkwemaginang (she folded the bark over it), mii sa gaa-izhina’inang (and so laid it away).</span>

44) > **Mii dash imaa gaa-izhi-atood wiigwaasing i’iw miskwi gaa-izhi kashkashkwemaginang, mii sa gaa-izhina’inang.**

And now, after she had put the blood upon the bark, she then folded the bark over it, and laid it away.

45) > **Aaniish piijiinag ogii-waabandaan.**

> Naturally, by and by she looked at it.

Aaniish piijiinag ogii-waabandaan (by and by she looked at it).

46) > **Ningoding igo aapashkwemaginang ogii-waabamaan abinoojiiyan, mii go i’iw gii- ganoonigod, o’ow dash ogii-igoon:**

Now, once when she opened the bark she beheld a babe, whereupon she was addressed, and this is what she was told:

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo aapashkwemaginang (once when she opened the bark) ogii-waabamaan abinoojiiyan (she beheld a child), mii go i’iw gii-ganoonigod (whereupon she was spoken to), o’ow dash ogii-igoon (and this is what she was told):</span>

47) > **“Nookomis,” ogii-igoon apii genoonigod.**

“O my grandmother\!” she was told at the time that she was addressed.

“Nookomis,” ogii-igoon apii genoonigod (“my grandmother,” she was told at the time she was addressed).

48) > **Aaniish mii sa zhigwa ogii-igoon: “Gi-kikenim na ayaawiyaan? Niin isa Nenabosho.”**

> So now this was what she was told: “Do you know who I am? Why, I am Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa zhigwa ogii-igoon (so now this is what she was told): “Gi-kikenim na ayaawiyaan? (“do you know who I am?) Niin isa Nenabosho.” (Why, I am Nenabosho.”)</span>
