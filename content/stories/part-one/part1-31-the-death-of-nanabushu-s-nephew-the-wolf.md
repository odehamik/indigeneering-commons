<span class="l-gloss">(31) The Death of Nenabosho’s Nephew, the Wolf (1)</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aatayaa (ah\!), geget minwendam Nenabosho (truly pleased was Nenabosho).</span>

1)  > **Aatayaa, geget minwendam Nenabosho.**

Ah, truly pleased was Nenabosho.

<span class="l-gloss">Wayaabaninig (on the next morning) goziwag (they moved camp).</span>

2)  > **Wayaabaninig goziwag([<span class="underline">i</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)).**

On the morrow \[morning\] they moved camp.

<span class="l-gloss">Bezhig igo odoozhiman (one of his nephews) gaye wiinawaa (along with them) ingoji igo izhi-maajaawag (off in another direction they went).</span>

3)  > **Bezhig igo odoozhiman gaye wiinawaa ingoji igo izhi-maajaawag.**

And with one of his nephews he went off in another direction.

<span class="l-gloss">Oganoonigoon iniw odoozhiman (he was addressed by his nephew): “Nenabosho, besho go giga-nibaamin (“Nenabosho, not far off shall we sleep for the night.”)</span>

4)  > **Oganoonigoon iniw([<span class="underline">ii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) odoozhiman: “Nenabosho, besho go giga-nibaamin.”**

> He was addressed by his nephew saying: “Nenabosho, not far away shall we stop for the night.”

Mii sa ena’anaad odoozhiman (and so he followed the trail of his nephew).

5)  > **Mii sa ena’anaad odoozhiman.**

> And so he followed the tracks of his nephew.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaa go apii zhigwa (some distance along by now) owaabamaan anidewaninig namadabinid (he saw him sitting in a spot bare of snow).</span>

6)  > **Gomaa go apii zhigwa owaabamaan anidewaninig\*([<span class="underline">iii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) namadabinid.**

Some distance on the way he saw (his nephew) seated in a spot free from snow.

<span class="l-gloss">“Noos (“my father), mii omaa ji-nibaaying (here is where we will sleep),” Nenabosho odigoon (Nenabosho was told).</span>

7)  > **“Noos, mii omaa ji-nibaaying,” Nenabosho odigoon.**

“My father, here is a place for us to sleep,” Nenabosho was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-ishkwaa-wiisiniwaad (after they had finished eating), aweningwaami (he settled in to sleep).</span>

8)  > **Gaa-ishkwaa-wiisiniwaad, aweningwaami.\***

After they had finished eating, he made ready to sleep.

Gii-zhi-gii-nibaad (then he had a sleep).

9)  > **Gii-zhi-gii-nibaad (gii-zhi-nibaad?).**

He then had a nap.

<span class="l-gloss">Megwaa go nanaamadabid (while he was still sitting up) baamaa go wenjimawinid Nenaboshoowan (all at once Nenabosho burst out weeping).</span>

10) > **Megwaa go nanaamadabid baamaa go wenjimawinid Nenaboshoowan.**

And while (the wolf) was yet sitting up, all of a sudden into weeping burst Nenabosho.

Azhijiizhaabinaad (then he waved him off with his hand).

11) > **Azhijiizhaabinaad.\***

<span class="l-gloss">Then he waved to him with the hand.([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Shii (“foh), indashkaa niin nimaanaabamigodog (I suppose he is having a bad dream about me),” odinaan (he said of him).</span>

12) > **“Shii, indashkaa niin nimaanaabamigodog,”([<span class="underline">iv</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) odinaan.**

“Foh, (I) fancy that he probably is having a bad dream about me,” he said of him.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa dayenibaanid (when he had slept enough), goshkoziwan (he woke up).</span>

13) > **Zhigwa dayenibaanid,([<span class="underline">v</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) goshkoziwan.**

When (Nenabosho) had sleep enough, he then woke up.

Aaniish, danakamigizi a’aw ma’iingan (now, busy at work was the wolf).

14) > **Aaniish, danakamigizi a’aw ma’iingan.**

Now, busy at work was the wolf.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniin enaabandaman (“what were you dreaming) gii-mawiyan? (that you wept?”)</span>

15) > **“Aaniin enaabandaman([<span class="underline">vi</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) gii-mawiyan?”**

“What were you dreaming about, that you should weep?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Edawaa (“alas), nindoozhim (my nephew) gi-maanaabamin (I had a bad dream about you).</span>

16) > **“Edawaa, nindoozhim gi-maanaabamin.**

“Ah, my nephew, I had a bad dream about you.

<span class="l-gloss">Waabang (tomorrow) a’aw mooz wii-bimi-nazhawad (you will go in pursuit of that moose).</span>

17) > **Waabang a’aw mooz wii-bimi-nazhawad.([<span class="underline">vii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))**

Tomorrow you will pursue a moose.

<span class="l-gloss">Ambe sa noo (please now), gego ishkwaa pagidooken i’iw mitigoons ziibiinsing (do not put off throwing the little stick into the brook) mii sa wa go zhigwa debaabamad a’aw mooz (even when you have the moose in sight).</span>

18) > **Ambe sa noo, gego ishkwaa pagidooken i’iw mitigoons ziibiinsing mii sa wa go zhigwa debaabamad a’aw mooz.**

> Please don’t you delay throwing a stick into the brook, even though you are then in sight of the moose.

Mii go iw wii-izhi-minjimendan.” (do try to keep that in mind.”)

19) > **Mii go iw wii-izhi-minjimendan.”**

Now, do try to keep that in mind.”

Zhigwa sa waabanini (now the morrow had come).

20) > **Zhigwa sa waabanini.**

Now the morrow was at hand.

<span class="l-gloss">Gigizheb maajaawan wii-biminizha’igenid (in the morning he set off to go in pursuit), Nenabosho ani-maajaa (Nenabosho started out), odoozhiman bima’anaad (following along the trail of his nephew).</span>

21) > **Gigizheb maajaawan wii-biminizha’igenid, Nenabosho ani-maajaa, odoozhiman bima’anaad.**

> In the morning, when (the Wolf) departed to go in pursuit (of game), Nenabosho set out; in the trail of his nephew he followed.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa ezhikawenid (now, by the trail he had made) i’iw ozhikawenigobanen iniw moozoon (the trail by which he was stalking the moose).</span>

22) > **Zhigwa sa ezhikawenid i’iw ozhikawenigobanen([<span class="underline">viii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) iniw moozoon.**

<span class="l-gloss">Now, by the trail he made, (the Wolf) was stalking the moose.([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))</span>

Aaniish aapiji ziigwanini (by the way, it was well on toward spring).

23) > **Aaniish aapiji ziigwanini.**

By the way, it was well on towards spring.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa iw ena’anaad (that was how he followed his trail) gaye mii iwe iw ezhi-nametoonid iniw odoozhiman (and that was how he could tell by the trail what his nephew was doing); mitigoons ani-apagidoonid (a little stick he had flung ahead) i’imaa bangii ziibiinswakamigaanig (down there at the little dry brook-bed);</span>

24) > **Mii sa iw ena’anaad gaye mii iwe iw ezhi-nametoonid iniw odoozhiman; mitigoons ani-apagidoonid i’imaa bangii ziibiinswakamigaanig;**

> That was how he trailed up (his nephew), and that was how he could tell by the trail (what) his nephew was doing; (he saw) that (his nephew) had flung a small stick ahead on going down into the dry bed of a little brook;

Zhigwa sa debaabamaanigoban iiniw moozoon (where then he had caught sight of the moose);

25) > **Zhigwa sa debaabamaanigoban iiniw moozoon;**

<span class="l-gloss">(He saw) where (the Wolf) had come in sight of the moose at the time;</span>

Mii dash geget gichi-enigok maajaanigobanen iiniw odoozhiman (and then truly with great speed his nephew had gone);

26) > **Mii dash geget gichi-enigok maajaanigobanen iiniw odoozhiman;**

and then really with great speed was his nephew going at the time;

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw zhigwa debibinaanigobanen a’aw ma’iingan (and then the wolf was overtaking it) aapiji bangii ziibiinswakamigaanig (at the bed of a very small dry brook).</span>

27) > **Mii dash i’iw zhigwa debibinaanigobanen([<span class="underline">ix</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) a’aw ma’iingan aapiji bangii ziibiinswakamigaanig.**

> And then now was the Wolf overtaking (the moose) at where there was a dry bed of a very small brook.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go miinooj aazhawigwaashkwanigobanen (but in a careless moment, when he tried to leap across), gaa-izhi-dishkiikaag i’iw ziibiins (the little brook spread apart), mii dash imaa naanaawagaam gaa-izhi-bangishing (and there in the very middle of the stream he fell).</span>

28) > **Mii go miinooj aazhawigwaashkwanigobanen, gaa-izhi-dishkiikaag i’iw ziibiins, mii dash imaa naanaawagaam gaa-izhi-bangishing.**

> But in an unguarded moment, when he tried to take it with a leap, apart spread the brook, and so far out yonder in the middle of the stream he fell.([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))

Pane go gaa-saswenig iiniw otawagan (and at once there was a ringing in his ears).

29) > **Pane go gaa-saswenig([<span class="underline">x</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) iiniw otawagan.**

And at once there was a ringing in his ears.

Mii sa go i’iw gaawiin gii-mooshkamosig (and then he did not come up to the surface).

30) > **Mii sa go i’iw gaawiin gii-mooshkamosig.**

And then he did not come up to the surface.

Aaniish mii sa gii-wanendang ji-apagidood iw mitigoons (well, this was because he had forgotten to throw the little stick).

31) > **Aaniish mii sa gii-wanendang ji-apagidood iw mitigoons.**

Well, this was because he had forgotten to throw the little stick (ahead of him).

<span class="l-gloss">Nenabosho zhigwa odebaabandaan ziibi bimitigweyaanig (Nenabosho then caught sight of a river flowing by), pane odoozhiman bekobiikawenid (straight down to the water he trailed his nephew).</span>

32) > **Nenabosho zhigwa odebaabandaan ziibi bimitigweyaanig, pane odoozhiman bekobiikawenid.**

> Nenabosho then came into sight of a river that went flowing by, straight on down to the water he trailed his nephew.

<span class="l-gloss">“Edawaa\! (“alas\!) Mii maawiin iniw gii-nisaabaawed a’aw nindoozhim.” (it may be that that nephew of mine has drowned.”)</span>

33) > **“Edawaa\! Mii maawiin iniw gii-nisaabaawed([<span class="underline">xi</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) a’aw nindoozhim.”**

“Alas\! It is possible that that nephew of mine has drowned.”

<span class="l-gloss">Eni-izhi-aazhawa’od (then he crossed over to the other side); miziwe niisaajiwan aano-izhaa (everywhere downstream he went, but in vain).</span>

34) > **Eni-izhi-aazhawa’od; miziwe niisaajiwan aano-izhaa.**

Then on over to the other side he went; everywhere downstream he went, but in vain.

Mii sa gaawiin ingoji oname’aasiin (and so nowhere did he find a trace of him).

35) > **Mii sa gaawiin ingoji oname’aasiin.**

And so nowhere saw he a sign of him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gichi-enigok ezhi-mawid Nenabosho (with great force then wept Nenabosho); mii sa gabe-giizhik babaamawid (whereupon all day long he wandered about weeping), aano-nandonewaad iiniw odoozhiman (seeking his nephew in vain).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Shkwaach midaasogon aano-nandone’iged (for a full ten days he searched in vain), mii sa gaawiin omikawaasiin (and so he did not find him).</span>

36) > **Gichi-enigok ezhi-mawid Nenabosho; mii sa gabe-giizhik babaamawid, aano-nandonewaad iiniw odoozhiman.**

> With great affliction then wept Nenabosho; whereupon all day long he wandered weeping, as he sought in vain for his nephew.

Aapiji sa zhigwa gawanaandam (now he was utterly starving).

37) > **Shkwaach midaasogon aano-nandone’iged, mii sa gaawiin omikawaasiin.**

For full ten days he sought, but without result, for he did not find him.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo niisaajiwan izhi-maajaad i’iw ziibi (now once, going along down the course of the river), ningoding sa babimaazhagaamed (once while walking along the shore) owaabamaan ogiishkimanisiin agoozinid (he saw a kingfisher perched aloft) i’imaa nibiikaang imaa inaabinid (looking down there into the water).</span>

38) > **Aapiji sa zhigwa gawanaandam (gwanaandam).**

Completely now was he starving.

<span class="l-gloss">Wegoneniiwinen ogaagiimaa’aan (what should he do but sneak up on it); besho odoodisaan (close up to it he came).</span>

39) > **Ningoding igo niisaajiwan izhi-maajaad i’iw ziibi, ningoding sa babimaazhagaamed owaabamaan ogiishkimanisiin agoozinid i’imaa nibiikaang imaa inaabinid.**

> Now once while down the course of the river he was going, once while he was walking along the shore, he saw a kingfisher perched aloft (and) looking down into the water.

<span class="l-gloss">Aanawii-izhi-nawadinaad (when he tried to grab it), bangii igo obishkobinaan (he just barely missed catching it).</span>

40) > **Wegoneniiwinen ogaagiimaa’aan;([<span class="underline">xii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) besho odoodisaan.**

What should he do but slip stealthily up to it; nigh up to it he came.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw gaa-ipinaagobanen (and the place where it was handled), mii iw gii-niishkaweyaandibed a’aw ogiishkimansii (was at the tuft on the kingfisher’s head).</span>

41) > **Aanawii-izhi-nawadinaad, bangii igo obishkobinaan.([<span class="underline">xiii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))**

In an attempt to grab it he just missed catching it.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-ganoonigod ogiishkimansiin (then he was addressed by the kingfisher): “Ojiideyaab Nenabosho niwii-wiindamowaaban (“about the anal gut I was going to tell Nenabosho) wiin iiniw odoozhimaan.” (concerning his nephew.”)</span>

42) > **Mii dash i’iw gaa-ipinaagobanen, mii iw gii-niishkaweyaandibed\* a’aw ogiishkimansii.**

> And the place where the kingfisher was seized (handled) at the time was by the tuft on its head.

<span class="l-gloss">“Diwe, nishiimisaa\! (“why, my little brother\!) Wegonen waa-wiindamawiyan? (what were you going to tell me?”)</span>

43) > **Ezhi-ganoonigod ogiishkimansiin: “Ojiideyaab Nenabosho niwii-wiindamowaaban wiin iiniw odoozhimaan.”**

> Then he was addressed by the kingfisher saying: “About the anal gut of his nephew was I going to tell Nenabosho.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Kaa, gidoozhim (“why, about your nephew). Ninganawaabamaa imaa nibiikaang gii-inaabiyaan (I was watching for him there as I looked into the water); mii aw genawaabamag (he was the one I was watching for).</span>

44) > **“Diwe, nishiimisaa\! Wegonen waa-wiindamawiyan?”**

O my little brother\! What were you going to tell me?”

<span class="l-gloss">Nenabosho, ambe sa noo (Nenabosho, listen now), giga-wiindamoon gaa-izhiwebizid (I will tell you what happened to him) a’awe menjinawesiyan (to him for whom you are longing).</span>

45) > **“Kaa, gidoozhim. Ninganawaabamaa imaa nibiikaang gii-inaabiyaan; mii aw genawaabamag.**

> “Why, about your nephew. I was watching for him yonder, where I was looking into the water; he was the one I was watching for.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii giniin i’iw gaa-izhi-ayaad a’aw gidoozhim (now this is what became of your nephew): ogimaa mishibizhii ogii-wodaapinaan iiniw gidoozhiman (the chief of the big lynxes has taken your nephew).</span>

46) > **Nenabosho, ambe sa noo, giga-wiindamoon gaa-izhiwebizid a’awe menjinawesiyan.([<span class="underline">xiv</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))**

> Nenabosho, listen\! I will declare to you what happened to him for whom you have a longing.

<span class="l-gloss">Imaa dash zaagidawaag iw ziibi (yonder where the river flows out into the open) mii imaa ayaag i’iw minisinadaawangaa (there is an island of sand); mii dash imaa endazhi-gii-zhigishing a’aw ogimaa mishibizhi (and it is there the chief of the big lynxes lies and passes the day) giishpin mizhakwadinig (when the sky is clear).</span>

47) > **Mii giniin i’iw gaa-izhi-ayaad a’aw gidoozhim: ogimaa mishibizhii ogii-wodaapinaan iiniw gidoozhiman.**

> Now, this was what became of your nephew: the chief of the big lynxes([<span class="underline">5</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) has seized your nephew.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji minogiizhigadinig (when the day is very fine) mii bijiinag agwaataad i’imaa minisinadaawangaang (then at last he comes out of the water onto that sandy island).</span>

48) > **Imaa dash zaagidawaag iw ziibi mii imaa ayaag i’iw minisinadaawangaa; mii dash imaa endazhi-gii-zhigishing([<span class="underline">xv</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) a’aw ogimaa mishibizhi giishpin mizhakwadinig.**

> Now, yonder, where the river flows out into the open, is an island of sand; and it is there that the chief of the big lynxes whiles the day away when the sky is clear.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenabosho, giishpin babizinidawisiwan (Nenabosho, if you \[do not\] heed what I say), gidaa-waabamaa a’aw gidoozhima’iwayaan (you can behold the skin of your nephew), gii-pakonaa gosha a’aw gidoozhim (for flayed indeed was that nephew of yours).</span>

49) > **Aapiji minogiizhigadinig mii bijiinag agwaataad i’imaa minisinadaawangaang.**

> When the day is very pleasant, then from the water he proceeds out upon the sandy island.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go i’iw ge-zhi-debwetawiyan (therefore shall you believe me) gaa-izhiwebizid i’iwe gaa-onji-wani’ad gidoozhim (concerning what befell your nephew when you lost him).</span>

50) > **Nenabosho, giishpin babizinidawisiwan,([<span class="underline">xvi</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html)) gidaa-waabamaa a’aw gidoozhima’iwayaan, gii-pakonaa gosha a’aw gidoozhim.**

> Nenabosho, if you \[do not\] heed what I say, you can behold the skin of your nephew, for flayed was that nephew of yours.

Baataniinowan genawenimigod (by many is he guarded).

51) > **Mii go i’iw ge-zhi-debwetawiyan gaa-izhiwebizid i’iwe gaa-onji-wani’ad gidoozhim.**

> Therefore shall you believe me concerning what befell your nephew when you lost him.

<span class="l-gloss">Giishpin idash wiin noondawiyan i’iw ge-ininaan (now, if you heed what I tell you), mii go iw waa-doodawad igo (then whatever you wish to do to him), ji-doodawadiban (that you may do to him).</span>

52) > **Baataniinowan genawenimigod.**

> By many is he guarded.

Ambe sa noo mitigwaab giga-ozhi’aa (therefore make yourself a bow).

53) > **Giishpin idash wiin noondawiyan i’iw ge-ininaan, mii go iw waa-doodawad igo, ji-doodawadiban.([<span class="underline">xvii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))**

> Now, if you harken to what I say to you, then whatsoever you may wish to do to (the chief of the big lynxes), that you may do to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gii-kiizhi’ad idash (and when you have finished it), miinawaa giga-ozhitoon i’iw bikwak i’iwe ge-yaabajitooyan (you shall next make the arrow you are to use).</span>

54) > **Ambe sa noo mitigwaab giga-ozhi’aa.**

> Therefore a bow do you make.

Giiga-miinin i’iw ge-naaba’igeyan.” (I will give you what you shall use for a point.”)

55) > **Gii-kiizhi’ad idash, miinawaa giga-ozhitoon i’iw bikwak i’iwe ge-yaabajitooyan.**

And when you have finished it, you shall next make the arrow which you are to use.

Nenabosho omiinigoon bezhig iiniw oshkazhiini (Nenabosho was given one of its claws).

56) > **Giiga-miinin i’iw ge-naaba’igeyan.”([<span class="underline">xviii</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/the-death-of-nenaboshorsquos-nephew-the-wolf-footnotes.html))**

I will give you what you shall use for a point (on your arrow).”

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-ganoonigod (then he was addressed): “Nenabosho, mii awe ge-naaba’igeyan i’iw gi-bikwak (“Nenabosho, that is what you shall use for the point of your arrow).</span>
