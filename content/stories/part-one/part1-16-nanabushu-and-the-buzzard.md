<span class="l-fiero">**(16) Nanabush and the Buzzards**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Fiero restoration by the Ojibwe Project / Baadwewdangig — Alan Corbiere, with Dr. Alana Johns & U-Toronto LIN458 (ojibweproject.weebly.com). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Corbiere’s Fiero and Jones’s 1919 English preserved verbatim (the source's table layout was rebuilt into clean lines); gloss line added.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash ezhi-maajaad, gii-babaamosed.**</span>

And so he started away, round about he travelled.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and so) ezhi-maajaad (when he started off), gii-babaamosed (he travelled about on foot).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding igo babaamosed inaabid ishpiming awiya ogii-waabamaan namadabinid aanakwadong.**</span>

Now, once while journeying about, he looked above (and) saw some one seated upon a cloud.

<span class="l-eng">*Ningoding igo (once, just) babaamosed (as he walked about) inaabid (looking up) ishpiming (above) awiya (someone) ogii-waabamaan (he saw him) namadabinid (sitting) aanakwadong (on a cloud).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Geget omisawinawaan. “Ambe dash gaye niin,” Gii-inendam gaa-izhi-ganoonaad: “Gaawiin ina gidaa-bi-izhaasii, nishiimisa?” ogii-inaan.**</span>

Truly envious was he of him. “Would that I (could) too,” he thought, after which he addressed him, saying: “Would you not come down, my little brother?” he said to him.

<span class="l-eng">*Geget (truly) omisawinawaan (he envies him). “Ambe dash gaye niin (oh, that I too),” gii-inendam (he thought) gaa-izhi-ganoonaad (then, having spoken to him): “Gaawiin ina (not, is it) gidaa-bi-izhaasii (you could not come here), nishiimisa (my little brother)?” ogii-inaan (he said to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-igod: “Gaawiin,” ogii-igoon.**</span>

Upon which he was told: “No,” he was told.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and then) gaa-igod (what he was told): “Gaawiin (no),” ogii-igoon (he was told by him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aaniin gegii-izhinaagwag minik awiya gaa-waabamag giin idash i’iw ji-bizindawisiiwaambanen?”**</span>

“How would it look, (in view of) as many as I have seen, for you not to heed me?”

<span class="l-eng">*“Aaniin (how) gegii-izhinaagwag (would it appear) minik awiya (as many as) gaa-waabamag (I have seen) giin idash (and you) i’iw (that) ji-bizindawisiiwaambanen (you would not listen to me)?”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Nenaboozhoo, Gi-gosin.”**</span>

“Nanabushu, I am afraid of you.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu), gi-gosin (I am afraid of you).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaawiin gidaagwashisii,” odinaan.**</span>

“You have no cause to fear me.” he said to him.

<span class="l-eng">*“Gaawiin (not) gidaagwashisii (you have no reason to fear me),” odinaan (he says to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-wayeshimaad, aanish mii sa gaa-ganoonigod: “Ambe sa noo, geget sa gi-misawinawin. Gonige naa waasa gidakwaabimidog,” odinaan.**</span>

And so after (Nanabushu) had deceived him, then was he brought into conversation with him. “Oh, really, I am so envious of you\! I fancy that afar must you be able to see,” he said to him.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and so) gaa-izhi-wayeshimaad (after he had deceived him), aanish (well) mii sa (so then) gaa-ganoonigod (he was brought into talk by him): “Ambe sa noo (oh, really now), geget sa (truly indeed) gi-misawinawin (I envy you). Gonige naa (I suppose) waasa (far) gidakwaabimidog (you must be able to see),” odinaan (he says to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Eye’, Geget.”**</span>

“Yes, (that is) true.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Eye’ (yes), geget (true).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“aw,” odinaan. Mii dash gaa-izhi-inaad: “Ambe sa noo, giga-wiijiiwin.”**</span>

“Good,” he said to him. Upon which he then said to him: “Pray, let me bear you company.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Aaw (good),” odinaan (he says to him). Mii dash (and then) gaa-izhi-inaad (what he said to him): “Ambe sa noo (come now), giga-wiijiiwin (I will go along with you).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Awawa,” ogii-igoon. Mii dash gaa-izhi-ombiiwaad i’imaa aanakwadong.**</span>

“Certainly,” he was told. Whereupon up they went to yonder cloud.

<span class="l-eng">*“Awawa (certainly),” ogii-igoon (he was told). Mii dash (and then) gaa-izhi-ombiiwaad (they went up) i’imaa (over there) aanakwadong (to the cloud).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash ezhi-maajaawaad, maajiiyaasinig i’iw aanakwad.**</span>

And so when they started away, with the wind went wafting the cloud.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and so) ezhi-maajaawaad (when they set off), maajiiyaasinig (it began to be borne by the wind) i’iw aanakwad (that cloud).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding agaawaa debinaagwadini i’iw aki zhigwa odinenimigoon. “indashiidog waawiyazh nendaginitaadoodawaad. Ambe sa noo, wawiyazh ninga-doodawaa,” Gii-inendam.**</span>

By and by hardly was the earth to be seen. Then thoughts concerning him did the other entertain. “Apparently he is ever playing a trick on some one. Now, I will play a trick on him,” (thus) he thought.

<span class="l-eng">*Ningoding (by and by) agaawaa (hardly) debinaagwadini (it could be seen) i’iw aki (the earth) zhigwa (now) odinenimigoon (he is thought of by the other). “Indashiidog (apparently he) waawiyazh (in tricky fashion) nendaginitaadoodawaad (keeps tricking people), ambe sa noo (now then), wawiyazh (a trick) ninga-doodawaa (I will play on him),” gii-inendam (he thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-bakweyaasininig i’iw aanakwad.**</span>

Thereupon apart then broke the cloud by reason of the wind.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (thereupon) gaa-izhi-bakweyaasininig (when it broke apart by the wind) i’iw aanakwad (that cloud).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Miinawaa ezhi-bakweyaasininig; eshkam igo agaasaani inaa ayaawaad.**</span>

Again it broke apart on account of the wind; gradually smaller grew the space where they were.

<span class="l-eng">*Miinawaa (again) ezhi-bakweyaasininig (when it broke apart by the wind); eshkam igo (gradually) agaasaani (it grew small) inaa (the space) ayaawaad (where they were).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii gegaa go enigokwabiwaad inigokwaani.**</span>

At last almost space enough for them to sit on was how much that yet remained.

<span class="l-eng">*Gegapii (at last) gegaa go (almost) enigokwabiwaad (as much as they could sit on) inigokwaani (that much was the size).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding igo gaa-izhi-daashkikaanig bebakaan gii-namadabiwag.**</span>

And anon when it was rent asunder, then in different places they sat.

<span class="l-eng">*Ningoding igo (and just then) gaa-izhi-daashkikaanig (when it split apart) bebakaan (separately) gii-namadabiwag (they sat).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aanish miinawaa ezhi-daashkaasininig ezhi-bazigwa’onid, ezhi-booniinid i’iwedi eni-michaanig i’iw aanakwad.**</span>

So when again it blew apart, then away flew the other, alighting upon yonder place where there was a bigger cloud.

<span class="l-eng">*Aanish (so) miinawaa (again) ezhi-daashkaasininig (when it blew apart) ezhi-bazigwa’onid (the other rose up and flew), ezhi-booniinid (alighting) i’iwedi (over yonder) eni-michaanig (where it was larger) i’iw aanakwad (that cloud).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash odaano-ganoonaan: “Aaniin gediyaan, nishiimisa” ogii-inaan.**</span>

Whereupon in vain he tried to speak to him, saying: “What will become of me, my little brother?” he said to him.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (whereupon) odaano-ganoonaan (he tries in vain to speak to him): “Aaniin gediyaan (what will become of me), nishiimisa (my little brother)?” ogii-inaan (he said to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii zhigwa gii-waabandang ji-bangishing. Zhigwa miinawaa bakweyaasinini mii imaa ayaad; gegapii igo bigoshkaani.**</span>

So then he knew that he was going to fall. Then again apart flew the place where he was; and in the end it broke completely.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii zhigwa (so then) gii-waabandang (he saw it) ji-bangishing (that he would fall). Zhigwa miinawaa (now again) bakweyaasinini (it broke apart by the wind) mii imaa ayaad (right where he was); gegapii igo (in the end) bigoshkaani (it broke to pieces).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Agaawaa odebaabandaan ozhaawashkwaakamigaanig.**</span>

Faintly could he see the green of the landscape.

<span class="l-eng">*Agaawaa (faintly) odebaabandaan (he can make it out in sight) ozhaawashkwaakamigaanig (where the land is green).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-bangishing apane ii ban babimibizod.**</span>

When he fell, a long while was he falling through the air.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-bangishing (when he fell) apane (a long while) ii ban (he kept) babimibizod (flying/falling through the air).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa e-zhi-debaabandang edaadaa gichi-mitigokaani ge-izhi-bangishing.**</span>

Now, when he came in full sight (of the earth, he saw) how so dreadfully wooded was the place into which he was to fall.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa (now) e-zhi-debaabandang (when he came in full sight) edaadaa (how dreadfully) gichi-mitigokaani (it was thick with great trees) ge-izhi-bangishing (where he was to fall).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhayiigwa gaa-izhi-biinjised gichi-mitigoon wiimbizinid.**</span>

Then down he fell into a great tree that was hollow.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhayiigwa (then) gaa-izhi-biinjised (when he fell inside) gichi-mitigoon (a great tree) wiimbizinid (that was hollow).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa gaa-izhi-biinjinikised, mii sa ezhi-bwaabwaanawi’od.**</span>

Thereupon when he had dropped into the hollow, then was he unable (to get out).

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa (thereupon) gaa-izhi-biinjinikised (when he dropped down inside), mii sa (so then) ezhi-bwaabwaanawi’od (he could not help himself out).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aano-wiikwaji’od awiiya ogii-noondawaa, bibaapinid ikwewa’, mii dash gaa-ikidonid: “Mii sa o’omaa ningoji endaad gaa-ikidong waabigaag,” ikidowa’ i’iw ikwewa’.**</span>

While vainly trying (to get out), he heard some one, those were women coming laughing; and this was what they were saying: “Now, somewhere hereabouts lives a Gray Porcupine, so they say,” said the women.

<span class="l-eng">*Aano-wiikwaji’od (vainly struggling) awiiya (someone) ogii-noondawaa (he heard), bibaapinid (laughing) ikwewa’ (women), mii dash (and) gaa-ikidonid (what they said): “Mii sa o’omaa (here it is) ningoji (somewhere) endaad (he lives) gaa-ikidong (so they say) waabigaag (the Gray Porcupine),” ikidowa’ (they said) i’iw ikwewa’ (those women).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-giigidod: “Waabigaagoowiyaan endaayaan.”**</span>

Then up he spoke, saying: “I am the Gray Porcupine that dwells here.”

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-giigidod (when he spoke up): “Waabigaagoowiyaan (I am the Gray Porcupine) endaayaan (who dwells here).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**O’ow dash ikidowa’: “Gonogena mikawang a’aw waabigaag, nishiim,” ogii-inaan mii aw majiikwewis.**</span>

And this they said: “Suppose we should find the Gray Porcupine, my little sister,” to the other said she that was older.

<span class="l-eng">*O’ow dash (and this) ikidowa’ (they said): “Gonogena (suppose) mikawang (we find him) a’aw waabigaag (that Gray Porcupine), nishiim (my little sister),” ogii-inaan (she said to her) mii aw majiikwewis (she, the elder one).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaa na giin gi-noondawaasii?” odigoon iniw oshimeyan. “Gi-mikawaanaan, maawiin.”**</span>

“And did you not hear him?” she was asked by her little sister. “We have found him, maybe.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Gaa na giin (and did you not) gi-noondawaasii (hear him)?” odigoon (she is told) iniw oshimeyan (by her little sister). “Gi-mikawaanaan (we have found him), maawiin (maybe).”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash mii imaa miinawaa gaa-izhi-ganoonaad: “Waabigaagoowiyaan endaayaan,” ogii-inaan. Mii dash gaa-izhi-maajikawa’ogod.**</span>

And so what he said before, he said to them again: “ I am the Gray Porcupine that dwells (here),” he said to them. Whereupon they began felling the tree he was in.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and so) mii imaa miinawaa (there again) gaa-izhi-ganoonaad (when he spoke to them): “Waabigaagoowiyaan (I am the Gray Porcupine) endaayaan (who dwells here),” ogii-inaan (he said to them). Mii dash (whereupon) gaa-izhi-maajikawa’ogod (they began chopping the tree down upon him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Nishiim, gii-kawawang, awegwenina ge-ayaanigwen ge-mikawaagwen, mii aw ge-onaabemid,” ogii-inaan iniw oshiimeyan. Mii sa zhigwa geget odaano-giishkikaa’ogon.**</span>

“My little sister, when we have felled the tree, then whosoever is there, (and) whichever the one that finds him, she will be the one to have him for a husband,” she said to her little sister. And so they truly chopped away unavailingly.

<span class="l-eng">*“Nishiim (my little sister), gii-kawawang (when we have felled it), awegwenina (whoever) ge-ayaanigwen (may be there) ge-mikawaagwen (whoever finds him), mii aw (that one) ge-onaabemid (will have him for a husband),” ogii-inaan (she said to her) iniw oshiimeyan (her little sister). Mii sa zhigwa (and so now) geget (truly) odaano-giishkikaa’ogon (they chop away at it in vain).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Wiikaage sa i’imaa dayaawi a’aw weshiime’im aawid,” gii-inendam aw Nenaboozhoo.**</span>

“I wish the younger one would be there,” thought Nanabushu.

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa sa odaashkika’ogoon i’iw nandogaa’ogod, biinish majiikwewis gakina gaa-piigoga’ang; mii sa gaa-izhi-gwiinawaabamaad.**</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Now, (the women) began splitting a tree in an effort to find him, (keeping it up) till the elder sister had it all in pieces; but she had failed to find him.</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa sa (now then) odaashkika’ogoon (they split the tree on him) i’iw nandogaa’ogod (seeking him by chopping), biinish (until) majiikwewis (the elder one) gakina gaa-piigoga’ang (had chopped it all to pieces); mii sa (and so) gaa-izhi-gwiinawaabamaad (she failed to see him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa aw ikwe weshiimemaawid i’imaa eyaad gegapii gaa-izhi-bookwisdood i’iw owaagaakwad.**</span>

And then the younger women yonder, where she was, finally broke her axe.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa aw ikwe (and then that woman) weshiimemaawid (the younger one) i’imaa eyaad (where she was) gegapii (at last) gaa-izhi-bookwisdood (when she broke) i’iw owaagaakwad (her axe).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Nishiim,” odinaan apii gaa-bookwisidoonid i’iw owaagaakwadooni; “Mii sa niin ji-onaabemiyaan,” ogii-inaan iniw oshimeyan.**</span>

“My little sister,” (the elder sister) said to her after she had broken her axe, “so it will be I who will have a husband,” she said to her little sister.

<span class="l-eng">*“Nishiim (my little sister),” odinaan (she says to her) apii gaa-bookwisidoonid (when she had broken) i’iw owaagaakwadooni (her axe); “Mii sa niin (so it is I) ji-onaabemiyaan (who will have a husband),” ogii-inaan (she said to her) iniw oshimeyan (her little sister).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Wekaage sa gaye wiin.” O’ow dash gii-inendam: “Ambe sa noo, mii eta go aabiding ji-biyaabaagidood i’iw owaagaakwad,” odinenimaan.**</span>

“Would the same happen to her too\!” And this he thought: “Now, would that only once she might strike (the tree) with her axe,” was the thought he had of her.

<span class="l-eng">*“Wekaage sa (would the same be) gaye wiin (to her too)\!” O’ow dash (and this) gii-inendam (he thought): “Ambe sa noo (now then), mii eta go aabiding (just once only) ji-biyaabaagidood (that she might strike a ringing blow) i’iw owaagaakwad (with that axe),” odinenimaan (such was his thought of her).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-aazhoowinaad; zhigwa geget odaashkika’ogoon; ezhi-bazigonjised.**</span>

Then he watched for her; presently was the tree really split up by her; then up he leaped.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-aazhoowinaad (when he watched for her across); zhigwa geget (now truly) odaashkika’ogoon (the tree was split open by her); ezhi-bazigonjised (then up he sprang).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Nenaboozhoowan iniw onji-bazigonjisewan, ani-gagawaabiwan. “Gegeti go waabigaag endaad inendamoog\!”**</span>

And there was Nanabushu leaping away, falling headlong with laughter as he went. “That it actually was the home of a Gray Porcupine they thought\!”

<span class="l-eng">*Nenaboozhoowan iniw (it was Nanabushu, that one) onji-bazigonjisewan (who sprang up from there), ani-gagawaabiwan (going off tumbling/rolling). “Gegeti go (that it actually) waabigaag endaad (was a Gray Porcupine’s home) inendamoog (they thought)\!”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa gaa-ani-izhi-mamaajaad Nenaboozhoo. “Amanjigizh ge-izhichigewaanen,” Gii-inendam; “wawiyazh ji-doodawag gaye wiin?” odinenimaan iniw binesiwan.**</span>

And so upon his way continued Nanabushu. “I wonder what I shall do,” he thought, “in order that I may play a trick on him too\!” such was his thought of the bird.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa (and so) gaa-ani-izhi-mamaajaad (when he went on his way) Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu). “Amanjigizh (I wonder) ge-izhichigewaanen (what I shall do),” gii-inendam (he thought); “wawiyazh (a trick) ji-doodawag (that I might play on him) gaye wiin (too)?” odinenimaan (such was his thought) iniw binesiwan (of the bird).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-madaabiid zaaga’igan, mii dash i’imaa mishawikwam gaa-izhi-ozhishing. “Ambe sa noo, ninga- amogoog anooj, binesiwag,” Gii-inendam.**</span>

When he came out upon a lake, then far out there on the ice he went and lay down, “Now, I shall be eaten by all kinds of birds,” he willed.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-madaabiid (when he came out to the shore of) zaaga’igan (a lake), mii dash i’imaa (then over there) mishawikwam (out on the ice) gaa-izhi-ozhishing (he went and lay down). “Ambe sa noo (now then), ninga-amogoog (I shall be eaten) anooj (by all kinds of) binesiwag (birds),” gii-inendam (he willed/thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Owiikwadenimaan iniw wawiyazh gaa-doodaagod.**</span>

He formed a scheme to get the one that had done him a trick.

<span class="l-eng">*Owiikwadenimaan (he scheines for him in his mind) iniw wawiyazh (that one tricky) gaa-doodaagod (who had done it to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa geget odamogoo’ andegwa’ enooji sa go binesiwa’.**</span>

Then truly was he eaten by crows and by various kinds of birds.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa geget (then truly) odamogoo’ (he is eaten by them) andegwa’ (the crows) enooji sa go (and all sorts of) binesiwa’ (birds).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-inaad: “Gego wiin i’iwidi ninjiiding ondamishikegon,” ogii-inaa’.**</span>

Then this he said to them: “Don’t you eat upon me yonder at my buttocks,” he said to them.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (then) gaa-inaad (what he said to them): “Gego wiin (don’t you) i’iwidi (over there) ninjiiding (at my rear) ondamishikegon (eat upon me),” ogii-inaa’ (he said to them).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget gaa-izhichigenid, aaniish bane go gaa-danwewetoonid.**</span>

Whereupon truly such was what they did, and a continuous din did they keep up.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash geget (whereupon truly) gaa-izhichigenid (such was what they did), aaniish (and) bane go (continually) gaa-danwewetoonid (they kept up a din there).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa sa owaabamaan obabaamaasimoononid iniw binesiwan. “Aa, mii sa gaawiin bi-izhaasiiwan\!” aanish owiikwadenimaan ji-bi-izhaanid.**</span>

At last he then saw the bird sailing about through the air. “Alas, he is not coming\!” for he longed in his mind for him to come.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa sa (at last) owaabamaan (he saw him) obabaamaasimoononid (sailing about on the wind) iniw binesiwan (that bird). “Aa (alas), mii sa gaawiin (so he does not) bi-izhaasiiwan (come here)\!” aanish (for) owiikwadenimaan (he longed for him in his mind) ji-bi-izhaanid (that he would come).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Eshkam igo dasing gaazhigadinigin beshonaagoziwan, gegapii ezhi-booniinid; naagewaasa go i’iwidi bangishing odoonzaabamigoon.**</span>

Gradually as the days came and went, nearer it could be seen, till at last it then alighted; and a good way off from yonder place where it came down was (Nanabushu) observed.

<span class="l-eng">*Eshkam igo (gradually) dasing (as often as) gaazhigadinigin (the days passed) beshonaagoziwan (it could be seen nearer), gegapii (at last) ezhi-booniinid (when it alighted); naagewaasa go (a good way off) i’iwidi (over there) bangishing (where it came down) odoonzaabamigoon (he was observed by it).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget aapiji ogosigoon. Zhigwa sa wiikaa obi-naazikaagoon.**</span>

<span class="l-gloss">And it was true that much was he feared (by the bird). Then after a while to where he was came (the bird).</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash geget (and it was true) aapiji (much) ogosigoon (he was feared by it). Zhigwa sa wiikaa (then after a while) obi-naazikaagoon (it came up to him).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**E-zhi-bakiiwagiid, e-zhi-gwaashkwaashkwaninid.**</span>

As he raised the muscle on his calf, away went the other hopping.

<span class="l-eng">*E-zhi-bakiiwagiid (as he raised the flesh of his leg), e-zhi-gwaashkwaashkwaninid (the other went off hopping).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash zhigwa eshkam igo gaawiin doondansiiwan gegapii igo owawangawenimigoon.**</span>

And then presently it gradually became less afraid, till at last (Nanabushu) was made free and easy with.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash zhigwa (and then now) eshkam igo (gradually) gaawiin doondansiiwan (it was no longer afraid), gegapii igo (till at last) owawangawenimigoon (he was treated freely and easily by it).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii igo o’ow ikidowan: “Aaniinde giinawaa ondamaasiweg o’owidi wiininwaakoshiiganed?” Mii sa geget i’iwidi wendamigod.**</span>

Then finally this it said: “Why do you not eat of him from the small of the back, where he is fat?” Thereupon truly from that place was he eaten.

<span class="l-eng">*Gegapii igo (then finally) o’ow ikidowan (this it said): “Aaniinde (why) giinawaa (you) ondamaasiweg (do not eat there of him) o’owidi (right here) wiininwaakoshiiganed (at the small of the back where he is fat)?” Mii sa geget (thereupon truly) i’iwidi (from that place) wendamigod (he was eaten by it).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa eshkam igo waasa izhi-biindikwenowan i’imaa ojiiding, gegapii go gaawiin zaagidikwenisiiwan.**</span>

Presently farther into the anus yonder it put its neck, then at last it did not take its neck out from there.

<span class="l-eng">*Zhigwa eshkam igo (presently more and more) waasa (far) izhi-biindikwenowan (it put its neck inside) i’imaa ojiiding (yonder at the anus), gegapii go (till at last) gaawiin zaagidikwenisiiwan (it did not draw its neck out).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-bazigonjised gashkidiyaandamawaad i’iw oshtigwaanini.**</span>

Then up he sprang closing his anus tight over the other’s head.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-bazigonjised (then up he sprang) gashkidiyaandamawaad (clamping his rear shut over) i’iw oshtigwaanini (its head).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“mishanim Nenaboozhoo, ningii-gagwaanisagi’ig\!”**</span>

“Confound Nanabushu, by him am I frightfully treated\!”

<span class="l-eng">*“Mishanim (confound) Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu), ningii-gagwaanisagi’ig (I am dreadfully treated by him)\!”*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Maajiibatood i’iw zaaga’igan. Aaniish aano-bimiwiikwaji’onid.**</span>

While (Nanabushu) went running along the lake,naturally the other tried in vain to get free.

<span class="l-eng">*Maajiibatood (when he set off running) i’iw zaaga’igan (along the lake). Aaniish (naturally) aano-bimiwiikwaji’onid (the other vainly struggled along to free itself).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa zhigwa beshwaabandang wekwaagamiiwaninig i’iw zaaga’igan ezhi-bagijidiyaamaad, mii dash gaa-izhi-bangishininid imaa michaya’ii.**</span>

<span class="l-gloss">And then presently, when nearing the far end of the lake, (Nanabushu) freed (the bird) from his anus, whereupon down it fell on the ice.</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa zhigwa (and then now) beshwaabandang (when he saw it near) wekwaagamiiwaninig (the far end of the water) i’iw zaaga’igan (the lake) ezhi-bagijidiyaamaad (when he released it from his rear), mii dash (whereupon) gaa-izhi-bangishininid (it fell) imaa michaya’ii (there on the ice).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-inaad: “Wiinaange giga-igoo ji-ani-akiiwang,” ogii-inaan. “Giga-wiinenimig aw anishinaabe.”**</span>

And this was what was said to it: “Buzzard shall you be called till the end of the world,” he said to it. “For your filth will you be loathed by the people.”

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash (and then) gaa-inaad (what he said to it): “Wiinaange (Buzzard) giga-igoo (you shall be called) ji-ani-akiiwang (until the end of the world),” ogii-inaan (he said to it). “Giga-wiinenimig (you will be loathed for your filth) aw anishinaabe (by the people).”*</span>
