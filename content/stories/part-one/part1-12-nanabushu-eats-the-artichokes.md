<span class="l-fiero">**(12) Nenaboozhoo eats the Artichokes**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Fiero restoration by the Ojibwe Project / Baadwewdangig — Alan Corbiere, with Dr. Alana Johns & U-Toronto LIN458 (ojibweproject.weebly.com). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Corbiere’s Fiero and Jones’s 1919 English preserved verbatim (the source's table layout was rebuilt into clean lines); gloss line added.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-ani-izhi-mamaajaad. Ningoding igo babimosed awiya ogii-waabamaa’.**</span>

So then upon his way he slowly went along. And once while travelling along he saw some creatures.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash gaa-ani-izhi-mamaajaad (so then he went slowly along on his way). Ningoding igo (and once) babimosed awiya (while walking along, someone) ogii-waabamaa’ (he saw them).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aaniin ezhinikaazoyeg?" ogii-inaa’. “Gonige gidamogoomwaadog?"**</span>

"What are you called?" he said to them. "Wonder if you may be eaten for food \!"

<span class="l-eng">*“Aaniin ezhinikaazoyeg?" (“what are you called?”) ogii-inaa’ (he said to them). “Gonige (“wonder if) gidamogoomwaadog?" (you might be eaten for food?”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Eye’,” ogii-igoo’. Mii dash gaa-igod: “Eye’, geget nindamogoomin.”**</span>

"Yes," he was told. Now, this was what he was told: “Yes, truly we are eaten for food.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Eye’,” (“yes,”) ogii-igoo’ (he was told). Mii dash gaa-igod (now this is what he was told): “Eye’, geget (“yes, truly) nindamogoomin.” (we are eaten for food.”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aaniish enikaageyeg i'i’w isa niibiwa amogooyeg?"**</span>

"What is your effect upon one after a great deal of you has been eaten?"

<span class="l-eng">*“Aaniish enikaageyeg (“what effect do you have) i’i’w isa niibiwa amogooyeg?" (when a great deal of you has been eaten?”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaawiin sa wiin igo gegoo nindinikaagesiimin. Mii sa eta go i'iw boogisikaageyaang."**</span>

“No (evil) effect of any sort do we leave. The only thing we do is to make one windy in the stomach.”

<span class="l-eng">*“Gaawiin sa wiin igo gegoo (“no, nothing at all) nindinikaagesiimin (do we cause as an effect). Mii sa eta go i’iw (the only thing it is) boogisikaageyaang." (that we make one windy in the stomach.”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-amwaad, aaniish ogii-igoo’ I’iw amondwaa; baamaa gaa-de-amwaad ogii-pooni’aaw.**</span>

Thereupon he ate them, for he was told that they were good to eat ; not till he had had his fill of them did he leave them alone.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash gaa-izhi-amwaad (thereupon he ate them), aaniish ogii-igoo’ (for he was told) i’iw amondwaa (that they were eaten); baamaa gaa-de-amwaad (not until he had eaten his fill of them) ogii-pooni’aaw (did he leave them alone).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Geget sa gii-wiingipogosiim,” ogii-naa.’**</span>

“Truly a pleasing taste you have," he said to them.

<span class="l-eng">*“Geget sa gii-wiingipogosiim,” (“truly you have a pleasing taste,”) ogii-naa.’ (he said to them).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash apii ani-mamaajaad.**</span>

And so then he kept straight on his course of travel.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash apii ani-mamaajaad (and so then he went on his way again).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding igo ani-babimosed baamaa go daadaanaaming, “Boo’\!” inwenid ezhi-maajiipatood,**</span>

Now, once as he was travelling along, of a sudden at the rear, "Po\!" came a sound. As he started running,

<span class="l-eng">*Ningoding igo (now once) ani-babimosed (as he was walking along) baamaa go daadaanaaming (suddenly from behind him), “Boo’\!” inwenid (“Po\!” something sounded), ezhi-maajiipatood (then he started running),*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Awenen gaa-inwed?" gii-inendam.**</span>

"Who was it made that noise?" he thought.

<span class="l-eng">*“Awenen gaa-inwed?" (“who was it that made that sound?”) gii-inendam (he thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Waasa begamibatood.**</span>

Afar was he now getting while on the run.

<span class="l-eng">*Waasa begamibatood (far off was he getting as he ran).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Megwaa go miinawaa ani-babimosed, “Boo’\!” inwewan awiya, ezhi-abamibagizod, “awenen gaa-inwed?" gii-inendam.**</span>

And while again he was walking along, "Po\!" was the sound something uttered; when round he whirled, "Who was it made that sound?" he thought.

<span class="l-eng">*Megwaa go miinawaa ani-babimosed (and while again he was walking along), “Boo’\!” inwewan awiya (“Po\!” something sounded), ezhi-abamibagizod (when he whirled around), “awenen gaa-inwed?" (“who was it that made that sound?”) gii-inendam (he thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Megwaa go miinawaa ani-babimosed, baanimaa go wenji-boogidid ezhi-maajiibatood.**</span>

And while again he was walking along, suddenly from the place where he broke wind he started running.

<span class="l-eng">*Megwaa go miinawaa ani-babimosed (and while again he was walking along), baanimaa go wenji-boogidid (suddenly from where he broke wind) ezhi-maajiibatood (he started running).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Ambe sa noo, ninga-gagwenaganaa a’aw gaa-inwed,” gii-inendam.**</span>

“Well, now, I will try to leave behind the one that is making the sound," he thought.

<span class="l-eng">*“Ambe sa noo (“well now), ninga-gagwenaganaa (I will try to leave behind) a’aw gaa-inwed,” (the one that made the sound,”) gii-inendam (he thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gwejikoko eninanaawajised baamaa go miinawaa, “poonh’\!” enwenid.**</span>

Just as he was about to slacken his speed, then suddenly again, "Po\!" was the sound something made.

<span class="l-eng">*Gwejikoko eninanaawajised (just as he was about to slacken his speed — \*stem flagged\*) baamaa go miinawaa (then suddenly again), “poonh’\!” enwenid (“Po\!” something sounded).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa zegizid. “Ambe sa noo, ninga-akamawaa a’aw gaa-inwed,” gii-inendam.**</span>

Thereupon he became afraid. "Well, now, I will watch for the one that uttered the sound," he thought.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa zegizid (thereupon he became afraid). “Ambe sa noo (“well now), ninga-akamawaa (I will lie in wait for) a’aw gaa-inwed,” (the one that made the sound,”) gii-inendam (he thought).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ezhi-akandwaadang i’iw omiikana; mii sa go gii-zhi-azhe-giiwe, mii dash imaa wenzaabandang i’iw omiikana.**</span>

Then he lay in wait for it beside the path; and then a short ways back he went, and so from there by the road he watched for it.

<span class="l-eng">*Ezhi-akandwaadang i’iw omiikana (then he lay in wait for it by his path); mii sa go gii-zhi-azhe-giiwe (and so back a ways he went), mii dash imaa (and then from there) wenzaabandang i’iw omiikana (he watched along his path).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Baanimaa go miinawaa obikwanaang owiya nwaandawaad.**</span>

Suddenly again at his back some creature he heard.

<span class="l-eng">*Baanimaa go miinawaa (suddenly again) obikwanaang owiya (at his back, someone) nwaandawaad (he heard).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa onjida enwenid, “poonh’\!’ ezhi-bazigonjised.**</span>

And as before, it uttered the same "Po\!” Then up he leaped to his feet.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa onjida enwenid (and as before, the same it sounded), “poonh’\!’ ezhi-bazigonjised (“Po\!” then up he sprang to his feet).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Da-baapiniziwaagan, awenesh wa’aw beminisha’ogwen\! Gechi-manidoowigwen,” gii-inendam ezhi-maajiibatood gichi-enigok.**</span>

“For goodness sake\! Wonder who it is that may be following me\! It must be a great manitou being," he thought as he started running at the very top of his speed.

<span class="l-eng">*“Da-baapiniziwaagan (“for goodness sake), awenesh wa’aw (who is this) beminisha’ogwen\! (that may be following me\!) Gechi-manidoowigwen,” (it must be a great manidoo being,”) gii-inendam (he thought) ezhi-maajiibatood gichi-enigok (as he started running at full speed).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Megwaa go ani-bimibatood, baamaa go miinawaa, “Poonh\!”**</span>

And while he was running along, suddenly again, “Po\!"

<span class="l-eng">*Megwaa go ani-bimibatood (and while he was running along), baamaa go miinawaa (suddenly again), “Poonh\!” (“Po\!”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gichi-waasa bangishin, aabamibagizod; aaniish, gaawiin owaabamaasiin aano-aswaganed.**</span>

A great distance off he landed, when round he whirled; why, he did not see the one at whom he tried in vain to aim a blow.

<span class="l-eng">*Gichi-waasa bangishin (a great distance off he landed), aabamibagizod (when he whirled around); aaniish, gaawiin owaabamaasiin (why, he did not see him) aano-aswaganed (the one at whom he tried in vain to aim a blow).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Baanimaa go miinawaa i'iwidi obikwanaang wenji-noondaagozinid, “Poonh\!”**</span>

And later again, from yonder at his back, was heard the sound of some one saying, “Po\!”

<span class="l-eng">*Baanimaa go miinawaa (and later again) i’iwidi obikwanaang (from yonder at his back) wenji-noondaagozinid (where the sound of someone was heard), “Poonh\!” (“Po\!”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Da-baapiniziwaagan\!” inendam ezhi-maajiibatood.**</span>

“For goodness sake\!” he thought as he started running.

<span class="l-eng">*“Da-baapiniziwaagan\!” (“for goodness sake\!”) inendam (he thought) ezhi-maajiibatood (as he started running).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii go imaa maajiibatood, miinawaa, “Poonh\!”**</span>

And there where he started running, again, "Po\!"

<span class="l-eng">*Mii go imaa maajiibatood (and there where he started running), miinawaa, “Poonh\!” (again, “Po\!”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Da-baapiniziwaagan\!” inendam enigok ezhi-maajiibatood;**</span>

“For goodness sake\!” he thought as with speed he started running.

<span class="l-eng">*“Da-baapiniziwaagan\!” (“for goodness sake\!”) inendam enigok (he thought) ezhi-maajiibatood (as with speed he started running);*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii igo besho apatoo, bimibatood enigok.**</span>

Then at last a short way he ran; as he went, he ran with speed.

<span class="l-eng">*Gegapii igo besho apatoo (then at last a short way he ran), bimibatood enigok (running along with speed).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa onjida e-nitang odashkweyaaming, “Poonh\!”**</span>

But what he heard behind him was still the same sound, “Po\!”

<span class="l-eng">*Mii sa onjida e-nitang (but still the same he heard) odashkweyaaming (behind him), “Poonh\!” (“Po\!”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii go endaso-bangishing, “poonh, poonh, poonh, poonh, poonh\!”**</span>

Then finally at every step he took it was, “Po, po, po, po, po \!”

<span class="l-eng">*Gegapii go endaso-bangishing (then finally at every step he took), “poonh, poonh, poonh, poonh, poonh\!” (“Po, po, po, po, po\!”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash i'iw miitagiini’w gaa-izhiwaad nishiimeyag sa gii-bi-waabamagwaa igi’n ashkipwaag. ‘nimboogizikaagemin,’ ningii-igoog.**</span>

“Why, this was just what my little brothers told me when I saw the artichokes. ‘We make people windy in the stomach,’ I was told.

<span class="l-eng">*Mii dash i’iw miitagiini’w (why, this is just the very thing — \*particle flagged\*) gaa-izhiwaad nishiimeyag (that my little brothers said) sa gii-bi-waabamagwaa igi’n ashkipwaag (when I came and saw those artichokes). ‘nimboogizikaagemin,’ (‘we make people windy in the stomach,’) ningii-igoog (I was told by them).*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Dakiin, eei, nimboogid\! Mii sa ge-ikidowaad anishinaabeg nizhisheyag, ji-ani-akiiwang.”**</span>

Oh, why, I am breaking wind\! That is what the people, my uncles, shall say till the end of the world.”

<span class="l-eng">*Dakiin, eei, nimboogid\! (oh, why, I am breaking wind\!) Mii sa ge-ikidowaad anishinaabeg (that is what the people will say) nizhisheyag (my uncles), ji-ani-akiiwang.” (until the end of the world.”)*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Megwaa go ani-babimosed, baanimaa go miinawaa, “Poonh\!” “Eei, nimboogid\!”**</span>

And while he went walking along, then of a sudden again, "Po\!" – “Why, I am breaking wind\!”

<span class="l-eng">*Megwaa go ani-babimosed (and while he was walking along), baanimaa go miinawaa (then of a sudden again), “Poonh\!” (“Po\!”) “Eei, nimboogid\!” (“why, I am breaking wind\!”)*</span>
