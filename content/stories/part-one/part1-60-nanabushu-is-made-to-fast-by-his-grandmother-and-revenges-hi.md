60\. NÄNABUSHU IS MADE TO FAST BY HIS GRANDMOTHER, AND REVENGES HIMSELF. \[Jones prints this title in ENGLISH only on p.450 — there is no Ojibwe-orthography title on the page; the Fiero title below is a \*reconstruction, flagged\* for M.\]

<span class="l-fiero">**NENABOZHO GII-IZHI-GIIGWISHIMO’AAGANIWID OOKOMISAN, MIINAWAA GII-AAPIDENIMAAD. \[\*reconstructed Fiero title — not printed on the page; flagged for M.\*\]**</span>

NÄNABUSHU IS MADE TO FAST BY HIS GRANDMOTHER, AND REVENGES HIMSELF.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Syrette — Part I Series 8 (Nos. 57–63), Fort William, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Ninguding dạc kīwäⁿ udigōn ō‘kumisạn: “Nōjis, kitā kīi‘gwicîm kuca. Mī wīnawā kā‘i‘jitcigäwāt kicicäyabạnig,” udinān.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding dash giiwenh odigoon ookomisan: “Noozhish, gidaa-giigwishim gosha. Mii wiinawaa gaa-izhichigewaad gizhishayabaanig,” odinaan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Now, once they say that he was told by his grandmother: “My grandson, you should indeed go into a fast. That was what your uncles of old used to do,” she said to him.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding dash giiwenh (now once, they say) odigoon ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother): “Noozhish (my grandson), gidaa-giigwishim gosha (you really ought to fast — \*giigwishim ‘fast for a vision’\*). Mii wiinawaa gaa-izhichigewaad gizhishayabaanig (that is what your uncles of old used to do — \*kicicäyabạnig ‘your late uncles’; gizhishayab-/grandfather-uncle term, stem flagged\*),” odinaan (she said to him).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(2) Mīdạc ägut: “Ānīn dạc kätijicitcigäyān, nō‘kumis.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash egod: “Aaniin dash ge-izhi-izhichigeyaan, nookomis.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon she was asked: “How, indeed, shall I do it, my grandmother?”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash egod (and so she was asked — \*egod ‘he is told/asked by her’, inverse\*): “Aaniin dash ge-izhi-izhichigeyaan (and how then shall I do it), nookomis (my grandmother).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(3) “Kīgamādcā, nōjis, kāwīn dạc kigawīsinizi,” udigōn ō‘kumisạn. “A‘pīdạc winibāyạn, nōjic, pīpāgimicin,” udigōn. “Kīcpîn dạc täpi‘tändạmān, kīgaganōnin imāⁿ tcīnibāyạn,” udinān. “Mīyä‘tagu imä kä‘u‘ndciki‘kändạman kä‘u‘ndcipimādisiyạn,” udigōn ō‘kumisạn.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Giga-maajaa, noozhish, gaawiin dash giga-wiisinizii,” odigoon ookomisan. “Apii dash wii-nibaayan, noozhish, biibaagimishin,” odigoon. “Giishpin dash debi’tendamaan, giga-ganoonin imaa ji-nibaayan,” odinaan. “Mii eta go imaa ge-onji-gikendaman ge-onji-bimaadiziyan,” odigoon ookomisan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“You shall go away, my grandson, and you shall not eat food,” he was told by his grandmother. “And when you wish to sleep, my grandson, call for me,” he was told. “And if I think you have gone far enough, then will I tell you to sleep there,” she said to him. “It is only by such means that you can know how you are to live in the future,” he was told by his grandmother.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Giga-maajaa, noozhish (you shall go off, my grandson), gaawiin dash giga-wiisinizii (and you shall not eat),” odigoon ookomisan (he is told by his grandmother). “Apii dash wii-nibaayan (and when you wish to sleep), noozhish, biibaagimishin (my grandson, call out to me),” odigoon (he is told). “Giishpin dash debi’tendamaan (and if I think it enough — \*debi’tendam ‘consider sufficient’\*), giga-ganoonin imaa ji-nibaayan (I shall tell you to sleep there),” odinaan (she says to him). “Mii eta go imaa ge-onji-gikendaman (it is only by that means you will come to know) ge-onji-bimaadiziyan (how you are to live),” odigoon ookomisan (he is told by his grandmother).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(4) Mīdạc kägä‘t Nänabucū weyābaninig kā‘i‘jiminigut ō‘kumisạn a‘ka‘ kä‘jä tcimīdcit; mīdac kägä‘t Nänabuc kīmīdcit, kayä kima‘kadäwīnang pạngī uskījink.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget Nenabozho wayaabaninig gaa-izhi-miinigod ookomisan akakanzhe ji-miijid; mii dash geget Nenabozh gii-miijid, gaye gii-makadewiinang bangii oshkiizhig.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*It was true that Nänabushu on the morrow ate the charcoal that had been given him by his grandmother; it was true that Nänabushu ate (it), and he blackened his face a little.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget Nenabozho (and so truly Nenabozho) wayaabaninig (on the next morning) gaa-izhi-miinigod ookomisan (was given by his grandmother) akakanzhe ji-miijid (charcoal to eat — \*a‘ka‘kä/akakanzhe ‘charcoal’, stem flagged\*); mii dash geget Nenabozh gii-miijid (and so truly Nenabozho ate it), gaye gii-makadewiinang bangii oshkiizhig (and he blackened his face a little — \*makadewiinang ‘he blackens (the face)’; oshkiizhig ‘face/eye’\*).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(5) Ānic mī äntōtạmowāgubạnän mäwija anicinābäg kī‘i‘gwicimowāt.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aaniish mii endoodamowaagobanen mewizha anishinaabeg gii-giigwishimowaad.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Now, that was what in olden times the people used to do when they fasted.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii endoodamowaagobanen (now, that is what they used to do — \*preterite dubitative\*) mewizha anishinaabeg (long ago the people) gii-giigwishimowaad (when they fasted).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(6) Mīdạc kīⁿwäⁿ Nänabucu mādcāt kikicäp. “Änigu‘k mādcān, kägu‘ ābinābi‘kän,” udigōn ō‘kumisạn. Mīdạc Nänabucu änigu‘k mädcat. Kabägijik pạbimusä, kayä pạbimiba‘tō aiyā‘pī. Mīdạc wunāgucininig ā‘pidci aiyä‘kusi kayä pakadā. Minawā pimusä uwīngä tibi‘katini; kāwīn owâbandạⁿzīn äjāt. Mīdạc kägä‘pī äjipīpāgit: “Ä‘eⁱ, nō‘kumis\! mīa‘pī omā tcinibāyān\!” Kumā‘pī onōndawān ō‘kumisạn na‘kwä‘tāgut: “Ē‘ēⁱ\! āwạs nawatc ijän\!”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash giiwenh Nenabozho maajaad gigizheb. “Enigok maajaan, gego aabinaabiken,” odigoon ookomisan. Mii dash Nenabozho enigok maajaad. Gabe-giizhig babimose, gaye babimibatoo ayaapii. Mii dash wanaagoshininig aapiji ayekozi gaye bakade. Miinawaa bimose owiinge dibikkadini; gaawiin owaabandanziin ezhaad. Mii dash gega-bii ezhi-biibaagid: “Hey-ey, nookomis\! mii apii omaa ji-nibaayaan\!” Goma apii onoondawaan ookomisan nakwetaagod: “Hey-ey\! awas nawach izhaan\!”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon they say that Nänabushu started out in the morning. “With all your speed go, (and) look not back,” he was told by his grandmother. And so Nänabushu went with all his speed. All day long he went walking about, and now and then he was running. Thereupon in the evening he was very tired and hungry. Again he walked till it was growing thoroughly dark; he did not see whither he was going. And then at last he cried aloud: “Hey-yo, my grandmother\! in this very place will I sleep\!” After a while he heard the voice of his grandmother answering in reply: “Hey there\! farther yet do you go\!”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash giiwenh Nenabozho maajaad gigizheb (and so they say Nenabozho set out in the morning). “Enigok maajaan (go with all your might), gego aabinaabiken (do not look back),” odigoon ookomisan (he is told by his grandmother). Mii dash Nenabozho enigok maajaad (and so Nenabozho went off hard). Gabe-giizhig babimose (all day he walked about), gaye babimibatoo ayaapii (and ran here and there now and then). Mii dash wanaagoshininig aapiji ayekozi gaye bakade (and so toward evening he was very tired and hungry). Miinawaa bimose owiinge dibikkadini (again he walked until it grew fully dark); gaawiin owaabandanziin ezhaad (he could not see where he was going). Mii dash gega-bii ezhi-biibaagid (and so at last he cried out): “Hey-ey, nookomis\! (Hey-yo, my grandmother\!) mii apii omaa ji-nibaayaan\! (right here is where I will sleep\!)” Goma apii onoondawaan ookomisan nakwetaagod (after a while he hears his grandmother answering him): “Hey-ey\! awas nawach izhaan\! (Hey there\! go a little farther yet\!)” \[vocables Ä‘eⁱ / Ē‘ēⁱ carried as printed\]</span>

<span class="l-orig">(7) Ānīc, mī gägä‘t; Nänabucu kägä‘t kitci‘ä‘nigu‘k mādcība‘tōd. Minawā mādcība‘tōd ä‘kōnāmut minawā pīpāgit: “Nō‘kumis, mī omā a‘pī tcinibayāyān\!” Cayīgwa minawā onōntawān: “Ē‘eⁱ, āwạs nawatc ijän\!”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Aaniish, mii geget; Nenabozho geget gichi-enigok maajiibatood. Miinawaa maajiibatood ekoonaamod miinawaa biibaagid: “Nookomis, mii omaa apii ji-nibaayaan\!” Zhayiigwa miinawaa onoondawaan: “Hey-ey, awas nawach izhaan\!”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Well, that (was what) truly (happened); Nänabushu truly began running at the very top of his speed. Again he began running as far as his wind could hold out, when again he called aloud: “My grandmother, in this very place will I sleep\!” This time again he heard her (say): “Hey there\! farther yet do you go\!”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, mii geget (well, so it truly was); Nenabozho geget gichi-enigok maajiibatood (Nenabozho truly set off running at top speed). Miinawaa maajiibatood ekoonaamod (again he ran as far as his breath held out — \*ä‘kōnāmut ‘to the limit of his wind’, stem flagged\*) miinawaa biibaagid (and again he cried out): “Nookomis, mii omaa apii ji-nibaayaan\! (my grandmother, right here is where I will sleep\!)” Zhayiigwa miinawaa onoondawaan (now again he hears her): “Hey-ey, awas nawach izhaan\! (Hey there, go farther yet\!)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(8) Mīsa Nänabucu minawā mādcība‘tōd änigu‘k kitci‘a‘yäna‘kōnāmut; wīngä aiyä‘kusi. Mīdạc minawā pīpāgit: “Mīna a‘pī omā tcinibāyạn?” udinān o‘kumisạn. Wīkāgu ōganōnigōn: “Ē‘eⁱ\! awas nawatc ican\!”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa Nenabozho miinawaa maajiibatood enigok gichi-aya-ena’koonaamod; owiinge ayekozi. Mii dash miinawaa biibaagid: “Miinaa apii omaa ji-nibaayan?” odinaan ookomisan. Wiikaa go oganoonigoon: “Hey-ey\! awas nawach izhaan\!”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon Nänabushu again began running with all his speed just as far as his wind would let him; very tired he became. And so again came his voice calling aloud: “Shall I sleep in the place right here?” he (thus) said to his grandmother. A long while afterwards he was addressed in the words: “Hey there\! farther on shall you go\!”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa Nenabozho miinawaa maajiibatood enigok (so Nenabozho again ran hard) gichi-aya-ena’koonaamod (just as far as his breath would carry him); owiinge ayekozi (he grew very tired). Mii dash miinawaa biibaagid (and so again he cried out): “Miinaa apii omaa ji-nibaayan? (May I sleep right here now?)” odinaan ookomisan (he says to his grandmother). Wiikaa go oganoonigoon (a long while after, she answers him): “Hey-ey\! awas nawach izhaan\! (Hey there\! go farther yet\!)”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(9) Mīdạc Nänabucu nickādisit. “Anîm\! Ānīn a‘kitut ‘a‘aⁿ? Ningakīwä,” inändam. Kitci änigu‘k madcat. Mīdạc wayībagu pạbiga ugikändän päcu‘ tagwicing ändawat. Mīdạc ani ayat ōwâbandan wāsa‘kwanämạgatinig wīgiwām. Mīdạc änändank Nänabucu: “Ningakimōsābama,” inändam. “Wägunän wändciwāsa‘kunawät?” Mīdạc kägä‘t kīmōtc ạjinaⁿzi‘kang ändawat. Mīdạc äjitapābandank, anîn kädicinawat ō‘kumisạn\! Owâbamän mägwa āminit ō‘kumisạn. Mīdạc kā‘i‘ciu‘da‘pinang mi‘tigōns payā‘tänig känwa‘kwạtinig kayä, mīdạc kā‘i‘jibā‘kindänag pạngī. Mīdạc kā‘i‘jisa‘kisitōd ickudänk iⁿ mi‘tig, mīdạc kā‘i‘jipiskạnäg; mīdạc kā‘i‘ji‘a‘gwunang omicōmisan udiyāning.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash Nenabozho nishkaadizid. “Anim\! Aaniin ekidod a’aw? Ninga-giiwe,” inendam. Gichi-enigok maajaad. Mii dash wayiiba go babige ogikendaan besho dagwishing endawaad. Mii dash ani-ayaad owaabandaan waasakwanemagadinig wiigiwaam. Mii dash enendang Nenabozho: “Ninga-gimoozaabamaa,” inendam. “Wegonen wenji-waasakonawed?” Mii dash geget giimooj azhi-naazikang endawaad. Mii dash ezhi-dapaabandang, aaniin ge-izhinawaad ookomisan\! Owaabamaan megwaa aaminid ookomisan. Mii dash gaa-izhi-oodaapinang mitigoons baayaatenig genwaakwadinig gaye, mii dash gaa-izhi-baakindenag bangii. Mii dash gaa-izhi-zakisidood ishkodeng iw mitig, mii dash gaa-izhi-biskanek; mii dash gaa-izhi-agwanang omishoomisan odiyaaning.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon Nänabushu became angry. “Wretch (that she is)\! What is she saying? I am going back,” he (thus) thought. At the very top of his speed he started. And so in a little while he suddenly became aware that close home was he arriving. Upon which as he drew near he saw that the wigwam was all lighted up inside. And then thought Nänabushu: “I will peep at her on the sly,” he thought. “Why is she making such a light?” And so truly on the sly did he approach where they lived. Thereupon he peeped in, and whom did he see but his grandmother\! He beheld his grandmother in the act of receiving amorous pleasure. Thereupon, after he had picked up a small stick that was dry and long, he then gently lifted the flap of the doorway. And so after he had lighted the stick in the fire, it flamed up into a blaze; whereupon he placed it against the buttocks of his grandfather.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenabozho nishkaadizid (and so Nenabozho grew angry). “Anim\! (the wretch\!) Aaniin ekidod a’aw? (what is she saying?) Ninga-giiwe (I will go back),” inendam (he thinks). Gichi-enigok maajaad (off he starts at top speed). Mii dash wayiiba go babige ogikendaan (and so soon, all at once, he realizes) besho dagwishing endawaad (that he is arriving near home). Mii dash ani-ayaad owaabandaan waasakwanemagadinig wiigiwaam (and as he goes on he sees the wigwam lit up inside). Mii dash enendang Nenabozho (and so Nenabozho thinks): “Ninga-gimoozaabamaa (I will spy on her secretly),” inendam. “Wegonen wenji-waasakonawed? (why is she making such a light?)” Mii dash geget giimooj azhi-naazikang endawaad (and so truly he steals up to where they live). Mii dash ezhi-dapaabandang (and so he peeps in), aaniin ge-izhinawaad ookomisan\! (and what does he see but his grandmother\!) Owaabamaan megwaa aaminid ookomisan (he sees his grandmother in the midst of coupling — \*āminit, body verb; rendered plainly per Jones\*). Mii dash gaa-izhi-oodaapinang mitigoons baayaatenig genwaakwadinig gaye (and so he picks up a small stick, dry and long), mii dash gaa-izhi-baakindenag bangii (and then he lifts the doorflap a little — \*baakinde- ‘open the flap’\*). Mii dash gaa-izhi-zakisidood ishkodeng iw mitig (and so he sets the stick afire in the fire), mii dash gaa-izhi-biskanek (and so it flames up — \*biskane- ‘blaze up’\*); mii dash gaa-izhi-agwanang omishoomisan odiyaaning (and so he holds it to his grandfather’s buttocks — \*odiyaaning ‘at his rump’\*).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(10) Mīdạc kā‘i‘jisa‘kisut aⁿ a‘kiwänⁿzi; mīdạc Nänabucu kā‘i‘ji‘kāsut ingutci; mīdạc nānāgä pimisägitciba‘tōd aⁿ a‘kiwänⁿji. Mīdạc Nänabucu kā‘i‘jinōsawâbamāt äni‘a‘‘pạ‘tōnit kā‘ạ‘niwāsa‘kunänitigu. Wī‘kādạc Nänabucu kī‘kiwä ändāwāt. Mīdạc nayāgin ạnigạnōnāt ō‘kumisạn: “Nō‘komis, nintagwicin.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-zakizod aw akiwenzii; mii dash Nenabozho gaa-izhi-gaazod ingoji; mii dash naanaage bimisegichibatood aw akiwenzh. Mii dash Nenabozho gaa-izhi-noosawaabamaad eni-a’a-batoonid gaa-ani-waasakonenidigo. Wiikaa dash Nenabozho gii-giiwe endawaad. Mii dash nayaagin ani-ganoonaad ookomisan: “Nookomis, nindagwishin.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Thereupon was the old man set on fire; upon which Nänabushu concealed himself in a certain place; and after a while out came the poor old man on the run. And so Nänabushu fixed his gaze upon him to see in what direction he was running as he went in flames. And after a while Nänabushu returned home. And then, while outside but on the way in, he spoke to his grandmother, saying: “My grandmother, I have come back home.”*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-zakizod aw akiwenzii (and so the old man was set ablaze); mii dash Nenabozho gaa-izhi-gaazod ingoji (and so Nenabozho hid himself somewhere); mii dash naanaage bimisegichibatood aw akiwenzh (and so after a while the old man ran out — \*pimisägitci-batōd ‘runs out (of doors)’, stem flagged\*). Mii dash Nenabozho gaa-izhi-noosawaabamaad eni-a’a-batoonid (and so Nenabozho watched him to see which way he ran) gaa-ani-waasakonenidigo (as he went off all aflame — \*waasakonene- ‘be in flames’, stem flagged\*). Wiikaa dash Nenabozho gii-giiwe endawaad (and after a long while Nenabozho returned to where they lived). Mii dash nayaagin ani-ganoonaad ookomisan (and so first, while still coming in, he speaks to his grandmother): “Nookomis, nindagwishin (my grandmother, I have arrived home).”</span>

<span class="l-orig">(11) “Ōⁿ, nojis,” udigōn. Awidạc mindimōyä ānawigu ogi‘kikänimān ōjisan kā‘i‘jictcigänit, kāwīndạc kägō kī‘i‘kitusī. Kayä iwä a‘pī kīsa‘kawāt, kī‘i‘kitu aⁿ mindimōyä: “Nyā, ogagwänisagi‘ā‘n ini‘ omicōmisan\!” kī‘i‘kitu.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Oonh, noozhish,” odigoon. Awi dash mindimooye aanawi go ogii-gikenimaan oozhisan gaa-izhichigenid, gaawiin dash gegoo gii-ikidosii. Gaye iwe apii gii-zakawaad, gii-ikido aw mindimooye: “Nyaa, ogagwaanisagi’aan iniw omishoomisan\!” gii-ikido.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“So I see, my grandson,” he was told. And though the old woman knew what her grandson had done, yet nothing did she say. And at the time when he burned (the old man), then said the old woman: “Pshaw, he played such a mean trick upon his grandfather\!” she said.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">“Oonh, noozhish,” odigoon (“So, my grandson,” he is told). Awi dash mindimooye aanawi go ogii-gikenimaan oozhisan gaa-izhichigenid (and though the old woman well knew what her grandson had done), gaawiin dash gegoo gii-ikidosii (yet she said nothing). Gaye iwe apii gii-zakawaad (and at the time he burned him), gii-ikido aw mindimooye (the old woman said): “Nyaa, ogagwaanisagi’aan iniw omishoomisan\! (Pshaw, he has done a wretched thing to his grandfather\! — \*gagwaanisagi’- ‘treat horribly’, stem flagged\*)” gii-ikido (she said).</span>

<span class="l-orig">(12) Mīdạc kīpīndigät Nanabucū, kāwin dạc pōtc kī‘a‘camāsīn Nänabucu. Mīdạc cigu kā‘i‘cikawicimut, mīdạc kā‘i‘nändank: “Magicā kigicäp ningatacamik pitcinạg,” inändạm Nänabucu kigicäkidạc udigōn: “Nōjis, kāwīn nōngum kigatacamisinōn. Käyābi kīga‘i‘gwicîm,” udinān.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gii-biindiged Nenabozho, gaawiin dash booch gii-ashamaasiin Nenabozho. Mii dash zhigo gaa-izhi-gawishimod, mii dash gaa-inendang: “Maagizhaa gigizheb ninga-ashamig bijiinag,” inendam Nenabozho; gigizheb idash odigoon: “Noozhish, gaawiin noongom giga-ashamisinoon. Geyaabi giga-giigwishim,” odinaan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And now inside went Nänabushu, but nothing whatsoever was Nänabushu given to eat. So then straight to bed he went, and this was what he thought: “Perhaps in the morning she will then feed me,” thought Nänabushu. But in the morning he was told: “My grandson, not to-day will I feed you. Longer yet shall you fast,” she said to him.*</span>
