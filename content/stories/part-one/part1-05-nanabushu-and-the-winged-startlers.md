<span class="l-orig">(5) Nänabushu and the Winged Startlers</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash zhayiigwa (and now then) ani-babimosed (as he was walking along) ningoting igo (all at once) ogii-waabamaan awiya (he saw something) okoshininid (lying heaped together).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash zhayiigwa ani-babimosed ningoting igo ogii-waabamaan awiya okoshininid.**</span>

> And now, while he was walking along, he suddenly saw something lying (together) in a heap.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gonige Gii-wiinzomwaadog?" (“might it be that you have a name?” — \*dubitative; stem flagged\*) ogii-inaan i’iw wayaabamaad (he said to the ones he saw).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gonige Gii-wiinzomwaadog?" ogii-inaan i’iw wayaabamaad.**</span>

> “I should like to know if perchance you have a name?” he said to the creatures which he saw.

<span class="l-gloss">"Mii nange," ogii-igoon (“to be sure,” he was told); "o’ow nindizhinikaazomin (“this is what we are called) Goshkongesiinag (little winged startlers) nindigoomin." (so we are named.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**"Mii nange," ogii-igoon; "o’ow nindizhinikaazomin, Goshkongesiinag, nindigoomin."**</span>

> “To be sure,” he was told; “this is our name, little winged startlers, such are we called.”

<span class="l-gloss">"Geget sa (“truly indeed) mii nangwani iw ezhinikaazoyeg\!" (so that is what you are called\!”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**"Geget sa, mii nangwani iw ezhinikaazoyeg\!"**</span>

“Oh, indeed, so that is what you are called\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-miijinaad (then, after he had eased himself upon them), o’ow dash gaa-izhi-inaad (this he then said to them): “Wa’aw gosha eta Goshkongesii (“this is really the only winged startler), ogow gosha eta," (these are the only ones,”) ogii-inaan (he said to them).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-miijinaad, o’ow dash gaa-izhi-inaad: “Wa’aw gosha eta Goshkongesii, ogow gosha eta,” ogii-inaan.**</span>

> Whereupon, after he had eased himself upon them, this he then said to them: “This is really the only winged startler, this is the only thing,” he said to them.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-ni-zhi-maajaad (thereupon he started on his way), gii-ani-babimosed (going along on foot).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-ni-zhi-maajaad, gii-ani-babimosed.**</span>

Thereupon on his way he started, on his way he went walking.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-madaabiid i’iw Gichigami (when he came out upon the sea), ogii-waabandaan Gichi-giishkaabikaanig (he saw a great steep cliff); inaabid (as he looked), Geget naawi-naagwadini iwidi nibiikaang (truly far down it seemed to where the water was).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Gaa-izhi-madaabiid i’iw Gichigami, ogii-waabandaan Gichi-giishkaabikaanig; inaabid, Geget naawi-naagwadini iwidi nibiikaang.**</span>

> When he came out upon the sea, he saw a very high cliff with steep sides; as he looked, truly far seemed the distance down to where the water was.

<span class="l-gloss">“Onjida maawiin nindaa-izhi-kwaashkwan (“with good reason would I leap down) ikwedog ataading (if a woman were up for a prize — \*ikwe + dubitative; staking/wager stem flagged\*), aapiji go dataakonjiigwaned (particularly if she were short from knee to groin — \*measure stem flagged\*).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Onjida maawiin nindaa-izhi-kwaashkwan ikwedog ataading, aapiji go dataakonjiigwaned.**</span>

> "With good reason would I leap down if a woman were up for a prize, particularly if she were short from knee to groin.

<span class="l-gloss">Onjida maawiin nintaa-izhi-kwaashkwan (with good reason would I leap down): ‘Awegwen ge-izhi-kwaashkwanigwen?’ (‘who is it that would jump down?’) ikidong (if it were said).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Onjida maawiin nintaa-izhi-kwaashkwan: ‘Awegwen ge-izhi-kwaashkwanigwen?’ ikidong.**</span>

With good reason would I leap down if some one should ask: 'Who will jump down?'

Onjida maawiin nindaa-izhi-kwaashkwan." (with good reason then would I leap down.”)

<span class="l-fiero">**Onjida maawiin nindaa-izhi-kwaashkwan."**</span>

With good reason then would I leap down.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go imaa eko-giibaabikaanig (there at the very edge of the cliff) niibawid (he stood).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii go imaa eko-giibaabikaanig niibawid.**</span>

There on the very brink of the cliff he stood.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow dash doodam (now this he did), miizan ezhi-waaninigaadenid (and his leg slipped / lost its footing — \*stem flagged\*).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**O’ow dash doodam, miizan ezhi-waaninigaadenid.**</span>

Now, this happened to him: as he started to jump, he lost his footing.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash zhigwa gii-odisaad (and so by now she had come) o’owedi gaa-miijinimind (to where they had been eased upon) mii i’iw oniijaanisa’ (that is, her young).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash zhigwa gii-odisaad o’owedi gaa-miijinimind mii i'iw oniijaanisa’.**</span>

> And so in the mean time (the mother) had come to where her young had been eased upon.

<span class="l-gloss">"Aaniin gaadiyeg?" (“what has happened to you?” — \*stem flagged\*) odinaa’ i’i’w oniijaanisa’ (she said to her children).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**"Aaniin gaadiyeg?" odinaa' i'i'w oniijaanisa’.**</span>

“What has happened to you?” she said to her children.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaa, nangwana Nenaboozhoo (“why, it was Nanabushu) ningii-miijinigonaan." (that eased himself upon us.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaa, nangwana Nenaboozhoo ningii-miijinigonaan.”**</span>

> “Why, it was by that old Nanabushu that we were eased upon.”

“Ningodanoo maawiin inaawegoban." (“something or other you must have said to him — \*dubitative preterite\*.”)

<span class="l-fiero">**“Ningodanoo maawiin inaawegoban.”**</span>

"Something or other must you have said to him.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin,” Gii-ikido (“no,” said) a’aw bezhig (one of them) i’imaa moowiing osibidood (moving about there in the slush of the dung).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaawiin,” Gii-ikido a’aw bezhig i’imaa moowiing osibidood.**</span>

> “Nay,” said one moving about there in the slush of the dung.

<span class="l-gloss">“Geget ninga-gwejimigonaan (“truly, we were questioned), ‘Aaniin ezhinikaazoyeg?’ (‘what are you called?’) ningii-igonaan (we were asked).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Geget ninga-gwejimigonaan, ‘Aaniin ezhinikaazoyeg?’ ningii-igonaan.**</span>

“Truly, we were asked, 'What is your name?' we were told.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ningii-inaanaan (and this is what we said to him), ‘Goshkongesiinag nindigoomin,’ (‘little winged startlers we are called,’) ningii-inaanaan (we said to him).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**O’ow idash ningii-inaanaan, ‘Goshkongesiinag nindigoomin,’ ningii-inaanaan.**</span>

And this was what we said to him, ‘Little winged startlers are we called,’ we said to him.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-ikido (and this he said): ‘Naabisa Goshkongesi\!’ (‘like the deuce, little winged startler\!’) ningii-igonaan (we were told by him); mii dash gaa-izhi-mamashkijishiyangid (whereupon he squirted upon us — \*stem flagged\*), mii dash gaa-ani-zhi-maajaad." (after which he went on his way.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**O’ow idash gii-ikido: ‘Naabisa Goshkongesi\!’ ningii-igonaan; mii dash gaa-izhi-mamashkijishiyangid, mii dash gaa-ani-zhi-maajaad.”**</span>

> And this he said: ‘Like the deuce (you are) little winged frighteners\!’ we were told by him; whereupon he squirted at us, after which he went his way.”

<span class="l-gloss">Wa’aw idash weniijaanizit (and the mother) gaa-izhi-giziibiiginaad (washed them with water); gaa-ishkwaa-giziibiiginaad (after she had finished washing them), zhigwa gaa-izhi-maajaad (she then started away).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Wa’aw idash weniijaanizit gaa-izhi-giziibiiginaad; gaa-ishkwaa-giziibiiginaad, zhigwa gaa-izhi-maajaad.**</span>

> Then the mother washed them with water; and after she had finished washing them, she then started away.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mishanim\!” (“confound him\!”) ogii-inaan Nenaboozhoowan (she said of Nanabushu).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Mishanim\!” ogii-inaan Nenaboozhoowan.**</span>

“Confound him\!” she said of Nanabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-adimaad (and so after she had overtaken him) i’imaa giishkaabikaang (yonder at the cliff), mii dash besho ogii-onzaabamaan (then from close by she peeped at him).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-adimaad i’imaa giishkaabikaang, mii dash besho ogii-onzaabamaan.**</span>

> And so after she had overtaken him yonder at the cliff, then close by she took a peep at him.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-inenimaad (now this was her thought of him): “Wiikaage sa miinawaa da-waaninigaadeni." (“I wish that again he would swing his leg.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-inenimaad: “Wiikaage sa miinawaa da-waaninigaadeni.”**</span>

Now, this was her thought of him: “I wish that again he would swing his leg.”

Zhigwa Geget ombigaadeni Nenaboozhoo (now, sure enough, up went Nanabushu’s leg).

<span class="l-fiero">**Zhigwa Geget ombigaadeni Nenaboozhoo.**</span>

Now, sure enough, up Nanabushu raised his leg.

<span class="l-gloss">“Onjida sa nindaa-izhi-kwaashkwan (“for a purpose would I leap) ningodwaakwa’aagan (one span of the hand) ekwo-jiingwaned adaading." (if the prize measured that from groin to knee — \*measure/wager stem flagged\*.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Onjida sa nindaa-izhi-kwaashkwan ningodwaakwa’aagan ekwo-jiingwaned adaading.”**</span>

> “For a purpose would I leap if the object of the prize measured one span of the hand from groin to knee.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-bazigwa’od a’a’w bine (thereupon as up flew the ruffed grouse), mii dash gaa-izhi-gwaashkwaninid (then he leaped) i’imaa giishkaabikaang (there off the steep cliff).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-bazigwa’od a’a’w bine, mii dash gaa-izhi-gwaashkwaninid i’imaa giishkaabikaang.**</span>

Thereupon as up flew the ruffed grouse, then (Nanabushu) leaped off the steep cliff.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’imaa gaa-izhi-booniid (and so after she had alighted there) ganawaabamaad animibizonid (she watched him as he went falling); mii dash i’iwidi nibiikaang (and then yonder into the water) gaa-izhi-bangishininid (he fell).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash i’imaa gaa-izhi-booniid ganawaabamaad animibizonid; mii dash i’iwidi nibiikaang gaa-izhi-bangishininid.**</span>

> And so after she had alighted there (on the edge), she watched him as he went falling; and then yonger into the water he fell.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’imaa gaa-onzaabimaad (and so from there she kept watch of him), geget gabeya’ii gii-inaandiiwan i’imaa nibiikaang (truly a long while was he under the water — \*stem flagged\*); wiikaa ayagoozid (a long time she sat perched up there — \*stem flagged\*).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash i’imaa gaa-onzaabimaad, geget gabeya’ii gii-inaandiiwan i’imaa nibiikaang; wiikaa ayagoozid.**</span>

> And so from there she kept watch of him, truly a long while was he gone in the water; a long time was she perched up there.

<span class="l-gloss">Onji-mookisewan (when he came up to the surface), mii go iwidi gaa-bi-inaabinid (straightway he looked over toward there); o’ow idash ogii-igoon (now this was she told by him): “Geget sa gi-goshkongesiw," (“of a truth, you are a winged startler,”) ogii-igoon (she was told).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Onji-mookisewan, mii go iwidi gaa-bi-inaabinid; o’ow idash ogii-igoon: “Geget sa gi-goshkongesiw,” ogii-igoon.**</span>

> When he came to the surface, straightway at yonder place he cast a look; now this was she told by him: “Of a truth, you are a winged startler,” she was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-giiwed a’aw bine (thereupon back home went the ruffed grouse); mii dash gaye wiin gaa-izhi-maajiiyaadagaad (and as for himself he started swimming inshore), mii sa gaa-izhi-agwaadaad (after which he went up out of the water).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gaa-izhi-giiwed a’aw bine mii dash gaye wiin gaa-izhi-maajiiyaadagaad, mii sa gaa-izhi-agwaadaad.**</span>

> Thereupon back home went the ruffed grouse; and as for himself he started swimming inshore, after which he then went out of the water.

Mii dash miinawaa gii-ani-maajaad (thereupon again he started on his way).

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash miinawaa gii-ani-maajaad.**</span>

Thereupon again he started on his way.
