(50) Nänabushu and the Ruffed Grouse

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa (again) gii-ani-maajaa (he went on his way) Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu) aapiji (very much) gaagiijidiiyed (being sore in the rear-end).</span>

1)  > **Miinawaa gii-ani-maajaa Nenaboozhoo aapiji gaagiijidiiyed.**

> On his way continued Nänabushu with a bottom exceedingly sore.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa (again) binensa’ (some young Ruffed Grouse) omikawaa’ (he found them) namadabinid (as they were sitting). “Aaniin (what) e-zhinikaazod (is named) gi-giwaa? (your mother?)”</span>

2)  > **Miinawaa binensa’ omikawaa’ namadabinid. “Aaniin e-zhinikaazod gi-giwaa?”**

> Next some young Ruffed Grouse he found, that were sitting down. “What is the name of your mother?”

“Goshkogesi.” (Goshkogesi — ‘A Frightener.’)

3)  > **”Goshkogesi.”**

> A Frightener.”

Goshkogesi (a frightener — the name carried as spoken).

<span class="l-gloss">„Naabisa’a (the deuce\!) goshkogesi\! (a frightener\!)” ikido (said) Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu).</span>

4)  > **„Naabisa’a goshkogesi\!” ikido Nenaboozhoo.**

> „The deuce\! she is a frightener,” said Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and so) gaa-izhi-miijinaad (when he had eased himself upon them), mii dash (then) gii-ani-maajaad (on his way he went).</span>

5)  > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-miijinaad, mii dash gii-ani-maajaad.**

> And so when he had eased himself upon them, then on his way he went.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii idash (now, when) degoshing (she arrived home) gichi-bine (the old Ruffed Grouse) owaabamaa’ (she saw them) oniijaanisa’ (her children) moowiwinid (being covered with dung). „Awenen (who) gaa-toodooneg? (did that to you?)”</span>

6)  > **Apii idash degoshing gichi-bine owaabamaa’ oniijaanisa’ moowiwinid. „Awenen gaa-toodooneg?”**

> Now, when home was come the old Ruffed Grouse, she saw her children covered with dung. “Who did that to you?”

<span class="l-gloss">„Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu) ningii-miijinigonaan. (eased himself upon us.)”</span>

7)  > **„Nenaboozhoo ningii-miijinigonaan.”**

> “By Nänabushu were we eased upon.”

<span class="l-gloss">Gii-nishkaadizi (she was angry) a’aw (that) bine (Ruffed Grouse).</span>

8)  > **Gii-nishkaadizi a’aw bine.**

> Angry was the Ruffed Grouse.

<span class="l-gloss">Ogii-noopinanaan idash (so she followed after him) Nenaboozhoon (Nänabushu), niigaan idash (and ahead) ani-izhaanid (where he was going) gii-poonii (she alighted) a’aw bine (that Ruffed Grouse).</span>

9)  > **Ogii-noopinanaan idash Nenaboozhoon, niigaan idash ani-izhaanid gii-poonii a’aw bine.**

> So she followed after Nänabushu, and in the path ahead of him the Ruffed Groused alighted.

<span class="l-gloss">Giishkaabikaang (at the edge of a cliff) ani-izhaaban (was going) Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu), bine dash (and the Ruffed Grouse) gii-gaazo (was hidden) zhingobiikaang (among the balsams).</span>

10) > **Giishkaabikaang ani-izhaaban Nenaboozhoo, bine dash gii-kaazo zhingobiikaang.**

> By the edge of a cliff was Nänabushu going, and the Ruffed Grouse was hidden among the balsams.

<span class="l-gloss">Apii idash (and when) imaa (there) bemosed (as he walked by) Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu), bine (the Ruffed Grouse) gezika \[also zesika\] (suddenly) gii-bazikwa’o (flew up), enigok (with all her might) gii-datawaange \[or daataawaange\] (she flapped her wings).</span>

11) > **Apii idash imaa bemosed Nenaboozhoo, bine gezika \[also zesika\] gii-pazikwa’o, enigok gii-datawaange \[or daataawaange\].**

> And when by the place Nänabushu passed, the Ruffed Grouse suddenly flew up; with all her might she flapped her wings.

<span class="l-gloss">Aataa\! (ah\!) mii apane (then off) Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu) gaa-izhi-gaabikise (tumbled over the precipice).</span>

12) > **Aataa\! mii apane Nenaboozhoo gaa-izhi-kaabikise.**

> Ah\! then off tumbled Nänabushu over the precipice.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and then) gii-jiishaabikidiyeshing (landing on his buttocks, he slid down the rock — \*stem flagged\*).</span>

13) > **Mii dash gii-jiishaabikidiyeshing.**

> And then, alighting upon his buttocks, down he slid.

<span class="l-gloss">Gii-inaabid (when he looked) imaa (there) giishkaabikaang (at the precipice), owaabandaanan (he saw them) oniigiin \[OR omigiin?\] (his sores) agokenig (sticking) imaa (there) asiniing (to the rock).</span>

14) > **Gii-inaabid imaa giishkaabikaang, owaabandaanan oniigiin \[OR omigiin?\] agokenig imaa asiniing.**

> On looking up at the precipice, he beheld his sores sticking there to the rock.

<span class="l-gloss">„Waakonag (lichens) giga-digoom (you shall be called),” odidaan (he said to them).</span>

15) > **„Waakonag giga-digoom,” odidaan.**

> „Lichens shall you be called,” he said to them.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and so) igiw (those) waakonag (lichens) Nenaboozhoo (Nänabushu’s) odoomigiiwidiyaan (were the sores of his bottom).</span>

16) > **Mii dash igiw waakonag Nenaboozhoo odoomigiiwidiyaan.**

> And so the lichens were sores from Nänabushu’s bottom.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa (again) mitigoonsan (the shrubs) ogii-ani-minjiminaan (he went grabbing hold of as he passed). „Miskwaabiimagoog (red willows) giga-digoom (you shall be called) ji-ani-akiiwang (till the end of the world).”</span>

17) > **Miinawaa mitigoonsan ogii-ani-minjiminaan. „Miskwaabiimagoog giga-digoom ji-ani-akiiwang.”**

> Next he went grabbing hold of the shrubs as he passed among them. “Red willows shall you be called till the end of the world.”
