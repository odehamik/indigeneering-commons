58\. NANABUSHU OBSCENELY JESTS WITH HIS GRANDMOTHER.

<span class="l-fiero">**58. Nenabozho Obscenely Jests with His Grandmother.**</span>

58\. Nanabushu Obscenely Jests with His Grandmother.

<span class="l-eng">*Narrated by Syrette — Part I Series 8 (Nos. 57–63), Fort William, per Corbiere roster; not printed on the page — verify.*</span>

<span class="l-fiero">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1917).***</span>

<span class="l-orig">(1) Mīdạc kiwän ninguting Nänabuco pạbimiba’tōd, käwin wi’kā kibisi’kāsī; ningutingdạc kiwän ạnipabimiba’tōd Nänabucu oganönigön ō’kumisan: “Nōjic, niwisäga’a’m,” udigōn.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash giiwenh ningoding Nenabozho babimibatood, gaawiin wiikaa gibizhi’gaazii; ningodingidash giiwenh ani-babimibatood Nenabozho oganoonigoon o’gomisan: “Noozhish, niwii-zaaga’am,” odigoon.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And now they say that once while Nänabushu was travelling about on the run, never did he come to a halt; and once they say that when he was running along, Nänabushu was addressed by his grandmother saying: “My grandson, I wish to go out,” he was told.*</span>

mii dash giiwenh — and now they say ∙ ningoding — once ∙ Nenabozho babimibatood — Nanabushu running about ∙ gaawiin wiikaa gibizhi’gaazii — never did he stop ∙ ningodingidash giiwenh — and once they say ∙ ani-babimibatood — as he was running along ∙ oganoonigoon o’gomisan — he was addressed by his grandmother ∙ Noozhish — my grandson ∙ niwii-zaaga’am — I wish to go out (to relieve myself) ∙ odigoon — he was told by her.

<span class="l-orig">(2) Nänabuc kayä win i’kito: “Pä’kädiyänin,” udinān ō’kumisan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Nenabozh gaye wiin ikido: “\*bekadiyanin\*,” odinaan o’gomisan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And Nänabushu in reply said: “Simply lean aside with your buttocks,” he said to his grandmother.*</span>

Nenabozh gaye wiin ikido — and Nanabushu for his part says ∙ \*bekadiyanin\* — simply lean aside with your buttocks (\*flagged\*: form not in lexicon, sense per Jones’s English) ∙ odinaan o’gomisan — he says it to his grandmother.

<span class="l-orig">(3) “Kawin, nōjis,” udinān, “niwīmīsi,” udinān ōjisan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gaawiin, noozhis,” odinaan, “niwii-miizii,” odinaan ojisan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Nay, my grandson,” she said to him, “I have need of relief,” she said to her grandson.*</span>

Gaawiin — no ∙ noozhis — my grandson ∙ odinaan — she says to him ∙ niwii-miizii — I have need of relief (I wish to defecate) ∙ odinaan ojisan — she says it to her grandson.

<span class="l-orig">(4) “Mīgu imā misin,” udinän o’kumisan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Mii go imaa miizin,” odinaan o’gomisan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Then do it there,” he said to his grandmother.*</span>

mii go imaa — then right there ∙ miizin — defecate (imperative) ∙ odinaan o’gomisan — he says it to his grandmother.

<span class="l-orig">(5) Anic, mi win kägä’t mindimōyä äjimizīgubanän imā. Midạc minawa aᵘ mindimōyä änät ujicä’yan: “Nōjis\!” udinan, “tcīga’kwa ạni’a’yaba’tōn. Niwi’a’niuda’pinän wâkisindimä’o’yän,” udigōn.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Anish, mii wiin geget mindimooye ezhi-miizigubanen imaa. Mii dash minawaa a’aw mindimooye enaad ojisheyan: “Noozhis\!” odinan, “jiigaakwaa ani-aya-batoon. Niwii-ani-odaapinaan waa-giisindimaa’oyaan,” odigoon.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Well, it was so that the old woman relieved herself at the time in that position. And so again the old woman said to her grandson: “O my grandson\!” she said to him, “by the edge of the woods do pass along as you run. I wish to get hold of something on the way to wipe myself at the anus,” he was told.*</span>

Anish, mii wiin geget — well, so it was indeed ∙ mindimooye ezhi-miizigubanen imaa — the old woman relieved herself there as related ∙ mii dash minawaa a’aw mindimooye enaad ojisheyan — and so again the old woman said to her grandson ∙ Noozhis — my grandson ∙ odinan — she says to him ∙ jiigaakwaa ani-aya-batoon — by the edge of the woods run along ∙ niwii-ani-odaapinaan — I wish to get hold of something along the way ∙ waa-giisindimaa’oyaan — with which to wipe myself at the anus ∙ odigoon — he was told by her.

<span class="l-orig">(6) “Kitöskun kisindimä’u’n,” udinän ō’kumisan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Gidooskon giisindimaa’on,” odinaan o’gomisan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“With your elbow wipe your anus,” he said to his grandmother.*</span>

gidooskon — your elbow ∙ giisindimaa’on — wipe yourself at the anus (imperative) ∙ odinaan o’gomisan — he says it to his grandmother.

<span class="l-orig">(7) Midac kägä’t aᵘ mindimōyä äjikisindimä’u’t odōskun. Mīdạc minawa au mindimōyä änät ōcisan: “Saga’kwang ạni’i’cän; niwi’a’nikisiya’kwisitön nintōskun,” udinan ōcicä’yan.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget a’aw mindimooye ezhi-giisindimaa’od odooskon. Mii dash minawaa a’aw mindimooye enaad ojisan: “Zaaga’akwaang ani-izhaan; niwii-ani-giiziiya’kwizitoon nindooskon,” odinan ojisheyan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*And it was true that the old woman wiped herself at the anus with her elbow. Thereupon again the old woman said to her grandson: “Into the thick woods do you go; for, as I go I wish to clean my elbow with the limbs,” she said to her grandson.*</span>

mii dash geget a’aw mindimooye ezhi-giisindimaa’od odooskon — and it was true the old woman wiped herself at the anus with her elbow ∙ mii dash minawaa a’aw mindimooye enaad ojisan — thereupon again the old woman said to her grandson ∙ zaaga’akwaang ani-izhaan — into the thick woods go along ∙ niwii-ani-giiziiya’kwizitoon nindooskon — I wish to clean my elbow as I go (with the branches) ∙ odinan ojisheyan — she says it to her grandson.

<span class="l-orig">(8) Midạc minawa Nänabucu änät ō’kumisan: “Sōbandan,” udinän.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash minawaa Nenabozho enaad o’gomisan: “\*zoonbandan\*,” odinaan.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Whereupon again Nänabushu said to his grandmother: “Lick it with your tongue,” he said to her.*</span>

<span class="l-gloss">mii dash minawaa Nenabozho enaad o’gomisan — whereupon again Nanabushu said to his grandmother ∙ \*zoonbandan\* — lick it (with your tongue) (\*flagged\*: Jones sōbandan; sense per English) ∙ odinaan — he says it to her.</span>

<span class="l-orig">(9) Anic, mi win minawa äjisōbandank ōtōskun, mīdac aᵘ mindimōyä änät ocicä’yan: “Nōjis\! nawate pangi i’ku-’kwänîn.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**Anish, mii wiin minawaa ezhi-zoonbandang odooskon, mii dash a’aw mindimooye enaad ojisheyan: “Noozhis\! nawadi bangii \*i’gokwenin\*.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*Well, it was the same again, for she licked her elbow with her tongue, whereupon the old woman said to her grandson: “O my grandson\! just raise your head a little.”*</span>

Anish, mii wiin minawaa ezhi-zoonbandang odooskon — well, it was the same again, she licked her elbow ∙ mii dash a’aw mindimooye enaad ojisheyan — whereupon the old woman said to her grandson ∙ Noozhis — my grandson ∙ \*nawadi bangii i’gokwenin\* — just raise your head a little (\*flagged\*: form not in lexicon, sense per English).

<span class="l-orig">(10) “Anic?” i’kito Nänabucu.</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Aaniish?” ikido Nenabozho.**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“Why?” said Nänabushu.*</span>

aaniish — why ∙ ikido Nenabozho — says Nanabushu.

<span class="l-orig">(11) “Niwiskwätcigä,” i’kito mindimōyä. Nänabucu i’kito: “Kuntan.”</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**“Niwii-zikwaajige,” ikido mindimooye. Nenabozho ikido: “Goondan.”**</span>

<span class="l-eng">*“I want to spit,” said the old woman. Nänabushu said: “Swallow it.”*</span>

niwii-zikwaajige — I want to spit ∙ ikido mindimooye — says the old woman ∙ Nenabozho ikido — Nanabushu says ∙ goondan — swallow it (imperative).
