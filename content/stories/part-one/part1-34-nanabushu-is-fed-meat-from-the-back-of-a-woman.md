(34) Nanabosho is fed Meat from the Back of a Woman

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, a’yiindaawag imaa anishinaabeg (now, some people were living there), Nenabosho oniijaanisa niizhiwa (Nenabosho’s two children) wiiwan gaye (and his wife).</span>

1)  > **Aaniish, a’yiindaawag imaa anishinaabeg, Nenabosho oniijaanisa niizhiwa wiiwan gaye.**

> Now, abiding at the place were some people, the two children of Nenabosho and his wife.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa imaa babiboonishiwaad (and so there, where they were wintering), aaniish inaa (why now), nawaj igo gaawiin gegoo ona’enimosiin (hardly anything at all had he laid up in store).</span>

2)  > **Mii sa imaa babiboonishiwaad, aaniish inaa, nawaj igo gaawiin gegoo ona’enimosiin.**

> And so there, where they passed the winter, why, hardly any food had they in store.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, mii sa zhigwa ji-biboonini (well, it was now getting to be deep winter), mii sa gaawiin wiikaa naa obiidoosiin gegoo (and never once did he bring anything home).</span>

3)  > **Aaniish, mii sa zhigwa ji-biboonini, mii sa gaawiin wiikaa naa obiidoosiin gegoo.**

> Well, it was now far into the winter, and never a single thing did he fetch home.

Aaniish mii sa bakadewaad (naturally, then, they grew hungry).

4)  > **Aaniish mii sa bakadewaad.**

> Naturally, therefore, they grew hungry.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo, babaamosed (and once, as he was walking about), anishinaabe ezhi-odisaad (he came upon some people); niizhiwa abinoojiiya abiwan (there were two children present) iiniw ininiwan (and that man).</span>

5)  > **Ningoding igo, babaamosed, anishinaabe ezhi-odisaad; niizhiwa abinoojiiya abiwan iiniw ininiwan.**

> And once, when walking about, to where some people were he came; there were two children, (and) at home was the man.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, mii sa ekidonid (so then he said): “Aaniin dash naa, giige-aasiwang a’aw gipiiwideminaan,” (“why now, let us feed this visitor of ours,”) odinaan wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

6)  > **Aaniish, mii sa ekidonid: “Aaniin dash naa, giige-aasiwang a’aw gipiiwideminaan,” odinaan wiiwan.**

> So, therefore, said (the man): “Why, let us feed our visitor,” (thus) he said to his wife.

Aaniish, shkwaandeng naasamabiwan iiniw ikwewan (now, facing the doorway sat that woman).

7)  > **Aaniish, shkwaandeng naasamabiwan iiniw ikwewan.**

> Now, with her face towards the door was the woman seated.

Ezhi-oo-nagoodakikwenid (then she hung up her kettle on the hook).

8)  > **Ezhi-oo-nagoodakikwenid.**

> Then she placed her kettle hanging from a hook.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, ezhi-giiniboodoonid omookomaanini (now, while he was sharpening his knife), mashkimodekewan iiniw wiiwini (his wife was weaving a bag).</span>

9)  > **Aaniish, ezhi-giiniboodoonid omookomaanini, mashkimodekewan iiniw wiiwini.**

> Now, while (the man) was sharpening his knife, his wife was weaving a bag.

Mii sa gaa-giizhiboodoonid i’iw omookomaanini (and when he had finished sharpening his knife, he moved over to sit beside her).

10) > **Mii sa gaa-giizhiboodoonid i’iw omookomaanini.**

> And when he had finished sharpening his knife, he moved over to sit next to his wife.

Obiminanaadabiidawaanini iiniw wiiwini (he edged over to sit close beside his wife).

11) > **Obiminanaadabiidawaanini iiniw wiiwini.**

> \---

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-gidashkaakonamawaad iiniw wiiwan odanikamaanini,1 (then, unfastening his wife’s shoulder-straps,) ezhi-midawaganebinaad (he laid her bare at the back); ezhi-pezhishwaad\* nawaawigan (then he sliced her down the middle of the back); mii sa ezhi-pakweninweshwaad (and so he sliced away a piece from her); gaawiin ganage maadabisiiwan iiniw ikwewan (not a whit did the woman stir).</span>

12) > **Ezhi-gidashkaakonamawaad iiniw wiiwan odanikamaanini,[<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/-nanabosho-is-fed-meat-from-the-back-of-a-woman-line-by-line.html#sdfootnote1sym) ezhi-midawaganebinaad; ezhi-pezhishwaad\* nawaawigan; mii sa ezhi-pakweninweshwaad; gaawiin ganage maadabisiiwan iiniw ikwewan.**

> Then, unfastening his wife’s shoulder-straps, he exposed her at the back; he then sliced her down the middle of the back with a knife; and he then sliced away a piece of fat from her; not a whit did his wife budge.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa gaa-izhi-pishagaawiganeshwaad iiniw wiiwini (and so, when he had sliced a piece from his wife’s back), ezhi-poodaakwewaanid i’imaa akikong (then it was put there into the kettle to boil).</span>

13) > **Mii sa gaa-izhi-pishagaawiganeshwaad iiniw wiiwini, ezhi-poodaakwewaanid i’imaa akikong.**

> And so, when he had sliced a piece from the back of his wife, she then put it into the kettle to boil.

<span class="l-gloss">Akakanzhe ezhi-oodaapinaminid (picking up some charcoal), ezhi-sinigonnamawaanid i’imaa obikwanaaning (he then rubbed it on her there at her back).2</span>

14) > **Akakanzhe ezhi-oodaapinaminid, ezhi-sinigonnamawaanid i’imaa obikwanaaning.**

> Picking up some charcoal, he then rubbed it on her back.[<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/-nanabosho-is-fed-meat-from-the-back-of-a-woman-line-by-line.html#sdfootnote2sym)

Ezhi-sagaakowaad miinawaa (then he fastened up her garment again).

15) > **Ezhi-sagaakowaad miinawaa.**

> Then he fastened her garment on again.

Mii sa gaawiin ganage gii-maadabisiiwan iiniw ikwewan (and so not a whit had the woman moved).

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa giizhisekwewan (when she had finished the cooking), “Aaniish, mii sa iw ezhi-wiisiniiyamban,” (“well, you may as well eat now,”) inaa Nenabosho (Nenabosho was told).</span>

16) > **Mii sa gaawiin ganage gii-maadabisiiwan iiniw ikwewan.**

> And not a whit had his wife moved.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget ezhi-wiisinid (thereupon truly he ate), pane go gaa-niishkininodenig (just as soon as the fat was boiled) mii sa ezhi-wiisinid (was when he ate).</span>

17) > **Zhigwa giizhisekwewan, “Aaniish, mii sa iw ezhi-wiisiniiyamban,” inaa Nenabosho.**

> So when she had finished with the cooking, “Well, you may now as well eat,” was told Nenabosho.

Gomaa minik shkwanjige Nenabosho (a certain amount Nenabosho left uneaten).

18) > **Mii dash geget ezhi-wiisinid, pane go gaa-niishkininodenig mii sa ezhi-wiisinid.**

> Thereupon truly did Nenabosho eat, forthwith after the fat was boiled was when he ate.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii iw izhi-kiiwewida giniijaanisag (“take that home to your children) naa minik eshkwanjigeyan.” (that much you have left over.”)</span>

19) > **Gomaa minik shkwanjige Nenabosho.**

> A certain part of it Nenabosho refrained from eating.

Mii sa zhigwa wii-giiwe (therefore now he was about to go home).

20) > **“Mii iw izhi-kiiwewida giniijaanisag naa minik eshkwanjigeyan.”**

> “That much which you saved do you take to your children.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Awegwenigish?” (“who in the world is it?”) gii-inendam (he thought).</span>

21) > **Mii sa zhigwa wii-giiwe.**

> Therefore now was he on the point of going back home.

Mii nangwana omashkoosoon gaa-odisaad (now it happened to be the elk that he had visited).

22) > **“Awegwenigish?” gii-inendam.**

> “Who in the world (is it)?” he thought.

Aaniish mii sa zhigwa gii-kikenimaad eyaawinid (so then now he knew who it was).

23) > **Mii nangwana omashkoosoon gaa-odisaad.**

> Now, it happened to be the elk whom he had visited.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa wii-giiwe (when he was about to go home), ezhi-aabawaad i’iw ogiizhoodebizona i’iw waaboozowayaana (he untied his warm mittens of rabbit-skin); ezhi-shengwaandaginaad i’iw ogiizhoodewona (then, tucking his mittens into the balsam boughs) ezhi-saaga’ang (he went outside).</span>

24) > **Aaniish mii sa zhigwa gii-kikenimaad eyaawinid.**

> So then at last he found out who it was.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaapii eni-ayaad (when he had got some distance off), ozaagijinisha’ogoo i’iw abinoojiinya (the children rushed out of doors after him).</span>

25) > **Zhigwa wii-giiwe, ezhi-aabawaad i’iw ogiizhoodebizona i’iw waaboozowayaana; ezhi-shengwaandaginaad i’iw ogiizhoodewona ezhi-saaga’ang.**

> As he was about setting out for home, he untied his mittens of rabbit-fur; then, putting his mittens in the balsam boughs (under the mat), he went out of doors.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-piibaagid (and then he called aloud) “Aay\!” ikido (“hey\!” he said); “gii-waniike gigiizhoode’onag\!” (“you forgot your mittens\!”)</span>

26) > **Gomaapii eni-ayaad, ozaagijinisha’ogoo i’iw abinoojiinya.**

> When some distance away he was come, out of doors rushed the children after him.

<span class="l-gloss">“Nindooshimag oga-biinaawa,” (“my nephews will fetch them,”) odinaa (he said to them).</span>

27) > **Mii dash ezhi-piibaagid “Aay\!” ikido; “gii-waniike gigiizhoode’onag\!”**

> And then one called aloud (to him): “Hey\!” he said, “you forgot your mittens\!”

Mii sa geget waabamimaawaad abinid (and then truly they saw where they were lying).

28) > **“Nindooshimag oga-biinaawa,” odinaa.**

> “My nephews will fetch them,” he said to them.

“Waasa go ojiwebinamawik (“and from far off throw them to him.)

29) > **Mii sa geget waabamimaawaad abinid.**

> And then they saw where they were.

Gaawiin giiga-pasemigosiiwa.” (he will surely say something to you.”)

30) > **“Waasa go ojiwebinamawik.**

> “And from afar do you throw them to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget waasa go waa-onji-webinamaago i’iw abinoojiiga (abinoojiinya?) (thereupon truly from afar the children meant to throw them to him, when,)</span>

31) > **Gaawiin giiga-pasemigosiiwa.”**

> He will not refrain from saying something to you.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Ish\!” odinaa (“stop\!” he said to them); “gaawonaagonewebinaawaag,” odinaa (“don’t throw them into the snow,” he said to them).</span>

32) > **Mii dash geget waasa go waa-onji-webinamaago i’iw abinoojiiga (abinoojiinya?).**

> Thereupon truly from afar were the children intending to throw them to him, when,

<span class="l-gloss">“Bi-miishiyokiko\!” (“come, hand them to me\!”) odinaa (he said to them).</span>

33) > **“Ish\!” odinaa; “gaawonaagonewebinaawaag,” odinaa.**

> “Stop\!” he said to them; “do not throw them into the snow, (lest they be lost,)” he said to them.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa geget ewishimiinigod (so truly they handed them to him), mii dash enishimaajiiba’idinid (whereupon they started racing back as they went).</span>

34) > **“Bi-miishiyokiko\!” odinaa.**

> “Come, hand them to me\!” he said to them.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, ogii-inaa aw awedi weniijaanisid (now, that parent of theirs had said to them): “Waasa go onji-webinamawik (“from far off hand them to him), gaawiin giiga-basemigosiiwaa.” (for he will surely say something to you.”)</span>

35) > **Aaniish mii sa geget ewishimiinigod, mii dash enishimaajiiba’idinid.**

> So accordingly he truly had them handed to him, whereupon back (the children) started racing as they went.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget aanawi-doodaminid (therefore they truly tried to do so), aaniish ogii-onjii-aanidish (but he turned them aside).</span>

36) > **Aaniish, ogii-inaa aw awedi weniijaanisid: “Waasa go onji-webinamawik, gaawiin giiga-basemigosiiwaa.”**

> Now, yonder parent of the children had said to them: “From afar do you hand them to him, for he willl not refrain from saying something to you.”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-inaad apii gaa-miinigod (and this is what he said to them when he was handed them): “Ambe sa noo, waabang koosiwaa dabiizhaa,” (“come now, tomorrow let your father come,”) ogii-inaa (he said to them).</span>

37) > **Mii dash geget aanawi-doodaminid, aaniish ogii-onjii-aanidish.**

> Therefore they truly tried to do so, but (Nenabosho) prevented them.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa gaa-izhi-kiiweba’idiwaad iigiw gwiiwisensag (when the boys had raced back home), ezhi-wiindamawaawaad oniigi’igowaa ekidonid Nenaboshoowan (they told their parents what Nenabosho had said): “Gebakademwaasa giinawaa\!” (“you must surely be hungry\!”) odinaa i’iw oniigii’igowa iigiw gwiiwisensag (the boys said to their parents).</span>

38) > **Mii dash gaa-inaad apii gaa-miinigod: “Ambe sa noo, waabang koosiwaa dabiizhaa,” ogii-inaa.**

> And this was what he said to them when he was given (the mittens): “Now, tomorrow let yor father come,” he said to them.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa ekidod inini (thereupon the man said): “Aanishinaa, ninga-izhaa,” ikido (“of course, I will go,” he said).</span>

39) > **Mii sa gaa-izhi-kiiweba’idiwaad iigiw gwiiwisensag, ezhi-wiindamawaawaad oniigi’igowaa ekidonid Nenaboshoowan: “Gebakademwaasa giinawaa\!” odinaa i’iw oniigii’igowa iigiw gwiiwisensag.**

> When the boys had raced back home, they told their parents what Nenabosho had said, “you must be hungry\!” the boys said to their parents.

Zhigwa sa ani-dagwishin endaawaad (meanwhile he was arriving at their home).

40) > **Mii sa ekidod inini: “Aanishinaa, ninga-izhaa,” ikido.**

> Thereupon said the man: “Of course, I will go,” he said.

Aaniish obiidawaa oniijaanisa i’iw gaa-ashaminid (now, he brought home to his children what he had been given to eat).

41) > **Zhigwa sa ani-dagwishin endaawaad.**

> In the mean while (Nenabosho) was arriving home.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa oganoonaan iiniw wiiwan (then he spoke to his wife): “Gaawiin sa naa wiikaa ji-omashkimodekeyan?” (“won’t you ever weave bags?”) odinaan iiniw wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

42) > **Aaniish obiidawaa oniijaanisa i’iw gaa-ashaminid.**

> Now, he fetched home to his children what had been given him to eat.

<span class="l-gloss">“Indashkaa miinawaa awiya gii-waabamaagwen i’iw ezhichigenid,” (“no doubt he must again have seen somebody doing that,”) odigoon iiniw wiiwan (he was told by his wife).</span>

43) > **Zhigwa oganoonaan iiniw wiiwan: “Gaawiin sa naa wiikaa ji-omashkimodekeyan?” odinaan iiniw wiiwan.**

> Then he spoke to his wife, saying: “Why do you never weave bags?” he said to his wife.

<span class="l-gloss">“Awawa izhichigen\!” (“go ahead and do it\!”) odinaan (he said to her).</span>

44) > **“Indashkaa miinawaa awiya gii-waabamaagwen i’iw ezhichigenid,” odigoon iiniw wiiwan.**

> “No doubt but that again he must have seen somebody doing that,” he was told by his wife.

Mii sa geget ezhi-mashkimodeked wayaabaninig a’aw ikwe (thereupon truly the woman set to weaving a bag on the morrow).

45) > **“Awawa izhichigen\!” odinaan.**

> Go ahead and do it\!” he said to her.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish abi a’aw Nenabosho (so Nenabosho was at home), obii’aan (he was waiting for him).</span>

46) > **Mii sa geget ezhi-mashkimodeked wayaabaninig a’aw ikwe.**

> Thereupon truly did the woman set to work weaving a bag on the morrow.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa bi-giigidoowa oniijaanisa (at last his children spoke up): “Nashke giniin, zhigwa biiwide\!” (“look, you\! now there’s a visitor\!”) odigoo (he was told).</span>

47) > **Aaniish abi a’aw Nenabosho, obii’aan.**

> So at home was Nenabosho, he was waiting for (his guest).

Aaniish mii sa ezhi-piindigaagowaad iiniw biiwiden (so thereupon the visitor came in where they were).

48) > **Zhigwa sa bi-giigidoowa oniijaanisa: “Nashke giniin, zhigwa biiwide\!” odigoo.**

> At last came his children, saying: “Oh, see\! Here is a visitor\!” he was told.

Mii sa namadabinid (and then he sat down).

49) > **Aaniish mii sa ezhi-piindigaagowaad iiniw biiwiden.**

> So thereupon in where they were came the visitor.

<span class="l-gloss">“Wegonena gegii-ge’ang a’aw biiwide?” (“what shall we feed this visitor?”) odinaan iiniw wiiwan (he said to his wife).</span>

50) > **Mii sa namadabinid.**

> And then he sat down.

<span class="l-gloss">“Wegonen dash i’iw eyaayan?” (“and what is it you have?”) odigoon iiniw wiiwan (he was told by his wife).</span>

51) > **“Wegonena gegii-ge’ang a’aw biiwide?” odinaan iiniw wiiwan.**

> “What shall we feed the visitor?” he said to his wife.

Aaniish maajiboodoon omookomaan (so he began filing his knife).

52) > **“Wegonen dash i’iw eyaayan?” odigoon iiniw wiiwan.**

> “Now, what do you have?” he was told by his wife.

Zhoozhamiingweniwan obiiwideman (their visitor wore a smile on his face).

53) > **Aaniish maajiboodoon omookomaan.**

> So he began filing his knife.

Mii zhigwa gii-oo-nagoodakikwed (then now he hung up the kettle).

54) > **Zhoozhamiingweniwan obiiwideman.**

> Then a smile was on the face of their visitor.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish zhigwa ogiigidiz gaa-gonamawaan odanikamaanini iiniw wiiwan (so now, when he had unfastened his wife’s shoulder-straps), ezhi-nitaawaganebinaad iiniw wiiwan (he laid his wife bare at the back); ezhi-pezhishwaad imaa bikwanaaning (then he sliced her there at the back).</span>

55) > **Mii zhigwa gii-oo-nagoodakikwed.**

> Then finally (Nenabosho) hung up the kettle.

Aaniish inaa, “Igoo\!” odigoon (naturally, “ouch\!” he was told).

56) > **Aaniish zhigwa ogiigidiz gaa-gonamawaan odanikamaanini iiniw wiiwan, ezhi-nitaawaganebinaad iiniw wiiwan; ezhi-pezhishwaad imaa bikwanaaning.**

> So when he had unfastened his wife’s shoulder-straps, he uncovered his wife at the back; then he sliced her down the back with a knife.

Zhooshamiingweniwan iiniw obiiwideman (his visitor wore a smile on his face).

57) > **Aaniish inaa, “Igoo\!” odigoon.**

> Naturally, “Ouch\!” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Daga shkomaa, Nenabosho,” (“pray, let me, Nenabosho,”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

58) > **Zhooshamiingweniwan iiniw obiiwideman.**

> There was a smile on the face of his visitor.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish, mii sa ezhi-ando-damaagod i’iw mookomaan (now, so then he was asked for the knife), mii dash egod (and this is what he was told): “Daga, Nenabosho\!” odigoon (“do, Nenabosho\!” he was told).</span>

59) > **Daga shkomaa, Nenabosho,” odigoon.**

> “Pray, let me, Nenabosho\!” he was told (by the visitor).

<span class="l-gloss">“Agashkomaa, Nenabosho\!” (“please, let me, Nenabosho\!”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

60) > **Aaniish, mii sa ezhi-ando-damaagod i’iw mookomaan, mii dash egod: “Daga, Nenabosho\!” odigoon.**

> Now, therefore when (Nenabosho) was asked for the knife, this he was told: “Do, Nenabosho\!” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-miinaad i’iw mookomaan (when he gave him the knife), ezhi-peshaawaganeshomind iiniw wiiwan (his wife was sliced down the back), mii sa gaawiin ganage maadabisiiwan (and so not a whit did she stir).</span>

61) > **“Agashkomaa, Nenabosho\!” odigoon.**

> “Please let me, Nenabosho\!” he was told.

Bakwadishomind iiniw wiininoon (what was sliced from her was her fat).

62) > **Ezhi-miinaad i’iw mookomaan, ezhi-peshaawaganeshomind iiniw wiiwan, mii sa gaawiin ganage maadabisiiwan.**

> When (Nenabosho) gave him the knife, then was his wife sliced down the back, and so not a whit did she wince.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa ogii-pakwadishwaanini (when the fat had been sliced from her), “Aaw, Nenabosho, mii iw ezhi-jiibaakwen,” (“now, Nenabosho, go ahead and cook,”) odigoon (he was told).</span>

63) > **Bakwadishomind iiniw wiininoon.**

> What was cut from her was her fat.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-odaapinaminid i’iw akakanzhe (taking up the charcoal) ezhi-sinagwanaminid i’imaa bikwanaaning iiniw wiiwan (he rubbed it there on the back of the wife).</span>

64) > **Zhigwa ogii-pakwadishwaanini, “Aaw, Nenabosho, mii iw ezhi-jiibaakwen,” odigoon.**

> When the fat was cut from her, “Now, Nenabosho, therefore now do you cook,” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa sa basigwiiwan (then, as he rose to his feet), “Mii iw, Nenabosho, da-izhi-wiisiniwag giniijaanisag,” (“that is it, Nenabosho, your children will eat,”) odigoowaan (they were told).</span>

65) > **Ezhi-odaapinaminid i’iw akakanzhe ezhi-sinagwanaminid i’imaa bikwanaaning iiniw wiiwan.**

> Taking up some charcoal, he rubbed (Nenabosho’s) wife with it on the back.

Mii sa geget apane mii sa gaa-ani-maajaanid iiniw biiwidemiwaan (thereupon truly, straightaway, their visitor went off on his way).

66) > **Zhigwa sa basigwiiwan, “Mii iw, Nenabosho, da-izhi-wiisiniwag giniijaanisag,” odigoowaan.**

> Then rising to his feet, “Now, Nenabosho, your children will eat,” they were told.

Mii sa zhigwa wiisiniwaad (thereupon now they ate).

67) > **Mii sa geget apane mii sa gaa-ani-maajaanid iiniw biiwidemiwaan.**

> Thereupon truly forthwith went the visitor upon his way.

68) > **Mii sa zhigwa wiisiniwaad.**

> Thereupon now did they eat.
