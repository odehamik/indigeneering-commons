<span class="l-gloss">## **(53) Nänabushu and the Woodpecker ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-and-the-woodpecker-footnotes.html))**</span>

Gii-ani-maajaa dash Nenaboozhoo (so on his way went Nänabushu).

1)  > **Gii-ani-maajaa dash Nenaboozhoo.**

> So on his way went Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa ogii-ani-odisaan (and next he went off to visit him) memen daanid (the red-head, where he was) wiimbinakadoong (in the hollow of a tree).</span>

2)  > **Miinawaa ogii-ani-odisaan memen daanid wiimbinakadoong.**

> Next he went to visit the red-head at where he was in the hole of a tree.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gii-piindiged (and so he went inside) endaanid (where he lived).</span>

3)  > **Mii dash gii-piindiged endaanid.**

> And so he went into where the (red-head) lived.

<span class="l-gloss">Meme dash gii-akwaandawe (now the red-head climbed up) imaa baate-zhingwaakong (there on a dead pine-tree).</span>

4)  > **Meme dash gii-akwaandawe imaa baate-zhingwaakong.**

> Now, the red-head climbed up a dead pine-tree.

<span class="l-gloss">Oganawaabamaan (he watched him) endoodaminid iniw memen (what that red-head was doing).</span>

5)  > **Oganawaabamaan endoodaminid iniw memen.**

> He watched what the red-head was doing.

<span class="l-gloss">Ani-gagwedikwe’o (he kept testing where to peck — \*stem flagged\*) imaa mitigong (there on the tree); aapiji dash ishpiming (and very high up) gichi-enigok (with all his might) madwekwe’od (the sound of him pecking was heard — \*stem flagged\*) mii imaa wenji-pangishininid esiban (then from there came falling a raccoon).</span>

6)  > **Ani-gagwedikwe’o imaa mitigong; aapiji dash ishpiming gichi-enigok madwekwe’od mii imaa wenji-pangishininid esiban.**

> (The red-head) kept testing where to peck on his way up the tree; and when very high up was heard the sound of him pecking with all his might, then down from there came falling a raccoon.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii wa nini gaa-kiizhizwaad (that was the very thing he cooked) gii-ashamaad Nenaboozhoon (when he fed Nänabushu).</span>

7)  > **Mii wa nini gaa-kiizhizwaad gii-ashamaad Nenaboozhoon.**

> That was what he cooked when he fed Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">Eshkwaa-wiisinid Nenaboozhoo (when Nänabushu was done eating), “Gaye niin ningad-ashamaa meme.” (“I too will feed the red-head.”)</span>

8)  > **Eshkwaa-wiisinid Nenaboozhoo, “Gaye niin ningad-ashamaa meme.”**

> When Nänabushu was done eating, “I too will feed the red-head.”

<span class="l-gloss">Niizh dash okanan (now, two bones) ogii-odaapinaanan (he took up).</span>

9)  > **Niizh dash okanan ogii-odaapinaanan.**

> Now, two bones he took.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-zhiga’ang eyiidawaya’ii (and so when he had sharpened them at both ends), mii dash gii-atood (then he placed them) odenigomaang (in his nostrils).</span>

10) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-zhiga’ang eyiidawaya’ii, mii dash gii-atood odenigomaang.**

> And so when he had sharpened them at both ends, he accordingly placed them in his nostrils.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash akwaandawed imaa mitigong (and so when he climbed up the tree) gaye wiin gagwedikwe (he too tested where to peck — \*stem flagged\*).</span>

11) > **Mii dash akwaandawed imaa mitigong gaye wiin gagwedikwe.**

> And when he climbed up the tree, he also tested where to peck.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji dash ishpiming e-yaad (and when he was very high up), mii iw gichi-enigok (then with all his might) ezhi-madwekwe’od (he was heard pecking — \*stem flagged\*).</span>

12) > **Aapiji dash ishpiming e-yaad, mii iw gichi-enigok ezhi-madwekwe’od.**

> And when very high up he was, he then heard pecking with all his might.

Mii dash ezhi-nisidizod (and thereupon he killed himself).

13) > **Mii dash ezhi-nisidizod.**

> Thereupon, he killed himself.

Gaawiin ogii-nisaasiin esibanan (he did not kill a raccoon).

14) > **Gaawiin ogii-nisaasiin esibanan.**

> He did not kill a raccoon.

Memen dash ogii-pimaaji’igoon (and by the red-head he was brought back to life).

15) > **Memen dash ogii-pimaaji’igoon.**

> And by the red-head was he brought back to life.

Mii iw gii-ani-maajad (and so on his way he went).

16) > **Mii iw gii-ani-maajad.**

> And so on his way he went.

Mii sa go naa ekoozid (and that is as far as it goes).

17) > **Mii sa go naa ekoozid.**

> And that is as far as (the story) goes.

**Footnote :**

<span class="l-gloss">(1) For other versions see Nos. 35 (p. 305) and 42 (p. 357).</span>
