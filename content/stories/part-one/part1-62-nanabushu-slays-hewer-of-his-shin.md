**(62) Nänabushu slays Hewer-of-his-Shin**

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash awe mindimooye (and so that old woman) gichi-minwendang (was very glad) waabamaad (when she saw him) miinawaa oozhisheyan (her grandson once again).</span>

1)  > **Mii dash awe mindimooye gichi-minwendang waabamaad miinawaa oozhisheyan.**

> Thereupon the old woman was very happy to see her grandson once more.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin dash wiin igo (and not at all did she) ogikendanziin (know it) bangii nawaj (a little more) gii-aanji-oskiniigiinid (that she had been made young again).</span>

2)  > **Gaawiin dash wiin igo ogikendanziin bangii nawaj gii-aanji-oskiniigiinid.**

> And not at all did she realize that she had been made younger.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash e-naad oozhisan (and so she said to her grandson): “Apane awiya (all the time someone) ningii-pi’odisigoog (kept coming to me here); izhi-waad (so they said) gii-nisigooyan (that you had been slain).</span>

3)  > **Mii dash e-naad oozhisan: “Apane awiya ningii-pi’odisigoog; izhi-waad gii-nisigooyan.**

> So then she said to her grandson: “All the time some creatures came to me here; they told me that you were slain.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa aanind indigoog (and again by some I would be told), ‘Nookomis (O my grandmother), nin-dagwishin (I have arrived home),’ onjida bi-izhiwaad (with some design they came saying so),” odinaan oozhisheyan (she said to her grandson).</span>

4)  > **Miinawaa aanind indigoog, ‘Nookomis, nin-dagwishin,’ onjida bi-izhiwaad,” odinaan oozhisheyan.**

> Again by some would I be told, ‘O my grandmother\! I have come home.’ With some design they came speaking thus to me,” she said to her grandson.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo nishkaadizid (and so Nänabushu was angry), mii dash e-naad ookomisan (and so he said to his grandmother): “Geyaabi giga-naganin (again I shall leave you).</span>

5)  > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo nishkaadizid, mii dash e-naad ookomisan: “Geyaabi giga-naganin.**

> Thereupon Nänabushu was angry, and he said to his grandmother: “Again shall I leave you.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin mashi ni-mikawaasii (not yet have I found them) aweneniidog (whoever they may be) jaagi’aagwen (the ones who must have made an end of) nin-gitiziimag (my elders, my kin).”</span>

6)  > **Gaawiin mashi ni-mikawaasii aweneniidog jaagi’aagwen nin-gitiziimag.”**

> Not yet have I found the probable ones that must have slain all my relatives.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa\! noozhis (alas, my grandson),” odinaan (she said to him), “gigii-kidimaagis (you are to be pitied). Gaawiin gidaa-kashki’osii (you will not be able) ji-izhaayan iwidi (to go there) ayaad aw gaa-kiiwooshi’ik (where dwells the one who orphaned you), \[gaa-giiwizi’ig\]” odinaan (she said to him).</span>

7)  > **“Aa\! noozhis,” odinaan, “gigii-kidimaagis. Gaawiin gidaa-kashki’osii ji-izhaayan iwidi ayaad aw gaa-kiiwooshi’ik, \[gaa-giiwizi’ig\]” odinaan.**

> “Alas\! my grandson,” she said to him, “you are to be pitied. You may not be able to go there where abides the one who made you an orphan,” she said to him.

“Aandish ayaad? (and where is it that he dwells?)”

8)  > **“Aandish ayaad?”**

> “And where is it?”

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii iwidi ayaamagag (there at yonder place is it) naawaya’ii gichi-gichigamiing (in the middle of the great sea) bezhig minis (one island).</span>

9)  > **“Mii iwidi ayaamagag naawaya’ii gichi-gichigamiing bezhig minis.**

> “At yonder place in the centre of the great sea is an island.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin dash wiikaa awiya (and never has anyone) izhaasii iwidi (gone there).</span>

10) > **Gaawiin dash wiikaa awiya izhaasii iwidi.**

> And never has any one gone there.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go aapiisikaad (and he goes for good — \*aapiisikaa- stem flagged\*) awiya e-zhaajin (whoever it is that goes there).</span>

11) > **Mii go aapiisikaad awiya e-zhaajin.**

> And he goes for good whoever goes there.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin dash gikendanziin (and it is not known) amanj ji-dagwishinoowaagwen iwidi (whether they ever arrived there), gonimaa gaye nwaande-nibowaagwen (or whether they died on the way — \*nwaande- stem flagged\*),” odigoon ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother).</span>

12) > **Gaawiin dash gikendanziin amanj ji-dagwishinoowaagwen iwidi, gonimaa gaye nwaande-nibowaagwen,” odigoon ookomisan.**

> I don’t know whether they have ever arrived at the place, or if they died on the way,” he was (thus) told by his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii dash iwidi ayaad aw manidoo (and over there dwells that manidoo); e-zhinikaazod Gaa-jiigiga’ang-okakwan (he is called Hews-Upon-His-Shin), mii e-nind (so it is said of him) mii apane ezhi-jiigiga’ang iw ogakawan (that always he hews upon his shin), inaa (so they say).</span>

13) > **“Mii dash iwidi ayaad aw manidoo; e-zhinikaazod Gaa-jiigiga’ang-okakwan, mii e-nind mii apane ezhi-jiigiga’ang iw ogakawan, inaa.**

> “And over there is a manitou; he is called Hews-upon-his-Shin, for it is said of him that ever is he hewing upon his shin.

<span class="l-gloss">Giishpin awiya izhaad iwidi (if anyone goes there) ayaabita izhi-aawang (at the halfway point) mii debwewe’ang ogakwan (one hears the sound he makes upon his shin),” odinaan (she said to him).</span>

14) > **Giishpin awiya izhaad iwidi ayaabita izhi-aawang mii debwewe’ang ogakwan,” odinaan.**

> It is said of him that if any one should go thither, then at half way to the place would one hear the sound he makes upon his shin,” she said to him.

<span class="l-gloss">“Oo\! (really\!)” odigoon oozhisheyan (she was told by her grandson). Mii dash geget giizhendang Nenaboozhoo (and so truly Nänabushu made up his mind), inendam wii-izhaad (he resolved that he would go).</span>

15) > **“Oo\!” odigoon oozhisheyan. Mii dash geget giizhendang Nenaboozhoo, inendam wii-izhaad.**

> “Really\!” was she told by her grandson. Thereupon in truth did Nänabushu make up his mind, he thought that he would go.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash e-naad ookomisan (and so he said to his grandmother): “Booch ninga-nandawaabamaa (I am bound to go look for him),” odinaan (he said to her).</span>

16) > **Mii dash e-naad ookomisan: “Booch ninga-nandawaabamaa,” odinaan.**

> And so he said to his grandmother: “Determined am I to go look for him,” he said to her.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo miinawaa gaa-izhi-ozhiitaad (and so Nänabushu again made ready) miinawaa gii-ozhitood osaawaanan (and made spear-pointed arrows), nisogon minik (enough for three days) ji-aabajitood (that he would use) ogii-izhi-ozhitoonan (so many he made).</span>

17) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo miinawaa gaa-izhi-ozhiitaad miinawaa gii-ozhitood osaawaanan, nisogon minik ji-aabajitood ogii-izhi-ozhitoonan.**

> Thereupon Nänabushu again made ready by making spear-pointed arrows, enough to last him three days was the number he made.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-kiizhitood (and so after he had finished them) miinawaa Nenaboozhoo (again Nänabushu) mamadesidood ojiimaanish (put his old canoe in order).</span>

18) > **Mii dash gaa-kiizhitood miinawaa Nenaboozhoo mamadesidood ojiimaanish.**

> And so after he had finished them, then again Nänabushu put his canoe in order.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash maajaad Nenaboozhoo (and then off went Nänabushu) gwayak gaa-ininamaagod ookomisan (straight where it had been pointed out to him by his grandmother).</span>

19) > **Mii dash maajaad Nenaboozhoo gwayak gaa-ininamaagod ookomisan.**

> And then away went Nänabushu straight towards where it had been pointed out to him by his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding dash giiwenh (now by and by, they say) Nenaboozhoo ani-babimishkaad (as Nänabushu went paddling along), zhigwa geget gegoo onoondam (presently truly he heard something) gwayak e-zhaad (straight ahead where he was going).</span>

20) > **Ningoding dash giiwenh Nenaboozhoo ani-babimishkaad, zhigwa geget gegoo onoondam gwayak e-zhaad.**

> Now, by and by, they say, as Nänabushuwent paddling along (in his canoe), presently something he truly heard straight (on the way) whither he was going.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-agwamod (then he let his canoe float quietly) nendotang (while he listened); zhigwa geget onoondaan (presently truly he heard it).</span>

21) > **Ezhi-agwamod nendotang; zhigwa geget onoondaan.**

> He let his canoe go floating quietly along upon the water while he listened to the sound; presently he clearly heard a sound.

<span class="l-gloss">“Denh denh,” inidam (such was the sound he heard). Mii e-nendang (and so he thought): “Na\! mii iwe (hark\! that is it) nookomis gaa-izhid (what my grandmother told me),” inendam (he thought).</span>

22) > **“Denh denh,” inidam. Mii e-nendang: “Na\! mii iwe nookomis gaa-izhid,” inendam.**

> “Tän, tän,” (such) was the sound he heard. Thereupon he thought: “Hark\! that is what my grandmother told me,” he thought.

Mii dash geget maajaad enigok (and so truly he set off with all his might).

23) > **Mii dash geget maajaad enigok.**

> And so truly he hurried on.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo gomaapii (Nänabushu, after a while) miinawaa nandotam (again listened for the sound) zhigwa miinawaa onoondam (and presently again he heard it).</span>

24) > **Nenaboozhoo gomaapii miinawaa nandotam zhigwa miinawaa onoondam.**

> Nänabushu later on again listened for the sound, presently again he heard it.

<span class="l-gloss">“Dwenh dwenh, dwenh,” (1) initam (such was what he heard).</span>

25) > **“Dwenh dwenh, dwenh,” ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) initam.**

> “Twän, twän, twän,” ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) was what he heard.

<span class="l-gloss">Nawaj beshodaagwadini (nearer now it sounded). Miinawaa maajaad enigok (again he set off with all his might).</span>

26) > **Nawaj beshodaagwadini. Miinawaa maajaad enigok.**

> Nearer it now sounded. Again he hastened on.

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo miinawaa ani-ayinaabid (another time, as he looked about), gego owaabandaan oniigaaniimi (he saw something ahead of him); indigo gegoo bemibeshibii’igaadeg (like something drawn as a line across); mii dash ayinaabid (and so as he looked), “Mii maawiin i’iw (perhaps that) zhayiigwa dayebinaagwak (now coming into view) minis e-zhaayaan (is the island I am going to),” inendam (he thought).</span>

27) > **Ningoding igo miinawaa ani-ayinaabid, gego owaabandaan oniigaaniimi; indigo gegoo bemibeshibii’igaadeg; mii dash ayinaabid, “Mii maawiin i’iw zhayiigwa dayebinaagwak minis e-zhaayaan,” inendam.**

> Another time, as he was looking about, he saw something in the way ahead of him; it was an object like a line drawn across his course; and then, as he looked, “Perhaps that is which is now coming into view is the island for which I am bound,” he thought.

Mii dash geget enigok bimishkaad (and so truly with all his might he paddled on).

28) > **Mii dash geget enigok bimishkaad.**

> Thereupon he truly hastened on with his canoe.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaapii dash miinawaa nandotam (and after a while again he listened), mii geyaapi enitang (and still he heard it the same). “Dwiinh dwiinh,” (2) initam (such was the sound he heard).</span>

29) > **Gomaapii dash miinawaa nandotam, mii geyaapi enitang. “Dwiinh dwiinh,” ([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) initam.**

> Farther on he listened again for the sound, and he heard it the same as before. “Twin, twin,” ([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) was the sound he heard.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii imaa gwayak endanitang (from straight over there he heard it) bi-midii bi-naagwadinig aki (where the land was coming into view).</span>

30) > **Mii imaa gwayak endanitang bi-midii bi-naagwadinig aki.**

> Straight from the yonder place where the land was coming into view he heard the sound.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii nangwana geget iw (it turned out truly to be that) gaa-izhinang (which he had seen).</span>

31) > **Mii nangwana geget iw gaa-izhinang.**

> It turned out truly to be (the island) that he had seen.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash miinawaa maajaad (and so again he went on), zhayiigwa owaabandaan weweni aki (now he saw the land plainly); mii dash miinawaa ezhi-nandotang (and so again he listened), mii miinawaa noondang (and again he heard it).</span>

32) > **Mii dash miinawaa maajaad, zhayiigwa owaabandaan weweni aki; mii dash miinawaa ezhi-nandotang, mii miinawaa noondang.**

> And now, as he continued on, he presently saw the land in plain sight; thereupon again he listened, and then again he heard the sound.

<span class="l-gloss">“Dwiinh dwiinh,” (3) initam (such was what he heard).</span>

33) > **“Dwiinh dwiinh,” ([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) initam.**

> “Twin, twin,” ([<span class="underline">3</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) was what he heard.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go ezhi-koshkwaagamiseg nibi (and then the water trembled — \*koshkwaagamise- stem flagged\*) epiichi-kizhiiweganaandang iw ogakwan (so loud was he hewing upon his shin — \*kizhiiweganaand- stem flagged\*) aw manidoo (that manidoo).</span>

34) > **Mii go ezhi-koshkwaagamiseg nibi epiichi-kizhiiweganaandang iw ogakwan aw manidoo.**

> And then the water trembled, so loud was the manitou hewing upon his shin.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash giiwenh Nenaboozhoo (and so, they say, Nänabushu) zhayiigwa ani-jegised imaa gwayak endanitang (now drove his canoe straight to where he heard the sound — \*jegise- stem flagged\*); geget eni-gabaad (truly as he went ashore) owaabandaan ondamoninig miikana (he saw a path leading off somewhere).</span>

35) > **Mii dash giiwenh Nenaboozhoo zhayiigwa ani-jegised imaa gwayak endanitang; geget eni-gabaad owaabandaan ondamoninig miikana.**

> Thereupon it is said that Nänabushu now drove his canoe straight for the place in the shore from whence he heard the sound come; truly as he went ashore he saw a path leading away somewhere.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash eni-zhi-kopa’adood \[gopii’adood?\] (and so as he followed it up from the shore — \*bracket Corbiere’s\*), owaabandaan wiigiwaamens badakidenig (he saw a small wigwam standing); mii dash giimooj eni-zhi-naazikang (and so secretly he went up to it); eni-zhi-dapaabandang (as he peeped in), owaabamaan akiwenziiyan nenaange-naasamabinid (he saw an old man seated squatting facing him), mii dash dakonaminid gegoo (and he held something in his hand).</span>

36) > **Mii dash eni-zhi-kopa’adood \[gopii’adood?\], owaabandaan wiigiwaamens badakidenig; mii dash giimooj eni-zhi-naazikang; eni-zhi-dapaabandang, owaabamaan akiwenziiyan nenaange-naasamabinid, mii dash dakonaminid gegoo.**

> And as he followed it up from the shore, he saw a small wigwam standing; and so secretly went he up to it; as he peeped in, he saw an old man seated in a squatting pose, facing him, and he had hold of something in his hand.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-waabamaad (and so as he watched him) bakite’aminid ogakwan (striking upon his shin), dibishkoo jiigiga’igeng (just like hewing upon a log) mii endoodang (such was his manner of doing it).</span>

37) > **Mii dash ezhi-waabamaad bakite’aminid ogakwan, dibishkoo jiigiga’igeng mii endoodang.**

> And as he watched him striking upon his shin, it was like hewing upon a log, such was his manner of doing it.

<span class="l-gloss">Gagwaanisagakamig dash (and frightful it was) epiitwewekanaadang (so loud was the sound he made when he struck).</span>

38) > **Gagwaanisagakamig dash epiitwewekanaadang.**

> And frightful was the sound that he made when he struck.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash wiin Nenaboozhoo (and as for Nänabushu), imaa niibawid ganawaabamaad (there he stood watching him).</span>

39) > **Mii dash wiin Nenaboozhoo, imaa niibawid ganawaabamaad.**

> And as for Nänabushu, there he stood observing him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gomaapii dash bi-gwekitaawan (after a while the other turned about) mii dash e-god (and so he was told): (4) “A-a-a Nenaboozhoo\! (aha, aha, aha, Nänabushu\!) Gi-bi-nandopanidaw ina? (have you come to make war on me?)” odigoon (he was told).</span>

40) > **Gomaapii dash bi-gwekitaawan mii dash e-god: ([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) “A-a-a Nenaboozhoo\! Gi-bi-nandopanidaw ina?” odigoon.**

> After a while the other then turned about, and said: ([<span class="underline">4</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/naumlnabushu-slays-hewer-of-his-shin-footnotes.html)) “Aha, aha, aha, Nänabushu\! Have you some to make war upon me?” he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aye’ (yes),” odinaan Nenaboozhoo (said Nänabushu to him).</span>

41) > **“Aye’,” odinaan Nenaboozhoo.**

> “Yes,” to him said Nänabushu.

<span class="l-gloss">“Awawasa\! (very well then\!)” Bekish baapikaazod (at the same time pretending to laugh — \*baapikaazo- stem flagged\*), epiichi-maanimaad Nenaboozhoon (so deeply did he scorn Nänabushu); gaawiin giiwaadendanzii (he had no doubt at all) ji-shaagooji’aad (that he would prevail over him).</span>

42) > **“Awawasa\!” Bekish baapikaazod, epiichi-maanimaad Nenaboozhoon; gaawiin giiwaadendanzii ji-shaagooji’aad.**

> “Very well, then\!” At the same time he made a pretence at laughing, so deep was his contempt of Nänabushu; he had no doubt but that he would prevail over him.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo (and as for Nänabushu too), “Ahaaw, wewiib\! (come, make haste\!)”</span>

43) > **Mii dash gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo, “Ahaaw, wewiib\!”**

> And as for Nänabushu, too, “Come, make haste\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget biiwanaangiid (and so truly slowly he rose from his couch — \*biiwanaangii- stem flagged\*), bi-pazigwiid gaye (and rose to his feet too); mii dash ezhi-pi-saaga’ang (and so he came out of doors).</span>

44) > **Mii dash geget biiwanaangiid, bi-pazigwiid gaye; mii dash ezhi-pi-saaga’ang.**

> And so, in truth, up he slowly rose from his couch, and up he rose to his feet; and then he came out of doors.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aaniish, giga-onaabandaamin \[onaabanjigemi\] (well, let us choose — \*bracket Corbiere’s\*) ge-dazhikotaadiyang (the place where we are to fight each other),” odigoon (he was told).</span>

45) > **“Aaniish, giga-onaabandaamin \[onaabanjigemi\] ge-dazhikotaadiyang,” odigoon.**

> “Well, let us pick out a place where we are to fight each other,” (Nänabushu) was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ahaaw (all right),” odinaan (he said to him).</span>

46) > **“Ahaaw,” odinaan.**

> “All right,” he said to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget wawenaabandamowaad (and so truly they looked carefully for a place). “Mii omaa (here is a place),” ikidowag (they said).</span>

47) > **Mii dash geget wawenaabandamowaad. “Mii omaa,” ikidowag.**

> Thereupon they truly sought for a place. “Here is a place,” they said.

Ayinaabi Jaagaga’ang-ogakwan (round about looked Hewer-of-His-Shin).

48) > **Ayinaabi Jaagaga’ang-ogakwan.**

> Round about looked Hewer-of-his-Shin.

<span class="l-gloss">Dedaataganaabid gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo (and Nänabushu too looked up into the air — \*dedaataganaab- stem flagged\*); gii-owenigaabawi (he stood in his place — \*owenigaabawi stem flagged\*), odashwiiwin dakonam (his shield he held), mitigwaabiin gaye (and his bow and arrows too).</span>

49) > **Dedaataganaabid gaye wiin Nenaboozhoo; gii-owenigaabawi, odashwiiwin dakonam, mitigwaabiin gaye.**

> And up into the air looked Nänabushu; he stood in his place, his shield he barried \[carried?\], so too his bow and arrows.

<span class="l-gloss">Wiin dash Jaagaga’ang-ogakwan (and as for Hewer-of-His-Shin), odakonaan asiniin bagamaagan (he held a war-club of stone).</span>

50) > **Wiin dash Jaagaga’ang-ogakwan, odakonaan asiniin bagamaagan.**

> And as for Hewer-of-his-shin, he held in his hand a war-club of stone.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash zhigwa ganoonidiwaad (and now they addressed each other), “Ahaaw dash\! (ready, then\!)”</span>

51) > **Mii dash zhigwa ganoonidiwaad, “Ahaaw dash\!”**

> Thereupon they now addressed each other, saying: “Ready\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Geget Nenaboozhoo gaye wiin obimwaan (truly Nänabushu shot at him), gaye wiin dash Jaagaga’ang-ogakwan bakitewaan obagamaagan (and in turn Hewer-of-His-Shin struck him with his war-club).</span>

52) > **Geget Nenaboozhoo gaye wiin obimwaan, gaye wiin dash Jaagaga’ang-ogakwan bakitewaan obagamaagan.**

> And truly Nänabushu then shot at him, and then in turn Hewer-of-his-shin struck him with his war-club.

Mii dash geget gichi-ojaanimii’idiwaad (and so truly they kept each other exceedingly hard pressed).
