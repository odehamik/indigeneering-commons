(51) Nanabushu and the Moose-Head

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and so) gii-ani-maajaad (he set out on his way).</span>

1)  > **Mii dash gii-ani-maajaad.**

> Thereupon he started on his way.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash (and then) gii-nagishkawaad ininiwan (he met a man), aapiji onizhishiwan (very handsome was he).</span>

2)  > **Mii dash gii-nagishkawaad ininiwan, aapiji onizhishiwan.**

> And then he met with a man, very handsome was he.

<span class="l-gloss">Aa (ah), miigwana’ (the feathers) oshtigwaaning (upon his head)\!</span>

3)  > **Aa, miigwana’ oshtigwaaning\!**

> Ah, the feathers upon his head\!

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoo oganoonaan (Nanabushu spoke to him): “Aa (well), niijii (my friend), aaniindi ezhaayan (where are you going)?”</span>

4)  > **Nenaboozhoo oganoonaan: “Aa, niijii, aaniindi ezhaayan?”**

> Nanabushu spoke to him, saying: "Well, my friend, whither are you bound?"

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa (oh), anishaa sa go (just for nothing) nimbabaamaadis (I am travelling about); giin idash (and you), aaniindi ezhaayan (where are you going)?” (1)</span>

5)  > **“Aa, anishaa sa go nimbabaamaadis; giin idash, aaniindi ezhaayan?” ([<span class="underline">1</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabush-and-the-moose-head-footnotes.html))**

> "Oh, I am just simply travelling about; and you, where are you going?"

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaa (oh), anishaa go naa (just simply) gaye niin (I too) nimbabaamaadis (am wandering about).”</span>

6)  > **“Gaa, anishaa go naa gaye niin nimbabaamaadis.”**

> "Oh, I too am simply wandering aimlessly about."

<span class="l-gloss">Mitigwaabiin odakonaan (a bow he was holding) a’aw inini (that man), Nenaboozhoo dash oganoonaan (and Nanabushu spoke to him): “Daa (why), niijii (my friend), geget sa onizhishi (truly handsome it is) gi-mitigwaab (your bow).</span>

7)  > **Mitigwaabiin odakonaan a’aw inini, Nenaboozhoo dash oganoonaan: “Daa, niijii geget sa onizhishi gi-mitigwaab.**

> A bow the man held in his hand, and Nanabushu addressed him, saying: "Why, my friend, truly handsome is your bow.

<span class="l-gloss">Shkomaa biish (just hand it over), ninga-gagwedaagibiinaa (I will test its pull).”</span>

8)  > **Shkomaa biish, ninga-gagwedaagibiinaa.”**

> Just you hand it over to me, I want to see how it pulls."

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa (ah), gaawiin (no)\! Gaa wiikaa (never) awiya nindawi’aasii (do I hand it to anyone).”</span>

9)  > **“Aa, gaawiin\! Gaa wiikaa awiya nindawi’aasii.”**

> "Ah, no\! never do I turn it over to any one."

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa (oh), maanoo (please), niijii (my friend), gaa na gegoo ajina (just for a little while)\!”</span>

10) > **“Aa, maanoo, niijii, gaa na gegoo ajina\!”**

> "Oh, please, my friend, just only for a moment\!"

<span class="l-gloss">Ginwenzh (a long while) ogii-dashimaan (he coaxed him).</span>

11) > **Ginwenzh ogii-dashimaan.**

> A long while, he coaxed him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gegapii (at last) ogii-miinigoon iniw mitigwaabiin (he was given the bow), Nenaboozhoo dash ogagwedaagibiinaan mitigwaabiin (and Nanabushu tested the pull of the bow).</span>

12) > **Gegapii ogii-miinigoon iniw mitigwaabiin, Nenaboozhoo dash ogagwedaagibiinaan mitigwaabiin.**

> At last he was given the bow, and Nanabushu tested the spring of the bow.

<span class="l-gloss">“Taa (why), geget sa minwaagizi (truly it pulls well).</span>

13) > **“Taa geget sa minwaagizi.**

> Why, to be sure, it pulls finely.

<span class="l-gloss">Shkomaa wiin (just) i’iwe gidasawaan (that arrow of yours).”</span>

14) > **Shkomaa wiin i’iwe gidasawaan.”**

> Just (hand me) over that arrow of yours."

<span class="l-gloss">Ginwenzh aanawi (a long while, even so) ogii-saagitaagoon (it was withheld from him), gegapii ogii-miinigoon (at last it was given to him).</span>

15) > **Ginwenzh aanawi ogii-saagitaagoon, gegapii ogii-miinigoon.**

> With all his pleading, yet a long while was it withheld from him, but finally it was given to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii idash gaa-izhi-naabisidood (and so he fitted it in place) i’imaa ajaabiing (there on the bowstring); mii idash gagwedaagibiinaad Nenaboozhoo mitigwaabiin (and as Nanabushu drew the bow), mii iw gaa-izhi-bimwaad (then he shot him) iniw iniwan gaa-nagishkawaapan (that man whom he had met).</span>

16) > **Mii idash gaa-izhi-naabisidood i’imaa ajaabiing; mii idash gagwedaagibiinaad Nenaboozhoo mitigwaabiin, mii iw gaa-izhi-bimwaad iniw iniwan gaa-nagishkawaapan.**

> Thereupon he fitted it in place on the bowstring ; and when Nanabushu pulled upon the bow, he thereupon shot the man whom he had met.

<span class="l-gloss">Moonzong gii-izhinaagoziwan (like a moose he looked) apii gaa-nisaad (when he had killed him); aapiji wiininoowan (very fat was he).</span>

17) > **Moonzong gii-izhinaagoziwan apii gaa-nisaad; aapiji wiininoowan.**

> Like a moose he looked, after (Nanabushu) had slain him ; he was ever so fat.

<span class="l-gloss">Gichi-minwendam Nenaboozhoo (greatly pleased was Nanabushu) gichi-wiisinid (to eat a great deal).</span>

18) > **Gichi-minwendam Nenaboozhoo gichi-wiisinid.**

> Highly pleased was Nanabushu to have a great heap of food.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii idash gaa-izhi-pi-gishkizhwaad iniw moozoon (and so he cut up the moose), aapiji dash wenizhishing wiiyaas (and very fine meat) ogii-kiizhizaan waa-miijid (he cooked, what he would eat); bimide gaye (and the grease too).</span>

19) > **Mii idash gaa-izhi-pi-gishkizhwaad iniw moozoon, aapiji dash wenizhishing wiiyaas ogii-kiizhizaan waa-miijid; bimide gaye.**

> Accordingly he cut the moose up into pieces, and very nice was the meat he cooked to eat ; and the grease too (was savory).

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-giizhidenig (when it was cooked), mii dash gii-agwaa’izekwed (then he took it out of the kettle).</span>

20) > **Gaa-giizhidenig, mii dash gii-agwaa’izekwed.**

> When it was done, he accordingly took it out of the kettle.

<span class="l-gloss">Zhigwa wii-maadanjiged (just as he was about to begin eating), giziibaakwad (there was a creaking sound).</span>

21) > **Zhigwa wii-maadanjiged, giziibaakwad.**

> Just as he was on the point of eating, there was a creaking noise.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ji’iin, ji’iin,” (a vocable, carried as sounded) inwenig (so it sounded).</span>

22) > **“Ji’iin, ji’iin,” inwenig.**

> "Tci?n, tci?n\!" was the way it sounded.

Gaawiin ominotanziin (he did not like the sound of it).

23) > **Gaawiin ominotanziin.**

> He did not like the sound.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ishte (now then), bizaan daga (do be still)\!”</span>

24) > **“Ishte, bizaan daga\!”**

> "Now, do you keep silent \!"

<span class="l-gloss">Eshkam igo gizhiiwe (louder and louder it sounded) i’iw giziibaakwad (that creaking).</span>

25) > **Eshkam igo gizhiiwe i’iw giziibaakwad.**

> Still louder grew the noise of the creaking.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoodazh gii-pasigwii (and Nanabushu rose to his feet), ogii-manizhaan bangii ozhoobiin (he sliced off a little of the tenderloin).</span>

26) > **Nenaboozhoodazh gii-pasigwii, ogii-manizhaan bangii ozhoobiin.**

> Nanabushu rose to his feet, sliced off a little bit of (fatty) tenderloin.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash edang i’iw giziibaakwad (and so he said to the creaking): “Geget sa gidoombiigis (truly you are making too much noise).</span>

27) > **Mii dash edang i’iw giziibaakwad: “Geget sa gidoombiigis.**

> And then he said to the creaking noise : "Really, too much of a noise are you making.

<span class="l-gloss">Gii-wanishkwem (you are disturbing me) wii-wiisiniyaan (when I want to eat).</span>

28) > **Gii-wanishkwem wii-wiisiniyaan.**

> You are annoying me when I want to eat.

<span class="l-gloss">Owe gaye giin (this too, you) miijin (eat).”</span>

29) > **Owe gaye giin miijin.”**

> This too do you eat."

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo atood i’iw bimide (and when Nanabushu put the fat) giziibaakwad ogii-dakwamigon (into the creaking place, it bit and held him).</span>

30) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo atood i’iw bimide giziibaakwad ogii-dakwamigon.**

> And when Nanabushu placed the fat in where the creaking noise was made, he was caught fast.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii imaa gii-agoojing gichi-ginwenzh (and so there he hung a very long while), biinish anooj awesiiyag (until all sorts of animals) – ma’iinganag (wolves); gwiingwa’aageg (wolverines/martens), ojiigag (fishers), waagoshag (foxes) – gii-dagwishinoog gii-amwaawaad moozoon (arrived and ate the moose); gaagaagiwag gaye (ravens too).</span>

31) > **Mii imaa gii-agoojing gichi-ginwenzh, biinish anooj awesiiyag – ma’iinganag; gwiingwa’aageg, ojiigag, waagoshag – gii-dagwishinoog gii-amwaawaad moozoon; gaagaagiwag gaye.**

> Accordingly there he hung for a great while, until all sorts of game-folk – wolves, martens, fishers, foxes – arrived to eat the moose; ravens too (arrived).

<span class="l-gloss">Baanimaa gakina gaa-gidamond a’aw mooz (not until the whole moose was eaten up) mii iw bijiinag (only then), gii-pagidamigod Nenaboozhoo i’iw giziibaakwad (was Nanabushu released by the creaking place).</span>

32) > **Baanimaa gakina gaa-gidamond a’aw mooz mii iw bijiinag, gii-pagidamigod Nenaboozhoo i’iw giziibaakwad.**

> Not till the whole of the moose was eaten up, was Nanabushu freed from the grip of the creaking place.

<span class="l-gloss">Aapiji wii-wiisini (very eager to eat was he) gii-izhaa i’imaa abinipan iniw moozoon (he went there where the moose had been).</span>

33) > **Aapiji wii-wiisini gii-izhaa i’imaa abinipan iniw moozoon.**

> Very eager was he to eat, and he went over to where the moose had been.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii eta okanan etenig (only its bones were there), oshtigwaanigegan gaye ateni (and its skull lay there too).</span>

34) > **Mii eta okanan etenig, oshtigwaanigegan gaye ateni.**

> Only its bones were left, its skull was there too.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash ezhi-waabamaad wawaabiganoojiiya’ (then he saw mice) biindigenid imaan mooz oshtigwaaning (going into there, the moose’s head).</span>

35) > **Mii dash ezhi-waabamaad wawaabiganoojiiya’ biindigenid imaan mooz oshtigwaaning.**

> Thereupon he saw some mice ([<span class="underline">2</span>](http://ojibweproject.weebly.com/nanabush-and-the-moose-head-footnotes.html)) go into the head of the moose.

<span class="l-gloss">Owaabandaan bangii atenig (he saw that a little was left) wiinindib (of the brains).</span>

36) > **Owaabandaan bangii atenig wiinindib.**

> He saw that a little bit of the brains was left.

<span class="l-gloss">“Apegish miijiyaan (would that I might eat it)\!” inendam (he thought) mii dash ezhi-ganoonaad waawaabiganoojiiya’ (and so he spoke to the mice): “Gidaa-gashkitoonaawaa na (could you bring it about) gaye niin i’iw ji-iniginiyaan (that I too might be the size) eyaniginiyeg (that you are)?”</span>

37) > **“Apegish miijiyaan\!” inendam mii dash ezhi-ganoonaad waawaabiganoojiiya’: “Gidaa-gashkitoonaawaa na gaye niin i’iw ji-iniginiyaan eyaniginiyeg?”**

> "Would that I might eat it \!" he thought. Thereupon he spoke to the mice, saying : " Could you bring it about so that I might be of the same size as you ?"

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa (ah), gaawiin (no)\!” odigoon (he was told).</span>

38) > **“Aa, gaawiin\!” odigoon.**

> "Ah, no\!" he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ozaam gi-mindid (you are too big),” odigoon (he was told).</span>

39) > **“Ozaam gi-mindid,” odigoon.**

> "Of too large a size are you," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aa (ah), maanoo (please) gaye niin niwii-piindige (I too want to go in) imaa mooz oshtigwaaning (there into the moose’s head)\!”</span>

40) > **“Aa, maanoo gaye niin niwii-piindige imaa mooz oshtigwaaning\!”**

> "Ah, please let me go into the head of the moose too\!"

<span class="l-gloss">“Ahaaw isa (all right then),” odigoon (he was told).</span>

41) > **“Ahaaw isa,” odigoon.**

> "All right, then," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Omaa eta gi-shtigwaaning (only here at your head) giga-dagaashiiniy (you will be made small).”</span>

42) > **“Omaa eta gi-shtigwaaning giga-dagaashiiniy”**

> "Only here at your head will you be made small."

Mii dash waawaabiganoojiiying gii-inikogo ishtigwaaned (and so to the size of a mouse his head became).

43) > **Mii dash waawaabiganoojiiying gii-inikogo ishtigwaaned.**

> Thereupon like unto the head of a mouse was the size of his head.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gii-piindikwenid (and so when he poked his head in), “Bekaa go wiisinin (slowly eat),” odigoon (he was told).</span>

44) > **Mii dash gii-piindikwenid, “Bekaa go wiisinin,” odigoon.**

> And so when he stuck his head in, "Slowly do you eat," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gego ombikweniken (do not raise your head),” odigoon (he was told).</span>

45) > **“Gego ombikweniken,” odigoon.**

> "Do not lift your head," he was told.

<span class="l-gloss">Ozaam idash Nenaboozhoo gii-ombikweni (but too much Nanabushu raised his head), mii dash gaa-izhi-michaanig oshtigwaan (and so his head grew large).</span>

46) > **Ozaam idash Nenaboozhoo gii-ombikweni, mii dash gaa-izhi-michaanig oshtigwaan.**

> Now, too high Nanabushu lifted his head, whereupon the size of his head enlarged.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin gii-gashki’osii (he was not able) ji-giichigotaad (to pull himself free).</span>

47) > **Gaawiin gii-gashki’osii ji-giichigotaad.**

> He was unable to get (his head) free.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-maajaad (and so he set off) dibi e-zhaagwenh (not knowing where he was going), gaawiin owaabandanziin e-zhaad (he did not see where he was going).</span>

48) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-maajaad dibi e-zhaagwenh, gaawiin owaabandanziin e-zhaad.**

> Thereupon he started off, not knowing whither he was going ; he did not see whither he was bound.

<span class="l-gloss">Gakina mitigoon betaakoshing (every tree he bumped into) ogagwejimaan (he asked it): “Awenen giin (who are you)?”</span>

49) > **Gakina mitigoon betaakoshing ogagwejimaan: “Awenen giin?”**

> Every tree he bumped against he asked of it : " Who are you ?"

<span class="l-gloss">Mina’ig naaningodinoo (a tamarack sometimes); wiigwaas naaniingodinoo (a birch sometimes); azaadiwan ningoding (a poplar once).</span>

50) > **Mina’ig naaningodinoo; wiigwaas naaniingodinoo; azaadiwan ningoding.**

> A tamarack (it was) sometimes ; a birch (it was) sometimes ; a poplar once.

<span class="l-gloss">Miinawaa mitigoon obitaakoshkawaan (again a tree he bumped into), “Awenen giin (who are you)?” odinaan (he said to it).</span>

51) > **Miinawaa mitigoon obitaakoshkawaan, “Awenen giin?” odinaan.**

> Another tree he bumped against. "Who are you?" he said to it.

Giizhik sa (a cedar).

52) > **Giizhik sa.**

> A cedar (it was).

<span class="l-gloss">“Mii sa besho jiigibiig (then near the water’s edge) indayaamidog (I must be),” inendam (he thought).</span>

53) > **“Mii sa besho jiigibiig indayaamidog,” inendam.**

> "Then close to the edge of the water I must be," he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Wiipago geget (soon indeed) nibi odakogaadaan (he stepped his foot into the water).</span>

54) > **Wiipago geget nibi odakogaadaan.**

> Soon then really into the water he stepped.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash igo gwayak gaa-ani-izhi-niiminaasiid (and so when he went straight in), gii-ani-bimaadage gwayak igo e-zhaad (he swam straight along the way he was going).</span>

55) > **Mii dash igo gwayak gaa-ani-izhi-niiminaasiid, gii-ani-bimaadage gwayak igo e-zhaad.**

> Thereupon, when straight into the water he went, off he went swimming towards the way he was bound.

<span class="l-gloss">Awiya onoondawaan (some people he heard) biipaaginid anishinaaben (the people calling out): “E’ii (hey), nashke gosha (just look), mooz bemaadaged (a moose swimming by)\! Aaw (come on)\! Mawinanaadaa (let us go after him)\!”</span>

56) > **Awiya onoondawaan biipaaginid anishinaaben: “E’ii, nashke gosha, mooz bemaadaged\! Aaw\! Mawinanaadaa\!”**

> Some people he heard calling aloud : " Hey \! Just look \! a moose is swimming by \! Come on \! Let us go for him \!"

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash geget ezhi-pooziwad anishinaabeg (and so truly the people got into their canoes) wii-nisaawaad moozoon (wanting to kill the moose).</span>

57) > **Mii dash geget ezhi-pooziwad anishinaabeg wii-nisaawaad moozoon.**

> Thereupon truly into their canoes went the people, in the hope of killing the moose.

Nenaboozhoo dash gii-washkibagizo (and Nanabushu turned about quickly).

58) > **Nenaboozhoo dash gii-washkibagizo.**

> Nanabushu then quickly turned about.

<span class="l-gloss">“E’ii (hey)\! Aazha washkaadage (now he is swimming back)\!”</span>

59) > **“E’ii\! Aazha washkaadage\!”**

> "Hey\! He is turning back\!"

Nenaboozhoo enigok bimaadage (Nanabushu was swimming hard).

60) > **Nenaboozhoo enigok bimaadage.**

> Nanabushu was swimming fast.

Anishinaabeg zhigwa besho bi-ayaawag (the people now were getting close).

61) > **Anishinaabeg zhigwa besho bi-ayaawag.**

> The people now were coming close.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash Nenaboozhoo zhigwa debakiinang (and when Nanabushu touched bottom), mii dash ezhi-maajiibatood bagwana (then off he ran blindly), gaawiin owaabandanziin e-patood (he did not see where he was running).</span>

62) > **Mii dash Nenaboozhoo zhigwa debakiinang, mii dash ezhi-maajiibatood bagwana, gaawiin owaabandanziin e-patood.**

> And when Nanabushu touched bottom, then off he started running without knowing where ; he did not see whither he was running.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gii-ozhashaabikishing (and then he slipped and fell), mii idash gii-paasesing i’iw moozoo-shtigwaanish (and so that wretched moose-head cracked open).</span>

63) > **Mii dash gii-ozhashaabikishing, mii idash gii-paasesing i’iw moozoo-shtigwaanish.**

> And then he stumbled and fell, where upon he cracked that wretched head of the moose.

Mii idash bijiinag gii-waabid (and only then could he see).

64) > **Mii idash bijiinag gii-waabid.**

> And that was when he could see.

Mii iw gii-ani-maajiibatood (and so off he started running).

65) > **Mii iw gii-ani-maajiibatood.**

> Accordingly away he started running.

<span class="l-gloss">Nenaboozhoon wiin gaa-izhinaagozinid (Nanabushu himself looked as he was again); mii apane dibi e-patoogwen Nenaboozhoo (and on he ran, who knows where, that Nanabushu).</span>

66) > **Nenaboozhoon wiin gaa-izhinaagozinid; mii apane dibi e-patoogwen Nenaboozhoo.**

> Nanabushu then took on his own form ; and off he ran, no one knew where.

Mii sa ekoozid (and that is the end).

67) > **Mii sa ekoozid.**

> And that is the end of (the story).
