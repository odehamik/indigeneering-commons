(2) Gii-gimoodid ishkode Nenabosho

Nenabosho Steals Fire

1)  > **Aaniish mii sa iw zhigwa gii-nitaawigi’aad a’aw mindimooyenh.** So accordingly then did the old woman bring him up.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa iw zhigwa (well, and so by this time) gii-nitaawigi’aad (she had raised him) a’aw mindimooyenh (that old woman).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa iw zhayiigwa (and so by and by) ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (he said to his grandmother): “Gaawiin ina ningoji anishinaabeg (is there nowhere any people) gi-kikenimaasiig (that you know of) ji-ayaawaad? (who are about?)”</span>

2)  > **Mii sa iw zhayiigwa<sup>i</sup> ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan: “Gaawiin ina ningoji anishinaabeg gi-kikenimaasiig ji-ayaawaad?”**

And so by and by he said to his grandmother: “Don't you know of a place where there are some people?”

3)  > **“Eye,” ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan. “O’owidi agaami-kichigami ayaawag igiw anishinaabeg.”**

“Yes,” he was told by his grandmother. “In yonder direction on the farther shore of the sea are some people.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Eye,” (“yes,”) ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother). “O’owidi agaami-kichigami (over yonder, on the far shore of the great sea) ayaawag igiw anishinaabeg (there are people).”</span>

4)  > **“Gonage gaa odayaasiinaawaadog i’iw ishkode?”<sup>ii</sup>**

“I am curious to know if they do not possess fire.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Gonage (I wonder whether) gaa odayaasiinaawaadog i’iw ishkode? (they might not have any fire?)”</span>

5)  > **“Eye,” ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan; “Geget odayaanaawaa i’iw ishkode.”**

“Yes,” he was told by his grandmother; “truly, they do possess some fire.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Eye,” (“yes,”) ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother); “Geget odayaanaawaa i’iw ishkode (truly they do have fire).”</span>

6)  > **O’owidi dash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan: “Ambe sa noo, ninga-naazikaan<sup>iii</sup> i’iw ishkode,” ogii-inaan ookomisan**.

Now, this was what he said to his grandmother: “Please let me go fetch the fire,” he said to his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">O’owidi dash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (and this he said to his grandmother): “Ambe sa noo (please now), ninga-naazikaan i’iw ishkode (let me go to that fire),” ogii-inaan ookomisan (he said to his grandmother).</span>

7)  > **O’owidi dash ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan: “Gaawiin gidaa-kashkitoosiin.** And this was what he was told by his grandmother: “Not will you be able to succeed.

<span class="l-gloss">O’owidi dash ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan (and this he was told by his grandmother): “Gaawiin gidaa-kashkitoosiin (you will not be able to do it).</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Geget aapiji oganawendaanaawaa (truly very closely they guard it) i’iw isa endaawaad (there where they live).</span>

8)  > **Geget aapiji oganawendaanaawaa i’iw isa endaawaad.**

Truly, a very careful watch do they keep over it there where they dwell.

9)  > **Akiwenzii i’imaa ayaa.**

An old man at the place abides.

Akiwenzii i’imaa ayaa (an old man stays there).

10) > **Mii go apane dasing<sup>iv</sup> giizhigadinig asabiin ozhi’aad.**

And all the while, as often as the day comes round, upon a net he works.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii go apane dasing giizhigadinig (and all the time, every day that comes) asabiin ozhi’aad (he works at a net).</span>

11) > **Gaawiin wiikaa ningoji izhaasii, mii go apane imaa biindig ayaad.**

Never anywhere does he go, but always there indoors he remains.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaawiin wiikaa ningoji izhaasii (never does he go anywhere), mii go apane imaa biindig ayaad (but always he stays there inside).</span>

12) > **Niizhiwa idash i’iw odaanisa’, mii eta moozhag agwajing eyaanid.”<sup>v</sup>**

Now, two are those daughters of his, and only they are continually out of doors.”

<span class="l-gloss">Niizhiwa idash i’iw odaanisa’ (and two are his daughters), mii eta moozhag agwajing eyaanid (and only they are always out of doors).”</span>

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (and this he said to his grandmother): “Maanoo ninga-izhaa (never mind, I will go),” ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (he said to his grandmother).</span>

13) > **O’ow idash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan: “Maanoo ninga-izhaa,” ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan.**

> And this he said to his grandmother: “Nevertheless I will go," he said to his grandmother.

14) > **“Awawa,”<sup>vi</sup> ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan.**

“Very well,” he was told by his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“Awawa,” (“Awawa,”) ogii-igoon iniw ookomisan (he was told by his grandmother).</span>

15) > **Aaniish mii sa apii zhigwa gaa-ikidod: “Ambe sa noo da-gashkadin<sup>vii</sup> o’ow gichigami, wiigwaasabakwaang da-wii-apiitadin o’ow gichigami.”** Now, this was what he then said afterwards: “I will that the sea shall freeze, as thick as the birch-bark covering of the lodge so let this sea freeze.”

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa apii zhigwa gaa-ikidod (well, and so then this is what he said): “Ambe sa noo da-gashkadin o’ow gichigami (let this sea now freeze over), wiigwaasabakwaang da-wii-apiitadin o’ow gichigami (as thick as a birch-bark lodge-covering let this sea freeze).”</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa geget gaa-izhiwebadinig (and so truly it happened) iw isa gaa-ikidod (just as he had said).</span>

16) > **Mii sa geget gaa-izhiwebadinig iw isa gaa-ikidod.**

It was true that it happened according as he had said.

17) > **“O’ow idash ninga-izhinaagos,” kii-ikido; “niindawaa ninga-waaboosoonsiw.”**

“Now, this is the way I shall look,” he said; “I will that I become a hare.”

<span class="l-gloss">“O’ow idash ninga-izhinaagos (and this is how I will look),” kii-ikido (he said); “niindawaa ninga-waaboosoonsiw (I will turn into a little hare instead).”</span>

18) > **Aaniish mii sa geget gaa-izhinaagozid.**

So accordingly that truly was the way he looked.

Aaniish mii sa geget gaa-izhinaagozid (well, and so truly that was how he looked).

19) > **Mii sa zhigwa gii-maajiiyaadagaakwad.<sup>viii</sup>**

Thereupon he then started on his way over the ice.

Mii sa zhigwa gii-maajiiyaadagaakwad (and so then he set out across the ice).

20) > **Mii sa geget gaawiin gii-dwaashizii.**

It was true that he did not break through (the ice).

Mii sa geget gaawiin gii-dwaashizii (and truly he did not break through the ice).

21) > **Aaniish mii sa ogii-kikenimaan i’imaa ayaanid i’iw Anishinaabe.**

Of course it was so that he knew that at yonder place the people were abiding.

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa ogii-kikenimaan (well, and so he knew) i’imaa ayaanid i’iw Anishinaabe (that the people were there).</span>

22) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-dagwishing i’imaa wenda’ibiinid, o’ow idash gii-inendam: “Ambegish sa bi-nibi-naadid a’aw ikwe,” gii-inendam.**

And so after he was come at the place where they drew water, this then he thought: “I wish that for water some woman would come,” he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash gaa-izhi-dagwishing i’imaa wenda’ibiinid (and so when he arrived there at the watering place), o’ow idash gii-inendam (this then he thought): “Ambegish sa bi-nibi-naadid a’aw ikwe (would that a woman come to fetch water),” gii-inendam (he thought).</span>

23) > **O’ow idash gii-izhichige i’imaa waa-ogwaaba’iminid i’iw nibi,**

And this he did there where she intended to dip up water:

O’ow idash gii-izhichige i’imaa waa-ogwaaba’iminid i’iw nibi (and this he did there where she meant to dip up the water),

24) > **mii dash gaa-izhi-agwaaweba’ogod i’iw gichigami mii imaa gii-titiba’ogod mii imaa iniw kwewan ge-onji-gwaaba’ibiinid.**

that after he was washed up by the waves of the sea, then he was tossed rolling to the place from which the woman was to draw water.

<span class="l-gloss">mii dash gaa-izhi-agwaaweba’ogod i’iw gichigami (so that he was washed ashore by the sea) mii imaa gii-titiba’ogod (there he was rolled along by the waves) mii imaa iniw kwewan ge-onji-gwaaba’ibiinid (there where that woman would draw water).</span>

25) > **O’ow idash gii-ikido: “Ambe sa noo ninga-waawiiyadenimik.”**

And this he said: “I wish that she would take me for a plaything.”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-ikido (and this he said): “Ambe sa noo ninga-waawiiyadenimik (would that she take me for a pretty plaything).”</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Aaniish mii sa akawaabamaad (well, and so he lay in wait for her) ji-bi-naasibiinid (to come down for water).</span>

26) > **Aaniish mii sa akawaabamaad ji-bi-naasibiinid.<sup>ix</sup>**

So thereupon he lay in wait for her to come for water.

27) > **Goniiginiin.<sup>x</sup> Geget ogii-waabamaan biidaasamosenid.**

Lo, truly he beheld her walking hitherward.

<span class="l-gloss">Goniiginiin (lo\!). Geget ogii-waabamaan biidaasamosenid (truly he saw her walking toward him).</span>

28) > **Mii dash zhigwa imaa dagwishinoon imaa sa ayaad, zhayigwa ogii-gwaaba’aminid.**

Thereupon soon was she come at the place where he was, at once she dipped out the water.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash zhigwa imaa dagwishinoon imaa sa ayaad (and so then she arrived there where he was), zhayigwa ogii-gwaaba’aminid (and at once she dipped up the water).</span>

29) > **Gaa-izhi-miikwaawaabamigod<sup>xi</sup> mii sa gaa-pi-izhi-debibinigod**<sup>1</sup>

As soon as he was discovered, forthwith was he seized upon.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-miikwaawaabamigod (as soon as she caught sight of him) mii sa gaa-pi-izhi-debibinigod (then he was seized).</span>

30) > **Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-siikoobiiginigod<sup>xii</sup> gaa-izhi-kiiwewinigod o’owidi gaa-izhi piindoomowind<sup>xiii</sup> iw wiiyaw**.<sup>2</sup>

And after the water had been rubbed from him, then was he taken over there to her home; in the bosom of her garment she put the creature.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-siikoobiiginigod (and so then the water was wrung out of him) gaa-izhi-kiiwewinigod (then he was carried home) o’owidi gaa-izhi-piindoomowind iw wiiyaw (over there his body was tucked into the bosom of her garment).</span>

31) > **Mii sa gaa-izhi-piindiganigod geget ogii-waabamaan akiwenziiyan namadabinid.**

And after he had been carried inside, truly he saw an old man that was seated (there).

<span class="l-gloss">Mii sa gaa-izhi-piindiganigod (and so when he had been carried inside) geget ogii-waabamaan akiwenziiyan namadabinid (truly he saw an old man sitting there).</span>

32) > **Geget asabikewan.**

Sure enough, he was at work making a net.

Geget asabikewan (sure enough, he was making a net).

33) > **O’ow idash ogii-inaan a’aw ikwe iniw omisenyan: “Nashke giniin,” ogii-inaan giimooch iniw omisenyan.**

And this said the woman to her elder sister: “I say,” said she in secret to her elder sister,

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ogii-inaan a’aw ikwe iniw omisenyan (and this the woman said to her elder sister): “Nashke giniin (look here),” ogii-inaan giimooch iniw omisenyan (she said quietly to her elder sister).</span>

34) > **“Nashke giniin wa’aw gaa-mikawag, waaboozoons.**

“see this creature that I have found, a little bunny\!

<span class="l-gloss">“Nashke giniin wa’aw gaa-mikawag (look at this one I found), waaboozoons (a little hare).</span>

35) > **Ambe eta waawiiyade\[t\]aagozi<sup>xiv</sup> wa’aw waaboozoons\!**

Oh, such a cunning thing is this dear little bunny\!

Ambe eta waawiiyadetaagozi wa’aw waaboozoons\! (oh how cunning this little hare looks\!)

36) > **Ambe sa noo geyegiin waawiiya\[d\]enim<sup>xv</sup> ni-misenh wa’aw isa waaboozoons.”**

I wish you would also think it cunning, elder sister, this little bunny.”

<span class="l-gloss">Ambe sa noo geyegiin waawiiyadenim ni-misenh (you too think it cute, my elder sister) wa’aw isa waaboozoons (this little hare).”</span>

37) > **O’ow idash ogii-igoon iniw omisenyan: “Giga-ayaawigonaan koosinaan giga onsomigonaan,<sup>xvi</sup>” ogii-igoon inow misenyan;**

Now, this was she told by her elder sister: “We shall be scolded by our father, on account of it shall we be taken to task,” she was told by her elder sister;

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ogii-igoon iniw omisenyan (and this she was told by her elder sister): “Giga-ayaawigonaan koosinaan (our father will scold us for it) giga-onsomigonaan (we will be taken to task over it),” ogii-igoon inow misenyan (she was told by her elder sister);</span>

38) > **giimooj idash ogii-igoon iniw omisenyan.**

and in secret she was told by her elder sister.

giimooj idash ogii-igoon iniw omisenyan (and in secret she was told by her elder sister).

39) > **Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-nandobagwid<sup>xvii</sup> gaa-izhi-pagidinigod i’imaa jiigi-ishkode wii pengwaawesagod.**

Accordingly, after she had searched in the bosom of her garment, then was he placed there beside the fire, that from the heat his hair might become dry.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-nandobagwid (and so then she searched in the bosom of her garment) gaa-izhi-pagidinigod i’imaa jiigi-ishkode (and set him down there beside the fire) wii-pengwaawesagod (so that he might dry from the heat).</span>

40) > **Mii dash i’iw baapi’aad igiw ikwewag wawiiya\[n\]enimaawaad iniw waabozoonsan.**

Thereupon laughed the women as they made a pet of the little bunny.

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash i’iw baapi’aad igiw ikwewag (and so the women laughed at him) wawiiyadenimaawaad iniw waabozoonsan (as they made a pet of the little hare).</span>

41) > **Ezhi-kikenimigowaad iniw oosiwaan. “Gidoombiigizim,<sup>xviii</sup>” ogii-igoowaan iniw oosiwaan.**

Then they were found out by their father. “You are noisy,” they were told by their father.

<span class="l-gloss">Ezhi-kikenimigowaad iniw oosiwaan (then they were found out by their father). “Gidoombiigizim,” (“you are noisy,”) ogii-igoowaan iniw oosiwaan (they were told by their father).</span>

42) > **O’ow idash ogii-inaawaan iniw oosiwaan: “Nashke giniin,” ogii-inaawaan.**

And this they said to their father: “See this,” they said to him,

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ogii-inaawaan iniw oosiwaan (and this they said to their father): “Nashke giniin (look here),” ogii-inaawaan (they said to him).</span>

43) > **“Nashke giniin wa’aw waaboozoons.”**

“see this little bunny\!”

“Nashke giniin wa’aw waaboozoons (look at this little hare).”

44) > **“she\!” ogii-igowaan iniw oosiwaan**.

“Beware\!” they were told by their father.

<span class="l-gloss">“she\!” (“she\!”) ogii-igowaan iniw oosiwaan (they were told by their father).</span>

45) > **“Gaawiin ina gigii-noondaziim igiw manidoog i’iw gii-niigiwaad?**

“Have you not heard of the manitous how they were born?

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaawiin ina gigii-noondaziim (have you not heard) igiw manidoog i’iw gii-niigiwaad? (of the manidoog, how they were born?)</span>

46) > **Gaa na mii aw bezhig aawisiidog. Awi-asig,”<sup>xix</sup> ogii-igowaan iniw oosiwaan.**

Perhaps this might be one of them. Go put it where you got it,” they were told by their father.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa na mii aw bezhig aawisiidog (perhaps this might be one of them). Awi-asig,” (go put it back,”) ogii-igowaan iniw oosiwaan (they were told by their father).</span>

47) > **“Geget sa gii-gagiibaadizim i’iw isa gii-odaapineg.”**

“Truly, indeed, were you foolish to take it.”

<span class="l-gloss">“Geget sa gii-gagiibaadizim (truly you were foolish) i’iw isa gii-odaapineg (to pick it up).”</span>

48) > **O’ow idash gii-ikido aw ikwe: “Etata gosha ni-wawiyadenimaa<sup>xx</sup> a’aw isa waaboozoons.”**

And this said the woman: “Such a precious pet do I think this little bunny\!”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-ikido aw ikwe (and this the woman said): “Etata gosha ni-wawiyadenimaa a’aw isa waaboozoons (oh how I treasure this little hare as a pet).”</span>

49) > **O’ow idash ogii-inaan iniw oosan: “Aaniin gegii-izhinaagwak<sup>3</sup> i’iw is age waaboozoonsiwid a’aw manidoo?” ogii-inaan iniw oosan.**

And this she said to her father: “How is it possible for a manitou to be a little bunny?” she said to her father.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ogii-inaan iniw oosan (and this she said to her father): “Aaniin gegii-izhinaagwak (how could it be possible) i’iw is age waaboozoonsiwid a’aw manidoo? (that a manidoo would be a little hare?)” ogii-inaan iniw oosan (she said to her father).</span>

50) > **O’ow idash gii-ikido: “Geget sa gaawiin gi-nitaa-noondazii.**

And this he said: “Truly, indeed, you are not heedful of what is told you.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-ikido (and this he said): “Geget sa gaawiin gi-nitaa-noondazii (truly you do not heed what you are told).</span>

Gaawiin ina gi-waabamisii epiichinaagoziyaan? (do you not see me, how aged I appear?)”

51) > **Gaawiin ina gi-waabamisii epiichinaagoziyaan?”<sup>xxi</sup>**

Do you not behold me, how far in years I am?”

52) > **O’ow idash gii-izhichige a’aw ikwe nawanj igo ogii-abizwaan<sup>xxii</sup> iniw waaboozoonsan;**

Now, this the woman did: in spite of what she was told, she exposed the little bunny to the heat of the fire;

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-izhichige a’aw ikwe (and this the woman did) nawanj igo ogii-abizwaan iniw waaboozoonsan (all the more she warmed the little hare at the fire);</span>

53) > **i’iw isa wii-pengwaaweswaad gaa-izhi-kwekwekishimaad<sup>xxiii</sup> imaa jiigishkode.**

that she might dry its hair, she turned it over with its other side near the fire.

<span class="l-gloss">i’iw isa wii-pengwaaweswaad (so as to dry its hair by the heat) gaa-izhi-kwekwekishimaad imaa jiigishkode (she turned it over and over there beside the fire).</span>

54) > **O’ow idash gii-inendam a’aw Nenabosho: “Mii maawiin iw zhigwa bengwaawesowaanen.”**

And this thought Nenabushu: “By this time surely must I be dry from the heat.”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-inendam a’aw Nenabosho (and this Nenabosho thought): “Mii maawiin iw zhigwa bengwaawesowaanen (by now surely I must be dry from the heat).”</span>

55) > **Aaniish obaapi’aawaan igiw ikwewag.**

Yet at him laughed the women.

Aaniish obaapi’aawaan igiw ikwewag (still the women laughed at him).

56) > **O’ow idash gii-inendam: “Ambe sa noo ninga-pakines.”<sup>xxiv</sup>**

And this he thought: “I wish a spark would fall upon me.”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-inendam (and this he thought): “Ambe sa noo ninga-pakines (would that a spark fall on me).”</span>

57) > **Geget ezhi-pakinezod.**

Sure enough, a spark fell upon him.

Geget ezhi-pakinezod (sure enough, a spark fell on him).

58) > **Gaa-izhinawadizod gaa-izhi-gichikwaashkwaanid.<sup>xxv</sup>**

After he was set on fire, then out of doors he leaped.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhinawadizod (after he caught fire) gaa-izhi-gichikwaashkwaanid (then he leaped out of doors with a great bound).</span>

59) > **O’ow idash gii-ikidowag igiw ikwewag, “Nashke giniin, ozaagijibadwaadaan<sup>xxvi</sup> i’iw ishkode\!” odinaawaan iniw oosiwaan.**

And this said the women: “Look at him, out of doors is he running with the fire\!” they said to their father.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-ikidowag igiw ikwewag (and this the women said), “Nashke giniin (look at him), ozaagijibadwaadaan i’iw ishkode\! (he is running outside with the fire\!)” odinaawaan iniw oosiwaan (they said to their father).</span>

60) > **“Ayii\!” gii-ikido a’aw akiwenzii;**

“Too bad\!” said the old man.

<span class="l-gloss">“Ayii\!” (“Ayii\!”) gii-ikido a’aw akiwenzii (said the old man);</span>

61) > **“geget sa gaawiin gi-nitaa-noondaziim gegoo aano-igoyegin.**

“Truly, indeed, are you unheedful of what is told you.

<span class="l-gloss">“geget sa gaawiin gi-nitaa-noondaziim (truly you do not heed) gegoo aano-igoyegin (anything you are told).</span>

62) > **Kaa na mii a’aw bezhig manidoo<sup>4</sup> bamakaminang<sup>xxvii</sup> iw gidishkodeminaan.”**

Doubtless it is one of the manitous that has come to take away this fire of ours.”

<span class="l-gloss">Kaa na mii a’aw bezhig manidoo (is this not one manidoo) bamakaminang iw gidishkodeminaan? (that has come carrying off this fire of ours?)”</span>

63) > **Gaa-izhi-pazigonjised a’aw akiwenzii aanoo-giimawinadang<sup>xxviii</sup> i’iw ojiimaan.**

Then, leaping to his feet, the old man ran to his canoe; but it was of no avail.

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-pazigonjised a’aw akiwenzii (then the old man sprang to his feet) aanoo-giimawinadang i’iw ojiimaan (and rushed in vain for his canoe).</span>

64) > **Aano-gii-izhi-madabiiwebinang, mii dash gaa-izhi-mikwamiikaanig.<sup>xxix</sup>**

He flung his canoe into the water; but it was of no use, for it was frozen with ice.

<span class="l-gloss">Aano-gii-izhi-madabiiwebinang (he flung it down toward the water in vain), mii dash gaa-izhi-mikwamiikaanig (for it was locked in ice).</span>

65) > **Mii dash anishaa go gaa-izhi-ganawaabamaawaad mishawi-gichigami ani ozhaawashkwaakonenid,**

And all they could do was to watch (the hare) far out on the great expanse burning with a blue flame,

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash anishaa go gaa-izhi-ganawaabamaawaad (and so all they could do was watch him) mishawi-gichigami ani-ozhaawashkwaakonenid (far out on the great sea, glowing with a blue flame),</span>

66) > **mii dash biinish gaa-izhi-pishkwaabamaawaad.<sup>xxx</sup>**

(watching him) till they beheld him pass out of sight.

mii dash biinish gaa-izhi-pishkwaabamaawaad (until at last they lost sight of him).

67) > **Aaniish gii-gwiinawi-izhichigewag.<sup>xxxi</sup>**

Of course they were helpless to do anything.

Aaniish gii-gwiinawi-izhichigewag (well, they were helpless to do anything).

68) > **Mii dash zhigwa odebaabandaan endaawaad wo’ow idash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan jibwaa-maajaad:**

And then presently he was coming in sight of his home. Now, this he had said to his grandmother before he started away:

<span class="l-gloss">Mii dash zhigwa odebaabandaan endaawaad (and so then he came in sight of their home) wo’ow idash ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan jibwaa-maajaad (and this he had said to his grandmother before he set out):</span>

69) > **“ashwiin, maagizhaa geget biidooyaan i’iw ishkode,” ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan.**

“Be prepared if perchance I truly happen to fetch the fire,” he (thus) had said to his grandmother.

<span class="l-gloss">“ashwiin (be ready), maagizhaa geget biidooyaan i’iw ishkode (in case I truly bring the fire),” ogii-inaan iniw ookomisan (he said to his grandmother).</span>

70) > **Gaa-izhi-kanoonaad apii debaabandang i’iw endaawaad, o’ow ogii-inaan abi baandigesed iniw ookomisan:**

Afterwards he addressed her when he was coming in sight of their home, and this he said to his grandmother when he came flying in:

<span class="l-gloss">Gaa-izhi-kanoonaad apii debaabandang i’iw endaawaad (then he called to her as he came in sight of their home), o’ow ogii-inaan abi-baandigesed iniw ookomisan (and this he said to his grandmother as he came flying in):</span>

71) > **“aatawebishin ninjaagis, nookoo\!”**

“Rub the fire off from me, I am burning up, my grandmother\!”

“aatawebishin ninjaagis, nookoo\! (rub the fire off me, I am burning up, my grandmother\!)”

72) > **Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-aatawebinaad a’aw mindimoyenh**.

Whereupon truly off from him did the old woman rub the fire.

Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-aatawebinaad a’aw mindimoyenh (and so truly the old woman rubbed the fire off him).

73) > **Mii dash i’iw imaa gaa-ondinamowaad i’iw ishkode.**

Therefore such was how they there came into possession of fire.

Mii dash i’iw imaa gaa-ondinamowaad i’iw ishkode (and so that is how they came by the fire there).

74) > **O’ow idash gii-ikido a’aw Nenabosho: “Mii sa iw gaa-izhinaagozid a’aw waabooz i’iw ji-niibing.”**<sup>5</sup>

And this said Nenabushu: “Therefore such shall be the look of the hare in the summer time.”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-ikido a’aw Nenabosho (and this Nenabosho said): “Mii sa iw gaa-izhinaagozid a’aw waabooz i’iw ji-niibing (such shall be the look of the hare in the summer time).”</span>
