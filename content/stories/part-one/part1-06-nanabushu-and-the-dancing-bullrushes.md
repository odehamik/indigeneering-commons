<span class="l-orig">(6) Nänabushu and the Dancing Bullrushes</span>

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding babimosed (once while he was walking along), “Aaniin?” gii-inendam (“What (is it)?” he thought).</span>

1)  **Ningoding babimosed, “Aaniin?” Gii-inendam.**

While he was once walking along, “What (is it)?” he thought.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gonige?” (“Wonder (what it can be)?”)</span>

2)  **“Gonige?”**

> “Wonder (what it can be)?”

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash gii-inendam (and this he thought): “Gonige gaawin ninga-gashkitoosiin\!” (“I wonder if I am unable to do it\!”)</span>

3)  **O’ow idash gii-inendam: “Gonige gaawin ninga-gashkitoosiin\!”**

Now, this he thought: “I wonder if I am unable to do it\!”

<span class="l-gloss">Ningoding igo babimosed (and once as he was walking along), ogii-waabamaa i’iw ininiwa’ (he saw those men) Gisji-oSaamiininit (gathered in a throng) wii-niimi’idwa’ (who were going to dance together).</span>

4)  **Ningoding igo babimosed, ogii-waabamaa i’iw ininiwa’ Gisji-oSaamiininit wii-niimi’idwa’**

And as he was once walking along, he saw some men fathered in a throng to dance together.

<span class="l-gloss">O’ow idash ogii-igoon iniw bezhig (and this he was asked by one of them): “Aaniin gaye giin ina, Nenaboozhoo (“What about you too, Nanabushu), giga-niim?” (are you going to dance?”) ogii-goon (he was asked).</span>

5)  **O’ow idash ogii-igoon iniw bezhig: “Aaniin gaye giin ina, Nenaboozhoo, Giga-niim?” ogii-goon.**

> And this he was asked by one of them: “You too, Nanabushu, are you going to dance?” he was asked.

<span class="l-gloss">“Aye´, nishiime sa ninga-niim gaye niin (“Yes, my little brother, I too am going to dance),” ogii-inaan (he said to him).</span>

6)  **“Aye´, nishiime sa ninga-niim gaye niin,” ogii-inaan.**

“Yes, my little brother, I too am going to dance,” he said to him.

<span class="l-gloss">Gakina bingwaashaagidiwa’ (they were all naked), mii dash gakina ezhi’onid i’iw (and the only dress they all wore was) babikwaangenaa batakibine’onid (a feather stripped to the top, standing perched upon their heads).</span>

7)  **Gakina bingwaashaagidiwa’, mii dash gakina ezhi’onid i’iw babikwaangenaa batakibine’onid.**

They were all naked; and the only dress they all had was a feather, with all but the top stripped from the shaft, standing perched upon their heads.

<span class="l-gloss">“Gaye giin ina giwii-niim?” (“And do you also wish to dance?”) Gii-inaa a´aw Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu was told).</span>

8)  **“Gaye giin ina giwii-niim?” Gii-inaa a´aw Nenaboozhoo.**

“And do you also wish to dance?” they said to Nanabushu.

“Mii na go iw iidog zhigwa wii-wiidookaazoyan? (“Without doubt it must be your wish now to take part?

9)  **“Mii na go iw iidog zhigwa wii-wiidookaazoyan?**

“Without doubt it must be your wish to participate?

<span class="l-orig">(10) Gi-noondam ina minik i’iw isa minik waa-niimi’iding? (Have you heard how many days they will be dancing?)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(10) Gi-noondam ina minik i’iw isa minik waa-niimi’iding?**</span>

Have you heard how many days they will be dancing?

<span class="l-orig">(11) Nishwaasogan wii-niimi’idim.” (Eight days they will be dancing.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(11) Nishwaasogan wii-niimi’idim.”**</span>

Eight days will they be dancing.”

<span class="l-orig">(12) Mii dash gaye wiin gaa-izhi’od (thereupon he himself dressed the same way), gii-batakibinwe’od (he had a feather standing upon his head).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(12) Mii dash gaye wiin gaa-izhi’od, gii-batakibinwe’od.**</span>

Thereupon he himself dressed in the same costume, he had a feather standing upon his head.

<span class="l-orig">(13) Mii dash apii maajitaanid (and now, when they began), naawaya’ii gaa-izhooshigaabawid (in their midst was where he stood) gaye qiin gii-niimid (and he too danced).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(13) Mii dash apii maajitaanid, naawaya’ii gaa-izhooshigaabawid gaye qiin gii-niimid.**</span>

Now, when they began, in their midst was where he stood and also danced.

<span class="l-orig">(14) Mii dash apii gii-maajitaanid (now, when they began), aaniish mii sa gaye wiin i’imaa gaa-indashiiweshimot (thereupon then and there did he dance); baabnaginange inakamigiziwa’ (a rousing time did they have).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(14) Mii dash apii gii-maajitaanid, aaniish mii sa gaye wiin i’imaa gaa-indashiiweshimot; baabnaginange inakamigiziwa’.**</span>

Now, when the others began (dancing), thereupon then and there did he dance; a rousing time did they have.

<span class="l-orig">(15) Geget sa gichi-niimi’idiwa i’imaa (of a truth, a great dance they had together there).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(15) Geget sa gichi-niimi’idiwa i’imaa.**</span>

Of a truth, a great dance they had together there.

<span class="l-orig">(16) Mii dash i’imaa endazhiweshimod (and now, there where he was dancing), niiyogon gaa-niimid (four days he danced).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(16) Mii dash i’imaa endazhiweshimod, niiyogon gaa-niimid.**</span>

And now, there where he was dancing, four days did he dance.

<span class="l-orig">(17) Aaniish i’imaa wiijiiwaad (so there he accompanied them) i’iw isa gaa-niiyogonagadanig (for a space of four days).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(17) Aaniish i’imaa wiijiiwaad i’iw isa gaa-niiyogonagadanig.**</span>

So there he helped them for a space of four days.

<span class="l-orig">(18) Mii dash apii gii-inind (and that was when he was told): “Ambe sa noo ayaagwaamizin (“Come now, display your zeal),” gii-inaa aw Nenaboozhoo (Nanabushu was told).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(18) Mii dash apii gii-inind: “Ambe sa noo ayaagwaamizin,” gii-inaa aw Nenaboozhoo.**</span>

And that was when they said to him: “We beg of you, display your zeal,” they said to Nanabushu.

<span class="l-orig">(19) “Gego noonde-aanishiidangen.” (“Don’t let up before the affair is over.”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(19) “Gego noonde-aanishiidangen.”**</span>

“Don’t let up before the affair is over.”

<span class="l-orig">(20) Mii dash apii zhigwa gii-ozhi’onid (now, that was when they began to dress/paint themselves); gaa-izhi-miskwaakwiyowenid (after they had painted themselves red), aaniish mii go gaye wiin gaa-izhi’od a’aw Nenaboozhoo (then the same did Nanabushu to himself).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(20) Mii dash apii zhigwa gii-ozhi’onid; gaa-izhi-miskwaakwiyowenid, aaniish mii go gaye wiin gaa-izhi’od a’aw Nenaboozhoo.**</span>

Now, that was when they began to paint themselves; after they were painted red, then the same to himself did Nanabushu.

<span class="l-orig">(21) Gii-naaniimid mii imaa (he kept right on dancing there at the place), aanish gaawiin aanawi mashi ayekozisii (for not even yet was he weary).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(21) Gii-naaniimid mii imaa, aanish gaawiin aanawi mashi ayekozisii.**</span>

He kept right on dancing there at the place, for not even yet was he weary.

<span class="l-orig">(22) Mii dash geget enigok niiminid (and so truly with much fervor he danced).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(22) Mii dash geget enigok niiminid.**</span>

And so truly qirh muxh fervor he danced.

<span class="l-orig">(23) Abaapish gaa ningodwaasogonagadinig (by the time the sixth day was up), mii dash apii e-ni-waabaninig (and when the morning light was coming on) zhigwa gikendang ayekozid (he began to realize that he was growing tired).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(23) Abaapish gaa ningodwaasogonagadinig, mii dash apii e-ni-waabaninig zhigwa gikendang ayekozid.**</span>

By the time the sixth day was up, and when the light of morning was coming on, he began to realize that he was growing tired.

<span class="l-orig">(24) Mii go iw ezhi-ziigisenig oshkiinzhigoon (thereupon the tears streamed from his eyes).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(24) Mii go iw ezhi-ziigisenig oshkiinzhigoon.**</span>

Thereupon the tears streamed from his eyes.

<span class="l-orig">(25) Geget sa nanoondendam jibwaa-waabininig (in good earnest was he anxious for the morning to come).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(25) Geget sa nanoondendam jibwaa-waabininig.**</span>

In good earnest was he anxious for the morning to come.

<span class="l-orig">(26) Gegapii igo gichi-mawi (then at last he wept aloud) i’iw epiichi-dibikadinig (for that the night was yet far from spent).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(26) Gegapii igo gichi-mawi i’iw epiichi-dibikadinig.**</span>

Then at last he wept aloud for that the night was yet far from spent.

<span class="l-orig">(27) Mii dash i’iw ji-ishkwaa-niimi’idinig (and now the dance would be over) ani-waabaninig (during the coming-on of the morning).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(27) Mii dash i’iw ji-ishkwaa-niimi’idinig ani-waabaninig.**</span>

And now the dance would be over during the coming-on of the orning.

<span class="l-orig">(28) Mii sa agaawaa ogashkidoon aano-niimid (and so hardly was he able to dance), aaniish ayekozi (for he was tired).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(28) Mii sa agaawaa ogashkidoon aano-niimid, aaniish ayekozi.**</span>

And so hardly was he able to dance, for he was tired.

<span class="l-orig">(29) Zhayiigwa aanawi bi-yaabanini (by and by, nevertheless, the dawn began to break), mii sa zhayiigwa nanaawajimonid (whereupon then they began whooping). Mii dash aanawi biidaabi-waabaninig (in spite of the breaking of the dawn),</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(29) Zhayiigwa aanawi bi-yaabanini, mii sa zhayiigwa nanaawajimonid. Mii dash aanawi biidaabi-waabaninig,**</span>

By and by, nevertheless, the dawn began to break, whereupon the others then began whooping. In spite of the breaking of the dawn,

<span class="l-orig">(30) aanish mii go iw ezhi-nasine mawid (he was nevertheless weeping all the while), epiichi-ayekozit (so very tired was he).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(30) aanish mii go iw ezhi-nasine mawid, epiichi-ayekozit.**</span>

he was nevertheless weeping all the while, so very tired was he.

<span class="l-orig">(31) Ningoding igo, e-shkwaa-webinigenid (now presently, after the others had ceased with their music), gii-niibawi megwe-zhaashaaganashkokaa (there he stood in amongst the bulrushes), aaniish gaa-izhinaagoskang iniw zhaazhaaganashkaan (for what he had met with was the bulrushes) miinangwona iniw gaa-izhinang (which he had supposed to be people dancing).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(31) Ningoding igo, e-shkwaa-webinigenid, gii-niibawi megwe-zhaashaaganashkokaa, aaniish gaa-izhinaagoskang iniw zhaazhaaganashkaan miinangwona iniw gaa-izhinang.**</span>

Now presently, after the others had ceased with their music, there he was standing in amongst the bulrushes, for what he had met with was the bulrushes, which he had supposed (to be people dancing).

<span class="l-orig">(32) Mii dash geget naanoondendam jibwaa-waabaninig (it was true that he had grown restless before it was morning), mii dash ezhi-mawid (and so he was weeping), epiichi-ayekozid (he was so tired).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(32) Mii dash geget naanoondendam jibwaa-waabaninig, mii dash ezhi-mawid, epiichi-ayekozid.**</span>

It was true that he had grown restless before it was morning, and so he was weeping, he was so tired.

<span class="l-orig">(33) Ningoding igo i’iw akawaabandang ji-bi-waabaninig (but of a sudden, while watching for the morrow to come), zhigwa gegedo waabandaan bi-waabaninig (he then truly saw the coming of the morning).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(33) Ningoding igo i’iw akawaabandang ji-bi-waabaninig, zhigwa gegedo waabandaan bi-waabaninig.**</span>

But of a sudden, while waiting for the morrow, he then truly saw the coming of the morning.

<span class="l-orig">(34) Mii dash gaa-inendang (and this is what he thought): “Ambegish ge-izhitine gibimi-baagaakaabang\!” (“would that at once the full light of day might appear\!”)</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(34) Mii dash gaa-inendang: “Ambegish ge-izhitine gibimi-baagaakaabang\!”**</span>

And this is what he thought: “would that at once the full light of day might appear\!”

<span class="l-orig">(35) Mii dash geget zhigwa dibishkoojaya’ii bimi-akwaabanini (whereupon truly now straight overhead had come the line of the morning light).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(35) Mii dash geget zhigwa dibishkoojaya’ii bimi-akwaabanini.**</span>

Whereupon truly now straight overhead had come the line of the morning light.

<span class="l-orig">(36) Aaniish mii go iw mookwishimod (now, by that time he was tired out from the dance).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(36) Aaniish mii go iw mookwishimod.**</span>

Now, by that time he was tired out by reason of the dance.

<span class="l-orig">(37) Mii dash inaabid (and so he looked); geget i’imaa, niibawid (truly, there where he was standing), goniginiin megwe zhaashaaganagashkokaa gii-niibawi (to his surprise he was standing in among the bulrushes).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(37) Mii dash inaabid; geget i’imaa, niibawid, goniginiin megwe zhaashaaganagashkokaa gii-niibawi.**</span>

And so he looked; truly, there where he was standing, to his surprise, was in among the bulrushes.

<span class="l-orig">(38) Mii sa ndawaa ezhi-naadagaame ayaad (on recognizing what it was, he made his way close to the shore).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(38) Mii sa ndawaa ezhi-naadagaame ayaad.**</span>

On recognizing (what he had been dancing with), he made his way close to the shore.

<span class="l-orig">(39) Mii dash nangwana i’iniw gaa-izhinang zhaashaaganashkoon (thereupon it became a fact that the bulrushes were what he had seen) anishinaabeng gaa-izhinang (he had taken them for people).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(39) Mii dash nangwana i’iniw gaa-izhinang zhaashaaganashkoon anishinaabeng gaa-izhinang.**</span>

Thereupon it became a fact that he had taken the bullrushes for people.

<span class="l-orig">(40) Mii nangwana iw apii ko i´iw gii-ani-dagwaagig (it had happened to be in the autumn) mii nangwana i´iw gii-gichi-naanooding (the season when there was always a strong wind blowing).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(40) Mii nangwana iw apii ko i´iw gii-ani-dagwaagig mii nangwana i´iw gii-gichi-naanooding.**</span>

It had happened to be in the autumn, during the season when there was always a strong wind blowing.

<span class="l-orig">(41) Mii nangwana i’iniw gaa-gii-izhinang iniw zhaashganashkoon (it was true that such was the way he had seen the bulrushes).</span>

<span class="l-fiero">**(41) Mii nangwana i’iniw gaa-gii-izhinang iniw zhaashganashkoon.**</span>

It was true that such was the way he had seen the bullrushes.

<span class="l-fiero">**(42) NAA mii dash inangwana i’imaa wawiyazh gii-izhiwebizid.**</span>

Therefore that was a time when he had a joke played on him.

<span class="l-orig">(42) NAA mii dash inangwana i’imaa wawiyazh gii-izhiwebizid (therefore that was a time when a joke was played on him).</span>
