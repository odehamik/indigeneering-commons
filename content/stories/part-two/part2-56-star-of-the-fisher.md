STAR OF THE FISHER.

*Narrated by Wāsāgunäckạnk (Series III; Bois Fort). — narrator per Jones, Ojibwa Texts Part II, Introduction p.ix (not credited on the story page itself).*

***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***

(1) Ānĭc ōdä’tōwag igi’ᵘ ạnicinābäg; kägä’t ki’tci∙ō∙dänāwan. Ānĭc äcibibōninig. Mīsa’ bạbipōniciwāt wī’kāgu māminonäntam pạcig ’aᵉa’ᵘ inini. Kāwĭn nibīnsinini; ạbä’pic wätcitcisänig ’iᵉi’ᵘ a’pī nābininig, mĭsa’ kāwīn nībīnsinini. Mīsa’ äcigwĭnawibi’tōwāt ’iᵉi’ᵘ tcipinībininik. "Ạmạntcigĭc äciwäbạtogwän?" iki’tōwag igi’ᵘ ạnicinābäg. Ānĭc mĭsa’ cigwa kiki’käntạmowāt pi’tcibibōninik. "Ạmạntcigĭc sạgaswä∙i∙tiyạng," ki’i’kito a bäjik. Mīsa’ kā∙i∙cimādci’tōt kīsạgaswä∙i∙wät ’aᵉa’ᵘ inini. Mĭsa cigwa kĭsạgaswä∙i∙tiwāt.

**Aaniish odaa’idoowag igiw anishinaabeg; geget gichi-oodenaawan. Aaniish ezhi-biiboninig. Mii sa babiibonishiwaad wiikaa go maaminonendam bezhig a’aw inini. Gaawiin niibinsinini; abaapii wechichisenig iw apii naabininig, mii sa gaawiin niibinsinini. Mii sa ezhi-gwiinawi-i’doowaad iw ji-biiboninik. "Amanj igizh ezhi-webadogwen?" ikidowag igiw anishinaabeg. Aaniish mii sa zhigwa gikikendamowaad bi-ji-biiboninik. "Amanj igizh zagaswe-idiyang," ikido a bezhig. Mii sa gaa-izhi-maaji’tood gii-zagaswe-iwed a’aw inini. Mii sa zhigwa gii-zagaswe-idiwaad.**

*Now, in a town did the people live; in a really large town they dwelt. Now, it happened to be in the winter-time. And so, while they were in winter camp, a certain man got to turning matters over in his mind. It was not getting summer; when the time was at hand for the summer to be, why, there was no summer. And so with an uncertain feeling they waited for the coming of summer. "Wonder what could have happened\!" said the people. Now, it so happened that in time they came to realize how far the winter had gone. "It may be well for us to hold a smoker," said a certain one. Thereupon the man made ready for holding the smoker. And so in a while they were gathered together for a smoke.*

Aaniish odaa’idoowag igiw anishinaabeg (now the people kept a town) — \*odaa’i- "keep/dwell in a town" stem flagged\*; geget gichi-oodenaawan (truly it was a really big town). Aaniish ezhi-biiboninig (now it being winter). Mii sa babiibonishiwaad (and so while they were wintering — \*babiibonishi- stem flagged\*) wiikaa go maaminonendam (after a while turned it over in his mind) bezhig a’aw inini (a certain man). Gaawiin niibinsinini (it was not becoming summer); abaapii wechichisenig (when the time was reaching) iw apii naabininig (the time for the summer to be), mii sa gaawiin niibinsinini (and still it was not becoming summer). Mii sa ezhi-gwiinawi-i’doowaad (and so uncertainly they awaited it — \*gwiinawi-i’doo- flagged\*) iw ji-biiboninik (the coming of summer; \*Jones: "the coming of summer"\*). "Amanj igizh ezhi-webadogwen?" ("wonder what could have happened?" — \*dubitative\*) ikidowag igiw anishinaabeg (said the people). Aaniish mii sa zhigwa gikikendamowaad (now then they came to realize) bi-ji-biiboninik (how far the winter had gone — \*Jones-witnessed; segmentation flagged\*). "Amanj igizh zagaswe-idiyang," ("perhaps we should hold a smoker,") ikido a bezhig (said a certain one). Mii sa gaa-izhi-maaji’tood (and so he set about) gii-zagaswe-iwed a’aw inini (that man’s holding of the smoker). Mii sa zhigwa gii-zagaswe-idiwaad (and so then they smoked together).

(2) Ānĭc ka’kina unānga∙a∙bi’tāgowān. Anīc ugi’känimāwān päjik inĭ’ᵘ uningwạniwān kägō wạnändạsinik. Mīsa’ cigwa pimōckinäbiwāt i∙i∙mā wĭgiwāming sạgaswä∙i∙tiwāt. Mīsa’ kāgīgitōwāt ogō’ᵘ ạnōtcigu niniwag i∙i∙mā igi’ᵘ nä∙ã∙ngabiwāt. O∙ō∙widạc ogi’i’nān ’aᵉa’ᵘ utcĭg: "Äye, ninki’känimāsa nīn ’aᵉa’ᵘ wädicĭctcigäwạnit. Kāwĭn niwạnänimāsī ’aᵉa’ᵘ äcĭctcigät; ugi’tciwĭnān ’iᵉi’ᵘ nībin; kāwĭn wĭpạgitĭnasīn ō∙o∙mā tcibīcāmạga’k ’iᵉi’ᵘ nībin," ugī∙i∙nā ’aᵉa’ᵘ utcĭg. "Kāwĭn pō’tc tātagwicinōmagasinōn ’iᵉi’ᵘ nībin; mĭgu iᵘ kwaya’k wĭndạmōnagu’k," ugī∙i∙gowān inĭ’ᵘ utcīgan.

**Aaniish gakina onaangwa’ibi’taagowaan. Aaniish ogii-gikenimaawaan bezhig iniw oningwaniwaan gego wanendasinik. Mii sa zhigwa bimooshkinebiwaad imaa wiigiwaaming zagaswe-idiwaad. Mii sa gaagiigidowaad ogow anooj igo ininiwag imaa igiw naa-aangabiwaad. Oo dash ogii-inaan a’aw ojiig: "Eya, ningikenimaa sa niin a’aw wedizhichigewanid. Gaawiin niwanenimaasii a’aw ezhichiged; ogii-jiwinaan iw niibin; gaawiin wii-bagidinasiin oomaa ji-bii-zhaamagak iw niibin," ogii-inaa a’aw ojiig. "Gaawiin booch da-dagwishinoomagasinoon iw niibin; mii go iw gwayak windamoonagok," ogii-igowaan iniw ojiigan.**

*Now, all were in the relation of sons-in-law to them. Now, they knew that the knowledge of one of their sons-in-law was not wanting in anything. And so then did they come filling up the place in the wigwam, smoking together in assembly. Thereupon these men held forth in talk upon various things, there where they were sons-in-law. And this to them said the Fisher: "Yea, I myself know who it is causing this. I am not at a loss to know the one that is doing this. There is, to be sure, a certain one doing it; he is holding back the summer; he is not willing to let the summer come hither to this place," to them said the Fisher. "There will be, perforce, no coming of the summer; and that is a truth which I now impart to you" they were told by the Fisher.*

Aaniish gakina onaangwa’ibi’taagowaan (now all of them were sons-in-law to them — \*onaangwa’i- "be son-in-law" stem flagged\*). Aaniish ogii-gikenimaawaan bezhig (now they knew of one) iniw oningwaniwaan (their son-in-law) gego wanendasinik (whose knowledge wanted in nothing). Mii sa zhigwa bimooshkinebiwaad (and so then they came filling the place) imaa wiigiwaaming (there in the wigwam) zagaswe-idiwaad (smoking together). Mii sa gaagiigidowaad ogow (and so these held forth) anooj igo ininiwag (the men, on various things) imaa igiw naa-aangabiwaad (there where they were sons-in-law). Oo dash ogii-inaan a’aw ojiig (and this the Fisher said to them): "Eya, ningikenimaa sa niin ("yea, I myself know) a’aw wedizhichigewanid (the one doing this). Gaawiin niwanenimaasii a’aw ezhichiged (I am not at a loss for the one doing it); ogii-jiwinaan iw niibin (he holds back the summer — \*jiwin- stem flagged\*); gaawiin wii-bagidinasiin (he will not let it loose) oomaa ji-bii-zhaamagak iw niibin," (the summer to come hither to this place,") ogii-inaa a’aw ojiig (said the Fisher). "Gaawiin booch da-dagwishinoomagasinoon iw niibin ("perforce the summer will not arrive); mii go iw gwayak windamoonagok," (and that is the truth I tell you,") ogii-igowaan iniw ojiigan (they were told by the Fisher).

*p469 (English) ² "The animal-folk." — Jones’s note on "said the people²" / the sons-in-law assembly, glossing the gathered as the animal-folk (cf. §3 manidōyäncag).*

(3) Ānĭc kägä’t ugi’känimāwān kägä’t täbwänit ’iᵉi’ᵘ ä’kitunit. Mīdạc kā∙i∙cikanōnāwāt: "Ānĭc kā∙i∙cĭctcigäyạnguban ’iᵉi’ᵘ tcinibingibạn?" ugī∙i∙nāwān.

**Aaniish geget ogii-gikenimaawaan geget debwenid iw ekidonid. Mii dash gaa-izhi-ganoonaawaad: "Aaniish gaa-izhichigeyangoban iw ji-niibingiban?" ogii-inaawaan.**

*Naturally, of course, they knew that he correctly spoke the truth in what he said. Thereupon they spoke to him, saying: "How shall we bring it about for the summer to come?" they asked of him.*

Aaniish geget ogii-gikenimaawaan (now truly they knew of him) geget debwenid iw ekidonid (that he spoke true in what he said). Mii dash gaa-izhi-ganoonaawaad (and so then they spoke to him): "Aaniish gaa-izhichigeyangoban ("how should we bring it about) iw ji-niibingiban?" (that summer should come? — \*preterite-dubitative\*) ogii-inaawaan (they asked him).

(5) " ’Āᵘ," ugi’i’gōn. "Mĭsạgu iᵘ kā∙i∙ciwäbisiyạng ānin kä∙i∙’kitoyạn. Ānĭc kāwĭn pitcĭnạg tānībinsinōn misawa anibābi’tōyạng ’iᵉi’ᵘ tcinibing. Ayā kuca ’aᵉa’ᵘ käbā’ku∙ā’t ’iᵉi’ᵘ nībinisä."

**" ’Aw," ogii-igon. "Mii sa go iw gaa-izhi-webiziyang aaniin ge-ikidoyan. Aaniish gaawiin bichiinag da-niibinsinoon misawaa ani-baabi’toyang iw ji-niibing. Ayaa gosha a’aw gaa-baa’ako’aad iw niibinise."**

*"Very well," he was told. "Therefore will we do whatsoever you shall say. For it will not be summer soon, however long we may continue to wait for the coming of the summer. There is really one that has shut up the birds of summer."*

" ’Aw," ogii-igon ("very well," he was told). "Mii sa go iw gaa-izhi-webiziyang ("and so this is how it shall be with us) aaniin ge-ikidoyan (whatever you shall say). Aaniish gaawiin bichiinag da-niibinsinoon (now it will not be summer soon) misawaa ani-baabi’toyang (however long we keep waiting) iw ji-niibing (for summer to come). Ayaa gosha a’aw (there really is one) gaa-baa’ako’aad iw niibinise." (who has shut up the birds of summer." — \*baa’ako’- "shut up" + niibinise "summer-bird"; stem flagged\*)

(6) "Cigwa," ugī∙i∙nāwān, "ạmbäsa, kunigä’kā käta∙i∙nändạzigwän tci∙a∙ntunä∙a∙nguban?"

**"Zhigwa," ogii-inaawaan, "ambesa, gonige gaa da-inendazigwen ji-andone’angoban?"**

"Zhigwa," ogii-inaawaan ("well now," they said to him), "ambesa, gonige gaa ("pray, who then) da-inendazigwen (might be willing — \*dubitative\*) ji-andone’angoban?" (to go seek the being? — \*andone’- "seek"; preterite-conditional flagged\*)

(7) "Äyeᵉ, mĭsa yä’ta ’iᵉi’ᵘ käkackitōyạnguban ’iᵉi’ᵘ tcinībigibạn; kāwīn a’pidci nibiwa kitānibāsīmin ’iᵉi’ᵘ a’pī∙ä∙yag ’iᵉi’ᵘwisa nībing. O∙o∙mini’k kitānibāmin, mi’tā’tcing kitānibāmin; mī’tā’ᵘ ä’pi’tcāg i∙i∙mā ayāg ’isi’ᵘ nībin. Ạmbäsa, ayāngwāmisiyu’k\! ānĭc mĭsạgu iᵘ wâbạng tcimādcaiyạng. Ānĭc, nīn ningạtibändān," ugi’i’gōwān inĭ’ᵘ utcīgan.

**"Eya, mii sa yeta iw ge-gashki’toyangoban iw ji-niibigiban; gaawiin aapiji niibiwa gida-nibaasiimin iw apii-eyag iw sa niibing. Onow minik gida-nibaamin, midaaching gida-nibaamin; mii daa-iw apiichaag imaa ayaag iw sa niibin. Ambesa, ayaangwaamiziyok\! aaniish mii sa go iw waabang ji-maajaayang. Aaniish, niin ningad-dibendaan," ogii-igowaan iniw ojiigan.**

*"Yea, this is the only way we shall succeed in having the summer come; not so very many times should we sleep (to get to) where the summer is. This is the number of times we should sleep, ten times we should sleep; that is how far away it is to where the summer is. Pray, be careful to do what you can\! for it is on the morrow that we shall depart. Well, I shall have charge of the undertaking," they were told by the Fisher.*

"Eya, mii sa yeta iw ge-gashki’toyangoban ("yea, this is the only way we could manage it) iw ji-niibigiban (for summer to come); gaawiin aapiji niibiwa gida-nibaasiimin (not very many times shall we sleep) iw apii-eyag iw sa niibing (to where the summer is). Onow minik gida-nibaamin (this many times shall we sleep), midaaching gida-nibaamin (ten times shall we sleep); mii daa-iw apiichaag (that is how far it is) imaa ayaag iw sa niibin (to where the summer is). Ambesa, ayaangwaamiziyok\! (pray, be careful\! — \*imperative\*) aaniish mii sa go iw waabang ji-maajaayang (for it is tomorrow we depart). Aaniish, niin ningad-dibendaan," (well, I shall have charge of it," — \*liaison future ningad-\*) ogii-igowaan iniw ojiigan (they were told by the Fisher).

(8) Mīsa’ cigwa wĭmāyawusät ’aᵉa’ᵘ utcĭg, ānĭc mĭsa cigwa mini’k imā nä∙ã∙ngabiwāt wīmādcāwāt. Ānĭc ka’kina ugīna’kwä’tāgōᵒ. Ānĭc ka’kina mĭsa’ cigwa kina’kundiwāt īgi’ᵘ nä∙ã∙ngabiwāt. **Mii sa zhigwa wii-mayaawosed a’aw ojiig, aaniish mii sa zhigwa minik imaa naa-aangabiwaad wii-maajaawaad. Aaniish gakina ogii-nakwetaagoo. Aaniish gakina mii sa zhigwa gii-nakomidiwaad igiw naa-aangabiwaad. Gaye wiin adik naa-aangabigoban imaa anishinaabeg odaa’idonid; gaye wiin waagosh naa-aangabigoban iw anishinaabeg odaa’idonid; gaye wiin amik naa-aangabigoban imaa anishinaabeg odaa’idonid; gaye wiin wazhashk naa-aangabigoban imaa anishinaabeg odaa’idonid; gaye wiin nigig naa-aangabigoban imaa anishinaabeg odaa’idonid; mii sa zhigwa, aaniish gakina awiya naa-aangabigoban minik awiya beboonishid ogow manidooyenzhag.**

*So then it was that the Fisher desired to lead the expedition, for it chanced that as many as were sons-in-law there wished to go. So by them all he met with approval. Now, all who therefore then came to agreement among themselves were they that were sons-in-law. Even so was Caribou then son-in-law there where the people were; even so was Fox then son-in-law where the people lived in a town; even so was Beaver then son-in-law at the place where the people dwelt in a town; even so was Muskrat then a son-in-law at the place where the people lived in a town; even so was Otter then a son-in-law at the place where the people dwelt in a town; and so on, for every creature of all these small animal-folk then spending the winter there was a son-in-law at the time.*

Mii sa zhigwa wii-mayaawosed a’aw ojiig (and so the Fisher wished to lead — \*mayaawose- "lead the way" flagged\*), aaniish mii sa zhigwa minik imaa naa-aangabiwaad (for as many as were sons-in-law there) wii-maajaawaad (wished to go). Aaniish gakina ogii-nakwetaagoo (now by all of them he met approval). Aaniish gakina mii sa zhigwa gii-nakomidiwaad (now all came to agreement) igiw naa-aangabiwaad (those who were sons-in-law). Gaye wiin adik naa-aangabigoban imaa anishinaabeg odaa’idonid (even so was Caribou son-in-law where the people kept a town); gaye wiin waagosh ... (even so was Fox ...); gaye wiin amik ... **Mii sa zhigwa gii-maajaawaad. Zhayiigwa dibikishkaawag, aaniish mii sa ezhi-gabeshiwaad. Miinawaa wayaabaninig ani-maajaawag.
