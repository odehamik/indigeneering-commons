TĀ·Ī·MISI.

**TAA-II-MISI.**

TĀ·Ī·MISI (THE STORY OF TĀ·Ī·MISI).

*Narrated by Wāsāgunäckạnk and/or Midāsugaⁿj (Series IV; Bois Fort). — narrator per Jones, Ojibwa Texts Part II, Introduction p.ix (“either … or both”; not credited on the story page).*

***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***

(1) Ānic ạnicinābäg tāwag, ōdänawi∙i∙tiwag. Päjigidạc ugāmāwi ạnicinābä. Mīsa’ o∙o∙dä‘tōwāt, ayāwạn ugwisisan ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ a‘kiwäⁿzi täbändạnk ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ ōdäna; kāwīniku mạci‘ ni‘ tāwigisīwạn, agā cīwạn igu. Mīdạc tacī‘käwāt witci∙ā∙t ạbinōtciyạn. Ninguting käga‘pī kā∙i∙cinisāt, mīsa’ kā∙i∙cictcigät.

**Aaniish anishinaabeg daawag, oodenawi’idiwag. Bezhig idash ogimaawi anishinaabe. Mii sa’ o-oodetoowaad, ayaawan ogwisisan a’aw akiwenzii debendang iw oodena; gaawiini go maji’ niitaawigisiiwan, agaashiiwan igo. Mii dash dazhiikewaad wiiji’aad abinoojiiyan. Ningoding gegapii gaa-izhi-nisaad, mii sa’ gaa-izhi-izhichiged.**

*Now, some people were continuing (there), they were living in a town together. And a certain old man was chief. And now here, where they abode, was a little son of the old man that ruled over the town; and not yet was he well on in years, he was yet (a small boy). And the while at play were the children in whose company he went. Now, once indeed he killed them, thus had he done.*

Aaniish anishinaabeg daawag (now, some people were dwelling), oodenawi’idiwag (they lived together in a town). Bezhig idash ogimaawi anishinaabe (and a certain man was chief). Mii sa’ o-oodetoowaad (and so where they had their town), ayaawan ogwisisan (he had a little son) a’aw akiwenzii (that old man) debendang iw oodena (who owned/ruled the town); gaawiini go maji’ niitaawigisiiwan (not yet was he well grown), agaashiiwan igo (he was still small). Mii dash dazhiikewaad (and so, busy at play) wiiji’aad abinoojiiyan (the children in whose company he went). Ningoding gegapii gaa-izhi-nisaad (once, at last, he killed them — \*nisaad ‘killed’\*), mii sa’ gaa-izhi-izhichiged (and so that is what he did).

*Translated by me on the basis of Dr. Jones’s field-notes.—T. M.*

(2) Ānic mānändạm ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ a‘kiwänzi wägwisisit, ānīc win nīgānizi iᵉi‘ᵘ ōdäna. Udinān īni‘ᵘ ogwisisan: “Kägu’ ijictcigä‘kän mīnawā ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ kā∙i∙cictcigäyạn. Kägä‘t nimānäntạm ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ kā∙i∙cictcigäyạn. Kīcpin mīnawā ijictcigäyạn mīsa’ tcī∙i∙cinạn ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ tcinicikäwisiyạn,” udinān īni‘ᵘ ugwisisan. Kägä‘t kabäya∙ī∙ ugītạcimān tcīkwaya‘kwātisinit. Mīsa’ kā∙i∙nāt īni‘ᵘ ugwisisan.

**Aaniish maanendam a’aw akiwenzii wegwisisid, aaniish wiin niigaanizi iw oodena. Odinaan iniw ogwisisan: “Gego izhichigeken miinawaa iw gaa-izhi-izhichigeyan. Geget nimaanendam iw gaa-izhi-izhichigeyan. Giishpin miinawaa izhichigeyan mii sa’ ji-izhinan iw ji-nazhikewizoyan,” odinaan iniw ogwisisan. Geget gabeya’ii ogii-dazhimaan ji-gwayako-aadizinid. Mii sa’ gaa-inaad iniw ogwisisan.**

*Now, in a bad state of mind was the old man that was father to the child, for he was the foremost one of the town. He said to his little son: “Repeat not the thing that you did. Verily, I dislike what you have done. If ever you do so again, then will I have you left alone by yourself,” he said to his little son. In truth, for a long time he charged him to live an upright life. Such was what he had told his little son.*

Aaniish maanendam a’aw akiwenzii (now, distressed was that old man) wegwisisid (who was father to the child), aaniish wiin niigaanizi iw oodena (for he was foremost in the town). Odinaan iniw ogwisisan (he said to his son): “Gego izhichigeken (do not do it) miinawaa iw gaa-izhi-izhichigeyan (again, that thing you did). Geget nimaanendam (truly I dislike) iw gaa-izhi-izhichigeyan (what you have done). Giishpin miinawaa izhichigeyan (if you do it again) mii sa’ ji-izhinan (then I will see to it) iw ji-nazhikewizoyan (that you are left alone by yourself — \*nazhikewizi- ‘be alone’ flagged\*),” odinaan iniw ogwisisan (he said to his son). Geget gabeya’ii ogii-dazhimaan (truly for a long time he charged him) ji-gwayako-aadizinid (to live an upright life — \*stem flagged\*). Mii sa’ gaa-inaad iniw ogwisisan (such is what he told his son).

(3) Mīdạc mīnawā kīmādcītạcī‘käwāt, mīdạc mīnawā wīdci∙ā∙t ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ ạbinōtcīyaᵍ ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ kwīwisäns. Mīsa’ mīnawā ninguting kā∙i∙cinisāt īni‘ᵘ ạbinōtcīyạn. Mīsa kī∙i∙gut īni‘ᵘ ōsạn: “Nintawā kīganici‘käwis,” ugī∙i∙gōn īni‘ᵘ ōsạn. Mīsa’ kägä‘t sāgitōwạn īni‘ᵘ ōsạn: “Ạmbäsinō, ạnicinābätug\! kusiyu‘k,” ugī∙i∙nā. Ānic win ugimāwi, pō‘tcidạc icīciwäbạtînig ä‘kitut.

**Mii dash miinawaa gii-maajiitaa-dazhiikewaad, mii dash miinawaa wiiji’aad iw abinoojiiyag a’aw gwiiwizens. Mii sa’ miinawaa ningoding gaa-izhi-nisaad iniw abinoojiiyan. Mii sa’ gii-igod iniw oosan: “Nindawaa gii-ganizhikewiz,” ogii-igon iniw oosan. Mii sa’ geget zaagidoowan iniw oosan: “Ambesino, anishinaabedog\! goziyok,” ogii-inaa. Aaniish wiin ogimaawi, boozh idash izhi-izhiwebadinig ekidod.**

*And so, when again they began to play, then once more in company with the children went the boy. Whereupon another time he slew a child. Accordingly he was told by his father: “Therefore now you shall be left alone by yourself,” he was told by his father. And then, of a truth, forth went his father, crying out: “Come, O ye people\! do you move away,” he said to them. Now, he was chief, and it had to be according as he said.*

Mii dash miinawaa gii-maajiitaa-dazhiikewaad (and so again, when they began to play), mii dash miinawaa wiiji’aad iw abinoojiiyag (again he went among the children) a’aw gwiiwizens (that boy). Mii sa’ miinawaa ningoding gaa-izhi-nisaad iniw abinoojiiyan (and again at one point he killed a child). Mii sa’ gii-igod iniw oosan (so he was told by his father): “Nindawaa gii-ganizhikewiz (therefore now you will be left alone — \*gani-/nazhike- ‘left by oneself’ flagged\*),” ogii-igon iniw oosan (he was told by his father). Mii sa’ geget zaagidoowan iniw oosan (and truly out went his father): “Ambesino, anishinaabedog\! (come, O people\!) goziyok (do you move camp),” ogii-inaa (he said to them). Aaniish wiin ogimaawi (now he was chief), boozh idash izhi-izhiwebadinig ekidod (and so it had to be as he said).

(4) Mīsa gägä‘t wäwip kī‘kusinis ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ ạnicinābäᵍ. Mīsa’ ‘iᵉi‘mā kā∙i∙ci∙u∙cictcigātänig tci∙a∙yāt. Mīsa’ kägä‘t cigwa kī‘kīji∙i∙nt i∙i∙mā tci∙a∙yāt; mīdạć kā∙i∙cinạgạnint, mīsa’ kīwäpinint.

**Mii sa’ geget wewiib gii-gozinizi iw anishinaabeg. Mii sa’ imaa gaa-izhi-o-izhichigaadenig ji-ayaad. Mii sa’ geget zhigwa gii-giizhi’ind imaa ji-ayaad; mii dash gaa-izhi-naganind, mii sa’ gii-webinind.**

*Thereupon truly did the people speedily move camp. And now a place had been prepared (for him) where he might remain. Accordingly, when in truth he was compelled to remain there, then he was deserted, then he was abandoned for good.*

Mii sa’ geget wewiib gii-gozinizi iw anishinaabeg (so truly in haste the people moved camp). Mii sa’ imaa gaa-izhi-o-izhichigaadenig ji-ayaad (and there a place had been made for him to stay). Mii sa’ geget zhigwa gii-giizhi’ind imaa ji-ayaad (so, truly, now he was settled there to stay); mii dash gaa-izhi-naganind (and so he was left behind), mii sa’ gii-webinind (and so he was abandoned for good).

(5) Mīdạc i∙i∙witi kī∙a∙yāwāt īgi‘ᵘ ạnicinābäg, pīnic ingu‘ki‘kinōnawin kī∙ai∙yāwag. Ningutingigu ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ päjig inini, uckī∙i∙nini, kī∙i∙nändạm “Ạmbäsinō, anagä‘ kā awiyā ningawiwīdō‘kāgusi\!” kī∙i∙nändạm ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ uskī∙i∙nini. Kā∙i∙cikạnōnāt wītcōcki∙i∙niniwạn mīsa’ cigwa kina‘kwä‘tāgut: “Ạmbäsinō, nạtunäwātā ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ kāgīpiwäpinint\! Kägä‘tsa nin ninki‘tcimami‘kwänimā ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ. O∙ō∙widạc ugī∙i∙gōn īni‘ᵘ wītcōckinawän: “Kāmāwīn pimādisisītug,” ugī∙i∙gōn īni‘ᵘ wītcōskinawän.

**Mii dash iwidi gii-ayaawaad igiw anishinaabeg, biinish ingo-gikinoonowin gii-ayaawag. Ningoding igo a’aw bezhig inini, oshkiniinini, gii-inendam “Ambesino, anage gaa awiya ningawii-wiidookaagosii\!” gii-inendam a’aw oshkiniinini. Gaa-izhi-ganoonaad wiijiishkiniinwan mii sa’ zhigwa gii-nakwetaagod: “Ambesino, nandawaabamaadaa a’aw gaa-gii-webinind\! Geget sa niin nin-gichi-mami’kwenimaa a’aw. O-oo idash ogii-igon iniw wiijiishkinawen: “Gaamaawiin bimaadizisiidog,” ogii-igon iniw wiijiishkinawen.**

*Accordingly then at yonder place did the people continue, during a full cycle of the seasons they abode there. Now, once on a time a certain man, a youth, thus thought: “Oh, wonder if by any one I am ever going to be helped\!” thus thought the youth. After he had spoken to his boon companion, then by him was he given answer: “Behold, let us go seek for him that was deserted\! Of a truth, a great deal have I been thinking of him.” And this he was told by his boon companion: “It is possible that he may not be alive,” he was told by his boon companion.*

Mii dash iwidi gii-ayaawaad igiw anishinaabeg (and so yonder the people continued), biinish ingo-gikinoonowin gii-ayaawag (a full year/cycle of seasons they stayed). Ningoding igo a’aw bezhig inini, oshkiniinini (once a certain man, a youth), gii-inendam (thus thought): “Ambesino, anage gaa awiya ningawii-wiidookaagosii\! (oh, I wonder whether I shall ever be helped by anyone — \*anage/awii- flagged\*)” gii-inendam a’aw oshkiniinini (thought the youth). Gaa-izhi-ganoonaad wiijiishkiniinwan (when he had spoken to his boon companion — \*wiijiishkiniinw- ‘boon companion’ flagged\*) mii sa’ zhigwa gii-nakwetaagod (then he was answered): “Ambesino, nandawaabamaadaa a’aw gaa-gii-webinind\! (come, let us go look for the one who was abandoned\!) Geget sa niin nin-gichi-mami’kwenimaa a’aw (truly I have thought much of him — \*mami’kwenim- flagged\*).” O-oo idash ogii-igon iniw wiijiishkinawen (and this he was told by his boon companion): “Gaamaawiin bimaadizisiidog (perhaps he is not alive),” ogii-igon iniw wiijiishkinawen (he was told by his boon companion).

(6) “Mānōsạgo kānạntawâbạmānān,” ugī∙i∙nān īni‘ᵘ wītcōskinawän.

**“Maanoo sa go gaa-nandawaabamaanaan,” ogii-inaan iniw wiijiishkinawen.**

*“But nevertheless let us go seek for him\!” he said to his boon companion.*

“Maanoo sa go gaa-nandawaabamaanaan (never mind — let us go seek him anyway),” ogii-inaan iniw wiijiishkinawen (he said to his boon companion).

(7) Mīdạc kägä‘t kā∙i∙cimādcāwāt, kīnạntawâbạmāwāt. Cigwa owâbạndānāwa i∙i∙mā kā∙u∙ndcikạninunt; kägä‘t käyābi kīpimādisiwạn. Ānic ā‘pidci inändạm ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ ackī∙i∙nini, mīdạc kā∙i∙ciwīdci∙ā∙wāt.

**Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-maajaawaad, gii-nandawaabamaawaad. Zhigwa owaabandaanaawaa imaa gaa-onji-gaganind; geget geyaabi gii-bimaadiziwan. Aaniish aapiji inendam a’aw oshkiniinini, mii dash gaa-izhi-wiiji’aawaad.**

*Thereupon truly thence they departed to go seek for (the boy). In time they beheld the place where he had been abandoned; in truth, he was yet alive. Now, very eager was the youth (to go to where the boy was), whereupon then they went (and) joined him company.*

Mii dash geget gaa-izhi-maajaawaad (so truly they set off), gii-nandawaabamaawaad (to go seek him). Zhigwa owaabandaanaawaa imaa gaa-onji-gaganind (presently they saw the place where he had been left — \*gaa-onji-gagan- ‘where abandoned’ flagged\*); geget geyaabi gii-bimaadiziwan (truly he was still alive). Aaniish aapiji inendam a’aw oshkiniinini (now the youth was very eager), mii dash gaa-izhi-wiiji’aawaad (and so they joined his company).

(8) “Kāmāwīn nindākacki∙u∙sī ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ tcipimusäyān,” kī∙i∙nändạm ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ.

**“Gaamaawiin nindaa-gashki’osii iw ji-bimosseyaan,” gii-inendam a’aw.**

*“Possibly I may not be able to travel,” thought (the boy).*

“Gaamaawiin nindaa-gashki’osii iw ji-bimosseyaan (perhaps I shall not be able to walk),” gii-inendam a’aw (thought he, the boy).

(9) Mīdạc cigwa kīwīdci∙ā∙wāt. Mīdạc äjini‘kāsut ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ uskī∙i∙nini Tā∙ī∙misi. Mīsa’ kāwin ugacki∙ā∙sī tciniwitcīwät, i∙i∙widac wändcikaskitōsik i∙i∙wisa tciwidcīwāt. “Ānic, ningīcawänimigōn ka‘kina.” Mīdạc kā∙i∙gut ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ uskīniniwaᵍ: “Ạmbäsinō, a∙tātitā\!” ugī∙i∙gō. Wī‘kāsa ugītäbwä‘tawāᵍ. “O∙ō∙witi\!” ugī∙i∙gō. Mīdạc kägä‘t. Oᵉ∙ō∙widạc ugī∙i∙gō. “Ạmbäsanō bạbīmwā‘tagwätā\!”

**Mii dash zhigwa gii-wiiji’aawaad. Mii dash ezhinikaazod a’aw oshkiniinini Taa-ii-misi. Mii sa’ gaawiin ogashki’aasii ji-niwiijiiwed, iwidi dash wenji-gashkitoosig iwi sa ji-wiijiiwaad. “Aaniish, ningii-zhawenimigoo gakina.” Mii dash gaa-igod iw oshkiniiniwag: “Ambesino, a-daadidaa\!” ogii-igoo. Wii’kaa sa ogii-debwetawaan. “O-oo-widi\!” ogii-igoo. Mii dash geget. O-oo-widash ogii-igoo. “Ambesino babimwa’-dagwedaa\!”**

*Thereupon then they kept him company. And this was the name of the youth, Tā ī’misi. It was impossible for (the boy) to go along, and on that account it was impossible for him to join them company. “Now, I have been pitied by all (the manitous).” Thereupon he was told by the youths: “Come, let us have a game\!” he was told. It was a long while before he yielded to their request. “Off over here\!” he was told. And thus it truly happened. And this he was told: “Let us play the game of shooting at the stick\!”*

Mii dash zhigwa gii-wiiji’aawaad (then they joined his company). Mii dash ezhinikaazod a’aw oshkiniinini Taa-ii-misi (and this is the name of the youth, \*Taa-ii-misi — name HARD-flagged\*). Mii sa’ gaawiin ogashki’aasii ji-niwiijiiwed (it was impossible for the boy to go along), iwidi dash wenji-gashkitoosig iwi sa ji-wiijiiwaad (and that was why he could not join their company). “Aaniish, ningii-zhawenimigoo gakina (now, I have been pitied by all — i.e. the manitous).” Mii dash gaa-igod iw oshkiniiniwag (then he was told by the youths — \*raised-g obviative/plural\*): “Ambesino, a-daadidaa\! (come, let us play a game\!)” ogii-igoo (he was told). Wii’kaa sa ogii-debwetawaan (long it was before he assented). “O-oo-widi\! (off this way\!)” ogii-igoo (he was told). Mii dash geget (and so it truly was). O-oo-widash ogii-igoo (and this he was told). “Ambesino babimwa’-dagwedaa\! (come, let us shoot at the stick\! — \*the shoot-the-stick game; stem flagged\*)”

*Blessed by manitou power in general.*

(10) Mīsa gägä‘t. Cigwa mādci‘tāwag; ānic kāwin, mīsạgu pa‘kināguwāt ga‘kina uskīniniwag. Mīsa’ ga‘kina nībiwa pi‘kwa‘kōn ugīpa‘kināgänạn. O∙ō∙dạc ugī∙i∙nā: “Kāwīnsạgunā nisitam,” ugī∙i∙nā. O∙o∙mā kwaya‘k ayā.

**Mii sa’ geget. Zhigwa maajiitaawag; aaniish gaawiin, mii sa go bakinaagowaad gakina oshkiniiniwag. Mii sa’ gakina niibiwa bikwakoon ogii-bakinaagenan. O-oo dash ogii-inaa: “Gaawiin sa go nisidam,” ogii-inaa. O-oomaa gwayak ayaa.**

*And thus it truly happened. Presently they began; but it was no use, for by him were all the youths beaten. And so from them all he won many arrows. And this he said to them: “It is not so very interesting,” he said to them.*

Mii sa’ geget (and so it truly happened). Zhigwa maajiitaawag (presently they began); aaniish gaawiin (but it was no use), mii sa go bakinaagowaad gakina oshkiniiniwag (for all the youths were beaten by him). Mii sa’ gakina niibiwa bikwakoon ogii-bakinaagenan (and so from them all he won many arrows — \*bakinaage- ‘win at game’; bikwak ‘arrow’\*). O-oo dash ogii-inaa (and this he said to them): “Gaawiin sa go nisidam (it is not so very interesting — \*nisidam flagged\*),” ogii-inaa (he said to them). O-oomaa gwayak ayaa (right here it is — i.e. straight this way).

(11) Mīdạc ga‘kina uskīníni kīwīcāmāt ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ uskīniniwaᵍ. “Ạmbäsinō, aiyāngwāmisiyu‘k\! Mī∙o∙mā ayāt, kwaya‘k ayāt.” Cayīgwa kägä‘t. Mīdạc cigwa kā∙i∙nāt kī‘kagī‘kimāt ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ uwitcīwâgạnaᵉ: “Ạmbäsinō, aiyāngwāmisiyu‘k\! Gägä‘t tā∙u∙‘tāgä ‘aᵉa‘ᵘ wâ∙a∙‘tawạng. Kīcpîn pa‘kinawinạng kāwīn kīgatagwicinzīmin,” udināᵉ ‘iᵉi‘ᵘ uwītcīwâgạnaᵉ. “Kägä‘t wīnigu nimānänimā tcipa‘kinawa‘k, mīdạc wändciwīcāminạgu‘k. Wâbạng nāwa‘kwäg mī∙i∙ᵘ tcī∙u∙disạnk.”

**Mii dash gakina oshkiniini gii-wiisaamaad iw oshkiniiniwag. “Ambesino, ayaangwaamiziyok\! Mii o-omaa ayaad, gwayak ayaad.” Zhayiigwa geget. Mii dash zhigwa gaa-inaad gii-gagiikimaad iw owiijiiwaagana: “Ambesino, ayaangwaamiziyok\! Geget daa-o’taage a’aw waa-o’dawang. Giishpin bakinawinang gaawiin giga-dagwishinziimin,” odinaan iw owiijiiwaaganan. “Geget wiini go nimaanenimaa ji-bakinawag, mii dash wenji-wiisaaminagok. Waabang naawakweg mii iw ji-odisang.”**

*Thereupon then the youth got together all the young men. “Come, be eager\! (It is) off this way where he is, straight (in this direction is) where he is.” Thus it truly happened. Thereupon then he spoke to his comrades, giving them exhortation: “Come, show your skill\! Truly clever at games is he with whom we are going to play. If he wins from us, then we shall not come back home,” he said to his companions. “Truly confident am I that I can win from him, and that is why I have got you together (to go along). To-morrow at noon is when we shall get to where he is.”*

Mii dash gakina oshkiniini gii-wiisaamaad iw oshkiniiniwag (then the youth gathered together all the young men). “Ambesino, ayaangwaamiziyok\! (come, be eager / be careful\!) Mii o-omaa ayaad (this way is where he is), gwayak ayaad (straight there he is).” Zhayiigwa geget (and so it truly was). Mii dash zhigwa gaa-inaad gii-gagiikimaad iw owiijiiwaagana (then he spoke exhorting his comrades): “Ambesino, ayaangwaamiziyok\! (come, show your skill / be careful\!) Geget daa-o’taage a’aw waa-o’dawang (truly clever at games is the one we are to play with — \*o’taage-/waa-o’dawang flagged\*).
