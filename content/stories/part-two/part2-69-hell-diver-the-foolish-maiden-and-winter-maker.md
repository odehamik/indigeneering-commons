CINGIBIS MẠTCIKI‘KWÄWIS KAYÄ KĀBIBŌNU‘KÄ KAYÄ.

**ZHINGIBIS MACHIKIIKWEWIS GAYE GAABIBOONIKE GAYE.**

HELL-DIVER, THE FOOLISH MAIDEN, AND WINTER-MAKER.

*(Narrator not credited on the page in this range — left for M.)*

***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***

I.

(1) I‘kwäwạg pimusäwạg, mạdciki‘kwäwis; mīsa’ pimusäwāt, ningutingiku sāga∙i∙gạn äcimạdāpīwāt cingibisạn kībạbā∙ai∙yạgamōwạn. Mīsa’ äjipībāgimāt mạtciki‘kwäwis oᵍowidạc udinān: “Cingibis\! pīnāta∙u∙cinān\!” udinān.

**Ikwewag bimosewag, majikiikwewis; mii sa bimosewaad, ningoding igo zaaga’igan ezhi-madaabiiwaad zhingibisan gii-babaa-ayagamoowan. Mii sa ezhi-biibaagimaad majikiikwewis o’ow idash odinaan: “Zhingibis\! biinaada’oshinaan\!” odinaan.**

*Women were walking along, (one was) the Foolish Maiden;¹ and so, while walking along, they came in course of time out upon a lake, where (they saw) a Diver moving about over the water. Accordingly to him called the Foolish Maiden, and this she said to him: “O Diver\! come across the water and get us\!” she said to him.*

Ikwewag bimosewag (women were walking along), majikiikwewis (one was the Foolish Maiden — \*name; maji-ikwe-wis, flagged\*); mii sa bimosewaad (and so as they walked along), ningoding igo zaaga’igan ezhi-madaabiiwaad (at length they came out upon a lake) zhingibisan gii-babaa-ayagamoowan (where a Diver was moving about over the water — \*stem flagged\*). Mii sa ezhi-biibaagimaad majikiikwewis (and so the Foolish Maiden called to him) o’ow idash odinaan (and this she said to him): “Zhingibis\! biinaada’oshinaan\!” (“O Diver\! come across the water and fetch us\!” — \*stem flagged\*) odinaan (she said to him).

*¹ Jones (p671): “For there were two, and both were the Foolish Maidens.” Fiero name/stem flags: majikiikwewis (the Foolish Maiden; maji-ikwe-wis), gii-babaa-ayagamoowan (“moving about over the water”), biinaada’oshinaan (“come across and fetch us”) — all flagged.*

(2) Mīsa’ kāwin kạnạgä mātạgạmusīwạn.

**Mii sa gaawiin gananage madagamoosiiwan.**

*But no move at all would he make towards them.*

Mii sa gaawiin gananage (and not at all) madagamoosiiwan (would he stir toward them — \*stem flagged\*).

(3) Mīnawā‘ku tānakạnōnān, kāwin kanạgä ubisiki‘tākusīwān. Wī‘kāsa’ udäbwä‘tāgowān kanōnāwāt. Äcipōsīguwāt i∙i∙mā utcīmāninīng, mīsa’ kābimiskāwād. Kāwin kạnạgä i∙i∙witi äntāwāt wī∙i∙cāsī ‘aᵍa’ᵘ cingibis; ningutciku‘ icimādcī∙u∙. Mīsa’ i∙i∙mā ningutci päpātaciwâwīwit. Ānīc mīgu‘ku‘ kāniwâbạninigin pōsiwāt; kāwīn pisān aiyāsīwạg. Mīsagu’ a‘pạnä ningutci pạpai∙i∙yāt aᵘ cingibis, mīdạc kā∙i∙ci∙ai∙yāt.

**Miinawaa go ganoonaan, gaawiin gananage obizikii’taagosiiwaan. Wiikaa sa odebwe’taagowaan ganoonaawaad. Ezhi-boosigowaad imaa odjiimaaniniing, mii sa gaa-bimiskaawaad. Gaawiin gananage iwidi endaawaad wii-izhaasii a’aw zhingibis; ningoji go izhi-maajii’o. Mii sa imaa ningoji bebaadaajiwaawiiwid. Aaniish mii go go gaa-niwaabaninigin boosiwaad; gaawiin bizaan ayaasiiwag. Mii sa go apane ningoji babaa-ayaad aw zhingibis, mii dash gaa-izhi-ayaad.**

*Another time she spoke to him, but no answer at all did they get from him. After speaking to him for a long while, they were given heed. When they were let into his canoe, they then paddled away. Not even to where he (and others) dwelt did Diver wish to go; off in another direction over the water he started. Accordingly off over there he wandered with (the women) for his wives. Now, as often as the morning came, they would embark in their canoe; they spent no time leisurely (anywhere). And so roundabout in (various) places continued Diver, and such was the way he passed the time.*

Miinawaa go ganoonaan (again she spoke to him), gaawiin gananage obizikii’taagosiiwaan (but no answer at all did they get from him — \*stem flagged\*). Wiikaa sa odebwe’taagowaan ganoonaawaad (after a long while they were heeded when they spoke to him). Ezhi-boosigowaad imaa odjiimaaniniing (when they were taken into his canoe), mii sa gaa-bimiskaawaad (then they paddled away). Gaawiin gananage iwidi endaawaad wii-izhaasii a’aw zhingibis (not even to where he dwelt would the Diver go); ningoji go izhi-maajii’o (off in another direction he set out — \*stem flagged\*). Mii sa imaa ningoji bebaadaajiwaawiiwid (and so roundabout he wandered there with them for his wives — \*stem flagged\*). Aaniish mii go go gaa-niwaabaninigin boosiwaad (now, as often as morning came they would embark); gaawiin bizaan ayaasiiwag (they were not idle anywhere). Mii sa go apane ningoji babaa-ayaad aw zhingibis (and so the Diver kept wandering about from place to place), mii dash gaa-izhi-ayaad (and that was the way he passed the time).

(4) Ninguting pimāca∙o∙wāt sāga∙i∙gạning ogiwâbamāwān pimācagāmänit ma‘kwạn. Udinān ‘aᵍa’ᵘ mạtciki‘kwäwis: “Naskäginīna\! ma‘kwa kāpimācagāmät\!” udinān īni’ᵘ unābäman.

**Ningoding bimaazha’owaad zaaga’iganing ogii-waabamaawaan bimaashagaamenid makwan. Odinaan a’aw majikiikwewis: “Naska giniina\! makwa gaa-bimaashagaamed\!” odinaan iniw onaabeman.**

*Once, while paddling along the edge of the lake, they saw a bear coming along the shore. To (Diver) said the Foolish Maiden: “Oh, look\! yonder is a bear coming along the shore\!” she said to her husband.*

Ningoding bimaazha’owaad zaaga’iganing (once, as they paddled along the edge of the lake) ogii-waabamaawaan bimaashagaamenid makwan (they saw a bear coming along the shore). Odinaan a’aw majikiikwewis (the Foolish Maiden said to him): “Naska giniina\! makwa gaa-bimaashagaamed\!” (“oh, look\! a bear coming along the shore\!”) odinaan iniw onaabeman (she said to her husband).

(5) Cigwasa’ inābiwạn, “Ōᵘⁿ,” i‘kitōwạn, “nintai wīna∙a∙ᵘ.”

**Zhigwa sa inaabiwan, “Oonh,” ikidowan, “nindai wiin a’aw.”**

*When he looked, “Oh,” he said, “that is my pet.”*

Zhigwa sa inaabiwan (when he looked), “Oonh,” ikidowan (“oh,” he said), “nindai wiin a’aw.” (“that one is my pet.” — \*nindai “my pet/dog”\*).

(6) “Tạgaskumāguta, kạnōc\!” udinān.

**“Daga\[skumaaguda\], ganoozh\!” odinaan. \[opening particle cluster FLAGGED — see §18 same form\]**

*“Oh, do please speak to it\!” she said to him.*

“Daga … ganoozh\!” (“oh, do please speak to it\!” — \*opening particle cluster Tạgaskumāguta flagged; ganoozh = speak to him, imper.\*) odinaan (she said to him).

(7) Cigwasa’ gägä‘t ukạnōnānini, o∙ō’∙dạc udinān: “Kwạtc, kwạtc, kwạtc, kwạtc, ma‘kōnsim, nintai’\!” i‘kitōwạn īni’ᵘ unābämiwạn. Ānīc cigwa nōndāgōn äjiku‘pība‘tōt aᵘ ma‘kwa. Unawatcipinānini umi‘tigwābīni äci∙i∙na∙ä∙nit; kwaya‘k äci∙i∙na∙ä∙nit kwaya‘k imā kānījiku‘pība‘tōnit. Äjikabāwāt, mīsa’ kumā a‘pī ạnicingicinōn, kīnisānigwän. Äcinōsibītābānāwāt, “Kägätigunā∙ kīgama‘ku‘kämin,” i‘kitōwạg. Mīsa’ cigwa i∙i∙mā ạgwāwānā‘ku‘käwāt.

**Zhigwa sa geget oganoonaanini, o’ow idash odinaan: “Kwach, kwach, kwach, kwach, makoonsim, nindai\!” ikidowan iniw onaabemiwan. Aaniish zhigwa noondaagoon ezhi-gobiibatood aw makwa. Onawajibinaanini omitigwaabiini ezhi-ina’enid; gwayak ezhi-ina’enid gwayak imaa gaa-niizhi-gobiibatoonid. Ezhi-gabaawaad, mii sa goma apii ani-zhingishinon, gii-nisaanigwen. Ezhi-noozibiidaabaanaawaad, “Geget igonaa, gii-ga-mako’ke-min,” ikidowag. Mii sa zhigwa imaa agwaawanaa-’ke-waad.**

*In a while he truly spoke to the bear, and this he said to it: “Here, here, here, here, my little cub, my pet\!” (so) said their husband. Now, as soon as he was heard, then up from the lake ran the bear. Quickly seizing his bow (and arrow), (Diver) then shot; straight he let fly the arrow in the very direction where (he had seen the bear) go running up from the shore. Then they went ashore, and then some distance on (they found the bear) lying (on the ground), no doubt slain by (Diver). Then, drawing (the bear) down by the water, “Verily, now we shall have some bear-meat to eat,” they said. So then in a while they had a rack put up (on which to dry the meat).*

Zhigwa sa geget oganoonaanini (in a while he truly spoke to it), o’ow idash odinaan (and this he said to it): “Kwach, kwach, kwach, kwach, makoonsim, nindai\!” (“here, here, here, here, my little cub, my pet\!” — \*the call carried as printed; makoonsim “my little bear-cub,” nindai “my pet”\*) ikidowan iniw onaabemiwan (so said their husband). Aaniish zhigwa noondaagoon (now, as soon as he was heard) ezhi-gobiibatood aw makwa (the bear came running up from the water — \*stem flagged\*). Onawajibinaanini omitigwaabiini (quickly seizing his bow) ezhi-ina’enid (he let fly straight — \*stem flagged\*); gwayak ezhi-ina’enid gwayak (straight, right in the line) imaa gaa-niizhi-gobiibatoonid (where it had come running up — \*stem flagged\*). Ezhi-gabaawaad (when they went ashore), mii sa goma apii ani-zhingishinon (some way on it lay stretched out — \*stem flagged\*), gii-nisaanigwen (slain, no doubt — \*dubitative\*). Ezhi-noozibiidaabaanaawaad (drawing it down to the water — \*stem flagged\*), “Geget igonaa, gii-ga-mako’ke-min,” ikidowag (“truly, now we shall get bear-meat,” they said — \*verb flagged\*). Mii sa zhigwa imaa agwaawanaa-’ke-waad (and so there they set up a drying-rack — \*stem flagged\*).

*the bear-call “Kwạtc …” carried as printed (cf. the same call to bear/caribou, p674). HEAVY stem flags this section: äjiku‘pība‘tōt / kānījiku‘pība‘tōnit (run up from the water), äci∙i∙na∙ä∙nit (let fly the arrow), Äcinōsibītābānāwāt (draw down to the water), kīgama‘ku‘kämin (we shall have bear-meat), ạgwāwānā‘ku‘käwāt (set up the meat-rack — cf. p672 ‘ku‘kä flag). “Kägätigunā∙” carries the p672 high-dot GLYPH-FLAG.*

(8) Cayīgwa cingibis ugīmi‘kwändān kä∙i∙cictcigät pabā∙a∙yāt ‘aᵍa’ᵘ, mīsa’ äcipata‘kisitäcink. Mīsa’ kigicäp, “Kägätsa nīngagīgizitā,” udinān īni’ᵘ wīwạn säsī‘kisinit. “Tạga, wī‘kwạta∙ā∙n\!” udinān; mī∙i∙’ᵘ wīn cāpunigạnạn kīcägunạng i∙i∙mā usitāng.

**Zhayiigwa zhingibis ogii-mikwendaan gaa-izhi-izhichiged babaa-ayaad a’aw, mii sa ezhi-badakisideshing. Mii sa gigizheb, “Gegetsa nin-ga-giigizide,” odinaan iniw wiiwan zesii’kizinid. “Daga, wiikwada’an\!” odinaan; mii iw wiin zhaabooniganan gii-zhegonang imaa ozidaang.**

*Now, Diver thought of something he would do while (thus) continuing aimlessly about, whereupon he snagged his foot. Accordingly in the morning, “Truly lame shall I now be,” he said to his wife that was older. “I say, do try to get it out\!” he said to her; for some needles had he stuck into his foot.*

Zhayiigwa zhingibis ogii-mikwendaan (now Diver thought of something) gaa-izhi-izhichiged babaa-ayaad a’aw (that he would do as he wandered idly about), mii sa ezhi-badakisideshing (whereupon he snagged his foot — \*stem flagged\*). Mii sa gigizheb (and so in the morning), “Gegetsa nin-ga-giigizide,” (“truly lame shall I now be,” — \*giigizide “be lame,” flagged\*) odinaan iniw wiiwan zesii’kizinid (he said to his older wife — \*“older” stem flagged\*). “Daga, wiikwada’an\!” (“come, do try to pull it out\!”) odinaan (he said to her); mii iw wiin zhaabooniganan gii-zhegonang imaa ozidaang (for needles he had stuck into his foot — \*zhegonang flagged\*).

(9) Mīdạcisạn ä‘kitut ‘aᵍa’ᵘ: “Ningīgagwānisạgändānạn īni’u cingibisiwizitāsạn,” udinān.

**Mii dash isan ekidod a’aw: “Nin-gii-gagwaanisagendaanan iniw zhingibisiwizidaasan,” odinaan.**

*And so then said she: “I so much loathe the hateful foot of a Diver,” she said to him.*

Mii dash isan ekidod a’aw (and so then said she): “Nin-gii-gagwaanisagendaanan (“I so loathe them) iniw zhingibisiwizidaasan,” (those hateful Diver-feet,” — \*zhingibis + -wi- + -zide “foot” + pejorative + obv. pl.\*) odinaan (she said to him).

(10) Mīsa’, “Tạga kīn,” udinān īni’ᵘ ucīmämāwạnit,¹ “wī‘kwata∙ạ∙n.”

**Mii sa, “Daga giin,” odinaan iniw ojiimemaawanid, “wiikwada’an.”**

*Whereupon, “Then you,” he said to the younger sister, “you try to take it out.”*

Mii sa, “Daga giin,” (and so, “you then,”) odinaan iniw ojiimemaawanid (he said to the younger sister — \*form flagged, see fn\*), “wiikwada’an.” (“you try to pull it out.”)

(11) Mīsa’ cigwa gäga‘t ugitcigwātạmāwān usạtāning īni’ᵘ cāpunigạnan.

Mii sa zhigwa geget ogichigwaadamawaan (and so then she pulled them out for him) ozidaaning iniw zhaabooniganan (from his foot, the needles — \*gichigwaad- “pull out,” flagged light\*).

(12) “Nyā, taga nā kayä nīn ānint mīcicin\!” udinān īni’ᵘ ucīmäyạn. “Nyā, naskäbina\! ‘Aᵘ mānu, mīcicin\!” udinān īni’ᵘ ucīmäyạn.

(14) Kägätsa udinān ‘aᵍa’ᵘ mạtciki‘kwäwis: “Kunigä mīgu aᵘ,” ugī∙i∙nān; “o∙ō∙wisa’ äcipäcigūwạnän\!”
