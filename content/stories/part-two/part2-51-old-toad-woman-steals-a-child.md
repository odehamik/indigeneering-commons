OLD-TOAD-WOMAN STEALS A CHILD.

*Narrated by Wāsāgunäckạnk (Series III; Bois Fort). — narrator per Jones, Ojibwa Texts Part II, Introduction p.ix (not credited on the story page itself).*

***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***

(1) I‘kwä a∙ī∙ntā. Ininiwạn ninguting pīndigäwạn; mīsa’ ā‘pidci udānuwīpäcwâbạmigōn, mīdic iᵉi’ᵘ kāwin inändạnzī aᵉa∙i∙kwä.

**Ikwe ayiindaa. Ininiwan ningoding biindigewan; mii sa aapiji odaanoo-wii-bezhewaabamigoon, mii dash i’iw gaawiin inendanzii a’aw ikwe.**

*A woman was abiding (there). Once on a time (she beheld) a man come into (the wigwam); and though he was very eager to know her in a friendly way, yet the woman was not willing.*

Ikwe ayiindaa (a woman was living there — \*stem flagged\*). Ininiwan ningoding biindigewan (once a man came in to her); mii sa aapiji (and so very much) odaanoo-wii-bezhewaabamigoon (he was eager to be intimate / friendly with her — \*the bezhe-/pezhe- “lie with, be familiar with” stem flagged\*), mii dash i’iw (and so it was) gaawiin inendanzii a’aw ikwe (that the woman did not consent / was not willing).

(2) Käga‘pī mādcā aᵉa’ᵘ inini, umicōmisạn udicānạn. Kā‘tagwicing äjikanōnāt unicōmisạn: "Nimicō\! kägä‘t nimạmitāwäntạm ānuwipäcwâbạmạg a∙i∙‘kwä."

**Gegapii maajaa a’aw inini, omishoomisan odizhaanan. Gaa-dagwishing ezhi-ganoonaad omishoomisan: "Nimishoo\! geget nimamitaawendam aanoo-wii-bezhewaabamag ikwe."**

*At last away went the man, to his grandfather he went. After he was come there, he then spoke to his grandfather, saying: "O my grandfather\! in a truly troubled state of mind I am, because of my failure in trying to have intimate knowledge of a woman."*

Gegapii maajaa a’aw inini (at last the man set off), omishoomisan odizhaanan (he went to his grandfather). Gaa-dagwishing (when he had arrived) ezhi-ganoonaad omishoomisan (he spoke to his grandfather): "Nimishoo\! ("my grandfather\!) geget nimamitaawendam (truly I am troubled in mind) aanoo-wii-bezhewaabamag ikwe." (because I tried in vain to be intimate with a woman." — \*bezhewaabam- stem flagged as in §1\*).

(3) Mīdạc äjikīgitut a‘kiwäⁿzī: "Kāwin sạnạgạsinōn iᵉi’ᵘ tcibäcwâbạmạt." Mīdạc iᵉi’ᵘ nạntunigäwạn iᵉi’ᵘ umackimutāni; ānic pạckwäwạc wämaskimutānit.

**Mii dash ezhi-giigidod akiwenzii: "Gaawiin zanagizinoon i’iw ji-bezhewaabamad." Mii dash i’iw nandonigewan i’iw omashkimodaani; aaniish bashkweweg wemashkimodaanit.**

*Thereupon then spoke the old man, saying: "It is not difficult for you to be intimate with her." Accordingly then he sought for something in his bag; now a miserable old skin he had for a bag.*

Mii dash ezhi-giigidod akiwenzii (and so the old man spoke): "Gaawiin zanagizinoon ("it is not difficult) i’iw ji-bezhewaabamad." (for you to be intimate with her.") Mii dash i’iw nandonigewan (and so he searched in) i’iw omashkimodaani (his bag); aaniish bashkweweg (now it was a \[worn\] hide) wemashkimodaanit (that he had for a bag — \*“miserable old skin bag,” form flagged\*).

(4) Tcîgwa kägō ōmīnigōn ā‘pidci ạgāsāni. "Nōcis, mīsa oᵉoᵘ nimạcki‘kīm, mī∙o∙wä kä∙u∙ndcipäcwâbạmạt waᵉa’ᵘ i‘kwä. A‘pī kīpīndigawạt, — māmwätc äbi‘tā tibi‘kak mī a‘pī käpīndigäyan iᵘ wīgiwâm, — a‘pī kīpīndigäyan, āpa∙ạ∙n; mīdạc iᵉi’ᵘ udickutāming pạgidinạn iᵉi’ᵘ mạcki‘ki. Kīpạgidinạmạn, mīdạc iᵉi’ᵘ kä∙i∙cipōdawạyạn. Cigwa ta∙u∙nickā. Kāwin tạmi‘kawīsī. Ā‘pidci kāwin ickwä‘kạmig tạtōtạnzī." Mīdạc iᵉi’ᵘ a‘pī mīnawä päjig ominigōn iᵉi’ᵘ macki‘ki. " ‘Tạga, kīgạnạnāntawīn\!’ kīga∙i∙nā, ‘mīgu iᵉi’ᵘ a‘pạnä kä∙i∙ci∙a∙yäyạn kīcpīn käyābi wīcīngänimiyạn’, kīga∙i∙nā."

**Zhigwa gegoo omiinigoon aapiji agaasaani. "Noozhis, mii sa o’ow nimashki’kiim, mii ’ow ge-onji-bezhewaabamad wa’aw ikwe. Apii gii-biindigawad, — maamwich aabita dibikak mii apii ge-biindigeyan iw wiigiwaam, — apii gii-biindigeyan, aabaa’an; mii dash i’iw odishkodeming bagidinan i’iw mashki’ki. Gii-bagidinaman, mii dash i’iw ge-izhi-boodaweyan. Zhigwa da-onishkaa. Gaawiin da-mikawizii. Aapiji gaawiin ishkwe-akamig da-doodanzii." Mii dash i’iw apii miinawaa bezhig omiinigoon i’iw mashki’ki. " ‘Daga, giga-nanaandawiin\!’ giga-inaa, ‘mii i’iw apane ge-izhi-ayaayan giishpin geyaabi wiizhiingenimiyan’, giga-inaa."**

*Presently (the man) was given something that was very small. "My grandson, this is my (magic) medicine, and by means of this will you become intimate with the woman. After you have entered into where she is, but it must really be at midnight that you go into the wigwam,—when you have entered within, then untie it; accordingly then upon her fire do you drop the medicine. After you have dropped (the medicine) there, then is the time that you should start up the fire. Presently up from bed she will rise. She will not be in her right mind. She will do all manner of things." Thereupon then another kind of medicine he was given. ’Pray, let me heal you\!’ you shall say to her, ’for this is the way you shall always be, if you continue wanting to hate me,’ you shall say to her."*

Zhigwa gegoo omiinigoon aapiji agaasaani (presently he was given something very small). "Noozhis, mii sa o’ow nimashki’kiim ("my grandson, this is my medicine), mii ’ow ge-onji-bezhewaabamad wa’aw ikwe (and by this you will become intimate with that woman). Apii gii-biindigawad (when you have entered to her), — maamwich aabita dibikak (— it must really be at midnight) mii apii ge-biindigeyan iw wiigiwaam (that you go into the wigwam), — apii gii-biindigeyan, aabaa’an (— when you have entered, untie it); mii dash i’iw odishkodeming bagidinan i’iw mashki’ki (then drop the medicine on her fire). Gii-bagidinaman (when you have dropped it), mii dash i’iw ge-izhi-boodaweyan (then is when you should start up the fire). Zhigwa da-onishkaa (presently she will rise up). Gaawiin da-mikawizii (she will not be in her right mind). Aapiji gaawiin ishkwe-akamig da-doodanzii (she will do all manner of things — \*idiom flagged\*)." Mii dash i’iw apii miinawaa bezhig omiinigoon i’iw mashki’ki (then he was given one other medicine). " ‘Daga, giga-nanaandawiin\!’ (" ‘now, let me heal you\!’) giga-inaa (you shall say to her), ‘mii i’iw apane ge-izhi-ayaayan (‘this is how you will always be) giishpin geyaabi wiizhiingenimiyan’ (if you still want to hate me’), giga-inaa." (you shall say to her.")

(5) Mīsa iᵘ cigwa äcimādcāt a∙i∙nini, mīdạc i∙i∙’ᵘ äcī∙i∙cāt īniwi‘kwäwạn. Māmwätcigu uta‘kawātōn tci∙ā∙bi‘tātibi‘kạtinig. Tcigwa’ äbi‘tātibi‘kạtini äjipīndigät kīmōtc. Äcipạgitinạnk i∙i∙māⁿ ickutänk iᵉi’ᵘ macki‘ki, kuniginīn āmạnisutāgut; änigu‘k unickābạ‘tōwạn, pä‘kic pōdawäwạn äjikīgitunit: "Kāwin pō‘tc wī‘kā kitābäcwâbamisī."

**Mii sa iw zhigwa ezhi-maajaad a’aw inini, mii dash i’iw ezhi-izhaad iniw ikwewan. Maamwich igo oda-akawaadoon ji-aabita-dibika’adinig. Zhigwa aabita-dibika’adini ezhi-biindiged giimooj. Ezhi-bagidinang i’imaa ishkodeng i’iw mashki’ki, goniginiin amanisotaagod; aniigook onishkaaba’toowan, baa’ikizh boodawewan ezhi-giigidonid: "Gaawiin booch wiikaa gidaa-bezhewaabamisii."**

*Accordingly then departed the man, whereupon he went to where the woman was. To make certain, he waited till it was midnight. When it was midnight, then he went secretly into where she was. When he dropped the medicine upon the fire, behold, the sound he made was heard by her; straightway up she leaped from her couch, and at the same time she kindled the fire. Then she spoke, saying: "Never will it be possible for you to have intimate knowledge of me."*

Mii sa iw zhigwa ezhi-maajaad a’aw inini (and so then the man departed), mii dash i’iw ezhi-izhaad iniw ikwewan (and went to that woman). Maamwich igo oda-akawaadoon (he waited deliberately) ji-aabita-dibika’adinig (until it was midnight). Zhigwa aabita-dibika’adini (when it was midnight) ezhi-biindiged giimooj (he went in secretly). Ezhi-bagidinang i’imaa ishkodeng i’iw mashki’ki (when he dropped the medicine there on the fire), goniginiin amanisotaagod (behold, the sound was heard by her — \*amaniso- “be startled by a sound” flagged\*); aniigook onishkaaba’toowan (at once she leapt up from her bed), baa’ikizh boodawewan (and at the same time kindled the fire — \*baa’ikizh flagged\*) ezhi-giigidonid (and spoke): "Gaawiin booch wiikaa gidaa-bezhewaabamisii." ("never at all will you be able to be intimate with me.")

(6) Nawạndicigu udānugạnōnān: "Pō‘tcigu kawītigämin."

**Nawaj idigo odaanoo-ganoonaan: "Booch igo giga-wiidigemin."**

*Yet, despite (what she said), he tried in vain to speak to her, saying: "I am determined to marry you."*

Nawaj idigo odaanoo-ganoonaan (despite this he tried in vain to speak to her — \*the nawandi-/aanoo- “in vain, despite” form flagged\*): "Booch igo giga-wiidigemin." ("I am bound to marry you.")

(7) Mi‘tig udōtā‘pinạmini wīpạki‘tä∙u∙gut.

**Mitig odoodaapinamini wii-bakite’ogod.**

*A club then she picked up to strike him with.*

Mitig odoodaapinamini (she picked up a stick / club) wii-bakite’ogod (intending to strike him with it).

(8) Intawä äjisāga∙ạ∙nk. Mīdạc imāⁿ agwạtcing äjiwawänabit. Kunāgu a‘pī cigwa kä∙i∙ciki‘känimāt wạnimi‘kawinit, mīdạc iᵉi’ᵘ äcipīndigät a∙i∙nini; kägä‘t owâbạmān kiwạnātisinit. Mīsa imāⁿ

**Indawaa ezhi-zaaga’ang. Mii dash imaa agwajiing ezhi-wawenabid. Gonaagoo apii zhigwa gaa-izhi-gikenimaad waniminikawinid, mii dash i’iw ezhi-biindiged a’aw inini; geget owaabamaan giwanaadizinid. Mii sa imaa**

*Thereupon out of doors then he went. And so there outside he sat down. Some time afterwards, when he knew that she was out of her wits, then it was that inside went the man; of a truth, he beheld her becoming beside herself.*

Indawaa ezhi-zaaga’ang (so he went out of doors). Mii dash imaa agwajiing ezhi-wawenabid (and there outside he sat down). Gonaagoo apii zhigwa (some time afterward, when) gaa-izhi-gikenimaad waniminikawinid (he knew that she had become deranged — \*wani-minikawi- “lose one’s senses” flagged\*), mii dash i’iw ezhi-biindiged a’aw inini (then the man went in); geget owaabamaan giwanaadizinid (truly he saw her becoming beside herself). (sentence continues onto p430:) Mii sa imaa…

(9) cigwa kīwī‘pämāt. Kāwin mi‘kawisīwạn. Mīdạc iᵉi’ᵘ kä∙i∙ckwā‘tāt, utāpa∙ā∙n umacki‘kīm; pạngī äciminānta∙ā∙t. Kägä‘t pạga‘kātisīwạn ugạnōnān. Mīsa iᵘ cigwạ nānāgạtawäntạnk, "Kägu’ käyābi cīngänimici‘kän. Kīcpīn käyābi wīcīngänimiyạn mīya‘pạnä mini‘k käbimātisiyạn kāwin kīgami‘kawisī," udinān.

**zhigwa giiwi-bamaad. Gaawiin mikawizii-wan. Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-ishkwaataad, odaapa’aan omashki’kiim; bangii ezhi-minaandaa’aad. Geget bagakaadizii-wan oganoonaan. Mii sa iw zhigwa nanaagadawendang, "Gego geyaabi zhiingenimishikan. Giishpin geyaabi wiizhiingenimiyan, miya-bane minik ge-bimaadiziyan gaawiin giga-mikawizii," odinaan.**

*And so then it was that he lay in the couch with her. She did not come back to her senses. Thereupon, when he was done, he untied his medicine; a little of it he made her smell. In truth, while she was yet in a wandering state of mind, he spoke to her. Accordingly, when she began to collect her thoughts, "Don’t you ever hate me any more. If you wish to hate me still, then as long as you live you shall never be in a right state of mind," he said to her.*

(…continuing from §8) zhigwa giiwi-bamaad (then he lay with her — \*the kiiwi-/giiwi- “lie alongside” reading flagged\*). Gaawiin mikawiziiwan (she did not come to her senses). Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhi-ishkwaataad (and so when he was finished), odaapa’aan omashki’kiim (he untied his medicine); bangii ezhi-minaandaa’aad (a little of it he made her smell). Geget bagakaadiziiwan oganoonaan (truly, while she was still wandering in mind, he spoke to her — \*bagakaadizi- “be clear/sound in mind” here in the negative sense flagged\*). Mii sa iw zhigwa nanaagadawendang (and so when she began to collect her thoughts), "Gego geyaabi zhiingenimishikan ("don’t hate me any more). Giishpin geyaabi wiizhiingenimiyan (if you still want to hate me), miya-bane minik ge-bimaadiziyan (then for as long as you live) gaawiin giga-mikawizii," (you will never be in your right mind,") odinaan (he said to her).

(10) Äjikīgitut aī‘kwä: "Mīgu iᵘ kä∙i∙ciwīdigäminān. Nawạtc nintācīngätān tcimi‘kawisiwān."

**Ezhi-giigidod a’aw ikwe: "Mii go iw ge-izhi-wiidigeminaan. Nawaj nindaa-zhiingendaan ji-mikawiziiwaan."**

*Then spoke the woman, saying: "Therefore then I will marry you. More would I dislike to be out of my right mind."*

Ezhi-giigidod a’aw ikwe (then the woman spoke): "Mii go iw ge-izhi-wiidigeminaan ("so then I will marry you). Nawaj nindaa-zhiingendaan (more would I hate) ji-mikawiziiwaan." (to be out of my right mind." — \*here the contrary sense of mikawi- “be in/out of one’s senses” flagged\*).

(11) Mīsa’ gägä‘t cigwa kīwīdigämāt. Kumāgu mini‘k äyāwāt cigwa ạbinōtciyan udayāwān a∙i∙‘kwä. Ānic nīciwaᵉ ạnimōcaᵉ äyāwāwāt. Ānic wīn ạbinōtci udayäⁿsaᵉ iᵉi’ᵘ ạnimōcaᵉ.

**Mii sa geget zhigwa gii-wiidigemaad. Gomaa go minik ayaawaad zhigwa abinoojiiyan odayaawaan a’aw ikwe. Aaniish niizhiwag animooshag ayaawaawaad. Aaniish wiin abinoojii odayenzan i’iw animooshag.**

*And so, in truth, then he married her. When for some time they had lived (together), then a baby the woman had. Now, two were the dogs they owned. Now, the child itself had the dogs for pets.*

Mii sa geget zhigwa gii-wiidigemaad (and so in truth he married her). Gomaa go minik ayaawaad (when they had lived together a while) zhigwa abinoojiiyan odayaawaan a’aw ikwe (the woman then had a baby). Aaniish niizhiwag animooshag ayaawaawaad (now, two dogs they had). Aaniish wiin abinoojii odayenzan i’iw animooshag (now the child itself had the dogs for its pets — \*odayenzan “little pet” flagged\*).

(12) Ā‘pidci ayä‘pitänti aᵉa’ᵘ inini kīyusät. Ningutingigu mādcāt a∙i∙nini. Kānimādcānit īni’ᵘ unāpämạn, kayä wīn uci‘tā wīmạnisät aᵉa’ᵘ i‘kwä; äjimādcāt, ānic ta‘kubisōwạn īni’ᵘ ugwisäⁿsạn; mīgu iᵉi’ᵘ a‘kawä intawä udōntcikanān pīndig īni’ᵘ unītcānisạn. Kä∙i∙citinä utawimạnōnạn īni’ᵘ umisimạn. Patạgwicink awäniban ugwisîsạn; ānugīwi‘tānạndu∙ạ∙tcigä. Mīsa’ wạni∙ā∙t īniyōgwisäⁿsạn, mīsa i∙i∙’ᵘ tci∙ä∙nigu‘k äjimawit, kạbägijik mawi. Änitạgwicink aᵉa’ᵘ īnini wīwạn mạdwämawiwạn. Änipīndigät wâbạmigōn wīwạn gīgitōwạn: "Mīsā‘pạnä kīngwisänān kīwạni∙a∙g."

**Aapiji aye’abidendi a’aw inini giiyosed. Ningodingigo maajaad a’aw inini. Gaa-ani-maajaanid iniw onaabeman, gaye wiin ozhiitaa wii-manised a’aw ikwe; ezhi-maajaad, aaniish dakobizowan iniw ogwisensan; mii go i’iw akawe indawaa odoonjikanaan biindig iniw oniijaanisan. Gaa-izhi-dinaa odawimanoonan iniw omisiman. Bi-dagwishing aweniban ogwisensan; aanoo-giiwi-daanandowaajige. Mii sa wani’aad iniw ogwisensan, mii sa i’iw ji-eni-go izhi-mawid, gabe-giizhig mawi. Ani-dagwishing a’aw inini wiiwan madwe-mawiwan. Ani-biindiged owaabamigoon wiiwan giigidowan: "Mii sa baane gingwisenaan giiwani’ag."**

*A very long while was the man absent from home when on the hunt for game. And once on a time away went the man. After her husband had departed, the woman too made ready to go gather fire-wood; when she departed, (she left) her little son strapped (to the cradle-board); now, it was only for a little while that she left her child inside (the lodge). Speedily she went to gather her fire-wood. On her return, gone was her little son; in vain roundabout she sought for tracks, but without avail. Therefore then she had lost her little son, whereupon bitterly she wept, throughout the whole day she wept. When the man was returning home, he heard the voice of his wife crying. When he went inside the lodge, he was seen by his wife, who spoke, saying: "And now gone is our dear son, whom I have lost."*

Aapiji aye’abidendi a’aw inini giiyosed (the man was very long absent hunting — \*aye’abidendi form flagged\*). Ningodingigo maajaad a’aw inini (and once the man set off). Gaa-ani-maajaanid iniw onaabeman (after her husband had gone), gaye wiin ozhiitaa wii-manised a’aw ikwe (the woman too got ready to gather firewood); ezhi-maajaad (when she left), aaniish dakobizowan iniw ogwisensan (her little son was strapped \[to the cradle-board\]); mii go i’iw akawe indawaa odoonjikanaan biindig iniw oniijaanisan (so just for a little while she left her child inside — \*odoonjikanaan “leave behind” flagged\*). Gaa-izhi-dinaa odawimanoonan iniw omisiman (in haste she went to gather her firewood — \*odawimanoonan form flagged\*). Bi-dagwishing aweniban ogwisensan (on her return her little son was gone); aanoo-giiwi-daanandowaajige (in vain she searched all about for tracks — \*form flagged\*). Mii sa wani’aad iniw ogwisensan (so she had lost her little son), mii sa i’iw ji-eni-go izhi-mawid (and so she just kept on weeping), gabe-giizhig mawi (all day long she wept). Ani-dagwishing a’aw inini (as the man came home) wiiwan madwe-mawiwan (he heard his wife crying). Ani-biindiged (as he went in) owaabamigoon wiiwan (he was seen by his wife) giigidowan (who spoke): "Mii sa baane gingwisenaan giiwani’ag." ("and now our dear little son is gone, whom I have lost.")
