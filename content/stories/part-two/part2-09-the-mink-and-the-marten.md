## **9. THE MINK AND THE MARTEN.**

## **SHAANGWESHI WA(A)BIZHESHI GAYE.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).*</span>

</div>


*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoding kiinwanh shaangweshi; pimaazhagaamebatoo ningoding idash owa(a)bamaan kiingoonyan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">There was once a Mink; he was running along the shore, when of a sudden he saw a fish.</span>

</div>


Ningoding kiinwanh (once, so the story goes) shaangweshi (a Mink); pimaazhagaamebatoo (he ran along the shore) ningoding idash (and at one point) owaabamaan kiingoonyan (he saw a fish).

2)  > **Ogosaan idash aanawi miigaanaad; apiji omisawenimaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, he feared it, but he was loath to fight it; with much desire he longed for (the fish).</span>

</div>


Ogosaan idash (now he feared it) aanawi miigaanaad (but was unwilling to fight it); apiji omisawenimaan (greatly he longed for it).

3)  > **"Apegish amwag," inendam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"I wish that I might eat it\!" he thought.</span>

</div>


“Apegish amwag (“would that I might eat it\!”), inendam (he thought).

4)  > **"Aaniin ge-doodamaan chi-nisag?" inendam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"What shall I do to kill it?" he thought.</span>

</div>


“Aaniin ge-doodamaan (“what shall I do) chi-nisag?” (to kill it?”) inendam (he thought).

5)  > **Ki-gakiiwebato dash.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Then he went running across the point of land.</span>

</div>


Ki-gakiiwebato dash (then he went running across the point).

6)  > **Miinawaa dash owa(a)baman kischi-kinoozhen, mii idash enaad:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">So next he saw a large Pike, and this he said to it:</span>

</div>


Miinawaa dash owaabaman kischi-kinoozhen (then next he saw a great Pike), mii idash enaad (and this he said to it):

7)  > **"Kischi-oogaa ningii-wa(a)bamaa omaanh awasi-gakiiwe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"A large Pickerel I saw over at the other end of this portage.</span>

</div>


“Kischi-oogaa (“a great Pickerel) ningii-waabamaa (I saw) omaanh awasi-gakiiwe (here at the other end of the portage).

8)  > **Anooj kidig, kigiikaamig isa go."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">All sorts of things is he saying about you; indeed, he is telling unpleasant things about you."</span>

</div>


Anooj kidig (all sorts of things he says of you), kigiikaamig isa go.” (indeed he speaks unpleasantly of you.”)

9)  > **dash ekidot a'aw oogoo:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon said Pickerel:</span>

</div>


dash ekidot a’aw oogoo (thereupon said that Pickerel):

10) > **"Aaniin ezhimid, a'aw mayaanaadisid naasawedaamikang?"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Why is he talking about me, that ugly creature of pendulous chin?"</span>

</div>


“Aaniin ezhimid (“why does he speak so of me), a’aw mayaanaadisid (that ugly creature) naasawedaamikang?” (with the pendulous chin?”)

11) > **Miinawaa desh gii-gakiiwebato.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Then he went running back over the portage.</span>

</div>


Miinawaa desh gii-gakiiwebato (then again he went running across the portage).

12) > **Mii dash miinawaa enaad ogaawan:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so again he said to Pickerel:</span>

</div>


Mii dash miinawaa enaad ogaawan (and so again he said to Pickerel):

13) > **"Kischi-ginoozhe ayaa omaanh awesi-gakiiwe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"There is a big Pike over at the other end of this portage.</span>

</div>


“Kischi-ginoozhe ayaa (“a great Pike is) omaanh awesi-gakiiwe (here at the other end of the portage).

14) > **Anooj gidig mayaanaadisid wekaniwid."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">All sorts of things is the ugly bony creature saying about you."</span>

</div>


Anooj gidig (all sorts of things he says of you) mayaanaadisid wekaniwid.” (that ugly bony creature.”)

15) > **Niibawa niibawatasing kii-gakiiwebato, anooj kii-a\[y\]inaajimod.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Many a time back and forth he went running over the portage, all sorts of things was he reporting.</span>

</div>


Niibawa niibawatasing (many times over and over) kii-gakiiwebato (he ran across the portage), anooj kii-ayinaajimod (all sorts of things reporting).

16) > **Kegapii idash kii-ikido:  
    > **And then finally (Pike) said:

Kegapii idash kii-ikido (and finally he said):

17) > **"Ningad-awi-miigaanaa."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"I will go fight him."</span>

</div>


“Ningad-awi-miigaanaa.” (“I will go and fight him.”)

18) > **Mii dash ki(i)noozhe kaa-izhi-kiiwitaakoshiwed.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so Pike swam around to the place,</span>

</div>


Mii dash kiinoozhe kaa-izhi-kiiwitaakoshiwed (and so Pike swam around to the place).

19) > **Kaye wiin idash shaangweshi kii-gakiiwebato, me idash miigaadiwaad ki(i)noozhe oogaa gaye.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">\[And Mink went running over the portage,\] whereupon with each other fought Pike and Pickerel.</span>

</div>


Kaye wiin idash shaangweshi kii-gakiiwebato (and Mink for his part ran across the portage), me idash miigaadiwaad (whereupon they fought one another) kiinoozhe oogaa gaye (Pike and Pickerel).

20) > **Shaangweshi dash ooganawa(a)bamaan miigaadi(i)nit.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, Mink watched them as they fought.</span>

</div>


Shaangweshi dash ooganawaabamaan (now Mink watched them) miigaadinit (as they fought).

21) > **Apii idash weyabamaad nisidinit, mii iwe ezhi-nagamod: —**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when he saw them killing each other, this was the song he sang: —</span>

</div>


Apii idash weyabamaad nisidinit (and when he saw them killing each other), mii iwe ezhi-nagamod (this is the song he sang): —

22) > **"Poonii'idiyok, kaa-mi(i)skwiwaapi(i)naniti(i)m\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Leave each other alone, you will draw blood."</span>

</div>


“Poonii’idiyok (“leave one another be), kaa-miskwiwaapiinanitiim\!” (you will draw blood from each other\!”)

23) > **Apii idash kaa-nisidinit mii iw kii-ayagwaadaabaanaad, wibakamigaang ogi-paabiindiganaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And after they had killed each other, he then drew them out of the water, into a little hold he fetched and put them.</span>

</div>


Apii idash kaa-nisidinit (and after they had killed each other) mii iw kii-ayagwaadaabaanaad (then he drew them out of the water), wibakamigaang (into a little hollow) ogi-paabiindiganaan (he fetched and put them).

24) > **Mii idash i(i)manh ka-iindaad ki(i)nwanj.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so there in that place he lived for a long time.</span>

</div>


Mii idash iimanh ka-iindaad kiinwanj (and so there he lived for a long time).

25) > **Apii idash eni-pi(i)boonining, ningoding ogii-nagishkawaan wa(a)bizheshiwan, mii idash ekidoowaad maamawi chi-daawaad pi(i)pooninig.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when winter came on, he once met Marten, whereupon they declared that they would live together during the winter.</span>

</div>


Apii idash eni-piboonining (and as winter was coming on), ningoding ogii-nagishkawaan waabizheshiwan (once he met Marten), mii idash ekidoowaad (whereupon they agreed) maamawi chi-daawaad piipooninig (to live together for the winter).

26) > **Me dash keget kii-oshitoowaad endaawaad; me idash i(i)manh kaa-onji-maamaajaawaad nandawenjigewaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">So thereupon, indeed, they built a place where to dwell; and so from thence they departed when they went to hunt for game.</span>

</div>


Me dash keget kii-oshitoowaad endaawaad (so indeed they built where they would dwell); me idash iimanh kaa-onji-maamaajaawaad (and so from there they set out) nandawenjigewaad (when they went hunting for game).

27) > **Wa(a)bizheshi wiin, wa(a)boozoon onooji'aan, pi(i)newan, ajidamoon, awa(a)biganoojii'an.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">As for the Marten, he killed the hares, ruffed grouse, squirrels, rats.</span>

</div>


Waabizheshi wiin (Marten for his part), waaboozoon onooji’aan (hunted hares), piinewan (ruffed grouse), ajidamoon (squirrels), awabiganoojii’an (rats).

28) > **Wiin dash shaangwenzhi, kiingoonyan eta onoojii'aan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And as for Mink, fishes only he killed.</span>

</div>


Wiin dash shaangwenzhi (and Mink for his part), kiingoonyan eta onoojii’aan (only fish he hunted).

29) > **Aanaawi dash ashandiiwag, pangii wa(a)bizheshi, kaa wiika odashamaaziin shaangwashiwan wa(a)boos oshti(i)gwaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Even though they gave each other food, yet but a little did Marten (give), never did he give Mink a rabbit's head to eat.</span>

</div>


Aanaawi dash ashandiiwag (even though they fed each other), pangii waabizheshi (Marten only a little), kaa wiika odashamaaziin shaangwashiwan (never did he feed Mink) waaboos oshtigwaan (a rabbit’s head).

30) > **Shaangweshi idash mindawe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so Mink became discontented.</span>

</div>


Shaangweshi idash mindawe (and so Mink grew discontented).

31) > **Kaye wiin idash shaangweshi kaawiin odashamaasiin wa(a)bizheshiiwan ozhigwanan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And now Mink did not even feed Marten a fish-tail.</span>

</div>


Kaye wiin idash shaangweshi (and Mink for his part) kaawiin odashamaasiin waabizheshiiwan ozhigwanan (did not feed Marten so much as a fish-tail).

32) > **Kaye wiin dash wa(a)bizheshi mi(i)ndawe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And as for himself, Marten grew sulky.</span>

</div>


Kaye wiin dash waabizheshi miindawe (and Marten for his part grew sulky).

33) > **Paanimaa dash ki(i)schi-kiiwedininig mii iw kabaatood oshtigwa(a)nan, kaawiin dash odashamaasiin shaangweshiwan ozhigwanan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, after a while a great wind blew from the north, and that was when he was cooking some heads, but he did not feed Mink with a tail.</span>

</div>


Paanimaa dash kiischi-kiiwedininig (now after a while a great north wind blew) mii iw kabaatood oshtigwaanan (that was when he was cooking heads), kaawiin dash odashamaasiin shaangweshiwan ozhigwanan (but he did not feed Mink a tail).

34) > **Ningoding idash kischi-kiiwediininig, me idash saaga'ang shaangweshi.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, once a great wind was blowing from the north, when out of doors went Mink.</span>

</div>


Ningoding idash kischi-kiiwediininig (now once a great north wind was blowing), me idash saaga’ang shaangweshi (whereupon out of doors went Mink).

35) > **"E'e, kichi-noodi(i)n\!" ikido.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Oh, a great wind\!" he said.</span>

</div>


“E’e, kichi-noodiin\!” (“oh, a great wind\!”) ikido (he said).

36) > **"Aanindi wendi(i)ng?" ikido wa(a)bizheshi.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"From whence is the wind blowing?" said Marten.</span>

</div>


“Aanindi wendiing?” (“from where is it blowing?”) ikido waabizheshi (said Marten).

37) > **"Pandaaboshtigwaaning i(i)sa ondaanimat."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"From the hare's head, of course, blows the wind."</span>

</div>


“Pandaaboshtigwaaning iisa ondaanimat.” (“from the hare’s head, of course, the wind blows.”)

38) > **Ningonding idash gaye wiin wa(a)bizheshi agwajiing izhaa,**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And presently Marten too went outside.</span>

</div>


Ningonding idash gaye wiin waabizheshi (and presently Marten too) agwajiing izhaa (went outside).

39) > **"E'e kichi-noodin\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Oh, a big wind\!" (said he).</span>

</div>


“E’e kichi-noodin\!” (“oh, a great wind\!”)

40) > **"Aanindi wending?"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"From whence is the wind blowing?"</span>

</div>


“Aanindi wending?” (“from where is it blowing?”)

41) > **"Ozhigwanesan sa ko kiimiijing mii iwidi wendaanimak," ikido w(a)abizheshi.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"From that part of the fish-tail (usually) eaten is of course the place from whence the wind is blowing\!" said Marten.</span>

</div>


“Ozhigwanesan sa ko kiimiijing (“from the little fish-tail that’s usually eaten) mii iwidi wendaanimak,” (that’s where the wind blows from\!”) ikido waabizheshi (said Marten).

42) > **Apii idash eni-niniibininig mii iw gii-pakewinidiiwaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, when summer came on, they separated from each other.</span>

</div>


Apii idash eni-niniibininig (now when summer was coming on) mii iw gii-pakewinidiiwaad (then they parted from each other).

43) > **Mii sa ekoosit.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">That is as far as (the story) goes.</span>

</div>


Mii sa ekoosit (that is as far as it goes).
