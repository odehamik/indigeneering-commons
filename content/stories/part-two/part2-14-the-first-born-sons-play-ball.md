## **14.MAJIIKIWISAG GAA-BAAGA’ADOWEWAAD.**

## **THE FIRST-BORN SONS PLAY BALL.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > Ningudingsa kīnwän kīdāwạg mạdcī‘kiwisạg; udätōwạg; ā‘pidci kistciōdäna ī·i·mān ayāwāt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding sa giiwen' gii-daawag majiikiwisag; oodetoowag; aapiji gichi-oodena imaa ayaawaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Once on a time, as the story goes, there lived some first-born sons; in a town they dwelt; exceedingly large was the town where they were.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ningoding sa giiwen’ (once on a time, they say) gii-daawag majiikiwisag (there lived some first-born sons); oodetoowag (they had a town); aapiji gichi-oodena (very large was the town) imaa ayaawaad (where they were).

2)  > Ạnōdc ijitcigäwạg udạminōwāt; tạsing kājigadinigin udaminōwạg.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Anooj izhichigewag odaminoowaad; dasing gaazhigadinigin odaminowag.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*All sorts of things they did in the way of games; as often as the days came round, they played at games.*</span>

</div>


Anooj izhichigewag odaminoowaad (all sorts of things they did in the way of play); dasing gaazhigadinigin (as often as the days came round) odaminowag (they played).

3)  > Ningudingidạc unākunigä ‘agaw'u mạdcī‘kiwis tci·a·‘tādiwād tcibāga·ā·dowäwād.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding idash onaakonige a'aw majiikiwis ji-ataadiwaad ji-baaga'adowewaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Now, once (one of) the first-born announced that there would be a ball-game.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ningoding idash onaakonige (now once he made the arrangement) a’aw majiikiwis (that first-born) ji-ataadiwaad (that they would have a contest) ji-baaga’adowewaad (that they would play ball).

4)  > Mīdạc gäegä‘t äjimādcitāwāt wī·a·‘tādiwāt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash geget ezhi-maajitaawaad wii-ataadiwaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Whereupon truly began they to get ready for the contest.*</span>

</div>


Mii dash geget ezhi-maajitaawaad (and truly so they began getting ready) wii-ataadiwaad (to have the contest).

5)  > Kayä wīn ‘aga'u päejik mạdcī‘kiwis päpạngī pa‘kān aiyendiwạg.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Gaye wiin a'aw bezhig majiikiwis bebangii bakaan *ayiyendiwag*(?).**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Now, another first-born (and his friends) did a little differently.*</span>

</div>


Gaye wiin a’aw bezhig majiikiwis (and another first-born too) bebangii bakaan (a little differently) ayiyendiwag (they were minded — \*fiero printed with editorial query\*).

6)  > Ābi‘ta ändạciwāt uwīwītcīwâwān wīpāga·ā·towäwāt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Aabita endazhiwaad owii-wiijiiwaawaan wii-baaga'adowewaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Half of them were on one side to play ball (against the other half).*</span>

</div>


Aabita endazhiwaad (half of them on their side) owii-wiijiiwaawaan (would go along with them) wii-baaga’adowewaad (to play ball).

7)  > ‘Agau päjig mạdcī'kiwis ugī·u·ndinān pigwā'kwạt wā·ā·bạdci‘tōwād, ujāwackuminạgạt ī·i·'u bigwā‘kwạt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**A'aw bezhig majiikiwis ogii-ondinaan bikwaakwad waa-aabajitoowaad, ozhaawashkominagad i'iw bikwaakwad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*One of the first-born took out the ball which they were to use, blue was the color of the ball.*</span>

</div>


A’aw bezhig majiikiwis (one of the first-born) ogii-ondinaan bikwaakwad (took out the ball) waa-aabajitoowaad (which they were to use), ozhaawashkominagad i’iw bikwaakwad (blue in color was that ball).

8)  > Mīdạc ä‘kidot ‘aga'u mạdcī'kiwis:


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash ekidod a'aw majiikiwis:**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon said the first-born:***  </span>
> <span class="l-fiero">**</span>

</div>


Mii dash ekidod a’aw majiikiwis (thereupon said that first-born):

9)  > "Wâbạnūng nīn nīngạtinạgatō," i‘kido.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Waabanoong niin ningad-inagadoo," ikido,**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Towards the east will I play for goal." he said.*</span>

</div>


“Waabanoong niin (“towards the east I) ningad-inagadoo,” (will play for goal,”) ikido (he said).

10) > "Kīnidạc," udinān īni'u wā·a·‘tawāt, "nīngābī·a·nung ina‘kakäyā."


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Giin idash," odinaan iniw waa-atawaad, "ningaabii'anong inakakeyaa."**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"And you," he said to them against whom he was to play, "toward the west."  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Giin idash,” (“and you,”) odinaan iniw waa-atawaad (he said to those he was to play against), “ningaabii’anong inakakeyaa.” (“toward the west.”)

11) > Mīdạc kī‘kagīgi·i·nāwāt kägicī‘kānit ininiwag.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii idash gii-gagiiginaawaad ge-gizhiikaanid ininiwag.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Accordingly they picked out the men that were fleet of foot.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii idash gii-gagiiginaawaad (accordingly they picked out) ge-gizhiikaanid ininiwag (the men that would be fleet of foot).

12) > Weyâbang kigijäb kīmādci‘tāwạg.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Weyaabang gigizheb gii-maajitaawag.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*On the morning of the next day they began (playing).  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Weyaabang gigizheb (on the morning of the next day, early) gii-maajitaawag (they began).

13) > Mēdạc cigwa nimbawāwāt, kāwīn nayānj awiya mijạgạdōsī.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash zhigwa nimbawaawaad, gaawiin nayaanzh awiya mizhagadoosii.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*And when they started the ball going, it was a long while before any one could make a goal.*</span>

</div>


Mii dash zhigwa nimbawaawaad (and so now they set the ball going), gaawiin nayaanzh awiya mizhagadoosii (for a long while no one could make a goal).

14) > Änī·i·ckwānā·u·‘kwag mī cigwa cāgōdci·i·nt, ocāgōdci·i·gōn īni'u pipōnisän.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Eni-ishkwaanaa'okweg mii zhigwa zhaagooji'ind, ozhaagooji'igoon iniw biboonisen.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Along in the afternoon was when (one of the first-born) was being beaten, he was being beaten by Winter-Wind.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Eni-ishkwaanaa’okweg (along in the afternoon) mii zhigwa zhaagooji’ind (then was one being beaten), ozhaagooji’igoon iniw biboonisen (he was being beaten by Winter-Wind).

15) > Käga‘pī kī·ạ·nicāgōdciwinā ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis, wīn dạc pipōnisä kīmījạgadō ningābī·a·nunk ina‘kakä.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii gii-ani-zhaagooji'winaa a'aw majiikiwis, wiin dash biboonise gii-mizhagadoo ningaabii'anong inagake.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*At last was the first-born being beaten, for Winter-Wind had made a goal on the side toward the west.*</span>

</div>


Gegapii gii-ani-zhaagooji’winaa a’aw majiikiwis (at last that first-born was being beaten), wiin dash biboonise (and Winter-Wind for his part) gii-mizhagadoo ningaabii’anong inagake (made a goal at the side toward the west).

16) > A‘pā·i·dạc kāpa‘kinawint ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis ugīkạnōnigōn pipōnisän:


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Apii idash gaa-bakinawind a'aw majiikiwis ogii-ganoonigoon biboonisen:**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*And when the first-born was beaten, he was addressed by Winter-Wind saying:* **  </span>
> <span class="l-fiero">**</span>

</div>


Apii idash gaa-bakinawind a’aw majiikiwis (and when that first-born was beaten) ogii-ganoonigoon biboonisen (he was addressed by Winter-Wind):

17) > "Ānīc, mīsa kīpa‘kinōnān," īnā ‘aga'u mādcī‘kiwis.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Aaniish, mii sa gii-bakinoonaan," inaa a'aw majiikiwis.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Well, therefore have I beaten you," was the first-born told.*</span>

</div>


“Aaniish, mii sa gii-bakinoonaan,” (“well, therefore have I beaten you,”) inaa a’aw majiikiwis (that first-born was told).

18) > "Pîdcīnạgigu wâbạnunk pā·ū·ndānima‘kin mīgō·i·'u cigwa tcîbinīskādagōtäg ‘ugu kījig tcigîmiwunk.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Bijiinag igo waabanong ba-ondaanimakin mii goo iw zhigwa ji-bi-niiskaadagooteg o'o giizhig ji-gimiwang.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"As soon as ever the wind blows from the east, then will foul weather hang aloft in this sky for the rain to fall.**  </span>
> <span class="l-src">***</span>

</div>


“Bijiinag igo waabanong ba-ondaanimakin (“as soon as ever the wind blows hither from the east) mii goo iw zhigwa (then it is that) ji-bi-niiskaadagooteg o’o giizhig (foul weather will hang aloft in this sky) ji-gimiwang. (for the rain to fall.)

19) > Mīsa·i· äjipa‘kinōnān," inān.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa i' ezhi-bakinoonaan," inaan.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Therefore such is what I have won from you," he was told.*</span>

</div>


Mii sa i’ ezhi-bakinoonaan,” (“therefore such is how I have won from you,”) inaan (he was told).

20) > Mīdạc ī·i·'u kā·u·ndci·i·jiwäba‘k.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash i'iw gaa-onji-izhiwebak.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*And that is what happens.*</span>

</div>


Mii dash i’iw gaa-onji-izhiwebak (and that is why it happens so).

21) > Kīcpîn wâbạnunk wändānima‘kin mīgu·i·'u cigwa mạdcigījiga‘k.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Giishpin waabanong wendaanimakin mii go iw zhigwa maji-giizhigak.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*When the wind blows from the east, then that is a sign for a bad day.*</span>

</div>


Giishpin waabanong wendaanimakin (when the wind blows from the east) mii go iw zhigwa maji-giizhigak (then it is that the day turns bad).

22) > Mī·i·'u kā·i·jipa‘kinawān-windibạnän ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii iw gaa-izhi-bakinawaan windibanen a'aw majiikiwis.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*It is because the first-born was once beaten in a contest.*</span>

</div>


Mii iw gaa-izhi-bakinawaan windibanen a’aw majiikiwis (it is because that first-born was once beaten in a contest — \*preterite participle\*).

23) > Kāwīn kiminwändazī pa‘kinawint.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Gaawiin giminwendazii bakinawind.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*He was not pleased to be beaten.*</span>

</div>


Gaawiin giminwendazii bakinawind (he was not pleased to be beaten).

24) > Minawā wī·ā·ndcī·e· ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Miinawaa wii-aanjii'e a'aw majiikiwis.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Over again did the first-born wish to play.*</span>

</div>


Miinawaa wii-aanjii’e a’aw majiikiwis (over again did that first-born wish to play).

25) > "Tạga, mīnawā a‘tādidāe\!" i‘kido ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Daga, miinawaa ataadidaa\!" ikido a'aw majiikiwis.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Come, let us have another game\!" said the first-born.*</span>

</div>


“Daga, miinawaa ataadidaa\!” (“come, let us have another game\!”) ikido a’aw majiikiwis (said that first-born).

26) > "Āwâwisa'," udigōn pipōnisän.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Aawaawisa." odigoon biboonisen.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Very well," he was told by Winter-Wind.*</span>

</div>


“Aawaawisa.” (“very well.”) odigoon biboonisen (he was told by Winter-Wind).

27) > Weyâbạning mī·i·'u minawā ujigābawiwād wībāga·ā·dowäwād.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Weyaabaninig mii iw miinawaa ozhigaabawiwaad wii-baaga'adowewaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*On the morrow they then took their places for another game of ball.*</span>

</div>


Weyaabaninig mii iw miinawaa (on the morrow then again) ozhigaabawiwaad (they took their places) wii-baaga’adowewaad (to play ball).

28) > "Kīwädinūnk nīn ningạtinạgatū," i‘kidō ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Giiwedinong niin ningad-inagadoo," ikido a'aw majiikiwis.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Towards the north will I play for goal," said the first-born.*</span>

</div>


“Giiwedinong niin ningad-inagadoo,” (“towards the north I will play for goal,”) ikido a’aw majiikiwis (said that first-born).

29) > "Gīn idạc, pipōnisä, câwanūnk ina‘kakä inagạdōn," undinān pipōnisän.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Giin idash, biboonise, zhaawanong inagake inagadoon." odinaan biboonisen.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"And you, Winter-Wind, towards the south do you play for goal," he said to Winter-Wind.*</span>

</div>


“Giin idash, biboonise, (“and you, Winter-Wind,) zhaawanong inagake inagadoon.” (toward the south do you play for goal.”) odinaan biboonisen (he said to Winter-Wind).

30) > Mī jigwa umbawâwāt, usāsā‘kwānigōwa känawâbamigowāt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii zhigwa ombawaawaad, ozaasagwaanigoowa genawaabamigowaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*So when they began playing, they were cheered on by the yells of them who were watching them.*</span>

</div>


Mii zhigwa ombawaawaad (so now they set the ball going), ozaasagwaanigoowa genawaabamigowaad (they were cheered on by the yells of those watching them).

31) > Kạbägījik menawā ubạbāmiwäpa·ā·nāwa pi‘kwā‘kwạt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Gabe-giizhik miinawaa obabaami-webaanaawa bikwaakwad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*All day long again they carried the ball back and forth and all around.*</span>

</div>


Gabe-giizhik miinawaa (all day long again) obabaami-webaanaawa bikwaakwad (they threw the ball about back and forth).

32) > Mîskuminagạt dạc ī'·i·'u pigwā‘kwāt.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Miskominagad dash i'w bikwaakwaad.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Red was the color of the ball.*</span>

</div>


Miskominagad dash i’iw bikwaakwaad (red in color was that ball).

33) > Ā‘pī·ä·ni·u·nāgucininig mī·i·'u cigwa minawā câgōdci·i·nt ‘agau mạdcī'kiwis.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Apii eni-onaagoshininig mii i'w zhigwa miinawaa zhaagooji'ind a'aw majiikiwis.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*When it was getting well on towards the evening, then again was the first-born being beaten.*</span>

</div>


Apii eni-onaagoshininig (when it was getting on toward evening) mii i’iw zhigwa miinawaa zhaagooji’ind a’aw majiikiwis (then again was that first-born being beaten).

34) > Käga‘pī mīnawā kīmījagạdō pipōnisä cāwạnunk ina´kakä.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Gegapii miinawaa gii-mizhagadoo biboonise zhaawanong inagake.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*At length again did Winter-Wind make a goal at the end towards the south.*</span>

</div>


Gegapii miinawaa gii-mizhagadoo biboonise (at length again did Winter-Wind make a goal) zhaawanong inagake (at the side toward the south).

35) > Mīdạc mīnawā äjikạnōnint ‘aga'u mạdcī‘kiwis:


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash miinawaa ezhi-ganoonind a'aw majiikiwis:**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon once more was the first-born addressed:***  </span>
> <span class="l-fiero">**</span>

</div>


Mii dash miinawaa ezhi-ganoonind a’aw majiikiwis (thereupon once more was that first-born addressed):

36) > "Ānīc, mīsa iu mīnawā kīpa‘kinōnān," inān.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Aaniish, mii sa iw miinawaa gii-bakinoonaan," inaan.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Well, therefore again have I beaten you," he was told.*</span>

</div>


“Aaniish, mii sa iw miinawaa gii-bakinoonaan,” (“well, therefore again have I beaten you,”) inaan (he was told).

37) > "Pîdcīnagigu kīwädonūnk pā·u·dānima‘kin mī·i·'u ka‘kina tcî·u·jimōwād kidockinīgīmạg, nīnidạc kāwīn ogaku‘tạnzīnāwa nindockinīgīmạg."


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Bijiinaagigo giiwedinong ba-odaanimakin mii i'w gakina ji-ozhimoowaad gidoshkiniigiimag, niin idash kaawiin oga-gotanziinaawa nindoshkiniigiimag."**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"As soon as ever the wind blows from the north, then will all your youths flee away, but of me will my youths not be afraid."*</span>

</div>


“Bijiinaagigo giiwedinong ba-odaanimakin (“as soon as ever the wind blows hither from the north) mii i’iw gakina ji-ozhimoowaad gidoshkiniigiimag (then will all your youths flee away), niin idash kaawiin oga-gotanziinaawa nindoshkiniigiimag.” (but my youths will not be afraid.”)

38) > Mīsa īgiu ka‘kina päbāmisätcig pinäsiwạg, mīwag īgi'u kā·a·‘tadiwagobạnän.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii sa igiw gakina bebaamisejig binesiwag, miiwag igiw gaa-atadiwagobanen.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Now, they were all the birds that fly about in the air, it was they that were in the contest.*</span>

</div>


Mii sa igiw gakina bebaamisejig binesiwag (now, they were all the birds that fly about), miiwag igiw gaa-atadiwagobanen (it was they that were in the contest — \*preterite participle\*).

39) > Ka‘kina nībinisän mīwanini'u kawīdcīwāgubạnän ‘aga'u mạdcī'kiwis kāku‘tạmuwād pîbōn.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Gakina niibinisen miiwan iniw gaa-wiijiiwaagobanen a'aw majiikiwis gaa-gotamowaad biboon.**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*All the birds of summer with whom the first-born played were the ones that feared the winter.*</span>

</div>


Gakina niibinisen (all the summer birds) miiwan iniw gaa-wiijiiwaagobanen a’aw majiikiwis (were the ones the first-born played alongside — \*preterite participle\*) gaa-gotamowaad biboon (the ones that feared the winter).

40) > Mī·i·'u kā·i·nāwindibạnän ‘aga'u mạdcī'kiwis:


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii iw gaa-inaawindibanen a'aw majiikiwis:**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*This, then, was the first-born told at the time:  </span>
> <span class="l-fiero">**  </span>
> <span class="l-src">***</span>

</div>


Mii iw gaa-inaawindibanen a’aw majiikiwis (this, then, was that first-born told at the time — \*preterite participle\*):

41) > "Nīnidạc pipōnisä.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Niin idash biboonise.**</span>
>
