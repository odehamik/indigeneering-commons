*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoding sa, giiwenh anishinaabeg daawag, oodetoowag.  
    > **Now, once on a time they say that some people were abiding there, they were living in a town.

Ningoding sa (now once on a time) giiwenh (so they say) anishinaabeg daawag (some people dwelt), oodetoowag (they lived in a town).

2)  > **Bezhig idash akiwenzii wiiwan gaye ayaawan, niizh idash oshkiniigikwen odayaawaawaan, odaanisiwaan.  
    > **There was an old man, and his wife was there, and two maidens who were their daughters.

Bezhig idash akiwenzii (and one old man) wiiwan gaye ayaawan (and his wife was there too), niizh idash oshkiniigikwen odayaawaawaan (and two young women they had), odaanisiwaan (their daughters).

3)  > **Apii idash netaawigiwaad, igiw ikwewag oganoonigowaan ogiiwaan:  
    > **And when they had come to maturity, the women were addressed by their mother saying:

Apii idash netaawigiwaad (and when they had grown up) igiw ikwewag (those women) oganoonigowaan ogiiwaan (were spoken to by their mother), saying:

4)  > **"Nindaanis, ningoji awi-ayaayok.  
    > **"My daughters, somewhere shall you go and stay.

“Nindaanis (my daughters) ningoji awi-ayaayok (go off and stay somewhere).

5)  > **Gego omaanh odenaang ayaakyegon."**  
    > Do not remain in this town."

Gego omaanh odenaang ayaakyegon (do not stay here in this town).”

6)  > **Mii dash gii-maajaawaad igiw oshkiniigikweg, babaamosewag; mii idash iko niibaawaad biinakamigaang.  
    > **Thereupon away started the maidens, about over the country they went tramping; and then it was continually their custom to sleep in a clean open place in the forest.

Mii dash gii-maajaawaad (and so off they went) igiw oshkiniigikweg (those young women), babaamosewag (they went tramping about); mii idash iko niibaawaad (and so it was their custom to sleep) biinakamigaang (in a clean open place on the ground).

7)  > **Ningoding idash debikadinig zhingishinowaad aapiji mizhakwadini; mii idash ganawaabamaawaad anangoo'.  
    > **Now, one night while they lay abed, the sky was thick with stars; and so they gazed up at the stars.

Ningoding idash debikadinig (now once when night fell) zhingishinowaad (as they lay down) aapiji mizhakwadini (the sky was very clear); mii idash ganawaabamaawaad anangoo’ (and so they gazed at the stars).

8)  > **Mii dash ekidod a'aw zeziikizid ikwe:  
    > **And now said the elder woman:

Mii dash ekidod (and so she said) a’aw zeziikizid ikwe (the older woman):

9)  > **"Niyaa, nishiime, nashke gosha anangoog ezhinaagoziwaad\!"**

> "Dear me, my little sister, do behold the sight of the stars\!"

“Niyaa (dear me), nishiime (my little sister), nashke gosha (do but look) anangoog ezhinaagoziwaad (how the stars appear)\!”

10) > **Mii dash geget inaabid a'aw oshiimenh imaanh.  
    > **Whereupon indeed then looked the younger sister.

Mii dash geget inaabid (and so indeed she looked up) a’aw oshiimenh imaanh (the younger sister, over there).

11) > **Mii dash ekidod a'aw zeziikizid:  
    > **So then said the elder one:

Mii dash ekidod (and so she said) a’aw zeziikizid (the older one):

12) > **"Aaniin giin a'aw apegish wiipemag enendaman?"  
    > **"Which of them, in your mind, would you wish to sleep with?"

“Aaniin giin a’aw (which one would you) apegish wiipemag enendaman (wish to lie with, in your mind)?”

13) > **"Niyaanh, bizaan daga\!  
    > **"Gracious me, do keep still\!

“Niyaanh (gracious me), bizaan daga (do be still)\!

14) > **Gego anooj ikidoken\!"  
    > **Cease talking such nonsense\!"

Gego anooj ikidoken (do not talk such nonsense)\!”

15) > **"Maanoo bina naan, nishiimenh\!"  
    > **"Oh, let us keep up the subject, my little sister\!"

“Maanoo bina naan (oh, let us keep it up), nishiimenh (my little sister)\!”

16) > **Gegapii idash gii-ikido a'aw weshiime'imind:  
    > **So at last said the younger sister:

Gegapii idash gii-ikido (and at last she spoke) a’aw weshiime’imind (the one who was the younger sister):

17) > **"Mii sa nii\[n\] inaa a'awe aapiji gaa-waabishkizid anang ge-wiipemag," ikido.  
    > **"Now, the one I should prefer to lie with is the star that looks so very white," she said.

“Mii sa niin inaa a’awe (the one for me would be) aapiji gaa-waabishkizid anang (the star that looks so very white) ge-wiipemag (that I would lie with),” ikido (she said).

18) > **"Giin dash?" odinaan omisenyan, "aaniin giin a'aw?"  
    > **"And you? "she said to her big sister, "which would you?"

“Giin dash (and you)?” odinaan omisenyan (she said to her older sister), “aaniin giin a’aw (which one for you)?”

19) > **Mii dash ekidod:  
    > **Whereupon she said:

Mii dash ekidod (and so she said):

20) > **"Mii sa nii\[n\] inaa awe aapiji gaa-miskwaanzhed.  
    > **"My choice is the one that looks so very red."

“Mii sa niin inaa awe (the one for me is that) aapiji gaa-miskwaanzhed (one that looks so very red).”

21) > **Gaa-ishkwaa-ikidowaad idash, mii iw gii-nibaawaad.  
    > **And after they had ceased talking, they then went to sleep.

Gaa-ishkwaa-ikidowaad idash (and after they had finished talking), mii iw gii-nibaawaad (then they slept).

22) > **Apii idash gweshkoziwaad gigizheb bebezhig ininiwa' owiipemaawaad; a'aw weshiime'imind oshkinawen owiipemaan, aw idash wemisenimind aapiji akiwenziiyan owiipemaan.  
    > **And when they woke in the morning, each was lying in bed with a man; she that was the younger lay with a youth, and she that was the elder lay with an exceedingly aged man.

Apii idash gweshkoziwaad gigizheb (and when they woke in the morning) bebezhig ininiwa’ owiipemaawaan (each was lying with a man); a’aw weshiime’imind (she who was the younger) oshkinawen owiipemaan (lay with a young man), aw idash wemisenimind (and she who was the elder) aapiji akiwenziiyan owiipemaan (lay with a very old man).

23) > **Mii iw gii-odaapinigowaad ishpiming anangoo', mii dash gii-wiijiwaawaad.  
    > **Therefore were they received up into the sky by the stars, and there they continued with them.

Mii iw gii-odaapinigowaad ishpiming anangoo’ (so they had been taken up into the sky by the stars), mii dash gii-wiijiwaawaad (and so they dwelt with them).

24) > **A'aw idash zeziikizid ikwe gaawiin ominwenimaasiin iniw akiwenziiyan.  
    > **Now, the elder woman was not pleased with the old man.

A’aw idash zeziikizid ikwe (now the older woman) gaawiin ominwenimaasiin (was not pleased with) iniw akiwenziiyan (that old man).

25) > **Ningoding idash gaa-maajaawaad igiw ikwewag, oganoonaan oshiimenyan:  
    > **So once, when the men were gone, she addressed her little sister, saying:

Ningoding idash gaa-maajaawaad igiw ikwewag (and once when the men had gone off), oganoonaan oshiimenyan (she spoke to her little sister), saying:

26) > **"Nishiimenh, nindawaa maajaadaa ningoji\!"  
    > **"My little sister, do therefore let us go to some place\!"

“Nishiimenh (my little sister), nindawaa maajaadaa ningoji (let us therefore go off somewhere)\!”

27) > **Mii dash geget gii-maajaawaad waasa gii-ani-izhaawag.  
    > **Accordingly, indeed, away they started, a long way off they went.

Mii dash geget gii-maajaawaad (and so indeed they set off) waasa gii-ani-izhaawag (far away they went).

28) > **Ningoding idash miinawaa babaa-ayindiwaad ogii-mikawaawaan miigwanan.  
    > **Now once, as they were simply going along, they discovered a feather.

Ningoding idash miinawaa babaa-ayindiwaad (and once as they were going along) ogii-mikawaawaan miigwanan (they found a feather).

29) > **Mii dash ekidowaad:  
    > **Whereupon they said:

Mii dash ekidowaad (and so they said):

30) > **"Dibi wenjiigwen a'aw miigwan\!"  
    > **"Wonder from whence came the feather\!"

“Dibi wenjiigwen a’aw miigwan (wonder where this feather came from)\!”

31) > **Miinawaa dash wiiba go omikawaawaan miigwanan; gegapii igo aapiji niibiwa miigwana' owaabamaawaan.  
    > **And not long afterwards they found another feather; and then at last ever so many feathers they saw.

Miinawaa dash wiiba go omikawaawaan miigwanan (and soon they found another feather); gegapii igo aapiji niibiwa miigwana’ owaabamaawaan (at last they saw a great many feathers).

32) > **Ningoding idash owaabandaanaawaa wiigiwaamens mindimooyenyan imaanh daawan; gii-biindigewag igiw ikwewag.  
    > **And presently they saw a small wigwam with an old woman abiding there; inside went the women.

Ningoding idash owaabandaanaawaa wiigiwaamens (and presently they saw a little wigwam) mindimooyenyan imaanh daawan (with an old woman living there); gii-biindigewag igiw ikwewag (the women went inside).

33) > **"Namadabiyok, noozhidog," ikido mindimooyenh.  
    > **"Be seated, my grandchildren," said the old woman.

“Namadabiyok (be seated), noozhidog (my grandchildren),” ikido mindimooyenh (said the old woman).

34) > **Niibiwa odayaan miijim, biijiigigwane-wiiyaas ogii-ashamigowaan idash.  
    > **Much she had of food, and the flesh of a fish-hawk they were fed.

Niibiwa odayaan miijim (much food she had), biijiigigwane-wiiyaas ogii-ashamigowaan idash (and fish-hawk flesh she fed them).

35) > **Ningoding igo namadabid a'aw mindimooyenh owiikobidoon biimanakwaan, mii dash imaanh gii-nisaad biijigigwanen.  
    > **Now presently, while the old woman was seated, she pulled upon a cord, and then there she killed a Fish-hawk.

Ningoding igo namadabid a’aw mindimooyenh (now once as the old woman sat) owiikobidoon biimanakwaan (she pulled on a cord), mii dash imaanh gii-nisaad biijigigwanen (and so there she killed a fish-hawk).

36) > **Bagoneyaani aki imaanh namadabid a'aw mindimooyenh; mii imaanh wendaabigamonig omigiskan.  
    > **There was an opening in the ground at the place where the old woman sat; it was from there that her hook came.

Bagoneyaani aki imaanh (there was a hole in the ground there) namadabid a’aw mindimooyenh (where the old woman sat); mii imaanh wendaabigamonig omigiskan (it was from there that her hook came on its line).

37) > **Ogagwejimigowaan dash mindimooyenyan:  
    > **And they were asked by the old woman:

Ogagwejimigowaan dash mindimooyenyan (and they were asked by the old woman):

38) > **"Aaniindi wenjiiyeg?"  
    > **"From whence came you?"

“Aaniindi wenjiiyeg (where do you come from)?”

39) > **Mii dash gii-wiindamawaawad gii-odaapinigowaad anangoo'.  
    > **Thereupon they informed her that they had been carried away by the stars.

Mii dash gii-wiindamawaawad (and so they told her) gii-odaapinigowaad anangoo’ (that they had been taken away by the stars).

40) > **Mii dash ekidod mindimooye:  
    > **Whereupon said the old woman:

Mii dash ekidod mindimooye (and so said the old woman):

41) > **"Giishpin ozhitooyeg niibiwa wiigob ga-boonikiyaabikinininim endanakiiyegoban ji-ayaayeg."  
    > **"If you will make a long cord, I will let you down, so that you can go back to the place where you live."

“Giishpin ozhitooyeg niibiwa wiigob (if you make a lot of basswood cord) ga-boonikiyaabikinininim (I will let you down by the line) endanakiiyegoban (to where you used to live) ji-ayaayeg (so you may be there).”

42) > **Mii dash geget gii-ozhitoowaad niibiwa wiigob.  
    > **Therefore truly they made a long cord.

Mii dash geget gii-ozhitoowaad niibiwa wiigob (and so indeed they made much basswood cord).

43) > **"Ozhitooyok wadapiiwazh.  
    > **"Make a basket of spruce-root.

“Ozhitooyok wadapiiwazh (make a basket of spruce-root).

44) > **Dakobidooyok weweni."  
    > **Fasten (the cord) securely."

Dakobidooyok weweni (tie it on securely).”

45) > **Gii-dakobidoowaad idash mii imaanh gii-asigowaad ookomisiwaan.  
    > **So after they had fastened (the cord), then into it were they put by their grandmother.

Gii-dakobidoowaad idash (and after they had tied it) mii imaanh gii-asigowaad ookomisiwaan (then there they were put in by their grandmother).

46) > **"Mii iwidi endanakiiyegoban ged-izhi-ayeg."  
    > **"Now to the place where you used to live is where you shall go."

“Mii iwidi endanakiiyegoban (over yonder where you used to live) ged-izhi-ayeg (is where you shall go).”

47) > **Mii dash geget gii-booziwaad imaanh wedabiiwezhing, mii idash boonaabiiginindwaa.  
    > **Thereupon truly they got into the spruce-root basket, whereupon they were let down by the cord.

Mii dash geget gii-booziwaad imaanh wedabiiwezhing (and so indeed they got into the spruce-root basket), mii idash boonaabiiginindwaa (and so they were let down by the line).

48) > **"Badagwiingweshinok.  
    > **"Cover up your faces.

“Badagwiingweshinok (cover up your faces).

49) > **Gego ganage inaabikegon.  
    > **Under no circumstances shall you look.

Gego ganage inaabikegon (on no account look out).

50) > **Baanimaa go gii-dagwishineg akiing mii iw ji-inaabiyeg."  
    > **Not till you have reached the earth, then may you look."

Baanimaa go gii-dagwishineg akiing (not until you have reached the earth) mii iw ji-inaabiyeg (then you may look).”

51) > **Ginwenzh idash eyaawaad odabii'ozhing.  
    > **Now, for a long time were they in the spruce-root basket.

Ginwenzh idash eyaawaad odabii’ozhing (and for a long time they were in the spruce-root basket).

52) > **Ikido a'a zeziikizid ikwe:  
    > **Said the woman that was older:

Ikido a’a zeziikizid ikwe (said the older woman):

53) > **Daga naan, nishiimenh, inaabidaa\!"  
    > **"I say, my little sister, do let us take a look\!"

“Daga naan (I say, do), nishiimenh (my little sister), inaabidaa (let us take a look)\!”

54) > **"Gego bina\! gigii-onji-igonaan gosha gookomisinaan."  
    > **"Please don't\! for you know we were forbidden by our grandmother."

“Gego bina (please don’t)\! gigii-onji-igonaan gosha gookomisinaan (for our grandmother forbade us).”

55) > **Miinawaa ikido a'aw ikwe:  
    > **Again said the woman:

Miinawaa ikido a’aw ikwe (again said the woman):

56) > **"Maanoo bina naa\! Inaabidaa\!"  
    > **"Oh, pshaw\! do let us look\!"

“Maanoo bina naa (oh, never mind)\! Inaabidaa (let us look)\!”

57) > **Od-aano-gii-onji’igoon oshiimenyan.  
    > **It was no use for her little sister to try to turn her from her purpose.

Od-aano-gii-onji’igoon oshiimenyan (in vain her little sister tried to dissuade her).

58) > **Gegapii dash gii-inaabi a'aw ikwe zeziikizid; mii dash waabandang, zhigwa debinagwadinig aki.  
    > **So at last looked the woman who was older; whereupon, as she beheld it, faintly was the earth then coming into view.

Gegapii dash gii-inaabi a’aw ikwe zeziikizid (and at last the older woman looked); mii dash waabandang (and so as she looked), zhigwa debinagwadinig aki (already the earth was faintly coming into view).

59) > **"Nyaanh, nashke gosha, nishiime\! inaabin gaye giin\!  
    > **"Oh, do see, my little sister\! do you look too\!

“Nyaanh (oh), nashke gosha (do but see), nishiime (my little sister)\! inaabin gaye giin (look, you too)\!

60) > **Zhigwa debinaagwad endanakiiyang."  
    > **Now into view is coming the place where we live."

Zhigwa debinaagwad endanakiiyang (already into view comes the place where we live).”

61) > **Gegapii idash ogii-wayezhimaan oshiimenyan.  
    > **So at last she prevailed upon her little sister.

Gegapii idash ogii-wayezhimaan oshiimenyan (and at last she won over her little sister).

62) > **Mii dash nayenzh gii-inaabiwaad; mii dash gaa-izhi-pakiskaanig wiigob, mii dash bangishinowaad.  
    > **Whereupon both then looked; and then broke the cord, whereupon they fell.

Mii dash nayenzh gii-inaabiwaad (and so both of them looked); mii dash gaa-izhi-pakiskaanig wiigob (and so the cord snapped), mii dash bangishinowaad (and so they fell).

63) > **Mii dash ezhi-nagamod a'aw ikwe:  
    > **And this song sang the woman:

Mii dash ezhi-nagamod a’aw ikwe (and so the woman sang):

64) > **"Bijigigwane wazasoning bangishinaan."  
    > **"In a fish-hawk's nest was where I fell."

“Bijigigwane wazasoning bangishinaan (into a fish-hawk’s nest I fall).”

65) > **Mii iwe ena'ang.  
    > **Such was the song she sang.

Mii iwe ena’ang (that was what she sang).

66) > **Mii dash geget wazasoning gii-bangishinowaad.  
    > **Thereupon truly into a nest they fell.

Mii dash geget wazasoning gii-bangishinowaad (and so indeed into a nest they fell).

67) > **Mii dash imaanh gii-ayaawaad, gaawiin gaski'osiiwag ji-nisaandawewaad.  
    > **And so there they remained, they were not able to come down (from it).

Mii dash imaanh gii-ayaawaad (and so there they stayed), gaawiin gaski’osiiwag ji-nisaandawewaad (they were not able to climb down).

68) > **Anooj awiya waabamaawaan bimosenid awesiiyan; oganoonaawaan iko ji-bina'ogoowaad.  
    > **And the various kinds of game-folk they saw walking past; all the while they spoke to them, asking that they be taken down.

Anooj awiya waabamaawaan bimosenid awesiiyan (all sorts of animals they saw walking past); oganoonaawaan iko ji-bina’ogoowaad (they kept calling to them to be taken down).

69) > **"Enh, gaawiin ningashkitoosiin ji-akwaandaweyaan," odigowaan.  
    > **"Why, I am unable to climb up," they were told

“Enh (yes), gaawiin ningashkitoosiin ji-akwaandaweyaan (I cannot climb up),” odigowaan (they were told).

70) > **Gaawiin odebwetaagosiiwaan gakina go awesiiya' od-aana-gii-ganoonaawaan, gaawiin dash gi-debwetanziiwa.**  
    > Not were they believed by all the game-folk whom they vainly addressed, and (the gamefolk) did not believe (what they heard).

Gaawiin odebwetaagosiiwaan (they were not believed) gakina go awesiiya’ od-aana-gii-ganoonaawaan (though they spoke in vain to all the animals), gaawiin dash gi-debwetanziiwa (and they did not believe).

71) > **Ningoding idash gwiingwa'aagen ogii-waabamaawaan.  
    > **Now, once the Wolverine they saw.

Ningoding idash gwiingwa’aagen ogii-waabamaawaan (and once they saw a wolverine).

72) > **Mii dash enaawaad:  
    > **And they said to him:

Mii dash enaawaad (and so they said to him):

73) > **"Daga ondaas, bi-naanzikawishinaan\!"  
    > **"Do come and take us down\!"

“Daga ondaas (come here), bi-naanzikawishinaan (come over to us)\!”

74) > **Mii dash geget gii-niisaandawe'aad iniw nitam zeziikizinid, miinawaa dash skwaaj a'aw oshiime'imaan.  
    > **Whereupon truly he took them down, first the one that was older, and next the other that was younger.

Mii dash geget gii-niisaandawe’aad (and so indeed he took them down) iniw nitam zeziikizinid (first the older one), miinawaa dash skwaaj a’aw oshiime’imaan (and next the younger sister).

75) > **Giimooj idash ogii-nagadaan ozegibanweyaab imaanh ozasoning.  
    > **Now, secretly (the elder woman) left behind in the nest the tape (she used in wrapping her hair-knot).

Giimooj idash ogii-nagadaan ozegibanweyaab (and secretly she left behind her hair-binding tape) imaanh ozasoning (there in the nest).

76) > **Mii idash gii-niisaandawewind igiw ikweg ogii-inaawaan iniw gwiingwa'aagen:  
    > **And after they were taken down, the women said to Wolverine:

Mii idash gii-niisaandawewind igiw ikweg (and after the women had been taken down) ogii-inaawaan iniw gwiingwa’aagen (they said to the wolverine):

77) > **"Meshkod giga-wiidigemigo," ogii-inaawaabanin.**  
    > "In return (for this) we will marry you," they (thus) said to him at the time.

“Meshkod giga-wiidigemigo (in return we will marry you),” ogii-inaawaabanin (so they said to him then).

78) > **Apii idash waasa degoshinowaad mii windamawaad a'aw ikwe:  
    > **So when far away they were come, then to him made known the woman:

Apii idash waasa degoshinowaad (and when they had got far away) mii windamawaad a’aw ikwe (the woman told him):

79) > **"Ningii-waniike nizegibanweyaab wazasoning.  
    > **"I forgot the wrapping-tape (of my hair-knot) at the nest.

“Ningii-waniike nizegibanweyaab wazasoning (I forgot my hair-tape at the nest).

80) > **Daga, naazikan\!"  
    > **Do go fetch it\!"

Daga, naazikan (go fetch it)\!”

81) > **Mii geget maajiibatood gwiingwa'aage naazikang zegibanweyaab.  
    > **Accordingly, in truth, on a run started Wolverine, as he went to get the wrapping-tape (of the hair-knot).

Mii geget maajiibatood gwiingwa’aage (and so indeed the wolverine ran off) naazikang zegibanweyaab (to go after the hair-tape).

82) > **Mii dash gii-maajibatoowaad ikwewag, waasa ozhimowaad.  
    > **Whereupon the women started running, afar they fled.

Mii dash gii-maajibatoowaad ikwewag (and so the women ran off), waasa ozhimowaad (far away they fled).

83) > **Gwiingwa'aagen idash onoopinanigowaan.  
    > **And by Wolverine were they pursued.

Gwiingwa’aagen idash onoopinanigowaan (and by the wolverine they were pursued).

84) > **E' e', gwiingwa'aage zhigwa bi-izhibatoo\!  
    > **Oh, how Wolverine then came running (back)\!

E’ e’, gwiingwa’aage zhigwa bi-izhibatoo (oh, how the wolverine came running back)\!

85) > **Aa, ikwewag enigo\[k\] bimibatoowag\!  
    > **My, but the women ran at the top of their speed\!

Aa, ikwewag enigo\[k\] bimibatoowag (my, how the women ran at full speed)\!

86) > **'Aa, zhigwa besho gwiingwa'aage\!  
    > **My, but how close now was Wolverene\!

Aa, zhigwa besho gwiingwa’aage (oh, how close now the wolverine was)\!
