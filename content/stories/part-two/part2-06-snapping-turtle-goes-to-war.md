## **6. MI'KINĀ'K NẠNDUBẠNI**

## **MIKINAAK NANDOBANI**

## **SNAPPING-TURTLE GOES TO WAR**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).*</span>

</div>


*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > Ninguding kīnwä mi‘kinā‘k wīnä‘tagu päejik.  
    > **Ningoding giiwen' mikinaak wiin eta go bezhig.  
    > ***Once on a time they say a Snapping-Turtle (was) all alone.***  
    > **

Ningoding (once) giiwen’ (they say) mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle) wiin eta go (he alone) bezhig (one).

2)  > Mīdạc kā·i·nändạng:  
    > **Mii dash gaa-inendang:  
    > ***And this was what he thought:***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) gaa-inendang (what he thought).

3)  > "Pānimān ningadạninạndawâbạmāg käwīdcīwạgwā."  
    > **"Baanimaa ningad-ani-nandawaabamaag ge-wiijiiwagwaa".  
    > ***"After a while I will go seek for those in whose company I shall go."***  
    > **

"Baanimaa (after a while) ningad-ani-nandawaabamaag (I will go looking for them) ge-wiijiiwagwaa (those I shall go with)."

4)  > Mīdạc kīmādcād; ningudingīdạc ogī·ạ·ni·u·di‘tān wạdciwänz; mēdạc imān kā·i·jād ugidā‘ki, mēidạc äjipībāgit:  
    > **Mii dash gii-maajaad; Ningoding idash ogii-ani-oditaan wajiwens; mii dash imaa gaa-izhaad ogidaaki, mii dash ezhi-biibaagid:  
    > ***Thereupon he departed; and by and by he came to a little hill on the way; and when he got to yonder hill-top, he then cried with a loud voice, saying:***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) gii-maajaad (he set out); ningoding idash (and by and by) ogii-ani-oditaan (he came along to) wajiwens (a little hill); mii dash imaa (and so there) gaa-izhaad (when he got to) ogidaaki (the hilltop), mii dash (and so) ezhi-biibaagid (he cried aloud):

5)  > "Awänän käwīdcīwạg tcînạndubạniyān?"  
    > **"Awenen ge-wiijiiwag ji-nandobaniyaan?"  
    > ***"With whom shall I go when I set out for war?"  
    > *

"Awenen (who) ge-wiijiiwag (will I go with) ji-nandobaniyaan (when I go to war)?"

6)  > Awi·a·dạc omạdwäna‘kwä‘tagōn:  
    > **Awiya dash omadwe-nakwetaagoon:  
    > ***Then came the sound of some one's voice answering him:***  
    > **

Awiya dash (then someone) omadwe-nakwetaagoon (was heard answering him).

7)  > "Nīn kīgawīdcīwîn tcînạndubạniyạn\!"


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Niin giga-wiijiiwin ji-nandobaniyan\!"**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"I will go with you when you go to war\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


"Niin (I) giga-wiijiiwin (will go with you) ji-nandobaniyan (when you go to war)\!"

8)  > "‘Āu, ondās\!" udinān.  
    > **"Aaw, ondaas\!" odinaan.  
    > ***"All right, come hither\!" he said to him.***  
    > **

"Aaw (all right), ondaas (come here)\!" odinaan (he said to him).

9)  > Päcudạc pā·ạ·yānit īni´u ininiwạn, ogạnōnān mi‘kinā‘k:  
    > **Besho dash baa-ayaanid iniw ininiwan, oganoonaan mikinaak:  
    > ***And when near by the man was come, to him Snapping-Turtle spoke, saying:* **  
    > **

Besho dash (and when nearby) baa-ayaanid (he was coming about) iniw ininiwan (that man), oganoonaan (spoke to him) mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle):

10) > "Ckumān pigagwädcīn käta·i·jīwumbạnän mīgāzoyạng\!"


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"*Shkomaa* bi-gagwejiin *ge-daa-izhiiwambanen* miigaazoyang."**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Just you come and show what you would do should we get into a fight\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


"Shkomaa (just) bi-gagwejiin (come and try) ge-daa-izhiiwambanen (what you would do) miigaazoyang (should we fight)\!"

11) > Pạgamāgạndac uda‘kunān ‘aga´u inini.  
    > **Bagamaagan dash odakonaan a'aw inini.  
    > ***Now, a war-club in his hand the man held.  
    > *

Bagamaagan dash (now a war-club) odakonaan (he held) a’aw inini (that man).

12) > Mīdạc käegā‘t pigagwädcīt.  
    > **Mii dash geget bi-gagwejiid.  
    > ***Thereupon truly hither he came, making a show (of what he would do).***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) geget (truly) bi-gagwejiid (he came making a show).

13) > "Hahahu´, hahahu´, hahahu´, hahahu´\!"  
    > **"Hahaho', hahaho', hahaho', hahaho'\!"  
    > ***"Hahahu, hahahu, hahahu, hahaho\!"***  
    > **

"Hahaho’, hahaho’, hahaho’, hahaho’\!"

14) > Cīgwa umān pạdạgwicininit īni´u ininiwạn ugīpîminīma·u·gōn ‘igi´u pạgạmāgạn.  
    > **Zhigwa omaa ba-dagwishininid iniw ininiwan ogii-bimi-niima'ogoon i'iw bagamaagan.  
    > ***When here the man was come, at (Snapping-Turtle) he came brandishing the club as if to strike him.  
    > *

Zhigwa (when) omaa (here) ba-dagwishininid (he was come) iniw ininiwan (that man) ogii-bimi-niima’ogoon (he brandished at him in passing) i’iw bagamaagan (that war-club).

15) > "Mēdạc kägat gīnibu, mī‘kinā‘k\!"


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Mii dash geget ginibo mikinaak\!"**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"And now truly you shall die, Snapping-Turtle\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


"Mii dash (and now) geget (truly) ginibo (you shall die), mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle)\!"

16) > Mīdạc kīgusāt.  
    > **Mii dash gii-goosad.  
    > ***At that he became afraid of him.***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) gii-goosad (he became afraid of him).

17) > "Intawātci kīwän," udinān, "māgicā kidānisigō mīgāding," udinān.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Indawaaj giiwen," odinaan, "maagizhaa gid-anisigoo miigaading," odinaan.**</span>

> <span class="l-eng">*"You had better go back home," he said to him, "lest perhaps you be slain in battle," he said to him.  </span>

</div>


"Indawaaj (you had better) giiwen (go home)," odinaan (he said to him), "maagizhaa (lest perhaps) gid-anisigoo (you be slain) miigaading (in battle)," odinaan (he said to him).

18) > Mīdạc kī‘kīwäd ‘aga´u inini kī·ạ·nimādcā dac mī‘kinā‘k; mīnawā ninguding kī·ạ·nia‘kwāmạdciwä wạdciwing.  
    > **Mii dash gii-giiwed a'aw inini gii-ani-maajaa dash mikinaak; miinawaa ningoding gii-ani-akwaamajiwe wajiwing.  
    > ***And so, when back home went the man, then on his way went Snapping-Turtle; on up another hill he climbed.***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) gii-giiwed (when he went home) a’aw inini (that man), gii-ani-maajaa dash (then on his way set out) mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle); miinawaa ningoding (and again at one point) gii-ani-akwaamajiwe (he climbed up) wajiwing (a hill).

19) > Mīnawā gīpīpāgi:  
    > **Miinawaa gii-biibaagi:  
    > ***Again he called with a loud voice:***  
    > **

Miinawaa (again) gii-biibaagi (he called aloud):

20) > "Awänän gäwīdcīwạgē tcînạndūpạniyān?"  
    > **"Awenen ge-wiijiiwaage ji-nandobaniyaan?"  
    > ***"With whom shall I go when I set out for war?"  
    > *

"Awenen (who) ge-wiijiiwaage (will I go with) ji-nandobaniyaan (when I go to war)?"

21) > Mēdạc mạdwäbībāgiwạn:  
    > **Mii dash madwe-biibaagiwan:  
    > ***Whereupon came the sound of voices calling aloud:***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) madwe-biibaagiwan (came the sound of voices calling).

22) > "Nīnawind\!"


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Niinawind\!"**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*"With us\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

<div class="four-layer">

>
> <span class="l-eng">*"Hither, hither\!" he said to them.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


"Ondaas (come here), ondaas (come here)\!" odinaan (he said to them).

24) > Kīpitạgwicinōgidạc mîskwādäsiwạg nībiwa.  
    > **Gii-bi-dagwishinoog idash miskwaadesiwag niibiwa.  
    > ***So came the Painted-Turtles, arriving in throngs.***  
    > **

Gii-bi-dagwishinoog idash (and they came arriving) miskwaadesiwag (the Painted-Turtles) niibiwa (many).

25) > Mīdạc änād:  
    > **Mii dash enaad:  
    > ***And then he said to them:***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) enaad (he said to them):

26) > "Skomān kạgwätcīyu‘k kädōdamägobạn mīgāsoyạnk\!"


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"*Skomaa* gagwejiiyog ge-doodamegoban miigaazoyang\!"**</span>

> <span class="l-eng">*"Just you show what you would do should we get into a fight\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

"Skomaa (just) gagwejiiyog (try, you) ge-doodamegoban (what you would do) miigaazoyang (should we fight)\!"

27) > Mīdac ka‘kina kā·i·ji·o·dci‘tāwād, tibickō asînīg kījināguziwạg.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Mii dash gakina gaa-izhi-ojitaawaad, dibishkoo asiniig gii-izhinaagoziwag.**</span>

Mii dash (and so) gakina (all) gaa-izhi-ojitaawaad (when they drew into their shells), dibishkoo asiniig (like stones) gii-izhinaagoziwag (they looked).

28) > "Ānīc, mīsa´ ogōu käwīdcī·ạ·gwā," i‘kido mi‘kinā‘k.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**"Aaniish mii saa ogow ge-wiijii'agwaa," ikido mikinaak.**</span>

"Aaniish (well), mii saa (it is) ogow (these) ge-wiijii’agwaa (that I will go with)," ikido mikinaak (said Snapping-Turtle).

29) > Mīdạc kīmādcāwād nạndubạniwād.  
    > **Mii dash gii-maajaawaad nandobaniwaad.  
    > ***Thereupon they set out to go to war.***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) gii-maajaawaad (they set out) nandobaniwaad (going to war).

30) > Ningutingidạc ạninîbāwād tibi‘kạdinig mạdwänạgạmō päejik mîskwādäsi, mīdạc äna·ạ·nk: —


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Ningoding idash ani-nibaawaad dibikadinig madwe-nagamo bezhig miskwaadesi, mii dash ena'ang: -**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*And one night when they were going to sleep, a certain Turtle was heard singing, and thus he sang:—*</span>

</div>


Ningoding idash (and one night) ani-nibaawaad (as they were going to sleep) dibikadinig (when night fell) madwe-nagamo (was heard singing) bezhig miskwaadesi (a certain Turtle), mii dash (and so) ena’ang (this he sang):

31) > "Mîcī‘känwā\! ōdänāngi kīdā‘tawänigōmin yāaha\!  
    > **Mishiikenwaa\! oodenaangi gidaatawenigoomin yaaha\!  
    > ***"O Snapping-Turtle\! in a town are we prophesied an evil fate, yaaha\!  
    > *

Mishiikenwaa\! (O Snapping-Turtle\!) oodenaangi (in a town) gidaatawenigoomin (we are foretold an evil fate) yaaha\!

32) > Nīndinā‘pawä yāaha, nīndinā‘pawä yāaha, yāaha\!"


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-fiero">**Nindinaabawe yaaha, nindinaabawe yaaha\!"**</span>
>
> <span class="l-eng">*Such was my dream, yaaha\! such was my dream, yaaha, yaaha\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Nindinaabawe (such was my dream) yaaha, nindinaabawe (such was my dream) yaaha\!

33) > Mī‘kinā‘kidạc nwāndawād unīckimigōn.  
    > **Mikinaak idash nwaandawaad onishkimigoon.  
    > ***And when Snapping-Turtle heard him, he was angered.***  
    > **

Mikinaak idash (and Snapping-Turtle) nwaandawaad (when he heard him) onishkimigoon (was angered by him).

34) > Mēdạc kā·i·ji·u·dā‘pinạng umō‘komān kī·i·nōdä i·i·´mān ayānit mīdạc gạgwädcimād:  
    > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-odaapinang omookomaan gii-inoode imaa ayaanid mii dash gagwejimaad:  
    > ***Thereupon, taking up his knife, he crawled over to where he was, and then asked him:***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) gaa-izhi-odaapinang (taking up) omookomaan (his knife) gii-inoode (he crawled) imaa ayaanid (to where he was) mii dash (and so) gagwejimaad (he asked him):

35) > "ānīn ä‘kidoyan?"


<div class="four-layer">

<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"Why, that in a town we shall probably all be slain, was what I dreamed," he said.*</span>

</div>


"Gaa (why), oodenaang (in a town) iizan (it seems) gakina (all) ginisigoomin (we shall be slain) nindinaabandam (was what I dreamed)," ikido (he said).

37) > Mēdạc mi‘kinā‘k kā·i·jikīckigwäjwād.  
    > **Mii dash mikinaak gaa-izhi-giishkigwezhwaad.  
    > ***Thereupon Snapping-Turtle cut off his head.***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle) gaa-izhi-giishkigwezhwaad (cut off his head).

38) > Mēdạc minawā kī·ạ·nimādcāwād.

    > ***And then they continued on their way.***  
    > **

Mii dash (and so) miinawaa (again) gii-ani-maajaawaad (they went on their way).

39) > A‘pī·i·dạc wädisāwād anicinābä odä‘tōnit, mi‘kinā‘k ogī·i·nāg ka‘kina ucîmagạnīcimag:  
    > **Apii dash wedisowaad anishinaabe oodetoonid, mikinaak ogii-inaag gakina ozhimaaganishima':  
    > ***And when they came to where some people had a town, Snapping-Turtle said to all his soldiers:  
    > *

Apii dash (and when) wedisowaad (they came to) anishinaabe oodetoonid (where people had a town), mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle) ogii-inaag (said to) gakina (all) ozhimaaganishima’ (his soldiers):

40) > "Mī·u·mān ayaiyu‘k.  
    > **Mii omaa ayaayog.  
    > ***"In this place do you remain.  
    > *

"Mii omaa (here) ayaayog (do you stay)."

41) > Ningạtijā, ningạnadawā‘to."

    > ***I will go thither, I will go to reconnoitre."***  
    > **

Ningad-izhaa (I will go), ninga-nadawaatoo (I will go to scout)."

    > **

Bezhig (one) owiijiiwaan (he went along with) omizhinaweman (his attendant).

43) > Mīdạc ijāwād ayānik wigiwāmạn, päejikidạc cābundawāning kī‘pīndigäwạg nībādîbi‘k.

Mii dash (and so) izhaawaad (going to) ayaanig wiigiwaaman (where there were lodges), bezhig idash (and into a certain) zhaabondawaaning (long-lodge) gii-biindigewag (they entered) nibaa-dibik (in the dead of night).

44) > Mēdạc änād uwīdcīwāgạnạn:

    > ***And this he said to his comrade:  
    > *

Mii dash (and so) enaad (he said to) owiijiiwaaganan (his comrade):
