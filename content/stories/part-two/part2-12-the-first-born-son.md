## **12. MAJIIKIWIS.**

## **THE FIRST-BORN SON.**

> *from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).*

*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoding isa giiwenh gii-daa majiikiwis.**

> Now, once on a time, they say, there dwelt a first-born son.

Ningoding isa giiwenh (now, once on a time, they say) gii-daa majiikiwis (there dwelt a first-born son).

2)  > **Midaachiwan ogwisan, odaanisa' gaye midaachiwa'.**

> Ten was the number of his sons, and his daughters were also ten.

Midaachiwan ogwisan (ten in number were his sons), odaanisa’ gaye midaachiwa’ (and his daughters were also ten).

3)  > **Mii idash ekidowaad igiw oskinaweg:  
    > **Thereupon said the youths:

Mii idash ekidowaad (and then they said) igiw oskinaweg (those youths):

4)  > **“Aaniish, noose, mii isa zhigwa ji-bakewinigooyan.  
    > **"Well, my father, the time is now at hand for us to be leaving you.

“Aaniish, noose (well, my father), mii isa zhigwa (now is the time) ji-bakewinigooyan (for us to be leaving you).

5)  > **Bakaan akiing ningad-izhaamin”.**

> To a different land are we going."

Bakaan akiing (to a different land) ningad-izhaamin” (are we going).

6)  > **Mii dash geget gii-a-bagidinaad ogwisisa'; mii idash gii-maajaawaad.**

> And so, in truth, he let his sons depart; whereupon they started away.

Mii dash geget (and so, in truth) gii-a-bagidinaad ogwisisa’ (he let his sons depart); mii idash gii-maajaawaad (whereupon they started away).

7)  > **Apii idash gaa-oditamowaad ge-daawaad mii iw gii-ozhitoowaad wiigiwaam.**

> And when they came to the place where they were going to live, they then built a wigwam.

Apii idash gaa-oditamowaad ge-daawaad (and when they reached where they were going to live) mii iw gii-ozhitoowaad wiigiwaam (they then built a wigwam).

8)  > **Mii dash imaa wenjiiwaad andawenjigewaad; anooji gegoo onitoonaawaa ge-miijiwaad.**

> And so from that place they set out when they went to hunt for game; all kinds of things they killed, what they were to eat.

Mii dash imaa wenjiiwaad andawenjigewaad (and so from that place they set out to hunt for game); anooji gegoo onitoonaawaa ge-miijiwaad (all kinds of things they killed, what they were to eat).

9)  > **Mii idash ezhichigewaad:  
    > **Now, this was what they did:

Mii idash ezhichigewaad (and this was what they did):

10) > **bezhigwanini omiikanaawa; nitam idash zeziikizid ani-bakemoni omiikana ezhaad nendawenjiged; miinawaa dash bezhig zeziikizid ani-bakemoni omiikana ezhaad nendawenjiged; mii go miinawaa eni-zaziikizid eni-bakemonik omiikana; mii go iw pane endoodamowaad nendawenjigewaad, biinish igo gakina babakewaad.  
    > **each of them had a road; now, the road of the eldest was the first to branch off towards where he was to hunt for game; and the road of the next eldest then branched off towards where he was to hunt for game;and so on, (as they stood) next in order of age, the road of each one went branching off; and now that was what they always did when they went to hunt, (the roads continued branching off) until all (the youths) had separated.

bezhigwanini omiikanaawa (each of them had a road); nitam idash zeziikizid (now first the eldest) ani-bakemoni omiikana (his road branched off) ezhaad nendawenjiged (where he went to hunt for game); miinawaa dash bezhig zeziikizid (and again the next eldest) ani-bakemoni omiikana (his road branched off) ezhaad nendawenjiged (where he went to hunt for game); mii go miinawaa eni-zaziikizid (and so again the next in age) eni-bakemonik omiikana (his road branched off); mii go iw pane endoodamowaad (and that was what they always did) nendawenjigewaad (when they hunted), biinish igo gakina babakewaad (until all of them had separated).

11) > **Ningoding idash degoshinowaad endaawaad owaabandaanaawaa awiya gii-dagwishininid endaawaad.**

> Now once, when they had come back home, they observed that somebody had come to the place where they lived.

Ningoding idash degoshinowaad endaawaad (now once, when they came home) owaabandaanaawaa (they observed) awiya gii-dagwishininid endaawaad (that somebody had come where they lived).

12) > **Weweni gii-nanaa'ichigaadeni biindig; ikwe ezhi-nametood; jiibaakwaan gaye ateni; weweni gaye gii-apishimoonike zhingobiinsa'; gaye dash misan atewan agwajiing.**

> Nice was the arrangement (of things) inside; it was like the work of a woman; and some cooking had been done; and carefully arranged were the balsam-boughs at the sleeping-places; and there was also some fire-wood outside.

Weweni gii-nanaa’ichigaadeni biindig (nicely arranged was it inside); ikwe ezhi-nametood (it was like the work of a woman); jiibaakwaan gaye ateni (and some cooking had been done); weweni gaye gii-apishimoonike zhingobiinsa’ (and carefully laid were the balsam-boughs for sleeping); gaye dash misan atewan agwajiing (and there was also firewood outside).

13) > **Mii idash ekidod zeziikizid majiikiwis:  
    > **Accordingly said the one who was eldest:

Mii idash ekidod (and then said) zeziikizid majiikiwis (the eldest, the first-born):

14) > **“Skomaa waabang ningad-ab ninga-bii'aa awegwen ayaawigwen”.**

> "I will simply remain at home to-morrow. I will wait to see who it can be."

“Skomaa waabang ningad-ab (I will simply remain at home tomorrow) ninga-bii’aa (I will wait for) awegwen ayaawigwen” (whoever it can be).

15) > **Geget idash weyaabaninig gii-abi majiikiwis, gaawiin dash awiiya ogii-odisigosiin.**

> And truly on the morrow the first-born remained at home, but by nobody was he visited.

Geget idash weyaabaninig (and truly on the morrow) gii-abi majiikiwis (the first-born remained at home), gaawiin dash awiiya ogii-odisigosiin (but by nobody was he visited).

16) > **Miinawaa dash weyaabaninig gakina gii-maajaawag.**

> Therefore on the next day all went away.

Miinawaa dash weyaabaninig (and again on the next day) gakina gii-maajaawag (all went away).

17) > **Mii dash miinawaa gii-dagwishinoogwen a'aw ikwe; miinawaa gii-jiibaakwe sa; gakina gaye weweni gii-biinichige, gaawiin dash imaa ayaasii.**

> And then evidently must the woman have come again; again she must have done some cooking; and everything was nicely cleaned (in the wigwam), but she was not there.

Mii dash miinawaa gii-dagwishinoogwen a’aw ikwe (and then evidently the woman must have come again); miinawaa gii-jiibaakwe sa (again she must have cooked); gakina gaye weweni gii-biinichige (and everything was nicely cleaned), gaawiin dash imaa ayaasii (but she was not there).

18) > **Miinawaa dash weyaabang ani-aanike-zaziikizid gii-ikido:  
    > **So on the next day he that was the next in age said:

Miinawaa dash weyaabang (and again on the next day) ani-aanike-zaziikizid (the next in age) gii-ikido (said):

19) > **“Skomaa ninitam ningad-ab”.**

> "Just let me take a turn remaining at home."

“Skomaa ninitam ningad-ab” (just let me take my turn remaining at home).

20) > **Geget weyaabaninig gii-abi gabe-giizhig, gaawiin dash gaye wiin awiiya ogii-odisigosiin.**

> Verily, on the morrow he staid at home all day long, and by nobody was he visited.

Geget weyaabaninig gii-abi gabe-giizhig (truly on the morrow he stayed home all day long), gaawiin dash gaye wiin awiiya ogii-odisigosiin (and by nobody was he too visited).

21) > **Mii go iw gaa-doodamowaad biinish igo zhaangaswi igiw oskinaweg.**

> And that was what happened even to all the other nine youths.

Mii go iw gaa-doodamowaad (and that was what happened) biinish igo zhaangaswi igiw oskinaweg (even to all nine of the youths).

22) > **Mii dash a'aw nayaamawi oshiimeyimind, mii aw wiinitam ebid.**

> And now there was the one who was their younger brother, it was now his turn to remain at home.

Mii dash a’aw nayaamawi oshiimeyimind (and now there was the one who was the youngest brother), mii aw wiinitam ebid (it was his turn to remain at home).

23) > **Apii dash gakina gaa-maajaanid osayeya' mii iw gii-odisigod ikwewan; geget zazegaa-ikwewan.**

> And when all his elder brothers had gone away, then was he visited by a woman; indeed, she was a beautiful woman.

Apii dash gakina gaa-maajaanid osayeya’ (and when all his elder brothers had gone) mii iw gii-odisigod ikwewan (then was he visited by a woman); geget zazegaa-ikwewan (indeed a beautiful woman).

24) > **Mii dash gii-bi-onabiitaagod wiidigemigod.**

> And so by his side she came to sit to be his wife.

Mii dash gii-bi-onabiitaagod (and so by his side she came to sit) wiidigemigod (to be his wife).

25) > **Apii idash degwishinowaad oskinaweg gii-gichi-minwendamoog wabamaawaad ikwewan wiidigebid oshiimeyiwaan.**

> And when back home came the youths, they were very happy to see the woman that was a wife to their younger brother.

Apii idash degwishinowaad oskinaweg (and when the youths came home) gii-gichi-minwendamoog (they were very happy) wabamaawaad ikwewan (to see the woman) wiidigebid oshiimeyiwaan (who was wife to their younger brother).

26) > **Mii dash iniw gaa-bami'igoowaad gii-jiibaakwenid, gakina gaye odaya'iimiwaan gii-nanaa'itood a'aw ikwe.**

> Thereupon by her were they waited upon, for them she cooked, and all their garments the woman fixed.

Mii dash iniw gaa-bami’igoowaad (and then by her were they waited upon) gii-jiibaakwenid (she cooked for them), gakina gaye odaya’iimiwaan gii-nanaa’itood a’aw ikwe (and all their garments the woman fixed).

27) > **Mii idash gaa-inaakonigewaad moozhag wiinitam ji-dagwishing aw waadiged inini; a'aw idash zeziikizid, majiikiwis, gaawiin gii-minwendanzii.**

> And now it had been agreed among them that the one who was married would always come home first; but he who was the eldest, the first-born, did not like it.

Mii idash gaa-inaakonigewaad (and it had been agreed among them) moozhag wiinitam ji-dagwishing (that always first to come home) aw waadiged inini (would be the married man); a’aw idash zeziikizid, majiikiwis (but he, the eldest, the first-born), gaawiin gii-minwendanzii (did not like it).

28) > **Gii-inendam:  
    > **He thought:

Gii-inendam (he thought):

29) > **“Apegish niin wiidigemagiban\!”**

> "Would that I had been the one to marry her\!"

“Apegish niin wiidigemagiban\!” (would that I had been the one to marry her\!)

30) > **Ningoding idash gigizheb eni-maajaawaad apii gaa-ani-baked a'aw majiikiwis; gii-nibaawi megwe-zhingob gaa-waabamaad gakina gaa-bimosenid wiijikiwenya'; mii iw gii-giiwed.**

> Now, one morning they were setting out one after another, when the first-born had left to go his way; he stood among some balsams, watching all his brothers as they went walking past; and then he went back home.

Ningoding idash gigizheb (now one morning) eni-maajaawaad (as they were setting out) apii gaa-ani-baked a’aw majiikiwis (when the first-born had branched off to go his way); gii-nibaawi megwe-zhingob (he stood among the balsams) gaa-waabamaad gakina gaa-bimosenid wiijikiwenya’ (watching all his brothers as they walked past); mii iw gii-giiwed (and then he went back home).

31) > **Mii dash gii-gaazod besho wiigiwaaming.**

> Thereupon he hid himself near the wigwam.

Mii dash gii-gaazod (and then he hid himself) besho wiigiwaaming (near the wigwam).

32) > **Mii dash aw ikwe apii gaa-wishkwaataad biindig, mii dash bi-zaaga'ang wii-manised.**

> And so, after the woman had finished her work indoors, she then went outside to gather some firewood.

Mii dash aw ikwe apii gaa-wishkwaataad biindig (and so after the woman had finished her work indoors), mii dash bi-zaaga’ang wii-manised (she then went outside to gather firewood).

33) > **Bezhig idash mitigoon ogii-bimi-ganawaabamaan baatemisan.**

> There was a tree which she observed had dry wood.

Bezhig idash mitigoon (now one tree) ogii-bimi-ganawaabamaan baatemisan (she observed had dry wood).

34) > **Mii dash majiikiwis waabandang waasamowin, mii idash a'aw mitig gakina gii-bigiskised.**

> And then the first-born beheld a flash of lightning, and at that the entire tree was splintered into pieces.

Mii dash majiikiwis waabandang waasamowin (and then the first-born beheld a flash of lightning), mii idash a’aw mitig gakina gii-bigiskised (and at that the whole tree was splintered to pieces).

35) > **Mii dash ezhi-aawadood misan a'aw ikwe.**

> And then the woman began carrying the fire-wood.

Mii dash ezhi-aawadood misan a’aw ikwe (and then the woman began carrying the firewood).

36) > **Ningoding idash animikogaabaawinid mii iwe gii-ikwoteskawaad omitigwaabiin a'aw majiikiwis, odasawaan ogii-naabisitoon odajaabiin; mii idash giimooj ezhi-naazikawaad wiinimon mii dash ezhi-bimwaad.**

> Now, once while she had her back turned towards him, then it was that with his knee the first-born strung his bow, his feathered arrow he fixed upon the cord; thereupon slyly he went up to his sister-in-law, and then shot her.

Ningoding idash animikogaabaawinid (now once while she stood with her back turned) mii iwe gii-ikwoteskawaad omitigwaabiin a’aw majiikiwis (then with his knee the first-born strung his bow), odasawaan ogii-naabisitoon odajaabiin (his feathered arrow he fixed upon the cord); mii idash giimooj ezhi-naazikawaad wiinimon (and then slyly he went up to his sister-in-law) mii dash ezhi-bimwaad (and then he shot her).

37) > **Ogii-bi-ganawaabamigoon.**

> By her he was observed when approaching.

Ogii-bi-ganawaabamigoon (by her he was observed when approaching).

38) > **“Baapiniziwaagan, majiikiwis, ezhichigeyan\!”**

> "What foolishness, first-born, in what you are doing\!"

“Baapiniziwaagan, majiikiwis, ezhichigeyan\!” (what foolishness, first-born, in what you are doing\!)

39) > **Mii dash bijiinag gii-ani-maajaad majiikiwis.**

> And then presently on his way went the first-born.

Mii dash bijiinag (and then presently) gii-ani-maajaad majiikiwis (on his way went the first-born).

40) > **Apii dash ba-dagwishing a'aw inini wewiidigemaaganid, gaawiin ayaasiiwan imaa endaawaad.**

> Now, when home had come the man who had the wife, not present was she there where they lived.

Apii dash ba-dagwishing a’aw inini wewiidigemaaganid (now when the married man came home), gaawiin ayaasiiwan imaa endaawaad (she was not there where they lived).

41) > **Mii dash gii-andawaabamaad; imaa dash endashi-maniseban iko a'aw ikwe mii imaa gii-mikawaad agaawaa bimaadizinid.**

> Thereupon he went to look for her; now at the place where the woman was wont to gather firewood was where he found her barely yet alive.

Mii dash gii-andawaabamaad (thereupon he went to look for her); imaa dash endashi-maniseban iko a’aw ikwe (and at the place where the woman was wont to gather firewood) mii imaa gii-mikawaad agaawaa bimaadizinid (there he found her barely yet alive).

Mii dash enaad (therefore he said to her):

43) > **“Awenen gaa-doodook?”**

“Awenen gaa-doodook?” (who did this to you?)

Mii dash ekidod aw ikwe (whereupon said the woman):

45) > **“Mii aw gisayeniwaa zeziikizid mii aw gaa-bi-bimod zhebaa”.**

> "It was that elder brother of yours, first to be born, he was the one who shot me this morning."

“Mii aw gisayeniwaa zeziikizid (it was that elder brother of yours, the eldest) mii aw gaa-bi-bimod zhebaa” (he was the one who shot me this morning).

Mii dash egod (so then he was told):

“Nindawaa ningoji-izhiwizhishin” (please take me away somewhere).

Mii dash geget gii-maajiinad (whereupon truly he started away with her).

51) > **Baanimaa gii-midaasogonagak bi-nandawaabamishin.”**

52) > **Mii dash gii-giiwed a'aw inini; gii-gashkendang.**

Mii dash gii-giiwed a’aw inini (thereupon back home went the man); gii-gashkendang (he felt sad about it).

“Mii gosha aw gisayenaan gaa-bimwaad” (it was indeed our elder brother who shot her).

61) > **“Gidinigaa'idiz, ozaam wiiba gii-bi-nandawaabamiyan”.**

Mii idash imaa ginwenzh gii-ayaawaad (thereupon at that place they continued for a long while).

251) > **Mii sa iw binewidis gii-agoode.**

> And so the gizzard of the ruffed grouse now hangs aloft.

Mii sa iw binewidis (and so the gizzard of the ruffed grouse) gii-agoode (now hangs aloft) (the closing that leaves the story to the listener’s own wondering).
