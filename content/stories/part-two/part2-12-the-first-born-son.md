## **12. MAJIIKIWIS.**

## **THE FIRST-BORN SON.**

1)  > **Ningoding isa giiwenh gii-daa majiikiwis.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, once on a time, they say, there dwelt a first-born son.</span>

</div>


Ningoding isa giiwenh (now, once on a time, they say) gii-daa majiikiwis (there dwelt a first-born son).

2)  > **Midaachiwan ogwisan, odaanisa' gaye midaachiwa'.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Ten was the number of his sons, and his daughters were also ten.</span>

</div>


Midaachiwan ogwisan (ten in number were his sons), odaanisa’ gaye midaachiwa’ (and his daughters were also ten).

3)  > **Mii idash ekidowaad igiw oskinaweg:  
    > **Thereupon said the youths:

Mii idash ekidowaad (and then they said) igiw oskinaweg (those youths):

4)  > **“Aaniish, noose, mii isa zhigwa ji-bakewinigooyan.  
    > **"Well, my father, the time is now at hand for us to be leaving you.

“Aaniish, noose (well, my father), mii isa zhigwa (now is the time) ji-bakewinigooyan (for us to be leaving you).

5)  > **Bakaan akiing ningad-izhaamin”.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">To a different land are we going."</span>

</div>


Bakaan akiing (to a different land) ningad-izhaamin” (are we going).

6)  > **Mii dash geget gii-a-bagidinaad ogwisisa'; mii idash gii-maajaawaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so, in truth, he let his sons depart; whereupon they started away.</span>

</div>


Mii dash geget (and so, in truth) gii-a-bagidinaad ogwisisa’ (he let his sons depart); mii idash gii-maajaawaad (whereupon they started away).

7)  > **Apii idash gaa-oditamowaad ge-daawaad mii iw gii-ozhitoowaad wiigiwaam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when they came to the place where they were going to live, they then built a wigwam.</span>

</div>


Apii idash gaa-oditamowaad ge-daawaad (and when they reached where they were going to live) mii iw gii-ozhitoowaad wiigiwaam (they then built a wigwam).

8)  > **Mii dash imaa wenjiiwaad andawenjigewaad; anooji gegoo onitoonaawaa ge-miijiwaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so from that place they set out when they went to hunt for game; all kinds of things they killed, what they were to eat.</span>

</div>


Mii dash imaa wenjiiwaad andawenjigewaad (and so from that place they set out to hunt for game); anooji gegoo onitoonaawaa ge-miijiwaad (all kinds of things they killed, what they were to eat).

9)  > **Mii idash ezhichigewaad:  
    > **Now, this was what they did:

Mii idash ezhichigewaad (and this was what they did):

10) > **bezhigwanini omiikanaawa; nitam idash zeziikizid ani-bakemoni omiikana ezhaad nendawenjiged; miinawaa dash bezhig zeziikizid ani-bakemoni omiikana ezhaad nendawenjiged; mii go miinawaa eni-zaziikizid eni-bakemonik omiikana; mii go iw pane endoodamowaad nendawenjigewaad, biinish igo gakina babakewaad.  
    > **each of them had a road; now, the road of the eldest was the first to branch off towards where he was to hunt for game; and the road of the next eldest then branched off towards where he was to hunt for game;and so on, (as they stood) next in order of age, the road of each one went branching off; and now that was what they always did when they went to hunt, (the roads continued branching off) until all (the youths) had separated.

bezhigwanini omiikanaawa (each of them had a road); nitam idash zeziikizid (now first the eldest) ani-bakemoni omiikana (his road branched off) ezhaad nendawenjiged (where he went to hunt for game); miinawaa dash bezhig zeziikizid (and again the next eldest) ani-bakemoni omiikana (his road branched off) ezhaad nendawenjiged (where he went to hunt for game); mii go miinawaa eni-zaziikizid (and so again the next in age) eni-bakemonik omiikana (his road branched off); mii go iw pane endoodamowaad (and that was what they always did) nendawenjigewaad (when they hunted), biinish igo gakina babakewaad (until all of them had separated).

11) > **Ningoding idash degoshinowaad endaawaad owaabandaanaawaa awiya gii-dagwishininid endaawaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now once, when they had come back home, they observed that somebody had come to the place where they lived.</span>

</div>


Ningoding idash degoshinowaad endaawaad (now once, when they came home) owaabandaanaawaa (they observed) awiya gii-dagwishininid endaawaad (that somebody had come where they lived).

12) > **Weweni gii-nanaa'ichigaadeni biindig; ikwe ezhi-nametood; jiibaakwaan gaye ateni; weweni gaye gii-apishimoonike zhingobiinsa'; gaye dash misan atewan agwajiing.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Nice was the arrangement (of things) inside; it was like the work of a woman; and some cooking had been done; and carefully arranged were the balsam-boughs at the sleeping-places; and there was also some fire-wood outside.</span>

</div>


Weweni gii-nanaa’ichigaadeni biindig (nicely arranged was it inside); ikwe ezhi-nametood (it was like the work of a woman); jiibaakwaan gaye ateni (and some cooking had been done); weweni gaye gii-apishimoonike zhingobiinsa’ (and carefully laid were the balsam-boughs for sleeping); gaye dash misan atewan agwajiing (and there was also firewood outside).

13) > **Mii idash ekidod zeziikizid majiikiwis:  
    > **Accordingly said the one who was eldest:

Mii idash ekidod (and then said) zeziikizid majiikiwis (the eldest, the first-born):

14) > **“Skomaa waabang ningad-ab ninga-bii'aa awegwen ayaawigwen”.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"I will simply remain at home to-morrow. I will wait to see who it can be."</span>

</div>


“Skomaa waabang ningad-ab (I will simply remain at home tomorrow) ninga-bii’aa (I will wait for) awegwen ayaawigwen” (whoever it can be).

15) > **Geget idash weyaabaninig gii-abi majiikiwis, gaawiin dash awiiya ogii-odisigosiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And truly on the morrow the first-born remained at home, but by nobody was he visited.</span>

</div>


Geget idash weyaabaninig (and truly on the morrow) gii-abi majiikiwis (the first-born remained at home), gaawiin dash awiiya ogii-odisigosiin (but by nobody was he visited).

16) > **Miinawaa dash weyaabaninig gakina gii-maajaawag.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Therefore on the next day all went away.</span>

</div>


Miinawaa dash weyaabaninig (and again on the next day) gakina gii-maajaawag (all went away).

17) > **Mii dash miinawaa gii-dagwishinoogwen a'aw ikwe; miinawaa gii-jiibaakwe sa; gakina gaye weweni gii-biinichige, gaawiin dash imaa ayaasii.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then evidently must the woman have come again; again she must have done some cooking; and everything was nicely cleaned (in the wigwam), but she was not there.</span>

</div>


Mii dash miinawaa gii-dagwishinoogwen a’aw ikwe (and then evidently the woman must have come again); miinawaa gii-jiibaakwe sa (again she must have cooked); gakina gaye weweni gii-biinichige (and everything was nicely cleaned), gaawiin dash imaa ayaasii (but she was not there).

18) > **Miinawaa dash weyaabang ani-aanike-zaziikizid gii-ikido:  
    > **So on the next day he that was the next in age said:

Miinawaa dash weyaabang (and again on the next day) ani-aanike-zaziikizid (the next in age) gii-ikido (said):

19) > **“Skomaa ninitam ningad-ab”.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Just let me take a turn remaining at home."</span>

</div>


“Skomaa ninitam ningad-ab” (just let me take my turn remaining at home).

20) > **Geget weyaabaninig gii-abi gabe-giizhig, gaawiin dash gaye wiin awiiya ogii-odisigosiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Verily, on the morrow he staid at home all day long, and by nobody was he visited.</span>

</div>


Geget weyaabaninig gii-abi gabe-giizhig (truly on the morrow he stayed home all day long), gaawiin dash gaye wiin awiiya ogii-odisigosiin (and by nobody was he too visited).

21) > **Mii go iw gaa-doodamowaad biinish igo zhaangaswi igiw oskinaweg.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And that was what happened even to all the other nine youths.</span>

</div>


Mii go iw gaa-doodamowaad (and that was what happened) biinish igo zhaangaswi igiw oskinaweg (even to all nine of the youths).

<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And now there was the one who was their younger brother, it was now his turn to remain at home.</span>

</div>


Mii dash a’aw nayaamawi oshiimeyimind (and now there was the one who was the youngest brother), mii aw wiinitam ebid (it was his turn to remain at home).

<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when all his elder brothers had gone away, then was he visited by a woman; indeed, she was a beautiful woman.</span>

</div>


Apii dash gakina gaa-maajaanid osayeya’ (and when all his elder brothers had gone) mii iw gii-odisigod ikwewan (then was he visited by a woman); geget zazegaa-ikwewan (indeed a beautiful woman).

<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so by his side she came to sit to be his wife.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gii-bi-onabiitaagod (and so by his side she came to sit) wiidigemigod (to be his wife).

<div class="four-layer">

26) > **Mii dash iniw gaa-bami'igoowaad gii-jiibaakwenid, gakina gaye odaya'iimiwaan gii-nanaa'itood a'aw ikwe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon by her were they waited upon, for them she cooked, and all their garments the woman fixed.</span>

</div>


Mii dash iniw gaa-bami’igoowaad (and then by her were they waited upon) gii-jiibaakwenid (she cooked for them), gakina gaye odaya’iimiwaan gii-nanaa’itood a’aw ikwe (and all their garments the woman fixed).

27) > **Mii idash gaa-inaakonigewaad moozhag wiinitam ji-dagwishing aw waadiged inini; a'aw idash zeziikizid, majiikiwis, gaawiin gii-minwendanzii.**

> <span class="l-gloss">And now it had been agreed among them that the one who was married would always come home first; but he who was the eldest, the first-born, did not like it.</span>

</div>

Ningoding idash gigizheb (now one morning) eni-maajaawaad (as they were setting out) apii gaa-ani-baked a’aw majiikiwis (when the first-born had branched off to go his way); gii-nibaawi megwe-zhingob (he stood among the balsams) gaa-waabamaad gakina gaa-bimosenid wiijikiwenya’ (watching all his brothers as they walked past); mii iw gii-giiwed (and then he went back home).
