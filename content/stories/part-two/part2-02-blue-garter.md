# 2\. WOZHAAWAASKOKASKITAASEPASON.  
BLUE-GARTER.


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-src">***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***</span>

</div>


*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoting kiiwenh aiyaawag niizh apinoochiinyag; pezhik kwiiwisens, pezhik kwesens aiyaawag.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Once upon a time, so the story goes, there lived two children; a boy (and) a girl there lived.</span>

</div>


Ningoting kiiwenh (once, so the story goes) aiyaawag (there were) niizh apinoochiinyag (two children); pezhik kwiiwisens (one a boy), pezhik kwesens (one a girl) aiyaawag (there were).

2)  > **Mii dash eshkam ani-minditoowaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And larger they grew as time went on.</span>

</div>


Mii dash (and then) eshkam (more and more) ani-minditoowaat (they grew bigger as time went on).

3)  > **Mii dash kiiwenh kwiiwisens nantawenchiget, nisaat wa(a)boozoon; kegaa wiin dash ikwesens, manise chiibaakwe kaye endaawaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then, according to the story, the boy began to hunt for game (and) killed rabbits; and as for the girl, she gathered firewood and did the cooking at where they lived.</span>

</div>


Mii dash kiiwenh (and then, so the story goes) kwiiwisens (the boy) nantawenchiget (went hunting for game), nisaat (killing) waaboozoon (rabbits); kegaa wiin dash (and as for the other one) ikwesens (the girl), manise (she gathered firewood) chiibaakwe (and cooked) kaye endaawaat (there at where they lived).

4)  > **Mii dash kiwe**\[nh\] **eshkam ani-minditoowaat; aw dash kiiwenh kwiwisens eshkam ani-kichi-oshkinawewi, kichi-awesiinyan kaye onisaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then, as the story goes, they continued getting larger; and then they say that the boy grew to be somewhat of a young man, and then big game he killed.</span>

</div>


Mii dash kiwenh (and then, so the story goes) eshkam ani-minditoowaat (they kept growing bigger); aw dash kiiwenh kwiwisens (and that boy, they say) eshkam ani-kichi-oshkinawewi (gradually became a grown young man), kichi-awesiinyan kaye onisaan (and big game animals he killed).

5)  > **Eshkam aapichi mino-aiyaawag kiiwenh.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">In a very much more comfortable way then they lived, so it is said.</span>

</div>


Eshkam aapichi mino-aiyaawag kiiwenh (more and more comfortably they lived, so it is said).

6)  > **Niingoding idash kiiwenh, aiyayaawaad, odinaan omisenyan:  
    > **And so once, according to the story, while they were yet living at the place, said (the youth) to his elder sister:

Niingoding idash kiiwenh (and once, so the story goes), aiyayaawaad (while they were still living there), odinaan omisenyan (he said to his elder sister):

7)  > **"Nimisenh\! mi sa shayiigwa chi-pakediyank."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"O my elder sister\! the time is soon at hand when we shall part from each other."</span>

</div>


“Nimisenh\! (“my elder sister\!) mi sa shayiigwa (now soon) chi-pakediyank.” (we shall part from each other.”)

8)  > **"Aaniin nangwana, nishiim," odigoon.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Very well, so let it come, my little brother," he was told.</span>

</div>


“Aaniin nangwana, nishiim,” (“very well then, my little brother,”) odigoon (he was told).

9)  > **"Aiyaangwaamisi(i)n sa kaye kiin wii-pi(i)maadisoyan\!**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"And do be very careful of yourself if you wish to live\!</span>

</div>


“Aiyaangwaamisin sa kaye kiin (“do be careful of yourself too) wii-piimaadisoyan\! (if you want to live\!)

10) > **Pooch, inange moozhag kiiga-wiijiindimin."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">However, it is impossible that we should always live together."</span>

</div>


Pooch, inange (however, of course) moozhag (always) kiiga-wiijiindimin.” (we cannot keep living together.”)

11) > **Mii dash kiiwenh aw os**\[h\]**kinawenh kaa-izhi-maajaat, miinawaa odigoon omisenyan:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon, so it is said, as the youth started to go, once more was he told by his elder sister:</span>

</div>


Mii dash kiiwenh (and so, they say) aw oshkinawenh (that youth) kaa-izhi-maajaat (as he set off), miinawaa odigoon omisenyan (again he was told by his elder sister):

12) > **"Nishi(i)m, mikwenimishi(i)n ningoting kiishpin kegoo aapichi sanagisiyan."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"My little brother, think of me if ever at any time you are in deep trouble over something."</span>

</div>


“Nishiim (“my little brother), mikwenimishin ningoting (think of me sometime) kiishpin kegoo aapichi sanagisiyan.” (if you are ever in deep trouble over something.”)

13) > **"Eye'," odinaan, "nimisenh."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"I will, my elder sister," he said to her.</span>

</div>


“Eye’,” (“I will,”) odinaan, (he said to her,) “nimisenh.” (“my elder sister.”)

14) > **Mii dash maajaat aw oshkinawe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then went the youth away.</span>

</div>


Mii dash maajaat aw oshkinawe (and then away went the youth).

15) > **Ningoting idash ani-papimoset aapichi aiyekosi.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And once, as he was walking along, very weary he became,</span>

</div>


Ningoting idash (and once), ani-papimoset (as he walked along), aapichi aiyekosi (he grew very weary),

16) > **Mii dash enendank, "Mii omaanh chi-nibaayaan."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">whereupon he thought, "Now in this place will I sleep."</span>

</div>


Mii dash enendank (whereupon he thought), “Mii omaanh chi-nibaayaan.” (“now here I will sleep.”)

17) > **Mii dash keget imaa gii-ani-pagidi(i)nank opimiwanaanens.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so truly there laid he down his little pack.</span>

</div>


Mii dash keget imaa (and so truly there) gii-ani-pagidiinank (he laid down) opimiwanaanens (his little pack).

18) > **Pangii dash niigaan anii-izhaa, nandawa(a)bandank aanindi wa(a)baninig ked-ani-izhi-maajaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now a little farther on he went, taking a look whither on the morrow he would go.</span>

</div>


Pangii dash niigaan aniizhaa (a little farther ahead he went), nandawaabandank (looking over) aanindi waabaninig ked-ani-izhi-maajaat (where on the morrow he would go).

19) > **Wayiiba dash neyaap azhekiiwe kii-atoot obimiwanaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And in a little while back he came to where he had put his pack.</span>

</div>


Wayiiba dash neyaap azhekiiwe (and in a little while back he returned) kii-atoot obimiwanaan (to where he had put his pack).

20) > **Eni-tagwishing idash oowa(a)bandaan aazha apishimoonikaatenik, kaye aasha kii-oshichikaatenik chi(i)-wiisinit chi(i)-mibaat kaye.** \[…chi-nibaat kaye\]


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when he was come, he saw that a bed had already been made, and that there was also prepared some food for him to eat, and a place for him to sleep.</span>

</div>


Eni-tagwishing idash (and when he arrived) oowaabandaan (he saw) aazha apishimoonikaatenik (that a bed was already made), kaye aasha kii-oshichikaatenik (and that there was already prepared) chii-wiisinit (for him to eat) chii-nibaat kaye (and for him to sleep).

21) > **Aw widash** \[o\]**shkinawe mamakadentam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon the youth wondered.</span>

</div>


Aw widash oshkinawe mamakadentam (and that youth was astonished).

22) > **"Mii sa kanabach nimisenh kaa-ishichiget," inendam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Now, perhaps my elder sister did it," he thought.</span>

</div>


“Mii sa kanabach nimisenh (“now, perhaps my elder sister) kaa-ishichiget,” (is the one who did it,”) inendam (he thought).

23) > **Mii dash ezhi-maajii-wiisinit nibaat gaye.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon he began to eat, and then he went to sleep.</span>

</div>


Mii dash ezhi-maajii-wiisinit (thereupon he began to eat) nibaat gaye (and then he went to sleep).

24) > **Wayaabaninig idash miinawaa maajaa.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And on the following day he started on.</span>

</div>


Wayaabaninig idash miinawaa maajaa (and on the next day again he set off).

25) > **Miinawaa wenaagoshininig mii go miinawaa naasaab ezhiwepisit.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">When again it came evening, once more the same thing happened to him.</span>

</div>


Miinawaa wenaagoshininig (and again when evening came) mii go miinawaa naasaab ezhiwepisit (the same thing happened to him again).

26) > **Wenaagoshininig kii-nibaa miinawaa, mii dash miinawaa maajaa wayaabaninig.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">In the evening he went to sleep again, and then he went on when the morrow came again.</span>

</div>


Wenaagoshininig kii-nibaa miinawaa (in the evening he slept again), mii dash miinawaa maajaa wayaabaninig (and then again he set off when morning came).

27) > **Ningoting idash kiiwenh ani-papimoset, omikaan aapichi onishishininik; minonaagwatinig; aapichi dash kiisisoon sakaasigenit.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now once, so it is said, as he went walking along, he found a place that was very pleasing; it offered a beautiful view; and very bright did the sun shine there.</span>

</div>


Ningoting idash kiiwenh (and once, so the story goes), ani-papimoset (as he walked along), omikaan aapichi onishishininik (he came upon a very beautiful place); minonaagwatinig (it looked good); aapichi dash kiisisoon sakaasigenit (and very brightly the sun shone there).

28) > **Kii-wawenapi, inendang:  
    > **He sat down (and) began thinking:

Kii-wawenapi (he sat down), inendang (thinking):

29) > **"Waagotogwen kaa-onji-naganaawagen nimisenh?"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Why did I ever leave my elder sister?"</span>

</div>


“Waagotogwen kaa-onji-naganaawagen nimisenh?” (“why ever did I leave my elder sister?”)

30) > **Kas**\[h\]**kendank; bekish megwaa dash kiinwenh naanagatawendank, paanimaa go odoonchi-kikenimaan awiya naasikaagot pikwanaang inakakeyaa.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">He was sad; at the same time, they say, while he was in deep thought, suddenly he felt that somebody was coming towards him from behind.</span>

</div>


Kashkendank (he was sad); bekish megwaa dash kiinwenh (and meanwhile, they say) naanagatawendank (as he was deep in thought), paanimaa go odoonchi-kikenimaan awiya (suddenly he sensed someone) naasikaagot pikwanaang inakakeyaa (coming toward him from behind).

31) > **Mii dash pi-kanoonikot:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And he was addressed by the one who came saying:</span>

</div>


Mii dash pi-kanoonikot (and then he was addressed by the one who came):

32) > **"Wegonen maa weshitooyan?**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"What are you doing there?</span>

</div>


“Wegonen maa weshitooyan? (“what are you doing there?

33) > **Wegonen wenji-kichi-naanagatawentaman, kaye kas**\[h\]**kendaman?" odigoon iiniw; ikwewan nangwana iiniw.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">For what are you in such deep thought, and why are you sad?" (thus) was he told by that one; a woman did that one turn out to be.</span>

</div>


Wegonen wenji-kichi-naanagatawentaman (why are you in such deep thought), kaye kashkendaman?” (and why are you sad?”) odigoon iiniw (he was told by that one); ikwewan nangwana iiniw (a woman that one turned out to be).

34) > **Aapichi dash minwendam aw** \[o\]**shki(i)nawe wa(a)bamaat awiya ke-kakanoonaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And very pleased was the youth when he saw some one with whom to talk.</span>

</div>


Aapichi dash minwendam aw oshkiinawe (and very pleased was the youth) waabamaat awiya ke-kakanoonaat (when he saw someone to talk with).

35) > **Mii desh kiiwenh ezhi-kaakiikitoowaat, piinish chi-ozhitoowaat sisaagi’itiwaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And thereupon, so it is said, they began to converse, keeping it up till they felt mutual love for each other.</span>

</div>


Mii desh kiiwenh (and then, so the story goes) ezhi-kaakiikitoowaat (they began to converse), piinish (until) chi-ozhitoowaat sisaagi’itiwaat (they came to love each other).

36) > **Mii desh keget saagi’itiwaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And really did they love each other.</span>

</div>


Mii desh keget saagi’itiwaat (and truly they loved each other).

37) > **Mii desh aw ikwe ekidot:  
    > **And then the woman said:

Mii desh aw ikwe ekidot (and then the woman said):

38) > **"Pesho iima nindaamin; kanabach kegaa negoti tepaa’igan apii nindaamin," ikito aw ikwe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Close by yonder place is our home; perhaps almost a measure is the distance where we live," said the woman.</span>

</div>


“Pesho iima nindaamin (“close by yonder is our home); kanabach kegaa negoti tepaa’igan apii nindaamin,” (perhaps about a measure away is where we live,”) ikito aw ikwe (said the woman).

39) > **Mii desh kiiwenh kaye wiin oshkinawe ezhi-kagwechimaat chi-wiidigemaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And they say that the youth also progressed so far as to ask her to be his wife.</span>

</div>


Mii desh kiiwenh kaye wiin oshkinawe (and then, they say, the youth too) ezhi-kagwechimaat chi-wiidigemaat (asked her to marry him).

40) > **Mii desh kiiwenh aw ekwe ezhi-nakwomaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And they say that the woman gave him her consent.</span>

</div>


Mii desh kiiwenh aw ekwe ezhi-nakwomaat (and then, they say, the woman consented to him).

41) > **"Eye'," odinaan, "mii go iw kaa-izhi-nantotoonaan chi-izhi-kagwetchimiyan."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Yes," she said to him, "that is precisely what I expected you would ask me."</span>

</div>


“Eye’,” (“yes,”) odinaan, (she said to him,) “mii go iw kaa-izhi-nantotoonaan (“that is just what I expected) chi-izhi-kagwetchimiyan.” (you would ask me.”)

42) > **Mii desh kiinwenh ezhi-wiindamawaat onchi oosan ogiin kaye.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then they say that she told him about her father and her mother.</span>

</div>


Mii desh kiinwenh ezhi-wiindamawaat (and then, they say, she told him) onchi oosan ogiin kaye (about her father and her mother).

43) > **Odinaan:  
    > **She said to him:

Odinaan (she said to him):

44) > **"Aapichi kiiga-minotootaagoog was**\[h\]**ki-wa(a)pamikwaa; aapichi kaye intawaa wiingwesiwag kegoo chi-wiitootookwaa.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Very nicely will they treat you when first they behold you; and very apt are they, I would tell you, should they wish to do you some ill.</span>

</div>


“Aapichi kiiga-minotootaagoog (“very nicely they will treat you) washki-waapamikwaa (when first they see you); aapichi kaye intawaa wiingwesiwag (and they are very apt, I tell you) kegoo chi-wiitootookwaa (to wish to do you some harm).

45) > **Aiyaangwaamisin dash, kanawenindison\!" odigoon.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">So then be careful, (and) be on your guard\!" (thus) was he told by her.</span>

</div>


Aiyaangwaamisin dash (so be careful), kanawenindison\!” (and guard yourself\!”) odigoon (he was told).

46) > **Mii dash kiiwe**\[nh\] **iiniw ikwewan odigoon:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so by the woman was he told:</span>

</div>


Mii dash kiiwenh iiniw ikwewan odigoon (and so by the woman he was told):

47) > **"Kego segisikan\!**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Don't be afraid\!</span>

</div>


“Kego segisikan\! (“don’t be afraid\!

48) > **Pane kiigo-kanawenimi(i)n.** \[…pane giga-kanawenimin…\]


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Always will I look out for you.</span>

</div>


Pane kiigo-kanawenimin. (always I will look out for you.)

49) > **Aanawi apichi machi-inaatisiwag, pooch dash nin awashime niibiwa nindayaan kas**\[h\]**ki'ewisiwin chi-wi(i)tookoonaan."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">In spite of the very baneful disposition they have, yet, for all that, much stronger is the power I have to help you."</span>

</div>


Aanawi apichi machi-inaatisiwag (although they are of a very evil disposition), pooch dash nin awashime niibiwa nindayaan (yet I have far greater) kashki’ewisiwin (power) chi-wiitookoonaan.” (to help you.”)

50) > **Mii dash, kiinwenh, miinawaa enaat:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And they say that again she said to him:</span>

</div>


Mii dash, kiinwenh (and then, they say), miinawaa enaat (again she said to him):

51) > **"Aanish, mii sa niin ked-ani-**\[i\]**zhi-kiiweyaan; paanimaa dash kiin wenaagoshik pa-tagwishinookan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Well, it is now time for me to go back home; and not until it is evening must you come and arrive there.</span>

</div>


“Aanish, mii sa niin (“well, now it is) ked-ani-izhi-kiiweyaan (time for me to go back home); paanimaa dash kiin wenaagoshik (and not until evening, for your part) pa-tagwishinookan (must you come and arrive there).

52) > **Kiishpin dash pi-dagwishinan endaiyaang kiigat-igoog niingii'ikoog:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when you are come at our home, you will be asked by my parents:</span>

</div>


Kiishpin dash pi-dagwishinan endaiyaang (and when you arrive at our home) kiigat-igoog niingii’ikoog (you will be asked by my parents):

53) > **‘Kigii-wa(a)bamaa maawin a'aw nindaani(i)sinaan Ozhaawas**\[h\]**kokas**\[h\]**kitaasepison?’**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">‘You have no doubt seen our daughter Blue-Garter?’</span>

</div>


‘Kigii-waabamaa maawin (‘have you no doubt seen) a’aw nindaaniisinaan Ozhaawashkokashkitaasepison?’ (our daughter Blue-Garter?’)

54) > **Mii dash ked-i(i)nat:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then shall you say:</span>

</div>


Mii dash ked-iinat (and then you shall say):

55) > **‘Kaawin nin, kaawin nin, kaawin ningii-kikenimaasi, kaa wiikaa awiya ningii-wa(a)bamaasi.’ Mii ged-inatwaa."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">‘Not I, I do not know her, never have I seen her.’ Thus shall you say to them."</span>

</div>


‘Kaawin nin, kaawin nin (‘not I, not I), kaawin ningii-kikenimaasi (I do not know her), kaa wiikaa awiya ningii-waabamaasi.’ (never have I seen her.’) Mii ged-inatwaa.” (thus you shall say to them.”)

56) > **Mii dash keget ezhi-maajaat aw ikwe waa-ishi-maajaat; megwaa dash kaa-giigitot.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then truly went the woman away to the place whither she was bound; and as she went, she was yet speaking.</span>

</div>


Mii dash keget ezhi-maajaat aw ikwe (and then truly the woman set off) waa-ishi-maajaat (toward where she was going); megwaa dash kaa-giigitot (and as she went she was still speaking).

57) > **Mii go imaa kaa-onji-wani'aat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then at a place over there was where he lost sight of her.</span>

</div>


Mii go imaa kaa-onji-wani’aat (and there was where he lost sight of her).

58) > **Komaapii dash kaye wiin kii-izhichige kaa-igot iiniw ikwewan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so after a while he did what he was told by the woman.</span>

</div>


Komaapii dash kaye wiin kii-izhichige (and after a while he, for his part, did) kaa-igot iiniw ikwewan (what he was told by the woman).

59) > **Mii desh ezhi-ti(i)paachimint aw ikwe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, this was what was told of the woman.</span>

</div>


Mii desh ezhi-tiipaachimint aw ikwe (now this is what was told of the woman).

60) > **Megwaa ayaat endaat mii kii-kikenimaat iiniw ininiwan mikweninigot.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">While she was at home, she was aware that the man was thinking about her.</span>

</div>


Megwaa ayaat endaat (while she was at home) mii kii-kikenimaat iiniw ininiwan mikweninigot (she knew that the man was thinking of her).

61) > **Mii desh ki-maajaat aw ikwe kii-nandawa(a)bamaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then off went the woman to look for him.</span>

</div>


Mii desh ki-maajaat aw ikwe (and then the woman set off) kii-nandawaabamaat (to look for him).

62) > **Mii desh ki-mikawaat; pikwanaaning kii-onji-naasikawaat kii-wa(a)bamaat.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And she found him; from behind came she up to him (and) saw him.</span>

</div>


Mii desh ki-mikawaat (and she found him); pikwanaaning kii-onji-naasikawaat (from behind she came up to him) kii-waabamaat (and saw him).

63) > **Mii nangwana kiiwenh aw ikwe endaawaat kii-ayaat moonzhag.**


<div class="four-layer">
