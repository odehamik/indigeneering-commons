## **15. WÄNDCĪWĀD ẠNIMUCẠG**

## **WENJIIWAAD ANIMOOSHAG**

## **THE ORIGIN OF DOGS**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).</span>

</div>


*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoding niizh anishinaabeg bimishkaawaad jiimaaning gichigaming iwidi giiwedinong inakake mii iw gaa-izhi-gichi-noodininig; mii dash gaa-izhiwebaazhiwaad mizhawagaam gichigaming; gaawiin ogii-waabandanziinaawaa aki.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Once on a time two men were paddling along in their canoe on the sea by a northerly route, when there arose a mighty wind; accordingly they were blown by the wind far out at sea; they did not see land.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ningoding (once) niizh anishinaabeg (two people) bimishkaawaad jiimaaning (were paddling along in a canoe) gichigaming (on the sea) iwidi giiwedinong inakake (yonder toward the north) mii iw gaa-izhi-gichi-noodin (when there arose a great wind).

2)  > **Aapiji gii-gichi-noodinini, gaawiin gii-gonaboshkaasiiwag; ginwenzh gichigaming gii-babaa-ayaawag.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Very strong blew the wind, but they were not capsized; a long while over the sea they continued aimlessly.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Aapiji gii-gichi-noodinini (very strongly it blew), gaawiin gii-gonaboshkaasiiwag (they were not capsized); ginwenzh gichigaming (for a long while on the sea) gii-babaa-ayaawag (they went about aimlessly).

3)  > **Ningoding gii-agwaaweba'oowag agaamakiing.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*In course of time they were carried by the sea upon shore.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ningoding (in time) gii-agwaaweba’oowag (they were washed ashore by the waves) agaamakiing (onto the land across the water).

4)  > **Gii-gabaawaad owaabandaanawaa awiya bimikawenid gichi-anishinaaben.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*When they landed, they saw the footprints of some mighty human being.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gii-gabaawaad (when they had landed) owaabandaanawaa (they saw) awiya bimikawenid (someone’s tracks passing) gichi-anishinaaben (of a great person).

5)  > **Mii dash gii-zegiziwaad ogii-gopiwidoonaawaa ojiimaaniwaa, mii dash i'imaa anaamoonag gii-gaazowaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Therefore, becoming alarmed, they carried their canoe up from the shore, and then there underneath they hid themselves.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash gii-zegiziwaad (and so, being afraid) ogii-gopiwidoonaawaa ojiimaaniwaa (they carried their canoe up from the shore), mii dash i’imaa anaamoonag (and then there underneath it) gii-gaazowaad (they hid themselves).

6)  > **Ningoding onoondaanaawaa gegoo madwesininig; mii dash inaabiwaad waabandaanaawaa gichi-bikwak atenig.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*By and by they heard the sound of something fall; and when they looked, they saw a huge arrow there.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ningoding (by and by) onoondaanaawaa gegoo madwesininig (they heard something fall with a sound); mii dash inaabiwaad (and when they peeked) waabandaanaawaa gichi-bikwak atenig (they saw a huge arrow lying there).

7)  > **Mii dash geget zegiziwaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon, to be sure, were they afraid.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash geget zegiziwaad (and then truly they were afraid).

8)  > **Wiiba go ogii-bi-odisikoowaan gichi-anishinaaben, oganoonigowaan:  
    > ***And in a little while to where they were came a great human being, and they were addressed by him saying:  
    > *

Wiiba go (in a little while) ogii-bi-odisikoowaan (they were come upon by) gichi-anishinaaben (a great person), oganoonigowaan (and they were spoken to by him):

9)  > **"Nishiime, gego zegizikegon\!  
    > ***"My little brothers, do not be afraid\!  
    > *

“Nishiime (my little brothers), gego zegizikegon\! (do not be afraid\!)

10) > **Niin misaabe egooyaan.  
    > ***I am he who is called Giant,  
    > *

Niin misaabe egooyaan (I am the one called Giant).

11) > **Gaawiin niin nimbanaaji'aasiig anishinaabeg," odigowaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*I do not destroy people," they were told.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gaawiin niin nimbanaaji’aasiig anishinaabeg (I do not harm people),” odigowaan (they were told by him).

12) > **Owaabamaawaan adikwan zhegwazonid, mii wiin iniw bebaabimwaad wii-amwaad a'aw misaabe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*They saw a caribou hanging from the girdle at his side; and that was what Giant had wandered off to shoot (with his arrow), that he might have it to eat.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Owaabamaawaan adikwan zhegwazonid (they saw a caribou hanging at his side), mii wiin iniw bebaabimwaad wii-amwaad a’aw misaabe (that was the thing Giant had gone roving to shoot, wanting to eat it).

13) > **Mii idash gaa-izhi-odaapinigowaad aapiji mindidowan iniw misaaben.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon were they taken up by Giant, who was so very huge.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii idash gaa-izhi-odaapinigowaad (and so they were taken up) aapiji mindidowan iniw misaaben (by that Giant who was so very big).

14) > **Ogii-biindomonaan iniw anishinaaben a'aw misaabe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*In the bosom of his garment Giant carried the people.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ogii-biindomonaan iniw anishinaaben a’aw misaabe (Giant carried the people in the bosom of his garment).

15) > **Mii dash gii-giiwewinigowaad endaanid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*And so they were carried back to where he lived.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash gii-giiwewinigowaad endaanid (and so they were carried back to where he lived).

16) > **Apii idash gaa-biindigewaad, naanaage gii-biindige aw wiindigoo.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*And when they had gone in, then after a while in went the Wīndigō.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Apii idash gaa-biindigewaad (and when they had gone in), naanaage gii-biindige aw wiindigoo (after a while the Wiindigoo came in).

17) > **Mii dash enaad iniw misaaben a'aw wiindigoo:  
    > ***Whereupon to Giant said the Wīndigō:  
    > *

Mii dash enaad iniw misaaben a’aw wiindigoo (and so the Wiindigoo said to Giant):

18) > **"Anishinaabeg gidayaawaag," odinaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"Some people you have," he said to him.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Anishinaabeg gidayaawaag (you have people),” odinaan (he said to him).

19) > **Ogii-gaanigowaan biindig endaanid iniw misaaben jibwaa-biindigenid iniw wiindigoon.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*They had been concealed inside of where Giant lived before the Wīndigō came in.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ogii-gaanigowaan biindig endaanid iniw misaaben (they had been hidden inside Giant’s dwelling by him) jibwaa-biindigenid iniw wiindigoon (before the Wiindigoo came in).

20) > **"Gaawiin anishinaabeg nindayaawaasiig," ikido a'aw misaabe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"No people have I," said Giant.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Gaawiin anishinaabeg nindayaawaasiig (I have no people),” ikido a’aw misaabe (said Giant).

21) > **"Geget," ikido a'aw wiindigoo.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"Yes, (you have)," said the Wīndigō.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Geget (truly, you do),” ikido a’aw wiindigoo (said the Wiindigoo).

22) > **Mii go ezhi-noondaagozid ikwanaamod.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Accordingly the sound of him was heard as he drew in his breath.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii go ezhi-noondaagozid (and then he was heard making a sound) ikwanaamod (as he drew in his breath).

23) > **"Oop\!" ikoonamod a'aw wiindigoo, epiichi-misawenimaad anishinaaben wii-amwaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"Up\!"1 was the way the Wīndigō sucked in his breath, so eager was he to have the people to eat.*</span>

</div>


“Oop\!” ikoonamod a’aw wiindigoo (“Oop\!” was the sound the Wiindigoo made drawing in his breath), epiichi-misawenimaad anishinaaben wii-amwaad (so much did he crave the people, wanting to eat them).


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">1 Uttered by drawing in the breath.</span>

</div>


24) > **Gegapii igo nishkaadizi ginwenzh gaganoonidiwaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*At length he grew angry while in long conversation with (Giant).  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gegapii igo nishkaadizi (at last he grew angry) ginwenzh gaganoonidiwaad (as the two of them talked together a long while).

25) > **Gegapii gaye wiin misaabe nishkaadizi.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Finally Giant too became angry.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gegapii gaye wiin misaabe nishkaadizi (finally Giant too grew angry).

26) > **Mitigonaagan ateni imaa biindig endaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*A wooden bowl was there inside of where he lived.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mitigonaagan ateni imaa biindig endaad (a wooden bowl lay there inside where he lived).

27) > **Mii dash i'iw gabaagigwaadang, mii dash imaa animoons ayaad imaa anaamaya'ii onaaganing, odayaansan a'aw misaabe.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*And so, prying it up with a stick, behold\! a dog was there underneath the bowl, the pet of Giant.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash i’iw gabaagigwaadang (and so, prying it up with a stick), mii dash imaa animoons ayaad (there was a little dog) imaa anaamaya’ii onaaganing (there beneath the bowl), odayaansan a’aw misaabe (the little pet of Giant).

28) > **"Daga, bashkwadaash, awi-miigaazh a'aw maji-anishinaabe."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"Up, Hairless, go fight the impious man\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Daga, bashkwadaash (come now, Hairless), awi-miigaazh a’aw maji-anishinaabe (go fight that bad person).”

29) > **Mii iw geget bazigwiid a'aw animoons;**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*So then truly up to his feet rose the dog;  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii iw geget bazigwiid a’aw animoons (and then truly the little dog rose to his feet);

30) > **gii-babawi, mii dash gii-ani-mindidod.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*he shook himself, whereupon he began growing in size.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


gii-babawi (he shook himself), mii dash gii-ani-mindidod (and then he began growing big).

31) > **Eshkam gii-ani-babawi a'aw animoosh, aapiji gii-ani-mindidod a'aw animoosh.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*The more the dog shook himself, ever so much larger he continued to grow.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Eshkam gii-ani-babawi a’aw animoosh (the more the dog kept shaking himself), aapiji gii-ani-mindidod a’aw animoosh (the bigger the dog kept growing).

32) > **Apii dash wayaabamaad animoon mindidonid gii-ani-zaaga'am a'aw wiindigoo.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*So, when the Wīndigō saw the dog that now was (so) big, then on out of doors he went.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Apii dash wayaabamaad animoon mindidonid (and when he saw the dog grown big) gii-ani-zaaga’am a’aw wiindigoo (the Wiindigoo went out of doors).

33) > **Ogii-gagaanzomaan odayaan ji-nisaad wiindigoon.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*(Giant) urged on his pet to slay the Wīndigō.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Ogii-gagaanzomaan odayaan ji-nisaad wiindigoon (he urged on his pet to kill the Wiindigoo).

34) > **Mii dash gii-miigaanaad iniw wiindigoon a'aw animoosh biinish ogii-nisaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Accordingly the dog fought with the Wīndigō till he slew him.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash gii-miigaanaad iniw wiindigoon a’aw animoosh (and so the dog fought that Wiindigoo) biinish ogii-nisaan (until he killed him).

35) > **Apii idash gaa-nisaad, miinawaa gii-biindige a'aw animoosh.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*And when he had slain him, then back inside came the dog.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Apii idash gaa-nisaad (and when he had killed him), miinawaa gii-biindige a’aw animoosh (the dog came back inside again).

36) > **Gii-babawi miinawaa; eshkam igo gii-agaashiinyi eniginiban miinawaa gii-inigini.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*He shook himself once more; and smaller he grew, as big as he was before was now his size again.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gii-babawi miinawaa (he shook himself again); eshkam igo gii-agaashiinyi (steadily he grew smaller) eniginiban miinawaa gii-inigini (and was once more the size he had been).

37) > **Miinawaa gii-biindige imaa anaamaya'ii onaaganishing.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Back he went beneath the miserable bowl.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Miinawaa gii-biindige imaa anaamaya’ii onaaganishing (again he went in there beneath the wretched bowl).

38) > **Mii dash egowaad iniw misaaben:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon they were told by Giant:  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash egowaad iniw misaaben (and so they were told by Giant):

39) > **"Nishiimeyidog, onjida ningii-inendam ji-bi-izhaayeg o'omaa.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"My little brothers, a purpose did I have in mind, that you should come here.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Nishiimeyidog (my little brothers), onjida ningii-inendam ji-bi-izhaayeg o’omaa (on purpose I had it in mind that you should come here).

40) > **Wii-waabamininagog.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*I wanted to see you.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Wii-waabamininagog (I wanted to see you).

41) > **Mii sa indawaa ji-giiweyeg.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Therefore now you may go back home.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii sa indawaa ji-giiweyeg (so now you may go home).

42) > **Wa'awe nindayaans gimiininim.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*This little pet of mine I will give to you.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Wa’awe nindayaans gimiininim (this little pet of mine I give to you).

43) > **Gaawiin miinawaa da-bi-giiwesii.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Not back here will he come again.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gaawiin miinawaa da-bi-giiwesii (he will not come back here again).

44) > **Gaawiin awiya animosh ayaasii iwidi giinawaa endanakiiyeg.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*There is no dog over there where you dwell.  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Gaawiin awiya animosh ayaasii iwidi giinawaa endanakiiyeg (there is no dog over there where you dwell).

45) > **Maagizhaa eniweg gad-aabaji'aawaa, gegaa go anishinaabeng da-izhiwebizi."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Perhaps to some kind of use you can put him, and almost like a human being will he behave."  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Maagizhaa eniweg gad-aabaji’aawaa (perhaps you can put him to some use), gegaa go anishinaabeng da-izhiwebizi (and almost like a person he will behave).”

46) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-ganoonaad iniw odayaansan a'aw misaabe:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Accordingly to his pet spoke Giant:  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-ganoonaad iniw odayaansan a’aw misaabe (and so Giant spoke to his little pet):

47) > **"Daga, giiwewizh ogow nishiimeyag\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"Come, do you take back home these little brothers of mine\!"  </span>
> <span class="l-eng">*</span>

</div>


“Daga, giiwewizh ogow nishiimeyag\! (come, take these little brothers of mine back home\!)”

48) > **Ogii-naazibiiwinaan iniw adayaansan; nawaj gii-ani-mindido a'aw bashkwadaash.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*He carried his little pet down to the water; much bigger now did Hairless continue to grow.*</span>

</div>


Ogii-naazibiiwinaan iniw adayaansan (he carried his little pet down to the water); nawaj gii-ani-mindido a’aw bashkwadaash (Hairless kept growing bigger).

49) > **Ogii-niibawi'aan iniw odayaan, mii dash imaa ogidaawigan gii-asaad iniw anishinaaben.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*He placed his pet in standing position, and then on the (dog´s) back he put the people.*</span>

</div>


Ogii-niibawi’aan iniw odayaan (he set his pet standing), mii dash imaa ogidaawigan gii-asaad iniw anishinaaben (and then on his back he placed the people).

50) > **"Aw," mii iw izhi-maajiizh, gwayak endanakiiwaad izhiwizh\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*"There, now do you bear them hence, straight to their native land do you carry them\!"*</span>

</div>


“Aw (now),” mii iw izhi-maajiizh (so off you take them), gwayak endanakiiwaad izhiwizh\! (straight to their homeland carry them\!)”

51) > **Mii dash gii-maajiibatood a'aw bashkwadaash, eshkam gii-ani-mindido; gii-aazhawagaamebatood iwe gichi-gichi-gaming; ayaabitawagaad eta gii-akobii biinish gii-dagwishing omaa akiing.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*And so, when off on a run started Hairless, bigger he kept on getting; through the water he ran, crossing over to the other shore of the great sea; half way up the legs only was he in the water, continuing thus till he reached this country here.*</span>

</div>


Mii dash gii-maajiibatood a’aw bashkwadaash (and so Hairless set off running), eshkam gii-ani-mindido (steadily he grew bigger); gii-aazhawagaamebatood iwe gichi-gichi-gaming (he ran across the very great sea); ayaabitawagaad eta gii-akobii (only halfway up his legs was he in the water) biinish gii-dagwishing omaa akiing (until he arrived here in this land).

52) > **Eshkam miinawaa gii-bi-agaashinyi apii gegaa ba-oditang owe aki, biinish miinawaa animoonsing gii-inigini a'aw bashkwadaash.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Smaller again he grew when on his way hither he was nearly reaching this land, continuing thus till as big as a little dog was Hairless.*</span>

</div>


Eshkam miinawaa gii-bi-agaashinyi (steadily again he grew smaller) apii gegaa ba-oditang owe aki (when he had nearly reached this land), biinish miinawaa animoonsing gii-inigini a’aw bashkwadaash (until Hairless was once more the size of a little dog).

53) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-naganigowaad iniw animoon, wiinawaa dash gii-giiwewag endaawaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*Thereupon were they abandoned by the dog, so then they themselves came on back to where they lived.*</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-naganigowaad iniw animoon (and so they were left behind by the dog), wiinawaa dash gii-giiwewag endaawaad (and they themselves went home to where they lived).

54) > **Baanimaa idash ningoding ogii-odisigowaan animoon, mii go iw gii-bi-inawemigowaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*And not till later on, to where they were did the dog come, whereupon he made up with them at once.*</span>

</div>


Baanimaa idash ningoding ogii-odisigowaan animoon (and not until some time later did the dog come to them), mii go iw gii-bi-inawemigowaad (whereupon he became kin to them at once).

55) > **Mii go iw miziwe gaa-izhiwebak akiing gii-odisigowaad animooshan igiw anishinaabeg.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*That, accordingly, is what happens everywhere upon earth when a dog comes to people.*</span>

</div>


Mii go iw miziwe gaa-izhiwebak akiing (and so it is that this happens everywhere on earth) gii-odisigowaad animooshan igiw anishinaabeg (when dogs come to people).

56) > **Mii iw bijiinag apii gii-ayaawaad animoog o'omaa akiing; mii aw bashkwadaash oniijaanisa', niibawa daswewaanagizi a'aw animoosh ezhinikaazod.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*That was then the time when they began having dogs here on earth; they were the offspring of Hairless, of many kinds of dogs they had, of every kind of appearance were the dogs; numerous is the kind that goes by the name of dog.  </span>
