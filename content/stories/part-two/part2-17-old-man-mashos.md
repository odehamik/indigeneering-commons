1)  > **Ningoding sa Omashoos gii-ayindaa; gaawiin awiya wiiwan ayaasiiwan; odaanisa' eta niizhiwa; oningwanan ayaawan, wiidigemaawaan iigiw ikwewag.**  
    > Once on a time Mashos was living (there); without his wife was he; his daughters numbered only two; his son-in-law was there, to him were the women wedded.

Ningoding sa (once upon a time) Omashoos gii-ayindaa (Mashos was living there); gaawiin awiya wiiwan ayaasiiwan (he had no wife); odaanisa’ eta niizhiwa (only two were his daughters); oningwanan ayaawan (his son-in-law was there), wiidigemaawaan iigiw ikwewag (married to him were the women).

2)  > **Naningoding idash ikido a'aw inini:  
    > **Now, once said the man:

Naningoding idash (now once in a while) ikido a’aw inini (the man said):

3)  > **"Apegish gii-gikendamaan ningoji ayaawaad gayaashkwag\!  
    > **"Would that I knew of a place where there were gulls\!

“Apegish gii-gikendamaan (“would that I knew of) ningoji ayaawaad gayaashkwag\! (somewhere gulls are\!)

4)  > **Nindaa-gii-naazikaanan waawanoon."  
    > **I would go fetch some eggs."

Nindaa-gii-naazikaanan waawanoon.” (I would go and fetch some eggs.”)

5)  > **Omashoos idash onoondawaan ikidonid oningwanan.  
    > **Now, Mashos heard his son-in-law speak.

Omashoos idash onoondawaan (now Mashos heard) ikidonid oningwanan (his son-in-law speaking).

6)  > **"Aaniin ekidod na'aangi?"  
    > **"What does son-in-law say?"

“Aaniin ekidod na’aangi?” (“what is the son-in-law saying?”)

7)  > **"Aaniin isa ezhi-babaamitawad i'iw ekidod?" odigoon odaanisan.  
    > **"Now, what interest have you in listening to what he says?" he was told by his daughter.

“Aaniin isa ezhi-babaamitawad (“why are you minding) i’iw ekidod?” (what he says?”) odigoon odaanisan (he was told by his daughter).

8)  > **"'Apegish gikendamaan ningoji ayaawaad gayaashkwag\!  
    > **"'Would that I knew of a place where there were gulls\!

“‘Apegish gikendamaan (“‘would that I knew) ningoji ayaawaad gayaashkwag\! (somewhere gulls are\!)

9)  > **Nindaa-gii-izhaa', ikido," odigoon odaanisan.  
    > **I would go thither,' he said," he was told by his daughter.

Nindaa-gii-izhaa’, ikido,” (I would go there,’ he says,”) odigoon odaanisan (she told him).

10) > **Mii dash ekidod Omaashos:  
    > **Thereupon said Mashos:

Mii dash ekidod Omaashos: (thereupon Mashos said:)

11) > **"Ayii'ii, iwidi sa ayaawag gayaashkwag.  
    > **"Why, far off yonder are some gulls.

“Ayii’ii, iwidi sa ayaawag gayaashkwag. (“why, far off yonder there are gulls.)

12) > **Aaniish, gigad-izhaamin isa," ikido Omaashos.  
    > **Why, we will go over there," said Mashos.

Aaniish, gigad-izhaamin isa,” (“well, we will go there,”) ikido Omaashos (said Mashos).

13) > **Mii dash booziwaad Omashoos ojiimaaning, gii-namadabiwan onigwanan Ojiimaaning; wiin dash Omaashos ezhi-bakite'aang ojiimaan, mii iw ezhi-maajiibidenig ojiimaan.  
    > **And when they got into Mashos’ canoe, then down sat his son-in-law; and when Mashos struck his canoe, then away it sped.

Mii dash booziwaad (and so they got aboard) Omashoos ojiimaaning (Mashos’ canoe), gii-namadabiwan onigwanan Ojiimaaning (his son-in-law sat down in the canoe); wiin dash Omaashos (and Mashos himself) ezhi-bakite’aang ojiimaan (struck his canoe a blow), mii iw ezhi-maajiibidenig ojiimaan (and so the canoe sped away).

14) > **Weditamowaad gayaashkwaabik, gii-gabewag moozhaginamowaad waawanoon.  
    > **When they came to the rocky cliff of the gulls, they went ashore to gather up the eggs.

Weditamowaad gayaashkwaabik (when they reached the gulls’ rocky cliff), gii-gabewag (they went ashore) moozhaginamowaad waawanoon (gathering up the eggs).

15) > **Apii dash niibiwa gaa-ayaawaad waawanoon, "Mii iw debiseg," odinaan oningwanan.  
    > **And when they had many eggs (gathered), "That is enough," he said to his son-in-law.

Apii dash niibiwa gaa-ayaawaad waawanoon (and when they had many eggs), “Mii iw debiseg,” (“that is enough,”) odinaan oningwanan (he said to his son-in-law).

16) > **Gaa-booziwaad, oganoonaan oningwanan:  
    > **On getting into (the canoe), he spoke to his son-in-law, saying:

Gaa-booziwaad (when they had got aboard), oganoonaan oningwanan: (he spoke to his son-in-law:)

17) > **"Tiwe\! ningii-waniikenan iwidi waawanoon, geyaabi ningii-wakosidoonaabanini.  
    > **"Pshaw\! I have forgotten some eggs over there, some more that I had put into a pile.

“Tiwe\! (“pshaw\!) ningii-waniikenan iwidi waawanoon (I have forgotten some eggs over there), geyaabi ningii-wakosidoonaabanini. (some more I had piled up.)

18) > **Daga naazikan\!" odinaan oningwanan.  
    > **Do go get them\!" he said to his son-in-law.

Daga naazikan\!” (“do go fetch them\!”) odinaan oningwanan (he said to his son-in-law).

19) > **Gii-gabe miinawaa a'aw inini.  
    > **Ashore again stepped the man.

Gii-gabe miinawaa a’aw inini (ashore again stepped the man).

20) > **Ga-gabenid oningwanan, gii-bakite'aan ojiimaanish, gii-naganaad oningwanan.  
    > **When his son-in-law had stepped ashore, then he struck his miserable canoe a blow, leaving his son-in-law behind.

Ga-gabenid oningwanan (when his son-in-law had stepped ashore), gii-bakite’aan ojiimaanish (he struck his wretched canoe a blow), gii-naganaad oningwanan (leaving his son-in-law behind).

    > **He spoke to the Gulls, saying:

Oganoonaa’ gayaashkwa’: (he spoke to the gulls:)

    > **"Now, I give you a man to eat\!

“A’aw gidashamininim a’aw inini\! (“here, I give you this man to eat\!)

23) > **Moozhag ginandodamawim inini."  
    > **Always have you asked of me a man."

Moozhag ginandodamawim inini.” (always you have been asking me for a man.”)

24) > **Mii geget bi-izhaawaad kischi-gayaashkwag wii-amwawaad iniw ininiwan.  
    > **Then truly came the great Gulls to feed upon the man.

Mii geget bi-izhaawaad (then truly came) kischi-gayaashkwag (the great gulls) wii-amwawaad iniw ininiwan (to devour that man).

25) > **Aw idash inini oganoonaa' gayaashkwa':

Aw idash inini (and the man) oganoonaa’ gayaashkwa’: (spoke to the gulls:)

26) > **"Bekaa, bekaa\! Gego amwoshikegon\!  
    > **"Hold, hold\! do not eat me\!

“Bekaa, bekaa\! (“hold, hold\!) Gego amwoshikegon\! (do not eat me\!)

27) > **Neshwaasogoniiyaan gigii-zhawenimimwaaban."  
    > **When I (once) fasted for eight days, you took pity upon me."

Neshwaasogoniiyaan (when I fasted eight days) gigii-zhawenimimwaaban.” (you took pity on me.”)

28) > **Gaawiin dash ogii-amwogoosii\[n\] iiniw gayaashkwan.

Gaawiin dash ogii-amwogoosii\[n\] iiniw gayaashkwan (and he was not eaten by the gulls).

29) > **Ogii-ganoonaa' idash gayaashkwa' a'aw inini ji-giiwewinigod.

Ogii-ganoonaa’ idash gayaashkwa’ a’aw inini (then the man spoke to the gulls) ji-giiwewinigod (to be carried back home).

30) > **Mii iw geget ezhi-bimiwinigod bezhig gischi-gayaashkwan.  
    > **Thereupon truly was he carried away by a certain Great-Gull.

Mii iw geget ezhi-bimiwinigod (thereupon truly he was carried off) bezhig kischi-gayaashkwan (by a certain great gull).

31) > **Aazha waasa ani-ayaa a'aw Omashoos.  
    > **Already far on his way had Mashos gone.

Aazha waasa ani-ayaa a’aw Omashoos (already far away had Mashos gone).

32) > **Mii dash imaanh dibishkoo gaa-ani-izhaad a'aw chi-gayaashk ogii-ani-miijinaan Omashoosan.  
    > **And so, when directly over him Great-Gull was flying, then did he mute upon Mashos.

Mii dash imaanh dibishkoo (and so when right above him) gaa-ani-izhaad a’aw chi-gayaashk (the great gull was passing), ogii-ani-miijinaan Omashoosan (he muted upon Mashos).

33) > **Omashoos idash gaa-izhi-gaashkibidood gayaashkwimo ogii-gojimaandaan.  
    > **And when Mashos scraped away the gull-mute, he examined its smell.

Omashoos idash gaa-izhi-gaashkibidood gayaashkwimo (and when Mashos scraped off the gull-dung) ogii-gojimaandaan (he smelled it).

34) > **"Mii igo iw ezhimaagamowewaad gayashkwaag ininiwan gaa-amwaawaadin."  
    > **"Such is the smell of their mute when the gulls have eaten a man."

“Mii igo iw ezhimaagamowewaad gayashkwaag (“such is the smell of the gulls’ dung) ininiwan gaa-amwaawaadin.” (when they have eaten a man.”)

35) > **Wiinitam gii-dagwishin a'aw inini endaawaad.  
    > **The first to arrive at home was the man.

Wiinitam gii-dagwishin a’aw inini (the man was the first to arrive) endaawaad (at their home).

36) > **Waawanoon bangii ogii-giiwewidoonan.

Waawanoon bangii ogii-giiwewidoonan (a few eggs he had carried home).

37) > **Niizhiwan onijaanisan a'aw inini.

Niizhiwan onijaanisan a’aw inini (two were the man’s children).

Apii idash mezhagaad a’aw Omashoos (and when Mashos came ashore by canoe), bi-naazibiiwan oozhisa’ (down to the water came his grandchildren); waawanoon odakonaanaawaan iigiw abinoojiiyag (eggs the children were holding in their hands).

“Aaniindi gaa-ondinameg?” (“where did you get them?”) odinaan Omashoos (Mashos said to them).

Mii dash enaad: (thereupon he said to them:)

“Mewi(n)zha gosha (“why, long ago) mishi-gayaashkwan ogii-amwogoon goosiwaa,” (the great gull ate up your father,”) odinaan (he said to them).

Gii-agaji (he was ashamed).

Ezhi-ganawaabamaad oningwanan (as he gazed at his son-in-law), oganoonigoon odaanisan: (he was addressed by his daughter:)

“Wegonen wenji-ganawaabamad (“why are you watching) a’aw nemadabid?” (the one sitting there?”)

    > **And this he said:

Mii dash ekidod: (and this he said:)

“Awiiya bimoodewan oskatigong,” (“something is crawling on his forehead,”) ikido (he said).

Miinawaa ningoding ikido a’aw inini: (another time the man said:)

Mii dash asiniin aano-gii-izhi-giizhaabikizwaad (and even though he heated a stone) mii idash ezhi-ditibiweba’owaad goonikaang (and rolled it along in the snow), aw idash asin wiiba gii-dakaabikizi (the stone soon cooled off).

180) > **Mii zhigwa gawajid.  
     > **Then did he begin to freeze.

Mii zhigwa gawajid (then he began to freeze).

181) > **Jiigibiig gichigamiing anoo-gii-apatoo.  
     > **Along the shore of the sea he ran, but to no purpose.

Jiigibiig gichigamiing anoo-gii-apatoo (along the shore of the sea he ran, but in vain).

182) > **Apii dash gewajid, "Mashkiigwaatigong ningad-izhinaagoz.**

And when he was on the point of freezing, "Like a tamarack will I look.

Apii dash gewajid (and when he was freezing), “Mashkiigwaatigong ningad-izhinaagoz. (“like a tamarack will I look.)

183) > **Mii imaa pane ji-badakizoyaan."  
     > **In this very spot shall I always stand."

Mii imaa pane ji-badakizoyaan.” (“in that very spot will I always stand.”)

184) > **Gii-izhaawaad gii-nandawaabamaawaad, ogiwaabamaawaan mashkiigwaatigoon badakizonid imaa jiigibiig gichigamiing.  
     > **On their going thither to seek for him, they saw a tamarack standing there by the shore of the sea.

Gii-izhaawaad gii-nandawaabamaawaad (when they went there to look for him), ogiwaabamaawaan mashkiigwaatigoon (they saw a tamarack) badakizonid imaa jiigibiig gichigamiing (standing there by the shore of the sea).

185) > **Mii iw gii-bakinaagod oningwanan a'aw Omashoos.

Mii iw gii-bakinaagod oningwanan a’aw Omashoos (thus Mashos was vanquished by his son-in-law).

186) > **Mii sa i' ekoozid.  
     > **That is as far as (the story) goes.

Mii sa i’ ekoozid (that is as far as the story goes).
