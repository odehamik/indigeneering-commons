MA‘KADÄ‘KĒWIN.

**MAKADEKEWIN.**

FASTING.

***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***

(1) Mägwā kī∙ạ∙binōdcī∙i∙wiyān mōⁿjạg ningī∙i∙gwicim; nōsibạn ningīkạgāⁿzumik. Kigīcäp ningīmīnik onāgạn mīdcîm a‘tänig; kayädạc a‘ka‘kaⁿjä odạ‘kunān päᵉjik tciwudā‘pinamān kunimāⁿ mīdcîm kumāⁿ gayä a‘ka‘kaⁿjä.

**Megwaa gii-abinoojii’iwiyaan moonzhag ningii’igwishim; noosiban ningii-gagaanzomig. Gigizhep ningii-miinig onaagan miijim atenig; gaye dash akakanzhe odakonaan bezhig ji-odaapinamaan gonimaa miijim gomaa gaye akakanzhe.**

*While I was a child, continually did I fast; by my father (who then was living) was I exhorted. In the morning I was offered a bowl containing some food; and some charcoal he held in his hand, to see which one I would take, whether (it would be) the food or else the charcoal.*

Megwaa gii-abinoojii’iwiyaan (while I was a child) moonzhag (continually) ningii’igwishim (I fasted) noosiban (my father, *the preterite -ban: who then was living*) ningii-gagaanzomig (he exhorted me). Gigizhep (in the morning) ningii-miinig (he gave me) onaagan (a bowl) miijim atenig (with food in it); gaye dash (and) akakanzhe (charcoal) odakonaan (he held in his hand) bezhig ji-odaapinamaan (for me to take one) gonimaa miijim (whether the food) gomaa gaye akakanzhe (or else the charcoal).

(2) Ningudingidạc ningīma‘kadä‘kä; ningudci ningīpabā∙i∙jā pina‘kạmigāng, komāⁿ gayä wạdciwing. Äniwäk sạnạgạt; nīcogun, pīnic nī∙o∙gun, pīnic ningotwāsogun, pinic cwāsugun, mī∙i∙’ᵘ ä‘ta mini‘k kā∙i∙jikaski‘tōyān.

**Ningoding idash ningii-makadeke; ningoji ningii-babaa-izhaa binakamigaang, gomaa gaye wajiwing. Aaniwek zanagad; niizhogon, biinish niiyogon, biinish ningodwaasogon, biinish zhwaasogon, mii iw eta minik gaa-izhi-gashkitooyaan.**

*Now, once I blackened (my face and fasted); off some where I wandered, going hither and thither in open places of the forest or else upon the mountain. It was a trying (experience); for the space of two days, for the space of four days, for the space of six days, for the space of eight days, only up to as far as that was I able to go.*

Ningoding idash (now, once) ningii-makadeke (I blackened — fasted); ningoji (off somewhere) ningii-babaa-izhaa (I wandered about) binakamigaang (in open places) gomaa gaye wajiwing (or else on the mountain). Aaniwek zanagad (it was rather hard — a trying thing); niizhogon (two days) biinish niiyogon (up to four days) biinish ningodwaasogon (up to six days) biinish zhwaasogon (up to eight days), mii iw eta minik (that was only as much) gaa-izhi-gashkitooyaan (as I was able to do).

(3) Ạnōdcîkägō ningīpawādān, — miziwä a‘kīng ayāmạga‘k nīngīpawādān; kistcigạmi gayä, kīcizōg, anạngōg kayä; ka‘kina gayä kīwi‘tākīji‘k wäwundānimak ningīpawādān, undinōg cwātcing wändānima‘k ningīpawādān.

**Anooj igegoo ningii-bawaadaan, — miziwe akiing ayaamagak ningii-bawaadaan; gichigami gaye, giizisoog, anangoog gaye; gakina gaye giiwitaa-giizhig wewendaanimak ningii-bawaadaan, ondinoog zhwaaching wendaanimak ningii-bawaadaan.**

*Concerning all sorts of things did I dream, — about what was everywhere on earth did I dream; and about the sea, the suns, and the stars; and about all things in the circle of the heavens from whence blew the winds, did I dream.*

Anooj igegoo (all sorts of things) ningii-bawaadaan (I dreamed of), — miziwe akiing ayaamagak (what is everywhere on earth) ningii-bawaadaan (I dreamed of); gichigami gaye (and the sea), giizisoog (the suns), anangoog gaye (and the stars); gakina gaye giiwitaa-giizhig (and all things around the circle of the sky) wewendaanimak (from where the winds blow) ningii-bawaadaan (I dreamed of), ondinoog (the winds) zhwaaching wendaanimak (from the eight places whence the wind comes) ningii-bawaadaan (I dreamed of them).

(4) Kayägu icipimīng äyāt mạnidō ningīpawānā; ningīgạgạnōnik, nīngīwīndạmāk ānīn käticiwäbisiyān. Ka‘kina gayä anạngunk ạnicinābäk ningīcawänimigōk. Mīdạc ‘iᵍi’ᵘ a‘pī kī∙ạ∙nipạwādạmān ạnōdcîgägō ījitcigäwin nạgamōwin kayä; icpîmīng äyāgin nạgạmonạn ningīnōndānạn.

**Gaye go ishpiming eyaad manidoo ningii-bawaanaa; ningii-gaganoonig, ningii-wiindamaag aaniin ged-izhiwebiziyaan. Gakina gaye anangong anishinaabeg ningii-zhawenimigoog. Mii dash i’iw apii gii-ani-bawaadamaan anooj igegoo izhichigewin nagamowin gaye; ishpiming eyaagin nagamonan ningii-noondaanan.**

*And about the manitou that was above did I dream; by him was I spoken to, by him was I given the knowledge of what would happen to me. And by all the people of the stars was I blessed. It was then that I constantly dreamed of every sort of observance and of song; of the songs that are on high did I hear.*

Gaye go (and also) ishpiming eyaad manidoo (the manitou that is above) ningii-bawaanaa (I dreamed of him); ningii-gaganoonig (he spoke to me), ningii-wiindamaag (he told me) aaniin ged-izhiwebiziyaan (what would happen to me). Gakina gaye anangong anishinaabeg (and by all the people of the stars) ningii-zhawenimigoog (I was blessed). Mii dash i’iw apii (it was then) gii-ani-bawaadamaan (that I went on dreaming of) anooj igegoo izhichigewin (every sort of observance) nagamowin gaye (and of song); ishpiming eyaagin nagamonan (the songs that are on high) ningii-noondaanan (I heard them).

(5) Kistcinībawa kījigōwininiwạg ningīcäwänimigōg; miziwä ningī∙i∙jiwinigōg a‘kīng icpîmīng gayä, äjināgwa‘k ningīwâbanda∙i∙gōg, miziwä kīwi‘tāgījik ayāmaga‘k kā‘pawādạmān.

**Gichi-niibawa giizhigowininiwag ningii-zhawenimigoog; miziwe ningii-izhiwinigoog akiing ishpiming gaye, ezhinaagwak ningii-waabanda’igoog, miziwe giiwitaa-giizhig ayaamagak gaa-bawaadamaan.**

*By a great throng of the sky-people was I blessed; everywhere over the earth and on high was I conveyed by them, how it (all) looked I was shown, how it was everywhere in the circle of the heavens that I had dreamed about.*

Gichi-niibawa giizhigowininiwag (a great many sky-people) ningii-zhawenimigoog (blessed me); miziwe (everywhere) ningii-izhiwinigoog (they carried me) akiing ishpiming gaye (over the earth and on high), ezhinaagwak (how it looked) ningii-waabanda’igoog (they showed me), miziwe giiwitaa-giizhig (everywhere around the circle of the sky) ayaamagak (what is there) gaa-bawaadamaan (which I had dreamed of).

(6) Ninguding idạc ningī∙i∙jiwīnigōg ayāmạga‘k tcīza‘kān; nīngīpīndigä; nībawa ī∙i∙māⁿ ningīwâbạmāg ogījigōwininiwạg, anicinābänk ijināgusiwạg. Ā‘pidci mino‘tāgusiwạg nạgạmowād: —  
“Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingūwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.”

**Ningoding idash ningii-izhiwinigoog ayaamagak jiisakaan; ningii-biindige; niibawa i’imaa ningii-waabamaag ogiizhigowininiwag, anishinaabeng izhinaagoziwag. Aapiji minotaagoziwag nagamowaad: —  
“Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingūwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.  
Ä‘kogījingōwạngī kābạbā∙i∙nābiyān.”**

*Now, once I was borne to where there was a soothsaying-lodge; I entered in; I saw many sky-people there, like human beings they looked. Very pleasing was the sound of their voices when they sang: —  
“As far as the ends of the sky have I wandered and seen.  
As far as the ends of the sky have I wandered and seen.  
As far as the ends of the sky have I wandered and seen.  
As far as the ends of the sky have I wandered and seen.  
As far as the ends of the sky have I wandered and seen.”*

Ningoding idash (now, once) ningii-izhiwinigoog (I was carried by them) ayaamagak jiisakaan (to where a soothsaying-lodge was); ningii-biindige (I entered); niibawa i’imaa (many there) ningii-waabamaag (I saw them) ogiizhigowininiwag (the sky-people), anishinaabeng izhinaagoziwag (like human beings they looked). Aapiji minotaagoziwag (very pleasing was the sound of them) nagamowaad (as they sang): — *the song is carried as printed, all five repetitions; the fourth repetition prints -ngū- where the others print -ngō-*

(7) Mī∙i∙ᵘ kā∙i∙nändạmān, “Nībiwa kägōn ningīcawänimigō, ka‘kina īgiᵘ ạnạngōg, aᵘ udcīgạnāng gayä, cwāswi anạngōg ā‘pidci mạmāndidōtcig, ā‘pidci gayä kistci wāsa ānind ayāwādcig anạngōg;” kāwī‘kā awīya udākạskitōsīn tci∙u∙disāt īni’ᵘ anạngōn. Äpī‘tci wâsa ayāwāt ānind anạngōg; tîbickō u∙u∙māⁿ a‘kīng äji∙a∙yāwād pämādizicitcig. Mī gayä ī∙i∙’ᵘ äjiwäba‘k iwidi anạngōk ayāwād anicinābäk.

**Mii iw gaa-inendamaan, “Niibiwa gegoon ningii-zhawenimigoo, gakina igiw anangoog, aw ojiiganaang gaye, zhwaaswi anangoog aapiji mamaandidojig, aapiji gaye gichi waasa aanind ayaawaajig anangoog;” gaawiikaa awiya odaa-gashkitoosiin ji-odisaad iniw anangoon. Epiichi waasa ayaawaad aanind anangoog; dibishkoo o’omaa akiing ezhi-ayaawaad bemaadizijig. Mii gaye i’iw ezhiwebak iwidi anangoog ayaawaad anishinaabeg.**

*Accordingly then did I think: “By many things have I been blessed, by all the stars, and by the fisher-star, and by eight stars that were so very big, and by some of the stars that were so very far away;” never would any one be able to reach those stars. Exceedingly far away were some of the stars; just as here on earth, where mortals are (where people are far apart). And it happens among the stars yonder as it does (here) where people are.*

Mii iw gaa-inendamaan (accordingly this is what I thought), “Niibiwa gegoon (by many things) ningii-zhawenimigoo (have I been blessed), gakina igiw anangoog (by all those stars), aw ojiiganaang gaye (and by the fisher-star), zhwaaswi anangoog (eight stars) aapiji mamaandidojig (that are so very big), aapiji gaye gichi waasa (and very far away) aanind ayaawaajig anangoog (some of the stars that are there);” gaawiikaa awiya (never anyone) odaa-gashkitoosiin (would be able) ji-odisaad (to reach) iniw anangoon (those stars). Epiichi waasa (so exceedingly far) ayaawaad aanind anangoog (are some of the stars); dibishkoo o’omaa akiing (just as here on earth) ezhi-ayaawaad bemaadizijig (the way the living are placed). Mii gaye i’iw ezhiwebak (so also does it happen) iwidi anangoog ayaawaad anishinaabeg (yonder among the stars where people are).

(8) Mī∙i∙’ᵘ kā∙i∙jiwâbạndạmān, pīnic nîswi ningīpawādānạn īni’ᵘ tcīsa‘kānạn. Nāgwạdōn kījigunk īni’ᵘ tcīsa‘kānạn anạngōk änagōdcinōwād. Inābiyu‘k kawâbamāwāg īgi’ᵘ täbādcimạgwa. Kayädạc ā‘pidcikistcizōngạn ī∙i∙’ᵘ päᵉjik tcīsa‘kān kā‘pawātạmān. Kīcpinidạc tcīsa‘kīyān änändạmān ī∙i∙’ᵘ guijiwäbạt. Kīcpîn mi‘kwänimạg päᵉjik mạnidō kā‘pawânạg mīgū∙i∙ᵘ cigwa pipīndigät. Nīnigu änändạmān mī∙i∙’ᵘ äjinạgạmut. Wâsa inābiwạk īgi’ᵘ ugījigōwininiwạg. Kīcpîn awiya mạdwätạcimit ninōndawā; amạndcigu a‘pī äyāgwän, kunimā ạgāmikistcigạmig ayāt mạdwäganōjit ninōndawā.

**Mii iw gaa-izhi-waabandamaan, biinish niswi ningii-bawaadaanan iniw jiisakaanan. Naagwadoon giizhigong iniw jiisakaanan anangoog enagoojinowaad. Inaabiyok gawaabamaawaag igiw debaajimagwaa. Gaye dash aapiji-gichi-zoongan i’iw bezhig jiisakaan gaa-bawaadamaan. Giishpin idash jiisakiiyaan enendamaan i’iw ge-izhiwebak. Giishpin mikwenimag bezhig manidoo gaa-bawaanag mii go i’iw zhigwa bi-biindiged. Niin igo enendamaan mii i’iw ezhi-nagamod. Waasa inaabiwag igiw ogiizhigowininiwag. Giishpin awiya madwe-dazhimid ninoondawaa; amanj igo apii eyaagwen, gonimaa agaami-gichigamig ayaad madwe-ganoozhid ninoondawaa.**

*Now, this was what I saw, up to as far as three soothsaying-lodges have I dreamed. Visible in the sky are the soothsaying-lodges, from the way the stars hang aloft. Do you look, (and) you (can) see them about which I am telling. And of very great power is one of the soothsaying-lodges of which I dreamed. If I divine, then what I have in mind would come to pass. If I should be mindful of a certain manitou of whom I had dreamed, then straightway would he come in. And concerning what I myself have in mind is what he sings. Afar do the sky-people see. Should some one utter his voice when speaking of me, I (could) hear him; no matter how far away he might be, no matter if he be on the farther shore of the sea speaking to me, I (can) hear him.*

Mii iw gaa-izhi-waabandamaan (this is what I saw), biinish niswi (up to three) ningii-bawaadaanan (I dreamed of them) iniw jiisakaanan (those soothsaying-lodges). Naagwadoon giizhigong (visible in the sky) iniw jiisakaanan (are the soothsaying-lodges) anangoog enagoojinowaad (from the way the stars hang aloft). Inaabiyok (do you look) gawaabamaawaag (and you can see them) igiw debaajimagwaa (the ones I am telling about). Gaye dash aapiji-gichi-zoongan (and of very great power) i’iw bezhig jiisakaan (is the one soothsaying-lodge) gaa-bawaadamaan (that I dreamed of). Giishpin idash jiisakiiyaan (if I divine) enendamaan (what I have in mind) i’iw ge-izhiwebak (that would come to pass). Giishpin mikwenimag (if I think of) bezhig manidoo (a certain manitou) gaa-bawaanag (whom I dreamed of) mii go i’iw zhigwa bi-biindiged (then straightway he comes in). Niin igo enendamaan (what I myself have in mind) mii i’iw ezhi-nagamod (is what he sings). Waasa inaabiwag (afar do they see) igiw ogiizhigowininiwag (the sky-people). Giishpin awiya madwe-dazhimid (should anyone be heard speaking of me) ninoondawaa (I hear him); amanj igo apii eyaagwen (no matter where he may be), gonimaa agaami-gichigamig ayaad (even on the farther shore of the sea) madwe-ganoozhid (heard speaking to me) ninoondawaa (I hear him).

(9) Pīnicigu mōckinä ī∙i∙’ᵘ tcīsa‘kān mini‘k pāndigäwāt ugījigōwininiwạg; tibickō wīgiwām wänicicing mī∙i∙’ᵘ äjināgwa‘k ạbīwinạn imāⁿ pīndik tcīsa‘kāning. Kīcpîn kayä awīya nạndawänimint anicinābäᵉ tcîbījāt mīgu kwīckucimint mīgu pījāt. Mīdạc kạgwädcimint: “Ānīn äjipimādisiyạn?”

**Biinish igo mooshkine i’iw jiisakaan minik baandigewaad ogiizhigowininiwag; dibishkoo wiigiwaam wenizhishing mii i’iw ezhinaagwak abiwinan i’imaa biindig jiisakaaning. Giishpin gaye awiya nandawenimind anishinaabe ji-bi-izhaad mii go gwiishkoshimind mii go bi-izhaad. Mii dash gagwejimind: “Aaniin ezhi-bimaadiziyan?”**

*In course of time the soothsaying-lodge becomes filled with the people of the sky that have entered in; like a wigwam that is fair is the aspect of the spaces there inside of the soothsaying-lodges. And should the presence of some person be desired, he is simply whistled for, whereupon he comes. And then he is asked: “What kind of life have you lived?”*

Biinish igo mooshkine (in time it is filled) i’iw jiisakaan (the soothsaying-lodge) minik baandigewaad (with as many as have entered) ogiizhigowininiwag (of the sky-people); dibishkoo wiigiwaam wenizhishing (like a wigwam that is fair) mii i’iw ezhinaagwak (so is the look of) abiwinan (the seats — the spaces) i’imaa biindig jiisakaaning (there inside the soothsaying-lodge). Giishpin gaye awiya nandawenimind (and if anyone is desired) anishinaabe ji-bi-izhaad (a person, that he should come) mii go gwiishkoshimind (he is simply whistled for) mii go bi-izhaad (and he comes). Mii dash gagwejimind (and then he is asked): “Aaniin ezhi-bimaadiziyan? (What kind of life have you lived?)”

(10) “Kāwīn, kāwīn, kāwīn ā‘pidci niminopimādisisīmin. Nānīngutinō niminopimātisīmin,” i‘kidō.

**“Gaawiin, gaawiin, gaawiin aapiji nimino-bimaadizisiimin. Naaningodinoo nimino-bimaadizimin,” ikido.**

*“Not, not, not have we (always) lived a very good life. Part of the time (only) have we lived a good life,” he says.*

“Gaawiin, gaawiin, gaawiin (not, not, not) aapiji (very) nimino-bimaadizisiimin (have we lived a good life — *exclusive we*). Naaningodinoo (only part of the time) nimino-bimaadizimin (have we lived a good life),” ikido (he says).

(11) Mōⁿjạg mi‘kinā‘k kī∙ạ∙nōnā tci∙a∙winạndomāt awiyạn; mī∙a∙wä askābäwis. Amạndcigō käticitogwän mīgo∙i∙’ᵘ käᵉgä‘t äjiwäbiziwāt. Mī īni’ᵘ utcistcāgwạn pạgāgīgitōnit. Amạndcigo änuwägwän mīgō∙i∙’ᵘ änwät i∙i∙’māⁿ tcīsa‘kāning pigāgīgitōt ‘aᵍa’ᵘ ạnicinābä. Ka‘kina awiya pindingäwạg imāⁿ tcīsa‘kāning; ka‘kina gayä awäⁿsīyạg a‘kīng äyātcig, ka‘kina gayä icpîmīng äyātcig pinäsiwạg, undinōg, ka‘kina gayä pīwâbi‘k ändaswäwānạgạ‘k, asinīg gayä, ka‘kina gayä nībing ändạna‘kīdcig pīndigäwạg. ‘Aᵍa’wigu tcāsa‘kīd anicināpä ä∙i∙nändạnk mī∙i∙’ᵘ änwäwāt īgi’ᵘ pāndigäwād; nạgạmōwag, ạnōdc ina∙ạ∙mōg.

**Moonzhag mikinaak gii-anoonaa ji-awi-nandomaad awiyan; mii awe oshkaabewis. Amanj igo ged-izhichigegwen mii go i’iw geget ezhiwebiziwaad. Mii iniw ojichaagwan begaagiigidonid. Amanj igo enwegwen mii go i’iw enwed i’imaa jiisakaaning bi-gaagiigidod a’aw anishinaabe. Gakina awiya biindigewag i’imaa jiisakaaning; gakina gaye awesiiyag akiing eyaajig, gakina gaye ishpiming eyaajig binesiwag, ondinoog, gakina gaye biiwaabik endaswewaanagak, asiniig gaye, gakina gaye niibing endanakiijig biindigewag. A’aw igo jaasakiid anishinaabe enendang mii i’iw enwewaad igiw baandigewaad; nagamowag, anooj ina’amoog.**

*Often is Snapping-Turtle employed to go and ask some one to come; he is the attendant. Whatsoever he may say will truly happen to the (people). It is his soul that does the talking. Whatsoever language he uses is the one the person speaks when doing his talking in the soothsaying-lodge. All creatures enter the soothsaying-lodge; likewise all the animal-folk that are on earth, also all the birds that are on high, the winds, and every kind of metal that is, and rocks, and all creatures that live and move in the summer-time, enter in. And what the man doing the soothsaying thinks is what the incomers say; they sing, all sorts of songs do they sing.*

Moonzhag (often) mikinaak (Snapping-Turtle) gii-anoonaa (is employed) ji-awi-nandomaad awiyan (to go and summon someone); mii awe oshkaabewis (he is the attendant). Amanj igo ged-izhichigegwen (whatever he may do/say) mii go i’iw geget (that truly) ezhiwebiziwaad (happens to them). Mii iniw ojichaagwan (it is his soul) begaagiigidonid (that does the talking). Amanj igo enwegwen (whatever language he uses) mii go i’iw enwed (that is the language spoken) i’imaa jiisakaaning (there in the soothsaying-lodge) bi-gaagiigidod a’aw anishinaabe (when the person comes to do his talking). Gakina awiya biindigewag (all beings enter) i’imaa jiisakaaning (into the soothsaying-lodge); gakina gaye awesiiyag akiing eyaajig (all the animal-folk on earth), gakina gaye ishpiming eyaajig binesiwag (all the birds on high), ondinoog (the winds), gakina gaye biiwaabik endaswewaanagak (and every kind of metal there is), asiniig gaye (and the rocks), gakina gaye niibing endanakiijig (and all that dwell in the summer) biindigewag (enter in). A’aw igo jaasakiid anishinaabe (the person who divines) enendang (what he thinks) mii i’iw enwewaad (is what they voice) igiw baandigewaad (those who have entered); nagamowag (they sing), anooj ina’amoog (all sorts of songs they intone).
