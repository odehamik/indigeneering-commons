## **7. LYNX AND THE SHE-SKUNK.**

## **BIZHIW ZHIGAAGOKWE GAYE.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-eng">*from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).*</span>

</div>


*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoding giiwenh gii-aayindaa bizhiw.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Once on a time, they say, there lived a Lynx.</span>

</div>


Ningoding giiwenh (once on a time, they say) gii-aayindaa bizhiw (there lived a Lynx).

2)  > **Zhigaagokwen owiidigemaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">A She-Skunk he had for a wife.</span>

</div>


Zhigaagokwen owiidigemaan (a She-Skunk he had for a wife).

3)  > **Mii dash imaanh endazhi-nandawenjiged aw bizhiw; anooji gegoo onitoon - waaboozoon, pinewan, zhiishiiban - waa-ashamaad wiiwan; naningodinong gaye amikwan onisaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">It was there that Lynx hunted for game; every kind of thing he killed, — rabbits, ruffed grouse, ducks, — what he wanted to feed his wife on; sometimes a beaver too he killed.</span>

</div>


Mii dash imaa (it was there) endazhi-nandawenjiged aw bizhiw (that this Lynx hunted for game); anooji gegoo onitoon (every kind of thing he killed) — waaboozoon, pinewan, zhiishiiban (rabbits, ruffed grouse, ducks) — waa-ashamaad (whatever he wanted to feed).

4)  > **Api idash eni-biboong onooji'aan amikwan aapiji wiininowan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when winter was coming on, he obtained some beavers that were very fat.</span>

</div>


Api idash eni-biboong (and when winter was coming on) onooji’aan amikwan (he obtained some beavers) aapiji wiininowan (that were very fat).

5)  > **Aapiji omisawenimaan wii-amwaad wiiwan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">He very much desired to eat his wife.</span>

</div>


Aapiji omisawenimaan (he very much desired) wii-amwaad wiiwan (to eat his wife).

6)  > **Gaawiin ogii-nisaasiin amikwan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">He had not killed a beaver (for some time).</span>

</div>


Gaawiin ogii-nisaasiin amikwan (he had not killed a beaver for some time).

7)  > **Ningoding idash odinaan wiiwan:  
    > **So once he said to his wife:

Ningoding idash odinaan wiiwan (so once he said to his wife):

8)  > **"Gidaa-nibaa gosha go gaazhigakin," odinaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"You should really sometimes sleep during the day," he said to her.</span>

</div>


“Gidaa-nibaa gosha go gaazhigakin (you should really sometimes sleep during the day),” odinaan (he said to her).

9)  > **A'aw idash ikwe ogikenimaan wii-nisigod onaabeman.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, the woman knew that it was the desire of her husband to kill her.</span>

</div>


A’aw idash ikwe (now, the woman) ogikenimaan wii-nisigod onaabeman (knew that it was the desire of her husband to kill her).

10) > **Giimooj obi-naazikaan endaawaad nandawaabamaad wiiwan ji-nibaanid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">In secret would he approach where they lived, to see if his wife was asleep.</span>

</div>


Giimooj obi-naazikaan endaawaad (in secret would he approach where they lived) nandawaabamaad wiiwan ji-nibaanid (to see if his wife was asleep).

11) > **Ningoding idash gaazhigak nibinaadid a'aw ikwe, owaabamaan onaabeman babaamosenid, agaaming babaamosenid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, once in the day-time, when the woman went to get some water, she saw her husband walking from place to place, roundabout on the other shore was he walking.</span>

</div>


Ningoding idash gaazhigak (now, once in the day-time) nibinaadid a’aw ikwe (when the woman went to get some water), owaabamaan onaabeman babaamosenid (she saw her husband walking from place to place), agaaming babaamosenid (walking about along the shore).

12) > **Aw idash ikwe amikwan gii-mooshkamowan imaanh onda'ibaaning.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And while the woman (was there), a beaver came up out of the water at the hole in the ice.</span>

</div>


Aw idash ikwe (and while the woman was there) amikwan gii-mooshkamowan (a beaver came up out of the water) imaa onda’ibaaning (there at the water-hole in the ice).

13) > **Ogii-nawadinaan ozidaaning iniw amikwan, ominjiminaan, mii iw ezhi-biibaagimaad onaabeman:  
    > **She seized the beaver by the feet, she held it tight, and then she called aloud to her husband:

Ogii-nawadinaan ozidaaning iniw amikwan (she seized the beaver by the feet), ominjiminaan (she held it tight), mii iw ezhi-biibaagimaad onaabeman (and then she called aloud to her husband):

14) > **"Biizhiw, ondaas\! amikwa awe\! minimjiminaa\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"O Lynx, hither\! Here is a beaver\! I have hold of him\!"</span>

</div>


“Biizhiw, ondaas\! (O Lynx, hither\!) amikwa awe\! (here is a beaver\!) minimjiminaa\! (I have hold of him\!)”

15) > **Bizhiw idash bi-inaabi.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, Lynx looked over to where she was.</span>

</div>


Bizhiw idash bi-inaabi (now, Lynx looked over to where she was).

16) > **"Bagidin megwaa ninooji'aa\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Let it go, for I am hunting it\!"</span>

</div>


“Bagidin (let it go) megwaa ninooji’aa\! (for I am hunting it\!)”

17) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-bagidinaad gii-giiwe a'aw ikwe endaawaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so, when she let it go, back went the woman to where they lived.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-bagidinaad (and so, when she let it go) gii-giiwe a’aw ikwe endaawaad (back went the woman to where they lived).

18) > **Ogii-atoonan misan gaye dash asiniin ogii-asaan imaanh ch-oshtigwaaniban; ogii-badagwana'aan idash.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">She put some fire-wood in place, and a stone she laid there for the head; and she covered up (the figure).</span>

</div>


Ogii-atoonan misan (she put some fire-wood in place) gaye dash asiniin ogii-asaan imaa ch-oshtigwaaniban (and a stone she laid there for the head); ogii-badagwana’aan idash (and she covered up the figure).

19) > **Mii idash gaa-izhi-gaazod biindig endaawaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon she hid inside of where they lived.</span>

</div>


Mii idash gaa-izhi-gaazod biindig endaawaad (thereupon she hid inside of where they lived).

20) > **Apii dash ba-dagwishing bekaa obi-naazikaan endaawaad.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when Lynx was arriving home, softly he approached where they lived.</span>

</div>


Apii dash ba-dagwishing (and when Lynx was arriving home) bekaa obi-naazikaan endaawaad (softly he approached where they lived).

21) > **Mii dash waabamaad awiiya nibaanid, mii dash odeshkan ba-dakonang.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when he saw some one asleep, he then took his horn (chisel) up in his hand.</span>

</div>


Mii dash waabamaad awiiya nibaanid (and when he saw some one asleep), mii dash odeshkan ba-dakonang (he then took his horn (chisel) up in his hand).

22) > **Mii dash ezhi-bazhibawaad iniw asiniin mii dash gii-gikendang asiniin bazhibawaad, misan gaye atenig imaanh.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when he stabbed the stone, he then learned that it was a stone he had stabbed, and that some fire-wood too was there.</span>

</div>


Mii dash ezhi-bazhibawaad iniw asiniin (and when he stabbed the stone), mii dash gii-gikendang asiniin bazhibawaad (he then learned that it was a stone he had stabbed), misan gaye atenig imaa (and that some fire-wood too was there).

23) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-nandawaabamaad wiiwan dibi gaa-izhaanigwen; agwajiing gaye miziwe gii-nandawaabanjiged, gawiin ogii-mikawaasiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon he sought for his wife, who had gone he knew not where; and everywhere out of doors he sought, but he did not find her.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-nandawaabamaad wiiwan (thereupon he sought for his wife) dibi gaa-izhaanigwen (who had gone he knew not where); agwajiing gaye miziwe gii-nandawaabanjiged (and everywhere out of doors he sought), gawiin ogii-mikawaasiin (but he did not find her).

24) > **Gii-biindige dash.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Then he went inside.</span>

</div>


Gii-biindige dash (then he went inside).

25) > **"Anooj ningad-izhichige; maagizhaa da-baapi dibi gaazogwen."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"All sorts of capers will I do; perhaps she will laugh, wherever she may be concealed."</span>

</div>


“Anooj ningad-izhichige (all sorts of capers will I do); maagizhaa da-baapi (perhaps she will laugh) dibi gaazogwen (wherever she may be concealed).”

26) > **Mii dash gaa-doodang:  
    > **Now, this was what he did:

Mii dash gaa-doodang (now, this was what he did):

27) > **ogii-zaagisitoon odininiiwiwin; ogii-makadewinaan onakwaya'ii.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">he stuck out his manhood; he blackened it at the end.</span>

</div>


ogii-zaagisitoon odininiiwiwin (he stuck out his manhood); ogii-makadewinaan onakwaya’ii (he blackened it at the end).

28) > **"Zhigaagokwe oga-wii-baapitoon odaya'iim dibi ayaagwen, endoo, endoo, endoo, endoo\!"**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Let She-Skunk laugh at what is her own, wherever she may be,</span>

</div>


“Zhigaagokwe oga-wii-baapitoon odaya’iim (let She-Skunk laugh at what is her own) dibi ayaagwen (wherever she may be), endoo, endoo, endoo, endoo\! (where, where, where, where\! — vocable refrain, carried verbatim)”


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">where, where, where, where\!"</span>

</div>


29) > **Aw idash ikwe gegapii gii-baapi ganawaabamaad endodaminid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now, the woman at last did laugh when she observed what he was doing.</span>

</div>


Aw idash ikwe gegapii gii-baapi (now, the woman at last did laugh) ganawaabamaad endodaminid (when she observed what he was doing).

30) > **Nwaandawaad baapinid wiiwan, gii-zaagijise, gii-andawaabanjige giiwitaya'ii endaawaad; gaawiin ogii-mikawaasiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">On hearing the sound of his wife laughing, he rushed out of doors, he went seeking everywhere roundabout where they dwelt; but he did not find her.</span>

</div>


Nwaandawaad baapinid wiiwan (on hearing the sound of his wife laughing), gii-zaagijise (he rushed out of doors), gii-andawaabanjige giiwitaya’ii endaawaad (he went seeking everywhere roundabout where they dwelt); gaawiin ogii-mikawaasiin (but he did not find her).

31) > **Miinawaa gii-biindige; biinish nising i'iw gii-doodam, aanawi miinawaa gii-bapiwan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Back within he returned; even a third time he did it, still again she would laugh.</span>

</div>


Miinawaa gii-biindige (back within he returned); biinish nising i’iw gii-doodam (even a third time he did it), aanawi miinawaa gii-bapiwan (still again she would laugh).

32) > **Gegapii idash gii-de-baapi a’aw ikwe, gaawiin miinawaa gii-baapisii.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">So at length the woman had her fill of laughing, no more did she laugh.</span>

</div>


Gegapii idash gii-de-baapi a’aw ikwe (so at length the woman had her fill of laughing), gaawiin miinawaa gii-baapisii (no more did she laugh).

33) > **Bizhiw idash gii-aanawenjige indawaa.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And Lynx ceased his merriment in consequence.</span>

</div>


Bizhiw idash gii-aanawenjige indawaa (and Lynx ceased his merriment in consequence).

34) > **Aanawi oganawaabamigoon wiiwan, gaawiin dash wiin owaabamaasiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Even though he was observed by his wife, yet he himself did not see her.</span>

</div>


Aanawi oganawaabamigoon wiiwan (even though he was observed by his wife), gaawiin dash wiin owaabamaasiin (yet he himself did not see her).

35) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-gawishimod jiig-ishikode, a'aw bizhiw odagoozitoon okaad ogidigwaang.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when he lay down to sleep beside the fire, Lynx hung one leg over the other knee.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-gawishimod jiig-ishikode (and when he lay down to sleep beside the fire), a’aw bizhiw odagoozitoon okaad ogidigwaang (Lynx hung one leg over the other knee).

36) > **Mii dash naanaazhinag onaazidi, mii dahs gaa-ikidod:**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Accordingly, as he rubbed the under side of his hip, this was what he said:</span>

</div>


Mii dash naanaazhinag onaazidi (accordingly, as he rubbed the under side of his hip), mii dahs gaa-ikidod (this was what he said):

37) > **"Nindaa-baapii'igoo maawiin Nengaawi-zaaga'iganiing niimi'iding neneyaashkinaazidiyegoojinaan."**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"I should doubtless be made fun of if I were at the dance at Sandy Lake with the hair on my rump hanging down."</span>

</div>


“Nindaa-baapii’igoo maawiin (I should doubtless be made fun of) Nengaawi-zaaga’iganiing niimi’iding (if I were at the dance at Sandy Lake) neneyaashkinaazidiyegoojinaan (with the hair on my rump hanging down).”

38) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-odaapinang mookomaan ogii-maanizhaan idash obwaam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Whereupon, picking up a knife, he sliced a piece off his ham.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-odaapinang mookomaan (whereupon, picking up a knife), ogii-maanizhaan idash obwaam (he sliced a piece off his ham).

39) > **Gaa-izhi-dakonand idash, "Ninga-miijiin," inendam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Then taking up (a piece) in his hand, "I will eat it," he thought.</span>

</div>


Gaa-izhi-dakonand idash (then taking up a piece in his hand), “Ninga-miijiin (I will eat it),” inendam (he thought).

40) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-abwed.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon he roasted it upon a spit.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-abwed (thereupon he roasted it upon a spit).

41) > **Gaa-giizhidenig ogii-miijiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">After it was done cooking, he ate it.</span>

</div>


Gaa-giizhidenig ogii-miijiin (after it was done cooking, he ate it).

42) > **Aapiji ominopidaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Very savory he found the taste of it.</span>

</div>


Aapiji ominopidaan (very savory he found the taste of it).

43) > **Miinawaa gwekaya'ii ogii-maanizhaan, miinawaa ogii-miijiin.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Another piece from the other side he sliced off, again he ate of it.</span>

</div>


Miinawaa gwekaya’ii ogii-maanizhaan (another piece from the other side he sliced off), miinawaa ogii-miijiin (again he ate of it).

44) > **Mii iw gii-debisiniid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon he had all he wanted to eat.</span>

</div>


Mii iw gii-debisiniid (thereupon he had all he wanted to eat).

45) > **Miinawaa dash gii-gawishimo; mizhiwe gagwedinidizo; wiisagendam omisad, ozaam gii-debisiniid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">So once more he lay down to sleep; all over was he feeling of himself; he had a pain in his belly, for too much had he eaten.</span>

</div>


Miinawaa dash gii-gawishimo (so once more he lay down to sleep); mizhiwe gagwedinidizo (all over was he feeling of himself); wiisagendam omisad (he had a pain in his belly), ozaam gii-debisiniid (for too much had he eaten).

46) > **Omikoojinaan omisad baapaagaanig.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">He felt that his belly was hard.</span>

</div>


Omikoojinaan omisad baapaagaanig (he felt that his belly was hard).

47) > **"Ganabaj niminiiw," inendam.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">"Perhaps I have pus in me," he thought.</span>

</div>


“Ganabaj niminiiw (perhaps I have pus in me),” inendam (he thought).

48) > **Mii dash gaa-izhi-maadizhang omisad, geget gegoon omikoojiinaan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so when he lanced his belly, sure enough, he felt hold of something.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gaa-izhi-maadizhang omisad (and so when he lanced his belly), geget gegoon omikoojiinaan (sure enough, he felt hold of something).

49) > **Mii dash waa-wiikobidood ikwaagatese.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And when he tried to pull on it, he flinched.</span>

</div>


Mii dash waa-wiikobidood (and when he tried to pull on it) ikwaagatese (he flinched).

50) > **Nawach enigok owiikobidoon, mii iw gii-bakibinaad omisadan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Still harder he pulled on it, whereupon he tore apart his belly.</span>

</div>


Nawach enigok owiikobidoon (still harder he pulled on it), mii iw gii-bakibinaad omisadan (whereupon he tore apart his belly).

51) > **Mii dash gii-ajijised shkodeng.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And then he fell headlong into the fire.</span>

</div>


Mii dash gii-ajijised shkodeng (and then he fell headlong into the fire).

52) > **Zhigaagokwe dash gii-basigonjise gii-agwaawebinaad, aazha aapiji gii-jaagizowan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now She-Skunk sprang to her feet to pull him out, but already was he very much burned.</span>

</div>


Zhigaagokwe dash gii-basigonjise (now She-Skunk sprang to her feet) gii-agwaawebinaad (to pull him out), aazha aapiji gii-jaagizowan (but already was he very much burned).

53) > **Mii dash aanawi gaa-izhi-bimaaji'aad onaabeman.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">But nevertheless she rescued her husband.</span>

</div>


Mii dash aanawi gaa-izhi-bimaaji’aad onaabeman (but nevertheless she rescued her husband).

54) > **Mii dash i'iw gaa-izhinaagozid bizhiw; giinwosaawiingwed, mii iw gaa-inaagozid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And that was how Lynx came to look so; he became yellow-eyed, for that was how he was burned.</span>

</div>


Mii dash i’iw gaa-izhinaagozid bizhiw (and that was how Lynx came to look so); giinwosaawiingwed (he became yellow-eyed), mii iw gaa-inaagozid (for that was how he was burned).

55) > **Gaawiin dash ogii-minwenimaasiin i'iw izhinaagozinid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">But she did not like the way he looked.</span>

</div>


Gaawiin dash ogii-minwenimaasiin i’iw izhinaagozinid (but she did not like the way he looked).

56) > **Ningoding dash mashkawaagonewading gii-maajaa a'aw ikwe gii-webinaad onaabeman.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">So once, when the snow was frozen hard, the woman departed, forsaking her husband.</span>

</div>


Ningoding dash mashkawaagonewading (so once, when the snow was frozen hard) gii-maajaa a’aw ikwe (the woman departed) gii-webinaad onaabeman (forsaking her husband).

57) > **Gaye wiin dash bizhiw nizhiwe gii-ayaa.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And so Lynx too was all alone.</span>

</div>


Gaye wiin dash bizhiw nizhiwe gii-ayaa (and so Lynx too was all alone).

58) > **Mii dash apane nizhikewizid, gaa wiikaa owiijiwaasiin wiiwan.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Thereupon was he always alone, never was he in the company of his wife.</span>

</div>


Mii dash apane nizhikewizid (thereupon was he always alone), gaa wiikaa owiijiwaasiin wiiwan (never was he in the company of his wife).

59) > **Ningoding dash babaa-andawenjiged, bizhiw ogii-waabandaan binewidis gii-agodenig\!**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">Now once, when he was out on a hunt for game, Lynx saw the gizzard of a ruffed grouse hanging aloft\!</span>

</div>


Ningoding dash babaa-andawenjiged (now once, when he was out on a hunt for game), bizhiw ogii-waabandaan binewidis gii-agodenig\! (Lynx saw the gizzard of a ruffed grouse hanging aloft\!)

60) > **Mii sa ekoozid.**


<div class="four-layer">

> <span class="l-gloss">And that is the end of (the story).</span>

</div>


Mii sa ekoozid (and that is the end of the story).
