ININI OGWISAN KĪ‘KĪ∙U∙GWICIMUNIT NISING.

**ININI OGWISAN GII-GII-GWIISHIMONID NISING.**

THE MAN’S SON WHO FASTED THREE TIMES.

*Narrated by Wāsāgunäckạnk (Series III; Bois Fort). — narrator per Jones, Ojibwa Texts Part II, Introduction p.ix (not credited on the story page itself).*

***from Ojibwa Texts collected by William Jones (1919).***

(1) Ānĭc ạnicinābäg a∙ĭ∙ntāwạg, udänawi∙i∙tiwag. A‘kiwäⁿzī udipänimā i⁰i‘u ạnicinābäᵉ. Wīwạn ayāwan, kayä ugwisan ayāwạn. Ningutingidạc wīwītigäwạn īni‘ᵘ ugwisạn, mīsa kägä‘t kā∙i∙ciwītigänit. Kumāku a‘pi cigwa unīdcānisiwạn īni‘ᵘ ugwisạn, kwiwisänsạn udōnīdcānisini.

**Aaniish anishinaabeg ayi-ndaawag, odanawi’idiwag. Akiwenzii odibenimaa i’iw anishinaabe. Wiiwan ayaawan, gaye ogwisan ayaawan. Ningoding idash wiiwidigewan iniw ogwisan, mii sa geget gaa-izhi-wiidigenid. Gomaa apii zhigwa oniijaanisiwan iniw ogwisan, gwiiwizhensan odooniijaanisini.**

*Now, some people were abiding (there), they were living together in a town. An old man ruled over the people. His wife was there, and his son was there. And once his son desired to marry, whereupon truly then he married. After a certain period of time his son got a child, a boy was the child he begot.*

Aaniish anishinaabeg ayi-ndaawag (now, some people were dwelling there), odanawi’idiwag (they lived together in a town — \*stem flagged\*). Akiwenzii odibenimaa i’iw anishinaabe (an old man ruled over the people). Wiiwan ayaawan (his wife was there), gaye ogwisan ayaawan (and his son was there). Ningoding idash wiiwidigewan iniw ogwisan (and once his son desired to marry), mii sa geget gaa-izhi-wiidigenid (whereupon truly then he married). Gomaa apii zhigwa oniijaanisiwan iniw ogwisan (after some time his son got a child), gwiiwizhensan odooniijaanisini (a little boy was the child he begot).

(2) Ningutingiku ä‘kusiwạn īni‘ᵘ wīwạn ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini, mīsagu kägä‘t wīnibunit. Käga‘pī ninguting äcinibunit, mī∙ā∙nawi pimusänit īni‘ᵘ kwiwisänsạn; pō‘tcigu mi‘i‘ a‘pi kīnibunit īni‘ᵘ wīwạn. Ānĭc ā‘pidci mānäntạm ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini, kayä a∙a∙ a‘kiwäⁿzī. Ningutingidạc inändạm ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini: “Amantcigic kī∙i∙gwicimut ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ ningwisîsäns\!” Kägä‘t uwawäci∙a∙n, kayä mīgwạnan upạta‘kibinä∙ō∙nān. Kā‘kīcī‘tā∙ā∙t äjimādcīnāt. Käga‘pī ạnimawiwạn īni‘ᵘ ugwisîsänsạn. Ạnigạganōnāt: “Mānū\! kī∙i∙gwicimun.” Mi‘tigōndạc unạntunä∙wān, mīdạc iwiti äji∙o∙ci‘tōt ‘i⁰i‘ᵘ wasiswan. Kā‘kīci‘tōt äji∙a∙‘kwāntawa∙ā∙t īni‘ᵘ ugwisîsạn; äjikạnōnāt: “Pāmā midāsagunaga‘k kabi∙ạ∙ntawâbạmin.” Ajikīwät awinini. Kā‘tagwicink ōganōnigōn ōsạn: “Kägä‘t kitinigā∙ā∙ kigisîsäns.”

**Ningoding igo aakoziwan iniw wiiwan a’aw inini, mii sa go geget wii-nibonid. Gegapii ningoding ezhi-nibonid, mii aanawi bimosenid iniw gwiiwizhensan; boojigo mii’iw apii gii-nibonid iniw wiiwan. Aaniish aapiji maanendam a’aw inini, gaye a’aw akiwenzii. Ningoding idash inendam a’aw inini: “Amanj igizh gii-gwiishimod a’aw ningwizhizhens\!” Geget owawezhi’aan, gaye miigwanan obadakibine’oonaan. Gaa-giizhii’taad ezhi-maajiinaad. Gegapii ani-mawiwan iniw ogwizhizhensan. Ani-ganoonaad: “Maanoo\! gii-gwiishimon.” Mitigoon idash onandone’waan, mii dash iwidi ezhi-oozhi’tood i’iw waziswan. Gaa-giizhi’tood ezhi-akwaandawa’aad iniw ogwizhizhensan; ezhi-ganoonaad: “Baamaa midaaso-gonagak ga-bi-andawaabamin.” Ezhi-giiwed a’aw inini. Gaa-dagwishing oganoonigoon oosan: “Geget gidinigaa’aa gigizhizhens.”**

*Now, once sick was the wife of the man, whereupon truly she was about to die. At last then she died, that was about when the little boy was able to walk; anyhow, that was when his wife died. Now, very sad was the man, and also the old man. And once thought the man: “I wonder if my little son had not better go into a fast\!” of a truth, he painted him, and stuck a feather (on his head). When he had him fixed up, he then led him away. At last on went his little son weeping. Along the way he spoke to him, saying: “Never mind\! but go you into a fast.” So for a tree he looked, whereupon over there he made a nest. When he had completed it, he then went up the tree with his son; then, speaking to him, he said: “Not till ten days are up will I come to seek for you.” Then back home went the man. After he was come, he was addressed by his father saying: “In truth, harm are you doing to your little son.”*

Ningoding igo aakoziwan iniw wiiwan a’aw inini (once the man’s wife became sick), mii sa go geget wii-nibonid (and so truly she was about to die). Gegapii ningoding ezhi-nibonid (at last then she died), mii aanawi bimosenid iniw gwiiwizhensan (about when the little boy could walk); boojigo mii’iw apii gii-nibonid iniw wiiwan (anyhow, that was when his wife died). Aaniish aapiji maanendam a’aw inini (now, very sad was the man), gaye a’aw akiwenzii (and the old man too). Ningoding idash inendam a’aw inini (and once the man thought): “Amanj igizh gii-gwiishimod a’aw ningwizhizhens\!” (“I wonder if my little son shouldn’t go into a fast\!” — \*amanj-igizh particle flagged\*). Geget owawezhi’aan (truly he painted/adorned him), gaye miigwanan obadakibine’oonaan (and stuck a feather on his head — \*stem flagged\*). Gaa-giizhii’taad ezhi-maajiinaad (when he had fixed him up, then he led him away). Gegapii ani-mawiwan iniw ogwizhizhensan (at last his little son went on weeping). Ani-ganoonaad (along the way he spoke to him): “Maanoo\! gii-gwiishimon.” (“Never mind\! go you into a fast.” — \*imperative reading flagged\*). Mitigoon idash onandone’waan (then he looked for a tree), mii dash iwidi ezhi-oozhi’tood i’iw waziswan (and over there he made a nest). Gaa-giizhi’tood ezhi-akwaandawa’aad iniw ogwizhizhensan (when he had finished it, he climbed up with his little son); ezhi-ganoonaad (then speaking to him): “Baamaa midaaso-gonagak ga-bi-andawaabamin.” (“not till ten days are up will I come to seek for you.”) Ezhi-giiwed a’aw inini (then the man went home). Gaa-dagwishing oganoonigoon oosan (after he arrived, his father addressed him): “Geget gidinigaa’aa gigizhizhens.” (“truly you are harming your little son.”)

(3) Mīdạc i⁰i‘ᵘ äjikīgitut ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini: “Nōs, kāna kī‘ki‘kädạⁿzīn nōngum kinibu ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ wātigämạgiban? Mīdạc i⁰i‘ᵘ wäntcipạgitinạg ningwisis tcigi∙u∙gwicimut. Nạckä dạc, kícpîn mīdāsugunīt kāwīn käyābi awīya tanibusi.”

**Mii dash i’iw ezhi-giigidod a’aw inini: “Noos, gaa na gii-gikendanziin noongom gii-nibo a’aw waa-wiidigemagiban? Mii dash i’iw wenji-bagidinag ningwisis ji-gii-gwiishimod. Nashke dash, gishpin midaaso-goniid gaawiin geyaabi awiiya da-nibosii.”**

*Thereupon then spoke the man, saying: “My father, do you not know that already dead is she whom I had married? That is why I have permitted my son to go into a fast. For behold, if he can (fast) for ten days, then no one will ever again die.”*

Mii dash i’iw ezhi-giigidod a’aw inini (thereupon the man spoke): “Noos, gaa na gii-gikendanziin (“my father, do you not know) noongom gii-nibo a’aw waa-wiidigemagiban? (that she whom I had married is now dead? — \*preterite participle\*) Mii dash i’iw wenji-bagidinag ningwisis ji-gii-gwiishimod (that is why I let my son go into a fast). Nashke dash, gishpin midaaso-goniid (now look, if for ten days he can do it) gaawiin geyaabi awiiya da-nibosii.” (then no one will ever again die.”)

(4) Mīsa‘ i⁰i‘ᵘ cigwa wätcitcisänig midāsugun, äji∙i∙cāt; änitäbābandank käyābi umādāciwaᵉ ‘i⁰i‘ᵘ mīgwanaᵉ patạ‘kibinwä∙u∙nini. Kägä‘t minwäntạm. Äji∙a∙‘kwāntawät ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini. Kā‘tagwicing äjikạnōnāt īni‘ᵘ ugwisîsäⁿsạn: “Ningwisis, ā‘pidcina kīpa‘katä?” Äjikạnōnigut: “Nōs, kāwīn nimpa‘kạtäsī, mī∙ā∙nawi ga‘kina pawānạg ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ manitō. Käyābi nīwīkī∙u∙gwicim. Tayōc mī∙i∙‘ᵘ ijikīwän.”

**Mii sa i’iw zhigwa wedaji-jisenig midaaso-gon, ezhi-izhaad; ani-debaabandang geyaabi omaadaashiwe i’iw miigwan badaki-binwe’onini. Geget minwendam. Ezhi-akwaandawed a’aw inini. Gaa-dagwishing ezhi-ganoonaad iniw ogwizhizhensan: “Ningwisis, aapiji na gii-bakade?” Ezhi-ganoonigod: “Noos, gaawiin nimbakadesii, mii aanawi gakina bawaanag a’aw manidoo. Geyaabi niwii-gii-gwiishim. Dayoozh mii’iw izhi-giiwen.”**

*Therefore, when the period of ten days was at hand, then thither he went; when on his way he came in sight of the place, still to and fro with the wind swayed the feathers that were stuck (on his son’s head). Truly pleased was he. Then up (the tree) climbed the man. When he was come (up there), he then spoke to his little son, saying: “My little son, are you very hungry?” Then was he addressed by him saying: “My father, I am not hungry, but nevertheless I have dreamed of all the manitous.¹ Still longer do I wish to fast. Therefore do you return home again.”*

Mii sa i’iw zhigwa wedaji-jisenig midaaso-gon (and so when the ten days were at hand), ezhi-izhaad (then he went there); ani-debaabandang (when on his way he caught sight of the place) geyaabi omaadaashiwe i’iw miigwan badaki-binwe’onini (still the stuck feather swayed in the wind — \*stem flagged\*). Geget minwendam (truly pleased was he). Ezhi-akwaandawed a’aw inini (then up the tree the man climbed). Gaa-dagwishing ezhi-ganoonaad iniw ogwizhizhensan (when he came up he spoke to his little son): “Ningwisis, aapiji na gii-bakade?” (“my little son, are you very hungry?”) Ezhi-ganoonigod (then he was addressed by him): “Noos, gaawiin nimbakadesii (“my father, I am not hungry), mii aanawi gakina bawaanag a’aw manidoo (but nevertheless I have dreamed of all the manitous). Geyaabi niwii-gii-gwiishim (still longer I wish to fast). Dayoozh mii’iw izhi-giiwen.” (so you may go home again.”)

*Jones’s footnote ¹ (foot of p.520 Ojibwe / p.521 English): "It is customary to fast when in mourning." — the marker ¹ stands at "go into a fast" / kī∙i∙gwicimut in the English; the note explains the mourning custom behind the father’s decision. Carried verbatim.*

(5) Kägä‘t äjikīwät ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini. Kā‘tagwicink äntāwāt, mīsa‘ undcita kāwīn minwäntạⁿzī aᵘ a‘kiwäⁿzī. Mīdạc kägä‘t ki‘tcikạnōnigut īni‘ᵘ ōsạn. Cigwa mīnawā udci‘tcisäni midāsugun; kāmidāsugunạgạtnig mīnawā äjimādcāt aᵘ inini. Änitäbābạndạnk ∙i∙i∙māⁿ ayānit īni‘ᵘ ugwisîsäⁿsạn, kāwīnigu nisitạm udicinawāsîn īni‘ᵘ ugwisîsäsạn. Äji∙a∙‘kwāntawät wäyābạmāt īni‘ᵘ ugwisîsạn, ä‘tawā, kuniginīn pici‘kiwạn\! Kāwīn iciminwäntạⁿzī ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ īnini. Mānū kīwäwạg, īniᵘ widcīwāt īni‘ᵘ piji‘kiwạn. Cigwa tagwicinōg äntāwāt. Äjipīndigäwāt äntāwāt. Cigwa owâbạmiguwān īniyōsạn. “Āninti kigwisäⁿs?” “Nōs, kāna kīwâbạmāsi? Mīgu ca waᵉa kā‘pici‘kīwit aᵘ ningwisis.”

**Geget ezhi-giiwed a’aw inini. Gaa-dagwishing endaawaad, mii sa onjida gaawiin minwendanzii aw akiwenzii. Mii dash geget gichi-ganoonigod iniw oosan. Zhigwa miinawaa odaji-jiseni midaaso-gon; gaa-midaaso-gonagadinig miinawaa ezhi-maajaad aw inini. Ani-debaabandang ii’imaa ayaanid iniw ogwizhizhensan, gaawiin igo nisidam odizhinawaasiin iniw ogwizhizhensan. Ezhi-akwaandawed weyaabamaad iniw ogwizhizhensan, etawaa, goniginiin bizhikiwan\! Gaawiin izhi-minwendanzii a’aw inini. Maanoo giiwewag, iniw wiijiiwaad iniw bizhikiwan. Zhigwa dagwishinoog endaawaad. Ezhi-biindigewaad endaawaad. Zhigwa owaabamigowaan iniw oosan. “Aaniindi gigwizhens?” “Noos, gaa na giwaabamaasii? Mii go zhaa wa’aw gaa-bizhikiiwid aw ningwisis.”**

*Truly then back home went the man. After he was come at home, thereupon with reason was the old man displeased. Accordingly then, in truth, he was given severe scolding by his father. In due time another period of ten days came around; when the ten days were up, then again departed the man. When he was come in sight of the place where his son was, he was at a loss to make out the way his son appeared to him. When up the tree he climbed to see his son, strange to behold, there was a buffalo\! Not pleased was the man (at the sight of it). Nevertheless back home they went, he went in company with the buffalo. In due time they arrived at home. Then they went into where they lived. Now was he seen by his father. “Where is your little son?” “My father, don’t you see him? This, in truth, is that son of mine who has since become a buffalo.”*

Geget ezhi-giiwed a’aw inini (truly the man went home). Gaa-dagwishing endaawaad, mii sa onjida gaawiin minwendanzii aw akiwenzii (after he arrived home, with reason the old man was displeased). Mii dash geget gichi-ganoonigod iniw oosan (and so truly he was scolded hard by his father). Zhigwa miinawaa odaji-jiseni midaaso-gon (in time another ten days came round); gaa-midaaso-gonagadinig miinawaa ezhi-maajaad aw inini (when the ten days were up the man set off again). Ani-debaabandang ii’imaa ayaanid iniw ogwizhizhensan (when he sighted where his little son was), gaawiin igo nisidam odizhinawaasiin iniw ogwizhizhensan (he could not make out how his son looked to him — \*stem flagged\*). Ezhi-akwaandawed weyaabamaad iniw ogwizhizhensan (when he climbed up to see his son), etawaa, goniginiin bizhikiwan\! (alas, behold, a buffalo\!) Gaawiin izhi-minwendanzii a’aw inini (the man was not pleased). Maanoo giiwewag, iniw wiijiiwaad iniw bizhikiwan (nevertheless home they went, he going along with the buffalo). Zhigwa dagwishinoog endaawaad (in time they arrived home). Ezhi-biindigewaad endaawaad (then they went into where they lived). Zhigwa owaabamigowaan iniw oosan (now he was seen by his father). “Aaniindi gigwizhens?” (“where is your little son?”) “Noos, gaa na giwaabamaasii? (“my father, don’t you see him?) Mii go zhaa wa’aw gaa-bizhikiiwid aw ningwisis.” (this is that son of mine who has become a buffalo.” — \*bizhikii- ‘become a buffalo’ stem flagged\*)

(6) “Ningwisis, mīnạgu iᵘ äciki‘tciminwäntạmạn ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ pici‘ki tci∙u∙gwisîsiyạn?”

**“Ningwisis, mii na go iw ezhi-gichi-minwendaman a’aw bizhiki ji-ogwizhisiyan?”**

*“My son, are you now so greatly pleased that you would have this buffalo for your son?”*

“Ningwisis, mii na go iw ezhi-gichi-minwendaman (“my son, are you so greatly pleased) a’aw bizhiki ji-ogwizhisiyan?” (that you would have this buffalo for your son?”)

(7) Mīnawā kīgitu ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ inini: “Nōs, kägu‘ nickātisi‘kän. Māgicā näyāp tā∙a∙nicinābäwi.”

**Miinawaa giigido a’aw inini: “Noos, gego nishkaadizikan. Maagizhaa neyaab da-anishinaabewi.”**

*Again spoke the man, saying: “My father, do not be angry. Perhaps he will be changed back into a human being.”*

Miinawaa giigido a’aw inini (again the man spoke): “Noos, gego nishkaadizikan (“my father, do not be angry). Maagizhaa neyaab da-anishinaabewi.” (perhaps he will become a human being again.”)

(8) Mī wīni∙i∙‘ᵘ äcictcigātänig ‘i⁰i‘ᵘ äntāt aᵘ a‘kiwäⁿsī; ābi‘tawint ạbiwạn ki‘tci∙a∙sinīn; mīnawā mi‘tigōn padạ‘kisōwạn imāⁿ äntāt aᵘ a‘kiwäⁿzī. Cigwa dạc udinān īni‘ᵘ ugwisisạn: “Tạga kudci‘taᵘ ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ kimicōmis īni‘ᵘ udasinīmạn\!”

**Mii wenii’iw ezhi-izhichigaadenig i’iw endaad aw akiwenzii; aabita-ayi’iing abiwan gichi-asiniin; miinawaa mitigoon badaki-zoowan imaa endaad aw akiwenzii. Zhigwa dash odinaan iniw ogwisan: “Daga gojitoo a’aw gimishoomis iniw odasiniiman\!”**

*Now, this was the way the place was made where dwelt the old man; at the rear of the lodge was a great stone; furthermore, there was a post standing there where lived the old man. And now (the father) said to his son: “Come, make trial of your grandfather’s stone\!”*

Mii wenii’iw ezhi-izhichigaadenig i’iw endaad aw akiwenzii (now this is how the place where the old man dwelt was made); aabita-ayi’iing abiwan gichi-asiniin (at the rear of the lodge there was a great stone — \*aabita-ayi’iing ‘at the rear’ flagged\*); miinawaa mitigoon badaki-zoowan imaa endaad aw akiwenzii (and a post stood there where the old man lived). Zhigwa dash odinaan iniw ogwisan (and now he said to his son): “Daga gojitoo a’aw gimishoomis iniw odasiniiman\!” (“come, make trial of your grandfather’s stone\!” — \*odasiniiman ‘his stone’ obviative\*)

(9) Kägä‘t pimipạsigwī aᵘ pici‘kī; pimināsi‘kawāt īni‘ᵘ asinīn äci∙ō∙mbiwäba∙ᵉwāt; ki‘tciwâsa icpĭming. I‘pisowạn īni‘ᵘ ạsinīn, näyāp pangicinōn. Kayä īni‘ᵘ mi‘tigōn; mīsa i⁰i‘ᵘ äjipạkwā‘kusänit īni‘ᵘ mi‘tigōn.

**Geget bimibazigwii aw bizhiki; biminaazikawaad iniw asiniin ezhi-ombiwebawaad; gichi-waasa ishpiming. Ipisowan iniw asiniin, neyaab bangishinoon. Gaye iniw mitigoon; mii sa i’iw ezhi-bakwaakozenid iniw mitigoon.**

*Truly up rose the Buffalo; on coming to where the stone was, he tossed it into the air; a great way up in the air it went, then back again it fell. The same (thing happened) to the post, whereupon then it was knocked to the ground.*

Geget bimibazigwii aw bizhiki (truly up rose the Buffalo); biminaazikawaad iniw asiniin (coming up to the stone) ezhi-ombiwebawaad (he tossed it up into the air — \*stem flagged\*); gichi-waasa ishpiming (a great way up in the air). Ipisowan iniw asiniin (the stone flew up — \*stem flagged\*), neyaab bangishinoon (then back it fell). Gaye iniw mitigoon (and the post too); mii sa i’iw ezhi-bakwaakozenid iniw mitigoon (and so the post was knocked to the ground — \*stem flagged\*).

(10) Mīsa‘ i‘ᵘ änäntạnk ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ a‘kiwäⁿzī: “Kägä‘t kạnạbạtc mạnito witug ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ nōjis.” Mīsa cigwa nōntamowāt īgi‘ᵘ mämāntitōwāt, īgi‘ᵘ mạnitōwipiji‘kiwạg ki‘tciwạdciwink ändanạ‘kīwāt, äjikīgitowāt: “Ạmbäsa, kutcigagwätibänimātā ‘aᵃa‘ᵘ kāki∙i∙gucinut\! Mīgu iᵘ wâbạnk tcimādcāyạnk tcimawinäwạnk.” Kägä‘t wäyābanĭnig äjimādcāwāt.

**Mii sa iw enendang a’aw akiwenzii: “Geget ganabaj manidoo widog a’aw noozhis.” Mii sa zhigwa noondamowaad igiw memaandidowaad, igiw manidoo-bizhikiwag gichi-wajiwing endana’kiiwaad, ezhi-giigidowaad: “Ambesa, goj-gagwe-dibenimaadaa a’aw gaa-gii-gwiishimonid\! Mii go iw waabang ji-maajaayang ji-mawinewang.” Geget weyaabaninig ezhi-maajaawaad.**

*Therefore then thought the old man: “In truth, perhaps my grandson may be a manitou.” Thereupon when (the Buffaloes) heard about it, the ones that were large, the manitou Buffaloes that dwell on a great mountain, they said: “Behold, let us try to prevail over him that has fasted\! Therefore to-morrow will we set forth to attack him.”² Truly, when the morrow was come, they then departed hence.*
