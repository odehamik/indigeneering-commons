> *Read the [entry note](/) first. Not a fable — an Anishinaabe ontology carried as story. Come in to wonder.*

*Fiero restoration by Weshki (sites.google.com/site/weshki). Four-layer gloss added by the Jones Corpus project, 2026-06-14 — Weshki’s Fiero and Jones’s English preserved unchanged; gloss line added.*

1)  > **Ningoding bezhig anishinaabe gii-bi-onjibaagoban owidi Baa'oting; bangi bookwaawigane.  
    > **Once a certain man came from yonder Sault; he was slightly hunched in the back.

Ningoding bezhig anishinaabe (once a certain man) gii-bi-onjibaagoban (came from) owidi Baa’oting (yonder Sault); bangi bookwaawigane (he was slightly hunched in the back).

2)  > **Mii dash omaanh gii-na'aangabigobanen, ikwewan omaa gaa-wiidigemaad megwaa anooj gii-izhichigewaad anishinaabeg, gii-manidookaazowaad.  
    > **And so here\* he dwelt with his wife's family; for here he had married a woman at a time when thepeople were accomplishing all sorts of things, when they did miracles through the manitou.

Mii dash omaanh gii-na’aangabigobanen (and so here he settled and dwelt), ikwewan omaa gaa-wiidigemaad (having married a woman here) megwaa anooj gii-izhichigewaad anishinaabeg (at a time when the people were doing all sorts of things), gii-manidookaazowaad (when they worked wonders through the manitou).

\* On the north shore of Lake Superior, either at Nipigun or Kaministiqua.

3)  > **Moonzhag gii-gischi-wiikondiwag wenizhishing miijim; gaa wiikaa sa ogii-ashamaasiwaawaan iniw anishinaaben, gaawiin ganage ogii-nandomaasiwaawaan gegoo waa-izhochigewaadin \[waa-izhichigewaadin\].  
    > **Many a great feast they celebrated with food that was choice; yet never did they feed that man, not even did they ever invite him when they were going to bring something to pass.

Moonzhag gii-gischi-wiikondiwag (many a great feast they held) wenizhishing miijim (with food that was choice); gaa wiikaa sa ogii-ashamaasiwaawaan iniw anishinaaben (yet never did they feed that man), gaawiin ganage ogii-nandomaasiwaawaan (nor did they ever invite him) gegoo waa-izhochigewaadin \[waa-izhichigewaadin\] (when they were going to do something).

4)  > **Ningoding idash beboong gii-amanisowaag gischi-jiingwenig aki, gii-mamaazikaa aki.  
    > **Now, once in the winter-time they became alarmed at a great rumbling in the earth, the earth shook.

Ningoding idash beboong (now once in the winter) gii-amanisowaag (they became alarmed) gischi-jiingwenig aki (at a great rumbling in the earth), gii-mamaazikaa aki (the earth shook).

5)  > **Mii dash gii-zegiziwaad.  
    > **Thereupon they became afraid.

Mii dash gii-zegiziwaad (thereupon they became afraid).

6)  > **"Mii sa awe wiindigoo," gi-ikidowag.  
    > **"It is the Wiindigoo, " they said.

“Mii sa awe wiindigoo,” (“it is the Wiindigoo,”) gi-ikidowag (they said).

7)  > **Eshkam igo besho gii-bi-ayaawan.  
    > **Nearer it kept coming.

Eshkam igo besho gii-bi-ayaawan (nearer and nearer it kept coming).

8)  > **Mii dash iw apii gii-atoowaad anookaajigan; aano-gii-gagwejindiwaad; gaawiin dash awiya ogii-gashkitoosiin ji-miigaanaad wiindigoon.  
    > **Accordingly then they made an offering of goods; they put questions one to another, but to no purpose; and no one had power to fight against the Wiindigoo.

Mii dash iw apii gii-atoowaad anookaajigan (and then they made an offering of goods); aano-gii-gagwejindiwaad (they questioned one another in vain); gaawiin dash awiya ogii-gashkitoosiin (but no one was able) ji-miigaanaad wiindigoon (to fight the Wiindigoo).

9)  > **Gegapii igo aapiji gii-mamaazikaa aki.  
    > **And at last ever so much did the earth tremble.

Gegapii igo aapiji gii-mamaazikaa aki (and at last ever so much did the earth tremble).

10) > **Gaawiin gaye ogas\[h\]kitoosiinawaa is\[h\]kote ji-biskaneg; aanind gaye anishinaabeg gaawiin mamikawisiiwag.  
    > **And they were unable to start a fire into a blaze; and some of the people too were (scared) out of their senses.

Gaawiin gaye ogas\[h\]kitoosiinawaa is\[h\]kote ji-biskaneg (and they could not make a fire blaze up); aanind gaye anishinaabeg gaawiin mamikawisiiwag (and some of the people were out of their senses).

11) > **Gegapii idash onandomaawaan iniw anishinaaben bwaakwaawiganenid.  
    > **So finally they sent for the man with the hunch in his back.

Gegapii idash onandomaawaan iniw anishinaaben (so finally they sent for the man) bwaakwaawiganenid (with the hunch in his back).

12) > **Ogii-bagidinamawaawaan iniw obagijigewiniwaa, ogii-igowaan idash:  
    > **They presented him with the offerings they had made. And they were told:

Ogii-bagidinamawaawaan iniw obagijigewiniwaa (they presented him with the offerings they had made), ogii-igowaan idash (and they were told by him):

13) > **"Mii na iw bijiinag mikwenimiyeg noongom zegiziyeg?"  
    > **"Is it only now, when you are scared, that you think of me?"

“Mii na iw bijiinag mikwenimiyeg (“is it only now that you think of me) noongom zegiziyeg?” (now that you are afraid?”)

14) > **Gaawiin ogii-odaapinanziinan iniw aano-miinind.  
    > **He did not accept what was vainly offered him.

Gaawiin ogii-odaapinanziinan (he did not accept) iniw aano-miinind (what was offered him in vain).

15) > **Gii-ani-zaaga'am, endaad gii-izhaad.  
    > **He went on out of doors, to where he dwelt he went.

Gii-ani-zaaga’am (he went on out of doors), endaad gii-izhaad (to where he dwelt he went).

16) > **Ogii-anoonaan wiiwan wewiib omakizinan ji-ozhitoonid.  
    > **He had his wife quickly make him some moccasins.

Ogii-anoonaan wiiwan (he had his wife) wewiib omakizinan ji-ozhitoonid (quickly make him moccasins).

17) > **A'aw idash ikwe wewiib gii-makizinike.  
    > **And the woman hurriedly went to work upon the moccasins.

A’aw idash ikwe (and the woman) wewiib gii-makizinike (hurriedly went to work on the moccasins).

18) > **Zhigwa aapiji besho bi-ayaa wiindigoo.  
    > **Already very close was the Wlndigo now coming.

Zhigwa aapiji besho bi-ayaa wiindigoo (already very close was the Wiindigoo now coming).

19) > **Bebezhigweg bas\[h\]gweginoon.  
    > **There was a dressed skin for each foot.

Bebezhigweg bas\[h\]gweginoon (there was a dressed skin for each foot).

20) > **Mii dash gii-maajaad a'aw bwaakwaawigang.  
    > **Thereupon departed the hunchback.

Mii dash gii-maajaad a’aw bwaakwaawigang (thereupon the hunchback departed).

21) > **Aazha aanind anishinaabeg gaawiin ganage maajiisiiwag.  
    > **Already some of the people did not even move.

Aazha aanind anishinaabeg (already some of the people) gaawiin ganage maajiisiiwag (did not even move).

22) > **"Ayaangwaamizin\!" odinaan wiiwan.  
    > **"Do you take care\!" he said to his wife.

“Ayaangwaamizin\!” (“do you take care\!”) odinaan wiiwan (he said to his wife).

23) > **Zaaga'igan imaanh gii-ayaani, wekwaagamiing zhingwaakwag ayaawag.  
    > **A lake was oyer there, at the far end (of which) were some pines.

Zaaga’igan imaanh gii-ayaani (a lake was over there), wekwaagamiing zhingwaakwag ayaawag (at its far end were some pines).

24) > **Waabang gigizheb bi-inaabikan imaanh zhingwaakwag gii-ayaawaad.  
    > **"To-morrow, in the morning, do you come and look there where the pines are.

Waabang gigizheb (tomorrow in the morning) bi-inaabikan imaanh (do you come and look there) zhingwaakwag gii-ayaawaad (where the pines are).

25) > **Ninga-waabamaa.  
    > **I am going to watch for him.

Ninga-waabamaa (I am going to watch for him).

26) > **Apii idash miigaadiyaang, maanoo wiinikam \[wiinitam\] da-noondaagozi.  
    > **And when we fight, just let the cry of him be heard first.

Apii idash miigaadiyaang (and when we fight), maanoo wiinikam \[wiinitam\] da-noondaagozi (just let his cry be heard first).

27) > **Baanimaa niin ishkwaaji ninga-noondaagoz," gii-ikido.  
    > **Not till afterwards will my cry be heard," he said.

Baanimaa niin ishkwaaji ninga-noondaagoz,” (not till afterwards will my cry be heard,”) gii-ikido (he said).

28) > **Wayaabang idash gigizheb gii-awi-inaabi a'aw ikwe.  
    > **So on the morrow, in the morning, thither went the woman to look.

Wayaabang idash gigizheb (so on the morrow in the morning) gii-awi-inaabi a’aw ikwe (the woman went there to look).

29) > **Mii dash ezhi-gii-waabamaad zaagikweninid, mii dash gii-zhagas\[h\]kitaanid.  
    > **Thereupon she saw his head sticking out (from where he was in hiding), and then (she saw) him bob down again.

Mii dash ezhi-gii-waabamaad zaagikweninid (thereupon she saw his head sticking out), mii dash gii-zhagas\[h\]kitaanid (and then she saw him bob down again).

30) > **Mii dash imaanh gii-bii'aad ji-bi-odisigod a'aw waa-miigaanaad.  
    > **And so there he waited for the coming of the one he was to fight.

Mii dash imaanh gii-bii’aad (and so there he waited) ji-bi-odisigod a’aw waa-miigaanaad (for the coming of the one he was to fight).

31) > **Biiwaabik-zaka'on obi-dakonaan a'aw wiindigoo.  
    > **A metal staff the Wiindigoo came holding in the hand.

Biiwaabik-zaka’on obi-dakonaan a’aw wiindigoo (a metal staff the Wiindigoo came holding in hand).

32) > **Mii dash gii-bazigwiid a'aw bwaakwaawigang.  
    > **And then up stood the hunchback.

Mii dash gii-bazigwiid a’aw bwaakwaawigang (and then up stood the hunchback).

33) > **Aanakwad epiitaasing gii-akooziwag.  
    > **Up as far as the clouds was how high they stood.

Aanakwad epiitaasing gii-akooziwag (up as far as the clouds was how high they stood).

34) > **Nitam ogii-makamaan i'iw zaka'on, ogii-apagidoon imaanh zaaga'iganing iw zaka'on; gakina gii-dwaase i'iw zaaga'igan.  
    > **First he took the staff from (the Wiindigoo), he flung the staff into yonder lake; all the ice of the lake was crushed.

Nitam ogii-makamaan i’iw zaka’on (first he took the staff away from him), ogii-apagidoon imaanh zaaga’iganing iw zaka’on (he flung the staff into yonder lake); gakina gii-dwaase i’iw zaaga’igan (all the ice of the lake was crushed).

35) > **Mii dash gii-noondaagozid wiindigoo, gakina gii-wanimikawiwag anishinaabeg.  
    > **And so when the cry of the Wiindigoo was heard, all the people fainted away.

Mii dash gii-noondaagozid wiindigoo (and so when the Wiindigoo’s cry was heard), gakina gii-wanimikawiwag anishinaabeg (all the people fainted away).

36) > **Ishkwaaj wiin gii-noondaagozi a'aw bwaakwaawigang, nawaj wiin gii-gizhiiwe; dibishkoo giizhig biigos\[h\]kaag mii iw gaa-inweg.  
    > **And afterwards was heard the cry of the hunchback, louder still was the sound of his voice; as if the sky were rent asunder, such was the sound of the cry.

Ishkwaaj wiin gii-noondaagozi a’aw bwaakwaawigang (afterwards the hunchback’s cry was heard), nawaj wiin gii-gizhiiwe (louder still was the sound of his voice); dibishkoo giizhig biigos\[h\]kaag (as if the sky were rent asunder) mii iw gaa-inweg (such was the sound of the cry).

37) > **Mii dash gii-miigaadiwaad aaniweg igo ginwenzh.  
    > **Thereupon they fought with each other for some time.

Mii dash gii-miigaadiwaad (thereupon they fought one another) aaniweg igo ginwenzh (for some time).

38) > **Odayan owiijiwaabanin a'aw wiindigoo.  
    > **With (her) pet dog was the Wiindigoo.

Odayan owiijiwaabanin a’aw wiindigoo (with her pet dog was the Wiindigoo).

39) > **Gii-ozhimo a'aw animoosh.  
    > **Away fled the dog.

Gii-ozhimo a’aw animoosh (away fled the dog).

40) > **Apii dash bekite'ond a'aw wiindigoo, "Yoo, nisayenh, ninisigoo\!"  
    > **And when (the Wiindigoo) was struck, " Yo, my elder brother, I am being slain\!"

Apii dash bekite’ond a’aw wiindigoo (and when the Wiindigoo was struck), “Yoo, nisayenh, ninisigoo\!” (“Yo, my elder brother, I am being slain\!”)

41) > **Mii iw gii-nisind a'aw wiindigoo.  
    > **Then was the Wiindigoo slain.

Mii iw gii-nisind a’aw wiindigoo (then was the Wiindigoo slain).

42) > **Ikwe a'aw\!  
    > **It was a woman\!

Ikwe a’aw\! (it was a woman\!)

43) > **Moonzhag idash gii-naniisaanendamoog anishinaabeg ji-bi-dagwishininid osayeyan.  
    > **And ever were the people in fear that her elder brother would come.

Moonzhag idash gii-naniisaanendamoog anishinaabeg (and ever were the people in fear) ji-bi-dagwishininid osayeyan (that her elder brother would come).

44) > **Miziwe omaanh gii-debwewidam gii-noondaagozid apii nesind a'aw wiindigoo.  
    > **Everywhere over this region was the sound of her voice heard when she, the Wiindigoo, was slain.

Miziwe omaanh gii-debwewidam (everywhere over this region the sound was carried) gii-noondaagozid (when her voice was heard) apii nesind a’aw wiindigoo (when she, the Wiindigoo, was slain).

45) > **Mii sa ekoozid.  
    > **That is as far as (the story) goes.

Mii sa ekoozid (that is as far as the story goes).
