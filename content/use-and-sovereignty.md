# Use & Sovereignty

This is **ceremonial and relational work, not content.** It is Indigenous work, held under Indigenous law, carried by Maya Chacaby (Odehamik) through MythOS substrate two.

**You may read it. That is not the same as the right to use it.**

Do not scrape, extract, train on, mine, index into a model, redistribute, or remix any part of this — the stories, the method, the footnotes, the figures, the numbers — **without consent.**

Consent here is **relational and specific.** It is not granted by silence. It is not granted by the absence of a wall. It is not granted by a file having loaded in your browser. The text being public is a fact about access, not a transfer of rights. To ask is the first protection. → [Request access](request-access)

The living language data — the lexicon, the morpheme ledger — is **not published here at all.** There is no public copy to take. It moves only by relation, vetted by the source.

The stories are public and **never explained.** Where they carry ceremonial work, it is named and left to the listener. Understanding lives in community, by relation — not in a download.

**Built by and for Indigenous people.** If you are an Indigenous engineer, a language-reclamation builder, someone growing an LLM that speaks your own language: this is for you, and the door is real. → [Request access](request-access)
